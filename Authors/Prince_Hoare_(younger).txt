'''Prince Hoare''' (1755&nbsp;– 22 December 1834) was an English painter, [[dramatist]] and [[librettist]]. "Prince" is a [[given name]], not a royal title.

==Life==
Hoare was born in [[Bath, Somerset|Bath]], the son of painter [[William Hoare]] and his wife. He was named 'Prince' after [[Prince Hoare (elder)|his father's brother]], a sculptor. He studied art from an early age, and became well known as a painter of  portraits and historical scenes. His sister [[Mary Hoare]] was also a noted painter.

Later in his life, Hoare wrote 20 plays. He also compiled the ''[https://books.google.co.uk/books?id=obM_AAAAcAAJ&pg=PA289&lpg=PA289&dq=Freetown+French+1794+Commodore&source=bl&ots=JhQBj-KaVO&sig=fANV9Pkj3AgR0_tdeGSSHR2zETE&hl=en&sa=X&ved=0ahUKEwjU76nW77bZAhWELsAKHeMgDcQQ6AEIQjAJ#v=onepage&q=Freetown%20French%201794%20Commodore&f=false Memoirs of Granville Sharp]'' (1820), based on the British [[abolitionism|abolitionist]]'s manuscripts, family documents and material from the African Institution, London.<ref>Hoare, Prince. ''Memoirs of Granville Sharp, Esq., Composed from his own Manuscripts and Other Authentic Documents in the Possession of his Family and of the African Institution London'', 2 vols. (1820, 2nd edn. 1828)</ref>
