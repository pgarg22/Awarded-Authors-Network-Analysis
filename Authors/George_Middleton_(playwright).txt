'''George Middleton''' (October 27, 1880 in [[Paterson, New Jersey]] &ndash; December 23, 1967 in [[Washington, D.C.]]) was an American playwright, director, and producer.

==Career==
In 1902 George Middleton first had his work produced professionally<ref name=LOCsummary/> when he worked on the stage adaptation of ''The Cavalier'' with [[Paul Kester]] and the novel's author, [[George W. Cable]].<ref name=Turner1966/> In 1911 he published ''Embers: And Other One-Act Plays''; it was among the earliest such collections published by an American.<ref name=Lewis1922/> Middleton authored many one-act plays and was a known proponent of the form.<ref name=Gainor2003/>

He collaborated with [[Guy Bolton]] several times. The comedy ''[[Polly With a Past (play)|Polly With a Past]]'' (1917) was one of their successes, running for 315 performances and making a star of [[Ina Claire]]. A [[Polly With a Past (film)|film adaptation]] was made in 1920, and in 1929 the stage musical ''Polly'' was based on it. In 1919 they had another success with ''Adam and Eva'', selected by theater critic [[Burns Mantle]] as one of the best plays of 1919–1920.<ref name=Mantle1920/> This play was also made into a [[Adam and Eva|film]].

In addition to his original work, Middleton also translated French plays, and dramatized novels.

He was an early crusader for the rights of playwrights and was instrumental in the creation of the [[Minimum Basic Agreement]] and the subsequent 1926 battle to get theatrical managers to agree to its terms.<ref name=Walsh2004/>

From 1927 to 1929 he was president of the [[Dramatists Guild of America]].<ref name=LOCsummary/> In 1947, he published a well-reviewed memoir, ''These Things are Mine''.<ref name=MilkwJrn/>

==Personal==
Middleton graduated from [[Columbia University]] in 1902.<ref name=ColU/><ref>{{Cite book|last=Columbia College (Columbia University). Office of Alumni Affairs and Development|url=http://archive.org/details/ldpd_12981092_015|title=Columbia College today|last2=Columbia College (Columbia University)|date=1967-69|publisher=New York, N.Y. : Columbia College, Office of Alumni Affairs and Development|others=Columbia University Libraries}}</ref>

On October 29, 1911, Middleton married actress and suffragette [[Fola La Follette|Flora Dodge 'Fola' La Follette]], the oldest child of U.S. Senator [[Robert Marion La Follette, Sr.]] of [[Wisconsin]] and [[Belle Case La Follette]]. His wife was the inspiration for his one-act play ''Tradition''.<ref name=Paxton2013/>
