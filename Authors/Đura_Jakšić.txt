'''Georgije "Đura" Jakšić''' ({{lang-sr-Cyrl|Георгије Ђура Јакшић}}; 27 July 1832 – 16 November 1878) was a Serbian poet, painter, writer, dramatist and [[Bohemianism|bohemian]].<ref name="auto">{{Cite book|url=https://fedorabg.bg.ac.rs/fedora/get/o:17559/bdef:Content/get|title=Slikarstvo Đure Jakšića|last=V. Mišić|first=Snežana|publisher=University of Belgrade|year=2017}}</ref>

==Biography==
[[File:Rodna kuća Đure Jakšić u Srpskoj Crnji - ulična fasada.jpg|thumb|left|Jakšić's birth house in Srpska Crnja]]

Đura Jakšić was born as Georgije Jakšić in [[Srpska Crnja]], [[Austrian Empire]] (present-day [[Serbia]]). His father was a [[Serbian Orthodox]] priest.<ref name="auto"/> Georgije's early education took place in [[Timișoara]] and [[Szeged]].<ref name="auto"/> He lived for a time in [[Zrenjanin]], where he began studying painting under [[Konstantin Danil]].<ref name="auto"/> He later studied [[fine arts]] in [[Vienna]] and [[Munich]]<ref name="auto"/> but the [[Revolutions of 1848 in the Austrian Empire|revolution of 1848]] interrupted his education, which he never finished. He took active part in the 1848 Revolution<ref name="auto4">{{Cite web|url=https://www.danas.rs/kultura/umetnik-revolucionarnog-doba/|title=Umetnik revolucionarnog doba|last=Tasić|first=Piše: J.|website=Dnevni list Danas|language=sr-RS|access-date=2020-01-05}}</ref> and was wounded while fighting in [[Srbobran]]. After the revolution he moved to [[Belgrade]], [[Principality of Serbia]], where he served as a schoolteacher, a [[lector]] in a state-owned printing office,<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.novosti.rs/vesti/beograd.74.html:627204-Beogradske-price-Oprostaj-od-velikog-Djure-Jaksica|title=Beogradske priče: Oproštaj od velikog Đure Jakšića|website=www.novosti.rs|language=sr-Latn|access-date=2020-01-05}}</ref> and in various other jobs, although he was often unemployed.<ref name="auto"/> As a political liberal, he was persecuted by authorities.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.prva.rs/zivot/zivotne-price/47732/djura-jaksic-lutalica-buntovnik-revolucionar|title=Đura Jakšić: Lutalica, buntovnik, revolucionar|website=prva.rs|language=sr|access-date=2020-01-05}}</ref> Jakšić died in 1878, having had taken part in the uprising against the Turks in [[Bosnia and Herzegovina]].

Jakšić is one of the most expressive representatives of Serbian [[Romanticism]]. According to Serbian literary critic [[Jovan Skerlić]], Jakšiċ was influenced mainly by [[Sándor Petőfi]], the great Hungarian poet of the 1848 Revolution, and [[Lord Byron]]'s poetry depicting the [[Greek War of Independence]].{{cn|date=January 2020}}

==Literary works==
Đura Jakšić  wrote about forty short stories<ref name="auto2">{{Cite web|url=https://www.kcns.org.rs/agora/djura-jaksic-umetnik-i-buntovnik/|title=ЂУРА ЈАКШИЋ – УМЕТНИК И БУНТОВНИК|date=2019-04-26|website=Културни центар Новог Сада|language=sr-RS|access-date=2020-01-05}}</ref> and three full-length dramas in verse on historical themes:

*''[[Stanoje Glavaš]]'' (1878)
*The Migration of the Serbs (''Seoba Srbalja'', 1864)
*Elizabeth the Montenegrin Queen (''Jelisaveta kneginja crnogorska'', 1868) and the novel ''Warriors''.

He also wrote poems, several of which are considered among the best of 19th-century Serbian poetry: ''[[Na Liparu]]'' (On the Lipar Hill),<ref name="auto2"/> ''[[Put u Gornjak]]'' (The Road to Gornjak) and ''[[Mila (poem)|Mila]]'', which is dedicated to his first love Mila, who he intended to marry but never found courage to tell her.<ref name="auto3">{{Cite web|url=http://politikin-zabavnik.co.rs/pz/tekstovi/bo%D1%98e-ljubavi|title=Сликари и модели {{!}} Politikin Zabavnik|website=politikin-zabavnik.co.rs|access-date=2020-01-05}}</ref> He also drew sketches of Mila, one of which later became his famous painting "[[Devojka u Plavom]]" (The Girl in Blue).<ref name="auto3"/> Other notable poems are ''Otadžbina'' (Fatherland), ''Veče'' (Evening) and ''Ponoć'' (Midnight). Jakšić published ''Pripovetke'' (Short Stories), which was released posthumously in two volumes on two occasions, 1882–1883 and 1902 in Belgrade.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://plus.sr.cobiss.net/opac7/bib/76468748|title=Приповетке. Књ. 1 :: COBISS+|last=Марибор|first=IZUM-Институт информацијских знаности|website=plus.sr.cobiss.net|language=sr|access-date=2020-01-05}}</ref> Through them he expressed his pessimism and bitterness about the harsh blows life and people had dealt him. A popular motif in Jakšić's work is the [[Battle of Kosovo]], [[Kosovo myth]], eagles and [[Serbian epic poetry]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://scindeks.ceon.rs/Error.aspx?aspxerrorpath=/article.aspx|title=Kosovski mit u poeziji Đure Jakšića|website=scindeks.ceon.rs|access-date=2020-01-05}}</ref>

==Artistic opus==
Jakšić painted around 200 paintings.<ref name="auto2"/> The main influences on Jakšić were [[Rembrandt]], [[Diego Velázquez]] and [[Peter Paul Rubens]]. Jakšić's work varies in quality,<ref name="auto1">{{Cite journal|last=Vrbaski|first=Milena|title=Tumačenje ličnosti i dela Đure Jakšića, diplomski rad M. Vrbaški.doc|url=https://www.academia.edu/32025709|language=en}}</ref> ranging from masterpieces to half-professional paintings. According to [[Novak Radonić]], the biggest problem with his painting is the improper use of anatomy.<ref name="auto1"/> His most praised picture is "[[The Lady in Blue]]",<ref name="auto1"/> which was used for promotion during the reopening of the [[National Museum of Serbia]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.blic.rs/kultura/vesti/devojka-u-plavom-koja-hoda-manekenka-ozivela-cuveni-portret-djure-jaksica/kmkv9sf|title="DEVOJKA U PLAVOM" KOJA HODA Manekenka oživela čuveni portret Đure Jakšića|last=Milojković|first=M.|website=Blic.rs|language=sr|access-date=2020-01-05}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.rts.rs/page/stories/sr/story/16/kultura/3572094/izlozba-djura-jaksic-izmedju-mita-i-stvarnosti-u-narodnom-muzeju-.html|title=Izložba "Đura Jakšić između mita i stvarnosti" u Narodnom muzeju|last=Serbia|first=RTS, Radio televizija Srbije, Radio Television of|website=www.rts.rs|access-date=2020-01-05}}</ref>

The following paintings by Đura Jakšić are part of the collection of the National Museum in Belgrade:

* Autoprotrait
* Battle of Montenegris
* Kosovo
* Night Watch (''Na straži'')
* ''Ubistvo Karađorđa'' (The Assassination of [[Karađorđe Petrović]])
* Strahinja Ban ([[Strahinja Banović]])
* Knez Lazar Hrebeljanović ([[Lazar of Serbia]])
* Girl in Blue (''Devojka u plavom'') 
* Portrait of director Ćirić
* Car Dušan ([[Stephen Uroš IV Dušan of Serbia]])
* Knjaz [[Milan Obrenović IV]]
*Nevesta Baja Pivljanina (The bride of [[Bajo Pivljanin]])<ref name="auto4"/>

Đura Jakšić  is included in [[The 100 most prominent Serbs]].{{cn|date=January 2020}} His house in [[Srpska Crnja]] is currently used as a Memorial Museum and for poetry performances.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.politika.rs/scc/clanak/356666/Poezija-pod-lipama|title=Поезија под липама|last=Ђукић|first=Ђуро|website=Politika Online|access-date=2020-01-05}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.kucadjurejaksica.rs/?p=1409|title=Скадарлија и кућа у којој је живео Ђура Јакшић-уточиште уметника и уметности {{!}} KUCA DJURE JAKSICA|language=sr-RS|access-date=2020-01-05}}</ref>

Jakšić was one of the leaders of Serbian Romanticism<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.rastko.rs/knjizevnost/jderetic_knjiz/jderetic-knjiz_07.html|title=Jovan Deretic: Kratka istorija srpske knjizevnosti|website=www.rastko.rs|access-date=2020-01-28}}</ref> and one of the country's greatest painters of that movement, together witk [[Novak Radonić]].<ref name="auto"/> Although he wrote a number of loosely organized romantic plays, he is mostly known for his paintings and poetry. His poems include sonnets, lyrics, patriotic songs and full-scale epics. His favorite theme is nature and the patriotism.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2020-08-08|title=Đura Jakšić: Portret razočaranog romantika i doživotnog rodoljuba|url=https://www.danas.rs/kultura/djura-jaksic-portret-razocaranog-romantika-i-dozivotnog-rodoljuba/|access-date=2021-01-03|website=Dnevni list Danas|language=sr-RS}}</ref>

The award for the best book of poetry in the [[Serbian language]] bears his name.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.danas.rs/kultura/dragan-jovanovic-danilov-dobitnik-nagrade-nb-djura-jaksic-iz-srpske-crnje/|title=Dragan Jovanović Danilov, dobitnik nagrade NB "Đura Jakšić" iz Srpske Crnje|website=Dnevni list Danas|language=sr-RS|access-date=2020-01-05}}</ref>

Jakšić was also a teacher and a professor; schools and colleges throughout Serbia bear his name.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://osdjurajaksicrakovica.nasaskola.rs/|title=ОШ "Ђура Јакшић" - Раковица|website=osdjurajaksicrakovica.nasaskola.rs|access-date=2020-01-05}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.osdjurajaksic.edu.rs/|title=ОШ"Ђура Јакшић" {{!}} Зрењанин|language=sr-RS|access-date=2020-01-05}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.os-djurajaksic.edu.rs/|title=ОШ "Ђура Јакшић|website=ОШ „Ђура Јакшић" Ковин|language=sr-rs|access-date=2020-01-05}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.osdjurajaksiczajecar.edu.rs/home|title=OS Djura Jaksic|website=www.osdjurajaksiczajecar.edu.rs|language=en|access-date=2020-01-05}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.djurajaksic.edu.rs/|title=АКТУЕЛНОСТИ|website=www.djurajaksic.edu.rs|access-date=2020-01-05}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://djurajaksic-paracin.edu.rs/|title=Osnovna skola "Djura Jaksic", Paracin|website=djurajaksic-paracin.edu.rs|access-date=2020-01-05}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.osdjurajaksickg.edu.rs/|title=Насловна - My website|website=www.osdjurajaksickg.edu.rs|access-date=2020-01-05}}</ref> A number of anecdotes about Jakšić was published.<ref>{{Cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=ubsZAAAAMAAJ&q=%C4%90ura+Jak%C5%A1i%C4%87|title=Đura Jakšić u zapisima i anegdotama|last=Kostić|first=Milan P.|date=1952|publisher=Novo pokoljenje|language=sr}}</ref>
