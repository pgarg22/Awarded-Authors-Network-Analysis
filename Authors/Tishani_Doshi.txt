'''Tishani Doshi''' (born 9 December 1975) is an [[India]]n [[poet]], [[journalist]] and dancer based in [[Chennai]].<ref>{{cite web|url= https://www.theguardian.com/profile/tishanidoshi|title= Tishani Doshi Profile|publisher=The Guardian|access-date=8 July 2021}}</ref> In 2006 she won the [[Forward Prizes for Poetry|Forward Prize]] for her debut poetry book ''Countries of the Body''. Her poetry book ''A God at the Door'' has been shortlisted for the 2021 Forward Forward Prize under best poetry collection category.<ref>{{cite web|url= https://www.theguardian.com/books/2021/jun/08/forward-poetry-prizes-shortlist-former-young-peoples-laureate-caleb-femi|title=Forward Poetry Prizes Shortlist|publisher=The Guardian|date=8 June 2021|access-date=8 July 2021}}</ref>

==Early life and education==
Doshi was born in [[Madras]], India, to a [[Welsh people|Welsh]] mother and [[Gujarati people|Gujarati]] father. She completed her graduation from Queen's College, [[North Carolina]]. She graduated with a master's degree in creative writing from the [[Johns Hopkins University]].<ref>{{cite web|url= https://www.poetryfoundation.org/poets/tishani-doshi|title=Tishani Doshi - Literary Profile|publisher=Poetry Foundation|access-date=8 July 2021}}</ref>

==Career==
Doshi works as a freelance writer and journalist. She has worked with choreographer [[Chandralekha (dancer)|Chandralekha]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://theasianwriter.wordpress.com/2008/11/11/a-pleasure-to-meet-tishani-doshi/|title=''A Pleasure to Meet Tishani Doshi'' interview|access-date=11 May 2009}}</ref> 
Her short story "Lady Cassandra, Spartacus and the dancing man" was published in its entirety in the journal ''[[The Drawbridge]]'' in 2007.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.thedrawbridge.org.uk/issue_7/lady_cassandra_spartacus_and_t/|title=Lady Cassandra, Spartacus and the dancing man|newspaper=[[The Drawbridge]]|date=Winter 2007 |access-date=11 May 2009}}</ref> Her poetry collection, ''Everything Begins Elsewhere'',<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.coppercanyonpress.org/books/everything-begins-elsewhere-by-tishani-doshi/|title=Copper Canyon Press: Everything Begins Elsewhere, poetry by Tishani Doshi|website=www.CopperCanyonPress.org|access-date=30 October 2017}}</ref> was published by [[Bloodaxe Books]] in the UK in 2012 and by [[Copper Canyon Press]] in the US in 2013.

==Award winning works==
In 2001 Tishani won the [[Eric Gregory Award]] for young poets under 30 years. Tishani's first poetry collection, ''Countries of the Body'' was launched in 2006 at the Hay-on-Wye festival on a platform with [[Seamus Heaney]], [[Margaret Atwood]], and others. The opening poem, "The Day we went to the Sea", won the 2005 [[British Council]]-supported All India Poetry Competition. The book won the 2006 [[Forward Poetry Prize]] for best first collection.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/2/hi/entertainment/5407622.stm|title=Tishani Doshi, 31, wins the £5,000 best first collection prize for ''Countries of the Body''|work=[[BBC News]]|date=2006-10-05 |access-date=11 May 2009}}</ref>  Her first novel, ''The Pleasure Seekers'', was published by [[Bloomsbury Publishing|Bloomsbury]] in 2010. It was long-listed for the [[Orange Prize]] in 2011,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.orangeprize.co.uk/prize.html|title=Corporate Website of Orange - orange.com|website=www.OrangePrize.co.uk|access-date=30 October 2017}}</ref> and also shortlisted for [[The Hindu Best Fiction Award]] in 2010.{{fact|date=July 2021}}

Her poetry book ''Girls Are Coming Out of the Woods''<ref>{{cite web|url=https://harpercollins.co.in/book/girls-are-coming-out-of-the-woods/|title=HarperCollinsPublishers India - Girls Are Coming Out of the Woods|website=HarperCollins.co.in|access-date=30 October 2017}}</ref> was a Poetry Book Society Recommendation and was shortlisted for the [[Ted Hughes Award]] in 2018. Her 2019 book, ''Small Days and Nights'', was shortlisted for the 2020 [[Ondaatje Prize]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=Shortlist for £10,000 Ondaatje Prize announced|url=https://www.booksandpublishing.com.au/articles/2020/04/21/149455/shortlist-for-10000-ondaatje-prize-announced/|last=|first=|date=2020-04-21|website=Books+Publishing|language=en-AU|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=2020-05-07}}</ref>
Tishani has been a finalist at Outlook-Picador Non-Fiction Competition. She also received honorary invitation to the poetry galas of [[Hay Festival]] of 2006 and Cartagena Hay Festival of 2007.{{fact|date=July 2021}}

==Other activities==
Tishani Doshi delivered the keynote address at the 13th annual St. Martin Book Fair<ref>{{cite web|url=http://houseofnehesipublish.com/sxm/st-martin-book-fair/|title=St. Martin Book Fair - Welcome to House of Nehesi Publishers|website=HouseOfNehesiPublish.com|access-date=30 October 2017}}</ref> on the Caribbean island of [[St. Maarten]] ([[Saint Martin (island)|St. Martin]]) in 2015. Her book ''The Adulterous Citizen – poems  stories  essays'' (2015) was launched at the festival by House of Nehesi Publishers.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://houseofnehesipublish.com/sxm/|title=Welcome to House of Nehesi Publishers|website=HouseOfNehesiPublish.com|access-date=30 October 2017}}</ref>

She writes a blog titled "Hit or Miss" on [[Cricinfo]],<ref>{{cite news|url=http://content.cricinfo.com/iplpage2/content/site/iplpage2/genre.html?genre=281|title=''Hit or Miss'' main page|access-date=11 May 2009}}</ref> a cricket-related website. In the blog, which she started writing in April 2009, Tishani Doshi makes observations and commentaries as a television viewer of the second season of the [[Indian Premier League]]. She is also collaborating with cricketer [[Muttiah Muralitharan]] on his biography, to be published when he retires.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://content.cricinfo.com/iplpage2/content/story/398082.html|title=First cricinfo article|access-date=11 May 2009}}</ref>
