'''Peter Ross Leckie''' (born 6 May 1957) is a Scottish writer of historical novels, best known for his ''[[Carthage]]'' trilogy. 

==Biography==
Leckie attended [[Drumtochty Castle]] Preparatory School and [[Fettes College]].<ref>[http://www.fettes.com/foundation/interviews/leckie.htm Fettes website]</ref> He studied classics at [[Corpus Christi College, Oxford]], where he was also President of the Junior Common Room.<ref>[http://www.canongate.net/RossLeckie Canongate]</ref><ref name="leckie">{{cite web |url=http://www.heraldscotland.com/sport/spl/aberdeen/a-classic-tale-of-two-cities-saturday-interview-ross-leckie-when-hollywood-called-the-edinburgh-fund-management-spinmeister-was-lost-for-words-finds-mark-williamson-1.110113 |title=A classic tale of two cities; Saturday interview: Ross Leckie When Hollywood called, the Edinburgh fund management spinmeister was lost for words |publisher=[[The Herald (Glasgow)]] |date=23 August 2003 |page=21 |accessdate=14 November 2014 |quote=Fact File 1957: Born in Irvine, Ayrshire. Education: Drumtochty Castle Preparatory School, Kincardineshire; Fettes College, Edinburgh; Corpus Christi College, Oxford University; Royal Agricultural College, Cirencester}}</ref> He met [[Vera Wülfing-Leckie|Vera Wülfing]], a student of languages from Germany, and they married in 1979. They moved to Scotland in 1981. The couple had four children.<ref name="Leckie">{{cite news|last=Leckie|first=Ross|author-link=Ross Leckie|date=8 February 2021|title=Dr Vera Wülfing-Leckie obituary|newspaper=[[The Guardian]]|url=https://www.theguardian.com/society/2021/feb/08/dr-vera-wulfing-leckie-obituary|access-date=9 February 2021}}</ref> In 1995 Leckie married Sophie Drinkall, and they had six children. They divorced in 2019.
