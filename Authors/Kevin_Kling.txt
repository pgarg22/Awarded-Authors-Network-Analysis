'''Kevin Kling''' is an [[United States|American]] [[Storytelling|storyteller]] and a commentator for [[National Public Radio]].

Kling grew up in [[Osseo, Minnesota]], and graduated from [[Gustavus Adolphus College]] in 1979 with a Bachelor of Arts in Theatre. He began his career in the [[Minneapolis-St. Paul|Twin Cities]] during the 1990s with two plays that wrote: ''21A'' and ''Fear and Loving in Minneapolis''.<ref name="NPR">{{cite web | title = Kevin Kling Returns | publisher = National Public Radio  | date = January 2002 | url = https://www.npr.org/programs/atc/features/2002/jan/kling/020104.kling.html | access-date = September 17, 2015}}</ref> His one-man show ''Home and Away'' premiered at the [[Seattle Repertory Theatre]] and then moved to [[Second Stage Theatre]] (NYC) under the direction of [[David Esbjornson]], also a Gustavus Adolphus College alumnus.<ref name="RRE">{{cite news | title = Storyteller receives A.P. Anderson Award | newspaper = Redwing Republican Eagle| date = May 24, 2009| url = http://www.republican-eagle.com/content/storyteller-receives-ap-anderson-award | access-date = September 17, 2015}}</ref><ref>{{cite web | title = Kevin Kling Profile at Festival of Homiletics | publisher =Festival of Homiletics  | url = https://www.festivalofhomiletics.com/blog.aspx?m=4349&post=3127 | access-date = September 17, 2015}}</ref> Kling and Minneapolis-based accordionist and singer Simone Perrin have collaborated on two works, ''How? How? Why? Why?'' and ''Breakin' Hearts and Takin' Names''.

In 1993, Kling won the [[Whiting Awards|Whiting Award]] for drama.<ref>{{cite web | title = Kevin Kling 1993 Whiting Award | publisher =Whiting Award Foundation  | url = http://www.whiting.org/awards/winners/kevin-kling#/ | access-date = September 17, 2015}}</ref> In 2009, he won the A. P. Anderson Award for Outstanding Contributions to Literature and the Arts in Minnesota.<ref name="RRE" />

Kling has also made regular storytelling contributions to [[NPR]]â€™s ''[[All Things Considered]]''. He has released several CD collections, including a boxed set, ''Collected Stories''. His first published book of short stories was ''The Dog Says How'' followed by four more titles.

Kling has not been slowed in his work by a birth defect that shriveled his left arm and a motorcycle accident that completely paralyzed his right arm.<ref name="NPR" />
