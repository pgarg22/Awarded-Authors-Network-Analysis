'''Karen Cushman''' (born October 4, 1941)<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.arnenixoncenter.org/finding_aids/anc0006.htm|title=Karen Cushman Papers|publisher=Arne Nixon Center |accessdate=2008-12-13}}</ref> is an [[American people|American]] writer of historical fiction.

==Career==
Her 1995 novel ''[[The Midwife's Apprentice]]'' won the [[Newbery Medal]] for children's literature, and her 1994 novel ''[[Catherine, Called Birdy]]'' won a [[Newbery Honor]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ala.org/ala/mgrps/divs/alsc/awardsgrants/bookmedia/newberymedal/newberyhonors/newberymedal.cfm|title=Newbery Medal and Honor Books, 1922-Present|publisher=American Library Association|accessdate=2008-12-13}}</ref> She has a bachelor of arts degree in Greek and English from [[Stanford University]]. In addition, she has a master's degree in human behavior as well as a master's degree in museum studies.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.achuka.co.uk/special/cushman.htm|title=Karen Cushman|last=Bowlan|first=Cheryl|publisher=ACHUKA|accessdate=2008-12-13}}</ref> For eleven years she was adjunct professor in the Museum Studies Department at [[John F. Kennedy University]] before resigning in 1996 to write full-time.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.childrensliteraturenetwork.org/aifolder/aipages/ai_c/cushman.php|title=Karen Cushman|last=Reimer|first=Julie|publisher=Children's Literature Network|accessdate=2012-05-29|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130715062614/http://www.childrensliteraturenetwork.org/aifolder/aipages/ai_c/cushman.php|archive-date=2013-07-15}}</ref> She lives and writes on [[Vashon Island, WA|Vashon Island]] near Seattle, Washington.<ref name=Mid2>Author's biography, inside rear cover, "The Midwife's Apprentice" 2nd edition, {{ISBN|978-0-547-72217-7}}</ref>
