'''Maiya Williams''' (born December 18, 1962 in [[Corvallis, Oregon]]) is an American [[author]], [[television producer]], and [[screenwriter]].

==Early life==
The daughter of a [[forester]], Williams grew up in New Haven, Connecticut and Berkeley, California.  She attended [[Berkeley High School (Berkeley, California)|Berkeley High School]], and graduated from [[Harvard College]] in 1984 where she was the first African-American officer of ''[[The Harvard Lampoon]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.chicagotribune.com/entertainment/ct-xpm-2014-01-29-ct-alexis-wilkinson-harvard-lampoon-20140130-story.html|title=Alexis Wilkinson's path to Harvard Lampoon president's chair|first=Christopher|last=Borrelli|website=chicagotribune.com|accessdate=16 December 2018}}</ref>

==Career==
Williams has written and produced various television series including ''[[Roc (TV series)|Roc]]'', ''[[Amen (TV series)|Amen]]'', ''[[The Fresh Prince of Bel Air]]'', ''[[The Wayans Bros.]]'', ''[[MADtv|MAD TV]]'', ''[[Rugrats]]'', ''[[Futurama]]'' and ''[[The Haunted Hathaways]]''.<ref>{{IMDb name|id=0931223}}</ref>

Williams wrote her first book for children, ''[[The Golden Hour (novel)|The Golden Hour]]'', in 2004. A time travel adventure set in the French Revolution, it was followed by ''The Hour of the Cobra'' (2006),  set in ancient Egypt, and ''The Hour of the Outlaw'' (2007), set in nineteenth century California. ''The Fizzy Whiz Kid: The Rise and Fall of a Hollywood Nobody'', a contemporary cautionary tale about fame, was published in 2010. Her most recent book is ''Middle School Cool'' (2014).<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.fantasticfiction.com/w/maiya-williams/|title=Maiya Williams|website=www.fantasticfiction.com|accessdate=16 December 2018}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Williams lives in Pacific Palisades with TV writer [[Patric M. Verrone]], their children, and dog.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.maiyawilliams.com/|title=Maiya Williams - Author of The Golden Hour Series|website=Maiya Williams|accessdate=16 December 2018}}</ref>
