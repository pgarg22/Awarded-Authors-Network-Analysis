'''Karen Watkins''' (née Jones, born 1948),<ref name="CC-novels">{{cite web|url=http://catrincollier.co.uk/novels.php|title=Catrin Collier – novels|access-date=1 June 2013|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130818002228/http://catrincollier.co.uk/novels.php|archive-date=18 August 2013|url-status=dead}}</ref> writing as '''Catrin Collier''', is a Welsh novelist known for her historical works, especially those in the ''Hearts of Gold'' series, set in her home town of [[Pontypridd]] between 1930 and 1950,<ref name="CC-novels" /> the first of which was adapted as a BBC drama in 2003. She also writes under the pen names '''Katherine John'''<ref name="CC-links">{{cite web|url=http://catrincollier.co.uk/links.php|title=Catrin Collier – links|access-date=1 June 2013|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110611224938/http://www.catrincollier.co.uk/links.php|archive-date=11 June 2011|url-status=dead}}</ref>/ '''K. A. John''' (crime novels),<ref name="WoW">{{cite web|url=http://www.literaturewales.org/writers-of-wales/i/129559/|title=COLLIER, CATRIN – List of Writers|work=The Writers of Wales Database|publisher=Literature Wales|access-date=2 June 2013|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140302052537/http://www.literaturewales.org/writers-of-wales/i/129559/|archive-date=2 March 2014|url-status=dead}}</ref> '''Katherine Hardy'''<ref name="Amazon">{{cite web|url=https://www.amazon.co.uk/Catrin-Collier/e/B001HOYHWU|title=Catrin Collier: Books, Biogs, Audiobooks, Discussions|publisher=[[Amazon.com]]|access-date=1 June 2013}}</ref> (novelisations of television programmes)<ref name="WoW" /> and '''Caro French'''<ref name="CC-links" /> (modern fiction).<ref name="WoW" />

== Early life ==

Collier is of [[East Prussia]]n descent, with her mother, Gerda Salewski, born in [[Allenstein]], East Prussia, in 1926.<ref name="CC-novels" /> Her father, Glyn Jones, was a [[Welsh Guards]]man serving in Germany when he met Gerda.<ref name="CC-novels" /> They were married in [[Pontypridd]] in July 1947.<ref name="CC-novels" /> Collier was born there in 1948 and grew up in the town.<ref name="CC-novels" />

As a [[comprehensive school]] teacher, she taught English and drama to [[A level]] in schools in [[Swansea]] and [[West Glamorgan]].<ref name="WoW" />

==Career==

Her book ''One last Summer'' is based on [[World War II|war-time]] diaries kept by her mother and maternal grandmother.<ref name="CC-novels" /> and is recommended by the Holocaust Memorial Day Trust for young people wanting to learn about [[the Holocaust]] .<ref name="HMDT">{{cite web|url=http://www.hmd.org.uk/resources/bibliography|title=Bibliography|access-date=1 June 2013}}</ref> It was a 2008 finalist for the [[Romantic Novelists' Association]]'s Book of the Year.<ref name="Marjacq" />

== Television ==

In 2003, ''Hearts of Gold'' was adapted by [[BBC Wales]] as a two-part drama, directed by [[Richard Laxton]] and co-written by [[Matthew Baylis]].<ref name="BBC-1">{{cite web|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/programmes/p00ky85t|title=Hearts of Gold, Episode 2, "You've been found out"|publisher=[[BBC]]|access-date=1 June 2013}}</ref> BBC Wales' head of drama [[Matthew Robinson (producer)|Matthew Robinson]] described her as "the [[Catherine Cookson]] of Wales".<ref name="Deans">{{cite web|url=https://www.theguardian.com/media/2002/jun/05/broadcasting.bbc2|title=BBC snaps up 'new Catherine Cookson'|last=Deans|first=Jason|date=5 June 2002|work=[[The Guardian]]|access-date=1 June 2013}}</ref>

==Other work==

As well as novels, she writes short stories, plays and non-fiction,<ref name="WoW" /> and has had work published in magazines including published in ''[[Woman (UK magazine)|Woman]]'', ''[[Woman's Own]]'' and ''[[Woman's Weekly (UK magazine)|Woman's Weekly]]''.<ref name="WoW" />

==Personal life==

After living in Germany<ref name="WoW" /> and America,<ref name="RHNZ">{{cite web|url=http://www.randomhouse.co.nz/books/catrin-collier/one-blue-moon-9780712615037.aspx|title=One Blue Moon by Catrin Collier|access-date=1 June 2013|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://archive.is/20130703160227/http://www.randomhouse.co.nz/books/catrin-collier/one-blue-moon-9780712615037.aspx|archive-date=3 July 2013}}</ref> Collier now lives on the [[Gower Peninsula]], near Swansea.<ref name="Accent">{{cite web|url=http://www.accentpress.co.uk/Contributor/547/Catrin-Collier.html|title=Catrin Collier|publisher=Accent Press|access-date=2 June 2013|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120503124222/http://www.accentpress.co.uk/Contributor/547/Catrin-Collier.html|archive-date=3 May 2012}}</ref> She is represented by the [[literary agent|literary agency]] Marjacq Scripts.<ref name="Marjacq">{{cite web|url=http://www.marjacq.com/books/catrin-collier|title=Catrin Collier|publisher=Marjacq Scripts|access-date=2 June 2013|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130106022517/http://www.marjacq.com/books/catrin-collier|archive-date=6 January 2013}}</ref>

She is a member of [[Swansea Writers' Group]], which encouraged her from the outset of her career before she had published a novel.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.catrincollier.co.uk/news.php |title=Archived copy |access-date=11 April 2014 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140413142347/http://www.catrincollier.co.uk/news.php |archive-date=13 April 2014 |url-status=dead }}</ref>

{{lang|cy|Ty Catrin}}, an adult education centre in Pontypridd, was named in her honour in 2002.<ref name="CaA">{{cite web|url=http://www.contactanauthor.co.uk/authorpage.php?id=220|title=Catrin Collier|work=ContactAnAuthor|access-date=2 June 2013|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20081014173409/http://www.contactanauthor.co.uk/authorpage.php?id=220|archive-date=14 October 2008|url-status=dead}}</ref>
