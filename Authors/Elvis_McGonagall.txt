'''Elvis McGonagall''' (born '''Richard Smith'''; December 22, 1960) is a [[Scotland|Scottish]] [[poet]] and [[stand-up comedy|stand-up comedian]] who is especially notable for [[poetry slam]] performances.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.manchesterconfidential.co.uk/Entertainment/Theatre-and-Comedy/Hovis-in-Wonderland |archive-url=https://archive.is/20120910081818/http://www.manchesterconfidential.co.uk/Entertainment/Theatre-and-Comedy/Hovis-in-Wonderland |url-status=dead |archive-date=10 September 2012 |title=Hovis in Wonderland |publisher=Manchester Confidential |date=1 April 2008 |accessdate=5 January 2012}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.mmegi.bw/2006/December/Tuesday19/357015713959.html|title=Botswana's oral poetress scoops award|publisher=Mmegi.bw|date=19 December 2006|accessdate=26 March 2009}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.accessmylibrary.com/coms2/summary_0286-28785235_ITM |title='It's like rap with positive feelings' |newspaper=Nottingham Evening Post |date=30 November 2006 |accessdate=26 March 2009 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20121015192053/http://www.accessmylibrary.com/coms2/summary_0286-28785235_ITM |archivedate=15 October 2012 }}</ref>

== Biography ==

McGonagall was born in [[Perth, Scotland]], and now lives in [[Stroud]] in [[South West England]].<ref>{{cite news|last=Logan|first=Brian|title=Elvis McGonagall|url=https://www.theguardian.com/culture/2009/aug/10/elvis-mcgonagall-fringe-review|accessdate=25 January 2014|newspaper=The Guardian|date=10 August 2009}}</ref> His stage name combines a reference to the notoriously bad poet [[William McGonagall]] with 'Elvis' "because it’s just so wrong, the wrongest name I could think of to go with McGonagall".<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.nickchurchill.org.uk/elvis-mcgonagall-purbecks-peoples-poet|title = Elvis McGonagall – Purbeck's people's poet – Nick Churchill}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.theguardian.com/culture/2009/aug/10/elvis-mcgonagall-fringe-review|title=Comedy review: Elvis McGonagall &#124; Gilded Balloon, Edinburgh|date=10 August 2009}}</ref>

== Poetry ==

=== Slam performances ===

McGonagall first performed publicly at the UK Allcomers' Slam at the [[Cheltenham Literature Festival]] in 2003, finishing runner-up: he went on to win the slam the following year.<ref name ="Elvis McGonagall">{{cite web|title=Elvis McGonagall: About|url=http://www.elvismcgonagall.co.uk/about.htm|accessdate=25 January 2014}}</ref>  His career saw significant progress in 2006, in which year McGonagall won the Spokefest UK Slam Championship and the World Slam Championship at the Rotterdam Poetry International Festival.<ref name ="Elvis McGonagall" />

McGonagall performs at poetry events nationwide, as well as compering at the Blue Suede Sporran Club.<ref>{{cite web|title=Elvis McGonagall|url=http://www.rbmcomedy.com/site/client.php?section_id=56|accessdate=25 January 2014|author=Richard Bucknall Management}}</ref>

=== Media performances ===

McGonagall has regularly appeared on [[BBC Radio 4|BBC Radio 4's]] [[Saturday Live (radio series)|''Saturday Live'']], beginning with contributions to the premier episode. He has appeared frequently on other BBC Radio 4 programmes, including [[Today (BBC Radio 4)|''Today'']], ''[[Last Word]]'' and ''[[Off the Page]]''as well as starring in two series of his own sitcom Elvis Mcgonagall Takes A Look On The Bright Side, Radio 4 Comedy of the Week stand-up special “Full Tartan Jacket” (2021) and a documentary “My Name Is Elvis” (2021).<ref name ="Elvis McGonagall" />

McGonagall has also appeared on various television programmes for a number of broadcasters. His credits include [[BBC One|BBC One's]] ''[[The One Show]]'', [[BBC Two|BBC Two's]] ''[[The Culture Show]]'' and [[Channel 4|Channel 4's]] 'Random Acts'.<ref name ="Elvis McGonagall" />
