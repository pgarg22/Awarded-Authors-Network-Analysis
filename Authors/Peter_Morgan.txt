'''Peter Julian Robin Morgan''', {{postnominals|country=GBR|size=100%|CBE}} (born Peter Julian Robin Morgenthau 10 April 1963) is a British [[screenwriter]] and [[playwright]] best known for his work on stage and film and television. He is known as the playwright behind ''[[The Audience (2013 play)|The Audience]]'' and ''[[Frost/Nixon (play)|Frost/Nixon]]'' and the screenwriter of ''[[The Queen (2006 film)|The Queen]]'' (2006), ''[[Frost/Nixon (film)|Frost/Nixon]]'' (2008), ''[[The Damned United]]'' (2009), and ''[[Rush (2013 film)|Rush]]'' (2013). Morgan is also known for writing the television films ''[[The Deal (2003 film)|The Deal]]'' (2003), ''[[Longford (film)|Longford]]'' (2006), and ''[[The Special Relationship (film)|The Special Relationship]]'' (2010). He currently serves as creator and showrunner of the [[Netflix]] series ''[[The Crown (TV series)|The Crown]]'' (2016–present).

In February 2017, Morgan was awarded a [[British Film Institute Fellowship]] (BFI).<ref name=morgan/>

==Early life==
Morgan was born in [[Wimbledon, London|Wimbledon]], [[London]]. His mother, Inga (''[[née]]'' Bojcek), was a [[Roman Catholic|Catholic]] [[Polish British|Pole]] who fled the [[Soviets]], and his father, Arthur Morgenthau, was a [[German Jews|German Jew]] who fled the [[Nazis]], arriving in London in 1933.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://business.highbeam.com/410951/article-1G1-162848160/impersonator|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150214174755/http://business.highbeam.com/410951/article-1G1-162848160/impersonator|url-status=dead|archive-date=14 February 2015|title=The Impersonator.(Peter Morgan) – HighBeam Business: Arrive Prepared}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=https://articles.latimes.com/2007/apr/22/entertainment/ca-morgan22/2|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150214181334/http://articles.latimes.com/2007/apr/22/entertainment/ca-morgan22/2|url-status=dead|archive-date=14 February 2015|work=Los Angeles Times|first=Patrick|last=Pacheco|title=No sides, no prisoners|date=22 April 2007}}</ref> His father died when Morgan was nine years old.<ref>Hanks, Robert (24 February 2007) [https://web.archive.org/web/20090215012117/http://www.independent.co.uk/news/people/profiles/peter-morgan-drama-king-437670.html "Peter Morgan: Drama king"], ''The Independent''. Retrieved on 14 January 2009.</ref> Morgan attended [[St Paul's School, London|St Paul's School]] in London<ref>{{cite web |title=The man who rewrites history |url=https://www.standard.co.uk/go/london/film/the-man-who-rewrites-history-7187030.html |website=Evening Standard |access-date=7 September 2019}}</ref> and boarding school at [[Downside School]], Somerset, and gained a degree in Fine Art from the [[University of Leeds]].

==Career==
=== 1990s ===
Morgan wrote television scripts during the 1990s, including an episode of ''[[Rik Mayall]] Presents...'' and the Comedy Premiere ''[[Comedy Premieres|The Chest]]''. He wrote the screenplay to the romantic comedy ''[[Martha, Meet Frank, Daniel and Laurence]]'' (1998).

=== 2000s ===
In 2002, Morgan had some success with the TV series ''[[The Jury (TV serial)|The Jury]]'' (2002). In 2003, Morgan broke through with ''[[The Deal (2003 film)|The Deal]]'', a television drama about the [[Blair-Brown deal|power-sharing deal between Tony Blair and Gordon Brown]] that was struck in the [[Granita (restaurant)|Granita]] restaurant in London in 1994. Blair was portrayed by [[Michael Sheen]], Sheen would return to playing Blair in ''The Queen'' and ''The Special Relationship''.

In 2006, ''[[The Queen (2006 film)|The Queen]]'' was released which showed how [[Death of Diana, Princess of Wales|the death of Princess Diana]] impacted Prime Minister [[Tony Blair]] and the [[British Royal Family|Royal Family]]. The film debuted at the [[Venice Film Festival]] where it received critical acclaim. The film received 6 [[Academy Award]] nominations including [[Academy Award for Best Picture|Best Picture]]. Morgan also received a nomination for [[Academy Award for Best Original Screenplay|Best Screenplay]]. Morgan received the [[Golden Globe Award]] for his screenplay from the [[Hollywood Foreign Press]], and [[Helen Mirren]] won numerous awards for playing the [[Elizabeth II of the United Kingdom|title role]] including the [[Academy Award for Best Actress|Academy Award]] and the [[Golden Globe Award for Best Actress - Motion Picture Drama|Golden Globe Award]]. 2006 also saw the release of ''[[The Last King of Scotland (film)|The Last King of Scotland]]'', the screenplay of which Morgan adapted with [[Jeremy Brock]]. In 2007 they jointly won a [[British Academy Film Awards|BAFTA Film Award]] for their work on the film. In May 2007, the 50th [[San Francisco International Film Festival]] honoured Morgan with the year's Kanbar Award for Excellence in Screenwriting.

Also in 2006, Morgan's first play, ''[[Frost/Nixon (play)|Frost/Nixon]]'', was staged at the [[Donmar Warehouse]] theatre in London. Starring [[Michael Sheen]] as [[David Frost]] and [[Frank Langella]] as [[Richard Nixon]], the play concerns [[The Nixon Interviews|the series of televised interviews]] that the disgraced former president granted Frost in 1977. These ended with his tacit admission of guilt regarding his role in the [[Watergate scandal]]. The play was directed by [[Michael Grandage]] and opened to enthusiastic reviews. The play transferred to Broadway in 2007 at the [[Bernard B. Jacobs Theatre]] where it ran from 21 April to 19 August. The play received 3 [[Tony Awards]] including one for Morgan for the [[Tony Award for Best Play|Best Play]]. Langella won the [[Tony Award for Best Actor in a Play]].

In 2008, the film adaptation of ''[[Frost/Nixon (film)|Frost/Nixon]]'' directed by [[Ron Howard]] was released, with Sheen and Langella playing the parts they had on stage. The film also starred [[Kevin Bacon]], [[Rebecca Hall]], [[Mathew Macfadyen]], [[Toby Jones]], [[Oliver Platt]], and [[Sam Rockwell]]. The film debuted at the [[London Film Festival]] to critical acclaim. [[Roger Ebert]] gave the film 4 stars writing praising the writing and the performances of the leads saying, "Frank Langella and Michael Sheen do not attempt to mimic their characters, but to embody them".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.rogerebert.com/reviews/frostnixon-2008|title= At the start, they both thought it would be Nixon/Frost|website= rogerebert.com|access-date= 1 November 2020}}</ref> Despite praise from critics the film was a box office failure. The film received 5 [[Academy Award]] nominations including [[Academy Award for Best Adapted Screenplay|Best Adapted Screenplay]] for Morgan.

In July 2009, filming began on ''[[The Special Relationship (film)|The Special Relationship]]'', the third film of Morgan's "Blair trilogy". The film focuses on Blair's (again played by Michael Sheen) relationship with US president [[Bill Clinton]], played by [[Dennis Quaid]], between 1997 and 2000. Morgan was originally scheduled to direct the film (making his directorial debut) but pulled out a month before filming began. He was replaced by [[Richard Loncraine]].<ref name="Andreeva">Andreeva, Nellie (23 June 2009). "[https://www.hollywoodreporter.com/hr/content_display/news/e3i0849a35b3cfeb859dd27aa3b7f5037c6 Peter Morgan leaves 'Relationship']". ''[[The Hollywood Reporter]]'' (Nielsen Business Media).</ref> The film debuted on [[HBO]] on 29 May 2010. The film received positive reviews with it receiving an 83% on [[Rotten Tomatoes]] with the consensus reading, "Well-cast and compellingly directed, The Special Relationship offers an intelligent look into the complex dynamic between two world leaders."<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.rottentomatoes.com/m/the_special_relationship|title= The Special Relationship|website= Rotten Tomatoes|access-date= 1 November 2020}}</ref> The film received 5 [[Primetime Emmy Award]] nominations including, one for Morgan for [[Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Writing for a Limited Series, Movie, or Dramatic Special|Outstanding Writing for a Limited Series or Television Movie]].

In 2008, Morgan was initially set to adapt the [[John le Carré]]'s ''[[Tinker, Tailor, Soldier, Spy]]'' into a screenplay for [[Working Title Films]] but dropped out and served as the film's executive producer.<ref>{{cite news|first= Francesca|last= Martin|title= Tinker, tailor, soldier, film star|url= http://books.guardian.co.uk/news/articles/0,,2283640,00.html|work= [[The Guardian]]|date= 4 June 2008|access-date= 4 June 2008|location=London}}</ref> In June 2009, it was announced that he would be the co-writer of ''[[Skyfall]]'', the 23rd [[James Bond film]].<ref>[http://news.bbc.co.uk/2/hi/entertainment/8098612.stm The Queen writer to pen new Bond], [[BBC News]], 13 June 2009</ref> ''[[The Daily Telegraph]]''{{'}}s Mandrake diary reported in April 2010 that Morgan had quit the production after [[Sam Mendes]] was hired to direct, and that [[Patrick Marber]] would replace him. MGM dismissed the ''Telegraph''{{'}}s claims as speculation.<ref>Eden, Richard (10 April 2010). "[https://www.telegraph.co.uk/culture/film/jamesbond/7575642/Sam-Mendes-in-James-Bond-mystery.html Sam Mendes in James Bond mystery]". ''[[The Daily Telegraph]]'' (Telegraph Media Group).</ref> In the end Morgan did not receive credit but rather it was [[Neal Purvis]], [[Neal Purvis and Robert Wade|Robert Wade]], and [[John Logan (writer)|John Logan]] who were credited as the film's screenwriters. He has since finished the script for ''[[Hereafter (film)|Hereafter]]'', a supernatural thriller "in the vein of ''[[The Sixth Sense]]''". [[DreamWorks Pictures|DreamWorks]] bought the screenplay [[spec script|on spec]] in March 2008.<ref>{{cite news|author= Siegel, Tatiana|title= Eastwood, Spielberg talking thriller|url= https://www.variety.com/article/VR1117995852.html?categoryid=13&cs=1&nid=2562|work= [[Variety (magazine)|Variety]]|publisher=[[Reed Business Information]]|date=13 November 2008|access-date=18 November 2008}}</ref> The development was later transferred to [[Warner Bros.]] and filming began in October 2009 under the direction of [[Clint Eastwood]].

=== 2010s ===
In 2013, the film ''[[Rush (2013 film)|Rush]]'' was released. The film is a [[Biographical film|biographical]] [[sports film]] centred on the [[Hunt–Lauda rivalry]] between two [[Formula One drivers]], the British [[James Hunt]] and the Austrian [[Niki Lauda]] during the [[1976 Formula One season|1976 Formula 1]] motor-racing season.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/film/rush/niki-lauda-james-hunt-facts-true-story/|title=Niki Lauda on Rush, James Hunt and the crash that changed his life|newspaper=telegraph.co.uk|access-date=4 August 2018}}</ref> The film was directed by [[Ron Howard]] and written by Morgan and starred [[Chris Hemsworth]], [[Daniel Brühl]], and [[Olivia Wilde]]. The film was a financial and critical success. The film received four [[British Academy Film Award]] nominations including [[BAFTA Award for Outstanding British Film|Outstanding British Film]].

In 2013, Morgan's play ''[[The Audience (2013 play)|The Audience]]'' debuted. The play revolves around the weekly meetings, called audiences, between [[Queen Elizabeth II]] and her  [[Prime Minister of the United Kingdom|prime ministers]] over the time period of her reign. [[Dame]] [[Helen Mirren]] reprised her role as the Queen. The play premiered in the [[West End theatre|West End]] at the [[Gielgud Theatre]] where she eventually won the [[Laurence Olivier Award for Best Actress|Olivier Award for Best Actress]]. A [[Broadway theatre|Broadway]] production opened in 2015 at the [[Gerald Schoenfeld Theatre]] which ran from 8 March to 15 June. Mirren also received the [[Tony Award for Best Actress in a Play]].

Morgan's next feature film would be [[Bohemian Rhapsody (film)|''Bohemian'' ''Rhapsody'']] (2018), a biopic about rock musician [[Freddie Mercury]] (portrayed by [[Rami Malek]]), the lead vocalist of the rock band [[Queen (band)|Queen]] was released.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/news/51942/218987|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20101015061213/http://www.rollingstone.com/music/news/51942/218987|url-status=dead|archive-date=15 October 2010|title=Writer Announced for Freddie Mercury Biopic|date=12 October 2010|access-date=12 October 2010|work=[[Rolling Stone]]}}</ref> Morgan has started writing the screenplay in 2010. The film was a massive box office success earning $903.7 million. The film however received a mixed critical response with critics praising Malek's central performances but criticizing its editing, directing and pacing. ''[[Time (magazine)|Time Magazine]]'' film critic [[Stephanie Zacharek]] wrote, "In strict filmmaking terms, ''Bohemian Rhapsody'' is a bit of a mess. Some of its scenes connect awkwardly, and it hits every beat of disaster and triumphs squarely, like a gong."<ref>{{cite web|url=https://time.com/5435386/bohemian-rhapsody-review/|title= Rami Malek Rises Above Bohemian Rhapsody's Messy Excess|website= Time Magazine|access-date= 1 November 2020}}</ref> Despite its criticism, the film [[List of accolades received by Bohemian Rhapsody|received various awards]] including four [[Academy Award]]s for [[Academy Award for Best Actor|Best Actor]], [[Academy Award for Best Sound Editing|Best Sound Editing]], [[Academy Award for Best Sound Mixing|Best Sound Mixing]], and [[Academy Award for Best Film Editing|Best Film Editing]].

Morgan is the creator and writer of the [[Netflix]] historical drama series ''[[The Crown (TV series)|The Crown]]'', a biographical story about the reign of Queen [[Elizabeth II]]. The first season starred [[Claire Foy]], [[Matt Smith (actor)|Matt Smith]], [[Vanessa Kirby]], as [[Queen Elizabeth II]], [[Prince Philip]], and [[Princess Margaret]]. [[Jared Harris]], and [[John Lithgow]] made supporting turns as [[King George VI]], and [[Winston Churchill]]. The series has received widespread critical acclaim and received multiple [[Primetime Emmy Award]] nominations including a wins for Foy for [[Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Lead Actress in a Drama Series|Outstanding Lead Actress in a Drama Series]], and Lithgow for [[Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Supporting Actor in a Drama Series|Outstanding Supporting Actor in a Drama Series]]. The casts saw changes for Seasons 3 and 4 with [[Olivia Colman]], [[Tobias Menzies]] and [[Helena Bonham Carter]] replacing, Foy, Smith, and Kirby. Morgan has received 3 [[Primetime Emmy Award]] nominations for writing the episodes, "[[The Crown (season 1)|Assassins]]", "[[The Crown (season 2)|Mystery Man]]", and "[[The Crown (season 3)|Aberfan]]".

=== 2020s ===
On 15 November 2020, the [[The Crown (season 4)|fourth]] series of ''The Crown'' was released to critical acclaim. According to the review-aggregator Rotten Tomatoes, the series holds 96% critics' consensus making it the highest rated series out of the four so far.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.rottentomatoes.com/tv/the_crown/s04|title= The Crown: Season 4|website= Rotten Tomatoes|access-date=8 June 2021}}</ref>

The season marks the introduction of [[Emma Corrin]] as [[Diana, Princess of Wales|Princess Diana]] and [[Gillian Anderson]] as British Prime Minister [[Margaret Thatcher]], with Olivia Colman, Tobias Menzies, Helena Bonham Carter and [[Josh O'Connor]] reprising their roles as Queen Elizabeth II, Prince Philip, Princess Margaret and [[Charles, Prince of Wales|Prince Charles]] respectively.

The fourth season has received many awards including [[American Film Institute Awards 2020|AFI Television Program of the Year]] for the fourth time in a row, four Golden Globes wins including [[78th Golden Globe Awards#Television|Best TV Series]] (in the last 40 years only another Drama Series, ''[[The Sopranos]]'' managed to achieve the same result in [[List of awards and nominations received by The Sopranos#Golden Globe Awards|2000]]), a Critics' Choice Award for Best Drama and 10 BAFTA nominations including Best Drama. In September 2021, the fourth season made history by winning in all 7 [[Primetime Emmys|Emmys]] Drama Categories<ref>{{cite web|url=https://decider.com/2021/09/20/the-crown-makes-emmys-history-by-winning-all-7-drama-categories/|title='The Crown' Makes Emmys History By Winning All 7 Drama Categories|website= Decider|access-date= 20 September 2021}}</ref> and earned [[Netflix]] its first major win in the history of the streaming giant (Best Drama),<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2021/09/19/arts/television/2021-emmys.html|title=With 'The Crown,' Netflix Finally Takes the Emmys Throne|website= The New York Times|access-date= 21 September 2021}}</ref> with Morgan receiving the award for Outstanding Writing. Prior to that, he  had personally received a [[73rd Writers Guild of America Awards#Television|WGA Award for Best Drama]] and a [[32nd Producers Guild of America Awards#Television|PGA Award for Outstanding Producer of Episodic Television, Drama]] for the fourth season.

Season five and six of ''The Crown'' are currently in production and will likely be released at the end of 2022 and 2023 respectively. Season six will be the last one according to creator Peter Morgan.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://variety.com/2020/tv/global/netflix-the-crown-season-six-returning-imelda-staunton-1234702228/|title=The Crown will get a sixth season after all|website= Variety|access-date= 8 June 2021}}</ref> [[Imelda Staunton]], [[Jonathan Pryce]] and [[Lesley Manville]] have been confirmed in the respective roles of Queen Elizabeth II, Prince Philip and Princess Margaret. Princess Diana will be played by [[Elizabeth Debicki]] and<ref>{{cite web|url=https://variety.com/2020/tv/news/the-crown-dominic-west-prince-charles-netflix-1234810172/|title=Dominic West Set to Play Prince Charles in Seasons 5 and 6 of 'The Crown'|website= Variety|access-date = 8 June 2021}}</ref> Prince Charles will be played by [[Dominic West]].

==Personal life==
Morgan married [[House of Schwarzenberg|Princess Anna Carolina von Schwarzenberg]], daughter of Czech politician and nobleman [[Karel Schwarzenberg|Prince Karl von Schwarzenberg]], civilly in London on 28 July 1997 and religiously at [[Murau]] in Austria on 6 September 1997. They have five children. They lived in [[Battersea]], [[South London]] until the winter of 2006, when the family moved to [[Vienna]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Peter Morgan Biography|url=https://www.imdb.com/name/nm0604948/bio?ref_=nm_ov_bio_sm|website=IMDB}}</ref> They separated in 2014, and he resides in [[London]]. Morgan was in a relationship with actress [[Gillian Anderson]] from summer 2016 until December 2020.

Morgan was appointed [[Commander of the Order of the British Empire]] (CBE) in the [[2016 New Year Honours]] for services to drama.<ref>{{London Gazette|issue=61450|supp=y|page=N9|date=30 December 2015}}</ref> In February 2017, he was awarded a [[British Film Institute Fellowship]] (BFI).<ref name=morgan>{{cite web|url=http://www.bfi.org.uk/news-opinion/news-bfi/announcements/peter-morgan-crown-queen-bfi-fellowship|title=The Crown writer Peter Morgan CBE to be awarded BFI Fellowship|date=2 February 2017|work=[[British Film Institute]]|access-date=9 February 2017}}</ref> In November 2019, Morgan was honoured by the [[American Film Institute]] with a tribute to his career at the AFI FEST 2019.<ref>{{cite web|title=Gala Premiere of THE CROWN Season Three at AFI FEST 2019|url=https://www.afi.com/news/gala-premiere-of-the-crown-season-three-at-afi-fest-2019/|website=[[American Film Institute]]|date=17 October 2019|access-date=23 April 2020}}</ref>
In July 2016, Morgan was presented with an [[Honorary Degree]] in Letters by the [[University of Leeds]], his alma mater.<ref>{{cite web|title=University announces honorary degrees 2016|url=http://www.leeds.ac.uk/news/article/3887/university_announces_honorary_degrees_2016|website=[[University of Leeds]]|date=8 July 2016|access-date=11 February 2020}}</ref>
