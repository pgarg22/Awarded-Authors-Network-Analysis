'''Kathryn Stripling Byer''' (November 25, 1944 – June 5, 2017), also called '''Kay Byer''', was an American poet and teacher. She was named by [[Governor of North Carolina|Governor]] [[Mike Easley]] as the fifth [[North Carolina Poet Laureate]] from 2005–09. She was the first woman to hold the position.

==Biography==
===Early life and education===
Kathryn Stripling was born in [[Camilla, Georgia]] in 1944. Her parents were C.M. Stripling, a farmer, and his wife, Bernice (née Campbell) Stripling.<ref name="New Georgia">{{cite web|last=Prestridge|first=Sam|title=Kathryn Stripling Byer (b. 1944)|url=http://www.georgiaencyclopedia.org/nge/Article.jsp?id=h-505|work=The New Georgia Encyclopedia|publisher=Georgia Humanities Council and the University of Georgia Press|accessdate=November 8, 2012}}</ref>

She went on to graduate with a [[Bachelor of Arts|bachelors]] in English from [[Macon, Georgia]]'s [[Wesleyan College]]<ref name=Sylva>{{cite news|title=Raleigh ceremony to celebrate Byer's selection as poet laureate|url=https://news.google.com/newspapers?id=kWxlAAAAIBAJ&sjid=AZQNAAAAIBAJ&dq=north-carolina-poet-laureate&pg=920%2C4882859|accessdate=November 8, 2012|newspaper=[[The Sylva Herald and Ruralite]]|date=June 9, 2005}}</ref><ref name=AAP>{{cite web|title=Kathryn Stripling Byer|url=http://www.poets.org/poet.php/prmPID/76|work=Poets.org|publisher=Academy of American Poets|accessdate=November 8, 2012}}</ref> and then received her [[Master of Fine Arts|M.F.A.]] from the [[University of North Carolina at Greensboro]], where she studied under [[Allen Tate]], [[Fred Chappell]], and [[Robert W. Watson]].<ref name="Literary Trails">{{cite book|last=Eubanks|first=Georgann|title=Literary Trails of the North Carolina Mountains|year=2007|publisher=University of North Carolina Press|location=Chapel Hill, North Carolina|page=50|isbn=978-0-8078-5833-2}}</ref> During this time at UNC-G, Byer decided to move to the mountains of North Carolina.<ref name="New Georgia"/>

===Career===
After receiving her M.F.A., Byer became [[Artist-in-residence|poet-in-residence]] at [[Western Carolina University]], 1988–98, as well as UNC-G in 1995 and [[Lenoir-Rhyne College]] in 1999.<ref name="New Georgia"/> She has published six full collections of poetry as well as some chapbooks. Her most recent collection is ''Descent'' published in 2012 by [[Louisiana State University Press]].<ref name=IW>{{cite news|last=Vitiello|first=Chris|title=Kay Byer, the N.C. Literary Hall of Fame and this weekend's N.C. Writers' Network conference|url=http://www.indyweek.com/indyweek/kay-byer-the-nc-literary-hall-of-fame-and-this-weekends-nc-writers-network-conference/Content?oid=3181954|accessdate=November 14, 2012|newspaper=Independent Weekly|date=October 31, 2012}}</ref>

===Poet laureateship===
In 2005, North Carolina Governor [[Mike Easley|Michael Easley]] appointed Byer to be the state's fifth [[poet laureate]] following [[Fred Chappell]] whose term ended in 2002. She was the first woman to hold the position.<ref name="past laureates">{{cite web|title=Past North Carolina Poets Laureate |url=http://www.ncarts.org/freeform_scrn_template.cfm?ffscrn_id=177&|publisher=North Carolina Arts Council|accessdate=November 5, 2012|url-status=dead|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20121027233637/http://www.ncarts.org/freeform_scrn_template.cfm?ffscrn_id=177&|archivedate=October 27, 2012}}</ref>

As part of her outreach program during her term as poet laureate, Byer maintained "My Laureate's Lasso", a blog that focused on North Carolina poets and poetry.<ref>{{cite web|title=My Laureate's Lasso|url=http://www.ncpoetlaureate.blogspot.com/|publisher=Kathryn Stripling Byer|accessdate=November 14, 2012}}</ref> She was also the judge for the North Carolina Poetry Society's Poet Laureate Award.<ref>{{cite web|title=Annual Adult Poetry Contest Judges|url=http://www.sleepycreek.org/poetry/judgesadult.htm|publisher=North Carolina Poetry Society|accessdate=14 November 2012|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20090629192836/http://www.sleepycreek.org/poetry/judgesadult.htm|archivedate=June 29, 2009}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Adult Contests and Awards|url=http://www.ncpoetrysociety.org/adultcontests/|publisher=North Carolina Poetry Society|accessdate=November 5, 2012}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Kathryn Stripling Byer was married to Western Carolina University professor Jim Byer. They had one daughter and last lived in [[Cullowhee, North Carolina]].<ref name="New Georgia"/>

==Death==
Kathryn Stripling  Byer died at the age of 72 in Cullowhee on June 5, 2017 from [[lymphoma]].{{citation needed|date=June 2017}}

==Works==
Some of Byer's poetry appeared in, among other periodicals, ''[[The Carolina Quarterly]]'', ''[[The Georgia Review]]'', ''[[The Hudson Review]]'', ''[[The Iowa Review]]'', ''[[Poetry (magazine)|Poetry]]'', and ''[[The Southern Review]]''.<ref name=AAP /> Her work often dealt with lives and hardships of western North Carolina mountain inhabitants, especially women, in earlier generations.<ref name="Literary Trails"/>

===Books===
Byer's books include:<ref name=AAP /><ref name=IW />
* ''The Girl in the Midst of the Harvest'' (1986), [[Associated Writing Programs]] award series
* ''Wildwood Flower'' (1992) 
* ''Black Shawl'' (1998)
* ''Catching Light'' (Louisiana State University Press, 2002)
* ''Wake'' (chapbook, 2003)<ref name="Literary Trails" />
* ''Coming to Rest'' (Louisiana State University Press, 2006)
* ''The Movable Nest: A Mother/Daughter Companion'' as co-editor with Kallet, Marilyn (Helicon Nine Editions, 2007)
* ''Southern Fictions'' (sonnet chapbook; Jacar Press, 2011)
* ''Descent'' (Louisiana State University Press, 2012)

===Essays===
Some of Byer's most notable essays include:<ref name="New Georgia" />
* "Turning the Windlass at the Well: Fred Chappell's Early Poetry" in ''Dream Garden: The Poetic Vision of Fred Chappell'' (1997)
* "Deep Water" in ''Bloodroot: Reflections on Place by Appalachian Women Writers'' (1998)
