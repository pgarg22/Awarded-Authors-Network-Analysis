'''Branislava Ilić''' ([[Niš]], 1970), is a [[playwright]], [[dramaturge]], [[screenwriter]], [[actress]] and [[prose]] writer from [[Serbia]].<ref>[http://rastko.rs/rastko/delo/15815 Branislava Ilić (biobibliografija)] Projekat Rastko, Drama i pozorište. (in Serbian)</ref>

==Biography==
Branislava attended the Secondary Acting School in Niš. She obtained her dramaturgy degree at the Faculty of Dramatic Arts in Belgrade. Her first professional engagement took place in the [[National Theatre in Niš|National Theater in Niš]].{{cn|date=July 2020}}

For three years she worked as a dramaturge at the [[National Theatre in Belgrade|National Theater in Belgrade]] (2008-2010). She has also collaborated at performances at the [[Serbian National Theater]] in [[Novi Sad]], [[Atelje 212]] in Belgrade, "Toša Jovanović" Theater in [[Zrenjanin]], [[Kruševac]] Theater, Puls Theater in [[Lazarevac]], [[Zvezdara Teatar]] in Belgrade, [[Madlenianum Opera and Theatre|Madlenianum]] in Zemun, Croatian National Theater "Ivan pl. Zajc" in [[Rijeka]], Bitef Theater in Belgrade, OP DADOV in Belgrade.{{cn|date=July 2020}}

Her short stories and dramas are published in magazines and collections. Dozens of her radio dramatic miniatures are produced as well as several screenplays for different TV formats.{{citation needed|date=January 2019}}

She is also a member of the [[Sterijino pozorje]] jury.<ref>[http://www.pozorje.org.rs/ziri-63-pozorja/ Sterijino pozorje jury], official site of the manifestation (Serbian)</ref> Selector of the [[Joakimfest]] 2021. <ref>[http://www.joakimvujic.com/joakimfest/jokamfest-20-en.html Joakimfest 2021], official site of the Joakimfest (Serbian)</ref>
