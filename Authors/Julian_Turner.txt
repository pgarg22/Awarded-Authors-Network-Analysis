{{short description|British poet and mental health worker (born 1955)}}
'''Julian Turner''' (born 1955) is a [[United Kingdom|British]] [[Poetry|poet]] and [[mental health]] worker.<ref>{{cite web|last=Harris|first=David|title=Crossing the Outskirts by Julian Turner|url=http://www.bookslut.com/fiction/2003_07_000156.php|publisher=BookSlut.com|accessdate=8 April 2012}}</ref> Turner was born in [[Cheadle Hulme]], [[Stockport]], then moved to [[Cheshire]] in 1955. He now lives in [[Otley]], [[Leeds]], [[West Yorkshire]], with his [[Domestic partnership|partner]] and their daughter.

He was educated at [[New College, Oxford]] ([[Matriculation|matriculated]] 1974) and [[Goldsmiths, University of London|Goldsmiths College, University of London]].

Turner was the Chief Executive of Leeds Mind until autumn 2010.

His poems have been published in periodicals including ''[[Poetry London]]'', ''The Reater'', and ''The Realto'', and in ''The Ring of Words: Poems from the [[Daily Telegraph]] [[Arvon Foundation|Arvon]] International Poetry Competition 1998'' (introduction by [[Andrew Motion]], [[Stroud, Gloucestershire|Stroud]]: Sutton, 1998)

In 1993, he won First Prize in the Nortwords poetry competition for his poem ''Whale Bone''.

In 2002, his collection ''Crossing the Outskirts'' was published by [[Anvil Press Poetry]]. It was a Poetry Book Society Recommendation (2002) and was shortlisted for the [[Forward Poetry Prize]] (Best First Collection) (2002).<ref>{{cite news|title=Poetry prize shortlist unveiled|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/2/hi/entertainment/2116202.stm|accessdate=8 April 2012|newspaper=BBC News|date=8 July 2002}}</ref> A number of poems in the collection deal with [[mental health]] issues. For example, 'Tennis Ball' is about [[suicide]] and 'The Director's Cut' is about [[self-harm]]. The poems in this collection observe traditional conventions of [[rhyme]] and metre.

In 2004, he won a [[Bridport Prize|Bridport Supplementary Prize]] for his poem 'The Gas Poker'.<ref>{{cite web|title=Bridport Prize 2004 - Poetry Prizewinners.|url=http://www.bridportprize.org.uk/winners2004.htm|publisher=Bridport Arts Centre|accessdate=8 April 2012|url-status=dead|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20120406153138/http://www.bridportprize.org.uk/winners2004.htm|archivedate=6 April 2012}}</ref>

His second collection, ''Orphan Sites'', was published by [[Anvil Press Poetry]] in 2006.

He was anthologised in Bloodaxe's Identity Parade (New British and Irish Poets), edited by Roddy Lumsden, in 2010

His third collection, Planet-Struck was published by Anvil in January 2011 and was also a Poetry Book Society recommendation.
