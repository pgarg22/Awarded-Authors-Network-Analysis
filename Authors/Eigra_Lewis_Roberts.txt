'''Eigra Lewis Roberts''' (born 7 August 1939) is a Welsh-language author of about 30 plays, short stories, children's books and novels.<ref name=Post>{{Cite news |url=http://www.dailypost.co.uk/news/tv/waunfawr-farm-sparks-childhood-memories-11488501 |title=Waunfawr farm sparks childhood memories for author in ''3 Lle'' |last=Bissett |first=Daniel |work=[[North Wales Daily Post]] |date=18 June 2016 |accessdate=20 October 2016 }}</ref> She has won several awards at the [[National Eisteddfod of Wales]].<ref name="3 Lle">{{Cite AV media |date=19 June 2016 |title=3 Lle: Eigra Lewis Roberts |trans-title=3 Places: Eigra Lewis Roberts |language=cy |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/iplayer/episode/p03xprzx/3-lle-cyfres-4-eigra-lewis-roberts |access-date=12 August 2017 |publisher=[[S4C]]}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Born in [[Blaenau Ffestiniog]], Roberts attended Ffestiniog County School, along with her fellow author [[John Rowlands (author)|John Rowlands]] and the poet [[Gwyn Thomas (poet)|Gwyn Thomas]].<ref name="3 Lle"/><ref>{{Cite news |url=https://www.independent.co.uk/news/people/john-rowlands-author-who-eschewed-popular-taste-in-order-to-explore-the-human-mind-and-his-own-inner-10256109.html |title=John Rowlands: Author who eschewed popular taste in order to explore the human mind and his own inner life |last=Stephens |first=Meic |work=[[The Independent]] |date=17 May 2015 |accessdate=22 October 2016}}</ref> Having graduated from [[University College of North Wales]] in [[Bangor, Gwynedd|Bangor]],<ref name="3 Lle"/> she taught in [[Holyhead]] and [[Llanrwst]] and now lives in [[Dolwyddelan]].<ref name=Post/><ref name=BBC>{{Cite web |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/cymru/gogleddorllewin/enwogion/llen/pages/eigra_lewis_roberts.shtml |title=Eigra Lewis Roberts |work=[[BBC Cymru]] |accessdate=22 October 2016 |language=cy}}</ref> Roberts has an [[honorary degree|honorary]] [[Master of Arts|MA]] from the [[University of Wales]].<ref name=BBC/><ref name=WO>{{Cite news |url=http://www.walesonline.co.uk/lifestyle/return-ticket-eigra-lewis-roberts-2350646 |title=''Return ticket'', by Eigra Lewis Roberts |work=[[Wales Online]] |date=25 March 2006 |accessdate=22 October 2016}}</ref>

==Career==
Aged 20, Roberts won the open novel prize at the 1959 [[Caernarfon]] [[National Eisteddfod of Wales]].<ref name=Post/><ref name="3 Lle"/> In the 1960s and 1970s she was known for writing about the lives and dissatisfaction of Welsh women in [[Social history of the United Kingdom (1945–present)|Post-war Britain]], a topic little covered Welsh authors at the time.<ref>{{Cite book |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=uTXiuub6Rx4C&q=Eigra+Lewis+Roberts |title=The Oxford Guide to Contemporary World Literature |last=Sturrock |first=John |publisher=[[Oxford University Press]] |page=434 |date=1997 |isbn=9780192833181 |access-date=22 October 2016}}</ref><ref>{{Cite book |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=7WquBwAAQBAJ&q=Eigra+Lewis+Roberts |title=The History of Wales |last=Jones |first=John Graham |publisher=[[University of Wales Press]] |date=2014 |isbn=9781783161706 |access-date=22 October 2016}}</ref><ref>{{Cite book |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=3cHdQC1cXLEC&q=Eigra+Lewis+Roberts |title=The Celts: History, Life, and Culture |year=2012 |publisher=[[ABC-CLIO]] |page=796 |isbn=9781598849646 |access-date=22 October 2016}}</ref> In the 1980s, she was the screenwriter adapting her novel ''Mis o Fehefin'' for the Welsh television programme ''Minafon''.<ref name="3 Lle"/><ref name=BBC/>

In 2006, Roberts wrote her first novel in English, the semi-autobiographical ''Return Ticket''.<ref name=WO/> That year she won the Crown in the [[Swansea]] National Eisteddfod for a collection of poems about [[Sylvia Plath]].<ref name=Post/><ref name="3 Lle"/><ref name=BBC/> In 2013, her work ''{{lang|cy|Parlwr Bach}}'' was shortlisted for the [[Wales Book of the Year]] award.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.literaryfestivals.co.uk/announcements/2013-wales-book-of-the-year-award-shortlist |title=2013 Wales Book of the Year Award Shortlist |publisher=Literary Festivals |date=14 May 2015 |accessdate=22 October 2016}}</ref>
