'''James Galvin''' (born 1951) is the author of seven volumes of poetry, and two novels.  He teaches at the [[Iowa Writers' Workshop]] in [[Iowa City, Iowa]].

==Biography==
James Galvin was born in [[Chicago, Illinois]] in 1951 and was raised in [[Northern Colorado]]. He earned a BA from  [[Antioch College]] in 1974 and a MFA from the [[Iowa Writers' Workshop|University of Iowa Writers' Workshop]] in 1977.<ref name="Writing University Iowa">{{cite web |title=James Galvin |url=https://www.writinguniversity.org/writers/james-galvin |website=Writing University |publisher=University of Iowa |access-date=23 November 2018}}</ref><ref name="Poetry Foundation">{{cite web |title=James Galvin b 1951 |url=https://www.poetryfoundation.org/poets/james-galvin |website=Poetry foundation |access-date=23 November 2018}}</ref>

After receiving his MFA, Galvin taught at [[Murray State University]] in Kentucky for two years and [[Humboldt State University]] in California for three years. He later joined the faculty at the prestigious [[Iowa Writers' Workshop]] where he continues to teach each year.<ref name="Writing University Iowa" /><ref name="Poetry Foundation" />

Galvin has published seven poetry collections and a compilation of his work, ''Resurrection Update: Collected Poems 1975–1997'' ([[Copper Canyon Press]], 1997). Galvin is also the author of the prose book ''The Meadow'' ([[Henry Holt and Company|Henry Holt]], 1992), which recounts the hundred-year history of a ranch on the [[Colorado]]–[[Wyoming]] border;<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.kirkusreviews.com/book-reviews/james-galvin/the-meadow/|title=The Meadow|date=February 15, 1992|accessdate=June 13, 2019|work=[[Kirkus Reviews]]}}</ref> and the novel ''Fencing the Sky'' (Henry Holt, 1999), about the destruction of [[rangelands]] and concomitant social changes in the [[western United States]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.kirkusreviews.com/book-reviews/james-galvin/fencing-the-sky/|title=Fencing the Sky|date=August 15, 1999|accessdate=June 13, 2019|work=[[Kirkus Reviews]]}}</ref>

Galvin has been the recipient of fellowships from the National Endowment for the Arts, the Ingram Merrill Foundation, and the Guggenheim Foundation.<ref name="Poets.org">{{cite web |title=poet James Galvin |url=https://www.poets.org/poetsorg/poet/james-galvin |website=Poets.org |accessdate=23 November 2018}}</ref>

Galvin divides his time between [[Iowa City, Iowa|Iowa City]], and  [[Wyoming]].<ref name="Poetry Foundation" />
