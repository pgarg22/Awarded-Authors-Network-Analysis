'''Tracey Scott Wilson''' is an American playwright, television writer, television producer, and screenwriter. She graduated from [[Rutgers University]] with a BA in English and from [[Temple University]] with an MA in English Literature.<ref>[http://www.newark.rutgers.edu/about-us/have-you-met-rutgers-newark/tracey-scott-wilson Rutgers Alumni Website.]</ref><ref>{{cite news| url=http://www.nj.com/entertainment/arts/index.ssf/2009/03/newark_playwright_tracey_scott.html| title=Newark playwright Tracey Scott Wilson's work on race, class reaches the Public| author= Linda A. Fowler| date=March 1, 2009| work=The Star Ledger}}</ref>

==Early life==

Born in [[Newark, New Jersey]], Wilson began writing fiction after graduating from Temple University. Finding herself unable to finish a novel, she decided to take a playwriting class. "I didn't see much theater as a kid, so I had no expectations....It just took me over."<ref>McKinley, Jesse, [https://www.nytimes.com/2003/12/07/theater/theater-finding-her-own-way-to-get-the-story-right.html "Finding Her Own Way to Get the Story Right"], ''The New York Times'', December 7, 2003.</ref> Wilson soon realized that she had found her métier and wrote a number of short plays. At the encouragement of her mentor, playwright Chiori Miyagawa, Wilson applied for and won a New York Theatre Workshop fellowship in 1998.<ref>Als, Hilton, [http://www.newyorker.com/archive/2003/12/22/031222crth_theatre "Blackout: Tracey Scott Wilson on Race, Ambition and Journalistic Ethics"], ''The New Yorker'', December 22, 2003.</ref>  It was also through this workshop that Wilson met [[Liesl Tommy]], with whom she would develop a long-standing and ongoing creative relationship.

==Theater career==

Wilson's work has received readings at [[New York Theatre Workshop]], Second Stage Theatre, [[The Public Theater]], the [[Williamstown Theatre Festival]] and [[Soho Theatre Writers' Centre|Soho Theater Writers' Center]] in London. Awards and residencies include two Van Lier fellowships from the New York Theatre Workshop, a residency at the [[Sundance Institute|Sun dance Institute]] Playwright's Retreat at Cross and the Sun dance Institute Theater Lab, the 2001 Helen Merrill Emerging Playwright Award, the 2003 AT&T Onstage Award, a 2004 [[Whiting Awards|Whiting Award]], the 2004 [[Kesselring Prize]], the 2007 L. Arnold Weissmuller Award and the 2007 Time Warner Storytelling Fellowship. In 2009, she was the writer-in-residence at the National Playwrights Conference at the Eugene O’Neill Theater Center. On February 29, 2014, the Joyce Foundation announced that Wilson was one of the recipients of its 2014 Joyce Awards.<ref>[http://www.joycefdn.org/awards/tracey-scott-wilson/ Joyce Foundation 2014 Announcement] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140203175322/http://www.joycefdn.org/awards/tracey-scott-wilson/# |date=2014-02-03 }}, February 29, 2014.</ref> With her prize, Wilson worked intimately with a community in Minneapolis, Minnesota, and wrote ''Prep'', which debuted at the [[Pillsbury House Theater]] during the theater's 2015 season.

Wilson's productions include ''Order My Steps'' for Cornerstone Theater Company's Black Faith/AIDS project in Los Angeles; ''Exhibit #9'', which was produced in New York City by New Perspectives Theatre Company and Theatre Outrageous; ''Leader of the People'', produced at New Georges; ''Buzzer'' at Pillsbury House Theater and the Public Theater in New York City; two 10-minute plays produced at the [[Guthrie Theater]] in Minneapolis, a 10-minute play produced at Actors Theatre of Louisville, and The Good Negro at the Public Theater in New York City.

Wilson's first major Manhattan production was in 2003 with ''The Story'' at the Public Theater.<ref>{{cite web|last=Allen|first=Morgan|title=The Story is Leaked, as Wilson Play Opens at Public Theater|url=http://www.playbill.com/news/article/83273-PHOTO-CALL-The-Story-is-Leaked-as-Wilson-Play-Opens-at-Public-Theatre|archive-url=https://archive.is/20131122131105/http://www.playbill.com/news/article/83273-PHOTO-CALL-The-Story-is-Leaked-as-Wilson-Play-Opens-at-Public-Theatre|url-status=dead|archive-date=22 November 2013|publisher=Playbill.com|accessdate=22 November 2013}}</ref> ''The Good Negro'' was produced at The Public and then at the [[Goodman Theater]] in 2010.  Wilson's ''Buzzer'' was produced as part of the Goodman's 2013-14 season and was added to the Public Theater's 2014-15 season in October 2014.<ref>[http://www.goodmantheatre.org/upcoming-season/ Goodman Theater, 2013-2014 Season.]</ref><ref>[http://www.broadway.com/buzz/177809/tracey-scott-wilsons-buzzer-will-make-its-new-york-premiere-at-the-public-theater-off-broadway/ Broadway.com]</ref> ''The Story'' and ''The Good Negro'' have been published by Dramatists Play Service.

She has taught and guest lectured at [[Brown University]], [[Yale University]], [[Rutgers University]] and [[New York University]].

==Television and Film Career==

In more recently years, Wilson has left the theater to expand her career into television.<ref>Neal, Justin, [http://www.startribune.com/entertainment/tv/190400771.html "'Buzzer' Playwright Tracey Scott Wilson Tries Her Hand at TV Drama"], ''Star Tribune'', February 11, 2013.</ref> Her episode of NBC's ''[[Do No Harm (TV series)|Do No Harm]]'' aired on August 18, 2013, and she was later staffed on the [[FX (TV channel)|FX]] drama series ''[[The Americans]]'', for which she was nominated for a [[Writers Guild of America Awards 2015#Television|Writers Guild of America Award]] in 2015.<ref>[http://awards.wga.org/wga-awards/nominees-winners WGA Nominees and Winners]</ref>  Wilson also received an [[Emmy]] nomination in 2018 for ''The Americans''.  Her work on the Americans also earned her a [[Peabody Award]].<ref>[http://www.peabodyawards.com/award-profile/the-americans-2018]</ref> She was recently a co-executive producer on 8 episodes of FX's ''[[Fosse/Verdon]]'', for which she was also nominated for an Emmy.<ref>[https://www.emmys.com/bios/tracey-scott-wilson]</ref>  In 2019, Wilson teamed up with playwright and television writer [[Bash Doran]] to write an episode of [[Channel 4]]'s ''[[Traitors (TV series)|Traitors]]''.{{citation needed|date=November 2019}}  However, Wilson chose to stay with her television home, and inked an overall deal with FX in 2018.<ref>[https://deadline.com/2018/09/writer-tracey-scott-wilson-ovverall-production-deal-fx-productions-the-americans-1202463222/]</ref>

In 2019, Wilson joined other [[Writers Guild of America|WGA]] members in firing her agents as part of [[Writers Guild of America|the Guild's]] stand against the [[Association of Talent Agents|ATA]] after the two sides were unable to come to an agreement on a new "Code of Conduct" that addressed the unfair practice of [[Movie packaging|packaging]].<ref>{{cite web |title=WGA: More Than 7,000 Writers Have Fired Their Agents |url=https://deadline.com/2019/04/writers-guild-agents-fired-total-hollywood-fight-packaging-1202599639/ |publisher=Deadline}}</ref>

Wilson has penned the screenplay for ''[[Respect (2021 American film)|Respect]]'', the [[Aretha Franklin]] biopic released in August 2021.<ref>[https://deadline.com/2019/10/respect-mgm-aretha-franklin-biopic-rounds-out-cast-with-forest-whitaker-marlon-wayans-mary-j-blige-more-1202763774/]</ref> Though the film generally received warm reviews, reviews for her screenplay were mixed.
