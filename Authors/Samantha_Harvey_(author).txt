{{short description|English writer}}
'''Samantha Harvey''' (born 1975), is an English [[author]] and a lecturer at [[Bath Spa University]]. She has published four novels: ''The Wilderness'' (2009), ''All Is Song'' (2012), ''Dear Thief'' (2014) and ''The Western Wind'' (2018). Harvey published a memoir, ''The Shapeless Unease'', in 2020.

==Education==
Harvey completed the [[Bath Spa University|Bath Spa]] Creative Writing MA course in 2005,<ref>Text on the inside of the backcover of ''The Wilderness''.</ref> and has also completed a postgraduate course in [[philosophy]] and a PhD in Creative Writing.

==Career==
Her first novel ''The Wilderness'' (2009), is written from the point of view of man developing [[Alzheimer's disease]],<ref name="website">http://samanthaharvey.co.uk/</ref> and describes through increasingly fractured prose the unravelling effect of the disease. Her second novel, ''All Is Song'' (2012), is a novel about moral and filial duty, and about the choice between questioning and conforming.<ref>Text on the inside cover of ''All Is Song''.</ref> The author has described the novel as a loose, modern day reimagining of the life of Socrates.<ref name="website" />

Her third novel, ''Dear Thief'', is a long letter from a woman to her absent friend, detailing the emotional fallout of a love triangle. The novel is said to be based on the [[Leonard Cohen]] song Famous Blue Raincoat.<ref>http://www.cbc.ca/radio/writersandcompany/samantha-harvey-interview-1.2936892/ {{Bare URL inline|date=November 2021}}</ref> It was published in 2014 by [[Jonathan Cape]]. Her fourth novel, ''The Western Wind,'' about a priest in fifteenth-century Somerset, was published in March 2018.<ref name="ttoi">{{cite news| newspaper=The Times of India | date=November 30, 2019 |title=Samantha Harvey wins the 2019 Staunch Book Prize | id={{pq|2319567929}}}}</ref>

Her short stories have appeared in [[Granta Magazine]]<ref name="website" /> and on [[BBC Radio 4]].<ref name="radio4">{{Cite web|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/programmes/b046p07v/|title = BBC Radio 4 - Skylines, African Beauty, by Samantha Harvey}}</ref>

Harvey's novels have been considered for many prizes, including the [[Man Booker Prize]], the [[Baileys Women's Prize for Fiction]], the [[James Tait Black Memorial Prize]], and the [[Orange Prize]]. In 2010 she was named one of the 12 best new British novelists by The Culture Show.<ref name="website" /> In 2019, ''The Western Wind'' won the [[Staunch Book Prize]].<ref name="ttoi" />

She is a Senior Lecturer on the MA in Creative Writing at Bath Spa University and a member of the Academy for the [[Rathbones Folio Prize]]. She was a member of the jury for the 2016 [[Scotiabank Giller Prize]].
