'''Henriette von Paalzow''', ''née'' '''Wach''' (1788, in [[Berlin]] – 30 October 1847, in Berlin) was a German historical novelist.

Henriette Wach was the sister of the painter [[Karl Wilhelm Wach]].<ref>Petra Wilhelmy-Dollinger, ''Die Berliner Salons: mit historisch-literarischen Spaziergänge'', Walter de Gruyter, 2000, p.171</ref> She married a Prussian officer, Major Paalzow, in 1816, though the marriage was dissolved in 1821. Her historical novels were compared to those of [[Walter Scott]].<ref>'Paalzow, Henriette', in Henry & Mary Garland, eds., ''The Oxford Companion to German Literature'', 3rd ed., Oxford University Press, 1997, p.646</ref>

==Works==
* (anon.) ''Godwie-Castle'', 3 vols, 1838
* ''St. Roche'', 3 vols, 1839. Translated into English by [[James Justinian Morier]] as ''St. Roche. A romance, from the German'', 1847
* ''Thomas Thurnau'', 3 vols, 1843. Translated into English by [[Mary Howitt]] as ''The citizen of Prage'', 1846.
* ''Jakob van der Rees'', 1844
