'''George Bazeley Scurfield''' was an English author, poet, and politician. He was born on 19 March 1920 in [[Leicestershire]], England, and died on 15 December 1991 in [[Norwich]], England. He married Cecilia Hopkinson in 1947 at [[Cambridge, England]]. They had five children, including their son the actor, [[Matthew Scurfield]], and daughters; Sarah, Lucy, Poly and Sophy.

== World War II ==

During [[World War II]], George Scurfield served as an officer with the [[Royal Artillery]] in the [[Middle East]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://perso.univ-lyon2.fr/~goethals/warpoet/WW2_poets.html |title=Archived copy |accessdate=2008-05-24 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20080513230540/http://perso.univ-lyon2.fr/~goethals/warpoet/WW2_poets.html |archivedate=13 May 2008}} Poetry and the Second World War: Brief biobibliographical notes about the poets writing during World War II</ref> He was awarded the [[Military Cross]] for his wartime services in the [[Far East]].<ref>http://www.joh.cam.ac.uk/library/special_collections/manuscripts/post_medieval/pmmw56/ St. John’s College University of Cambridge</ref> This [[military decoration]] is awarded to officers of the [[British Army]] in recognition of an act or acts of exemplary gallantry during active operations against the enemy.

== Politics ==

In the 1960s Scurfield entered [[municipal]] politics. He twice ran in [[Cambridge City Council, England]] elections as a [[Labour Party (UK)|Labour Party]] candidate. First in Market Ward, in May 1962 when he came in 3rd, losing to the [[Liberal Party (UK)|Liberal]] candidate, Peter Calvert.<ref>http://www.cix.co.uk/~rosenstiel/camelect/citymkt.htm {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080906120148/http://www.cix.co.uk/~rosenstiel/camelect/citymkt.htm |date=6 September 2008 }} Cambridge City Council Elections - Market Ward</ref> In May 1963 Scurfield was elected to City Council in Petersfield, where he served through 1965.<ref>http://www.cix.co.uk/~rosenstiel/camelect/cllrs1.htm {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080604202548/http://www.cix.co.uk/~rosenstiel/camelect/cllrs1.htm |date=4 June 2008 }} Cambridge City Councillors by Ward 1945-1967</ref> In September 1967 Scurfield again ran for City Council in Cambridge, placing second to the [[Conservative Party (UK)|Conservative]] candidate, David Lane.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.geocities.com/by_elections/67.html |title=1966 By Election Results |date=2009-10-25 |archive-url=https://www.webcitation.org/5knbeTWbn?url=http://www.geocities.com/by_elections/67.html |archive-date=25 October 2009 |url-status=dead}}</ref> He was the Labour Candidate for Cambridge in the 1970 General Election.

== Author ==

George Scurfield, with his wife Cecilia, wrote two charming and useful works on home baking. These books have become [[cook book]] classics,<ref>http://www.geraldeneholt.com/essays.htm Home-Made Cakes and Biscuits: Foreword by Geraldene Holt, 1994 edition</ref> and both remain in print to this day. The first, ''Home Baked'', about the process of hand [[baking]] bread, was originally published in 1956. The companion volume, ''Home-Made Cakes and Biscuits'', first published in 1975, is equally engaging<ref>http://www.wakefieldpress.com.au/books/homemadecakesand.html{{Dead link|date=December 2019 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }} Wakefield Press: Home-Made Cakes and Biscuits</ref> and practical.

*"Brother If You Could See Me Now", Seven Magazine of People's Writing (April - June, 1944)
*"The Colonel", Seven Magazine of People's Writing (July - September, 1944)
*"Evening", Seven Magazine of People's Writing (October - December, 1944
*"Song and Dance" published in "The War Decade: An Anthology of the 1940s" by Andrew Sinclair, 1989 (page 34)

*''Gardening for Fun'' by George Scurfield, published 1959.
*''Home Baked, A Little Book of Bread Recipes'' ({{ISBN|0-571-09844-4}}), by George and Cecilia Scurfield, Illustrated by Nora Kay. (1959), 86 pages.
*''Home-Made Cakes and Biscuits'' ({{ISBN|1 897959 109}}), by George and Cecilia Scurfield.
*''New York Times Menu Cook Book'', by George and Cecilia Scurfield, published by H&R (New York, 1966).
*''Fakenham and Folkestone Etcetera'' ({{ISBN|0948400072}}), published by The Larks Press, 1988, 356 pages.
*''The Bitter Mangoes'', memoirs of his years at St John's and his wartime service in the Far East for which he was awarded the [[Military Cross]], 1938–45. Written in 1991. 101 pages.
