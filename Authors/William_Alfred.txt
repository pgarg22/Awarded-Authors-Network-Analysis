{{short description|American playwright}}
'''William Alfred''' (August 16, 1922 – May 20, 1999) was an American [[playwright]], [[poet]], and [[professor]] of [[English literature]] at [[Harvard University]].<ref name="academicworks.cuny.edu">{{Cite web|url=https://academicworks.cuny.edu/bc_arch_alfred/|title=William Alfred {{!}} Manuscript Collections {{!}} City University of New York (CUNY)|website=academicworks.cuny.edu|access-date=2019-12-05}}</ref>

== Biography ==
Alfred was born into an Irish family in [[Brooklyn]], [[New York (state)|New York]].<ref name=":3">{{Cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=HkKsfnScXo0C&q=%22william+alfred%22+%22harvard%22&pg=PA130|title=Souvenirs of a Blown World: Sketches for the Sixties, Writings about America, 1966-1973|last=Mcdonald|first=Gregory|date=2011-01-04|publisher=Seven Stories Press|isbn=978-1-58322-993-4|language=en}}</ref> His father was a bricklayer and his mother was a telephone operator.<ref name="Harvard obit">{{cite web|url=https://news.harvard.edu/gazette/1999/05.27/alfred.obit.html|title='The Professor' William Alfred Dies at Age 76|date=6 July 2008|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20080706160043/https://news.harvard.edu/gazette/1999/05.27/alfred.obit.html|archivedate=6 July 2008|accessdate=4 June 2018}}</ref> He graduated from [[St. Francis Preparatory School]] in 1940.<ref name=":3" />

Alfred was drafted in 1943, two years into his undergraduate studies at [[Brooklyn College]].<ref name=":3" /> He served in the [[U.S. Army|Army]] tank corps and quartermaster's corps<ref name="Harvard obit" /> in [[World War II]] for four years.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|url=https://news.harvard.edu/gazette/story/2003/04/william-alfred/|title=William Alfred|date=2003-04-03|website=Harvard Gazette|language=en-US|access-date=2019-12-05}}</ref> While in the army, he was taught Bulgarian at a language school and then stationed in the South Pacific, where he wrote poems for ''American Poet''.<ref name=":3" /> Alfred completed his B.A. from Brooklyn College in 1948 with the help of the [[G.I. Bill]].<ref name=":0" /><ref name=":3" />

Alfred is a double graduate of Harvard University, where he specialized in the literature of Medieval England, receiving his [[Artium Magister|A.M.]] and [[Ph.D.]] in English in 1949 and 1954 respectively.<ref name=":1">“Past Recipients of the Harvard Medal” ''Harvard Alumni Association,'' 2019. Retrieved from https://alumni.harvard.edu/sites/default/files/page/files/Harvard%20Medal_Past%20Recipient%20List.pdf</ref><ref name=":0" /> While at Harvard, Alfred took a creative writing course under [[Archibald MacLeish]], where he wrote his play, ''Agamemnon''.<ref name=":4">{{Cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=IlYEAAAAMBAJ&q=%22william+alfred%22+%22harvard%22&pg=PA89|title=LIFE|last=Inc|first=Time|date=1966-04-22|publisher=Time Inc|language=en}}</ref>

He began teaching at Harvard the same year he received his doctorate and was appointed full professor in 1963.<ref name=":0" /> In 1980, he was named Abbott Lawrence Lowell Professor of the Humanities.<ref name=":0" />

He retired in 1991.<ref name="Harvard obit" />

== Personal life ==
Alfred was a lifelong Catholic and attended mass at nearby Saint Paul's Church.<ref name=":0" />

His great grandmother, Anna Maria Egan, immigrated to the United States.<ref name=":4" />

Alfred's play ''[[Hogan's Goat]]'' helped launch [[Faye Dunaway]]'s career in the 60's.<ref name=":2">{{Cite web|url=https://www.samuelfrench.com/a/1051/william-alfred|title=William Alfred {{!}} Samuel French|website=www.samuelfrench.com|language=en|access-date=2019-12-05}}</ref> They maintained a close relationship and remained lifelong friends.<ref name=":2" />

Alfred was close friends with fellow poets [[Elizabeth Bishop]]<ref>{{Cite book|url=https://archive.org/details/elizabethbishop00bret|url-access=registration|page=[https://archive.org/details/elizabethbishop00bret/page/432 432]|quote=william alfred harvard.|title=Elizabeth Bishop: Life and the Memory of It|last=Millier|first=Brett C.|date=1992-03-15|publisher=University of California Press|isbn=978-0-520-91719-4|language=en}}</ref> and [[Robert Lowell]].<ref name="academicworks.cuny.edu"/>
