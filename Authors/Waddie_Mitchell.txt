'''Bruce Douglas "Waddie" Mitchell''' (born 1950 in [[Elko County, Nevada]]) is an American [[Cowboy poetry|cowboy poet]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Waddie Mitchell|url=http://www.onlinenevada.org/waddie_mitchell|work=Online Nevada Encyclopedia|access-date=22 September 2011}}</ref> He sometimes performs his poems with a guitarist playing in the background. {{citation needed|date=September 2011}} Mitchell has made eight CDs including ''[[That No Quit Attitude]], [[Lone Drifting Rider]] ''and his most recent, ''[[Sweat Equity]]''.He and cowboy singer and friend Don Edwards released ''[[The Bard and the Balladeer Live From Cowtown]]. ''Mitchell has written four books, ''[[Waddie's Whole Load]], [[A Cowboy's Night Before Christmas]], [[Lone Driftin' Rider]] ''and a 2015 compilation ''[[One Hundred Poems]]. ''He was chosen to write a poem describing the West for the [[2002 Winter Olympics]]' Olympic Arts Festival.<ref>{{cite web|title=That No Quit Attitude|url=http://saltlake2002.paralympic.org/para_info/cultural_olympiad/essay_poem/waddie_poem.html|work=Essays & Poems|publisher=Olympic Arts Festival|access-date=22 September 2011}}</ref> He is a co-founder of the [[National Cowboy Poetry Gathering]].<ref>{{cite journal|title=Waddie Mitchell|url=http://weberstudies.weber.edu/archive/archive%20D%20Vol.%2021.2-25.2/Vol.%2021.3/Mitchell%20Poe.htm|journal=Weber: The Contemporary West|date=Springâ€“Summer 2004|volume=21|issue=3}}</ref>

The [[National Cowboy & Western Heritage Museum]] presented the 2012 Wrangler Award for Best Original Composition of the year to Guy Porter Gillette (1945-2013) and his brother, William Pipp Gillette (born 1946), sons of the photographer [[Guy Gillette (photographer)|Guy Gillette]], for their work on Mitchell's song, "Trade Off."<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.findagrave.com/memorial/122964042|title=Guy Porter Gillette|publisher=findagrave.com|author=Perry L. Williams|date=January 7, 2014|access-date=June 30, 2015}}</ref>
