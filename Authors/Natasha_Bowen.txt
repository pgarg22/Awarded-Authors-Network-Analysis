'''Natasha Bowen''' is a Nigerian-Welsh writer and teacher. She writes [[fantasy]] books for young adults.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|title=Natasha Bowen |url=https://www.penguinrandomhouse.com/authors/2197740/natasha-bowen|access-date=2021-11-11|website=Penguin Random House |language=en-US}}</ref><ref name=":1">{{Cite web|date=2021-11-05|title=Q&A With Natasha Bowen, Skin of the Sea|url=https://diversebooks.org/qa-with-natasha-bowen-skin-of-the-sea/|access-date=2021-11-11|website=We Need Diverse Books|language=en-US}}</ref> She is best known for her novel ''[[Skin of the Sea]]''.<ref name=":3">{{Cite news|title=SKin of the Sea by Natasha Bowen |language=en-US|work=[[The New York Times]] |url=https://www.nytimes.com/books/best-sellers/young-adult-hardcover/|access-date=2021-11-11|issn=0362-4331}}</ref>

== Early life and education ==
Natasha Bowen was born in [[Cambridge]], England to a Nigerian [[Yoruba people|Yoruba]] father and a Welsh mother. She grew up in Cambridge with little exposure to her Nigerian heritage, with which she would later connect in life and use in writing her novel.<ref name=":2">{{Cite web|date=2021-10-22|title=An Indies Introduce Q&A With Natasha Bowen|url=https://www.bookweb.org/news/indies-introduce-qa-natasha-bowen-1626948|access-date=2021-11-11|website= American Booksellers Association}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=A Mermaid Tests Her Fate in West African-Inspired Tale Skin of the Sea|url=https://gizmodo.com/a-mermaid-tests-her-fate-in-the-west-african-mythology-1847909248|access-date=2021-11-11|website=Gizmodo|language=en-us}}</ref> She studied Creative Writing at [[Bath Spa University]]. After graduating, she moved to [[East London]] where she became a teacher.<ref name=":0" />

== Personal life==
Bowen is married and has three children. She lives in the UK with her family.<ref name=":0" />

== Career ==
Bowen's debut novel ''[[Skin of the Sea]]'', inspired by her Nigerian heritage, [[African folklore]], [[Yoruba culture]], and her love for [[mermaid]]s, was published on 2 November 2021 by [[Random House]]. She wrote the novel while teaching full-time in a school.<ref name=":1" /><ref name=":2" />

The novel follows Simi, a [[Mami Wata]] who travels across sea and land in search of the Supreme Creator after breaking a law that threatens the existence of all Mami Wata. The book entered the [[The New York Times Best Seller list|New York Times]]<ref name=":3" /> and Indie bestseller lists and received several positive reviews from book reviewers.<ref>{{Cite news|last=Paxson|first=Caitlyn|date=2021-10-28|title='Skin of the Sea' sends its little mermaid swimming into very different waters|language=en|work=NPR|url=https://www.npr.org/2021/10/28/1049805130/skin-of-the-sea-natasha-bowen-review|access-date=2021-11-11}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=Children's Book Review: Skin of the Sea by Natasha Bowen. |url=https://www.publishersweekly.com/978-0-593-12094-1|access-date=2021-11-11|website=PublishersWeekly.com|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.kirkusreviews.com/book-reviews/natasha-bowen/skin-of-the-sea/|title=SKIN OF THE SEA &#124; Kirkus Reviews|via=Kirkus Reviews}}</ref>
