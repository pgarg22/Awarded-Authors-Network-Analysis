'''Janet Laurence''', (born 1937 as Janet Duffell<ref name="enotes">[http://www.enotes.com/topics/janet-laurence enotes.com Janet Laurence articles] Retrieved on 5 February 2014.</ref>), also known by her [[pen name]] '''Julia Lisle''', is a British author and cookery writer.

== Cookery writer ==
From 1978, Laurence began to write cookery articles for ''[[Country Life (magazine)|Country Life]]'' and ''[[The Daily Telegraph]]''. Eventually she became the sole author of the Telegraphs weekly 'Bon Viveur' column.<ref name="enotes" /> She also wrote a series of articles in Country Life about historical cooking.<ref name="ff">[http://www.fantasticfiction.co.uk/l/janet-laurence/ Fantastic Fiction Janet Laurence page] Retrieved on 5 February 2014.</ref> She has written or co-written eight cookery books, notably books about Scandinavian cooking.

== Crime fiction ==
Laurence's first crime novel, 'A Deepe Coffyn', was published in 1989,<ref name="enotes" /> the first of ten contemporary novels featuring cookery writer Darina Lisle and policeman William Pigram. She also wrote one non-series mystery fiction book, 'To Kill The Past'.<ref name="enotes" /> Laurence has also written four historical crime novels - three books where the detective is a fictionalised [[Canaletto]] (the Italian artist),<ref name="enotes" /> and one set in early [[Edwardian]] times.<ref name="promcrime">Hayes, Lizzie. [http://promotingcrime.blogspot.co.uk/2012/08/deadly-inheritance-by-janet-laurence.html ‘Deadly Inheritance’ by Janet Laurence], ''Promoting Crime blog'' Retrieved on 5 February 2014.</ref> Laurence was the 1998/1999 Chair of the [[Crime Writers' Association]],<ref name="cwa1">[http://www.thecwa.co.uk/history.html History of the CWA] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100529220736/http://www.thecwa.co.uk/history.html |date=29 May 2010 }} Retrieved on 5 February 2014.</ref> and has been Chair of a number of CWA award judging panels including in 2007 and 2008 for the CWA Ellis Peters Award for best Historical Crime Novel<ref name="cwa2007">[http://www.thecwa.co.uk/daggers/2007/historical.html The CWA Ellis Peters Historical Award 2007] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140107081903/http://www.thecwa.co.uk/daggers/2007/historical.html |date=7 January 2014 }} Retrieved on 5 February 2014.</ref><ref name="cwa2008">[http://www.thecwa.co.uk/daggers/2008/historical.html The CWA Ellis Peters Historical Award 2008] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140116123152/http://www.thecwa.co.uk/daggers/2008/historical.html |date=16 January 2014 }} Retrieved on 5 February 2014.</ref> and in 2013 for the CWA International Award.<ref name="cwa2013">[http://www.thecwa.co.uk/daggers/2013/international.html 2013 CWA awards 'CWA International Dagger shared'] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130817071511/http://www.thecwa.co.uk/daggers/2013/international.html |date=17 August 2013 }} Retrieved on 5 February 2014.</ref>

== Other work ==
Laurence has written three contemporary women's novels under the pen name Julia Lisle,<ref name="enotes" /><ref name="promcrime" /> a book on how to write cookery books,<ref name="amazon">[https://www.amazon.co.uk/s/ref=sr_nr_n_11?rh=n%3A266239%2Cn%3A66%2Ck%3Ajanet+laurence&keywords=janet+laurence&ie=UTF8&qid=1391641997&rnid=1025612 List of cookery books by Janet Laurence on amazon.co.uk] Retrieved on 5 February 2014.</ref> and a book on how to write crime fiction.<ref name="iac">[http://itsacrimeukbooks.wordpress.com/2011/07/12/writing-crime-fiction-making-crime-pay-janet-laurence/ Review of 'Writing Crime Fiction' on 'It's A Crime'] Retrieved on 5 February 2014.</ref>
