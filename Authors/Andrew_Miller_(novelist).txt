'''Andrew Brooke Miller''' [[Royal Society of Literature|FRSL]] (born 29 April 1960) is an English [[novelist]].

==Life and career==
Miller was born in [[Bristol]]. He grew up in the West Country and has lived in Spain, Japan, Ireland and France.<ref>{{cite web|first=Dan |last=Eltringham |url=http://www.ft.com/cms/s/2/d7b2e8f2-9766-11e0-af13-00144feab49a.html#axzz1iTqYQL4Y |title=Small talk: Andrew Miller |publisher=FT.com |date=18 June 2011 |access-date=4 January 2012}}</ref> He was educated at [[Dauntsey's School]], and after gaining a first-class degree in English at [[Middlesex University|Middlesex Polytechnic]],<ref>{{cite news|first=Kira |last=Cochrane |url=https://www.theguardian.com/books/2012/jan/25/andrew-miller-interview |title=Andrew Miller: my morbid obsession |newspaper=Guardian |date=25 January 2012 |access-date=26 January 2012}}</ref> completed an MA in [[UEA Creative Writing Course|Creative Writing]] at the [[University of East Anglia]] in 1991. In 1995 he wrote a PhD in Critical and Creative Writing at [[Lancaster University]]. For his first book ''[[Ingenious Pain]]'' he received three awards, the [[James Tait Black Memorial Prize|James Tait Black Memorial Award for Fiction]],<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ed.ac.uk/about/people/tait-black/fiction |title=Previous winners - fiction &#124; James Tait Black Prize winners &#124; People |publisher=Ed.ac.uk |date=11 November 2011 |access-date=1 December 2011 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110927024752/http://www.ed.ac.uk/about/people/tait-black/fiction |archive-date=27 September 2011 }}</ref> the [[International Dublin Literary Award]];<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.impacdublinaward.ie/win99.htm |title=International IMPAC DUBLIN Literary Award |publisher=Impacdublinaward.ie |year=2000 |access-date=1 December 2011 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20111011031642/http://impacdublinaward.ie/win99.htm |archive-date=11 October 2011 }}</ref> and the [[Grinzane Cavour Prize]] in Italy.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://literature.britishcouncil.org/writer/andrew-miller |title=Andrew Miller &#124; British Council Literature |publisher=Literature.britishcouncil.org |access-date=13 January 2016}}</ref> The book has been translated into 36 languages. Miller currently lives in [[Witham Friary]] in [[Somerset]] with his daughter Frieda.
