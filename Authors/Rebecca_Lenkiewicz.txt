'''Rebecca Lenkiewicz''' (born 1968) is a British playwright and screenwriter. She is best known as the author of ''[[Her Naked Skin]]'' (2008), which was the first original play written by a living female playwright to be performed on the Olivier stage of the [[Royal National Theatre]].<ref>{{cite news|last=Gardner|first=Lyn|url=https://www.theguardian.com/stage/2015/jul/07/rebecca-lenkiewicz-interview-ida-the-invisible-jane-wenham-witch-walkern|title=Rebecca Lenkiewicz: this government is 'determined to crush the poor'|work=The Guardian|date=7 July 2015|access-date=29 September 2015}}</ref>

Several of Lenkiewicz's plays have been published individually, and in 2013 Faber & Faber published a collection.<ref>{{cite book |title=Rebecca Lenkiewicz: Plays. 1: The Night Season; Shoreditch Madonna; Her Naked Skin; The Painter  |publisher=Faber and Faber |date=8 July 2013 |isbn=978-0571302918 |oclc=851561303}}</ref>

==Early life and education==
Lenkiewicz was born in [[Plymouth]], Devon, the daughter of Celia Mills and Peter Quint, a playwright. Her stepfather is artist [[Robert Lenkiewicz]].<ref name=“turningtables”/> Her sister is the artist Alice Lenkiewicz<ref>[http://alenkiewicz.com/ "Alice Lenkiewicz"], ''Alice Lenkiewicz'', 21. August 2015.</ref> and her brother is the artist Wolfe von Lenkiewicz,<ref>[http://wolfevonlenkiewicz.com/ "Wolfe Von Lenkiewicz"], ''Wolfe von Lenkiewicz'', 21. August 2015.</ref> who are both the children of Robert Lenkiewicz. Her other brothers are Peter Mills and Thomas Mills.

She attended [[Hyde Park Junior School]] and then [[Plymouth High School for Girls]] before progressing to a BA in Film and English at the [[University of Kent]] from 1985 to 1989, then later to a BA Acting Course at the [[Central School of Speech and Drama]] from 1996 to 1999. Initially she worked as an actor at the [[Royal Shakespeare Company]] and the [[Royal National Theatre]], notably in Sir [[Peter Hall (director)|Peter Hall]]'s production of ''[[The Bacchae]]''.<ref>[http://www.britishtheatreguide.info/otherresources/interviews/RebeccaLenkiewicz.htm "Rebecca Lenkiewicz – At the Crossroads"], ''British Theatre Guide'', 2004. Retrieved 9 July 2013.</ref><ref>[https://archive.today/20130710024253/http://www.thisisplymouth.co.uk/Actress-pens-naked-truths/story-12681685-detail/story.html%23axzz2YTF50XUA#axzz2YTF50XUA "Actress pens naked truths"], ''The Plymouth Herald'', 29. August 2008. Retrieved 9 July 2013.</ref><ref name=“turningtables”>{{cite news|first=Kate|last=Kellaway|url=https://www.theguardian.com/stage/2008/jun/29/theatre.features|title=Turning the Tables|newspaper=[[The Observer]]|publisher=[[Observer Media]]|location=London, England|date=29 June 2008|access-date=9 July 2013}}</ref> 
==Writing==
===Theatre===
Lenkiewicz's first play was ''Soho: A Tale of Table Dancers'' which she wrote for the [[Royal Shakespeare Company]] Fringe in 2000. It won a Fringe First award at the [[Edinburgh Festival Fringe]]. [[Helen Raynor]]'s production was revived in London on 2 February 2001, the first play to be staged at the [[Arcola Theatre]]. Lenkiewicz also appeared in the play in the role of Stella. Her second play, ''The Night Season'' (2004), set in [[Sligo]], tells the story of an Irish family, the Kennedys, and their attempts to find love. It was staged at the [[Royal National Theatre]] in the Cottesloe auditorium by [[Lucy Bailey (director)|Lucy Bailey]].<ref>[http://www.britishtheatreguide.info/reviews/nightseason-rev.htm "The Night Season"], ''British Theatre Guide'', 2004. Retrieved 8 July 2013.</ref> In 2005, Lenkiewicz's ''Shoreditch Madonna'', directed by [[Sean Mathias]], was performed at the [[Soho Theatre]]. A tale of love among the artists in an East London gallery, it starred [[Francesca Annis]] and [[Leigh Lawson]].<ref>[http://www.thestage.co.uk/reviews/review.php/8685/shoreditch-madonna "Shoreditch Madonna"], ''The Stage, Reviews'', 15 July 2005. Retrieved 8 July 2013.</ref><ref>[http://www.britishtheatreguide.info/reviews/shoreditch-rev.htm "The Shoreditch Madonna"], ''British Theatre Guide'', 2005. Retrieved 8 July 2013.</ref>

In 2006 Lenkiewicz wrote the script for the dance drama, ''Justitia'', which was directed and choreographed by [[Jasmin Vardimon]]. It was initially performed at the [[Peacock Theatre]] and has since been on tour.<ref>[http://www.jasminvardimon.com/media/press/JVC_Spring2011PressRelease.pdf "Press Release"], ''Jasmin Vardimon Company'', 2011. Retrieved 25 July 2013.</ref> This was followed by ''Invisible Mountains'' toured London schools as part of the National Theatre "Interact" project.<ref>[http://www.nationaltheatre.org.uk/sites/all/libraries/files/documents/Invisible_Mountains_workpack.pdf "Invisible Mountains"] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140814083516/http://www.nationaltheatre.org.uk/sites/all/libraries/files/documents/Invisible_Mountains_workpack.pdf |date=14 August 2014 }}, ''National Theatre Education'', 2006. Retrieved 25 July 2013.</ref> She and Abdulkareem Kasid also created a new version of ''[[Histoire du soldat|The Soldier's Tale]]'', a music theatre piece by [[Igor Stravinsky]] and [[Charles Ferdinand Ramuz]], set in Iraq.  It was staged at the [[Old Vic]].<ref>[http://www.britishtheatreguide.info/reviews/soldierstale-rev.htm "The Soldier's Tale"] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080328021913/http://www.britishtheatreguide.info/reviews/soldierstale-rev.htm |date=28 March 2008 }}, ''British Theatre Guide'', 2006. Retrieved 8 July 2013.</ref> Then in August, her hour-long play ''Blue Moon Over Poplar'' was staged by the [[National Youth Theatre]] company at the Soho Theatre as part of the NYT's Golden Jubilee.<ref>[http://www.britishtheatreguide.info/reviews/bluemoonpoplar-rev.htm "Blue Moon over Poplar"], ''British Theatre Guide'', 2006. Retrieved 8 July 2013.</ref>

In 2008, her adaptation of [[Henrik Ibsen]]'s ''[[An Enemy of the People]]'' opened at the Arcola Theatre, directed by its founder [[Mehmet Ergen]].<ref>[http://www.britishtheatreguide.info/reviews/enemypeople-rev "An Enemy of the People"], "British Theatre Guide'', 2008. Retrieved 8 July 2013.</ref> ''[[Her Naked Skin]]'', directed by [[Howard Davies (director)|Howard Davies]], premiered on the Olivier stage at the NT in July 2008. It describes the struggles faced by two [[suffragette]]s immediately prior to [[World War I]]. ''Faeries'', was staged at The Egg, [[Theatre Royal Bath]]. ''Faeries'' is an original drama for children, using puppetry. It tells the story of a girl evacuated during [[World War II]], and the adventures she has when she spends a night in the park. It was commissioned by the [[Royal Opera House]].
<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.gazetteandherald.co.uk/leisure/theatreandarts/2414408.bthe_egg_theatre_royal_bath_faeriesb/|title=The Egg, Theatre Royal Bath: Faeries|newspaper=[[The Wiltshire Gazette and Herald]]|date=31 July 2008|access-date=16 July 2013}}</ref>

''The Lioness'' (June 2010) was performed at the Tricycle Theatre. It describes meetings that [[Elizabeth I]] had with [[John Knox]] and [[Robert Devereux, 2nd Earl of Essex]].<ref>[http://www.nickhernbooks.co.uk/Book/339/Women-Power-and-Politics-Then.html "The Lioness: Women, Power and Politics"], ''Nick Hern Books'', 3 June 2010. Retrieved 16 July 2013.</ref> Lenkiewicz adapted Ibsen's ''[[Ghosts (play)|Ghosts]]'' for a production at the [[Arcola Theatre]] in August 2010.<ref>[https://www.theguardian.com/stage/2009/aug/02/ghosts-arcola-review-ibsen/ "Ghosts"], ''The Guardian|The Observer'', 2 August 2010. Retrieved 28 August 2013.</ref> The National Youth Theatre, at the Tramway Theatre in Glasgow, performed ''Stars over Kabul'' (September 2010). It tells the story of a young woman growing up in Kabul.
<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.heraldscotland.com/arts-ents/stage-visual-arts/stars-over-kabul-tramway-glasgow-1.1057151|title=Stars Over Kabul, Tramway, Glasgow|newspaper=[[The Scottish Herald]]|location=Glasgow, Scotland|date=24 September 2010|access-date=16 July 2013}}</ref>

In January 2011 her play ''[[The Painter (play)|The Painter]]'' on the life of [[J.M.W. Turner]] premiered at the [[Arcola Theatre]] to mark its move into new premises.<ref>[http://www.britishtheatreguide.info/reviews/painter-rev "The Painter"], "British Theatre Guide'', 2011. Retrieved 8 July 2013.</ref> Lenkiewicz's adaptation of [[Henry James]]' novella ''[[The Turn of the Screw]]'' was performed at London's [[Almeida Theatre]] (18 January – 16 March 2013). It was directed by [[Lindsay Posner]]<ref>[http://www.almeida.co.uk/event/turn "World Premiere: The Turn of the Screw"], ''Almeida Theatre'', 8 July 2013. Retrieved 8 July 2013.</ref>

In 2014 Lenkiewicz wrote a short play, ''We Two Alone'', inspired by ''[[King Lear]]'' and commissioned by RIFT Theatre for their Shakespeare in Shoreditch Festival. The production was directed by Tess Farley and Connor Abbott of Outbreak Theatre.<ref>[https://archive.today/20150211095917/http://www.outbreaktheatre.com/%23!past-productions/c19cn#!past-productions/c19cn "We Two Alone"], ''Outbreak'', Retrieved 12 February 2015.</ref> Lenkiewicz's 2015 ''Jane Wenham: The Witch of Walkern'' concerned one of the last witch-hunts and trials in England, that of [[Jane Wenham (alleged witch)|Jane Wenham]], whose outsider status is juxtaposed with the lot of other women in the village, including the daughter of a woman executed for witchcraft, a former slave, and a widow trying to run the village taphouse on her own.<ref>{{cite news|newspaper=[[The Guardian]] |url=https://www.theguardian.com/stage/2015/sep/29/jane-wenham-the-witch-of-walkern-review-rebecca-lenkiewicz|title=Jane Wenham: The Witch of Walkern review – a heady brew of sex and sorcery |date=29 September 2015 |first=Lyn |last=Gardner}}</ref> Her play, ''The Invisible'' was directed by Michael Oakley at the Bush Theatre. It ran from 3 July - 15 August 2015.<ref>[http://www.bushtheatre.co.uk/event/the-invisible/ "The Invisible"], ''Bush Theatre'', Retrieved 21 August 2015.</ref>

Lenkiewicz's shorter works include a contribution to ''24 Hour Plays'' (June 2005), ''Flowers in her Hair'' (March 2009), ''The Typist'' (June 2010), ''That Almost Unnameable Lust'' (Nov 2010). She translated ''Avec Norm'' (2004) by Serge Boucher, which was performed in a public reading at the Centre des Auteurs Dramatiques (31 July 2007).<ref>[http://www.cead.qc.ca/_cead_repertoire/id_document/7536/ "Avec Norm"], ''CEAD'', Retrieved 22 July 2013.</ref>

===Radio ===
She wrote numerous productions for BBC Radio, such as: ''Fighting for Words'' (2005), ''Caravan of Desire'', ''Blue Moon over Poplar'' (both 2006), ''The Man in the Suit'', ''Sarah and Ken'', ''Betty Lives in a Little Yellow House in Texas'' (all 2010), ''Burning Up'' and ''The Phone'' (both 2011). She also wrote an adaptation of ''Dracula'' and ''The Winter House'' (2012), and a dramatisation of Anne Tyler's ''Dinner at the Homesick Restaurant'' (May 2013) and ''Ladder of Years''.<ref name="CV">{{cite web |title = Rebecca Lenkiewicz CV|url = http://www.casarotto.co.uk/assets/x/53959.pdf|publisher = Casarotto, Ramsey and Associates|access-date = 2015-02-01}}</ref>

===Film and television===
Lenkiewicz wrote on ''[[Secret Diary of a Call Girl]]'' for television. She co-wrote the Polish-language film ''[[Ida (film)|Ida]]'' (2013) with [[Paweł Pawlikowski]], its director. The film is set in Poland in the 1960s and is the story of what happens when a novitiate nun first learns that she is an orphan of Jewish parenthood. The first version of the screenplay was written in English by Lenkiewicz and Pawlikowski, when it had the working title ''Sister of Mercy''. Pawlikowski then translated the screenplay into Polish and revised it.<ref name="Costa">{{cite news |title=Playwright Rebecca Lenkiewicz: 'I had evil thoughts as a child' |last=Costa |first=Maddy |work=The Guardian |date=20 January 2013 |url=https://www.theguardian.com/stage/2013/jan/20/rebecca-lenkiewicz-playwright }}</ref><ref name="Bloom">{{cite news |title=Courage of Conviction: A Conversation with ''Ida'' Director Pawel Pawlikowski |first=Livia |last=Bloom |date=5 May 2014 |work=[[Filmmaker (magazine)|Filmmaker]] |url=http://filmmakermagazine.com/85840-ida/#.VLEMXSvF-So |access-date=2015-01-10}}</ref> The screenplay for ''Ida'' won the European Screenwriter category at the [[27th European Film Awards]] in 2014,<ref>{{cite web |url=http://europeanfilmawards.eu/en_EN/winner/389/743 |title=Ida |work=European Film Awards |access-date=23 December 2014}}</ref> and the Oscar for Best Foreign Film at the ceremony on 22 February 2015. She co-wrote, with director Sebastian Leilo, the script for the adaptation of ''[[Disobedience_(2017_film)|Disobedience]]'' in 2017. 

She and collaborators [[Wash Westmoreland]] and [[Richard Glatzer]] wrote the screenplay for ''[[Colette (2018 film)|Colette]]'' in 2018.
