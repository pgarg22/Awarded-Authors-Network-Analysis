'''Nick Payne''' (born 1984) is a British playwright and screenwriter.

==Early life and education==
Payne studied at the [[University of York]] and subsequently at the [[Central School of Speech and Drama]].  He is also a graduate of the [[Royal Court Theatre|Royal Court]] Young Writer's Program.

==Career==
In 2008 Payne worked at the bookshop of the National Theatre.<ref name=lift>[https://www.independent.co.uk/arts-entertainment/theatre-dance/features/lift-off-for-the-writer-with-stars-in-his-eyes-8435913.html "Lift off for the writer with stars in his eyes"]. ''Independent'', 2 January 2013. Matilda Battersby</ref>  His first play ''If There Is I Haven't Found It Yet'' opened at the [[Bush Theatre]] in October 2009 and received a positive response from critics at the ''[[Evening Standard]]'' and the ''[[Financial Times]]''.  It won the [[George Devine Award]]. In September 2012 it was staged at New York's Laura Pels Theatre, starring [[Jake Gyllenhaal]].<ref>[http://www.ft.com/cms/s/2/986fb06a-0639-11e2-bd29-00144feabdc0.html#axzz2CmNADOHf "If There Is I Haven’t Found It Yet, Laura Pels Theatre, New York"]. ''Financial Times'',  review of New York production, 24 September 2012, by Brendan Lemon</ref>

Payne's second play ''Wanderlust'' opened in September 2010, directed by [[Simon Godwin]], at the [[Royal Court Theatre]] upstairs and also garnered excellent reviews. In November, Payne was shortlisted for the [[Evening Standard]]'s Most Promising Playwright Award, but lost out to [[Anya Reiss]].

He took part in the [[Bush Theatre]]'s 2011 project ''[[Sixty Six Books]]'', for which he wrote a piece based upon a book of the [[King James Bible]].

''[[Constellations (play)|Constellations]]'' opened at [[Royal Court Theatre]] on 13 January 2012. Directed by [[Michael Longhurst]] and starring [[Rafe Spall]] and [[Sally Hawkins]], it explores love, friendship and the notion of free will against the backdrop of quantum physics. It was extremely well received, with [[Charles Spencer (journalist)|Charles Spencer]] in the ''Daily Telegraph'' commenting that "Nick Payne's drama lasts just over an hour but packs in more than most shows manage in three times that length. It is playful, intelligent and bursting with ideas, but also achieves a powerful undertow of emotion"<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/culture/theatre/theatre-reviews/9027655/Constellations-at-the-Royal-Court-review.html|title=Constellations, at the Royal Court, review|first=Charles|last=Spencer|date=3 November 2017|publisher=|access-date=3 November 2017|via=www.Telegraph.co.uk}}</ref> while Paul Taylor in the ''Independent'' wrote that "one would be hard put to begin to do justice to the dazzling way it creates {{sic|it own}} rules, while at the same time being wise enough not to jettison the old rule book either".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.independent.co.uk/arts-entertainment/theatre-dance/reviews/constellations-theatre-upstairs-royal-court-london-6292328.html|title=Constellations, Theatre Upstairs, Royal Court, London|date=20 January 2012|website=Independent.co.uk|access-date=3 November 2017}}</ref> It transferred to the Duke of York's Theatre in November 2012. That month it won the [[Evening Standard Theatre Awards|Evening Standard Theatre Award]] for Best Play.<ref name="Publishing2013">{{cite book|author=Bloomsbury Publishing|title=Whitaker's Shorts 2014: The Year in Review|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=-BJwAgAAQBAJ&pg=PT319|date=7 November 2013|publisher=Bloomsbury Publishing|isbn=978-1-4729-0615-1|pages=319–}}</ref> In January 2013 Payne revealed that a film adaptation was under way.<ref name=lift/> This plan was later shelved.

In August 2013 his play ''The Same Deep Water As Me'' opened at the [[Donmar Warehouse]], with a cast including [[Nigel Lindsay]], [[Daniel Mays]] and [[Marc Wootton]]. In January 2014 ''Blurred Lines'', a piece he devised with the director [[Carrie Cracknell]], opened at the [[Royal National Theatre|National Theatre]]'s Shed. In 2014, two episodes of ''[[The Secrets (TV series)|The Secrets]]'' which were written by Payne were broadcast on [[BBC One]].

''Incognito'' was a co-production between [[Live Theatre Company|Live Theatre]], nabokov, HighTide Festival Theatre and in association with [[North Wall Arts Centre|The North Wall]] in spring 2014, which previewed at Live Theatre in April 2014, before going to HighTide Festival and [[North Wall Arts Centre|The North Wall, Oxford]]. It returned to Live Theatre in May and then had a sell-out run at [[Bush Theatre|The Bush Theatre, London]]. ''Incognito'' was produced Off-Broadway (in New York) by the [[Manhattan Theatre Club]] with support from the [[Alfred P. Sloan Foundation]] at New York's City Center in 2016.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://scienceandfilm.org/articles/2709/on-stage-nick-paynes-incognito|title=Sloan Science & Film|website=ScienceAndFilm.org|access-date=27 May 2016}}</ref> The play stars [[Charlie Cox]], [[Heather Lind]], Morgan Spector, and [[Geneva Carr]] and is directed by [[Doug Hughes]].

The American premiere of ''Constellations'' opened on [[Broadway theatre|Broadway]] in January 2015 at Manhattan Theatre Club's [[Samuel J. Friedman Theatre]], starring Academy Award nominee [[Jake Gyllenhaal]] and [[Ruth Wilson]]. ''Constellations'' reunites Gyllenhaal with Payne and Longhurst, who are also making their Broadway debuts with the production. The three previously collaborated on the American premiere of ''If There Is I Haven't Found It Yet''. It is directed by Michael Longhurst.<ref>[http://www.playbill.com/news/article/jake-gyllenhaal-will-make-broadway-debut-starring-in-nick-paynes-constellat-322398 "Jake Gyllenhaal Will Make Broadway Debut Starring in Nick Paynes 'Constellation' "] playbill.com</ref> The play was next mounted in 2016 at Washington DC's Studio Theatre, starring Lily Balatincz and Tom Patterson and directed by Studio Theatre Artistic Director David Muse. The production was nominated for six Helen Hayes Awards, with Balatincz winning the Helen Hayes Award for Best Actress in a Play and Patterson winning the Helen Hayes Award for Best Actor in a Play.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/entertainment/theater_dance/the-2017-helen-hayes-awards-recipients/2017/05/15/8e1e90b2-397a-11e7-a058-ddbb23c75d82_story.html|title=The 2017 Helen Hayes Awards Recipients|website=Washington Post|access-date=3 November 2017}}</ref> In November 2016, ''Constellations'' opened at Canadian Stage in [[Toronto]], under the direction of Peter Hinton.<ref>[https://www.thestar.com/entertainment/stage/2016/11/11/constellations-gives-us-highlight-reel-of-a-hundred-relationships-review.html "Constellations gives us highlight reel of a hundred relationships: review"]. ''Toronto Star'', Carly Maga, 11 November 2016</ref>

''A Life'' ran Off-Broadway from February 2019 to March 2019 and transferred to Broadway at the Hudson Theatre in July 2019. The play ran with ''Sea Wall'' and starred Jake Gyllenhaal, directed by Carrie Cracknell. The plays were nominated for the 2020 Tony Award as best play.
