'''Seanan McGuire''' (pronounced SHAWN-in)<ref>{{cite web|url=http://seananmcguire.com/survey.php|title= Silly Survey Bio|website= SeananMcGuire.com|access-date=February 19, 2013}}</ref> (born January 5, 1978, in [[Martinez, California]]) is an American author and [[Filk music|filker]]. In 2010, she was awarded the [[John W. Campbell Award for Best New Writer]] by the 2010 [[World Science Fiction Convention]]. Her novella ''[[Every Heart a Doorway]]'' received the 2016 [[Nebula Award for Best Novella]],<ref name="Neb17">{{cite web |url=http://www.sfwa.org/2017/05/nebula-award-recipients-announced/ |title=Nebula Award Recipients Announced |publisher=[[Science Fiction and Fantasy Writers of America]] |date=2017-05-20 |access-date=2017-05-21}}</ref> the 2017 [[Hugo Award for Best Novella]],<ref name="Hugo17">{{cite web |url=http://www.thehugoawards.org/hugo-history/2017-hugo-awards/ |title=2017 Hugo Awards |publisher=World Science Fiction Society |access-date=2017-08-11 |archive-url=https://www.webcitation.org/query?url=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.thehugoawards.org%2Fhugo-history%2F2017-hugo-awards%2F&date=2017-08-11 |archive-date=2017-08-11 |url-status=live}}</ref> and the 2017 [[Locus Award for Best Novella]].<ref name="Loc17">{{cite web|url=http://www.locusmag.com/News/2017/06/do-not-touch-2017-locus-awards-winners/|title=2017 Locus Awards Winners|date=2017-06-24|website=www.locusmag.com|publisher=Locus Online News|language=en|access-date=2017-06-25}}</ref>

McGuire is known for her urban fantasy novels. She adopted the [[pseudonym]] '''Mira Grant''' to write science fiction/horror.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.npr.org/2018/07/22/631255027/writer-seanan-mcguire-speaks-on-her-many-genres|title=Readers See Themselves In The Many Worlds Of Seanan McGuire|authorlink=Petra Mayer|last=Mayer|first=Petra|date=July 22, 2018|website=NPR|access-date=October 26, 2019}}</ref> As Grant, she published the political thriller/zombie series Newsflesh, comprising the books ''[[Feed (Grant novel)|Feed]]'' (2010), ''[[Deadline (Grant novel)|Deadline]]'' (2011), ''[[Blackout (Grant novel)|Blackout]]'' (2012), and ''Feedback'' (2016). ''Blackout'' was nominated for a 2013 [[Hugo Award]] for Best Novel. Additionally, in 2017, ''The Wine in Dreams'' was published as part of the novella collection, ''[[Star Wars: From a Certain Point of View|Star Wars: Canto Bight]]'', a tie-in to the 2017 film ''[[Star Wars: The Last Jedi]]''.

In 2013, McGuire received a record five Hugo nominations in total, two for works as Grant and the other three under her own name.<ref name=Grdn/>

== Biography ==
McGuire was born in California. She has described her interests as including "swamps, long walks, long walks ''in'' swamps, things that live in swamps, horror movies, strange noises, musical theater, reality TV, comic books, finding pennies on the street, and venomous reptiles."<ref>{{cite web|url=http://seananmcguire.com/bio.php|title=Seanan McGuire: Biography|website=seananmcguire.com|access-date=2018-03-28}}</ref> She identifies as [[Pansexuality|pansexual]],<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://justlovereviews.com/2017/09/07/women-in-queer-sff-seanan-mcguire/|title=Women in Queer SFF: Interview with Seanan McGuire|date=Sep 7, 2017|access-date=Sep 26, 2019}}</ref> or [[Bisexuality|bisexual]],<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://twitter.com/seananmcguire/status/590979380660178944|title=I am not a lesbian when I am with a woman. I am not straight when I am with a man. I am not asexual when I am with neither. I'm bi.|first=Seanan|last=McGuire|date=Apr 22, 2015|access-date=Sep 26, 2019}}</ref> and [[Gray asexuality|demisexual]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://twitter.com/seananmcguire/status/831207171643695104|title=My name is pronounced SHAWN-in, and while there are many aspects to the book that are not mine, I identify as demisexual.|first=Seanan|last=McGuire|date=Feb 13, 2017|access-date=Nov 24, 2019}}</ref>

== Awards and nominations ==
McGuire was awarded the [[John W. Campbell Award for Best New Writer]] by the 2010 [[World Science Fiction Convention]].<ref name="Hugo">{{cite web|url=http://www.thehugoawards.org/campbell-history/ |title=John W. Campbell Award History|website=www.thehugoawards.org}}</ref>

In 2010, McGuire's novel ''Feed'' (written under the [[pseudonym]] Mira Grant) ranked #74 in a [[National Public Radio]] listener poll of the top 100 [[thriller (genre)|thriller]] novels of all time.<ref name="Audience Picks: Top 100 'Killer Thrillers'">{{cite news|url=https://www.npr.org/templates/story/story.php?storyId=128718927 |title=Audience Picks: Top 100 'Killer Thrillers'|work=NPR|date=2010}}</ref> ''Feed'' was also selected by ''[[Publishers Weekly]]'' as one of their Best Books of 2010.<ref name="Publishers Weekly Best Books of 2010">{{cite web|url=http://www.publishersweekly.com/pw/by-topic/book-news/awards-and-prizes/article/45070-best-books-of-2010.html?page=3|title=Publishers Weekly Best Books of 2010|access-date=28 October 2018}}</ref> It was nominated for the 2011 Shirley Jackson Award, as well as the 2011 [[Hugo Award for Best Novel]].<ref name="Hugo11">{{Cite web|url=http://www.renovationsf.org/cgi-sys/suspendedpage.cgi|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110429050214/http://www.renovationsf.org/hugo-intro.php|url-status=dead|title=Account Suspended|archive-date=April 29, 2011|website=www.renovationsf.org|access-date=Sep 26, 2019}}</ref> Its sequel, ''Deadline'', was also nominated for the 2012 Hugo Award for Best Novel. The stand-alone Newsflesh story "Countdown" was nominated for the 2012 Best Novella Hugo. Her [[filk]] album ''Wicked Girls'' was nominated for the 2012 Best Related Work Hugo. The ''[[SF Squeecast]]'', a podcast to which she contributes, won the 2012 [[Hugo Award]] for best fancast.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.thehugoawards.org/hugo-history/2012-hugo-awards/|title=2012 Hugo Awards|publisher=The Hugo Awards|access-date=April 9, 2012|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://www.webcitation.org/66nnsGGRI?url=http://www.thehugoawards.org/hugo-history/2012-hugo-awards/|archive-date=April 9, 2012}}</ref>

McGuire was the inductee into the 2012 [[Darrell Awards]] Hall of Fame for her contributions to mid-south science fiction.<ref name="Darrell Awards">{{cite web|url=http://freepages.misc.rootsweb.ancestry.com/~timgatewood/sf/darrell/|title=The Darrell Awards|access-date=28 October 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160411200829/http://freepages.misc.rootsweb.ancestry.com/~timgatewood/sf/darrell/|archive-date=11 April 2016|url-status=dead}}</ref>

''SF Squeecast'' won its second Hugo in 2013. ''Blackout'' received a 2013 Hugo nomination for Best Novel. In addition, McGuire received three other nominations (a record five nominations in a single year): best novella (as Grant) and twice for best novelette (both as McGuire).<ref name=Grdn>{{cite news|last=Flood|first=Alison|title=Seanan McGuire gets record five nominations for Hugo awards|url=https://www.theguardian.com/books/2013/mar/31/seanan-mcguire-hugo-awards-shortlist|access-date=31 March 2013|newspaper=The Guardian|date=31 March 2013}}</ref>

McGuire won the 2016 [[Nebula Award]] for Best Novella for ''Every Heart a Doorway''.<ref name="Neb17"/> McGuire has won six [[Pegasus Award]]s:<ref name="pegasus">{{cite web|url=http://www.ovff.org/pegasus/people/seanan-mcguire.html|title=Pegasus Awards – Seanan McGuire|website=www.ovff.org|access-date=28 October 2018}}</ref>

''Every Heart a Doorway'' was nominated for a [[World Fantasy Award—Long Fiction|World Fantasy Award]] in 2017. ''Beneath the Sugar Sky'' was nominated for the 2019 [[World Fantasy Award for Best Novella]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.worldfantasy.org/world-fantasy-awards%e2%84%a0-2019/|title=World Fantasy Awards℠ 2019 {{!}} World Fantasy Convention|language=en-US|access-date=2019-07-25}}</ref>

McGuire has won the [[American Library Association]]'s [[Alex Award]] twice: in 2017 for ''Every Heart a Doorway''<ref name="alex17">{{cite web|url=http://www.ala.org/yalsa/2017-alex-awards|title=Alex Awards 2017|website=www.ala.org|access-date=21 November 2018|date=2018-02-13}}</ref> and in 2018 for ''Down Among the Sticks and Bones''.<ref name="alex18">{{cite web|url=http://www.ala.org/news/press-releases/2018/02/yalsa-announces-2018-alex-awards|title=Alex Awards 2018|website=www.ala.org|access-date=21 November 2018|date=2018-02-12}}</ref> This makes her the first author to win the award in two consecutive years.<ref name="alextor">{{cite web|url=https://www.tor.com/2018/02/12/seanan-mcguires-down-among-the-sticks-and-bones-martha-wells-all-systems-red-2018-alex-award-winners-ala/|title=Alex Awards Tor Press Release|website=www.tor.com|access-date=21 November 2018|date=2018-02-12}}</ref>

McGuire has received five nominations for the [[Hugo Award for Best Series]]. The October Daye series was nominated in 2017, 2019, and 2021, and the InCryptid series was nominated in 2018 and 2020.
