'''Jean Marie Auel''' ({{IPAc-en|aʊ|l}}; {{née|'''Untinen'''}}; born February 18, 1936) is an American writer who wrote the ''[[Earth's Children]]'' books, a series of novels set in [[prehistoric Europe]] that explores human activities during this time, and touches on the interactions of [[Cro-Magnon]] people with [[Neanderthal]]s. Her books have sold more than 45 million copies worldwide.<ref>[http://www.publishersweekly.com/pw/by-topic/book-news/deals/article/43338-new-jean-m-auel.html Publishers Weekly]</ref>

==Early years==
Auel was born Jean Marie Untinen in 1936 in [[Chicago]].<ref name="bluebook">{{cite web|url=https://www.oregonencyclopedia.org/articles/auel_jean_m_1936_/|title=Jean M. Auel (1936-)
|publisher=[[Oregon Encyclopedia]]|date=January 2021|access-date=April 27, 2021}}</ref> She is of Finnish descent, the second of five children of Neil Solomon Untinen, a housepainter, and Martha (''née'' Wirtanen) Untinen.

Auel attended [[University of Portland]].<ref name="updist" /> While a student, she joined [[Mensa International|Mensa]]<ref>{{cite journal|title=They're Accomplished, They're Famous, and They're Mensans|journal=Mensa Bulletin|publisher=American Mensa|issn=0025-9543|date=July 2004|issue=476|page=27}}</ref> and worked at [[Tektronix]] as a clerk (1965–1966), a circuit-board designer (1966–1973), a technical writer (1973–1974), and a credit manager (1974–1976). She earned an [[Master of Business Administration|MBA]] from the [[University of Portland]] in 1976.<ref name="updist">{{cite web|url=http://www.up.edu/alumni/default.aspx?cid=7480&pid=2850|title=Alumni: Distinguished Alumni Awards| publisher=[[University of Portland]]|access-date=2010-05-03}}</ref> She received honorary degrees from her [[alma mater]], [[Pacific University]], [[Portland State University]], the [[University of Maine]] and the [[Mount Vernon College for Women]].<ref>[http://www.authorsroad.com/JeanMarieAuel.html The Authors Road]</ref>

==Career as novelist==
In 1977, Auel began extensive library research of the [[Quaternary glaciation|Ice Age]] for her first book. She joined a [[survival skills|survival class]] to learn how to construct an ice cave, and learned primitive methods of making fire, tanning leather, and knapping stone from the [[indigenous peoples|aboriginal]] skills expert Jim Riggs.<ref>[https://books.google.com/books?id=cxI3snAr_7IC&pg=PP8&lpg=PP8&dq=jean+auel+jim+riggs&source=bl&ots=wMuEaIyWtl&sig=K-BHD4pVacSLIQo_oP2LKiuPWY4&hl=en&ei=zwRMTdS3HZG6sQOAhvmQCg&sa=X&oi=book_result&ct=result&resnum=10&sqi=2&ved=0CGEQ6AEwCQ The Valley of Horses - Acknowledgements]</ref>

''[[The Clan of the Cave Bear]]'' was nominated for numerous literary awards, including an [[American Booksellers Association]] nomination for best first novel.<ref>[http://www.randomhouse.com/features/auel/webroot/authorqa.html Jean M. Auel :: Author Q&A]<!-- Bot generated title --> from [[Random House]]</ref> It was also later adapted into a screenplay for [[The Clan of the Cave Bear (film)|the film of the same name]].

After the sales success of her first book, Auel has been able to travel to the sites of prehistoric ruins and relics, and also to meet many of the experts with whom she had been corresponding. Her research has taken her across Europe from France to [[Ukraine]], including most of what [[Marija Gimbutas]] called [[Old European culture|Old Europe]]. In 1986, she attended and co-sponsored a conference on modern human origins at the School of American Research, Santa Fe.<ref>Stringer, Christopher & Gamble, Clive ''In Search of the Neanderthals'' plate 96 (1993, Thames and Hudson, London) {{ISBN|0-500-27807-5}}</ref> She has developed a close friendship with Doctor [[Jean Clottes]] of France, who was responsible for the exploration of the [[Cosquer Cave]] discovered in 1985 and the [[Chauvet Cave]] discovered in 1994.<ref>[http://www.randomhouse.com/features/auel/webroot/video.html Jean M. Auel :: Video Interviews]<!-- Bot generated title --> from [[Random House]]</ref><ref>[http://donsmaps.com/auel.html "An Evening With Jean Auel"]<!-- Bot generated title --> from donsmaps.com</ref>

In October 2008, Auel was named an Officer of the [[Ordre des Arts et des Lettres]] by the French Minister of Culture and Communication.<ref>{{cite web| title=Jean Auel wins French award | date=October 13, 2008 | url= http://blog.oregonlive.com/books/2008/10/jean_auel_wins_french_award.html | publisher=[[The Oregonian]] | work= Bookmarks (a literary blog)| author=Jeff Baker| access-date=2010-05-04}}</ref>

==Bibliography==
By 1990, Auel's first three books in her ''[[Earth's Children]]'' series had sold more than 20 million copies worldwide and  been translated into 18 languages; [[Crown Publishing Group|Crown Publishers]] paid Auel about $25 million for the rights to publish ''The Plains of Passage'' and the two subsequent volumes.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.time.com/time/printout/0,8816,971440,00.html|archive-url=https://archive.today/20120912161304/http://www.time.com/time/printout/0,8816,971440,00.html|url-status=dead|archive-date=September 12, 2012|title=Books: Queen of The Ice Age Romance|publisher=[[Time (magazine)|Time]]|date=October 22, 1990|access-date=2010-05-04}}</ref> By May 2002, on the cusp of the publication of the fifth book, the series had sold 34 million books.<ref name="time2002">{{cite web| url=http://www.time.com/time/printout/0,8816,971440,00.html| archive-url=https://archive.today/20120912161304/http://www.time.com/time/printout/0,8816,971440,00.html| url-status=dead| archive-date=September 12, 2012|title=Books: Romancing The Stone Age|publisher=[[Time (magazine)|Time]]|date=May 13, 2002|access-date=2010-05-04}}</ref> The sixth and final book in the series, ''The Land of Painted Caves'', was published in 2011.<ref name="publishers weekly">{{cite web|url=http://www.publishersweekly.com/pw/by-topic/book-news/deals/article/43338-new-jean-m-auel.html| title=New Jean Auel|date= May 27, 2010|access-date=2010-05-27}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Jean Marie Untinen married Ray Bernard Auel after high school. They have five children and live in [[Portland, Oregon]]<ref name="bluebook"/> in the [[Goose Hollow, Portland, Oregon|Goose Hollow]] neighborhood.<ref name=prince>{{cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=YABWNNHirLIC&pg=PP1|title=Portland's Goose Hollow|last=Prince|first=Tracy J.|isbn=978-0-7385-7472-1|page=125|year=2011|publisher=Arcadia Publishing|location=Charleston, South Carolina}}</ref>
