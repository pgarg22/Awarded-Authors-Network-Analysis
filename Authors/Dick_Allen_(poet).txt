'''Richard Stanley Allen''' (August 8, 1939 – December 26, 2017) was an [[American poetry|American]] poet, literary critic and academic.

==Early life==

The son of Richard Sanders Allen, a writer and historian, and Doris (née Bishop), a postmaster, Allen was educated at the College of Liberal Arts at [[Syracuse University]] (A.B. 1961), then at the [[Brown University]] graduate school (M.A. 1964), and subsequently undertook two years of post-Masters work.

==Career==
Having been a teaching assistant at Brown whilst studying, he went on to teach creative writing and English literature at [[Wright State University]] from 1964-8, then the [[University of Bridgeport]].<ref>Science Fiction and Fantasy Literature, vol. 2, R. Reginald, 1979, pg 794</ref> When he retired, he was the Charles A. Dana Endowed Chair Professor at the University of Bridgeport.

From July 1, 2010, through June 30, 2015, he served as Connecticut's [[poet laureate]]. During this time he wrote the poem "Solace" in remembrance of the victims of the shootings at the Sandy Hook Elementary School in Newton, CT. This poem was subsequently set to music by the composer William Bolcom.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.wnpr.org/post/simple-solemn-tribute-sandy-hook-victims|title = A Simple, Solemn Tribute to Sandy Hook Victims|date = 11 December 2013}}</ref>

Allen was co-editor of several anthologies of science fiction and science fiction criticism,<ref name=autogenerated1>[http://www.expansivepoetryonline.com/journal/poemsf122003.html Dick Allen, Space Sonnets segment, Skeltonics for Poets and Others<!-- Bot generated title -->]</ref> and his book, ''Overnight in the Guest House of the Mystic,'' was a finalist for the [[National Book Critics Circle Award|1984 National Book Critics Circle Award for Poetry]].<ref>{{cite web |title=All Past National Book Critics Circle Award Winners and Finalists |url=http://bookcritics.org/awards/past_awards/ |publisher=National Book Critics Circle |access-date=March 24, 2016 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190427180857/http://bookcritics.org/awards/past_awards/ |archive-date=27 April 2019 |url-status=dead }}</ref> He was one of the founders of the Expansive Poetry movement. His influences included [[Ralph Waldo Emerson]], [[A.E. Housman]], [[Ben Jonson]], [[Robert Frost]]<ref>{{cite web|title=Dick Allen |url=http://www.poetryfoundation.org/bio/dick-allen|publisher=Poetry Foundation |access-date=March 24, 2016}}</ref>

His poems appeared in many journals, including ''[[Poetry magazine|Poetry]]'', ''[[The Atlantic Monthly]]'', ''[[The New Republic]]'', ''[[The New Yorker]]'', ''[[The American Poetry Review]]'', ''[[Ploughshares]]'', ''[[The Kenyon Review]]'', ''[[The Hudson Review]]'', ''[[The Massachusetts Review]]'',  ''[[Yale Review|The Yale Review]]'', ''Boulevard'',  ''[[Gettysburg Review|The Gettysburg Review]]'',  ''JuxtaProse Literary Magazine'', and ''[[The New Criterion]]''.<ref name=autogenerated1 /> Allen died on December 26, 2017 after a heart attack.<ref>[http://www.courant.com/entertainment/arts-theater/hc-obit-dick-allen-poet-1228-story.html ''Hartford Courant'' obituary Dick Allen poet]</ref>
