'''Joana Vaz''' ({{circa|1500}}&ndash;after 1570) was a [[Renaissance humanist]] and poet in the court of [[Catherine of Austria, Queen of Portugal]].<ref name="boxer">{{cite book |title=João de Barros: Portuguese Humanist and Historian of Asia |first=Charles Ralph |last=Boxer |publisher=Concept Publishing Company |year=1981 |pages=17-18 |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=BFvIGilXkVQC&pg=PA18}}</ref><ref name="stevenson">{{cite book |title=Women Latin Poets: Language, Gender, and Authority, from Antiquity to the Eighteenth Century |first=Jane |last=Stevenson |publisher=Oxford University Press |year=2005 |isbn=9780198185024 |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=iiMbQDFUfPoC&pg=PA217 |page=217}}</ref>

Catherine's court included a number of scholarly women, including Vaz, Catherine's cousin [[Maria of Portugal, Duchess of Viseu|Maria]], and the sisters Angela and [[Luisa Sigea de Velasco]].<ref name="boxer"/> Vaz served as a [[maid of honour]] to Catherine and was reportedly director of the ''[[aula regia]]''.<ref name="stevenson"/> Vaz was fluent in Latin, Greek and Hebrew, and is reported to have written extensively in the first.<ref name="boxer"/> She served as a tutor to Catherine's cousin and to the princess, also called [[Maria Manuela, Princess of Portugal|Maria]].<ref>{{cite book |title=Documenting the Early Modern Book World: Inventories and Catalogues in Manuscript and Print |first1=Malcolm |last1=Walsby |first2=Natasha |last2=Constantinidou |publisher=Brill |year=2013 |page=102 |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=Ze_IAQAAQBAJ&pg=PA102}}</ref>

Vaz was clearly considered to be an authority by her contemporary scholars: the poet [[Francisco de Sá de Meneses]] referred to her as such in a letter,<ref>{{cite book |title=Women Telling Nations |first1=Amelia |last1=Sanz |first2=Francesca |last2=Scott |first3=Suzan |last3=van Dijk |publisher=Rodopi |year=2014 |isbn=9789401211123 |pages=41-42 |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=8uKaBQAAQBAJ&pg=PA41}}</ref> and [[Pope Paul III]] was reported to have admired her writing.<ref name="boxer"/> The poet [[Clenardus]] described her as "elegantly trained in literature" and asked her to contribute a poem for the funeral of [[Erasmus]].<ref>{{cite book |title=Poemata Latina |first=André |last=de Resende |translator=John R. C. Martyn |publisher=Edwin Mellen Press |year=1998 |isbn=9780773483316 |page=446 |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=zIhiAAAAMAAJ}}</ref>
