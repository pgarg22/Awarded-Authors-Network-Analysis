'''Zeng Lili''' ({{lang|zh-hans|曾黎力}}; 13 February 1942 – 18 July 2018), better known by her [[pen name]] '''Ling Li''' ({{zh|s=凌力|hp=Líng Lì}}), was a Chinese writer and historian. She was born in [[Yan'an]]. Her ancestral home is [[Yudu County]], [[Jiangxi Province]]. She graduated from PLA's Xi'an Institute of Telecommunication Engineering (now Xidian University). Having worked as a missile engineer for 12 years, she transferred to the institute of Qing Dynasty History at [[Renmin University]] and became a scholar. She was the vice chairman of the [[Beijing Writers Association]], and a member of Chinese Writers Association's 6th committee. She was awarded the [[Lao She Literary Award]] in 2000.
