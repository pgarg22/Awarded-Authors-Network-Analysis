'''Sarah Janet Maas''' (born March 5, 1986) is an American fantasy author, best known for her debut series ''[[Throne of Glass]]'' published in 2012 and ''[[A Court of Thorns and Roses]]'' series, published in 2015.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.goodreads.com/author/show/3433047.Sarah_J_Maas |title=Sarah J.Maas | publisher=[[Goodreads]] | access-date=June 19, 2015}}</ref> Her newest work is the ''Crescent City'' series. As of 2021, she has sold over twelve million copies of her books and has been translated into thirty-seven languages.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Andreeva |first1=Nellie |title=‘A Court Of Thorns And Roses’ Series Based On Fantasy Books In Works At Hulu From Ron Moore & Sarah J. Maas |url=https://deadline.com/2021/03/a-court-of-thorns-and-roses-series-fantasy-books-hulu-ron-moore-sarah-j-maas-1234722918/ |website=Deadline |access-date=May 16, 2021}}</ref>

== Early life ==
Sarah J. Maas was born on March 5, 1986, in [[New York City, New York]].<ref name="isfdb">{{cite web | url=http://www.isfdb.org/cgi-bin/ea.cgi?176175 | title=Sarah J. Maas | publisher=ISFDB | access-date=June 19, 2015}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://twitter.com/SJMaas/status/511958475207749633|title=Sarah J. Maas on Twitter|work=Twitter|access-date=January 20, 2016}}</ref>

In 2008, Maas graduated [[magna cum laude]] from [[Hamilton College (New York)|Hamilton College]] in [[Clinton, Oneida County, New York]], where she majored in [[creative writing]] and minored in [[religious studies]].<ref name="about">{{cite web | url=http://sarahjmaas.com/about-sarah/ | title=About Sarah | publisher=Sarah J. Maas | access-date=June 19, 2015}}</ref>

== Personal life ==
Maas lives in [[Pennsylvania]] with her husband, Josh, her son Taran, and her dog, Annie. Her son was born in June 2018. Maas met Josh on the first day of her freshman year of college.<ref name="personal life" />

==Career==

Maas began writing what would become her debut novel, ''[[Throne of Glass]]'', when she was sixteen years old.<ref name="about"/><ref name="stephbowe">{{cite web |title=Interview with Sarah J. Maas |url=http://www.stephbowe.com/2009/10/interview-with-sarah-j-maas.html |publisher=Steph Browe |date=October 11, 2009 |access-date=June 19, 2015}}</ref> After writing several chapters of the novel, then titled ''Queen of Glass'', Maas posted them on [[FanFiction.Net#FictionPress|FictionPress.com]], where it was one of the most popular stories on the site. It was later removed from the site when Maas decided to publish the novel.<ref name="stephbowe"/> The story line of the series is based on the story of [[Cinderella]], with the premise of "What if Cinderella was not a servant, but an assassin? And what if she didn't attend the ball to meet the prince, but to kill him, instead?"<ref name="stephbowe"/><ref>{{cite news |first=Serena |last=Chase |title=Review: ''Throne of Glass'' by Sarah J. Maas |work=[[USA Today]] |date=August 16, 2012 |access-date=June 19, 2015 |url=http://books.usatoday.com/happyeverafter/post/2012-08-15/throne-of-glass-review-sarah-j-maas/825478/1}}</ref> In 2008, Maas started sending the story to agents before signing with Tamar Rydzinski of The Laura Dial Literary Agency in 2009.<ref name="stephbowe"/> ''[[Throne of Glass]]'' was purchased in March 2010 by [[Bloomsbury Publishing|Bloomsbury]], who later purchased two additional books in the series.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.goodreads.com/author_blog_posts/1975482-big-news-reveal-part-2 |title=Big News Reveal |author=Sarah Maas |publisher=Goodreads |date=January 16, 2012 |access-date=June 19, 2015}}</ref> The series is available in 15 countries and 35 languages.<ref name="faq">{{cite web |url=http://sarahjmaas.com/extras/faq/ |title=FAQ |publisher=Sarah J. Maas |access-date=June 19, 2015}}</ref><ref name="about"/> While several prequel novellas set two years before the first novel were also published, these were later condensed into one book, ''The Assassin's Blade''.<ref name="backward">{{cite web |url=http://abackwardsstory.blogspot.co.uk/2012/01/interview-with-sarah-j-maas-author-of.html |title=Interview with Sarah J. Maas |publisher=A Backwards Story |date=January 14, 2012 |access-date=June 19, 2015}}</ref> The second book of the series, ''Crown of Midnight'' was a New York Times young adult best-seller.<ref name="isfdb"/><ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.nytimes.com/best-sellers-books/2013-09-15/young-adult/list.html |title=Best Sellers – Young Adult |work=New York Times |date=September 15, 2013 |access-date=June 19, 2015}}</ref> The final book in the series, ''Kingdom of Ash'', was released on October 23, 2018; the finished series comprised seven books.<ref name="faq"/><ref name=":0">{{cite web |url=https://www.bloomsbury.com/us/kingdom-of-ash-9781619636118/ |title=Kingdom of Ash |website=bloomsbury.com |access-date=June 4, 2018}}</ref>

''[[A Court of Thorns and Roses]]'', Maas' second fantasy series, is a loose retelling of the traditional ''[[Beauty and the Beast]]''.<ref name="bustle"/><ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.theguardian.com/childrens-books-site/2015/jun/15/a-court-of-thorns-and-roses-sarah-j-maas-review |title= A Court of Thorns and Roses by Sarah J. Maas – review |work=[[The Guardian]] |date=June 15, 2015 |access-date=June 19, 2015}}</ref> The first book of the trilogy was written in 2009, but was not published until 2015. Due to the success and popularity of the original series, it was extended and a spin-off series was announced which would feature stories of other popular characters. The fourth book in the series and the first of the spin-offs, ''A Court of Silver Flames'' published on February 16, 2021.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.goodreads.com/work/best_book/51666936-a-court-of-silver-flames|title=A Court of Silver Flames (A Court of Thorns and Roses, #4)|website=www.goodreads.com|accessdate=April 15, 2021}}</ref> The series is set to be adapted into a television series for [[Hulu]].<ref>{{cite web |last1=Andreeva |first1=Nellie |title=‘A Court Of Thorns And Roses’ Series Based On Fantasy Books In Works At Hulu From Ron Moore & Sarah J. Maas |url=https://deadline.com/2021/03/a-court-of-thorns-and-roses-series-fantasy-books-hulu-ron-moore-sarah-j-maas-1234722918/ |website=[[Deadline Hollywood|Deadline]] |access-date=October 12, 2021 |date=March 26, 2021}}</ref>

On May 16, 2018, Maas announced her third fantasy series which is also her first adult fantasy series, ''Crescent City''. The first book, titled ''House of Earth and Blood'', was released by Bloomsbury on March 3, 2020.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.goodreads.com/work/best_book/46924772-house-of-earth-and-blood |title=House of Earth and Blood (Crescent City, #1) |website=goodreads.com |access-date=October 21, 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.instagram.com/p/Bi2CqvTBAX2/ |title=Sarah J. Maas on Instagram: "So ridiculously excited to finally be able to announce this (link in bio for more details)!! I've been working on Crescent City for several…" |website=Instagram |access-date=August 20, 2018}}</ref> It was ranked one of the top twenty Science Fiction & Fantasy books of 2020 on [[Kobo eReader|Kobo]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Our top 20 Science Fiction & Fantasy picks of 2020 |url=https://www.kobo.com/us/en/list/our-top-20-science-fiction-fantasy-picks-of-2020/2MXFT5seusYwJQjYkx2udA |website=Kobo |access-date=November 1, 2021}}</ref> The sequel, ''House of Sky and Breath'', has a release date of February 15th, 2022.<ref>{{Cite web|title=See the cover for Sarah J. Maas' next 'Crescent City' novel 'House of Sky and Breath'|url=https://ew.com/books/see-the-cover-for-sarah-j-maas-next-crescent-city-novel-house-of-sky-and-breath/|access-date=2021-10-02|website=EW.com|language=en}}</ref>

==Writing style and influences==
In an interview with ''Writers & Artists'', Maas told them that "Music—especially movie scores and classical music" is her inspiration of writing.<ref name="Writers&Artists">{{cite web|url=https://www.writersandartists.co.uk/writers/advice/550/a-writers-toolkit/interviews-with-authors/|title=Interview with Sarah J. Maas|website=Writers & Artists}}</ref> She continued on to say that ''[[Sabriel]]'' written by [[Garth Nix]] and ''[[The Hero and the Crown]]'' by [[Robin McKinley]] "kindled my love for reading fantasy and my desire to write it..."<ref name="Writers&Artists"/> Her character development has been lauded as one of her best qualities for storytelling, with morally grey characters and strong world building.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Noorbakhsh |first1=Sonia |title=What Makes Sarah J. Maas A No. 1 NYT Bestselling Fantasy Author? |url=https://studybreaks.com/culture/reads/sarah-j-maas-nyt-bestselling-fantasy-author/ |website=Studybreaks |access-date=November 29, 2021 |date=November 6, 2021}}</ref>

Maas has mentioned that, "The sense of discovery is why I love writing so much. It’s a total thrill for me."<ref>{{Cite web|date=June 13, 2020|title=Sarah J Maas: Struggle with guilt of wanting to spend time with son when on a deadline|url=https://www.hindustantimes.com/books/sarah-j-maas-struggle-with-guilt-of-wanting-to-spend-time-with-son-when-on-a-deadline/story-PckWCe8ARKTVsseuw6ykUL.html|access-date=February 27, 2021|website=Hindustan Times|language=en}}</ref>
