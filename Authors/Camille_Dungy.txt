'''Camille T. Dungy''' (born 1972) is an [[United States|American]] [[poet]] and professor.

==Career==
Born in [[Denver, Colorado]], Dungy graduated from [[Stanford University]] (BA) and the [[University of North Carolina, Greensboro]], where she earned her MFA.<ref>[https://www.poetryfoundation.org/poets/camille-t-dungy "Camille T. Dungy"], Poetry Foundation.</ref>

She is the author of four poetry collections – ''Trophic Cascade'' ([[Wesleyan University Press]], 2016), ''Smith Blue'' ([[Southern Illinois University Press]], 2011), ''Suck on the Marrow'' ([[Red Hen Press]], 2010) and ''What to Eat, What to Drink, What to Leave for Poison'' (Red Hen Press, 2006) – as well as a recent collection of essays entitled ''Guidebook to Relative Strangers'' ([[W.W. Norton]], 2017). Dungy is editor of ''Black Nature: Four Centuries of African American Nature Poetry'' (UGA, 2009), co-editor of ''From the Fishouse: An Anthology of Poems that Sing, Rhyme, Resound, Syncopate, Alliterate, and Just Plain Sound Great'' (Persea, 2009), and assistant editor of ''Gathering Ground: A Reader Celebrating Cave Canem’s First Decade'' (University of Michigan Press, 2006).<ref>[http://www.fishousepoems.org/artist/dungy-camille/ "Camille T. Dungy"], From the Fishouse – an audio archive of emerging poets.</ref> Her poems have appeared in literary journals and magazines, including ''[[The American Poetry Review]]'', ''[[Poetry (magazine)|Poetry]]'', ''[[Callaloo (journal)|Callaloo]]'', ''[[The Missouri Review]]'',<ref>{{Cite journal | url = https://books.google.com/books?id=bVCxAAAAIAAJ&q=Camille+Dungy | journal = The Missouri Review | title=Poetry| pages = 135–137| publisher = University of Missouri--Columbia. Dept of English| year = 2001}}</ref> ''[[Crab Orchard Review]]'', ''Poetry Daily''. She is also a contributor to [[Margaret Busby]]'s 2019 anthology ''[[New Daughters of Africa]]''.<ref>{{cite magazine|url=https://newint.org/features/2019/02/11/long-read-enter-new-daughters-africa|title=The New Daughters of Africa|magazine=[[New Internationalist]]|date=2019-04-17|access-date=2021-06-06}}</ref>

Dungy's honors include fellowships from the [[National Endowment for the Arts]], the [[Virginia Commission for the Arts]], and the [[Bread Loaf Writers' Conference]], [[Cave Canem Foundation|Cave Canem]], the [[American Antiquarian Society]], and the [[Sewanee Writers' Conference]], and she is the recipient of the 2011 [[American Book Award]], a 2010 [[California Book Award]] silver medal, a two-time recipient of the Northern California Book Award, and a two-time [[NAACP Image Award]] nominee.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.torchpoetry.org/Fall%2007/camilledungy.htm |title=''Torch'' > Camille T. Dungy Bio and Poems |access-date=2009-07-22 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090610091717/http://www.torchpoetry.org/Fall%2007/camilledungy.htm |archive-date=2009-06-10 |url-status=dead }}</ref><ref>[http://www.camilledungy.com/Biography.htm Bio], Camille T. Dungy website.</ref> Recently a professor in the Creative Department at [[San Francisco State University]] (2011–2013), she is currently a professor in the English Department at [[Colorado State University]].<ref>[https://english.colostate.edu/faculty-staff/cdungy/ "Camille Dungy"] at Colorado State University.</ref> In 2019, Dungy was awarded a [[Guggenheim Fellowship]] for her poetry.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.coloradoan.com/story/life/2019/04/22/colorado-state-university-professor-awarded-guggenheim-fellowship/3540651002/|title=CSU English professor awarded prestigious Guggenheim Fellowship|last=Udell|first=Erin|website=Coloradoan|language=en|access-date=2020-01-29}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.gf.org/fellows/all-fellows/camille-t-dungy/|title=John Simon Guggenheim Foundation {{!}} Camille T. Dungy|language=en-US|access-date=2020-01-29}}</ref>
