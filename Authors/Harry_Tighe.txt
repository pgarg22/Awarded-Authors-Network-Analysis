'''Harry Tighe''' (1877–1946) was an Australian playwright and novelist.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.ausstage.edu.au/pages/contributor/227990|title=AusStage|website=www.ausstage.edu.au|access-date=2017-09-22}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.hat-archive.com/harry_tighe.htm|title=Harry Tighe|website=www.hat-archive.com|access-date=2017-09-22}}</ref> Born in [[Newcastle, New South Wales]]<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://nla.gov.au/nla.news-article118832625|title=HARRY TIGHE|date=1922-10-25|work=Evening News (Sydney, NSW : 1869–1931)|access-date=2017-09-22|pages=8}}</ref> He was Cambridge educated and spent the most active part of his career in Britain. He spent three mature years living in [[Cremorne, New South Wales|Cremorne]], Sydney <ref>{{Cite news|url=http://nla.gov.au/nla.news-article32900995|title=AUSTRALIAN AUTHORS.|date=1935-09-18|work=West Australian|location=Perth|access-date=2017-09-22|pages=10}}</ref> during which he tried theatre production <ref>{{Cite news|url=http://nla.gov.au/nla.news-article224253300|title=DRAMA : DANCING|date=1930-07-17|work=The Sun|location=Sydney|access-date=2017-09-22|pages=29}}</ref> as a founder of the [[Independent Theatre]]<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://nla.gov.au/nla.news-article51689742|title="THE BUSH FIRE"|date=1931-04-03|work=The Examiner|location=Launceston, Tas.|access-date=2017-09-22|pages=8}}</ref> His passage to Australia was booked when he died.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://nla.gov.au/nla.news-article170269013|title=Obituary.|date=1937-01-27|work=Daily Mercury|location=Mackay, Qld.|access-date=2017-09-22|pages=7}}</ref>
