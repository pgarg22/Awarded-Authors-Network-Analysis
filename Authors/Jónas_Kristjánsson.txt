'''Jónas Kristjánsson''' (10 April 1924 – 7 June 2014) was an [[Iceland]]ic [[scholar]] and [[novelist]], and one-time director of the [[Árni Magnússon Institute for Icelandic Studies]]. In this position, he played a crucial role in the return of Icelandic manuscripts to Iceland from Denmark, representing Iceland in negotiations with the Danish authorities from 1972-86.<ref name="W. McT 2014">R[ory] W. McT[urk], 'Jónas Kristjánsson 10 April 1924-7 June 2014', ''Saga-Book'', 38 (2014), 113-15 (at 114).</ref>

==Career==
Jónas was a teacher at the [[Samvinnuskóli]] from 1952–55, archivist at the [[National Archives of Iceland]] from 1957–63, and the director of the [[Árni Magnússon Institute for Icelandic Studies]] from 1972-94 (from which position he retired upon reaching the age limit).<ref name="resign">[http://www.mbl.is/mm/gagnasafn/grein.html?grein_id=149301 Fjársjóður Íslands Jónas Kristjánsson er að láta af störfum forstöðumanns], ''[[Morgunblaðið]]'' (in Icelandic), August 7, 1994 (long article on his career); R[ory] W. McT[urk], 'Jónas Kristjánsson 10 April 1924-7 June 2014', ''Saga-Book'', 38 (2014), 113-15 (at 114).</ref> He spent 1978-79 in the UK.<ref>R[ory] W. McT[urk], 'Jónas Kristjánsson 10 April 1924-7 June 2014', ''Saga-Book'', 38 (2014), 113-15 (at 113).</ref> Jónas was also a member of the [[Norwegian Academy of Science and Letters]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.dnva.no/c26849/artikkel/vis.html?tid=40108|title=Gruppe 5: Filologi og språkvitenskap|publisher=[[Norwegian Academy of Science and Letters]]|language=Norwegian|accessdate=10 January 2011}}</ref>
On May 31, 1991 he received an [[Honorary degree|honorary doctorate]] from the Faculty of Humanities at [[Uppsala University]], [[Sweden]]<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.uu.se/en/about-uu/traditions/prizes/honorary-doctorates/|title = Honorary doctorates - Uppsala University, Sweden}}</ref>

==Works==

Jónas was best known for his works on [[Icelandic sagas]], laying emphasis on their literary nature and working on several stylistic and syntactic problems.<ref name="inst1">Davies, Karin. [https://news.google.com/newspapers?id=LbUyAAAAIBAJ&sjid=nuYFAAAAIBAJ&pg=2907,5228962&dq=j%C3%B3nas-kristj%C3%A1nsson&hl=en Iceland works to preserve its own folk tales], ''[[Lawrence Journal-World]]'' ([[Associated Press]]), November 17, 1991</ref><ref name="expert">[https://pqasb.pqarchiver.com/thestar/access/432946101.html?dids=432946101:432946101&FMT=ABS&FMTS=ABS:FT&type=current&date=Nov+12,+2001&author=&pub=The+Record&desc=Second+Viking+site+sought&pqatl=google Second Viking site sought],  ''[[Waterloo Region Record|The Record (Kitchener, Ontario)]]'', November 12, 2001 ("Prof Jonas Kristjansson an expert on the Norse sagas")</ref><ref name="expert 2">[https://pqasb.pqarchiver.com/boston/access/49067479.html?dids=49067479:49067479&FMT=ABS&FMTS=ABS:FT&type=current&date=Feb+04,+2000&author=Colin+Nickerson,+Globe+Staff&pub=Boston+Globe&desc=NEW+CLUES+EMERGE+ON+THE+VIKINGS'+VOYAGE&pqatl=google New Clues Emerge on the Vikings' Voyage], ''[[The Boston Globe]]'', February 4, 2000 ("Jonas Kristjansson one of Iceland's foremost scholars of the sagas")</ref> His 1972 doctoral thesis, ''Um fóstbræðra sögu'' presented new arguments about the dating of ''[[Fóstbræðra saga]]'' (arguing that it was not, as had previously been thought, relatively archaic, but relatively late).<ref>Jónas Kristjánsson, ''Um fóstbræðrasögu'', Rit (Stofnun Árna Magnússonar á Íslandi), 1 (Reykjavík: Stofnun Árna Magnússonar, 1972)</ref>

Jónas was an important editor of Icelandic texts, namely ''[[Dínus saga drambláta]]'',<ref>Jónas Kristjánsson (ed.), ''Dínus saga Drambláta'', Riddarasögur, 1 (Reykjavík: Háskóli Íslands, 1960).</ref> ''[[Viktors saga ok Blávus]]'',<ref>''Viktors saga ok Blávus'', ed. by Jónas Kristjánsson, Riddarasögur, 2 (Reykjavík: Handritastofnun Íslands, 1964)</ref> and a number of sagas for the [[Íslenzk fornrit]] series, on whose editorial board he sat from 1979,<ref name="W. McT 2014"/> including ''[[Svarfdæla saga]]'',<ref>''Eyfirðinga s̨ogur'', ed. by Jónas Kristjánsson, Íslenzk fornrit, 9 (Reykjavík: Hið íslenzka fornritafélag, 1956); cf. ''Svarfdælasaga'', ed. by Jónas Kristjánsson, Rit Handritastofnunar Íslands, 2 (Reykjavík: Handritastofnun Íslands, 1966).</ref> overseeing the editions of the ''[[bishops' sagas|biskupa sögur]]'' with [[Þórður Ingi Guðjónsson]],<ref>''Biskupa sögur'', Íslenzk fornrit, 15-17 (Reykjavík: Hið Íslenzka Fornritafélag, 2002-3)</ref> and co-editing the 2014 edition of the [[Poetic Edda]] with [[Vésteinn Ólason]].

Jónas wrote two historical novels, one of which, ''The Wide World'', is set in Viking age North America,{{Citation needed|date=October 2010}} and he translated [[Will Durant|Will Durant's]] ''The Life of Greece'' and [[Arthur Miller|Arthur Miller's]] ''[[Death of a Salesman]]'' into Icelandic.<ref name="W. McT 2014"/>
