'''Julian Kornhauser''' (born 20 September 1946 in [[Gliwice]], [[Poland]]) is a Polish poet and literary critic.

He was born to a [[Jews|Jewish]] father and a [[Catholic Church|Catholic]] mother,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ronslate.com/on-been-and-gone-poems-by-julian-kornhauser-translated-by-piotr-florczyk-marick-press/|title=on Been and Gone, poems by Julian Kornhauser, translated by Piotr Florczyk (Marick Press) – On the Seawall|publisher=}}</ref> as a son of Jakub and Małgorzata Kornhauser. He is an author of poems, novels and literary sketches. He also published translations of [[Serbian literature|Serbian]] and [[Croatian literature|Croatian]] poetry. At present, he works as a professor at the [[Jagiellonian University]] in [[Kraków]]. Through his daughter [[Agata Kornhauser-Duda]], his son-in-law is [[Andrzej Duda]], President of Poland for the [[Law and Justice]] party.
