'''John Meaney''' (born 1957 in London) is a British [[science fiction author]].

==Biography==
Meaney grew up in London and [[Slough, England]] with his brother Colm (who is not the same as the Star Trek actor [[Colm Meaney]]). He has studied martial arts since childhood and has a [[Black belt (martial arts)|black belt]] in [[shotokan karate]]. Meaney originally studied at [[Birmingham University]] and holds a combined degree in [[Physics]] and [[Computer Science]] from the [[Open University]]. He has done postgraduate work at [[Oxford University]] and is a part-time [[Information technology|IT]] consultant.

==Writing career==
Meaney's science fiction began appearing in 1992 with "Spring Rain" in the July 1992 issue of ''[[Interzone (magazine)|Interzone]]''. He has published over a dozen short pieces as of 2006. His novelette "Sharp Tang" was shortlisted for the [[BSFA award|British Science Fiction Association Award]] in 1995. His first and second novels, ''To Hold Infinity'' and ''Paradox'', were on the BSFA shortlists for Best Novel in 1999 and 2001. ''To Hold Infinity'' was also selected as one of the ''[[Daily Telegraph]]'''s "Books of the Year"[https://web.archive.org/web/20061003034911/http://www.booksattransworld.co.uk/catalog/author.htm?authorid=3031] [http://johnmeaney.tripod.com/johnmeaney.html#content]. He lives in [[Kent]] with his wife Yvonne.
