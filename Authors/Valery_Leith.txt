'''Tricia Sullivan''' (born July 7, 1968 in [[New Jersey]], United States) is a [[science fiction]] writer. She also writes fantasy under the pseudonym '''Valery Leith'''.

She moved to the United Kingdom in 1995. In 1999 she won the [[Arthur C. Clarke Award]] for her novel ''Dreaming in Smoke''.<ref name="tor2017">{{cite web|url=http://www.tor.com/2017/05/03/announcing-the-2017-arthur-c-clarke-award-shortlist/ |last=Alexander |first=Niall |title=Announcing the 2017 Arthur C. Clarke Award Shortlist |date=3 May 2017 |publisher=[[Tor.com]] |accessdate=4 May 2017}}</ref><ref name="clarkewinners">{{cite web|url=https://www.clarkeaward.com/award-winners/ |title=Award winners| publisher=Arthur C. Clarke Awards |accessdate=4 May 2017}}</ref> Her novels ''Maul'', ''Lightborn'', and ''Occupy Me'' have also been shortlisted for the Clarke award, in 2004, 2011, and 2017 respectively.<ref name="clarke04">{{cite web|url=http://www.infinityplus.co.uk/nonfiction/clarkes2004.htm |last=Roberts |first=Adam |title=The Arthur C Clarke Shortlist 2004 |publisher=[[Infinity Plus]] |accessdate=4 May 2017}}</ref><ref name="clarke11">{{cite web | url=https://www.clarkeaward.com/award-winners/shortlists/ | title=Award Shortlists | publisher=The Arthur C. Clarke Award | accessdate=April 8, 2018}}</ref><ref name="tor2017" /><ref name="clarke17">{{cite web|url=http://csff-anglia.co.uk/clarke-shadow-jury/shortlist-announced-the-arthur-c-clarke-award/ |title=Shortlist Announced â€“ The Arthur C. Clarke Award |publisher=The Anglia Ruskin Centre for Science Fiction and Fantasy |date=3 May 2017 |accessdate=4 May 2017}}</ref>

Sullivan has studied music and martial arts. Her partner is the martial artist Steve Morris, with whom she has three children. They live in [[Shropshire]].
