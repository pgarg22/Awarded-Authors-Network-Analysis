'''Matthew Zapruder''' (1967) is an American [[poet]], editor, translator, and professor.

His second poetry collection, ''The Pajamaist'', won the 2007 [[William Carlos Williams Award]] from the [[Poetry Society of America]], and was chosen by ''Library Journal'' as one of the top ten poetry volumes of 2006. His first book, ''American Linden'', won the Tupelo Press Editors' Prize.<ref>[http://www.blueflowerarts.com/mzapruder.html Author's Booking Agency: Blue Flower Arts - Author Page - Matthew Zapruder] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090619034431/http://www.blueflowerarts.com/mzapruder.html |date=2009-06-19 }}</ref> His most recent book of poetry, ''Sun Bear'', brings the strangeness of poetry closer to everyday life.

== Life ==
His poems have appeared in ''The Boston Review, [[The Believer (magazine)|The Believer]], Fence, Bomb, McSweeney's, Jubilat, Conduit, Harvard Review, [[The New Republic]],'' ''[[The New Yorker]]'', and ''[[The Paris Review]]''. In 2007, he was a Lannan Literary Fellow in Marfa, Texas.<ref name=aaas>News release and web page, [http://www.amacad.org/news/poetry.aspx "Young Poets Recognized by the American Academy of Arts and Sciences Five Receive Academy Prize in Honor of May Sarton"] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20081231100740/http://www.amacad.org/news/poetry.aspx |date=2008-12-31 }}, December 15, 2008, at the American Academy of Arts and Sciences website, retrieved December 17, 2008</ref> He is the winner of the Tupelo Poetry Editors' Prize and the 2008 May Sarton poetry award from the American Academy of Arts and Sciences. As of late 2008, German and Slovenian language editions of his poems were planned from Luxbooks and Serpa Editions. Luxbooks is also publishing a separate German language graphic novel version of his poem "The Pajamaist."<ref name=aaas/>

He was co-founder (with [[Brian Henry]]) and editor-in-chief of Verse Press, which has since become [[Wave Books]] and moved from Amherst, Massachusetts to Seattle, Washington. Matthew Zapruder and Joshua Beckman, who became friends when Beckman performed a reading in Amherst, are co-editors of Wave Books.<ref name="pub weekly">[http://www.publishersweekly.com/article/CA6593356.html ''Publishers Weekly'' 9/8/2008 ''Joshua Beckman and Matthew Zapruder: Bringing Poets and Readers Together'' by Craig Morgan Teicher]</ref>

He is an editor for [[Wave Books]], and teaches at [[Saint Mary's College of California]].<ref>{{Cite web | url=http://www.stmarys-ca.edu/english/faculty?facid=143976 |title = Faculty}}</ref>

Zapruder received his B.A. from [[Amherst College]], with a BA in Russian Literature, his M.A. from the [[University of California, Berkeley]] with an MA in Slavic Languages, and his M.F.A. from the [[MFA Program for Poets & Writers]] at the [[University of Massachusetts Amherst]].<ref name="pub weekly" /> He teaches in the low residency MFA program at the University of California, Riverside-Palm Desert and at the Juniper Summer Writing Institute at the University of Massachusetts, Amherst. He taught at [[New York University]], [[The New School]], the [[University of Houston]], [[St. Mary's College of California]], and [[University of California, Berkeley]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://english.berkeley.edu/contact/person_detail.php?person=217 |accessdate=March 10, 2015 }}{{dead link|date=May 2016|bot=medic}}{{cbignore|bot=medic}}</ref> He lives in San Francisco and is the brother of American musician and songwriter [[Michael Zapruder]], and is the guitarist in the American band The Figments.

In 2011, he was a [[Guggenheim Fellow]].<ref>[http://www.gf.org/fellows/17037-matthew-j-zapruder Matthew J. Zapruder - John Simon Guggenheim Memorial Foundation] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140222203730/http://www.gf.org/fellows/17037-matthew-j-zapruder |date=2014-02-22 }}</ref>
He had a [[Lannan Foundation]] Residency in [[Marfa, Texas]].
He won the May Sarton prize, [[American Academy of Arts and Sciences]].

In the fall of 2012, Zapruder's poetry was adapted and performed at [[Carnegie Hall]] with composition by [[Gabriel Kahane]] and [[Brooklyn Rider]].
