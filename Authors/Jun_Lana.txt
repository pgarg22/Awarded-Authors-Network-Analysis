'''Rodolfo R. Lana Jr.''' (born October 10, 1972), known professionally as '''Jun Robles Lana''', is a [[Philippines|Filipino]] filmmaker.<ref>{{cite web|title=Jun Lana’s historic win in Russian fest|url=http://www.philstar.com/entertainment/2015/09/27/1504397/jun-lanas-historic-win-russian-fest|website=Philstar Global}}</ref> The winner of 11 [[Palanca Awards|Palanca Awards for Literature]], he became the youngest member of the [[Palanca Awards#Palanca Hall of Fame|Palanca Hall of Fame]] in 2006.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://services.inquirer.net/print/print.php?article_id=20114 |title=Jun Lana: A Winner in Two Worlds |publisher=[[Philippine Daily Inquirer]] |date=2006-09-10 |access-date=2008-03-06 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080322173416/http://services.inquirer.net/print/print.php?article_id=20114 |archive-date=2008-03-22 }}</ref>
In 2015, he directed the multi-awarded film "Shadow Behind The Moon" which won the Best Director, NETPAC and FIPRESCI awards at the 13th Pacific Meridian Film Festival.<ref>{{cite web|title=Jun Robles Lana, LJ Reyes win top honors at Pacific Meridian Film Festival in Russia|url=http://www.rappler.com/entertainment/news/106845-lj-reyes-jun-lana-best-actress-director-pacific-meridian-film-festival-russia|website=Rappler}}</ref> At the 20th International Film Festival of Kerala, he won the Best Director award for the same film.<ref>{{cite web|title=Lana wins in India|url=http://entertainment.inquirer.net/185508/lana-wins-in-india|website=Philippine Daily Inquirer}}</ref>

==Writing career==
He was born Rodolfo Lana Jr. in [[Makati]], and attended local schools.

Interested in writing from an early age, he began to write and submit plays in Filipino language to competitions. He has adopted '''Jun Lana''' as his pen name. At age 19, he received an "Honorable Mention" citation in the 1991 Palanca Awards in the category ''Dulang May Isang Yugto'' (One-Act Play in [[Filipino language|Filipino]]), for his play ''Eksodo''.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.geocities.com/palanca_awards/1991.html |title=The Don Carlos Palanca Memorial Awards for Literature – 1991 |access-date=2008-03-06 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20071208181256/http://www.geocities.com/palanca_awards/1991.html|archive-date=2007-12-08}}</ref> The following year, Lana won Third Prize in the same category for ''Churchill''.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.geocities.com/palanca_awards/1992.html |title=The Don Carlos Palanca Memorial Awards for Literature – 1992 |access-date=2008-03-06 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20091022095638/http://geocities.com/palanca_awards/1992.html|archive-date=2009-10-22}}</ref>

In the next eight years, Lana won nine more Palanca Awards for his Filipino-language [[screenplay]]s and [[teleplay]]s, including First Prizes for the screenplays ''Karinyo-Brutal'' (1995) and ''Mga Bangka sa Tag-araw'' (1996); and for the teleplays ''Sa Daigdig ng mga Taksil'' (1995), and together with Peter Ong Lim, for ''Pula'' (1997).<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.geocities.com/palanca_awards/1995.html |title=The Don Carlos Palanca Memorial Awards for Literature – 1995 |access-date=2008-03-06 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20071207221852/http://www.geocities.com/palanca_awards/1995.html|archive-date=2007-12-07}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.geocities.com/palanca_awards/1996.html |title=The Don Carlos Palanca Memorial Awards for Literature – 1996 |access-date=2008-03-06 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20091022175144/http://geocities.com/palanca_awards/1996.html|archive-date=2009-10-22}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.geocities.com/palanca_awards/1997.html |title=The Don Carlos Palanca Memorial Awards for Literature – 1997 |access-date=2008-03-06 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080727215856/http://www.geocities.com/palanca_awards/1997.html|archive-date=2008-07-27}}</ref>

In 2006, Lana's teleplay ''Milagrosa'' won his fifth First Prize Palanca Award and his 11th overall. With his fifth First Prize, Lana was inducted into the Palanca Hall of Fame.

==Film and television work==

Since 1998, Lana has written screenplays for such directors as Marilou Diaz-Abaya Mel Chionglo and Maryo J. de los Reyes. His screenplay for Diaz-Abaya's ''Sa Pusod ng Dagat'' (1998) won Lana the Best Screenplay award from the Brussels European Film Festival|Brussels International Film Festival in 1998.<ref>{{cite news |title=ALBERT MARTINEZ IS BEST ACTOR IN BRUSSELS FILMFEST |url=http://www.newsflash.org/199811/sb/sb000618.htm |author=Sol Jose Vanzi |publisher=Philippine Headline News Online |date=1998-11-11 |access-date=2008-03-06 }}</ref>

He has won two [[FAMAS award|FAMAS Best Screenplay awards]] — in 1998 for ''[[Jose Rizal (film)|Jose Rizal]]'' (shared with Ricky Lee and Peter Ong Lim) and in 1999 for ''Soltera'' (shared with Jerry Lopez Sineneng). His screenplay for ''Jose Rizal'' also won awards from the [[Metro Manila Film Festival]] and the Star Awards for Movies. In 2005, Lana's Palanca-award-winning play ''Mga Estranghero at ang Gabi'' (1994) was adapted for film by Rody Vera. Renamed ''Pusang Gala'' and directed by Ellen Ongkeko-Marfil, the film was nominated for several FAMAS awards, including a Best Story nomination for Lana.

Lana made his film directorial debut with ''Gigil'' (2006), starring Katrina Halili. The following year, he wrote and directed ''Roxanne''. Since 2006, Lana has been employed by [[GMA Network|GMA Television Network]], where he functions as a creative consultant for the drama department, and as head writer of ''Magpakailanman'' and other shows.  Lana also directs for television, sometimes in collaboration with actor Cesar Montano. For GMA Network, he directed his Palanca Award-winning teleplay ''Milagroso'', which was aired as a television special and became a finalist at the 2006 Asian TV Awards. Lana has also directed television episodes for  ''Love2Love'', ''Wag Kukurap'', and ''Fantastikids''.

His 2012 film ''[[Bwakaw]]'' was selected as the Filipino entry for the [[Academy Award for Best Foreign Language Film|Best Foreign Language Oscar]] at the [[85th Academy Awards]], but it did not make the final shortlist.<ref name="Oscars">{{cite news|url=http://www.philstar.com/Article.aspx?articleId=849938&publicationSubCategoryId=200 |title='Bwakaw' to compete at the Oscars |access-date=17 September 2012 |date=17 September 2012 |work=Philippine Star}}</ref>

In 2014, Lana and frequent filmmaking partner, Perci Intalan, established The IdeaFirst Company, a company for creative content creation and consultancy. The company has produced many films of which has been awarded in numerous screenings in the Philippines and overseas.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.adobomagazine.com/philippine-news/multi-awarded-film-duo-opens-the-ideafirst-company/|title=Multi-awarded film duo opens The IdeaFirst Company|last=salleh|date=2015-06-16|website=adobo Magazine Online|language=en-US|access-date=2019-11-08}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://theideafirstcompany.com/|title=Welcome to The IdeaFirst Company -|website=theideafirstcompany.com|language=en-US|access-date=2019-11-08}}</ref>

In the 29th [[Tokyo International Film Festival]], his 2016 film ''[[Die Beautiful]]'' won the festival's Audience Choice Award, and also the Best Actor award for [[Paolo Ballesteros]].

2019 he won the Best Director Award for his feature film ''Kalel, 15'' at the [[Tallinn Black Nights Film Festival]]. The film had its world premiere in the Estonian capital where it was screened in the official selection.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://poff.ee/en/awards-of-the-23rd-tallinn-black-nights-film-festival/|title=Awards of the 23rd Tallinn Black Nights Film Festival|last=Aava|first=Hannes|date=2019-11-30|website=PÖFF - Pimedate Ööde filmifestival|language=en-US|access-date=2019-12-01}}</ref>

== Personal life ==
Lana is gay and married Perci Intalan, a former [[5 (TV channel)|TV5]] executive, in a same-sex wedding ceremony in New York City on October 14, 2013.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.pep.ph/news/40738/Former-TV5-executive-Perci-Intalan-marries-director-Jun-Lana-in-New-York|title=Former TV5 executive Perci Intalan marries director Jun Lana in New York| date=15 October 2013 |work=Philippine Entertainment Portal}}</ref>
