'''Christine De Luca''' (born 4 April 1947) is a Scottish poet and writer from [[Shetland]], who writes in both [[English language|English]] and [[Shetland dialect]].{{efn-lr|De Luca frequently refers to [[Shetland dialect]] as ''Shetlandic'', however, this term is considered controversial and is commonly disliked by native speakers.<ref>{{Cite journal|last=Velupillai|first=Viveka|date=2019-09-24|title=Gendered inanimates in Shetland dialect - comparing pre-oil and contemporary speech|url=https://www.jbe-platform.com/content/journals/10.1075/eww.00032.vel?crawler=true|journal=English World-Wide|publisher=John Benjamins Publishing Company|volume=40|issue=3|pages=270|doi=10.1075/eww.00032.vel|issn=0172-8865|eissn=1569-9730|url-access=subscription|access-date=2020-02-29|via=John Benjamins e-Platform|doi-access=free}}</ref>}} Her poetry has been translated into many languages. She was appointed [[Edinburgh]]'s [[Makar]], or poet laureate from 2014 to 2017. De Luca is a global advocate for the [[Shetland dialect]] and literature of the [[Northern Isles]] of Scotland.

==Early life and education==
De Luca was born Christine Pearson in [[Walls, Shetland|Walls]], [[Shetland]]. Her father, Sandy Pearson, was the headmaster of Happyhansel Primary School in [[Shetland]]. De Luca moved to [[Edinburgh]] in her late teens to study at [[Edinburgh University]].<ref name="Scottish Review of Books">{{cite web |title=My Life in Poetry with Christine De Luca |url=https://www.scottishreviewofbooks.org/free-content/my-life-in-poetry-with-christine-de-luca/ |website=Scottish Review of Books |accessdate=21 September 2018}}</ref> After graduation, she taught high school for several years and later obtained a Masters in Educational Research in 1980.<ref name="Poem Hunter">{{cite web |title=Christine De Luca |url=https://www.poemhunter.com/christine-de-luca/ |website=Poem Hunter |accessdate=21 September 2018}}</ref><ref name="City of Edinburgh Council">{{cite web |title=Christine De Luca |url=https://www.edinburghmakar.org.uk/edinburghs-makars/christine-de-luca/ |website=City of Edinburgh Council |accessdate=22 September 2018 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180922135922/https://www.edinburghmakar.org.uk/edinburghs-makars/christine-de-luca/ |archive-date=22 September 2018 |url-status=dead }}</ref><ref>{{Cite journal|last=De Luca|first=Christine|date=1980|title=Towards a sound 'foundation': a consideration for the possibility of planning a geography curriculm for slow learners in secondary three and four|url=https://era.ed.ac.uk/handle/1842/32786|language=en}}</ref>

==Writing career==
De Luca's first three poetry collections were published by the [[Shetland Library]]. Her first collection, ''Voes and Sounds'' was published in 1994 and her second work, ''Wast Wi Da Valkyries'', was published in 1997. Both collections won the Shetland Literary Prize.<ref name="Scottish Poetry Library">{{cite web |title=Christine de Luca (b. 1947) |url=http://www.scottishpoetrylibrary.org.uk/poetry/poets/christine-de-luca |website=Scottish Poetry Library |accessdate=20 September 2018}}</ref> A third collection, 'Plain Song', was published in Shetland and Edinburgh in 2002.<ref name="Poem Hunter" />

In 2004, De Luca's [[Pamphlet (poetry)|pamphlet]], ''Drops in Time's Ocean'', was published by Hansel Co-operative Press. It is based on eight generations of De Luca's family's history.<ref name="Poem Hunter" /> Her poetry collection, ''Parallel Worlds'' (Luath Press) was published in 2005. A bi-lingual volume of Du Luca's poetry was published in 2007 by éditions fédérop; ''Parallel Worlds (''Mondes Parallèles''), Poems Translated from English and Shetlandic'', include De Luca's first four volumes of poetry and recent work, and was translated by Jean-Paul Blot and De Luca. In 2007, the collection won the poetry prize at the 9th Salon International du Livre Insulaire in [[Ouessant]].<ref name="Scottish Poetry Library" /><ref name="Poem Hunter" />

De Luca's sixth poetry collection, ''North End of Eden'' was published by Luath Press in 2010. In 2011, De Luca's novel, ''And Then Forever'', was published by the [[Shetland Times]]. Her [[Pamphlet (poetry)|pamphlet]], ''Dat Trickster Sun'', (Mariscat Press 2014), was short-listed for the [[Michael Marks Awards for Poetry Pamphlets]] award in 2014.<ref name="Poetry Archive" /><ref name="Scottish Poetry Library" /> This was translated into Italian by Francesca Romana Paci in 2015 (''Questo sole furfante'') and published by Trauben, Torino.

De Luca was appointed Edinburgh's Makar (poet laureate) in 2014. She served as poet laureate from 2014 to 2017.<ref name="Scottish Poetry Library" /> Her poems have been chosen four times (2006, 2010, 2013 and 2015), by the [[Scottish Poetry Library]] for its annual 20 ''Best Scottish Poems'' list.<ref name="Poetry Archive">{{cite web |title=Christine De Luca|url=https://www.poetryarchive.org/poet/christine-de-luca |website=Poetry Archive |accessdate=20 September 2018}}</ref>
De Luca's work has been translated into several languages, including French, Italian, Swedish, Norwegian, Danish, Icelandic, Finnish, Estonian, Latvian, Polish, and Welsh.<ref name="Poem Hunter" />

==[[Northern Isles]] advocacy==
[[File:WIKITONGUES-_Christine_speaking_Shetlandic.webm|thumb|right|De Luca speaking about the [[Shetland dialect]].]]
De Luca is an advocate for the Shetland dialect, travelling internationally to share her native dialect with similar linguistic cultures, like [[Scandinavia]] and [[Iceland]]. De Luca is a co-founder of Hansel Co-operative Press, a non-profit cooperative, which promotes literary and artistic work in Shetland and [[Orkney]].<ref name="Scottish Review of Books" />

De Luca has focused on promoting her native language through work with [[Shetland]] children. She has written children's stories in the [[Shetland dialect]].<ref name="Scottish Poetry Library" /> De Luca translated [[Roald Dahl]]’s ''George’s Marvellous Medicine'' into Shetland dialect as ''Dodie’s Phenomenal Pheesic'' (Black and White Publishing, 2008). Also published in 2016 by Black and White Publishing are two [[Julia Donaldson]] books, translated by De Luca: ''Da Trow'' (''The Troll'') and ''The Shetland Gruffalo's Bairn'', (''The Gruffalo's Child'').<ref name="Shetland Dialect website">{{cite web |title=Christine De Luca |url=https://www.shetlanddialect.org.uk/christine-de-luca |website=Shetland Dialect.org |accessdate=21 September 2018}}</ref>
