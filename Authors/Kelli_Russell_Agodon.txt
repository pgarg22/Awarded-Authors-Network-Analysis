'''Kelli Russell Agodon''' (born in [[Seattle]]) is an American [[poet]], writer, and editor. She is the cofounder of ''Two Sylvias Press''<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://twosylviaspress.com/about.html|title=About|website=Two Sylvias Press}}</ref> and she serves on the poetry faculty at the Rainier Writing Workshop, a low-residency MFA program at [[Pacific Lutheran University]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.plu.edu/mfa/staff/|title=Faculty &#124; MFA - Creative Writing &#124; PLU|website=www.plu.edu}}</ref>

==Life==
She was raised in Seattle, and graduated from the [[University of Washington]] and [[Pacific Lutheran University]] Rainier Writing Workshop with an MFA in creative writing.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://english11poets.pbworks.com/Kelli-Russell-Agodon |title=English 11 Poets / Kelli Russell Agodon |publisher=English11poets.pbworks.com |date= |accessdate=2012-10-21}}</ref> She lives in [[Washington (state)|Washington state]].<ref>[http://www.pw.org/content/kelli_russell_agodon ] {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20081201140907/http://www.pw.org/content/kelli_russell_agodon |date=December 1, 2008 }}</ref>  Her works have appeared in the ''Atlantic Monthly,'' ''American Poetry Review'', ''Prairie Schooner'',<ref>{{cite web|url=http://muse.jhu.edu/login?uri=/journals/prairie_schooner/v080/80.4agodon.html|title=Project Muse|publisher=Muse.jhu.edu|accessdate=2012-10-21}}</ref> ''North American Review'', ''Image'',<ref>{{cite web|url=http://imagejournal.org/page/journal/back-issues/issue-57 |title=Image ◊ Journal ◊ Back Issues ◊ Issue 57 |publisher=Imagejournal.org |date= |accessdate=2012-10-21}}</ref> ''5 a.m'', ''Meridian'', ''Calyx''.,<ref>[http://www.proaxis.com/~calyx/journals/w9900.html ] {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090308115122/http://www.proaxis.com/~calyx/journals/w9900.html |date=March 8, 2009 }}</ref> [[poets.org]],<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://poets.org/poet/kelli-russell-agodon|title=About Kelli Russell Agodon &#124; Academy of American Poets|first=Academy of American|last=Poets|website=poets.org}}</ref> and ''[[The Los Angeles Review]]''.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://losangelesreview.org/queen-kelli-russell-agodon/|title=Queen Me by Kelli Russell Agodon|date=August 26, 2019}}</ref>

She lives in the Northwest.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.escapeintolife.com/poetry/kelli-russell-agodon/ |title=Kelli Russell Agodon |publisher=Escape Into Life |date=2012-10-03 |accessdate=2012-10-21}}</ref>
She was the Editor-in-Chief of the ''[[Crab Creek Review]]'' from 2009 until 2014. She is the co-founder of Two Sylvias Press.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.pw.org/content/kelli_russell_agodon_0 |title=Kelli Russell Agodon &#124; Directory of Writers &#124; Poets & Writers |publisher=Pw.org |date=2010-03-31 |accessdate=2012-10-21}}</ref> She is the Co-Director of the Poets on the Coast: A Weekend Writing Retreat for Women.

Her most recent book, ''Hourglass Museum'' (White Pine Press, 2014), was a Finalist for the Washington State Book Awards and shortlisted for the Julie Suk Prize in Poetry.
