'''Charles Wright''' (born August 25, 1935) is an American poet. He shared the [[National Book Award]] in 1983 for ''Country Music: Selected Early Poems''<ref name=nba1983/> and won the [[Pulitzer Prize]] in 1998 for ''Black Zodiac''.<ref name=pulitzer/> From 2014 to 2015, he served as the 50th [[Poet Laureate of the United States]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.infoplease.com/ipea/A0002280.html|title=Poets Laureate of the United States}}</ref>

==Early life and education==

Wright was born in [[Pickwick Dam]], [[Tennessee]]. Wright attended [[Christ School (North Carolina)]] in Asheville for his junior and senior years where he helped coach football, served as vice president of his class, and became a member of the honors program.{{Citation needed|date=January 2018}} While at Christ School, he enveloped himself in the literature that would inspire him to write. By the time he graduated in 1953 he had read everything [[William Faulkner]] had written. He then matriculated at [[Davidson College]] and graduated with a BA in history in 1957.<ref name=":1">{{Cite web|url=https://www.britannica.com/biography/Charles-Wright|title=Charles Wright {{!}} Biography, Poetry, & Facts|website=Encyclopedia Britannica|language=en|access-date=2019-11-06}}</ref> He received a master's degree from the University of Iowa in 1963,<ref name=":1" /> and attended the [[University of Iowa Writers' Workshop]]. He was a [[U.S.-Italy Fulbright Commission|Fulbright Scholar]] at the [[Sapienza University of Rome]]<ref name=":1" /> and at the [[University of Padua]].

== Teaching career ==
From 1966 to 1983, he taught at the [[University of California, Irvine]].<ref name=":1" /> Fellow Colleagues poets [[Robert Peters (playwright)|Robert Peters]] and [[James L. McMichael]] and novelist [[Oakley Hall]] shared during this time directorship of the university's well-known Master of Fine Arts program.<ref>{{cite web |first=Tomas Q. |last=Morin |date=December 2003 |url=https://www.awpwriter.org/magazine_media/writers_chronicle_view/2547/an_interview_with_ai/ref |title=An Interview with Ai |publisher=Association of Writers & Writing Programs}}</ref> He went to the University of Virginia in 1983, where he stayed until he retired in 2010.<ref name=":1" />

He was a chancellor of the [[Academy of American Poets]] and Souder Family Professor of English at the [[University of Virginia]] in [[Charlottesville, Virginia|Charlottesville]].

== Poet  ==
Wright began writing poetry while stationed in Italy during his army service, from 1957 to 1961, in the United States Army Intelligence Corps in Verona.<ref name=":0" /><ref name=":1" />  On June 12, 2014, the Library of Congress announced that Wright would serve as [[Poet Laureate]] of the United States beginning on September 25, 2014.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://time.com/2864086/who-is-charles-wright-the-new-poet-laureate/|title=New Poet Laureate Charles Wright: Who Is He?|author=Lily Rothman|work=[[Time (magazine)|Time]] |date=June 12, 2014}}</ref> He retired from the position in May 2015.<ref>{{cite news | url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/news/arts-and-entertainment/wp/2015/05/01/a-pair-of-u-s-poets-laureate-for-the-price-of-one/ | title=A pair of U.S. poets laureate for the price of one | first=Ron | last=Charles | date=May 1, 2015 | work=[[The Washington Post]] | access-date=March 3, 2016}}</ref>

==Works==
Beside the award-winning books ''Country Music'' (1982) and ''Black Zodiac'' (1997), Wright has published ''Chickamauga'', ''Buffalo Yoga'', ''Negative Blue'', ''Appalachia'', ''The World of the Ten Thousand Things: Poems 1980-1990'', ''Zone Journals'' and ''Hard Freight''. His work also appears in [[Blackbird (journal)|''Blackbird'']], an online journal of literature and the arts.

Wright has published two works of criticism, ''Halflife'' and ''Quarter Notes''.

== Recognition ==
His translation of [[Eugenio Montale]]'s ''The Storm and Other Poems'' won him the [[PEN Translation Prize]] in 1979. In 1993, he received the [[Ruth Lilly Poetry Prize]] for his lifetime achievement. In 1996 he won the [[Academy of American Poets|Lenore Marshall Poetry Prize]] from the Academy of American Poets for the collection ''Chickamauga'' (1995).<ref name=":1" /> ''Black Zodiac'' (1997) won him the [[National Book Critics Circle Award]] and the 1998 [[Pulitzer Prize for Poetry|Pulitzer Prize]].<ref name=":1" />
