'''Dezső Szomory''' (born Moshe Weisz;{{sfn|Fenyvesi|2003|p=23}} 2 June 1869 – 30 November 1944) was a Hungarian Jewish writer and dramatist. In his [[history play]]s and other works, he developed a unique tone and style of Budapest Hungarian;{{sfn|Sarlos|1969|p=441}} his work has been compared to that of [[Marcel Proust]].{{sfn|Fenyvesi|2003|p=23}} He died during the [[Holocaust in Hungary|Holocaust]] while living under [[Raoul Wallenberg#Mission to Budapest|Swedish protection]] in Budapest, suffering "starvation, loneliness, and depression".{{sfn|Turán|2013|p=49}}
