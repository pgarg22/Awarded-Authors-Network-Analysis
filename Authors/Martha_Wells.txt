'''Martha Wells''' (born September 1, 1964)<ref name="Locus interview">{{cite web|url=http://locusmag.com/2018/08/martha-wells-unburied-future/|title=Martha Wells: Unburied Future|date=August 13, 2018|website=Locus Online}}</ref> is an American writer of [[speculative fiction]]. She has published a number of fantasy novels, young adult novels, media tie-ins, short stories, and nonfiction essays on fantasy and science fiction subjects. Her novels have been translated into twelve languages.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.marthawells.com/bib.htm|title=Martha Wells – Bibliography|website=official site|access-date=2018-12-02}}</ref> Wells has won two [[Nebula Award|Nebula Awards]], three [[Locus Award|''Locus'' Awards]], and two [[Hugo Awards]] for herand her science fiction series ''[[The Murderbot Diaries]]''. She is also known for her fantasy series ''[[Ile-Rien]]'' and ''The Books of the Raksura.'' Wells is praised for the complex, realistically detailed societies she creates; this is often credited to her academic background in [[anthropology]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.farsector.com/quadrant/interview-wells.htm|title=Shaun Farrell interviews Martha Wells for Far Sector SFFH March 2006|website=www.farsector.com}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.actusf.com/spip/article-3899.html|title=ActuSF Interview with Martha Wells}}</ref> 

==Life==
Martha Wells was born in [[Fort Worth, Texas]], and has a B.A. in [[Anthropology]] from [[Texas A&M University]].<ref name="Locus interview" /> She lives in [[College Station, Texas]], with her husband. She was involved in [[SF/F]] fandom in college and was chairman of [[AggieCon]] 17.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://cepheids.org/main.php?g2_itemId=1872|title=AggieCon XVII Program exerpt.|website=cepheids.org|language=en-US|access-date=2018-12-02}}</ref>

==Career==
As an aspiring writer Wells attended many local writing workshops and conventions, including the [[Turkey City Writer's Workshop]] taught by Bruce Sterling.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://refiction.com/author-interviews/interview-with-martha-wells|title=Interview with Martha Wells|last=Re:Fiction|date=September 19, 2017}}</ref> She has also taught writing workshops at [[ArmadilloCon]], [[WorldCon]], [[ApolloCon]], and Writespace Houston,<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.writespacehouston.org/writers-family-reunion.html|title=Writers' Family Reunion|website=Writespace Writing Center|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180612143303/https://www.writespacehouston.org/writers-family-reunion.html|archive-date=2018-06-12}}</ref> and was the Special Workshop Guest at [[FenCon]] in 2018.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.fencon.org/guests.aspx|title=FenCon XV – September 21–23, 2018 |website=www.fencon.org}}</ref>

Her first published novel, ''The Element of Fire'' (1993), was a finalist for that year's [[Compton Crook Award]], and a runner-up for the 1994 [[William Crawford Award]]. Her second novel, ''City of Bones'' (1995), received a starred review from ''[[Publishers Weekly]]'' and a black diamond review from ''[[Kirkus Reviews]]'', and was on the 1995 ''[[Locus (magazine)|Locus]]'' Recommended Reading List for fantasy. Her third novel, ''[[The Death of the Necromancer]]'' (1998), was nominated for a [[Nebula Award]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.locusmag.com/SFAwards/Db/NomLit147.html#5528 |work=[[Locus (magazine)|Locus]] |title=The ''Locus'' Index to SF Awards Index |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20081203131501/http://www.locusmag.com/SFAwards/Db/NomLit147.html |archive-date=December 3, 2008 |access-date=June 25, 2018|url-status=dead}}</ref> ''The Element of Fire'' and ''The Death of the Necromancer'' are stand-alone novels which take place in the country of [[Ile-Rien]], which is also the setting for the ''Fall of Ile-Rien'' trilogy: ''The Wizard Hunters'' (2003), ''The Ships of Air'' (2004), and ''The Gate of Gods'' (2005). Her fourth novel was a stand-alone fantasy, ''Wheel of the Infinite''. In 2006, she released a revised edition of ''The Element of Fire''.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.marthawells.com/element.htm|title=The Element of Fire by Martha Wells|website=official site|access-date=2018-12-02}}</ref>

She has written media [[tie-in]]s, including ''Reliquary'' and ''Entanglement'' set in the ''[[Stargate Atlantis]]'' universe, "Archaeology 101", a short story based on ''[[Stargate SG-1]]'' for issue No. 8 (Jan/Feb 2006) of the official ''Stargate Magazine'', and a ''[[Star Wars]]'' novel, ''Empire and Rebellion: Razor's Edge''.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.marthawells.com/stargate.htm |title=Media Tie-ins|website=MarthaWells.com}}</ref>

Her fantasy short stories include "The Potter's Daughter" in the anthology ''Elemental'' (2006), which was selected to appear in ''The Year's Best Fantasy #7'' (2007).<ref>{{Cite book|title=Year's best fantasy 7|publisher=Tachyon Publications|year=2007|isbn=9781892391506|editor-last=Hartwell|editor-first=David G.|editor-link=David G. Hartwell|edition=1st|location=San Francisco|oclc=153153135|editor-last2=Cramer|editor-first2=Kathryn|editor-link2=Kathryn Cramer}}</ref> This story features one of the main characters from ''The Element of Fire''. Three prequel short stories to the ''Fall of Ile-Rien'' trilogy were published in ''[[Black Gate Magazine]]'' in 2007<ref>{{cite journal|url=http://www.blackgate.com/black-gate-magazine-issue-10-spring-2007|journal=Black Gate|issue=10|title=Table of Contents}}</ref><ref>{{cite journal|url=http://www.blackgate.com/black-gate-magazine-issue-11-summer-2007|journal=Black Gate|issue=11|title=Table of Contents}}</ref> and 2008.<ref>{{cite journal|url=http://www.blackgate.com/black-gate-12-summer-2008|journal=Black Gate|issue=12|title=Table of Contents}}</ref>

Wells' longest-running fantasy series is ''The Books of the Raksura'', which included five novels and two short fiction collections published by Night Shade Books: ''The Cloud Roads'' (2011), ''The Serpent Sea'' (2012), ''The Siren Depths'' (2012), ''Stories of the Raksura Vol 1: The Falling World & The Tale of Indigo and Cloud'' (2014), ''Stories of the Raksura Vol 2: The Dead City & The Dark Earth Below'' (2015), ''The Edge of Worlds'' (2016), and ''The Harbors of the Sun'' (2017). The series was nominated for the [[Hugo Award for Best Series]] in 2018,<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.worldcon76.org/entry/press-release-worldcon-76-announces-2018-hugo-award-finalists | title=Press Release: WorldCon 76 Announces 2018 Hugo Award Finalists}}</ref> and ''The Edge of Worlds'' was reviewed in ''[[The New York Times]]''.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.nytimes.com/2016/04/24/books/review/review-the-edge-of-worlds-by-martha-wells-and-more.html |title=Otherworldy: The Latest in Science Fiction and Fantasy|work=[[The New York Times]]|first=N. K.|last=Jemisin|author-link=N. K. Jemisin|date=April 19, 2016|access-date=June 25, 2018}}</ref>

Wells has written two young adult fantasy novels, ''Emilie and the Hollow World'' and ''Emilie and the Sky World'', published by Angry Robot/Strange Chemistry in 2013 and 2014.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.marthawells.com/yafantasy.htm |title=Young Adult Fantasy by Martha Wells|website=MarthaWells.com}}</ref>

Wells was toastmaster of the [[World Fantasy Convention]] in 2017,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://wfc2017.org/wfc2017/|title=World Fantasy 2017 – An annual gathering and reunion of professionals, collectors, and others interested in the field of light and dark fantasy art and literature.|website=wfc2017.org|access-date=February 22, 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180307050905/http://wfc2017.org/wfc2017/|archive-date=March 7, 2018|url-status=dead}}</ref> where she delivered a speech called "Unbury the Future"<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.tor.com/2017/11/07/unbury-the-future-martha-wells-full-speech-from-the-2017-world-fantasy-awards/|title='Unbury the Future': Martha Wells' Full Speech from the 2017 World Fantasy Awards|date=November 7, 2017}}</ref> about marginalized creators in the history of science fiction and fantasy, movies, and other media, and the deliberate suppression of the existence of those creators. The speech was well-received and generated a great deal of discussion.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://litreactor.com/columns/world-fantasy-2017-a-mixed-montage|title=World Fantasy Con 2017: A Mixed Montage}}</ref>

During 2018, Wells was the leader of the story team and lead writer for the new [[Dominaria]] expansion of the card game ''[[Magic: the Gathering]]''.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Whitbrook|first1=James|title=Scifi Author Martha Wells Is Bringing Magic: The Gathering Back to Where It All Began|url=https://io9.gizmodo.com/scifi-author-martha-wells-is-bringing-magic-the-gather-1822976803|access-date=14 February 2018|work=[[io9]]}}</ref>

In May 2018, her ''Murderbot Diaries'' novella ''[[All Systems Red]]'' was number 8 on ''[[The New York Times]]'' Bestseller List for Audio.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.nytimes.com/books/best-sellers/2018/05/01/audio-fiction/ |title=Audio Fiction Books Bestsellers|work=[[The New York Times]]|date=May 1, 2018|access-date=June 19, 2019}}</ref> The book won the 2017 [[Nebula Award for Best Novella]],<ref name=2017Nebula>{{cite web|url=https://nebulas.sfwa.org/nominated-work/4876/|title=''All Systems Red''|publisher=[[Nebula Award]]s|via=nebulas.sfwa.org|access-date=May 20, 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180520124440/https://nebulas.sfwa.org/nominated-work/4876/|archive-date=May 20, 2018|url-status=live}}</ref> the 2018 [[Hugo Award for Best Novella]],<ref name=2018Hugo>{{cite web|url=http://www.thehugoawards.org/hugo-history/2018-hugo-awards/|title=2018 Hugo Awards|publisher=[[Hugo Awards]]|access-date=August 20, 2018|via=thehugoawards.org|archive-url=https://www.webcitation.org/6yNVFQj1C?url=http://www.thehugoawards.org/hugo-history/2018-hugo-awards/|archive-date=April 2, 2018|url-status=live}}</ref> the 2018 [[Locus Award for Best Novella]],<ref name=2018Locus>{{cite web |url=https://locusmag.com/2018/06/2018-locus-awards-winners/ |title=2018 Locus Awards Winners|work=Locus}}</ref> and the [[American Library Association]]'s [[Alex Award]],<ref>{{cite web|url=http://ala.unikron.com/2018/|title=American Library Association announces 2018 youth media award winners|publisher=[[American Library Association]]|date=February 12, 2018|access-date=May 9, 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180213002511/http://ala.unikron.com/2018/|archive-date=February 13, 2018|url-status=dead|df=mdy-all}}</ref> and was nominated for the 2017 [[Philip K. Dick Award]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.philipkdickaward.org/2018/01/philip-k-dick-award-nominees-announced.html|title=Philip K. Dick Award Nominees Announced|publisher=[[Philip K. Dick Award]]|date=January 11, 2018|access-date=May 9, 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180320091921/http://www.philipkdickaward.org/2018/01/philip-k-dick-award-nominees-announced.html|archive-date=March 20, 2018|url-status=dead}}</ref> It was followed by the sequel novellas ''[[Artificial Condition]]'' (2018), ''[[Rogue Protocol]]'' (2018), and ''[[Exit Strategy (Wells novel)|Exit Strategy]]'' (2018);<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.nytimes.com/2018/11/30/books/review/martha-wells-exit-strategy.html/ |title=Ghosts, Robots, and Monsters: A Round-up of New Sci-Fi and Fantasy|work=[[The New York Times]]|date=November 30, 2018|access-date=May 6, 2020}}</ref> a short story, "Compulsory" (2018);<ref name="Compulsory">{{cite web|url=https://www.wired.com/story/future-of-work-compulsory-martha-wells/|title=The Future of Work: 'Compulsory' by Martha Wells|website=[[Wired (magazine)|Wired]]|date=December 17, 2018|access-date=February 4, 2019}}</ref> and a full novel sequel, ''[[Network Effect]]'' (2020), which made ''[[The New York Times]]'' Bestseller List for Novel.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.tor.com/2019/03/11/announcing-the-title-of-martha-wells-murderbot-diaries-novel/|title=Murderbot Will Return in...''Network Effect''|website=Tor.com|first=Lee|last=Harris|date=March 11, 2019|access-date=March 12, 2019}}</ref> On April 26, 2021, [[Tor.com]] publishing announced that they had signed a deal with Wells for six books, including three more Murderbot Diaries.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.tor.com/2021/04/26/tordotcom-publishing-acquires-six-martha-wells-books-including-three-murderbot-diaries/|website=Tor.com|first=Molly|last=Templeton|date=April 26, 2021|access-date=April 26, 2021|title='Tordotcom Publishing Acquires Six Martha Wells Books—Including Three Murderbot Diaries'}}</ref>
