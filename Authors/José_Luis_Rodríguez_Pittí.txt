'''José Luis Rodríguez Pittí''' is a contemporary writer, videoartist and documentary photographer.<ref>«Directorio de escritores vivos de Panamá». Panamá: Universidad Tecnológica de Panamá.</ref> 

==Biography==
Rodríguez Pittí was born in [[Panama City]] in 29 March 1971. He grew up in [[Mexico City]], Santiago de Veraguas and Panamá City. He is resident of [[Toronto]], Canada.<ref>Bethancourt, Minerva (2009). «Literary production in search of new horizons». The Panama Post.</ref><ref> Lombana, Cristina (2016). «Café 10». Panamá: Radio 10.</ref>

He is the author of short stories, poems and essays.<ref>Carbonell, José Antonio (2004). Ciudad y literatura. Bogotá: Convenio Andrés Bello.</ref><ref>Morales, Fulvia (2007). Cuento que te quiero cuento. Panamá: 9Signos.</ref><ref>Jaramillo Levi, Enrique (2003). La minificción en Panamá. Bogotá: Universidad Pedagógica Nacional.</ref> Rodríguez Pittí is author of the books "Panamá Blues" (Panamá, 2010, {{ISBN|978-9962-683-02-5}}), "miniTEXTOS" ([[Flash fiction]] anthology, Panamá, 2008), "Sueños urbanos" (Panamá, 2008, {{ISBN|9962-00-473-X}}) and "Crónica de invisibles" (Panamá, 1999, {{ISBN|9962-802-06-7}}). Most of his stories and essays were published in literary magazines and newspapers. 

In 1994, the [[Universidad de Panamá]] awarded him with the Premio "Darío Herrera". Other literary honors received are Accesit in the Premio Nacional "Signos" 1993 (Panamá), Concurso Nacional de Cuentos "José María Sánchez" 1998 (Panamá), Concurso "Amadís de Gaula" 1999 (Soria, España) and the Concurso "Maga" de Cuento Corto 2001 (Panamá) <ref>Jaramillo Levi, Enrique (2001). «Premio Maga de cuento corto». Revista Maga.</ref>

President of the Writers Association of Panama from 2008 to 2010.<ref>Monroy, Mireya (2007). «Gremio Literario Panameño». Diario La Prensa.</ref> Founder and President of [[Fundación El Hacedor]] (since 2007).<ref>Domínguez, Daniel (2008). «El artista y la sociedad». Diario La Prensa.</ref>

From 1990 to 1995 he traveled extensively in the Panamanian region of [[Azuero Peninsula|Azuero]] to collect stories and photograph, the body of three photo essays: "Viernes Santo en Pesé", "Cuadernos de Azuero", and "Noche de carnaval". Other photography essays are "De diablos, diablicos y otros seres de la mitología panameña" and "Regee Child". In 1994 he won the Premio Canon "Día de la Tierra". Some of his photographs are cover art of books published in Panamá. His work has been exhibited in Panamá, México, Canadá and Italy.<ref>Domínguez, Daniel (2012). «Resultados de una muestra». Diario La Prensa.</ref>

Graduated from the [[Universidad Tecnologica de Panama|Universidad Tecnológica de Panamá]], was [[Computer Vision]], Programming Languages and [[Deep Learning]] Professor <ref>Rosenshain, Rella (2008). «Convergencia por las letras». Diario La Prensa.</ref> at the Universidad Tecnológica de Panamá and Universidad Santa María la Antigua.
