'''Ann Lauterbach''' (born 1942)<ref name="Library of Congress Online Catalog">[http://catalog.loc.gov/cgi-bin/Pwebrecon.cgi?DB=local&Search_Arg=ann+lauterbach&Search_Code=GKEY^*&CNT=100&hist=1&type=quick Library of Congress Online Catalog]</ref> is an [[United States|American]] poet, essayist, art critic, and professor.

==Early life==
Lauterbach was born and raised in New York City, and earned her B.A. from the [[University of Wisconsin]]. She lived in London for eight years, working in publishing and for art institutions, including London's Thames and Hudson art publishing house. On her return to the U.S., she worked in art galleries in New York before she began teaching.<ref>[http://www.poets.org/poet.php/prmPID/598 Academy of American Poets > Ann Lauterbach Biography]</ref>

==Poetry==
Her most recent poetry collection is ''Spell'' ([[Penguin Books]], 2018). Her poems have been published in literary journals and magazines including ''Conjunctions,'' and in anthologies including ''American Hybrid: A Norton Anthology of New Poetry'' ([[W.W. Norton]], 2009) and ''American Women Poets in the 21st Century: Where Lyric Meets Language'' ([[Wesleyan University Press]], 2002).<ref name="Library of Congress Online Catalog"/>

==Teaching==
She has taught at [[Brooklyn College]], [[Columbia University]], the [[Iowa Writers Workshop]], [[Princeton University]], and at the City College of New York and Graduate Center of CUNY. Since 1991 she has taught at [[Bard College]], and is currently a David and Ruth Schwab Professor of Languages and Literature there, where she teaches and co-directs the Writing Division of the M.F.A. program, and lives in Germantown, New York.<ref name="nationalbook.org"/><ref name="background">{{cite web

Her honors include fellowships from the [[John Simon Guggenheim Memorial Foundation|Guggenheim Foundation]], the [[Ingram Merrill Foundation]], the [[MacArthur Foundation|John D. and Catherine T. MacArthur Foundation]], and the New York State Foundation for the Arts.<ref name="nationalbook.org">{{Cite web |url=http://www.nationalbook.org/nba2009_p_lauterbach.html |title=The National Book Foundation > 2009 National Book Award Finalist > Ann Lauterbach |access-date=2010-02-25 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100104083308/http://www.nationalbook.org/nba2009_p_lauterbach.html |archive-date=2010-01-04 |url-status=dead }}</ref><ref>[http://www.nyfa.org/nyfa_artists_detail.asp?pid=1751 New York Foundation for the Arts > Artists Fellowship 1998 Poetry] {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100617140402/http://www.nyfa.org/nyfa_artists_detail.asp?pid=1751 |date=June 17, 2010 }}</ref>
