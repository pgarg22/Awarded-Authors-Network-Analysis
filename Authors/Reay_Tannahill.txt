'''Reay Tannahill'''{{Pronunciation-needed}} (9 December 1929 â€“ 2 November 2007) was a British [[historian]], [[non-fiction]] [[writer]], and [[novelist]], best known perhaps for two [[non-fiction]] bestsellers: ''Food in History'' and ''Sex in History''. She also wrote under the pseudonym '''Annabel Laine'''.<ref name="crimefictioniv">{{Citation|title=Reay Tannahill's Pseudonym|url=http://www.crimefictioniv.com/Part_22.html|date=2012-07-15}}</ref> Her novel ''Passing Glory'' won in 1990 the [[Romantic Novel of the Year Award]] by the [[Romantic Novelists' Association]].<ref name="RoNAAwards">{{Citation|title=Awards by the Romantic Novelists' Association|url=http://www.romanticnovelistsassociation.org/index.php/awards|date=2012-07-15}}</ref>

==Biography==

===Personal life===
Reay Tannahill was born on 9 December 1929<ref name="heraldscotland">{{Citation|title=Reay Tannahill at The Herald Scotland|url=http://www.heraldscotland.com/reay-tannahill-1.869415|date=2012-07-15}}</ref> in [[Glasgow]], Scotland,<ref name="independent">{{Citation|title=Reay Tannahill at the Independent|url=https://www.independent.co.uk/news/obituaries/reay-tannahill-accidental-pioneer-of-food-history-and-bestselling-romantic-novelist-760621.html|date=2012-07-15}}</ref> where she was brought up.<ref name="hoddersystems">{{Citation|title=Reay Tannahill's Biography|url=http://authorpages.hoddersystems.com/ReayTannahill/author.asp|date=2012-07-15}}</ref> Her forename was the maiden name of her mother, Olive Reay.<ref name="independent" /> She was educated at [[Shawlands Academy]], and obtained an MA in History and a postgraduate certificate in Social Sciences at the [[University of Glasgow]].<ref name="hoddersystems" />

In 1958, she married Michael Edwardes; the marriage ended in divorce in 1983 and he died in 1990.<ref name="heraldscotland" />

Until her death on 2 November 2007 she lived in a smart terraced house in [[London]] near [[Tate Britain]].<ref name="independent" />

===Career===
Before she started to write, she worked as a probation officer, advertising copywriter, newspaper reporter, historical researcher and graphic designer.<ref name="heraldscotland" /> She published her first non-fiction book in 1964. With the international success came with the book ''Food in History'', her publisher suggested a companion volume on the second great human imperative, [[Sex in History]]. For her 2002 revised edition of ''Food in History'', she won the [[Premio Letterario Internazionale Chianti Ruffino Antico Fattore]].<ref name="independent" />

She also wrote historical [[romance novel]]s, and in 1990, her novel ''Passing Glory'' won in 1990 the [[Romantic Novel of the Year Award]] by the [[Romantic Novelists' Association]].<ref name="RoNAAwards" />

She belonged to the [[Arts Club and the Authors' Club]], and was chairman of the latter from 1997 to 2000.<ref name="independent" />
