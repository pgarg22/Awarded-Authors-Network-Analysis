'''Christopher Stasheff''' (15 January 1944<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.worldswithoutend.com/author.asp?ID=4477|title=Christopher Stasheff|website=Worlds Without End|access-date=June 13, 2018}}</ref> – 10 June 2018<ref>[https://www.facebook.com/edward.stasheff/posts/10213449878575470 Announcement of his death] on his son's [[Facebook]] page</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://kryptonradio.com/2018/06/11/author-christopher-stasheff-succumbs-to-parkinsons-disease/|title=Author Christopher Stasheff Succumbs To Parkinson's Disease|date=June 11, 2018|access-date=June 13, 2018}}</ref>) was an American [[science fiction author]] and [[fantasy author]]<ref>{{cite journal|last=Weis|first=Margaret|date=November 1, 1997|title=A MAGIC-LOVER'S TREASURY OF THE FANTASTIC|journal=[[Kirkus Reviews]]|publisher=Aspect/Warner|quote=Twenty substantial stories featuring magic in all its many and startling guises...Wrapping up the all-star cast are  Orson Scott Card...and Christopher Stasheff}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|title=Williamson lectureship set|date=March 30, 2008|work=The Portales News-Tribune|pages=Section State and Regional News|quote=Also speaking at the lectureship is Christopher Stasheff, associate professor of communication at ENMU...Stasheff's work includes the "Warlock of Gramarye" series, "Rogue Wizard" and the novel "Saint Vidicon to the Rescue." He has also contributed to series such as "Wing Commander 'End Run'" and "Mage Knight Stolen Prophecy."}}</ref> whose novels include ''[[The Warlock in Spite of Himself]]''<ref>{{cite news|title=Real Men Don't Wait for the Movie|date=November 26, 2003|work=[[Aberdeen American News]]|page=4a|quote=But there are lots of books that have exactly the type of male heroes we ought to emulate..."The Warlock in Spite of Himself" (and its many sequels) by Christopher Stasheff...gives us insight into every stage of marriage from courtship, to the raising of children, to the empty nest.}}</ref> (1969) and ''Her Majesty's Wizard'' (1986). He received a bachelor's degree and an M.A. in radio-TV at the [[University of Michigan]] and a Ph.D. in Theater from the [[University of Nebraska–Lincoln|University of Nebraska]]. From 1972 to 1987 he taught at [[Montclair State University|Montclair State College]], then moved to [[Champaign, Illinois]], and became a full time writer. In 2000 he resumed teaching radio and television, at [[Eastern New Mexico University]] in [[Portales, New Mexico]]. He retired in 2009 and moved back to Champaign.  Chris died on June 10, 2018 from [[Parkinson's disease]].<ref name="NewsGaz-2018-06-26">{{cite news |title=A Life Remembered: Prolific sci-fi author used people of C-U as his muses
