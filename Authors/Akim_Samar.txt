'''Akim Dmitriyevich Samar''' ({{lang-ru|Аким Дмитриевич Самар}}, 1916–1943) was a [[Soviet Union|Soviet]] poet and novelist regarded as the first [[Nanai-language]] writer.

Born in the [[Russian Far East]] in 1916, Samar was a student at [[Institute of the Peoples of the North]] in [[Leningrad]] and joined the [[Red Army]] after [[Nazi Germany]] [[Operation Barbarossa|invaded the Soviet Union]]. He died at the [[Battle of Stalingrad]] in 1943. His works included the poetry collections ''Songs of the Nanai'' (1938) and ''Poems'' (1940) and the 1941 [[novel]] ''A Poor Man's Son''.
