'''Nicolás Fernández de Moratín''' (1737–1780) was the father of one of the most important [[Spain|Spanish]] writers and [[dramatists]] of the [[Neoclassicism|neoclassical]] era, [[Leandro Fernández de Moratín]]. He himself was involved in the Spanish literary movement of the day and heavily influenced his son. He wrote ''Arte de las putas'', a poem, and ''La petimetra'', a new comedy. He was also a member of the Tertulia de la Fonda de San Sebastián where he met famous authors like [[José Cadalso]] and [[Tomás de Iriarte]].
