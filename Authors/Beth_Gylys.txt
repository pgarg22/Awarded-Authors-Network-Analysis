'''Beth Ann Gylys'''<ref>{{cite news|title=Marriages: Licenses Issued July 5 to 9|newspaper=Erie Times-News|date=July 19, 2010}}</ref> (born 1964 [[Passaic, New Jersey]]) is a [[poet]] and professor of English and Creative Writing at [[Georgia State University]]. She has published five poetry collections, three of which have won awards.

==Early life and education==
Gylys grew up in [[Pittsburgh]], Pennsylvania, and graduated from [[Allegheny College]] with a bachelor's degree in 1986.<ref>[http://sites.allegheny.edu/alumni/alumni-profiles/beth-gylys-86/ Beth Gylys '86] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120319195410/http://sites.allegheny.edu/alumni/alumni-profiles/beth-gylys-86/ |date=2012-03-19 }} - Allegheny College profile</ref> She went on to receive a master's degree from [[Syracuse University]] and a Ph.D. in English and Creative Writing from [[University of Cincinnati]]. She has also attended the [[Stonecoast MFA Program in Creative Writing|Stonecoast Writers Conference]] in [[Portland, Maine]].<ref>{{cite journal|title=High Five|journal=JMWW|date=Summer 2006|url=http://jmww.150m.com/Gylys.html|accessdate=February 5, 2013|url-status=dead|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20120305135116/http://jmww.150m.com/Gylys.html|archivedate=March 5, 2012}}</ref>

==Career==
Gylys formerly taught at [[Mercyhurst College]] in [[Erie, Pennsylvania]].<ref name="merciad">{{cite web|url=http://merciad.mercyhurst.edu/content/singers-perform-poetry-matchbook|title=Singers perform poetry in 'Matchbook'|work=Merciad}}</ref> She is currently a professor of English and Creative Writing at Georgia State University.<ref name=gsu>{{cite web|url=http://www.english.gsu.edu/people.php?req=gylys|title=Department of English - Beth Gylys|publisher=Georgia State University|url-status=dead|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20091114194930/http://english.gsu.edu/people.php?req=gylys|archivedate=2009-11-14}}</ref> Her poems have appeared in ''[[The Paris Review]]'',<ref>[http://www.theparisreview.org/back-issues/136 Issue 136] - ''The Paris Review''</ref> ''[[The Southern Review]]'', ''[[The Kenyon Review]]'', ''[[The New Republic]]'', ''[[The Antioch Review]]'',<ref>[http://antiochcollege.org/antioch_review/back_issues/summer_2001/ Summer 2001 Issue] {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20101125002149/http://antiochcollege.org/antioch_review/back_issues/summer_2001/ |date=November 25, 2010 }} ''The Antioch Review''</ref> and ''[[Columbia University#Publications|The Columbia Review]]''.<ref name=gsu />

===Events===
Gylys' poem "Erratic Gardener" was featured on an episode of [[Garrison Keillor]]'s ''[[A Prairie Home Companion]]'' in 1999.<ref>{{cite news|last=Schneider|first=Michael|title='Bodies That Hum' by Beth Gylys|url=http://old.post-gazette.com/books/reviews/19990815review314.asp|accessdate=February 5, 2013|newspaper=Pittsburgh Post-Gazette|date=August 15, 1999}}</ref> Her book of personal ads, titled ''Matchbook'', has been set to music by composer [[Dan Welcher]].<ref name="merciad" /> She was a featured guest poet at the January 2013 meeting of the [[Georgia Poetry Society]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Beth Gylys - Morning Speaker|url=http://georgiapoetrysociety.org.s151994.gridserver.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/01/Winter-2012-GPS-Newsletter.pdf|work=Winter 2013 Newsletter|publisher=Georgia Poetry Society|accessdate=February 5, 2013}}</ref>

==Personal life==
She is married to [[Thomas Forsthoefel]] who is Professor of Religious Studies at Mercyhurst College and the [[Erie County, Pennsylvania|Erie County, PA]], [[poet laureate|Poet Laureate]].<ref name="merciad" />
