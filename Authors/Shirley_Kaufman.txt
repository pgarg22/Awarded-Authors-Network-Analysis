'''Shirley Kaufman Daleski''' (June 5, 1923 in [[Seattle]] - September 25, 2016 in [[San Francisco]]) was an American-Israeli [[poet]] and translator.<ref>{{cite encyclopedia| url=http://jwa.org/encyclopedia/article/kaufman-shirley| title=Shirley Kaufman| encyclopedia=Jewish Women: A Comprehensive Historical Encyclopedia| author= Lois Miller Bar-Yaacov| accessdate=30 June 2009}}</ref>

==Life==
Her parents immigrated from [[Poland]]. She grew up in Seattle and graduated from [[Garfield High School (Seattle, Washington)|James A. Garfield High School]] in 1940 and from the [[University of California, Los Angeles]] in 1944, and in 1946 she married Dr. Bernard Kaufman, Jr.  They had three daughters: Sharon (b. 1948), Joan (b. 1950) and Deborah (b. 1955). She studied at [[San Francisco State University]] with [[Jack Gilbert]].

She married Hillel Matthew Daleski and immigrated to [[Jerusalem, Israel]] in 1973.

Her daughter, poet and playwright Debra Kaufman, made a short film about her poem "Ezekiel's Wheels".<ref>{{cite journal| url=http://findarticles.com/p/articles/mi_qn4176/is_20070807/ai_n19444306/?tag=content;col1| title=Poetry, film blend, thanks to filmmaker's mother| journal=Oakland Tribune| date=Aug 7, 2007| author=Jennifer Modenessi| access-date=July 1, 2009| archive-date=December 9, 2019| archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20191209184115/http://findarticles.com/p/articles/mi_qn4176/is_20070807/ai_n19444306/?tag=content;col1| url-status=dead}}</ref>

Her work has appeared in ''[[Ploughshares]]'',<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.pshares.org/Authors/authorDetails.cfm?prmAuthorID=799|title = Read by Author &#124; Ploughshares}}</ref> ''[[Harper's Magazine|Harper's]]'',<ref>http://www.harpers.org/archive/1965/01/0014763</ref> ''[[The American Poetry Review]]'',<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.aprweb.org/issues/recent.shtml |title=Archived copy |accessdate=2009-07-01 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090415211440/http://www.aprweb.org/issues/recent.shtml |archive-date=2009-04-15 }}</ref> and ''[[The New Yorker]]''.<ref>https://www.newyorker.com/magazine/bios/shirley_kaufman/search?contributorName=shirley%20kaufman</ref>

She died from Alzheimer's disease at the age of 93.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.legacy.com/obituaries/sfgate/obituary.aspx?pid=181618755|title=Shirley Kaufman Daleski Obituary|date=October 2, 2016|access-date=December 21, 2016|via=SFGATE Obituaries}}</ref>
