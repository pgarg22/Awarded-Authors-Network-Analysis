'''Harry E. Northup''' (born September 2, 1940) is an American actor and poet. As an actor, he made frequent appearances in the films of [[Martin Scorsese]] and [[Jonathan Demme]].

==Personal life and career==
Northup was born in [[Amarillo, Texas]]. He lived in 17 places by age 17, but mostly in [[Sidney, Nebraska]], where he graduated from high school in 1958. From 1958 to 1961 he served in the United States Navy, where he attained the rank of Second Class Radioman.  From 1963 to 1968, he studied [[Method acting]] with [[Frank Corsaro]] in New York City.<ref name="thecinemastore.co.uk">[https://www.thecinemastore.co.uk/Magazines/Horror_Retro/_CINEMA_RETRO_UK_Magazine_Issue_14_Vol_5_2302.html Cinema Retro interview by Raymond Benson]{{Dead link|date=January 2020 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref>

Northup received his B.A. in English from [[California State University, Northridge]], where he studied poetry with Ann Stanford. He has made a living as an actor for over 30 years and has been in 37 films, including [[Martin Scorsese]]'s first six feature films: ''[[Who's That Knocking at My Door]]'', ''[[Boxcar Bertha]]'', ''[[Mean Streets]]'', ''[[Alice Doesn't Live Here Anymore]]'', ''[[Taxi Driver]]'' and ''[[New York, New York (1977 film)|New York, New York]]''. He was Mr. Bimmel in [[Jonathan Demme]]'s film ''[[The Silence of the Lambs (film)|The Silence of the Lambs]]'' and starred in ''[[Over the Edge (film)|Over the Edge]]'' and ''[[Fighting Mad (1976 film)|Fighting Mad]]''.<ref>in am/fm magazine, Mar 22, 2020:https://www.amfm-magazine.tv/harry-e-northup-from-acting-in-martin-scorsese-films-to-writing-poetry/</ref>

Northup has been a member of the [[Academy of Motion Picture Arts and Sciences]] since 1976.<ref name="thecinemastore.co.uk"/> His second wife, poet and novelist Holly Prado, died on June 14, 2019.<ref>[https://www.latimes.com/local/california/la-me-holly-prado-poet-dead-20190625-story.html] Los Angeles Obit</ref> He has a son, Dylan, by his first marriage.<ref>[http://oac.cdlib.org/findaid/ark:/13030/kt0f59q1gm Northup's Archives]</ref> Poets such as [[Walt Whitman]],<ref>LA Reader, article by James Cushing, Friday, September 3, 1982, vol 4, No. 45, p. 12</ref> [[Leland Hickman]],<ref>LA Reader, James Cushing, September 3, 1982, vol 4,#45, p. 12</ref> [[Paul Blackburn (U.S. poet)|Paul Blackburn]],<ref>Chiron Review, Issue 78, Spring, 2005 p.4</ref> Ann Stanford,<ref>LA Reader, article by James Cushing, Friday, September 3, 1982, vol 4, No. 45, p 13]</ref> [[William Carlos Williams]],<ref>Chiron Review, Issue 78, Spring, 2005, p. 4 & 5</ref> and Holly Prado<ref>Lummox Journal, July 2001, p. 11</ref> have influenced Northup's poetry. He conceived and coordinated the weekly poetry reading series "Poetry on Melrose" at Gasoline Alley in Los Angeles, from 1986 to 1988. Poets who read at the venue include [[Robert Peters (playwright)|Robert Peters]], [[Jack Hirschman]] and [[Lewis MacAdams]].<ref>[http://libraries.ucsd.edu/collections/sca/ Special Collections & Archives], UC San Diego Library</ref>

==Awards and honors==
The City of Los Angeles, as represented by the L.A. City Council, awarded Northup a Certificate of Recognition on November 15, 2006.<ref>[[Cinema Retro]] vol 5, Issue No. 14 2009 interview by Raymond Benson</ref>
