'''Karin Tanabe''' is a historical fiction novelist who is best known for her works ''The Gilded Years: A Novel'', a novel about the first [[African Americans|African-American]] graduate of [[Vassar College]], and ''The Diplomat's Daughter: A Novel'', a love story set in a [[Japanese American internment]] camp.<ref name="npr">{{cite web|title='The Diplomat's Daughter' Is A Story Of Love In An Internment Camp|url=https://www.npr.org/2017/07/09/536263113/the-diplomats-daughter-is-a-story-of-love-in-an-internment-camp|website=NPR |date=9 July 2017|accessdate=18 May 2020}}</ref> [[National Public Radio]] has described her as a "master of historical fiction".<ref name="npr2">{{cite web|title='Karin Tanabe's 'A Hundred Suns' Explores Indochina Of The 1930s|url=https://www.npr.org/2020/04/11/832360483/karin-tanabes-a-hundred-suns-explores-indochina-of-the-1930s|website=NPR |date=11 April 2020|accessdate=18 May 2020}}</ref>

==Biography==

Tanabe is a first-generation American who grew up in [[Washington, D.C.]] with foreign parents.<ref name="npr2"/> Her father is the former ''Book World'' editor at the [[Washington Post]].<ref>{{cite web|title='The Price of Inheritance,' by Karin Tanabe|url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/entertainment/books/the-price-of-inheritance-by-karin-tanabe/2014/07/30/fe9af9da-f0bb-11e3-bf76-447a5df6411f_story.html
|website=Washington Post |date=1 August 2020|accessdate=18 May 2020}}</ref>

Tanabe graduated from [[Vassar College]] and currently lives in [[Washington, D.C.]] with her husband and daughter. Until 2017, she was a reporter at [[Politico]].<ref name="aalbc">{{cite web|title=Karin Tanabe|url=https://aalbc.com/authors/author.php?author_name=Karin+Tanabe|website=African American Literature Book Club |accessdate=18 May 2020}}</ref><ref name="simonandschuster">{{cite web|title=Karin Tanabe|url=https://www.simonandschuster.com/authors/Karin-Tanabe/405400533|website=Simon & Schuster |accessdate=18 May 2020}}</ref>

==List of works==
* ''The List: A Novel'' (2013) - a novel about a young reporter inspired by Tanabe's experiences at [[Politico]]
* ''The Price of Inheritance: A Novel'' (2014) - a drama set in the high-end antique furniture world
* ''The Gilded Years: A Novel'' (2016) - a historical fiction novel about the first [[African Americans|African-American]] graduate of [[Vassar College]]
* ''The Diplomat's Daughter'' (2017) - a love story set in a [[Japanese American internment]] camp
* ''A Hundred Suns: A Novel'' (2020) - a thriller set in 1930s [[French Indochina]]
* ''A Woman of Intelligence'' (2021) - [[Cold War]] spy novel
