'''Vim Karénine''' (born August 6, 1933) is an American and French poet, a haiku poet and a novelist.

[[File:Vim karenine 1995.jpg|thumb]]

==Biography==

Vim Karénine (Antony de Vial), son of [[François de Vial]] who was a diplomat and a [[Minister Plenipotentiary]] of France,<ref>François de Vial, plenipotentiary minister French Diplomatic Directory 1945, 1952,1955,</ref> was born in [[Poitiers]], France, and grew up in Switzerland, Hungary, and Italy.<ref>''[[Le Monde]]'' newspaper, 1990, "Who is Antony de Vial?".</ref> His family moved afterwards to Canada. After his secondary education at [[Collège Stanislas (Quebec)]],<ref>Stanislas in Montreal P.Q.:fellowship directory</ref> in [[Montreal]], Karénine moved to Paris, France. He earned a bachelor's degree from the [[University of Poitiers]]<ref>Poitiers University fellows directory</ref>  Vim published his first poems in a student journal.

He enlisted in the [[French Navy]] for two years, and served aboard the cruiser ''[[French cruiser Jeanne d'Arc (1930)|Jeanne d' Arc]]'',  visiting the Far East, North and South America, Australia and New Zealand.  Antony de Vial graduated from the [[Carmes Seminary]] in Paris, to become a Catholic priest on December 17, 1966. He then moved to the United States, involving himself with [[American Indian Movement|A. I. M.]] groups.  He was a resident in [[Silver Spring, Maryland]], near Washington D.C. and [[Baltimore]], and acquired [[Citizenship in the United States|U. S. citizenship]].<ref>[[Le Monde]] newspaper 1990 "Who is Antony de Vial?".</ref>

Karénine usually writes his poems on a computer, but if inspiration strikes him when he's away from it, "I've been known to start poems on napkins and scraps of paper, too".  He is known for his spontaneous method of writing, covering topics such as Catholic spirituality<ref>McCoy, Jerry A. and Silver Spring Historical Society. Historic Silver Spring. Charleston, SC: Arcadia Publishing, 2005.</ref> and travel.  In August 2012 his comprehensive collection of [[Emily Dickinson]] translations was published by Editions Orizons in France, with a general introduction by poet and editor {{ill|Guy Chambelland|lt=Guy Chambelland|fr}}.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://editionsorizons.fr/index.php/menus-abimes-emily-dickinson.html|title=Menus abîmes}}</ref>  He eventually retired to Paris.<ref>[[Le Figaro]] 18 March 1986 chronicle "Those discreet and secret men" : Antony de Vial</ref>
