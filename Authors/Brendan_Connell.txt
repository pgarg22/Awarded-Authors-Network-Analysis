'''Brendan Connell''' (born 1970) is an American author and translator. Though his work often falls into the horror and fantasy genres, it has also often been called unclassifiable and avant-garde.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.fright.com/edge/LifeOPolycrates.htm|title=THE LIFE OF POLYCRATES by Brendan Connell--Fright.com review|website=www.fright.com}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.fright.com/edge/MissHomicide.htm|title=MISS HOMICIDE PLAYS THE FLUTE by BRENDAN CONNELL--Fright.com review|website=www.fright.com}}</ref> His style has been compared to that of [[J.K. Huysmans]]<ref>Metrophilias, introduction by Daniel Corrick</ref> and [[Angela Carter]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.infinityplus.co.uk/nonfiction/intjvm2.htm|title=Jeff VanderMeer interviewed - infinity plus non-fiction|website=www.infinityplus.co.uk}}</ref> Some of his shorter fiction, such as that contained in his collection ''Metrophilias'', has been referred to as prose poetry.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.publishersweekly.com/978-1-943813-07-0|title=Fiction Book Review: Metrophilias  by Brendan Connell. Snuggly,   $12.50 ISBN 978-1-943813-07-0|publisher=}}</ref>

Influences he has cited include [[Balzac]] and [[Ponson du Terrail]].<ref name="risingshadow.net">{{cite web|url=https://www.risingshadow.net/articles/334-an-interview-with-brendan-connell|title=An interview with Brendan Connell|website=www.risingshadow.net}}</ref> He has also written many lyrics for the Serbian band Kodagain.<ref name="risingshadow.net"/>
