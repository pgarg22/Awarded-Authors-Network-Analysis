'''Jennifer Niven''' is a New York Times and international best selling American author who is best known for the 2015 young adult book, ''[[All the Bright Places]]''.

==Life and career==

Niven grew up in [[Indiana]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Jennifer Niven: Life is too short to be closed off to possibility |url=https://www.theguardian.com/childrens-books-site/2015/jan/26/jennifer-niven-interview-all-the-bright-places |website=The Guardian |accessdate=11 December 2018 |date=26 January 2015}}</ref> As well as writing novels, Niven has also worked as a screenwriter, journalist and an associate producer at [[American Broadcasting Company|ABC Television]].<ref name=panmacnillan>{{cite web |title=Jennifer Niven |url=https://www.panmacmillan.com/authors/jennifer-niven/9aa5fd41-7c9f-4220-3c25-08d5dd18a060 |website=www.panmacmillan.com |accessdate=11 December 2018}}</ref>

Her first two books were non-fiction narratives called ''The Ice Master'' (published in 2000) and ''[[Ada Blackjack]]: A True Story of Survival in the Arctic'' (published in 2003).<ref>{{cite web |last1=Niven |first1=Jennifer |title=The ice master : the doomed 1913 voyage of the Karluk |url=https://trove.nla.gov.au/work/33301370?selectedversion=NBD22869475 |website=National Library of Australia |publisher=London : Pan |accessdate=11 December 2018 |language=English |date=2001}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |last1=Niven |first1=Jennifer |title=Ada Blackjack : a true story of survival in the Arctic |url=https://trove.nla.gov.au/work/31676963?selectedversion=NBD43934819 |website=National Library of Australia |publisher=New York : Hyperion |accessdate=11 December 2018 |language=English |date=2004}}</ref> In 2010, she published a memoir of her years in high school called ''The Aqua Net Diaries: Big Hair, Big Dreams, Small Town''.<ref>{{cite web |title=The Aqua Net Diaries: Big Hair, Big Dreams, Small Town |url=https://m.barnesandnoble.com/w/the-aqua-net-diaries-jennifer-niven/1111870667 |website=Barnes & Noble |accessdate=11 December 2018 |language=en}}</ref>

She began writing a series of historical novels in 2009. The first, ''Velva Jean Learns to Drive'', was based on a short film of the same name that she had made. It won an [[Emmy Award]] and the [[Colin Higgins Award for Screenwriting]].<ref name=panmacnillan /> The series also includes, ''Velva Jean Learns to Fly'', ''Becoming Clementine'' and ''American Blonde''.<ref>{{cite web |title=Velva Jean Series |url=https://www.penguinrandomhouse.com/series/DYV/velva-jean-series/ |website=PenguinRandomhouse.com |accessdate=11 December 2018}}</ref>

Niven's first young adult novel, ''[[All the Bright Places]]'' was released in 2015. The narrative follows two teenagers, Violet and Finch who are struggling with mental health issues.<ref>{{cite web |title=All the Bright Places by Jennifer Niven – review |url=https://www.theguardian.com/childrens-books-site/2016/jan/23/all-the-bright-places-jennifer-niven-review |website=The Guardian |accessdate=11 December 2018 |date=23 January 2016}}</ref> It won a 2015 [[Goodreads]] choice award for Best Young Adult Fiction<ref>{{cite web |title='Go Set a Watchman' among Goodreads' best books of 2015 |url=https://edition-m.cnn.com/2015/12/01/living/goodreads-choice-awards-2015-feat/index.html?r=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.google.co.uk%2F |website=CNN |accessdate=11 December 2018 |language=en}}</ref> and was longlisted for the 2015 [[Guardian Children's Fiction Prize]].<ref>{{cite web |last1=Drabble |first1=Emily |title=The Guardian children's fiction award 2015 longlist |url=https://www.theguardian.com/childrens-books-site/gallery/2015/jul/10/the-guardian-childrens-fiction-award-2015-longlist |website=The Guardian |accessdate=11 December 2018 |date=10 July 2015}}</ref> It has been adapted into a [[All the Bright Places (film)|film]] by Netflix and stars [[Elle Fanning]], [[Justice Smith]], Keegan-Michael Key, Alexandra Shipp, and Luke Wilson.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Shiner |first1=Fionn |title=All the Bright Places casts Keegan-Michael Key, Luke Wilson and Alexandra Shipp |url=https://www.mirror.co.uk/film/bright-places-casts-keegan-michael-13362406 |website=mirror |accessdate=11 December 2018 |date=4 October 2018}}</ref> Production on the film began in October 2018<ref>{{cite web |title=Netflix Film "All The Bright Places" Begins Production |url=https://news.newonnetflix.info/news/netflix-film-all-the-bright-places-begins-production/ |website=New On Netflix: NEWS |accessdate=11 December 2018 |date=4 October 2018}}</ref> and it was released on 28 February 2020.<ref>{{cite web |title=All the Bright Places review – teen charm can't lift maudlin Netflix drama |url=https://www.theguardian.com/film/2020/feb/28/all-the-bright-places-review-netflix |website=the Guardian |accessdate=6 September 2020 |language=en |date=28 February 2020}}</ref>

She released another bestselling young adult novel in 2016 called ''Holding Up the Universe'',<ref>{{cite web |last1=Wilson |first1=Teri |title='The Fault in Our Stars' Fans Are Going to Be Obsessed with This New Book |url=https://www.teenvogue.com/story/holding-up-the-universe-jennifer-niven-interview |website=Teen Vogue |access-date=4 December 2020 |language=en-us}}</ref> and her third young adult novel, ''Breathless'', was released in September, 2020.<ref>{{cite web |title=Breathless by Jennifer Niven: 9781524701963 {{!}} PenguinRandomHouse.com: Books |url=https://www.penguinrandomhouse.com/books/546884/breathless-by-jennifer-niven/ |website=PenguinRandomhouse.com |access-date=4 December 2020}}</ref>
