'''Christopher Middleton''' (10 June 1926 &ndash; 29 November 2015)<ref name="december">{{cite web|url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/news/obituaries/12058242/Christopher-Middleton-poet-obituary.html|title=Christopher Middleton, poet - obituary|work=telegraph.co.uk|publisher=[[The Daily Telegraph]]|date=18 December 2015|accessdate=19 December 2015}}</ref> was a British poet and translator, especially of [[German literature]].

==Life==
He was born John Christopher Middleton in [[Truro]], [[Cornwall]], in 1926. Following four years' service in the [[Royal Air Force]],<ref>{{cite web|title=Christopher Middleton|url=http://www.poetryarchive.org/poetryarchive/singlePoet.do?poetId=1003|work=The Poetry Archive|accessdate=17 February 2013}}</ref> he studied at [[Merton College, Oxford]], matriculating in 1948.<ref name="MCreg">{{cite book|editor1-last=Levens|editor1-first=R.G.C.|title=Merton College Register 1900-1964|date=1964|publisher=Basil Blackwell|location=Oxford|page=392}}</ref> He then held academic positions at the [[University of Zürich]] and [[King's College London]].<ref name=MCreg /> In 1966 he took up a position as Professor of Germanic Languages & Literature at the [[University of Texas, Austin]], retiring in 1998.<ref name=PF>{{cite web|title=Christopher Middleton|url=http://www.poetryfoundation.org/bio/christopher-middleton|publisher=Poetry Foundation|accessdate=15 February 2013}}</ref> Middleton has published translations of [[Robert Walser (writer)|Robert Walser]], [[Nietzsche]], [[Friedrich Hölderlin|Hölderlin]], [[Goethe]], [[Gert Hofmann]] and many others. He has received various awards, including the [[Geoffrey Faber Memorial Prize]] and the [[Schlegel-Tieck Prize]] for translation.<ref>{{cite web|title=The Schlegel-Tieck Prize for German Translation: Past Winners|url=http://www.societyofauthors.org/schlegel-tieck-past-winners|publisher=The Society of Authors|accessdate=15 February 2013|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130510180247/http://www.societyofauthors.org/schlegel-tieck-past-winners|archive-date=10 May 2013|url-status=dead}}</ref>

Middleton married Mary Freer in 1953; they had two daughters and a son.<ref name=MCreg /> They divorced in 1969.<ref name="december" /> Middleton died on 29 November 2015.<ref name="december" /><ref>[https://www.independent.co.uk/news/obituaries/christopher-middleton-poet-celebrated-for-his-urgent-and-vivid-verse-and-his-translations-especially-a6756446.html Christopher Middleton: Poet celebrated for his urgent and vivid verse and his translations, especially from German]</ref>
