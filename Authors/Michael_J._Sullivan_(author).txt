'''Michael J. Sullivan''' is a ''New York Times'', ''USA Today'', and ''Washington Post'' bestselling [[American literature|American writer]] of [[High fantasy|epic fantasy]] and [[science fiction]], best known for his debut series [[Riyria Revelations|''The Riyria Revelations'']], which has been translated into fourteen languages.<ref>{{cite web | last = | first = | title = Author Biography: Michael J. Sullivan | publisher = Michael J. Sullivan | date = | url = http://riyria.blogspot.com/p/about.html | doi = | accessdate = 2012-02-12 }}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Orbit acquiert la trilogie de Michael J. |url=http://www.fantasy.fr/articles/view/15996|publisher=Fantasy.fr|accessdate=10 November 2012}}</ref> In 2012 [[io9]] named him one of the "Most Successful Self-Published Sci-Fi and Fantasy Authors".<ref>{{cite web|last=DOBBS|first=MICHAEL ANN|title=The Most Successful Self-Published Sci-Fi and Fantasy Authors|url=http://io9.com/5911634/the-most-successful-self+published-sci+fi-and-fantasy-authors|publisher=io9|accessdate=10 November 2012}}</ref> His books have been translated into French, German, Spanish, Czech, Dutch, Polish, Hungarian, Japanese, Georgian, Bulgarian, Russian, Portuguese, Italian and Turkish.

==Career==
Sullivan started writing books at a young age, in order to entertain himself.<ref>{URL|http://www.wottaread.com/knowing-michael-j-sullivan-interview}</ref> He started The Riyria Revelations to teach his daughter, who suffered from [[dyslexia]], how to read.<ref>http://authorpreneurindia.blogspot.com/2015/11/interview-2-kirtida-gautam-interviews.html{{Dead link|date=April 2020 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref>

Sullivan has written three series. [[Riyria Revelations|''The Riyria Revelations'']] and [[Riyria Chronicles|''The Riyria Chronicles'']] were published by [[Orbit Books]] (fantasy imprint of [[Hachette Book Group]]) and ''The Legends of the First Empire'' released by [[Del Rey Books]] (owned by [[Random House]]). The ''Riyria Revelations'' is a six-book epic fantasy series while the ''Riyria Chronicles'' is an ongoing series that centers on the early adventures of the two main protagonists of the former series.<ref>{{cite web | last = Moore | first = Tammy | title = The Crown Conspiracy: Michael J. Sullivan | publisher = SF Site | date = | url = http://www.sfsite.com/10b/cc306.htm | doi = | accessdate = 2010-01-16 }}</ref> ''The Legends of the First Empire'' is a six-book epic fantasy series set in the same world as the ''Riyria books'', but in the distant past, making it a standalone series. His first science fiction novel, ''Hollow World'', was released by [[Tachyon Publications]] in April 2014.<ref>{{cite web | title = Hollow World | publisher = Tachyon Publications | date =  | url = http://www.tachyonpublications.com/book/Hollow_World.html | doi =  | accessdate = 2013-09-14 | archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20130928052444/http://www.tachyonpublications.com/book/Hollow_World.html | archive-date = 2013-09-28 | url-status = dead }}</ref>  His current project is a new series (The Rise and Fall). It's a trilogy set in his world of Elan and falls between Legends of the First Empire and the Riyria Chronciles from a timeline standpoint.  The first two books (Nolyn & Farilane) are written and he should begin the third and final book by the end of summer 2020.

==Reception==
Sullivan has two New York Times bestsellers (Age of War and Age of Legend), and three books on the USA Today bestseller list (Age of Swords, Age of War, Age of Legend). He has had seven works selected for the [[Goodreads Choice Award]], six for Best Fantasy: ''The Emerald Storm'' in 2010,<ref>{{cite web|title=2010 Goodreads Choice Awards - Fantasy|url=http://www.goodreads.com/award/choice/2010#41637-Fantasy|publisher=goodreads|accessdate=8 December 2012}}</ref> ''Percepliquis''<ref>{{cite web|title=2012 Goodreads Choice Awards - Fantasy|url=http://www.goodreads.com/choiceawards/best-fantasy-books-2012|publisher=[[Goodreads]]|accessdate=8 December 2012}}</ref> in 2012, and ''The Crown Tower''<ref>{{cite web|title=2013 Goodreads Choice Awards - Fantasy|url=https://www.goodreads.com/choiceawards/best-fantasy-books-2013=goodreads|accessdate=17 December 2013}}</ref> in 2013, Age of Myth in 2016, Age of Swords in 2017,  Age of War in 2018 and 1 for Best Science Fiction: ''Hollow World'' in 2014.<ref>{{cite web|title=2014 Goodreads Choice Awards - Science Fiction|url=https://www.goodreads.com/choiceawards/best-science-fiction-books-2014|accessdate=24 January 2015}}</ref> ''Theft of Swords'' was selected as one of [[Barnes & Noble]]'s 2011 Best Fantasy Books,<ref>{{cite web|title=The Best Fantasy Releases of 2011 |url=http://bookclubs.barnesandnoble.com/t5/Explorations-The-BN-SciFi-and/The-Best-Fantasy-Releases-of-2011/ba-p/1242340 |publisher=Barnes & Nobles |accessdate=8 December 2012 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20120215094119/http://bookclubs.barnesandnoble.com/t5/Explorations-The-BN-SciFi-and/The-Best-Fantasy-Releases-of-2011/ba-p/1242340 |archivedate=15 February 2012 }}</ref> shortlisted for the 2013 Audie Award for Fantasy,<ref>{{cite web|title=The 2013 Winners and Finalists Audiobook of the Year|url=http://www.audiofilemagazine.com/2013_audies.html|publisher=AudioFile Magazine|accessdate=21 March 2014}}</ref> and also one of [[Library Journal]]'s 2011 Best SF/Fantasy Books.<ref>{{cite web|title=Best Books 2011: SF/Fantasy|url=http://reviews.libraryjournal.com/2011/11/best-of/best-genre/best-books-2011-sf-fantasy/|publisher=Library Journal|accessdate=8 December 2012|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170701172842/http://reviews.libraryjournal.com/2011/11/best-of/best-genre/best-books-2011-sf-fantasy/|archive-date=1 July 2017|url-status=dead}}</ref> ''Age of Myth'' was a 2016 [[Amazon's Best Books of the Year]] selection in the Science Fiction and Fantasy category.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.amazon.com/b/?ie=UTF8&node=10207069011 |title=The Best Books of 2016 |work=Amazon.com |author= |date= |accessdate=April 28, 2017}}</ref>
