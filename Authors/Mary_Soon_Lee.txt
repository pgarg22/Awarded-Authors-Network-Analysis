'''Mary Soon Lee'''  (born 1965) <ref>[http://www.isfdb.org/cgi-bin/ea.cgi?1362 ISFDB]</ref> is a [[British people|British]] [[speculative fiction]] writer and poet.<ref>{{cite web|last=Jones|first=Donna|title=On Spec: The Canadian Magazine Of The Fantastic vol 17 no. 3 # 62 Fall 2005|url=http://www.sfcrowsnest.com/articles/books/2006/On-Spec-The-Canadian-Magazine-Of-The-Fantastic-vol-17-no-3--62-Fall-2005-10068.php|publisher=SF Crowsnest|accessdate=26 June 2013|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170804084253/http://www.sfcrowsnest.com/articles/books/2006/On-Spec-The-Canadian-Magazine-Of-The-Fantastic-vol-17-no-3--62-Fall-2005-10068.php|archive-date=4 August 2017|url-status=dead}}</ref>

==Life and career==
Mary Soon Lee was born in [[London, England]], and attended Trinity Hall, [[Cambridge University]], where she graduated with a [[Master of Arts]] in mathematics and a diploma in [[computer science]]. After graduating, she worked as a Lisp programmer at the software company [[Harlequin (software company)|Harlequin]]. She began writing in 1991 on TV scripts, and in 1992 turned to short stories, making her first short story sale to ''Strange Days'' magazine.<ref>{{cite web|last=Walters|first=Trent|title=A Conversation With Mary Soon Lee|url=http://www.sfsite.com/02b/msl122.htm|publisher=SF Site|accessdate=26 June 2013}}</ref> Her work has appeared in several professional magazines and anthologies including ''[[The Magazine of Fantasy & Science Fiction|Fantasy & Science Fiction]]'', ''[[Interzone (magazine)|Interzone]]'' and ''[[Analog Science Fiction and Fact|Analog]]''.<ref>{{cite web|last=Yant|first=Christie|title=Author Spotlight: Mary Soon Lee|url=http://www.lightspeedmagazine.com/nonfiction/author-spotlight-mary-soon-lee/|publisher=[[Lightspeed (magazine)|Lightspeed]]|accessdate=26 June 2013}}</ref> She currently resides in the United States.
