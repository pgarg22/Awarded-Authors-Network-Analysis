'''Elizabeth E. Wein''' ({{IPAc-en|w|iː|n}}, born October 2, 1964) is an American-born writer best known for her [[Young adult literature|young adult]] [[historical fiction]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://biography.jrank.org/pages/651/Wein-Elizabeth-E-ve-1964-Sidelights.html|title=Elizabeth E(ve) Wein (1964-) - Sidelights|website=biography.jrank.org|language=en|access-date=2019-05-10}}</ref> She holds both American and British citizenship.

== Personal life ==
Elizabeth E. Wein was born in [[New York City]], [[New York (state)|New York]] on October 2, 1964. She moved to [[England]] when she was three. When she was six, her father, Norman Wein, was sent to the [[University of the West Indies]] in [[Jamaica]], where she lived from 1970 to 1973. As a child, she was fluent in [[Jamaican Patois|Jamaican patois]].<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|url=https://www.elizabethwein.com/biography|title=Biography|website=Elizabeth Wein|language=en|access-date=2019-05-10}}</ref>

Wein moved back to the United States when her parents separated, and she was raised by her mother Carol Flocken in [[Harrisburg, Pennsylvania]] until her death in a car accident in 1978, after which Wein lived with her maternal grandparents. She wrote her first novel at age 11. Wein attended [[Yale University]] and, after a year of work-study in England, spent seven years getting a PhD in Folklore at the [[University of Pennsylvania]]. Wein moved to England with her English husband Tim in 1995 and settled in [[Scotland]] in 2000. She and Tim have two children.<ref name=":0" />

Wein has a passion for planes, and she possesses a [[private pilot licence]] which she received in 2003.<ref name=":0" />

==Writing career==
Wein's first five books recount a version of the [[King Arthur]] stories which moves the narrative to the kingdom of [[Aksum]] in 6th century [[Ethiopia]]. The stories focus on her interpretation of Medraut ([[Mordred]]) and his half-Aksumite, half-British son Telemakos.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.hbook.com/2014/05/authors-illustrators/interview-elizabeth-wein/|title=An Interview with Elizabeth Wein — The Horn Book|website=www.hbook.com|access-date=2019-05-10}}</ref>

Her 2012 novel, ''[[Code Name Verity]]'', is a World War II thriller focusing on the friendship between two young British women,<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.slj.com/2012/07/interviews/under-cover/straighten-up-and-fly-right-elizabeth-weins-new-spy-thriller-will-break-your-heart-under-cover/|title=Library Journal|website=www.slj.com|access-date=2019-05-10}}</ref> a transport pilot and a spy. It received critical acclaim;<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2012/05/13/books/review/code-name-verity-by-elizabeth-wein.html|title='Code Name Verity,' by Elizabeth Wein|last=Ingall|first=Marjorie|date=2012-05-11|work=The New York Times|access-date=2019-05-10|language=en-US|issn=0362-4331}}</ref> it was awarded an [[List of Edgar Allan Poe Award for Best Young Adult Novel winners|Edgar Award for Best Young Adult Novel]] and designated a [[Michael L. Printz Award]] Honor book in 2013.<ref>{{Cite chapter|title=Something About the Author|last=|first=|publisher=Gale|year=2019|isbn=|location=|pages=197|chapter=Elizabeth Wein (1964-)|volume=343}}</ref> A follow-up novel, ''[[Rose Under Fire]]'', also set in World War II,<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.egmont.co.uk/books/author/522/elizabeth-wein|title=Elizabeth Wein – Egmont|website=www.egmont.co.uk|access-date=2019-05-10}}</ref> tells the story of an [[Air Transport Auxiliary]] pilot who is captured and sent to the [[Ravensbrück concentration camp]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.scottishbooktrust.com/profile-author/87598|title=Author details {{!}} Scottish Book Trust|website=www.scottishbooktrust.com|language=en|access-date=2019-05-10}}</ref>

Wein's short stories have been published in collections edited by [[Ellen Datlow]], [[Terri Windling]], and [[Sharyn November]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.worldswithoutend.com/author.asp?ID=2874|title=Elizabeth Wein|website=Worlds Without End|language=en|access-date=2019-05-10}}</ref> She is a regular reviewer for the ''New York Times Book Review.''<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.nytimes.com/search/?action=click&contentCollection&region=TopBar&WT.nav=searchWidget&module=SearchSubmit&pgtype=Homepage|title=The New York Times - Search|website=www.nytimes.com|access-date=2019-05-10}}</ref>
