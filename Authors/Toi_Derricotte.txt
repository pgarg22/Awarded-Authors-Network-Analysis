'''Toi Derricotte''' (pronounced '''''DARE'''-ah-cot'' ) (born April 12, 1941) is an [[United States|American]] [[poet]]. She is the author of six poetry collections and a literary memoir. She has won numerous literary awards, including the 2020 Frost Medal for distinguished lifetime achievement in poetry awarded by the [[Poetry Society of America]], and the 2021 [[Wallace Stevens Award]], sponsored by the [[Academy of American Poets]]. From 2012–2017, Derricotte served as a Chancellor of the Academy of American Poets. She is currently a  professor [[Emeritus|emerita]] in writing at the [[University of Pittsburgh]] .

==Early life==
The only child of Benjamin and Antonia (née Baquet) Webster, '''Toi Derricotte''' was born '''Toinette Webster'''
on April 12, 1941 in [[Hamtramck, Michigan]]. Her parents divorced when she was a teenager. A [[Catholic Church|Catholic]], she attended [[Girls Catholic Central High School]] in [[Detroit]], where she graduated in 1959.<ref name="Contemporary American Women Poets">{{cite book |last1=Love |first1=Monifa A. |title=Contemporary American Women Poets: A to Z |date=2002 |publisher=Greenwood |isbn=978-0313317835 |pages=89–91}}</ref> She went to [[Mass in the Catholic Church|Mass]] every day.<ref>{{Cite journal|last=Schwartz|first=Claire|date=2017|title=An Interview with Toi Derricotte|url=https://www.jstor.org/stable/10.2979/transition.124.1.29|journal=Transition|issue=124|pages=157–175|doi=10.2979/transition.124.1.29|issn=0041-1191}}</ref>

She later attended  [[Wayne State University]], where she studied psychology, but her studies were interrupted by an unplanned pregnancy,
and her marriage to artist, Clarence Reese. Their son, Anthony was born in 1962. The couple later divorced.  Derricotte changed her studies to [[Special Education]] and  graduated with a BA in 1965.<ref name="Poets.org">{{cite web |title=About Toi Derricotte |url=https://poets.org/poet/toi-derricotte |website=Poets.org |access-date=7 October 2021}}</ref>

==Career==
Derricotte worked in [[Detroit]] for [[ManpowerGroup|Manpower Inc.]] from 1964–1966 and at the Farand School from 1966–1968. She later married Bruce Derricotte in 1967.<ref name="Contemporary American Women Poets" /> They later moved to [[New Jersey]] where she worked as a teacher and participated in a special program, teaching poetry to students from kindergarten through high school.<ref name="PittNews 2019">{{cite web |last1=Monroy |first1=Matthew |title=Poet Toi Derricotte digs deep into her past for her latest book|url=https://pittnews.com/article/152114/featured/poet-toi-derricotte-digs-deep-into-her-past-for-her-latest-book/ |website=The PittNews |access-date=8 October 2021}}</ref>
With easy access to New York City, Derricotte participated in writing workshops and began writing each day.<ref name="African American Registry" />

Derricotte published her first poetry collection,  ''The Empress of the Death House'', ([[Broadside Lotus Press|Lotus Press]]) in 1978. In 1983, [[Firebrand Books]] published her second poetry collection, ''Natural Birth''. In 1984, she won a poetry fellowship from the [[National Endowment for the Arts]],  and a  [[MacDowell (artists' residency and workshop)|MacDowell fellowship]]. She later earned an MA in [[English Literature]] from [[New York University]]. In 1989, the [[University of Pittsburgh Press]] published Dericotte's third poetry collection, ''Captivity''. The collection had has second (1991) and third (1993) printings.
<ref name="African American Registry">{{cite web |title=Toi Derricotte, one of Michigan’s finest
|url=https://aaregistry.org/story/toi-derricotte-one-of-michigans-finest/ |website=AAREG |access-date=8 October 2021}}</ref>

In 1991, Derricotte became an associate professor at the [[University of Pittsburgh]]. In 1996, [[W. W. Norton and Company|Norton Publishing Company]] accepted for publication Derricotte's literary memoir, ''The Black Notebooks, An Interior Journey'', a book she began in 1974 when her family became one of the first black families to move into [[Upper Montclair, New Jersey]]. The memoir won the 1998 [[Anisfield-Wolf Book Award]] for Non-Fiction.<ref name="Annisfield-Wolf Book Awards">{{cite web |title=Winners |url=https://www.anisfield-wolf.org/winners/ |website=Annisfield-Wolf Book Awards |access-date=9 October 2021}}</ref>

Derricotte co-founded the Cave Canem Foundation, a national poetry organization in 1996, with American writer, [[Cornelius Eady]]. Cave Canem is a national poetry organization that supports the professional growth of [[African- American]] poets.  In 2016, she and Eady accepted the National Book Foundation's Literarian Award for Outstanding Service to the American Literary Community on behalf of Cave Canem.<ref name="Wash Post Cave Canem">{{cite news|last1=Charles|first1=Ron|title=National Book Foundation honors group that supports African American poetry|url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/entertainment/books/national-book-foundation-honors-group-that-supports-african-american-poetry/2016/09/19/8c6a22ca-7d16-11e6-bd86-b7bbd53d2b5d_story.html|accessdate=October 8, 2021|work=The Washington Post|date=September 19, 2016}}</ref>

The University of Pittsburgh Press published Derricotte's fourth, fifth and sixth poetry collections: ''Tender'' in 1997, ''The Undertaker's Daughter'' in 2011, and ''"I": New and Selected Poems'' in 2019. In October 2019, '"I": New and Selected Poems' was longlisted for the National Book Award for Poetry.<ref name="African American Registry" />

In 2012, Derricotte was elected a Chancellor of the Academy of American Poets. She served as a Chancellor from 2012–2017. She is currently a professor emerita in writing at the University of Pittsburgh.<ref name="Univ of Pittsburg 2019">{{cite web |title=Toi Derricotte’s latest poetry collection ‘I’ longlisted for National Book Award |url=https://www.utimes.pitt.edu/news/toi-derricotte-s-latest |website=University of Pittsburgh |access-date=7 October 2021}}</ref>
