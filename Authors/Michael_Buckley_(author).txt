'''Michael William Buckley'''<ref>{{cite journal|title=Ohio Birth Index, 1908-2011|journal=Ohio Department of Health|date=September 10, 1969|url=http://birth-records.mooseroots.com/l/10184545/Michael-William-Buckley|accessdate=October 13, 2015}}{{Dead link|date=April 2020 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref> (born August 16, 1969) is a bestselling children's author whose works include ''[[The Sisters Grimm (novel series)|The Sisters Grimm]]'' and the ''[[N.E.R.D.S.]]'' book series.

==Early life==
Born in [[Akron, Ohio]], Michael Buckley attended [[Ohio University]], where he graduated with honors.<ref name=NYTimes>{{cite web | url=https://query.nytimes.com/gst/fullpage.html?res=9500E3D6113CF93BA2575AC0A9639C8B63 | title=WEDDINGS/CELEBRATIONS; Alison Fargis, Michael Buckley|work=[[The New York Times]] | date =September 18, 2005 | accessdate=June 5, 2009 }}</ref> Shortly after graduating from Ohio University in 1986, Buckley moved to New York City.<ref name="About Buckley">{{Cite web|title = About Buckley|url = http://michaelbuckleywrites.com/about/|website = I Don't Have Time For This Blog|access-date = February 17, 2016|language = en-US|archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20181126134955/https://www.michaelbuckleywrites.com/about/|archive-date = November 26, 2018|url-status = dead}}</ref>

==Career==
Sometime after college Buckley moved to New York City to start an internship with the ''[[Late Show with David Letterman]]'', then moved into a television production job where he worked on documentaries.<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.pcnr.com/news/2007/0710/Cultural_Events/030.html | title=Reading by Author Michael Buckley Part of Harry Potter Weekend Events | work=[[Putnam County News and Recorder]] | date=July 10, 2007 | accessdate=June 5, 2009 }}</ref>
His books are published by [[Harry N. Abrams, Inc.|Harry N. Abrams]]. Buckley worked as a stand-up comic as well as a TV copywriter before he started to write ''The Sisters Grimm'' series.<ref name="About Buckley"/> Buckley had originally planned for ''The Sisters Grimm'' to be a television series until his wife suggested turning it into a book.<ref name="loc.gov">{{Cite web|title = Michael Buckley {{!}} National Book Festival - Library of Congress|url = https://www.loc.gov/bookfest/kids-teachers/authors/michael_buckley|website = www.loc.gov|access-date = February 17, 2016}}</ref>

In 2010, Buckley and his writing partner, Joe Deasy, created an animated series for [[Cartoon Network]] called ''[[Robotomy]]''. Buckley and Deasy executive produced as well as wrote the first season that starred [[Patton Oswalt]] and [[Dana Snyder]] ([[Master Shake]] from ''[[Aqua Teen Hunger Force]]''). The series aired in CN's prime time block.

He is currently writing the screenplay for a ''[[N.E.R.D.S. (film)|N.E.R.D.S.]]'' animated feature developed by [[Elton John]]'s [[Rocket Pictures]].<ref name=Deadline1>{{cite news|last=Fleming|first=Mike|title=Elton John's Rocket Pictures Turning Kidbook 'Nerds' Into Animated Feature|url=https://www.deadline.com/2012/01/elton-johns-rocket-pictures-turning-kidbook-nerds-into-animated-feature/#|accessdate=January 21, 2012|newspaper=Deadline|date=January 20, 2012}}</ref> 
He also recently finished his new series, Undertow.

Michael Buckley is married to Alison Fargis, a literary agent and co-owner of [[Paul Fargis|Stonesong Press]], and they have a son, Finn.<ref name=NYTimes />
