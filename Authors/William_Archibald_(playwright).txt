'''William Archibald''' (7 March 1917 – 27 December 1970) was a [[Trinidad]]ian-born playwright, dancer, choreographer and director, whose stage adaptation of [[Henry James]]' ''[[The Turn of the Screw]]'' was made into the 1961 British horror film ''[[The Innocents (1961 film)|The Innocents]]''.

==Biography==
Born John William Wharton Archibald in [[Trinidad]] of European descent, Archibald was educated at [[Saint Mary's College, Trinidad and Tobago|St Mary's College]] in [[Port-of-Spain]].<ref name="NYTimesObit">"William Archibald, Playwright, Actor and Dancer, Is Dead at 53" (29 December 1970). ''The New York Times''.</ref>

Leaving Trinidad in 1937, Archibald enrolled at the [[Academy of Allied Arts]] in New York to study dance, making his Broadway début as a principal in the revue ''[[One for the Money (musical)|One for the Money]]''.<ref>Hill, Errol G. and Hatch, James V., 2003. ''A History of African American Theatre''. Cambridge: Cambridge University Press, p. 279. {{ISBN|978-0-521-62443-5}}.</ref> Archibald produced his first saleable stage writing for the choreographer [[Charles Weidman]]: a verse accompaniment for ''On My Mother's Side''.<ref>''Theatre Arts Magazine'' (January 1951), p. 52.</ref> He also wrote the text for [[José Limón]]'s ''War Lyrics''.<ref>Garafola, Lynn ed., 2001. ''José Limón: An Unfinished Memoir''. Middletown: Wesleyan University Press, p. 95. {{ISBN|978-0-8195-6374-3}}.</ref>

In 1945, Archibald wrote the book and lyrics for ''Carib Song'', which was staged at the [[Adelphi Theatre (New York)|Adelphi Theatre]] with choreography by [[Katherine Dunham]] and a score by Baldwin Bergersen. Archibald then went on to write another musical, three plays and the libretto for a ballet opera: ''Bay Harbour''. His first play, ''[[The Innocents (play)|The Innocents]]'', based on the [[Henry James]] novella ''[[The Turn of the Screw]]'', opened on Broadway in 1950. With [[Truman Capote]] he wrote the screenplay for the 1961 film, winning an [[Edgar Award]] for [[List of Edgar Allan Poe Award for Best Motion Picture Screenplay winners|Best Motion Picture Screenplay]] from the [[Mystery Writers of America]]. Archibald also co-wrote, with [[George Tabori]], the script for the Alfred Hitchcock film ''[[I Confess (film)|I Confess]]''.

William Archibald died on 27 December 1970 in New York of infectious hepatitis.<ref name="NYTimesObit" />

His brother was Trinidadian playwright and historical writer Douglas (Jack) Archibald.<ref>Rubin, D. and Solorzano, C. eds, 2000. ''The World Encyclopedia of Contemporary Theatre: The Americas, Volume 2''. London: Routledge, p. 188. {{ISBN|978-0-415-22745-2}}.</ref>
