'''Steven Pressfield''' (born September 1, 1943) is an American author of [[historical fiction]], non-fiction, and screenplays.

== Early life ==
Pressfield was born in [[Port of Spain, Trinidad]], in 1943, while his father was stationed there, in the [[United States Navy|Navy]].

== Education ==
Pressfield graduated from [[Duke University]] in 1965 and in 1966 joined the [[United States Marine Corps|Marine Corps]].<ref name="stevenpressfield">{{cite web|last=Pressfield|first=Steven|title=About|url=http://www.stevenpressfield.com/about/|accessdate=May 2, 2012}}</ref>

== Career ==
Pressfield was an advertising copywriter, schoolteacher, tractor-trailer driver, bartender, oilfield [[roustabout]], attendant in a mental hospital, fruit-picker in Washington state, and screenwriter.<ref name="stevenpressfield" /> His struggles to make a living as an author, including the period when he was homeless and living out of the back of his car, are detailed in his book ''[[The War of Art (book)|The War of Art]]''.<ref name="stevenpressfield" />

Pressfield's first book, ''[[The Legend of Bagger Vance (novel)|The Legend of Bagger Vance]]'', which was loosely based on the [[Bhagavad Gita]],  was published in 1995, and was made into a 2000 [[The Legend of Bagger Vance|film of the same name]] directed by [[Robert Redford]] and starring [[Will Smith]], [[Charlize Theron]], and [[Matt Damon]].<ref>{{cite web|title=The Legend of Bagger Vance|url=https://www.imdb.com/title/tt0146984/|accessdate=2012-06-12}}</ref>

His second novel, ''[[Gates of Fire]]'' (1998), is about the [[Lacedaemon|Spartans]] and the battle at [[Thermopylae]].<ref>Gal Perl Finkel, [http://www.jpost.com/Opinion/Wars-are-won-by-preparation-and-not-by-courage-alone-486511 Wars are won by preparation and not by courage alone], [[The Jerusalem Post]], April 8, 2017.</ref> It is taught at the [[United States Military Academy|U.S. Military Academy]],<ref>{{cite news| last=Samet|first=Elizabeth D.|title=Matriculation: In the Valley of the Shadows|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2007/09/30/magazine/30WestPoint-t.html?pagewanted=print|accessdate=May 2, 2012|work=The New York Times|date=2007-09-30}}</ref> the [[United States Naval Academy]], and the [[The Basic School|Marine Corps Basic School at Quantico]].<ref>{{cite web|last=Pressfield|first=Steven|title=About|url=http://www.stevenpressfield.com/about/|accessdate=2011-06-15}}</ref><ref>{{cite news| url=http://articles.latimes.com/2008/jun/28/entertainment/et-book28|work=Los Angeles Times|first=Tony|last=Perry|title=Hunting 'the Desert Fox'|date=2008-06-28}}</ref>

In 2012, Pressfield launched the publishing house [http://www.blackirishbooks.com/ Black Irish Books] with his agent Shawn Coyne.<ref>{{cite web|last=Pressfield|first=Steven|title=Announcing Turning Pro|url=http://www.stevenpressfield.com/2012/06/announcing-turning-pro/|accessdate=2012-06-12}}</ref>

== Works ==
===Fiction===
* ''[[The Legend of Bagger Vance (novel)|The Legend of Bagger Vance]]'' (see ''[[The Legend of Bagger Vance]]'' for the film), about a young man coming to terms with his spiritual demons through the medium of [[golf]] (1995)
* ''[[Gates of Fire]]'', about the [[Battle of Thermopylae]] (1998), {{ISBN|0385492510}}
* ''[[Tides of War]]'', a Novel of [[Alcibiades]] and the [[Peloponnesian War]] (2000), {{ISBN|0385492529}}
* ''[[Last of the Amazons]]'', in which [[Theseus]], the legendary King of Athens, sets sail to the north coast of the Black Sea inhabited by a race of female warriors (2002), {{ISBN|038550098X}}
* ''[[The Virtues of War]]'', about [[Alexander the Great]] (2004), {{ISBN|0385500998}}
* ''[[The Afghan Campaign]]'', about [[Alexander the Great|Alexander the Great's]] conquests in Afghanistan (2006), {{ISBN|038551641X}}
* ''[[Killing Rommel]]'' (2008), a fictionalized account of a patrol of the British [[Long Range Desert Group]] during the [[North African Campaign]] of [[World War II]], {{ISBN|0385519702}}
* ''[[The Profession]]'' (2011), {{ISBN|9780385528733}}.  Pressfield's first book set in the future, where military force is for hire everywhere. Oil companies, multinational corporations and banks employ powerful, cutting-edge mercenary armies to control global chaos and protect their riches.
* ''[[A Man at Arms]]'' (2021), {{ISBN|9780393540970}}, a novel set in Jerusalem and the Sinai desert in the first century AD.

* ''The Artist's Journey: The Wake of the Hero's Journey and the Lifelong Pursuit of Meaning'' (2018), {{ISBN|9781936891542}}

== Filmography ==
Prior to publishing his first original works of fiction, Pressfield wrote several Hollywood screenplays including 1986's ''[[King Kong Lives]]'', 1988's ''[[Above the Law (1988 film)|Above the Law]]'' starring [[Steven Seagal]] and directed by [[Andrew Davis (director)|Andrew Davis]], 1992's ''[[Freejack]]'', a work of [[science fiction]] starring [[Emilio Estevez]], [[Mick Jagger]], and [[Anthony Hopkins]], and 1993's ''[[Joshua Tree (1993 film)|Joshua Tree]]'' (a.k.a. ''Army of One'') starring [[Dolph Lundgren]] and [[George Segal]]. ''Joshua Tree'' was directed by [[Academy Award]] and [[Bafta]] winning [[stunt double|stuntman]] [[Vic Armstrong]].

His novel ''The Legend of Bagger Vance'' was made into a 2000 [[The Legend of Bagger Vance|film]] starring [[Matt Damon]] as the golf pro and [[Will Smith]] as his spiritual [[guide]] and was widely criticized for its use of the "[[Magical Negro]]" as a plot device.

Pressfield also appeared as one of the historians in [[History (U.S. TV channel)|The History Channel]]'s 2007 documentary ''[[Last Stand of the 300]]'' and a commentator on an episode of the History Channel's ''Decisive Battles'' series featuring Alexander the Great on July 30, 2004.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.greeknewsonline.com/steven-pressfield-exploring-ancient-greece-through-fiction/|title=Steven Pressfield: Exploring Ancient Greece through Fiction|work=greeknewsonline.com}}</ref>
