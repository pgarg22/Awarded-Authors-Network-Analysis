'''Thomas Owen''' (real name Gérald Bertot) is often credited with [[Jean Ray (author)|Jean Ray]] and [[Franz Hellens]] as a pillar of Belgium weird fiction and as part of the golden age of Belgium [[fantastique]] fiction.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Gauvin|first1=Edward|title=Jean Muno’s Unusual Tales|url=http://weirdfictionreview.com/2014/01/jean-munos-unusual-tales/|accessdate=7 June 2014}}</ref> He wrote over 300 short stories in his lifetime, most being either fantasy or weird fiction.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Gauvin|first1=Edward|title=An Accounting by Thomas Owen|url=http://weirdfictionreview.com/2012/09/an-accounting/|accessdate=7 June 2014}}</ref>

== Biography ==

Thomas Owen started as an author of detective fiction but switched to the fantastique with 1942's ''l’Initiation à la Peur''. Eventually he became close friends with one of the founders of the Belgian school of the strange, [[Jean Ray (author)|Jean Ray]]. They remained close friends until Ray's death in 1964.
