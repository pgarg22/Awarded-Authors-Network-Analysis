'''James Fenton''' (5 June 1931 â€“ 3 February 2021)<ref name= "BBC">{{cite news|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-northern-ireland-55925359|title=James Fenton: Ulster-Scots poet dies aged 89|work=[[BBC News]]|date=3 February 2021|access-date=3 February 2021}}</ref> was a linguist and poet who wrote in [[Ulster Scots dialects|Ulster Scots]].

==Biography==
He grew up in [[Drumdarragh]] and in [[Ballinaloob]], [[County Antrim]]. His home language of childhood was Ulster Scots. Educated at [[Stranmillis College]] in [[Belfast]], and later [[Queen's University Belfast|Queen's University]], he became a teacher at schools in Belfast.

His poetry in Ulster Scots, at times lively, contented, wistful, was written in contemporary Ulster Scots, and particularly the dialect of Ballinaloob ("Belnaloob" in his poem ''Thonner an Thon'').

==Books==
James Fenton's record and study of Scots and Scots words used in Ulster, ''The Hamely Tongue''<ref>''The Hamely Tongue'', The Ullans Press {{ISBN|1-905281-07-2}}</ref> has been published by the Ullans Press. A collection of his own poetry has been published by the Ullans Press too; ''Thonner an Thon''.<ref>''Thonner an Thon'', The Ullans Press {{ISBN|0-9530350-7-7}}</ref>

James Fenton lived in [[Glengormley]], [[Newtownabbey]].

==Death==
Fenton died on 3 February 2021, aged 89.<ref name= "BBC"/>
