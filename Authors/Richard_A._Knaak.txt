'''Richard A. Knaak''' (born May 28, 1961 in [[Chicago]]<ref name="LitMast">{{cite book| author= William Laskowski| title=Guide to Literary Masters & Their Works | url=http://search.ebscohost.com/login.aspx?direct=true&db=lfh&AN=103331LM44919790304197&site=lrc-live | date=January 2007}}</ref>) is the [[author]] of ''[[Dragonlance]]'' novels, ''[[Dragonrealm]]'', six novels for [[Blizzard Entertainment]]'s ''[[Diablo (series)|Diablo]]'' series, and ten works in the [[Warcraft]] universe. He has also written five non-series fantasy books.

==Early life and education==
Richard Allen Knaak was born May 28, 1961 in Chicago, Illinois, to James Richard Knaak and Anna Maria (Trappen) Knaak.<ref name="LitMast"/> He attended the [[University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign]], studying chemistry at first,<ref>http://www.richardaknaak.com/Bio.html</ref> and subsequently [[rhetoric]], and earned a bachelor's degree in 1984.<ref name="LitMast"/> He worked as a warehouseman, resume writer, and office clerk before becoming a full-time freelance writer in 1988.<ref name="LitMast"/>

==Career==
After reading [[Andre Norton]]'s ''Storm over Warlock'', he became a fan of fantasy and science fiction.<ref name="LitMast"/> He eventually sold his first [[short story]] in 1986, and it was published in 1987.{{citation needed|date=January 2013}} His [[Dragonlance]] titles include ''[[The Legend of Huma]]'', ''Reavers of the Blood Sea'', ''[[Kaz the Minotaur]]'', ''[[Land of the Minotaurs]]'', and ''The Citadel'', in addition to short stories in various anthologies.<ref name="wizardsbio">{{cite web|url=http://ww2.wizards.com/Books/Wizards/Bios/default.aspx?doc=RichardAKnaak|title=Richard A Knaak|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20090224005425/http://ww2.wizards.com/Books/Wizards/Bios/default.aspx?doc=RichardAKnaak|archivedate=February 24, 2009}}</ref> He also wrote the ten-volume Dragonrealm series, and the first Dragonrealm story, "Firedrake," helped him get his first Dragonlance project.<ref name="wizardsbio"/> He has also written several stand-alone volumes, including the contemporary fantasies ''Frostwing'', ''King of the Grey'', and ''Dutchman'', plus the heroic adventure ''The Janus Mask''.<ref name="wizardsbio"/> He has also worked on novels based in the worlds of the computer games ''Warcraft'' and ''Diablo''.<ref name="wizardsbio"/>

Knaak lists [[Roger Zelazny]], [[Edgar Rice Burroughs]], and [[Edgar Allan Poe]] as major influences,<ref name="LitMast"/> and has even listed a few of his favorite fellow authors, those being [[Robert E. Howard]], [[Glen Cook]], [[L. Sprague de Camp]], [[Lawrence Watt-Evans]], [[Harry Turtledove]], [[Jennifer Roberson]], [[Laurell K. Hamilton]], [[Harry Harrison (writer)|Harry Harrison]], and [[Robert J. Sawyer|Robert Sawyer]].
