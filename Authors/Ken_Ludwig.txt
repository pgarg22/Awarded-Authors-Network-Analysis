'''Ken Ludwig''' is an American [[playwright]] and [[theatre director]] whose work has been performed in more than 30 countries in over 20 languages.

==Personal life==
Ken Ludwig was born in [[York, Pennsylvania]]. His father was a doctor and his mother was a former Broadway chorus girl.<ref>[https://www.washingtonpost.com/wp-dyn/content/article/2010/03/18/AR2010031802278.html Arana, Marie. Playwright "Ken Ludwig's childhood dream www.washingtonpost.com, March 21, 2010]</ref> Ludwig was educated at the [[York Suburban Senior High School]], York PA. He received degrees from [[Haverford College]], [[Harvard University]] (where he studied music with Leonard Bernstein), [[Harvard Law School]], and [[Cambridge University]] (Trinity College). His older brother, [[Eugene Ludwig]], served as President Clinton's Comptroller of the Currency.{{citation needed|date=October 2019}}

==Career==
Ken Ludwig's first [[Broadway theatre|Broadway]] play, ''[[Lend Me a Tenor]]'' (1989), which [[Frank Rich]] of the ''[[New York Times]]'' called "one of the two great farces by a living writer",<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.nytimes.com/1989/03/03/theater/reviews-theater-when-one-tenor-is-much-like-another.html|title=Reviews/Theater; When One Tenor Is Much Like Another|first=Frank|last=Rich|date=March 3, 1989|via=NYTimes.com}}</ref> won three [[Tony Awards]] and was nominated for nine. His second Broadway and [[West End theatre|West End]] production, ''[[Crazy for You (musical)|Crazy for You]]'' (1992), ran for over five years and won the [[Tony Award]], [[Drama Desk Award|Drama Desk]], [[Outer Critics Circle Award|Outer Critics Circle]], LA Drama Critics Circle, [[Helen Hayes]], and [[Laurence Olivier Awards]] as Best Musical. Other Broadway credits include ''[[Moon Over Buffalo]]'' (1995) with [[Carol Burnett]] and [[Lynn Redgrave]] (on Broadway) and [[Frank Langella]] and [[Joan Collins]] at the Old Vic in London. For Broadway, he wrote the book for a musical adaptation of ''[[The Adventures of Tom Sawyer (musical)|The Adventures of Tom Sawyer]]'' (2001), and a new adaptation of the classic [[Ben Hecht]]-[[Charles MacArthur]] play, ''[[Twentieth Century (play)|Twentieth Century]]'' (2004) starring [[Alec Baldwin]] and [[Anne Heche]]. A revival of ''Lend Me A Tenor ''opened on Broadway in 2010, starring [[Tony Shalhoub]] and [[Justin Bartha]].

Among Ludwig's other works are ''Shakespeare in Hollywood'', which was presented at [[Arena Stage]] in [[Washington, D.C.]], in 2003 and won the Helen Hayes Award for Best Play of the Year; ''[[Leading Ladies]]'', which premiered at the [[Alley Theatre]] in association with [[Cleveland Play House]] in 2004; ''[[Be My Baby (2005 play)|Be My Baby]]'', at the [[Alley Theatre]] in 2005, with [[Hal Holbrook]] and [[Dixie Carter]]; and the completion of [[Thornton Wilder]]'s adaptation of [[George Farquhar]]'s [[Restoration comedy]] ''[[The Beaux' Stratagem]]'', staged at the [[Shakespeare Theatre Company]] in Washington, D.C., in 2006. Ludwig's adaptation of ''[[The Three Musketeers]]'' opened at Bristol Old Vic in England in December 2006.

Ludwig wrote an adaptation of [[Robert Louis Stevenson]]'s ''[[Treasure Island]]'', which premiered at the Alley Theatre in April 2007, played at the Theatre Royal, Haymarket on London's [[West End theatre|West End]] in 2008, and won the AATE Distinguished Play Award for Best Adaptation of the Year. Another stage adaptation of the [[George Gershwin|George]] and [[Ira Gershwin]] film ''[[An American in Paris]]'' premiered at the Alley Theatre in Houston as ''The Gershwins' An American in Paris''<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.alleytheatre.org/Alley/The_Gershwins_An_American_in_Paris_EN.asp?SnID=1126088537|title=Alley Theatre Official Website - Whoops!|website=www.alleytheatre.org}}</ref> in May 2008.

Ludwig's play, ''A Fox on the Fairway'',<ref name="The Fox on the Fairway">[http://www.kenludwig.com/fox_on_the_fairway/fox_on_the_fairway.php]</ref> a comedy set in the world of golf, premiered in October 2010 at [[Signature Theatre (Arlington, Virginia)]] in Arlington, Virginia directed by [[John Rando]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.playbill.com/article/ken-ludwigs-new-comedy-fox-on-the-fairway-opens-at-arlingtons-signature-john-rando-directs-com-172920|title=Ken Ludwig's New Comedy, Fox on the Fairway, Opens at Arlington's Signature; John Rando Directs|first=Kenneth|last=Jones|date=October 24, 2010|website=Playbill}}</ref>

''[[The Game's Afoot]]'' is Ludwig's comedy-mystery about the actor William Gillette who originated the role of Sherlock Holmes. It premiered at Cleveland Play House in November 2011, directed by Aaron Posner and featuring Donald Sage Mackay and [[Rob McClure]]<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.playbill.com/article/ken-ludwigs-the-games-afoot-or-holmes-for-the-holidays-opens-in-cleveland-nov-30-com-185000|title=Ken Ludwig's The Game's Afoot (or Holmes for the Holidays) Opens in Cleveland Nov. 30|first=Adam|last=Hetrick|date=November 30, 2011|website=Playbill}}</ref> 
<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.cleveland.com/arts/2011/11/ken_ludwigs_the_games_afoot_co.html|title=Ken Ludwig's 'The Game's Afoot' is a fictional adventure of famed Sherlock Holmes actor William Gillette|first1=rea|last1=Simakis|first2=The Plain|last2=Dealer|date=November 20, 2011|website=cleveland}}</ref> and won the 2012 Edgar Allan Poe Award for Best Mystery of the Year.

The world premiere of his first play for children, ''Twas The Night Before Christmas'', opened at The Adventure Theatre ([[Glen Echo Park, Maryland]]) in November 2011.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.kenludwig.com/twas_the_night_before_christmas/twas_the_night_before_christmas.php|title=''Twas The Night Before Christmas'' www.kenludwig.com}}</ref> He and his son, Jack Ludwig co-wrote an adaptation of Charles Dickens' A Christmas Carol entitled ''Tiny Tim's Christmas Carol'' which also premiered at The Adventure Theatre  in November 2014.{{citation needed|date=March 2016}}  His play ''[[Baskerville: A Sherlock Holmes Mystery]]'' premiered as a co-production at Arena Stage (Washington, D.C.) in January 2015 and [[McCarter Theatre]] Center in March 2015.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.playbill.com/article/ken-ludwigs-baskerville-a-sherlock-holmes-mystery-makes-world-premiere-tonight-com-339380|title=Ken Ludwig's Baskerville: A Sherlock Holmes Mystery Makes World Premiere Tonight|first=Carey|last=Purcell|date=January 16, 2015|website=Playbill}}</ref>

His play, ''A Comedy of Tenors'' premiered at the [[Cleveland Playhouse]] in September 2015 in a co-production with the [[McCarter Theatre]] Center and starred Rob McClure. The play has several characters from ''Lend Me A Tenor'' and takes place in Paris in the 1930s.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.playbill.com/article/broadway-actors-populate-world-premiere-of-ken-ludwigs-lend-me-a-tenor-follow-up-opening-tonight-com-361851|title=Broadway Actors Populate World Premiere of Ken Ludwig's Lend Me a Tenor Follow-Up, Opening Tonight|first=Andrew|last=Gans|date=September 12, 2015|website=Playbill}}</ref> The play opened at the McCarter Theatre in October 2015.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.mccarter.org/acomedyoftenors/|title=404 &#124; McCarter Theatre Center|website=www.mccarter.org}}</ref>

At the request of the Agatha Christie Estate, he adapted the Agatha Christie novel ''[[Murder on the Orient Express]]'' to a stage play, which premiered at the McCarter Theatre Center, Princeton, New Jersey, on March 14, 2017, directed by Emily Mann, the cast featured [[Allan Corduner]] as Detective Hercule Poirot, [[Veanne Cox]] as Princess Dragomiroff, [[Maboud Ebrahimzadeh]] as Michel, [[Julie Halston]] as Mrs. Hubbard, Susannah Hoffman as Mary Debenham, Alexandra Silber as Countess Andrenyi, Juha Sorola as MacQueen, Samantha Steinmetz as Greta Ohlsson, [[Max von Essen]] as Ratchett/Col. Arburthnot, and Evan Zes as Bouc.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.playbill.com/article/max-von-essen-julie-halston-and-more-star-in-murder-on-the-orient-express|title=Max Von Essen, Julie Halston, and More Star in Murder on the Orient Express|first=Andrew|last=Gans|date=March 15, 2017|website=Playbill}}</ref>

In December 2019, Ludwig's play Dear Jack, Dear Louise opened at [[Arena Stage]]. Dear Jack, Dear Louise is the story of his parentsâ€™ courtship during World War II when his father, U.S. Army Captain Jacob S. Ludwig, was stationed in Oregon and became pen pals with his mother, Louise Rabiner, an aspiring actress in New York City. They met and fell in love through letters and telegrams.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.arenastage.org/tickets/1920-season/dear-jack-dear-louise/|title=Dear Jack, Dear Louise|website=www.arenastage.org}}</ref>

Other works include ''Sullivan & Gilbert'' which was a co-production of the [[Kennedy Center for the Performing Arts]] and the National Arts Centre of Canada and starred Fritz Weaver and Noel Harrison. The play was voted Best Play of 1988 by the Ottawa critics. He wrote a new adaptation of ''Where's Charley?'' for the Kennedy Center, ''Divine Fire'', and a mystery, ''Postmortem''. He co-wrote the 1990 Kennedy Center Honors which appeared on CBS television and received an Emmy Award nomination. Also for television he wrote a pilot for Carol Channing. For film, he wrote ''Lend Me A Tenor'' screenplay for Columbia Pictures, the original draft of the Muppet Movie for Disney Films and ''All Shook Up'' for Touchstone Pictures, directed by Frank Oz.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.kenludwig.com/biography.php|title=Biography www.kenludwig.com}}</ref>

===Other===
In 2014, Ludwig won a Falstaff Award for his book "How to Teach Your Children Shakespeare," published by Penguin Random House, in the category of "Best Book, Publication, or Recording".<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.playshakespeare.com/falstaff-awards/falstaff-awards-2014|title=Falstaff Awards 2014 |first=William|last=Shakespeare|website=www.playshakespeare.com}}</ref><ref>[https://www.washingtonpost.com/news/style-blog/wp/2015/02/06/ken-ludwig-wins-falstaff-award/ "Ken Ludwig wins Falstaff Award" ''Washington Post'', February 6, 2015]</ref> Other honors include two [[Olivier Awards]], three [[Tony Award]] nominations, two Tony Awards, two [[Helen Hayes Award]]s, the [[Edgar Award]], the Edwin Forest Award for Services to the American Theatre, the Pennsylvania Governor's Award for Excellence in the Arts, the Samuel French Award, and an honorary doctorate from [[York University]].

In 2006, ''[[The Times]]'' said of Ludwig "...There is hardly a regional theatre in [[United States|America]] that hasn't a work of his scheduled." {{citation needed|date=January 2015}}His work has been performed in over 25 countries throughout the world, with translations into at least 16 languages.

Ludwig currently serves on the Artistic Advisory Board of Gulfshore Playhouse.{{citation needed|date=March 2016}}
