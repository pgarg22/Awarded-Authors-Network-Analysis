'''Carole Wilkinson''' (born 1950) is an [[Australian]] writer, best known for ''[[Dragonkeeper]]'' (2003).<ref>{{cite web |url=http://orlabs.oclc.org/identities/lccn-n2001-21607 |title= Wilkinson, Carole |access-date=6 April 2010 |author= |date= |year= |publisher=WorldCat Identities |pages= |language= |quote= }}
</ref>

==Career==
Wilkinson was born in [[Derby, England]]. The family [[emigrated]] to [[Australia]] when she was 12 in 1963. She worked as a laboratory assistant until the age of 40, when she decided on a change of career.

To help achieve her goal she studied at a [[tertiary education|tertiary]] level. During her time at University she showed some of her writing to a friend who worked in the [[publishing]] industry. This sample led to a commission to write her first novel for teenagers.

Since that time she has gone on to write numerous books for educational and trade publishers in Australia. She has also written episodes for children's television production.

In 2011 Carole went to St Ignatius College, Adelaide. Classes Year 5 Red, Yr 5 Gold and Year 5 Blue were reading the books as well.

Wilkinson's daughter [[Lili Wilkinson]] also writes for young adults.

==Dragonkeeper==
''[[Dragonkeeper]]'' was published in 2003 by [[Black Dog Books (Australian publisher)|Black Dog Books]]. Set in [[Han dynasty]] [[China]], the story describes the adventures of Ping and an ageing dragon. It has won a number of awards including:
*2003 [[Aurealis Award]] (Young Adults) 
*2004 [[Children's Book Council of Australia]] Book of the Year Award (Young Readers)<ref>{{cite web|url=http://cbca.org.au/2009.htm|title=Previous Winners|access-date=6 April 2010|publisher=Children's Book Council of Australia|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100105155208/http://cbca.org.au/2009.htm|archive-date=5 January 2010|df=dmy}}</ref>
*2004 [[Queensland Premier's Literary Awards|Queensland Premier's Literary Award]] (Best Children's Book)
*2006 [[Kalbacher Klapperschlange]] (German Children's Choice Award)

''Dragonkeeper'' was also shortlisted for the [[New South Wales Premier's Literary Awards]] (2004) for the Patricia Wrightson Prize for Children's Books.<ref>[https://web.archive.org/web/20071005194644/http://www.arts.nsw.gov.au/awards/LiteraryAwards/litawards.htm NSW Premier's Literary Awards]. arts.nsw.gov.au. 26 June 2007</ref> The book secured US Publication and the US version of the book was published in April 2005. It was published in the UK on 30 April 2005.

The first sequel to ''[[Dragonkeeper]]'' was published in September 2005, ''[[Garden of the Purple Dragon]]''. ''Dragon Moon'' followed in 2007. There is also a prequel to ''Dragonkeeper'', a book called ''Dragon Dawn'', about Danzi's adventures before he was put in [[Shaanxi|Huangling]] Mountain.
