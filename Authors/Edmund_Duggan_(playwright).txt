'''Edmund Duggan''' (1862 – 2 August 1938) was an Irish-born actor and playwright who worked in Australia. He is best known for writing a number of plays with [[Bert Bailey]] including ''[[The Squatter's Daughter (play)|The Squatter's Daughter]]'' (1907) and ''[[On Our Selection (1912 play)|On Our Selection]]'' (1912). His solo career was less successful than Bailey's.<ref>[http://nla.gov.au/nla.news-article18515209 "When They Paid A Shilling To Hiss The Villain." ''The Sunday Herald (Sydney)'' 14 Dec 1952: 14]</ref> His sister [[Eugenie Duggan|Eugenie]] was known as "The Queen of Melodrama" and married noted theatre producer [[William Anderson (theatre)|William Anderson]], for whom Duggan frequently worked as an actor, writer and stage manager.

Between 1892 and 1895 Duggan and [[James A. South|South]]'s "Her Majesty's Dramatic Company", toured New South Wales with (''inter alia'') ''[[La Tosca]]'',<ref>{{cite news |url=http://nla.gov.au/nla.news-article138364262 |title=La Tosca |newspaper=[[The Cootamundra Herald]] |location=New South Wales, Australia |date=20 July 1892 |access-date=11 March 2020 |page=5 |via=Trove }}</ref> ''[[All for Gold]]'',<ref>{{cite news |url=http://nla.gov.au/nla.news-article114702482 |title=All for Gold |newspaper=[[The Riverine Herald]] |location=New South Wales, Australia |date=10 October 1892 |access-date=11 March 2020 |page=2 |via=Trove }}</ref> ''[[Greta (play)|Greta]]''.<ref>{{cite news |url=http://nla.gov.au/nla.news-article19023322 |title=Greta |newspaper=[[The Maitland Mercury and Hunter River General Advertiser]] |location=New South Wales, Australia |date=3 June 1893 |access-date=11 March 2020 |page=7 |via=Trove }}</ref> ''[[For the Term of His Natural Life|His Natural Life]]'' and ''[[Robbery Under Arms]]''.<ref>{{cite news |url=http://nla.gov.au/nla.news-article167952268 |title=Victoria Theatre, Bewcastle |newspaper=[[Truth (Sydney newspaper)]] |location=New South Wales, Australia |date=29 December 1895 |access-date=11 March 2020 |page=2 |via=Trove }}</ref> consistently receiving good notices.

Duggan's wife died two years before he did and he was survived by two daughters.<ref>[http://nla.gov.au/nla.news-article12456500 "Mr. Edmund Duggan." ''The Argus (Melbourne)'' 3 Aug 1938: 11]</ref><ref>{{cite news |url=http://nla.gov.au/nla.news-article167296900 |title=The Passing of Ned Duggan |newspaper=[[The Newcastle Sun]] |issue=6438 |location=New South Wales, Australia |date=4 August 1938 |accessdate=6 April 2016 |page=12 |via=National Library of Australia}}</ref>
