'''Aimee Nezhukumatathil''' ({{IPAc-en|ˈ|eɪ|m|i|_|n|ə|ˌ|z|u|k|u|m|ə|ˈ|t|ɒ|t|ɪ|l}};<ref>{{cite AV media|people=Nezhukumatathil, Aimee|date=2016-09-01|title=Aimee Nezhukumatathil: "Letter to the Northern Lights"|medium= |language=en|url=https://soundcloud.com/poets-org/aimee-nezhukumatathil-letter-to-the-northern-lights|access-date=2021-02-14|publisher=Academy of American Poets}}</ref> [[Malayalam|Malayalam Abugida]]: നേഴുകുമാറാത്തിൽ; {{IPA-ml|n̪eɻukuməʈət̪ʰil|lang}};  born in [[Chicago, Illinois]]) is an [[United States|American]] [[poet]] and essayist. Nezhukumatathil draws upon her [[Filipino people|Filipina]] and [[Malayali]] Indian background to give her perspective on love, loss, and land.

==Biography==
Nezhukumatathil received her BA and MFA from the [[Ohio State University]]. In 2016–17 she was the John and Renee Grisham Writer-in-Residence at the [[University of Mississippi]]'s MFA program. She has also taught at the [[Kundiman Retreat]] for Asian American writers.<ref name="auto">{{Cite web|url=http://www.tupelopress.org/nezhuk.shtml|title=Tupelo Press > Author Page > Nezhukumatathil|accessdate=Aug 29, 2019}}</ref> She is professor of English in the University of Mississippi's MFA program. She is married to the writer Dustin Parsons. They live in [[Oxford, Mississippi]], with their two sons.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Aimee Nezhukumatathil {{!}} Extended Bio|url=http://aimeenez.net/extended-bio/|access-date=2019-01-22|website=aimeenez.net}}</ref>

==Work==
She is author of four poetry collections. Her first collection, ''Miracle Fruit'', won the 2003 Tupelo Press Prize and the Global Filipino Literary Award in Poetry, was named the ''ForeWord Magazine'' Book of the Year in Poetry, and was a finalist for the [[Asian American Literary Award]] and the Glasgow Prize. Her second, ''At the Drive-In Volcano'', won the 2007 Balcones Poetry Prize. With [[Ross Gay]], in 2014 she co-authored the epistolary nature chapbook, ''Lace & Pyrite''.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2018-08-29|title=Our Wholeness, Our Togetherness: A Conversation with Aimee Nezhukumatathil & Ross Gay|url=https://aaww.org/our-wholeness-our-togetherness-a-conversation-with-aimee-nezhukumatathil-ross-gay/|access-date=2021-05-26|website=Asian American Writers' Workshop|language=en-US}}</ref> ''Oceanic'' was published in 2018 and won the 2019 [[Mississippi Institute of Arts and Letters]] award for poetry.<ref>{{Cite web|title=on Oceanic by Aimee Nezhukumatathil – The Georgia Review|url=https://thegeorgiareview.com/posts/on-oceanic-by-aimee-nezhukumatathil/|access-date=2021-05-26|website=thegeorgiareview.com|language=en-US}}</ref> She is also the author of the ''New York Times'' bestselling book of essays ''World of Wonders: In Praise of Fireflies, Whale Sharks, and Other Astonishments'', which was published in 2020 by [[Milkweed Editions]] and was a Barnes & Noble Book of the Year, as well as an NPR 2020 Best Book of the Year.<ref>{{Cite book|url=https://www.kirkusreviews.com/book-reviews/aimee-nezhukumatathil/world-of-wonders-fireflies/|title=WORLD OF WONDERS {{!}} Kirkus Reviews|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|title=Hardcover Nonfiction Books - Best Sellers - Books - Jan. 17, 2021 - The New York Times|language=en-US|work=The New York Times|url=https://www.nytimes.com/books/best-sellers/2021/01/17/hardcover-nonfiction/|access-date=2021-05-26|issn=0362-4331}}</ref><ref>{{Cite book|url=https://milkweed.org/book/world-of-wonders|title=World of Wonders|date=2020-01-15|language=en}}</ref>

Of her process, Nezhukumatathil has stated: "I never set out to write a book—even after 4 books, I still find that prospect daunting. Instead, I focus on the individual poems, getting those done week after week. And sometimes some quiet times in between too."<ref>{{Cite web|last=Nezhukumatathil|first=Aimee|date=2019-05-15|title=INTERVIEW WITH Aimee Nezhukumatathil|url=https://fourwayreview.com/interview-with-aimee-nezhukumatathil/|access-date=2021-05-26|website=FOUR WAY REVIEW|language=en-US}}</ref>

Among Nezhukumatathil's awards are a 2020 [[Guggenheim Fellowship]] in poetry, a Mississippi Arts Commission Fellowship grant, inclusion in the ''Best American Poetry'' series, a 2009 [[National Endowment for the Arts]] Literature Fellowship in poetry,<ref>[http://www.nea.gov/grants/recent/09grants/litFellows.html National Endowment for the Arts > 2009 Grant Awards > Literature Fellowships - Poetry] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090711092401/http://www.nea.gov/grants/recent/09grants/litFellows.html |date=2009-07-11 }}</ref> and a Pushcart Prize for the poem "Love in the Orangery". Her poems and essays have appeared in ''New Voices: Contemporary Poetry from the United States'',<ref>{{cite book| title=New Voices: Contemporary Poetry from the United States | editor= H.L. Hix | editor-link= H.L. Hix | publisher= [[Irish Pages]] | year=2008| isbn=978-0-9544257-9-1}}
</ref> ''[[The American Poetry Review]]'', ''FIELD'', ''[[Prairie Schooner]]'', ''[[Poetry (magazine)|Poetry]]'', ''[[New England Review]]'', and ''[[Tin House]]''.<ref name="auto"/> Nezhukumatathil serves as poetry editor for ''[[Orion (magazine)|Orion]]'' magazine.
