'''Hans Magnus Enzensberger''' (born 11 November 1929) is a German [[author]], [[poet]], translator and editor. He has also written under the [[pseudonym]] '''Andreas Thalmayr'''.

== Life ==
Enzensberger was born in 1929 in [[Kaufbeuren]], a small town in [[Bavaria]] and is the eldest of four boys.<ref name="guardian.co.uk">{{cite web|url=https://www.theguardian.com/books/2010/may/15/hans-magnus-enzensberger-interview|title=A life in writing: Hans Magnus Enzensberger|work=[[The Guardian]]|date=15 May 2010|last=Oltermann|first=Philip|access-date=3 August 2014}}</ref> He is part of the last generation of intellectuals whose writing was shaped by first-hand experience of the Third Reich.<ref>Contemporaries include Günter Grass (born in 1927), Martin Walser (1927) and Jürgen Habermas (1929).</ref> The Enzensberger family moved to [[Nuremberg]], the ceremonial birthplace of [[National Socialism]], in 1931.<ref name="guardian.co.uk"/> [[Julius Streicher]], the founder and publisher of [[Der Stürmer]], was their next-door neighbour. Hans Magnus joined the [[Hitler Youth]] in his teens, but was expelled soon afterwards. "I have always been incapable of being a good comrade. I can't stay in line. It's not in my character. It may be a defect, but I can't help it."<ref name="guardian.co.uk"/>

Enzensberger studied [[literature]] and [[philosophy]] at the universities of [[Friedrich-Alexander-University, Erlangen-Nuremberg|Erlangen]], [[Albert Ludwigs University of Freiburg|Freiburg]] and [[University of Hamburg|Hamburg]], and at the [[University of Paris|Sorbonne]] in [[Paris]], receiving his doctorate in 1955 for a thesis about [[Clemens Brentano]]'s poetry.<ref name="griffinpoetryprize.com">{{Cite web|url=http://www.griffinpoetryprize.com/lifetime-recognition.php?t=5|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100410233207/http://www.griffinpoetryprize.com/lifetime-recognition.php?t=5|url-status=dead|title=Griffin Poetry Prize Lifetime Recognition Award profile|archive-date=10 April 2010}}</ref> Until 1957 he worked as a radio editor in [[Stuttgart]]. He participated in several gatherings of [[Group 47]]. Between 1965 and 1975 he lived briefly in the US and Cuba<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.newyorker.com/books/under-review/a-new-translation-of-an-anti-heroic-german-doorstopper-of-1968|title=A New Translation of an Anti-Heroic German Doorstopper of 1968|first=George|last=Blaustein|date=26 November 2018|via=www.newyorker.com}}</ref> and edited the magazine ''Das Kursbuch''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://kursbuch-online.de/uber-das-kursbuch/|title=Über das Kursbuch|access-date=3 August 2014|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140601111845/http://kursbuch-online.de/uber-das-kursbuch/|archive-date=1 June 2014}}</ref> Since 1985 he has been the editor of the prestigious book series ''Die Andere Bibliothek'', published in Frankfurt, and now containing almost 250 titles.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.spiegel.de/kultur/literatur/andere-bibliothek-hans-magnus-enzensberger-will-kuendigen-a-333961.html|title="Andere Bibliothek": Hans Magnus Enzensberger will kündigen|language=de|access-date=3 August 2014|publisher=[[Der Spiegel]]}}</ref> Together with [[Gaston Salvatore]], Enzensberger was the founder of the monthly ''TransAtlantik''.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.literaturportal-bayern.de/werke?task=lpbwork.default&id=63|title=Zeitschriften|website=www.literaturportal-bayern.de}}</ref> His own work has been translated into more than 40 languages.<ref name="griffinpoetryprize.com"/>

Enzensberger is the older brother of the author [[Christian Enzensberger]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.a-e-m-gmbh.com/andremuller/interview%20mit%20hans%20magnus%20enzensberger.html|title=Interview mit Hans Magnus Enzensberger|access-date=3 August 2014|language=de}}</ref>

He lives in [[Munich]].

== Work ==
Enzensberger has a sarcastic, ironic tone in many of his poems.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.literaturkritik.de/public/rezension.php?rez_id=608|title=Mit englischer Behendigkeit Hans Magnus Enzensberger als Nachdichter|last=Schmid|first=Helge|date=November 1999|publisher=literaturkritik.de|language=de|access-date=3 August 2014}}</ref> For example, the poem "Middle Class Blues" consists of various typicalities of middle class life, with the phrase "we can't complain" repeated several times, and concludes with "what are we waiting for?". Many of his poems also feature themes of civil unrest over economic and class based issues. Though primarily a poet and essayist, he also makes excursions into theater, film, opera, radio drama, reportage, translation. He has written novels and several books for children (including ''[[The Number Devil]]'', an exploration of mathematics) and is co-author of a book for [[German as a foreign language]] ''(Die Suche)''. He also invented and collaborated in the construction of a machine which automatically composes poems. It was used during the [[2006 Football World Cup]] to commentate on games.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.sportcal.com/News/PressReleases/89296?sportID=256&size=25|title=Press Release: The Artistic and Cultural Programme of the Federal Government for the 2006 FIFA World Cup Germany|access-date=9 May 2017|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.zeit.de/fussball/wm/poesieautomat/idee|title=Warum man einen Poesie-Automaten baut...|access-date=9 May 2017|language=de|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20060717032950/http://www.zeit.de/fussball/wm/poesieautomat/idee|archive-date=17 July 2006}}</ref>

With [[Irene Dische]] he wrote the libretto for [[Aulis Sallinen]]'s fifth opera ''[[Palatsi (opera)|The Palace]]''.<ref name="thoughts">{{Cite web|url=http://composers.musicfinland.fi/musicfinland/fimic.nsf/0/A0D36DCB163557E6C2257537003FB04D?opendocument|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160122081800/http://composers.musicfinland.fi/musicfinland/fimic.nsf/0/A0D36DCB163557E6C2257537003FB04D?opendocument|url-status=dead|title=Some Thoughts on The Palace by Aulis Sallinen, 1995. At the Music Finland site.|archive-date=22 January 2016}}</ref>

In 2009, Enzensberger received a special Lifetime Recognition Award given by the trustees of the Griffin Trust for Excellence in Poetry,<ref name="griffinpoetryprize.com"/> which also awards the annual [[Griffin Poetry Prize]].
