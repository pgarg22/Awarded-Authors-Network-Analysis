'''Alfred John Church''' (29 January 1829 – 27 April 1912) was an English [[classical scholar]].
[[File:Alfred John Church.png|thumb|Alfred John Church]]
Church was born in London and was educated at [[King's College, London]], and [[Lincoln College, Oxford]]. He took holy orders and was an assistant-master at [[Merchant Taylors' School, Northwood|Merchant Taylors' School]] from 1857-70.  He subsequently served as headmaster of [[Henley-on-Thames]] Royal Grammar School from 1870–73, and then of [[King Edward VI School, Retford]] from 1873-80.<ref>Crockford's Clerical Directory, 1910</ref> From 1880 until 1888 he was professor of [[Latin]] at [[University College, London]].

While at University College in partnership with [[s:Author:William Jackson Brodribb|William Jackson Brodribb]],<ref>{{cite magazine|title=CHURCH, Alfred John|magazine=Who's Who|year=1907|volume= 59|page=333|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=yEcuAAAAYAAJ&pg=PA333}}</ref> he translated [[Tacitus]] and edited [[Pliny the Younger|Pliny's]] Letters [[Epistulae (Pliny)|(''Epistulae'')]]. Church also wrote a number of stories in English re-telling of classical tales and legends for young people (Stories from [[Virgil]], Stories from [[Homer]], etc.). He also wrote much Latin and English verse, and in 1908 published his ''Memories of Men and Books''. Church died in [[Richmond, London|Richmond]], [[Surrey]].
