'''Martín Espada''' (born 1957) is a [[Latino (demonym)|Latino]] poet, and professor at the [[University of Massachusetts Amherst]], where he teaches poetry.  [[Puerto Rico]] has frequently been featured as a theme in his poems.<ref>[http://www.poets.org/poet.php/prmPID/246 Academy of American Poets profile]</ref>

== Life and career ==
Espada was born in [[Brooklyn, New York]]. He was introduced to political activism at an early age by his father, a leader in the Puerto Rican community and the civil rights movement. Espada received a [[Bachelor of Arts|B.A.]] in history from the [[University of Wisconsin–Madison]] and a [[Juris Doctor|J.D.]] from [[Northeastern University (Boston, Massachusetts)]]. For many years, he worked as a tenant lawyer and a supervisor of a legal services program. In 1982, Espada published his first book of political poems, ''The Immigrant Iceboy's Bolero'', featuring photography by his father. This was followed by ''Trumpets from the Islands of their Eviction'' (1987) and ''Rebellion is the Circle of a Lover's Hands''.<ref>[http://www.poetryfoundation.org/archive/poet.html?id=81893 Poetry Foundation profile]</ref> In 2001, he was named the first [[Poet Laureate]] of [[Northampton, Massachusetts]].<ref>[http://billmoyers.com/guest/martin-espada/ Bill Moyers website]</ref>

In 2018, Espada received the [[Ruth Lilly Poetry Prize]], a lifetime achievement award given by the Poetry Foundation to a living U.S. poet that carries a $100,000 prize. Espada was the first Latino recipient of the honor.<ref>{{Cite web | url=https://www.poetryfoundation.org/foundation/press/146607/martin-espada-awarded-2018-ruth-lilly-poetry-prize | title=Martín Espada awarded 2018 Ruth Lilly Poetry Prize | website=poetryfoundation.org | access-date=2018-05-03}}</ref>

In 2009, Espada performed in ''[[The People Speak (film)|The People Speak]],'' a documentary feature film that uses dramatic and musical performances of the letters, diaries, and speeches of everyday Americans, based on historian [[Howard Zinn]]'s ''[[A People's History of the United States]]''.<ref>[http://www.thepeoplespeak.com/pages/credits/ ''The People Speak''] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100713025528/http://www.thepeoplespeak.com/pages/credits/ |date=2010-07-13 }}</ref>

Martín Espada lives in Leverett, Massachusetts
