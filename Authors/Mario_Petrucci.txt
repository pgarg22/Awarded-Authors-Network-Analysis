'''Mario Petrucci''' (born 1958) is a poet, literary translator, educator and broadcaster. He was born in [[Lambeth]], London and trained as a physicist at Selwyn College in the [[University of Cambridge]] and later completed a PhD in vacuum crystal growth at [[University College London]].  He is also an ecologist, having a BA in Environmental Science from [[Middlesex University]]. Petrucci was the first poet to be resident at the [[Imperial War Museum]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.iwm.org.uk/collections/item/object/1030008396|title=Poetry by the Imperial War Museum Poet-in-Residence, Mario Petrucci, 1999|publisher=Imperial War Museum}}</ref> and with [[BBC Radio 3]]
.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/radio3/listenup/newwriting.shtml|title=BBC – Listen Up! – New Music and Writing|last=BBC|publisher=BBC}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://commapress.co.uk/authors/mario-petrucci|title=Mario Petrucci – CommaPress|website=commapress.co.uk}}</ref>
His first major collection, ''Shrapnel and Sheets'' (1996), won a [[Poetry Book Society]] Recommendation.  He has been much involved in radio broadcasting and in the educational sector, in creative writing and literary mentoring.  He has generated many educational resources that incorporate creative writing, science and ecology.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.rlf.org.uk/fellowshipscheme/writing/mission_possible.cfm|title=STUDY SKILLS PROJECT|website=rlf.org.uk}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.writeideas.org.uk/creativescience/|title=Creative Writing <-> Science|website=writeideas.org.uk|access-date=15 June 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080828020659/http://writeideas.org.uk/creativescience/|archive-date=28 August 2008|url-status=dead}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=L-eyJJiBYs4|title=Science poetry: Mario Petrucci|last=New Scientist|date=10 July 2007|via=YouTube}}</ref> Petrucci's poetry has also been deployed in a number of films. For instance, ''Heavy Water: a film for Chernobyl'' and ''Half Life: a Journey to Chernobyl'' were based on his award-winning poetry collection on [[Chernobyl]]. Produced by Seventh Art Productions,<ref>[http://www.heavy-water.co.uk/ 'Heavy Water: a film for Chernobyl' – the award-winning film by Phil Grabsky, David Bickerstaff & Mario Petrucci (Seventh Art Productions, 2006)]</ref> these films have garnered awards such as the [[Cinequest]] as well as screenings on mainstream television and at major cultural venues such as [[Tate Modern]] (in 2007).  2012 saw Petrucci shortlisted for the [[Ted Hughes Award]]
<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://poetrysociety.org.uk/competitions/ted-hughes-award/|title = Ted Hughes Award – the Poetry Society}}</ref> with a vast poetry soundscape (among the largest ever created) entitled ''Tales from the Bridge''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.atomictv.com/TFTB.html|title=Tales form The Bridge|website=atomictv.com}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://poetrysociety.org.uk/poems/from-tales-from-the-bridge/|title = *from* Tales from the Bridge – the Poetry Society}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=53hX3DTrlvM|title=Tales From The Bridge – Martyn Ware|last=KitmonstersTV|date=1 August 2012|via=YouTube}}</ref>
<ref>{{cite web|url=http://illustriouscompany.co.uk/news/2012|title=News 2012 – Illustrious Company|website=illustriouscompany.co.uk}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.kitmonsters.com/blog/tales-from-the-bridge|title=KitMonsters – Tales From The Bridge}}</ref> 
This installation spanned the Thames (on the [[Millennium Bridge, London|Millennium Bridge]], London) as part of the London [[2012 Cultural Olympiad]].  Collaborators for the project included [[Martyn Ware]] ([[The Human League]]) and [[Eric Whitacre]], whose music was used.
