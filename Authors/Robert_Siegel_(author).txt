'''Robert Harold Siegel''' (born 18 August 1939 in [[Oak Park, Illinois]]; died 20 December 2012 in [[South Berwick, Maine]]<ref name="OBIT">[http://www.taskerfh.com/fh/obituaries/obituary.cfm?o_id=1892372&fh_id=12298 Obituary for Robert Harold Siegel], taskerfh.com, access date 24 July 2014</ref>) was an American [[poet]] and novelist. He wrote four books of poetry and five children's novels.

==Life and career==
Siegel graduated from [[Wheaton College (Illinois)|Wheaton College]] in 1961, and received an MA in writing from [[Johns Hopkins University]] and a PhD in [[English literature]] from [[Harvard University]]. Siegel was a professor at [[Dartmouth College]], [[Princeton University]], and [[Goethe University]] in [[Frankfurt, Germany]], and directed the graduate creative writing program for 23 years at the [[University of Wisconsin–Milwaukee]] where he was [[professor emeritus]] of English until his death. His poetry has received awards from the [[National Endowment for the Arts]], the [[Ingram Merrill Foundation]], ''[[Poetry (magazine)|Poetry]]'', ''[[Transatlantic Review]]'', and has been nominated twice for The [[Pushcart Prize]] for Poetry. His children's fiction includes the award-winning ''[[Whalesong (novel)|Whalesong]]''<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.wemtaonline.org/se3bin/clientgenie.cgi |title=Wisconsin's Golden Archer Award:Winners |publisher=Wisconsin Educational Media and Technology Association |accessdate=7 August 2010 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20080724043240/http://www.wemtaonline.org/se3bin/clientgenie.cgi |archivedate=24 July 2008 }}</ref> trilogy, which has been translated into seven languages.

He lived in [[Maine]], where he died of cancer in December 2012.<ref name="OBIT" />

==List of works==
Poetry:
* 1973 "The Beasts & The Elders"
* 1980 "In a Pig's Eye"
* 2005 "The Waters Under the Earth"
* 2006 "A Pentecost of Finches: New and Selected Poems"
* 2013 "Within This Tree of Bones: New and Selected Poems"

Children's Literature:
* 1975 ''A Tale Whose Time Has Come''
* 1980 ''[[Alpha Centauri (novel)|Alpha Centauri]]''
* 1981 ''[[Whalesong (novel)|Whalesong]]'', [[Crossways Books]]
* 1982 '' The Kingdom of Wundle''
* 1994 ''White Whale''
* 1994 ''The Ice at the End of the World''
