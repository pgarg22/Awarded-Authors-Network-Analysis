'''Paula Brackston''' (aka '''P. J. Brackston''', '''P. J. Davy''', and '''Mabli Roberts''') is the [[New York Times Bestselling List|''New York Times'' bestselling]]<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://us.macmillan.com/thewitchsdaughter/paulabrackston/9781250004086|title=The Witch's Daughter {{!}} Paula Brackston {{!}} Macmillan|newspaper=US Macmillan|language=en-US|access-date=2016-12-22}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=http://search.ebscohost.com/login.aspx?direct=true&db=edsglr&AN=edsgcl.315669018&site=eds-live|title=Best Sellers: Combined Print & E-Book Fiction: Sunday, January 20th 2013|date=2013-01-20|work=The New York Times Book Review|access-date=2017-05-30|publisher=The New York Times Company|page=21|id=Gale Document Number: GALE{{!}}A315669018|via=Literature Resource Center}}</ref> author of '''''The Witch's Daughter''''' and other [[historical fantasy]] novels. She also writes the fantasy crime [[Brothers Grimm]] Mystery series under the [[pseudonym]] P. J. Brackston.<ref name=":2">{{Cite web|url=http://www.mybookishways.com/2015/08/catching-up-with-paula-brackston-author-of-lamp-black-wolf-grey.html|title=Catching up with Paula Brackston, author of Lamp Black, Wolf Grey {{!}} My Bookish Ways|website=www.mybookishways.com|access-date=2016-12-22}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=http://search.ebscohost.com/login.aspx?direct=true&db=neh&tg=UI&an=1135495&site=novp-live|title=Author: Brackston, Paula|work=NoveList Plus|access-date=2017-05-30}}</ref>

== Life and writing career ==
Prior to solidifying her career as a fiction writer, Brackston worked as a [[Groom (profession)|groom]] on a racing yard, a travel agent, a secretary, an English teacher, and a [[Goatherd|goat herd]].<ref name=":1">{{Cite web|url=http://civilian-reader.blogspot.com/2013/09/an-interview-with-paula-brackston.html|title=Civilian Reader: An Interview with PAULA BRACKSTON|last=Fergus|first=Stefan|date=2013-09-19|website=Civilian Reader|access-date=2016-12-22}}</ref> She attended [[Lancaster University]], where she received her [[M.A.]] in creative writing. Brackston is also a [[Visiting Lecturer|visiting lecturer]] at the [[University of Wales, Newport]].<ref name=":0" />

She was born in [[Dorset, England]], and grew up in [[Wales]], where she now lives with her partner and their two children.<ref name=":4">{{Cite news|url=https://us.macmillan.com/author/paulabrackston|title=Paula Brackston {{!}} Authors {{!}} Macmillan|work=US Macmillan|access-date=2018-01-24|language=en-US}}</ref> She has lived in [[Brecon Beacons National Park]] in Wales, where ''The Winter Witch'' takes place, and spent six years in [[central London]] near [[Fitzroy Square]], where ''The Midnight Witch'' is set in [[Stuart period|seventeenth-century England]]. Elizabeth Anne Hawksmith, the main character of ''The Witch's Daughter'', lived in Brackston's hometown of Dorset.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.theguardian.com/books/2015/jan/16/science-fiction-novels-january-review-roundup|title=The best science fiction in January – review roundup|last=Brown|first=Eric|date=2015-01-16|work=The Guardian|access-date=2017-05-30|language=en-GB|issn=0261-3077}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.publishersweekly.com/978-1-250-00608-0|title=Fiction Book Review: The Midnight Witch by Paula Brackston|work=PublishersWeekly.com|access-date=2017-05-30|language=en}}</ref><ref name=":0">{{Cite web|url=https://thelitbitch.com/2014/03/25/interview-the-midnight-witch-by-paula-brackston/|title=Interview: The Midnight Witch by Paula Brackston|date=2014-03-25|website=The Lit Bitch|access-date=2016-12-22}}</ref>

Brackston's debut novel, ''The Witch’s Daughter'',<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.kirkusreviews.com/book-reviews/paula-brackston/witchs-daughter/|title=THE WITCH'S DAUGHTER by Paula Brackston|date=October 12, 2010|work=Kirkus Reviews|access-date=May 30, 2017}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.popmatters.com/review/159075-the-witchs-daughter-by-paula-brackston/|title=The Multi-Stranded Tale, 'The Witch's Daughter', Can't Quite Leave Genre Conventions Behind|last=Maine|first=David|date=May 29, 2012|work=PopMatters|access-date=2017-05-30}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.marieclaire.com/culture/a5743/the-witchs-daughter-book-review/|title=What We're Reading: The Witch's Daughter|last=Shipley|first=Kelci|date=2011-01-12|work=Marie Claire|access-date=2017-05-30|language=en}}</ref> was originally published in February 2009 under the title ''The Book of Shadows'' and was intended as the first book in the proposed Shadow Chronicles series, followed by ''The Winter Witch''.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.kirkusreviews.com/book-reviews/paula-brackston/winter-witch/|title=THE WINTER WITCH by Paula Brackston|date=October 29, 2012|work=Kirkus Reviews|access-date=May 30, 2017}}</ref><ref name=":3">{{Cite book|title=The Book of Shadows|last=Brackston|first=Paula|date=2009-02-02|publisher=Snowbooks Ltd|isbn=9781905005970|location=London|language=English}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.theguardian.com/books/2013/nov/20/science-fiction-round-up-reviews|title=Science fiction roundup – reviews|last=Brown|first=Eric|date=2013-11-20|work=The Guardian|access-date=2017-05-30|language=en-GB|issn=0261-3077}}</ref> However, each of the novels are standalones that explore witches and their experiences through different times and settings.<ref name=":1" /> ''The Witch’s Daughter'' spawned a sequel titled ''The'' ''Return of the Witch'' in 2016. In ''Lamp Black, Wolf Grey'', Brackston introduces [[Merlin]] as one of the main characters in the story.<ref name=":2" />
