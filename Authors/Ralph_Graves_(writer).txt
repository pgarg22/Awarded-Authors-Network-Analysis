'''Ralph Augustus Graves''' (October 17, 1924 â€“ June 10, 2013) was an American reporter, editor, and writer. He authored several novels and edited and contributed to famous periodicals such as [[Life (magazine)|''Life'']] magazine.{{Citation needed|date=November 2018}}

==Life==
Graves was born October 17, 1924 in [[Washington, D.C.]] to Elizabeth and Ralph Graves. His father was an editor of ''[[National Geographic Society|National Geographic]]'' magazine and ''[[Washington Times (1894-1939)|The Washington Times]]''.<ref>''Who's Who in the Nation's Capitol'' Consolidated Publishing Company, 1921</ref>

His mother later married Francis Sayres, the last United States High Commissioner to the [[Philippines]] and the family resided there in the U.S. Embassy during his term. Graves was a high school student at the time of their arrival. They were evacuated from the embassy on Christmas Eve 1941 to the headquarters of General [[Douglas MacArthur]] in [[Corregidor Island|Corregidor]], living in underground bunkers until escaping to Australia by [[submarine]] early in 1942.<ref>[http://www.wiredforbooks.org/ralphgraves/index.htm Interview with Ralph Graves from Wired for Books] Audio Interview with Ralph Graves</ref>

Graves served in the [[United States Army Air Forces]] during the [[World War II]]. His service began in 1943 and concluded in 1945. Graves attained the rank of [[sergeant]] before leaving the armed forces.{{Citation needed|date=November 2018}}

Graves attended [[Williams College]] and later, [[Harvard University]] where he was a member of the [[Phi Beta Kappa Society|Phi Beta Kappa]] honor society. He earned his [[Bachelor of Arts|B.A.]] from Harvard, being graduated in 1948.{{Citation needed|date=November 2018}}

Graves married Patricia Monser in 1950. Graves' marriage to Monser produced two children before the couple divorced. Graves remarried to Eleanor Mackenzie in 1958 and they also had two children.{{Citation needed|date=November 2018}}

Graves lived in [[New York City]] and maintained a second home in [[Sarasota, Florida|Sarasota]], Florida.{{Citation needed|date=November 2018}}

==Professional career==

Graves was employed as a writer and reporter at ''Life'' magazine from 1948 to 1958. He steadily rose through the ranks at ''Life'' magazine, serving as the articles editor, the assistant managing editor, and the managing editor before leaving the publication in 1972. Graves moved to Time, Inc. where he served as the senior staff editor and editorial director, among other positions. Graves was the final Managing Editor of ''Life'' Magazine when it issued the final Edition on December 29, 1972.  His comments celebrated Life and reflected the sense of loss of a generation of American's at the loss of Life. Graves was a writer and held a chair on the Citizens' Crime Commission of New York.{{Citation needed|date=November 2018}}

==Literary works==

Graves' writing career began in 1949 with the publication of ''Thanks for the Ride''. His second book, ''The Lost Eagles'', was published in 1955.  This is a historical novel in which a fictional Roman, Severus Varus, searches for the legionary emblems lost by his kinsman, [[Quinctilius Varus]], at the [[Battle of the Teutoburg Forest]].{{Citation needed|date=November 2018}}

After a long break from writing books during his time at Life magazine, Graves resumed his literary career with the publication of ''August People'' in 1985. This work was followed in 1989 by ''Share of Honor'', an in-depth look into Graves' personal involvement with the [[Empire of Japan|Japanese]] occupation of the [[Philippines]] during World War II.{{Citation needed|date=November 2018}}

Graves' novel, ''Orion: The Story of a Rape''. was a fictionalized account of the rape of his daughter that occurred in [[Manhattan]] in 1983.{{Citation needed|date=November 2018}}

Graves died of [[Renal failure|kidney failure]] on June 10, 2013, at his home in Manhattan.<ref>[https://www.nytimes.com/2013/06/16/business/media/ralph-graves-a-managing-editor-of-life-magazine-dies-at-88.html?ref=obituaries Ralph Graves, a Managing Editor of Life Magazine, Dies at 88].
