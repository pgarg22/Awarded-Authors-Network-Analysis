'''Susan Higginbotham''' is an American [[historical fiction]] author and attorney. She has written on the [[Middle Ages]] and the [[Wars of the Roses]].

==Personal life==
Susan Higginbotham earned her [[undergraduate]] degree from [[Virginia Commonwealth University]], and her [[master's degree]] in English literature from [[Hunter College]] in [[New York City]].<ref name=PBTP/><ref name=CT/> She received her law degree from [[Campbell University]], and began working for a legal publisher, a position she still holds today.<ref name=PBTP>{{Cite web| last = | first = | title =Interview with Susan Higginbotham, author of The Stolen Crown and Giveaway | pages =| newspaper = Peeking Between the Pages | location = | date = 26 March 2010 | url =http://www.peekingbetweenthepages.com/2010/03/interview-with-susan-higginbotham.html | accessdate = 5 January 2010}}</ref><ref name=OW>{{cite web |url=http://www.susanhigginbotham.com/about.html |title=About me |author=Susan Higginbotham |publisher=Official website |accessdate=5 January 2011}}</ref>

Higginbotham is married, with two children. They live in [[Brunswick, Maryland]].<ref name=PBTP/><ref name=CT>{{cite web |url=http://favoritepastimes.blogspot.com/2009/08/interview-with-susan-higginbotham.html |title=Interview with Susan Higginbotham |author=Cindy Thomson |publisher=Favorite PASTimes.blogspot.com|accessdate=5 January 2011}}</ref>

==Publishing career==
Higginbotham began working on her first novel, ''The Traitor's Wife'' in 2003, and after reading some articles on [[self-publishing]], she self-published in 2005.<ref name=ST>{{cite web |url=http://steventill.com/2009/04/14/interview-with-author-susan-higginbotham/ |title=Interview with Author Susan Higginbotham |author= Till, Steven and Susan Higginbotham |publisher=StevenTill.com |accessdate=5 January 2011}}</ref> It won [[ForeWord (magazine)|''ForeWord'' magazine]]'s 2005 Silver Award for historical fiction and the 2008 Independent Publisher Book Awards' Gold Medal for Historical/Military Fiction.<ref name=PBTP/> According to [[WorldCat]], the book is held in 252 libraries.<ref name=wc>[http://www.worldcat.org/wcidentities/lccn-n2008-61456  WorldCat identities record]</ref> It is a fictional account of the life of [[Eleanor de Clare]], wife of [[Hugh le Despenser the Younger]].<ref name=CAO>{{cite web |url=http://infotrac.galegroup.com/itw/infomark/28/855/16070586w16/purl=rc1_CA_0_H1000199202&dyn=3!xrn_1_0_H1000199202?sw_aep=clic_stthomas |title=Susan Higginbotham  |author=|work=Contemporary Authors Online |date=2011 |accessdate=31 October 2013}} {{Subscription needed}}</ref> [[Sourcebooks]] contacted her in 2008, and offered to republish it,<ref name=ST/> resulting in it reaching number 8 in ''[[The Boston Globe]]''{{'}}s list of fiction best sellers.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.highbeam.com/doc/1P2-30035116.html |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131110125202/http://www.highbeam.com/doc/1P2-30035116.html |url-status=dead |archive-date=November 10, 2013 |title=Best Sellers |work=[[The Boston Globe]] |date=November 6, 2011 |access-date=October 31, 2013}} {{Subscription needed}}</ref> Her next novel was ''Hugh and Bess'' (2007), a sequel to her first novel, depicting the life of Eleanor and Hugh's eldest son, [[Hugh le Despencer, Baron le Despencer (1338)|Hugh]]. It was reissued in August 2009.<ref name=CT/> Her next book, ''The Stolen Crown'', was published in 2010 and is a fictional depiction of [[Catherine Woodville, Duchess of Buckingham]]. Her 2011 novel, ''The Queen of Last Hopes'', features [[Margaret of Anjou]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.highbeam.com/doc/1G1-268309496.html |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131110125159/http://www.highbeam.com/doc/1G1-268309496.html |url-status=dead |archive-date=November 10, 2013 |title=The Queen of Last Hopes |work=[[Renaissance Magazine]] |date=July 1, 2011 |last=Obeso |first=Dionne |access-date=October 31, 2013}} {{Subscription needed}}</ref> Higginbotham's most recent works, ''Hanging Mary'', ''The First Lady and the Rebel,'' and ''John Brown's Women,'' are set in 19th-century America.
