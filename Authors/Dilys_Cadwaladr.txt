'''Dilys Cadwaladr''' (19 March 1902 – January 1979<ref>Eigra Lewis Roberts, ''Merch yr oriau mawr''. Tŷ ar y Graig, 1981 (Welsh language).</ref>) was a [[Welsh language|Welsh-language]] poet and fiction writer. Her work also gained readers in English translation.

==First female crowned bard==
Dilys Cadwaladr is notable for being the first woman to win the [[Crowning of the Bard|Crown]] at the [[National Eisteddfod of Wales]]. This she achieved in 1953 at [[Rhyl]].<ref>{{Cite web |title=Some facts and figures regarding Gorsedd ceremonies |url=https://museum.wales/957/?display_mode=text |website=National Museum Wales |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20171107165809/https://museum.wales/957/?display_mode=text |archivedate=7 November 2017}}</ref><ref>[https://www.peoplescollection.wales/items/19253 A photograph of the event. Retrieved 1 February 2020.]</ref>

Her story "The Foolish Maid" appears in English translation in the collection ''My Heart on My Sleeve'' (Honno Welsh Women's Press, [[Aberystwyth]], 2013), which remained in print in 2020.<ref>[https://www.honno.co.uk/?s=My+heart+on+my+sleeve&search=Search Retrieved 19 January 2020.]</ref> Earlier, her story "The Divorce" had appeared in English in 1928 in the periodical ''The Welsh Outlook".<ref>[https://www.iberlibro.com/primera-edicion/Welsh-Outlook-Monthly-Journal-National-Social/20280970750/bd Vol. 15, No. 5, 1928. Retrieved 19 January 2020.]</ref>

==Personal life==
Cadwaladr had a close relationship with the elderly poet [[Dewi Emrys]] (1881–1952), by whom she had a daughter in 1930. Dilys Cadwaladr lived on [[Bardsey Island]] for several years in the 1940s, as a farmer and as the schoolteacher to the island's children.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Enlli and the Arts |url=http://www.bardsey.org/the-arts |website=Bardsey Island Trust |accessdate=18 September 2016}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |title=Dewi Emrys and his lost chair |url=http://www.s4c.cymru/caban/?p=9616&lang=en |website=[[S4C]] |date=25 July 2014}}</ref>
