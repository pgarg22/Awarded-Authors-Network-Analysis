'''Leigh Bardugo''' ({{lang-he|לי ברדוגו}}) is an [[Israel]]i-American fantasy author. She is best known for her [[Young adult fiction|young adult]] [[Characters of the Grishaverse|Grishaverse]] novels, which include the ''[[Six of Crows]]'' duology, the ''[[Shadow and Bone]] trilogy'', and the ''[[King of Scars]]'' series. She also received acclaim for her [[paranormal]] fantasy adult debut, ''[[Ninth House]]''. The ''Shadow and Bone'' and ''Six of Crows'' series have been adapted into [[Shadow and Bone (TV series)|''Shadow and Bone'']] by [[Netflix]] and ''Ninth House'' will be adapted by [[Amazon Studios]]; Bardugo is an executive producer on both works.

==Early life==
Bardugo was born in [[Jerusalem]], and grew up in [[Los Angeles]], California, U.S.<ref name="PW">{{cite web|last=Jones|first=Michael M.|title=Spring 2012 Flying Starts: Leigh Bardugo|url=https://www.publishersweekly.com/pw/by-topic/authors/profiles/article/52823-spring-2012-flying-starts-leigh-bardugo.html|work=[[Publishers Weekly]]|date=June 29, 2012|access-date=February 22, 2018}}</ref> where she was raised by her grandparents.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Lee|first1=Stephan|title=Veronica Roth and Leigh Bardugo in conversation about YA lit|url=http://www.ew.com/article/2013/06/04/veronica-roth-leigh-bardugo-divergent-siege-and-storm-interview/|website=[[Entertainment Weekly]]|access-date=May 25, 2018|date=June 4, 2013}}</ref> She is a [[secular Judaism|non-practicing Jew]] and of [[Sephardic Jewish]] ([[Moroccan Jews|Moroccan-Jewish]]) descent on her father's side,<ref>{{Cite web|date=2015-07-12|title=Leigh Bardugo: Grounded in fantasy|url=https://cebudailynews.inquirer.net/63170/63170|access-date=2020-06-11|website=INQUIRER.net|language=en}}</ref> and of [[Ashkenazi Jewish]]  ([[Russian-Jewish]] and [[Lithuanian Jews|Lithuanian-Jewish]]) descent on her mother's side.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.deseret.com/2015/9/29/20573210/young-adult-author-talks-religion-teens-and-the-message-of-her-popular-fiction#leigh-bardugo-at-the-edinburgh-international-book-festival-2014-24nd-august-2014-picture-by-russell-g-sneddon-writer-pictures-writer-pictures-via-ap-images|title=Young adult author talks religion, teens and the message of her popular fiction|work=Deseret News|first=Chandra|last=Johnson|date=September 25, 2015|access-date=February 16, 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://lbardugo.tumblr.com/post/78911893926/hi-leigh-bardugo-if-you-married-would-you-change|title=Anonymous asked|author=Leigh Bardugo|via=Tumblr|date=March 7, 2014|access-date=February 16, 2020}}</ref> 

She attended [[Yale University]], graduating with a degree in English in the spring of 1997.<ref name="PW" /><ref>{{cite web|url=https://yalealumnimagazine.com/blog_posts/1187|title=Leigh Bardugo '97: A novelist's fantasy comes true|website=Yale Alumni Magazine|publisher=[[Yale University]]|date=September 13, 2012|access-date=April 26, 2018}}</ref> She was a member of the [[Wolf's Head (secret society)|Wolf's Head]] secret society.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Leigh Bardugo On 'Ninth House'|url=https://www.npr.org/2019/10/06/767636941/leigh-bardugo-on-ninth-house|access-date=2021-04-08|website=NPR.org|language=en}}</ref> Before publishing her first novel, she worked in copywriting and journalism, as well as makeup and special effects.<ref>{{cite web|last=Jones|first=Michael M.|title=Spring 2012 Flying Starts: Leigh Bardugo|url=https://www.publishersweekly.com/pw/by-topic/authors/profiles/article/52823-spring-2012-flying-starts-leigh-bardugo.html|work=Publishers Weekly|date=June 29, 2012|access-date=February 22, 2018}}</ref><ref name="VF">{{cite web|last=Ritz|first=Jessica|title=Why You Should Be Reading Leigh Bardugo's Y.A. Novels—Even if You're an Adult|url= https://www.vanityfair.com/culture/2015/09/leigh-bardugo-six-of-crows-grisha-trilogy-interview |work=[[Vanity Fair (magazine)|Vanity Fair]]|date=September 25, 2015|access-date=February 22, 2018}}</ref>

==Career==
Bardugo's debut novel, ''[[Shadow and Bone]]'', the first book in the Grisha trilogy, was published in 2012 by [[Macmillan Publishers|Macmillan]].<ref>{{cite web |last1= Gentry |first1= Baihley |title= Unstoppable: YA Fantasy Author Leigh Bardugo on World-Building and Having Faith in Your Abilities {{!}} The WD Interview |url= http://www.writersdigest.com/editor-blogs/there-are-no-rules/interviews/the-wd-interview-ya-fantasy-author-leigh-bardugo|website=Writer's Digest|access-date=26 May 2018|date=February 2, 2018}}</ref> ''Shadow and Bone'' was nominated for the Romantic Times Book Award and the South Carolina Children's Book Award, named an Indie Next List Book, and reviewed in The New York ''[[The New York Times|Times]]''.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://us.macmillan.com/shadowandbone/leighbardugo/9781250027436/|title=''Shadow and Bone''|website=Macmillan Publishers |access-date=February 24, 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|last=Taylor|first=Laini|title= A Gleam Off the Samovar|url= https://www.nytimes.com/2012/06/17/books/review/shadow-and-bone-by-leigh-bardugo.html|work=[[The New York Times]]|date=June 15, 2012|access-date=February 24, 2018}}</ref> The novel hit #8 on [[The New York Times Best Seller list|''The New York Times'' Best Seller list]],<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.nytimes.com/books/best-sellers/2012/06/24/chapter-books/|title=Children's Chapter Books: Best Sellers: June 24, 2012|work=The New York Times|date=June 24, 2012|access-date=April 26, 2018}}</ref> and had been optioned for film by [[David Heyman]] and [[DreamWorks Pictures|DreamWorks]].<ref>{{cite web|last=Breznican|first=Anthony|title=''Harry Potter'' producer to make ''Shadow and Bone''|url=http://www.ew.com/article/2012/09/12/dreamworks-harry-pottershadow-and-bone/|work=Entertainment Weekly|date=September 12, 2012|access-date=February 22, 2018}}</ref> The other books in the trilogy, ''Siege and Storm'' and ''Ruin and Rising'', were published by Macmillan in 2013 and 2014 respectively. Bardugo defines ''Shadow and Bone'' genre as ''Tsarpunk'' - a fantasy with inspiration from early 19th century Russia.<ref>{{Cite web|last=TheBookAddictedGirl|date=2013-06-20|title=Shadow and Bone by Leigh Bardugo - why should you read it?|url=http://www.theguardian.com/books/2013/jun/20/teen-book-club-why-read-shadow-and-bone|access-date=2021-08-28|website=the Guardian|language=en}}</ref>

The ''[[Six of Crows]]'' duology (''Six of Crows'' and ''Crooked Kingdom'') was published by Macmillan in 2015 and 2016. It is set in the same universe as the Grisha trilogy (sometimes collectively referred to as the "Grishaverse"). ''Six of Crows'' was named a New York ''Times'' Notable Book and an ALA-YALSA Top Ten Pick of 2016.<ref name="auto">{{cite web|last=Heldman|first=Breanne|title= DC Comics favorites becoming YA novels |url= http://ew.com/article/2016/03/31/dc-comics-ya-novels-wonder-woman/|work=Entertainment Weekly|date=March 31, 2016|access-date=February 24, 2018}}</ref> ''The Language of Thorns'', a collection of Grisha fairy tales and folk tales, was published by Macmillan in 2017.

Bardugo then wrote the first book in the DC Icons series, which are novelizations of [[DC Comics]]' biggest superheroes;<ref name="auto"/> her ''Wonder Woman: Warbringer'' was published by [[Penguin Random House]] in 2017.

In 2019, Bardugo published her first adult novel, ''Ninth House'', which won the 2019 Goodreads Choice Award for best fantasy novel.<ref>{{cite web |title=Goodreads Choice Awards 2019 |url=https://www.goodreads.com/choiceawards/best-fantasy-books-2019 |website=Goodreads |access-date=26 October 2020}}</ref>

Bardugo also has essays and short stories in anthology collections such as ''Last Night, A Superhero Saved My Life'', ''Slasher Girls and Monster Boys'', and ''Summer Days and Summer Nights''. Her books have been translated into 22 languages and published in over 50 countries.<ref name="VF"/>

Bardugo appeared on a Grishaverse panel alongside showrunner Heisserer at [[New York Comic Con]] in October 2020.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.polygon.com/entertainment/2020/9/9/21418267/new-york-comic-con-2020-schedule-panels-modok-animaniacs-walking-dead|title=New York Comic Con 2020 unveils more panels from its all-digital con|journal=Polygon|first=Susana|last=Polo|date=17 September 2020|access-date=18 September 2020}}</ref>

==Adaptations==
{{Main article|Shadow and Bone (TV series)}}
In September 2012, [[DreamWorks Pictures|DreamWorks]] acquired the movie rights to ''Shadow and Bone'' with [[David Heyman]] and Jeffrey Clifford producing,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.hollywoodreporter.com/heat-vision/dreamworks-shadow-bone-lands-writer-421048 |title=DreamWorks' ''Shadow and Bone'' Lands Writer |website=The Hollywood Reporter |date=2013-02-12 |access-date=2015-04-01}}</ref> although this project was not realized. In January 2019, [[Netflix]] ordered an eight-episode series based on the ''Shadow and Bone'' and ''Six of Crows'' book series.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://deadline.com/2019/01/netflix-orders-shadow-and-bone-series-leigh-bardugo-grishaverse-fantasy-novels-1202532783/|title=Netflix Orders ''Shadow And Bone'' Series Based On Leigh Bardugo's Grishaverse Novels From Eric Heisserer & Shawn Levy|first1=Nellie |last1=Andreeva|first2=Denise|last2=Petski|date=January 10, 2019|website=[[Deadline Hollywood]]|access-date=January 15, 2019}}</ref> 

In October 2019, [[Amazon Studios]] announced that it would adapt ''Ninth House''. Bardugo is set to executive produce the project alongside Pouya Shahbazian.<ref>{{cite journal|url=https://deadline.com/2019/10/grishaverse-authorleigh-bardugo-ninth-house-amazon-studios-tv-series-development-1202756743/|title=Grishaverse Author's ''Ninth House'' Novel To Be Developed As TV Series By Amazon Studios|journal=Deadline Hollywood|first=Nellie |last=Andreeva|date=October 10, 2019|access-date=November 1, 2019}}</ref>

==Personal life==
In the acknowledgements section of ''Six of Crows'', Bardugo reveals she suffers from [[osteonecrosis]] and sometimes needs to use a [[Walking stick|cane]]; this was a source of inspiration for one of the story's six [[Protagonist|protagonists]], master thief and gang boss Kaz Brekker, who uses a cane.<ref>{{cite book |last=Bardugo |first=Leigh |date=February 6, 2018 |title=Six of Crows |language=en |publisher=Square Fish; Reprint edition |isbn=978-1250076960}}</ref>

Bardugo was a singer in the band Captain Automatic in 2006-2007.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://captainautomatic.bandcamp.com/ |title=Bandcamp - Captain Automatic |access-date=February 13, 2018}}</ref>
