'''Lasus of Hermione''' ({{lang-el|Λάσος ὁ Ἑρμιονεύς}}) was a [[Greek lyric poet]] of the 6th century BC from the city of [[Ermioni|Hermione]] in the [[Argolis_(ancient_region)#Argolis|Argolid]].  He is known to have been active at [[Athens]] under the reign of the [[Peisistratus|Peisistratids]]. [[Pseudo-Plutarch]]'s ''De Musica'' credits him with innovations in the [[dithyramb]] hymn. According to [[Herodotus]], Lasus also exposed [[Onomacritus]]'s forgeries of the [[oracle]]s of [[Musaeus of Athens|Musaeus]].<ref>[[Herodotus]] 7.6.3-5</ref>
