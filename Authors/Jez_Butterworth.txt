'''Jeremy "Jez" Butterworth''' (born March 1969) is an English playwright, screenwriter, and film director. He has written screenplays in collaboration with his brothers, [[John-Henry Butterworth|John-Henry]] and Tom.

==Life and career==
Butterworth was born in London, England. His brother Steve is a producer and brothers Tom and [[John-Henry Butterworth|John-Henry]] are also writers. He attended [[Verulam School|Verulam Comprehensive School]], [[St Albans]] and [[St John's College, Cambridge]].

Butterworth's play ''[[Mojo (play)|Mojo]]'', which premiered at the [[Royal Court Theatre]] in 1995, won the 1996 [[Laurence Olivier Award|Laurence Olivier]],<ref>[https://officiallondontheatre.com/olivier-awards/winners/olivier-winners-1996/ "Olivier Awards, 1996"] officiallondontheatre.com, retrieved 10 February 2018</ref> an [[Evening Standard Award|Evening Standard]], The Writer's Guild and the [[George Devine]] Awards, and the Critic's Circle Award. Butterworth wrote and directed the film adaptation of ''Mojo'', released in 1997. The film featured [[Harold Pinter]].<ref>[http://www.bfi.org.uk/films-tv-people/4ce2b7fd38129 " ''Mojo'' Film"] bfi.org, retrieved 10, February 2018</ref> A major influence on Butterworth's work is 2005 [[Nobel Prize in Literature|Nobel Literature Laureate]] Harold Pinter: "I know and admire Harold Pinter enormously. He has a ginormous influence on me. Conversations with him have inspired my work."<ref name=Piepenburg/>

He directed and co-wrote ''[[Birthday Girl]]'' (2001), which was produced by his brother Steve and starred [[Nicole Kidman]].<ref>Arnoldi, Matt. [https://www.bbc.co.uk/films/2002/06/10/birthday_girl_2002_review.shtml "Film review - 'Birthday Girl'"] BBC.co.uk., 20 June 2002</ref>

Butterworth received positive reviews of his play ''The Night Heron'', which premiered in the West End at the [[Royal Court Theatre]] in 2002. ''The Guardian'' reviewer wrote: "Can a play be simultaneously very good and very bad? I believe so."<ref>Billington, Michael. [https://www.theguardian.com/stage/2002/apr/19/theatre.artsfeatures "Theatre. 'The Night Heron'"] ''The Guardian'', 18 April 2002</ref> ''The Winterling'' also ran at the Royal Court in 2006. The britishtheatreguide wrote: "''The Winterling'' can be a difficult play but contains rich veins of comedy."<ref>Fisher, Philip. [http://www.britishtheatreguide.info/reviews/winterling-rev "Reviews. ''The Winterling''"] britishtheatreguide, 2006, retrieved 9 February 2018</ref>

In May 2007 Butterworth received the [[E. M. Forster Award]] from the [[American Academy of Arts and Letters]].

His play ''Parlour Song''<ref>Butterworth, J. (2009) ''Parlour Song'', [[Nick Hern Books]], London. {{ISBN|978-1-84842-026-7}}</ref> opened to "rave reviews" at the [[Atlantic Theatre Company]], [[Off-Broadway]] in March 2008.<ref name=Piepenburg>{{Cite news |first=Erik |last=Piepenburg |title=An Edge-of-Town Story as Simple as the Blues |url=https://www.nytimes.com/2008/03/23/theater/23piep.html |work=The New York Times|date=23 March 2008 |access-date=23 March 2008}}</ref> The [[Almeida Theatre]] presented its European première in March 2009.

Butterworth's fourth play for the Royal Court Theatre was ''[[Jerusalem (play)|Jerusalem]]'', which premiered in July 2009 to positive reviews. Described as a "contemporary vision of life in [England's] green and pleasant land", ''Jerusalem'' was the second important Butterworth production in London in 2009.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2009/07/20/theater/20time.html?ref=arts|work=[[the New York Times]]|title=Time, and the Green and Pleasant Land|first=Ben|last=Brantley|date=19 July 2009}}</ref> The production starred [[Mark Rylance]] as Johnny Byron, and featured [[Mackenzie Crook]] as Ginger in a supporting role. It was a sell-out at the Royal Court, won the [[Evening Standard Theatre Award]] and [[Critics' Circle Theatre Award]] for the best play of 2009 and, with the same cast, transferred to the [[Apollo Theatre]] on Shaftesbury Avenue in January 2010. ''Jerusalem'' opened on Broadway in April 2011, with many of the original UK cast.<ref name=jerusalem>[http://www.playbill.com/production/jerusalem-music-box-theatre-vault-0000013724# " 'Jerusalem' Broadway"] Playbill, retrieved 9 February 2018</ref> It returned to London later that year, again playing at the Apollo. In January 2014 Jerusalem opened at the [[San Francisco Playhouse]],<ref>{{cite web|title=San Francisco Playhouse|url=https://www.sfplayhouse.org/sfph/jerusalem/|publisher=San Francisco Playhouse|access-date=20 June 2020}}</ref> where it also received rave reviews.<ref>{{cite web|title=SF Gate|date=27 January 2014|url=http://www.sfgate.com/performance/article/Jerusalem-review-Epic-exhausting-with-5177216.php|publisher=SF Gate|access-date=14 May 2014}}</ref> ''Jerusalem'' was nominated for the 2011 [[Tony Award]], Play<ref>{{cite news| url=https://www.theguardian.com/theguardian/2011/may/14/saturday-interview-jez-butterworth | location=London | work=The Guardian | first=Aida | last=Edemariam | title=The Saturday interview: Jez Butterworth | date=14 May 2011}}</ref> and Mark Rylance won the 2011 Tony Award for Best Performance by an Actor in a Play.<ref name=jerusalem/>

Jez and John-Henry Butterworth were named recipients of the [[Writers Guild of America West]]'s 2011 [[Paul Selvin Award]] for their screenplay for the film ''[[Fair Game (2010 film)|Fair Game]]'' (2010), directed by [[Doug Liman]] and starring [[Naomi Watts]] and [[Sean Penn]].

On 26 October 2012, Butterworth's play ''The River'' opened at the Royal Court Theatre, starring [[Dominic West]], [[Laura Donnelly]] and [[Miranda Raison]], with an appearance by [[Gillian Saker]].<ref>{{cite book|last1=Butterworth|first1=Jez|title=The River|date=2012|publisher=Nick Hern Books|location=London|isbn=9781848422896}}</ref><ref>{{cite journal|last1=Lawson|first1=Mark|author-link=Mark Lawson|title=How The River shows the power of a theatre programme|journal=[[The Guardian]]|date=30 October 2012}}</ref> ''The River'' had its US premiere on Broadway at the [[Circle in the Square Theatre]] in a limited engagement in October 2014, starring [[Hugh Jackman]] and directed by [[Ian Rickson]].<ref>Hetrick, Adam. [http://www.playbill.com/news/article/190945-The-River-Starring-Tony-Winner-Hugh-Jackman-Will-Open-at-Broadways-Circle-in-the-Square-This-Fall "''The River'', Starring Tony Winner Hugh Jackman, Will Open at Broadway's Circle in the Square This Fall"] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140512214908/http://www.playbill.com/news/article/190945-The-River-Starring-Tony-Winner-Hugh-Jackman-Will-Open-at-Broadways-Circle-in-the-Square-This-Fall |date=12 May 2014 }} playbill.com, 9 May 2014</ref> Reception was positive, with London critics finding the work "lyrical"; "beautifully written" and "suffuse[d] with wonder and beauty".<ref>{{cite web|title=High praise for Butterworth play|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/entertainment-arts-20120810|work=BBC News|access-date=1 March 2018|date=29 October 2012}}</ref>

===''The Ferryman''===
Butterworth's play ''[[The Ferryman (play)|The Ferryman]]'' opened at the [[Royal Court Theatre]] in April 2017. Directed by [[Sam Mendes]],<ref name="playwright">{{cite web |last=Hewis |first=Ben |title=Sam Mendes to direct Jez Butterworth play in new Royal Court season |website=WhatsOnStage.com |date=31 October 2017 |url=http://www.whatsonstage.com/london-theatre/news/sam-mendes-to-direct-new-jez-butterworth-play_42131.html | access-date=April 5, 2017}}</ref> it became the fastest selling play in the Royal Court Theatre's history.<ref>{{cite news | url=https://www.londontheatre.co.uk/theatre-news/news/jez-butterworths-the-ferryman-transfers-to-the-gielgud-theatre |title=Jez Butterworth's The Ferryman transfers to The Gielgud Theatre | publisher=London Theatre | work=londontheatre.co.uk | date=8 February 2017 | access-date=9 February 2018}}</ref> Set in rural Derry in 1981 and focussing on the events surrounding the deaths of the [[1981 Irish hunger strike|IRA hunger strikers]], it received 15 five-star reviews, including all the major UK papers. ''[[The Irish Times]]'' said "Although Butterworth is English, ''The Ferryman'' feels like a thoroughly Irish play, not only because there is not a single false note in the dialogue."<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.irishtimes.com/news/world/uk/north-dominating-more-than-political-stage-in-london-1.3145942|title=North dominating more than political stage in London|last=Staunton|first=Denis|website=The Irish Times|language=en|access-date=2019-12-04}}</ref> The ''[[HuffPost|Huffington Post]]'' called it "one of the two or three greatest plays of the decade". However, ''[[The Guardian]]'' 's Sean O'Hagan wrote "I'm from Northern Ireland and it doesn't ring true" and it was "so close to a cultural stereotype as to be offensive".<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.theguardian.com/commentisfree/2017/jul/16/jez-butterworth-the-ferryman-irish-stereotypes-sam-mendes |work=the Guardian|title=Critics loved ''The Ferryman''. But I'm from Northern Ireland, and it doesn't ring true|first=Sean|last=O'Hagan|date=16 July 2017}}</ref> Two weeks later ''The Irish Times'' printed an article by ''[[Father Ted]]'' actor Gerard Lee<ref>{{cite web|url=http://newisland.ie/product/forsaken/|title=Forsaken|website=New Island Books|access-date=28 January 2019}}</ref> entitled "In defence of ''The Ferryman''" which challenged negative comments, calling the play "layered and powerful".<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.irishtimes.com/culture/stage/in-defence-of-the-ferryman-by-jez-butterworth-1.3173470|work=the Irish Times|title=In defence of The Ferryman by Jez Butterworth|first=Gerard|last=Lee|date=1 August 2017}}</ref>

''The Ferryman'' won the 2017 [[Evening Standard Theatre Awards|''Evening Standard'' Award]] for Best Play, the 2018 [[Critics' Circle Theatre Award|Critics' Circle Award]] for Best New Play, the 2018 [[WhatsOnStage Awards|WhatsOnStage Award]] for Best New Play and the 2018 [[Laurence Olivier Award for Best New Play|Olivier Award for Best New Play]].<ref>[https://officiallondontheatre.com/olivier-awards/winners/olivier-winners-2018/ "Olivier Awards 2018"] officiallondontheatre.com, retrieved June 3, 2019</ref> It has played for over 350 performances at the Gielgud Theatre and transferred to Broadway in October 2018. The play won the 2019 [[Drama Desk Award]] for Outstanding Play and Tony Award for Best Play.<ref>Fierberg, Ruthie. [http://www.playbill.com/article/tootsie-hadestown-and-the-ferryman-lead-2019-drama-desk-award-winners# " 'Tootsie', 'Hadestown', and 'The Ferryman' Lead 2019 Drama Desk Award Winners"] Playbill, June 2, 2019</ref>
