'''Rod Jellema''' (1927â€“2018) was an [[United States|American]] [[poet]], [[teacher]], and [[translator]].

==Life==
Jellema held a B.A. from [[Calvin College]] and a PhD from the [[University of Edinburgh]] (Scotland).<ref>{{Cite journal|last=Jellema|first=Roderick|date=1962|title=Victorian critics and the orientation of American literature, with special reference to the reception of Walt Whitman and Henry James|url=https://era.ed.ac.uk/handle/1842/18995|language=en}}</ref> He began teaching at the [[University of Maryland, College Park]] in 1955, where he founded and directed the creative writing program.  At the time of his death, he was Professor Emeritus.

He was the author of five books of poetry and three translations. His work was awarded the [[Hart Crane Memorial Poetry Contest]], the [[Pieter Jelles Prize]] (Piter Jellespriis) ([[North Frisian language|Friesland]]) and a [[Columbia University Translation Prize]] for his translations of [[North Frisian language|Frisian]] poetry.  He was the recipient of fellowships from the [[National Endowment for the Arts]], and [[Yaddo]].  His work appeared in various publications including ''[[Atlanta Review]]'', ''[[Beltway Poetry Quarterly]]'', ''[[FIELD (magazine)|Field]]'', ''[[Many Mountains Moving]]'', ''[[Plum Review]]'', and ''[[Poet Lore]]''.

He divided his time between [[Washington, DC]], [[San Juan, Puerto Rico]], and the Lake Michigan dunelands near [[Montague, Michigan]].

He had been working on an early history of New Orleans jazz titled ''Really Hot: A New Hearing for Old New Orleans Jazz'' (co-authored with the late [[Gordon Darrah]]).<ref>http://www.rodjellema.com/ projects on author website</ref>
