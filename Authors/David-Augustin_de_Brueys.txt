'''David-Augustin de Brueys''' (18 September 1641{{snd}}25 November 1723) was a French [[theology|theologian]] and [[playwright]]. He was born in [[Aix-en-Provence]]. His family was [[Calvinism|Calvinist]], and he studied theology. After writing a critique of [[Jacques-Bénigne Bossuet]]'s work, he was in turn converted to [[Catholicism]] by [[Jacques-Bénigne Bossuet|Bossuet]] in 1681, and later became a [[priest]].<ref>[http://www.newadvent.org/cathen/03004c.htm David-Augustine de Brueys] - [[Catholic Encyclopedia]] article</ref>

After his conversion, he was actively engaged in propagating the faith. He also managed to be joint editor with [[Palaprat]] in the production of plays.<ref>{{Nuttall|title=Brueys, David Augustin de|inline=1}}</ref> He died in [[Montpellier]].
