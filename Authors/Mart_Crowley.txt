'''Edward Martino Crowley''' (August 21, 1935 â€“ March 7, 2020) was an American playwright best known for his 1968 play ''[[The Boys in the Band (play)|The Boys in the Band]]''.<ref>{{cite news |last=Talbot |first=Mary |url=http://www.nydailynews.com/archives/nydn-features/man-band-changed-theater-revival-eve-theater-folk-recall-mart-crowley-boys-put-gays-spotlight-article-1.723711 |title=How One Man's ''Band'' Changed Theater on Revival's Eve: Theater Folk Recall That Mart Crowley and his ''Boys'' Put Gays in Spotlight |work=[[New York Daily News]] |date=June 19, 1996 |accessdate=May 25, 2012 }}</ref><ref name="roca2002">{{cite news |last=Roca |first=Octavia |url=https://www.sfgate.com/entertainment/article/Boys-to-Men-Mart-Crowley-s-latest-play-2759490.php |title=''Boys'' to ''Men'': Mart Crowley's latest play takes ''Boys in the Band'' through the past 30 years |work=[[San Francisco Chronicle]] |date=October 26, 2002 |accessdate=May 25, 2012 }}</ref><ref name="advocatewood">{{cite news |last=Kinser |first=Jeffrey |title=Mart Crowley on His Friend Natalie Wood |url=http://www.advocate.com/news/daily-news/2011/11/23/mart-crowley-natalie-wood |work=[[The Advocate (LGBT magazine)|The Advocate]] |date=November 23, 2011 |accessdate=May 25, 2012 }}</ref>

==Biography==
Crowley was born in [[Vicksburg, Mississippi]]. After graduating from [[The Catholic University of America]] (studying acting and show business) in [[Washington, D.C.]] in 1957, Crowley headed west to [[Hollywood]], where he worked for a number of television production companies before meeting [[Natalie Wood]] on the set of her film ''[[Splendor in the Grass]]''.<ref>[[Robert Wagner|Wagner, Robert J.]] ''[https://books.google.com/books?id=-CHEi7io_a8C&lpg=PA177&vq=Mart%20Crowley&pg=PA138#v=snippet&q=Mart%20Crowley&f=false Pieces of My Heart]''. New York: [[HarperCollins Publishers]], 2008. 138. ''[[Google Books]]''. Web. May 25, 2012.</ref> Wood hired him as her assistant, primarily to give him ample free time to work on his gay-themed play ''[[The Boys in the Band (play)|The Boys in the Band]]'',<ref>Dunlap, David W. [https://www.nytimes.com/1996/06/09/theater/theater-in-a-revival-echoes-of-a-gay-war-of-words.html?n=Top%2fReference%2fTimes%20Topics%2fSubjects%2fT%2fTheater THEATER: In a Revival, Echoes of a Gay War of Words]." ''[[The New York Daily News]]'' June 9, 1996. Web. May 25, 2012.</ref><ref name="query.nytimes.com">{{Cite web|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2000/06/11/nyregion/journey-to-an-overlooked-past.html|title=Journey to an Overlooked Past (Published 2000)|first=Marcia|last=Biederman|date=June 11, 2000|accessdate=October 6, 2020|via=NYTimes.com}}</ref> which opened [[off-Broadway]] on April 14, 1968 and enjoyed a run of 1,000 performances. Crowley became part of Wood's inner circle of friends that she called "the nucleus", whose main requirement was that they pass a "kindness" test.

''[[The Boys in the Band (play)|The Boys in the Band]]'' was adapted into a film in 1970 directed by [[William Friedkin]].<ref>{{cite news |last=Canby |first=Vincent |title=''The Boys in the Band'' (1970) review |newspaper=[[The New York Times]] |date=March 18, 1970 |accessdate=May 25, 2012 |url=https://movies.nytimes.com/movie/review?res=9E00E6D8173EE034BC4052DFB566838B669EDE }}</ref>

Crowley's 2002 sequel to ''The Boys in the Band'' was entitled ''[[The Men from the Boys]]''.<ref>Roca 2002, [http://www.sfgate.com/cgi-bin/article.cgi?f=/c/a/2002/10/26/DD55988.DTL&ao=all p. 1].</ref>

In 2018 ''Boys in the Band'' was restaged on [[Broadway theatre|Broadway]] in a 50th anniversary revival featuring [[Matt Bomer]], [[Jim Parsons]], [[Zachary Quinto]], and [[Andrew Rannells]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://variety.com/2018/legit/reviews/the-boys-in-the-band-review-broadway-1202824603/|title=Broadway Review: 'The Boys in the Band'|first=Marilyn|last=Stasio|date=June 1, 2018|accessdate=October 6, 2020}}</ref>

Crowley also wrote and produced ''Remote Asylum'' and the autobiographical ''A Breeze from the Gulf''. In 1979 and 1980, Crowley served first as the executive script editor and then producer of the [[American Broadcasting Company|ABC]] series ''[[Hart to Hart]]'', starring Wood's husband Robert Wagner and [[Stefanie Powers]]. His other credits include the teleplays for ''There Must Be a Pony'' (1986), ''Bluegrass'' (1988), ''People Like Us'' (1990), and a reunion special of ''Hart to Hart'' in 1996.

Crowley appeared in at least four documentaries: ''[[The Celluloid Closet]]'' (1995), about the depiction of [[homosexuality]] in cinema; ''[[Dominick Dunne: After the Party]]'' (2007), a biography of Crowley's friend and producer [[Dominick Dunne]]; ''Making the Boys'' (2011), a documentary about the making of ''The Boys in the Band''; and ''The Boys in the Band: Something Personal'' (2020), a documentary about the Netflix remake of the movie. Crowley was openly gay.

Crowley died in [[Manhattan]] on March 7, 2020.<ref>{{cite news|last=Meyer|first=Dan|title='The Boys in the Band' Playwright Mart Crowley Dies at 84|work=Playbill|date=March 9, 2020|accessdate=March 9, 2020|url=https://www.playbill.com/article/the-boys-in-the-band-playwright-mart-crowley-dies-at-84}}</ref><ref name="nyt">{{cite web|title=Mart Crowley, 'Boys in the Band' Playwright, Dies at 84|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2020/03/09/arts/mart-crowley-dead.html|last=Genzlinger|first=Neil|work=The New York Times|date=March 9, 2020|accessdate=March 9, 2020}}</ref> He suffered a heart attack, after which he underwent open-heart surgery and died while recovering.<ref>{{cite news|title=Playwright Mart Crowley, Best Known For THE BOYS IN THE BAND, Has Died at 84|work=Broadway World|date=March 8, 2020|accessdate=March 8, 2020|url=https://www.broadwayworld.com/article/Playwright-Mart-Crowley-Best-Known-For-THE-BOYS-IN-THE-BAND-Has-Died-at-84-20200308}}</ref>
