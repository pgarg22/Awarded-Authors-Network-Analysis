'''Rachel Caine''' was the pen name of '''Roxanne Longstreet Conrad''' (April 27, 1962 – November 1, 2020) who was an American writer of science fiction, [[fantasy]], [[Mystery fiction|mystery]], suspense, and [[Horror fiction|horror]] novels.

==Personal life==
Conrad grew up in [[West Texas]] and graduated from [[Socorro High School (El Paso, Texas)|Socorro High School]] in [[El Paso, Texas]], in 1980. She earned a bachelor's degree in accounting from the [[Rawls College of Business]] at [[Texas Tech University]] in 1985, with minor in music. Caine wrote and published novels and short stories since 1990. She was a professional musician who played with notable musicians including [[Henry Mancini]], [[Peter Nero]], and [[John Williams]].<ref>{{cite news |last=Bexte |first=Martina |url=http://www.bookloons.com/cgi-bin/Columns.asp?type=Interview&name=Rachel%20Caine |title=Rachel Caine e-interviewed |publisher=BookLoons |date=February 2004}}</ref>

From 1999, Conrad was employed in corporate communications as a web designer, editor, corporate communications manager, and finally as director of corporate communications for a large multinational company. She took an eight-month hiatus for most of 2008 to meet pressing deadlines, and retired from her position to write full-time in 2010.<ref>{{cite web |work=Cynsations |title=Author Interview: Rachel Caine on Glass Houses |url=http://cynthialeitichsmith.blogspot.com/2006/10/author-interview-rachel-caine-on-glass.html |last=Smith |first=Cynthia Leitich |date=October 9, 2006}}</ref> She lived in north Texas with her husband, artist Cat Conrad.<ref name="fwst3">{{cite news |first=David |last=Martindale |work=Fort Worth Star-Telegram |title=Third name is the charm for woman who was leery of being author |url=http://www.star-telegram.com/living/story/1478567.html |date=July 9, 2009}}</ref> Conrad did most of her writing at home and on the road during appearances.<ref>{{cite news |last=Warner |first=Bethany K. |url=http://bkwriter.blogspot.com/2006/04/author-answers-with-rachel-caine.html |title=Author Answers with Rachel Caine |work=Word Nerd |date=April 11, 2006}}</ref>

In 2018, Conrad was diagnosed with [[soft tissue sarcoma]].<ref name=TexasGov>[https://www.cedarparktexas.gov/departments/library/author-interviews/-loadingmode-EditContent/-fsiteid-1#Rachel%20Caine%20Link Author Spotlight: Rachel Caine], at the [[Cedar Park, Texas|Cedar Park Library]]; retrieved July 16, 2020</ref> She died on November 1, 2020.<ref>{{Cite web|title=https://twitter.com/rachelcaine/status/1323050702969704448|url=https://twitter.com/rachelcaine/status/1323050702969704448|access-date=2020-11-02|website=Twitter|language=en}}</ref>

====Weather Warden (Adult Urban Fantasy)====
Joanne Baldwin is a Weather Warden and part of an organization that rents space from the UN.  Their mission is supposed to be alleviating natural disasters before millions of people die.  Joanne shows up on the run, in her hot Mustang "Delilah", from a murder of which she has been accused; she thinks if she can find Lewis Orwell, with all three Warden capabilities—Earth, Fire, and Weather—who is in hiding, she can get help.  Lewis' last known action among the Wardens was to steal three bottles containing Djinn, the supernatural beings, many of whom partner with Wardens to do their work.  Her life becomes one huge complication as she learns a lot of truths people do not want her to know.

==== The ''Outcast Season'' (Adult Urban Fantasy) ====
On August 24, 2007, Caine announced on her mailing list and her blog that she had sold a four-book series called "Outcast Season" to [[Roc Books]]. Describing the upcoming series, she said "This series is a spin-off of the Weather Warden series, and will follow the story of a Djinn who's been cut off from her fellows, and must rely on the goodwill of a Warden partner for her very existence. Along the way, she has to face enemies and challenges that the Djinn can't, or won't face themselves."<ref>{{cite web |url=http://rachelcaine.livejournal.com/93495.html |last=Caine |first=Rachel |title=Outcast Season! And upcoming cake-serving events! |work=The Weather Report |date=August 24, 2007 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110721165156/http://rachelcaine.livejournal.com/93495.html |archive-date=July 21, 2011 |df=mdy-all }}</ref> The series was published yearly in February since 2009.

====Stillhouse Lake====
In 2017, Caine began publication of an intense new thriller series about Gwen Proctor, the ex-wife of an infamous serial killer who is constantly on guard for her kids against Internet trolls and real-life stalkers. When a body turns up in the lake outside their front door, and it appears to be connected to her ex-husband's methods, the spotlight's now on Gwen and her kids ... and she might never escape it again. 'Stillhouse Lake' is one of five finalists for the IATWA Thriller Awards 2018.<ref>{{Cite web|title=2021 Thriller Awards – International Thriller Writers|url=https://thrillerwriters.org/thriller-awards/|access-date=November 2, 2020|website=thrillerwriters.org}}</ref>
