'''Sara Sheridan''' (born 7 June 1968) is a Scottish activist and writer who works in a variety of genres, though predominately in historical fiction. She is the creator of the Mirabelle Bevan mysteries.

==Biography==

Born Sara Louise Goodwin, Sheridan comes from [[Edinburgh]] and was educated at [[Trinity College, Dublin]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.foyles.co.uk/sara-sheridan|title=Sara Sheridan, author of the 1950s-set Mirabelle Bevan mystery series ('Miss Marple with an edge') picks her favourite historical crime fiction, including books by Agatha Christie, Josephine Tey and Caleb Carr.|website=Foyles|language=en-GB|access-date=20 January 2017}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.harpercollins.co.uk/cr-102254/sara-sheridan|title=Sara Sheridan|last=Archipelago|first=World|website=HarperCollins UK|language=en|access-date=20 January 2017}}</ref><ref>{{Cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=28W8BQAAQBAJ&q=sara+sheridan+author+trinity+college+dublin&pg=PT1|title=Brighton Belle: A Mirabelle Bevan Mystery|last=Sheridan|first=Sara|date=20 March 2012|publisher=Birlinn|isbn=9780857901866|language=en}}</ref> She is a former member of the Society of Authors Committee for Scotland<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.foyles.co.uk/sara-sheridan|title=Sara Sheridan, author of the 1950s-set Mirabelle Bevan mystery series ('Miss Marple with an edge') picks her favourite historical crime fiction, including books by Agatha Christie, Josephine Tey and Caleb Carr.|website=Foyles|language=en-GB|access-date=2017-08-08}}</ref> and the Crime Writers Association. From 2009 to 2015 Sheridan sat on the board of writers' collective, '26'. She both managed and took part in the ''26 Treasures'' projects which took place at the V&A, Kensington, the Museum of Childhood, Bethnal Green, The National Museum of Scotland, Edinburgh and the Museum of  Childhood, Edinburgh. The resulting book, ''26 Treasures'' won the Literature Category at the British  Design Awards 2013. Sheridan also ran the organisation's annual charity advent calendar, ''26 Stories for Christmas''. In 2014 she actively campaigned in favour of Scottish independence and in 2016 supported the Remain campaign in the UK's EU Referendum. Sheridan has one daughter, Molly by her first marriage to Irish businessman, Seamus Sheridan. She married her second husband, Alan Ferrier in 2011 and the couple live in Scotland.

==Work==

Sheridan's first book, contemporary commercial fiction, ''Truth or Dare'' entered the ''Sunday Times'' top 50 when it was published in 1998. It was nominated for the Saltire Prize and was also listed in the Scottish Libraries Top 100 Books. In the successive two years Sheridan wrote two more novels in the same genre, ''Ma Polinski's Pockets'' and ''The Pleasure Express''. During this period, she also co-wrote two short films, ''Fish Supper''<ref>[https://sites.google.com/site/sarasplaysite/sara-sheridan-s-work/projects/short-films "SS Short Films: ''Fish Supper''"].</ref> starring [[Lynda Bellingham]] and ''The Window Bed'', which was nominated for a Sky Movies Max Award in 2001. She was then commissioned by specialist publisher [[Barrington Stoke]] to write a novella for reluctant readers, called ''The Blessed and The Damned''.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.aaaedinburgh.co.uk/5261-2/|title = Sara Sheridan novels to be turned into major new TV series|date = 25 January 2016}}</ref>

In 2003, she switched genres to historical fiction and funded the move by ghostwriting.{{citation needed|date=October 2015}} She now writes two series of historical novels: one based on the real lives of late Georgian/early Victorian adventurers (''The Secret Mandarin'' – 2009, [[Secret of the Sands]] – 2011, ''On Starlit Seas'' – 2016, The Ice Maiden - 2018) and a series of 1950s cosy crime noir mysteries featuring her fictional ex-secret service heroine, Mirabelle Bevan (''[[Brighton Belle (Sheridan novel)|Brighton Belle]]'' – 2012, ''[[London Calling (Sheridan novel)|London Calling]]'' – 2014, ''British Bulldog'' – 2015, ''Operation Goodwood'' – 2016, ''Russian Roulette'' – 2017','Indian Summer'' – 2019. ''Highland Fling'' 2020 and Celtic Cross - 2021).'' [https://www.thebookseller.com/news/1950s-set-mirabelle-bevan-mysteries-little-brown-320841 The Mirabelle Bevan Mysteries were optioned by STV in 2015 but were not developed for television].{{citation needed|date=October 2015}}'' In 2017 On Starlit Seas'' was shortlisted for the ''[http://www.wilbur-niso-smithfoundation.org/index.php?p=news/shortlist-announced-for-the-2017-wilbur-smith-adventure-writing-prize-1 Wilbur Smith Adventure Writing Prize].'' Sheridan has also written a children's picture book, (''I'm Me'' – 2010) which was inspired by her relationship with her niece and a pan-European picture book written with her daughter'' (Monsters Unite - 2019).''

Sheridan occasionally appears as a commenter on TV and radio in the UK.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://skynews.skypressoffice.co.uk/newstranscripts/murnaghan-70914-paper-review-keir-starmer-sara-sheridan-jeffrey-archer |title=Archived copy |accessdate=2014-09-25 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://archive.is/20140925132405/http://skynews.skypressoffice.co.uk/newstranscripts/murnaghan-70914-paper-review-keir-starmer-sara-sheridan-jeffrey-archer |archivedate=25 September 2014 |df=dmy-all }}</ref> She has reported from both [[Tallinn]], Estonia<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/iplayer/episode/p00jq0d2/From_Our_Own_Correspondent_Libya_and_Estonia/|title = BBC World Service - from Our Own Correspondent, Libya and Estonia}}</ref> and [[Sharjah (emirate)|Sharjah]], United Arab Emirates<ref>http://www.bbc.co.uk/programmes/p00mbwkb</ref> for [[BBC Radio 4]].<ref>[https://www.bbc.co.uk/iplayer/episode/p00jq0d2/From_Our_Own_Correspondent_Libya_and_Estonia/ BBC Radio 4’s "From Our Own Correspondent"].</ref> She has contributed to several British newspapers, including writing blog articles for ''[[The Guardian]]'' in 2011, 2012, 2013 and 2014.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.theguardian.com/edinburgh/2011/mar/01/edinburgh-sara-sheridan-secret-sands-history|title = Sara Sheridan on how history informs the present|website = [[TheGuardian.com]]|date = March 2011}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.theguardian.com/edinburgh/2011/mar/10/edinburgh-sara-sheridan-secret-sands-gay-rights|title = Sara Sheridan on getting gay rights right|website = [[TheGuardian.com]]|date = 10 March 2011}}</ref> She has also appeared, talking about history and feminism on RTE radio in 2017 and 2018. In 2017 she wrote a Love Letter to Europe for The National. In 2013 she appeared on The History of the Lady on [[BBC Radio 4]]'s ''[[Woman's Hour]]''. Sheridan also writes blog articles for the ''[[Huffington Post]]''<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.huffingtonpost.co.uk/sara-sheridan/who-do-you-think-you-are_b_3266193.html|title=Who do You Think You Are?|date=14 May 2013}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.huffingtonpost.co.uk/sara-sheridan/writers-earnings-cultural-myth_b_3136859.html|title = What Writers Earn: A Cultural Myth|date = 24 April 2013}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.huffingtonpost.co.uk/sara-sheridan/how-scotland-nurtures-its-writing-talent_b_3717674.html|title = How Scotland Nurtures Its Writing Talent|date = 8 August 2013}}</ref> and occasionally writes for the [[BBC]] online <ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-scotland-31663862|title = How the political spectrum is widening for women in Scotland|work = BBC News|date = 8 March 2015}}</ref> and as a cultural commentator on BBC Radio Scotland.

Sheridan also writes TV-tie in books. One for the second series of ITV's hit drama series, [[Victoria (UK TV series)|"Victoria"]] based on the life of Queen Victoria (2017) and most recently for ITV's adaptation of Jane Austen's last, unfinished novel: Sanditon (2019).

She remapped Scotland for Historic Environment Scotland (''Where are the Women? An imagined female atlas of Scotland - 2019),'' which was included in the David Hume Institute's First Minister's Summer Reading List 2019''.''<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.davidhumeinstitute.com/past/2019/6/16/first-ministers-reading-list-2019-launch|title=First Minister's Reading List 2019 Launch|website=David Hume Institute|language=en-US|access-date=2019-10-10}}</ref> The book is credited with inspiring Claire Mitchell QC to found the Accused Witches of Scotland campaign in 2019 to seek a pardon for Scotland's witches and raise a national memorial.

A portrait of her by Scottish artist Sophie Mckay Knight, the result of a creative collaboration, was featured by ''[[The Guardian]]'' Art & Design column in 2015<ref>{{Cite news|title = A look inside the artist's studio: Sophie McKay Knight paints Sara Sheridan|url = https://www.theguardian.com/artanddesign/ng-interactive/2015/oct/09/a-look-inside-the-artists-studio-sophie-mckay-knight-paints-sara-sheridan|website = The Guardian|accessdate = 16 October 2015|last1 = MacLeod|first1 = Murdo|last2 = Shields|first2 = Fiona|last3 = MacLeod|first3 = Murdo|last4 = Shields|first4 = Fiona}}</ref> before going on to public exhibition in the National Gallery of Scotland.

In 2016 Sheridan co-founded ''REEK''.<ref>https://www.urbanreivers.scot/reek/</ref> with her daughter, make up artist, Molly Sheridan – a fragrance company that spoke out against the lack of female memorialisation throughout history and challenged beauty industry norms. REEK's fragrances memorialised 'heroic, unapologetic and passionate women from history to the present day' and its first scent was launched in 2016 in memory of the Jacobite women. It has been called the 'first feminist fragrance'. In 2017 the company launched an eau de parfum in celebration of the memory of witches. The company closed in 2020 and its artefacts were collected by the National Museum of Scotland and Glasgow Women's Library.
