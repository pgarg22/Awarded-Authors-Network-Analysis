'''Kathryn Stockett''' is an American novelist. She is known for her 2009 [[debut novel]], ''[[The Help]]'', which is about [[African-American]] [[maid]]s working in white households in [[Jackson, Mississippi|Jackson]], [[Mississippi]], during the 1960s.

==Personal life==
Stockett grew up in Jackson, Mississippi.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Kathyrn Stockett from Jackson, Mississippi, Author of the Help|url=https://www.mswritersandmusicians.com/mississippi-writers/kathryn-stockett#:~:text=Kathryn%20Stockett%20was%20born%20in%201969%20in%20Jackson,%20Mississippi.&text=The%20story%20was%20inspired%20by,agreed%20to%20be%20her%20agent.|access-date=2020-06-25|website=www.mswritersandmusicians.com}}</ref> After graduating from the [[University of Alabama]] with a degree in English and Creative Writing, she moved to [[New York City]]. She lived there for 16 years and worked in magazine publishing and marketing. She is divorced and has a daughter.<ref name="creative_loafing_atlanta" /><ref name="daily_telegraph">{{cite news|url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/news/worldnews/northamerica/usa/5844739/The-maids-tale-Kathryn-Stockett-examines-slavery-and-racism-in-Americas-Deep-South.html|title=The maid's tale: Kathryn Stockett examines slavery and racism in America's Deep South|last=Calkin|first=Jessamy |date=July 16, 2009|work=The Daily Telegraph|access-date=2009-10-20|location=London}}</ref>

Reflective of her first novel, Stockett was very close to an [[African American]] [[domestic worker]].<ref name="nyt_feb_2009">{{cite news|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2009/02/19/books/19masl.html|title=Racial Insults and Quiet Bravery in 1960s Mississippi|last=Maslin|first=Janet|date=February 18, 2009 |work=New York Times|access-date=2009-10-20}}</ref>

A lawsuit was filed in a Mississippi court by Ablene<!-- Most RS write "Ablene".--> Cooper, a maid who used to work for Stockett's brother. It claimed that Stockett used her likeness in the book.<ref name=James >{{Citation |last=James |first=Susan |date=February 22, 2011 |title=Black Maid Sues, Says 'The Help' Is Humiliating |publisher=[[ABC News]] |url=https://abcnews.go.com/Health/lawsuit-black-maid-ablene-cooper-sues-author-kathryn/story?id=12968562&singlePage=true |access-date=July 26, 2015 }}</ref> A [[Hinds County, Mississippi]] judge threw the case out of court, citing the [[statute of limitations]].<ref name=clarion_led>{{cite web|last=Mitchell|first=Jerry|title='The Help' lawsuit tossed out|url=http://www.clarionledger.com/article/20110817/NEWS/108170333/-Help-lawsuit-tossed-out|work=[[The Clarion Ledger]]|access-date=12 September 2011}}</ref> Stockett denies her claim of stealing her likeness and says she only met her briefly.<ref name=clarion_led />
