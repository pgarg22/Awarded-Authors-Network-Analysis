'''Samuel D. Hunter''' (born 1981)<ref name=MacArthur>{{cite web |url=http://www.macfound.org/fellows/918/ |title=Samuel D. Hunter - MacArthur Foundation |publisher=John D. and Catherine T. MacArthur Foundation |accessdate=2014-10-13}}</ref> is an American [[playwright]] living in [[New York City]].<ref name=NewDram>{{cite web |url=http://newdramatists.org/samuel-d-hunter |title=Samuel D. Hunter - New Dramatists |publisher=New Dramatists |accessdate=2014-10-13}}</ref>

Hunter was born and raised in [[Moscow, Idaho]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.boiseweekly.com/boise/best-idaho-born-genius/BestOf?oid=3311990 |title=Best Idaho - Born 'Genius' |work=Boise Weekly |publisher=Boise Weekly |accessdate=2014-10-13}}</ref> He is best known for plays ''A Bright New Boise'', which won the 2011 [[Obie Award]] for playwriting, and ''The Whale'', which won the 2013 [[Drama Desk Award]] and the 2013 [[Lucille Lortel Awards|Lucille Lortel Award]] for Outstanding Play.<ref name=NewDram/> He is also the recipient of a 2014 [[MacArthur Fellows Program|MacArthur Fellowship]].<ref name=MacArthur/> Hunter is also a writer and producer for the television show ''[[Baskets (TV series)|Baskets]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.imdb.com/name/nm5926708/ |title=Samuel D. Hunter - IMDb |publisher=IMDb |accessdate=2019-07-05}}</ref>
