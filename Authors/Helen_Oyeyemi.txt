'''Helen Oyeyemi''' (born 10 December 1984) is a [[British people|British]] novelist and writer of short stories. Since 2014 her home has been in [[Prague]].<ref name="Hoggard">{{cite news|last1=Hoggard|first1=Liz|title=Helen Oyeyemi: 'I'm interested in the way women disappoint one another'|url=https://www.theguardian.com/books/2014/mar/02/helen-oyeyemi-women-disappoint-one-another|access-date=20 November 2016|work=The Guardian|date=2 March 2014|location=London}}</ref><ref name="Bradshaw">{{cite web|last1=Bradshaw|first1=M. René|title=What is Not Yours is Not Yours by Helen Oyeyemi|date=16 March 2016|url=http://www.thelondonmagazine.org/what-is-not-yours-is-not-yours-by-helen-oyeyemi/|work=[[The London Magazine]]|access-date=20 November 2016}}</ref>

==Life and career==
Oyeyemi was born in Nigeria and was raised in [[Lewisham]], [[South London]] from when she was four.<ref name="npr">{{cite web|last=Quinn|first=Annalisa|date=7 March 2014|title=The Professionally Haunted Life Of Helen Oyeyemi|url=https://www.npr.org/2014/03/07/282065410/the-professionally-haunted-life-of-helen-oyeyemi|access-date=31 January 2021|work=NPR}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|last=Hoggard|first=Liz|date=2 March 2014|title=Helen Oyeyemi: 'I'm interested in the way women disappoint one another'|url=https://www.theguardian.com/books/2014/mar/02/helen-oyeyemi-women-disappoint-one-another|access-date=31 January 2021|work=The Guardian}}</ref> Oyeyemi wrote her first novel, ''[[The Icarus Girl]]'', while studying for her [[Advanced Level (UK)|A-levels]]<ref name=jordan>{{cite web|last=Jordan|first=Justine|title=Mr Fox by Helen Oyeyemi – review|url=https://www.theguardian.com/books/2011/jun/11/mr-fox-helen-oyeyemi-review|work=The Guardian|access-date=30 January 2012|date=11 June 2011}}</ref> at [[Cardinal Vaughan Memorial School]]. While studying at [[Corpus Christi College, Cambridge]], Oyeyemi's plays ''Juniper's Whitening'' and ''Victimese'' were performed by fellow students to critical acclaim and subsequently published by [[Methuen Publishing|Methuen]].{{citation needed|date=January 2020}} In 2007 [[Bloomsbury Publishing|Bloomsbury]] published Oyeyemi's second novel, ''[[The Opposite House]]'', which is inspired by [[Culture of Cuba|Cuban]] [[mythology]].<ref>{{cite episode|series=[[Tell Me More]]|network=[[NPR]]|url=https://www.npr.org/templates/story/story.php?storyId=11384738|title=Oyeyemi's 'Opposite House'|date=26 June 2007}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|work=[[The Los Angeles Times]]|url=http://www.latimes.com/books/jacketcopy/la-ca-jc-helen-oyeyemi-20140302,0,4626669.story#axzz2vL4tcyvs|first=Stacey|last=D'Erasmo|author-link= Stacey D'Erasmo |date=27 February 2014|title=Helen Oyeyemi's 'Boy, Snow, Bird' turns a fairy tale inside out}}</ref> Her third novel, ''[[White Is for Witching]]'', was published by [[Picador (imprint)|Picador]] in May 2009. A fourth novel, ''[[Mr. Fox (novel)|Mr Fox]]'', was published by Picador in June 2011,<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.theguardian.com/books/2012/may/13/mr-fox-helen-oyeyemi-review|title=Mr Fox by Helen Oyeyemi – review|first=Anita|last=Sethi|author-link=Anita Sethi|newspaper=[[The Observer]]|date=13 May 2012}}</ref> and a fifth, [https://www.theguardian.com/books/2014/mar/22/boy-snow-bird-review-helen-oyeyemi ''Boy, Snow, Bird'', in 2014].<ref name="NPR">{{cite web|url=https://www.npr.org/2014/03/07/282065410/the-professionally-haunted-life-of-helen-oyeyemi|title=The Professionally Haunted Life of Helen Oyeyemi|last=Quinn|first=Annalisa|date=7 March 2014|work=[[NPR]]}}</ref>

Oyeyemi published a story collection ''What Is Not Yours Is Not Yours'', in 2016.<ref>{{Cite book|title = What Is Not Yours Is Not Yours|publisher = Riverhead Books|date =8 March 2016|location = Place of publication not identified|isbn = 9781594634635|language = en|first = Helen|last = Oyeyemi}}</ref> Oyeyemi's latest work, a novel titled ''Gingerbread'', was published 5 March 2019.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Review {{!}} Helen Oyeyemi's 'Gingerbread' recipe: Fairy tales with a dash of surrealism|url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/entertainment/books/helen-oyeyemis-gingerbread-recipe-fairy-tales-with-a-dash-of-surrealism/2019/02/26/7ec22c84-3930-11e9-a2cd-307b06d0257b_story.html |first=Ron |last=Charles |website=The Washington Post|language=en|date=26 February 2019|access-date=2020-05-29 }}</ref>

===Professional awards and recognition===
Her novel ''[[White Is for Witching]]'' was a 2009 [[Shirley Jackson Award]] finalist<ref>{{Cite web|website=The Shirley Jackson Awards|title=2009 Shirley Jackson Awards Winners|url=https://www.shirleyjacksonawards.org/award-winners/2009-shirley-jackson-awards-winners/|access-date=2020-05-29}}</ref> and won a 2010 [[Somerset Maugham Award]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=Helen Oyeyemi - Literature|url=https://literature.britishcouncil.org/writer/helen-oyeyemi|website=literature.britishcouncil.org|access-date=2020-05-29}}</ref> In 2009 Oyeyemi was recognized as one of the women on [[Venus Zine|Venus Zine's]] "25 under 25" list.<ref>{{Cite web|title=You'll Want To Get Helen Oyeyemi's New Book 'What is Not Yours is Not Yours' After Reading These Reviews|url=http://woman.ng/2016/03/youll-want-to-get-helen-oyeyemis-new-book-what-is-not-yours-is-not-yours-after-reading-these-reviews/|last=Woman.NG|date=2016-03-16|website=Woman.NG|language=en-US|access-date=2020-05-29}}</ref> In 2013 she was included in the [[Granta#Granta Best of Young British Novelists|Granta Best of Young British Novelists list]].<ref>{{cite journal|url=http://www.granta.com/Archive/123 |title=Granta 123: Best of Young British Novelists 4 |journal=[[Granta]] |year=2013 |issue=123 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130417233828/http://www.granta.com/Archive/123 |archive-date=17 April 2013 |url-status=dead}}</ref> ''Boy, Snow, Bird'' was a finalist for the [[Los Angeles Times Book Prize]] in 2014.<ref>{{Cite news |first=Clare |last=Swanson |title= L.A. Times Book Prize Finalists Announced |date=March 5, 2015 |url=https://www.publishersweekly.com/pw/by-topic/industry-news/awards-and-prizes/article/65774-2014-l-a-times-book-prize-finalists-named.html|website=www.publishersweekly.com|access-date=2020-05-29}}</ref> ''[https://www.nytimes.com/2016/03/20/books/review/what-is-not-yours-is-not-yours-by-helen-oyeyemi.html What Is Not Yours Is Not Yours]'' won the [[PEN Open Book Award]]: for an exceptional book-length work of literature by an author of colour published in 2016.<ref>[http://pen.org/2017-pen-literary-awards-winners/ "2017 PEN America Literary Awards Winners"], Pen America, 22 February 2017.</ref>

Oyeyemi was a judge on the Booktrust [[Independent Foreign Fiction Prize]] for 2015,<ref name=Irvine>{{cite news|last1=Irvine|first1=Lindesay|title=Jenny Erpenbeck Wins Independent Foreign Fiction Prize|url=https://www.theguardian.com/books/2015/may/27/jenny-erpenbeck-wins-independent-foreign-fiction-prize|access-date=24 June 2015|work=The Guardian|date=27 May 2015}}</ref> and served as a judge for the 2015 [[Scotiabank Giller Prize]].<ref>{{cite news |first=Mark |last=Medley |url=https://www.theglobeandmail.com/arts/books-and-media/the-giller-prize-expands-its-jury-to-five-people/article22442102/ |title=The Giller Prize expands its jury to five people |newspaper=[[The Globe and Mail]] |date=14 January 2015}}</ref>
