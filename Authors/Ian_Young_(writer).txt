'''Ian Young''' (born January 5, 1945) is an English-Canadian poet, editor, literary critic, and historian. While a student at the [[University of Toronto]], he was a founding member of the [[University of Toronto Homophile Association]], the first post-Stonewall gay organization in Canada.<ref name="aldrich" /><ref name=":0">{{Cite book|last=McLeod, Donald W. (Donald Wilfred)|title=Lesbian and gay liberation in Canada : a selected annotated chronology, 1964-1975|date=1996|publisher=ECW Press/Homewood Books|isbn=1550222732|location=Toronto|oclc=35108319}}</ref> He founded Canada's first gay publishing company, [[Catalyst Press]], in 1970,<ref name="aldrich">{{cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=zHCOboO86eAC&dq=ian+young+gay&pg=PA458|title=''Who's Who in Contemporary Gay and Lesbian History: From World War II to the Present Day''|last=Aldrich|first=Robert|date=2002|publisher=Psychology Press|page=458|access-date=2014-10-01|isbn=9780415291613}}</ref> printing over thirty works of poetry and fiction by Canadian, British, and American writers until the press ceased operation in 1980.<ref name=":0" /> His work has appeared in ''[[Canadian Notes & Queries]]'', ''[[The Gay & Lesbian Review Worldwide]]'', ''[[Rites (magazine)|Rites]]'' and ''[[Continuum (magazine)|Continuum]]'',<ref name="ryerson">{{cite web|url=https://library.ryerson.ca/sexdiv/authors/young/|title=Authors: Ian Young|date=2014|publisher=[[Ryerson University]] Library & Archives|access-date=2014-10-01}}</ref> as well as in more than fifty anthologies.<ref name="dube">{{cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=hdc7TeOxuA8C&dq=ian+young+gay+writer&pg=SL20-PA51|title=''Best Gay Stories 2012''|last=Dube|first=Peter|date=2012|publisher=[[Lethe Press]]|page=151|access-date=2014-10-01|isbn=9781590213865}}</ref> He was a regular columnist for ''[[The Body Politic (magazine)|The Body Politic]]'' from 1975 to 1985<ref name="aldrich" /> and for ''Torso'' between 1991 and 2008.<ref name="percy" />

Young is best known for his work as editor of the anthology ''The Gay Muse''<ref name="new">{{cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=Mkh2vJ_9GpEC&dq=ian+young+gay+writer&pg=PA420|title=''Encyclopedia of Literature in Canada''|last=New|first=William|date=2002|publisher=[[University of Toronto Press]]|page=420|access-date=2014-10-01|isbn=9780802007612}}</ref> and the bibliography ''The Male Homosexual in Literature''.<ref name="gunn">{{cite web|url=http://www.lambdaliterary.org/reviews/09/18/all-of-me-can-you-take-all-of-me-by-dirk-vanden/|title='All of Me (Can You Take All of Me?)' by Dirk Vanden|last=Gunn|first=Drewey Wayne|date=September 18, 2010|publisher=Lambda Literary Review|access-date=2014-09-24}}</ref> He edited ''The Male Muse: A Gay Anthology'', the first English-language anthology of poetry with gay male themes.<ref name=":0" /> In 1974, a shipment of ''The Male Muse'' was seized and burned by British customs officials.<ref name=":0" />

He was interested in ceremonial magic during the 1980s and was a founding member of the Hermetic Order of the Silver Sword.<ref name="percy">{{cite web|url=http://www.williamapercy.com/wiki/index.php?title=Ian_Young|title=Ian Young|last=Percy|first=William|date=December 2, 2011|access-date=2014-10-01}}</ref>

His recent book, ''Encounters with Authors'' (2013), featured historical and critical essays on the work of three noted Canadian LGBT writers, [[Scott Symons]], [[Robin Hardy (Canadian writer)|Robin Hardy]] and [[Norman Elder]].<ref>[http://www.lambdaliterary.org/reviews/08/26/encounters-with-authors-essays-on-scott-symons-robin-hardy-norman-elder-by-ian-young/ "‘Encounters with Authors: Essays on Scott Symons, Robin Hardy, Norman Elder’ by Ian Young"]. [[Lambda Literary Foundation]], August 26, 2013.</ref>
