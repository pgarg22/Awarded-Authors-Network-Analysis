'''Sutardji Calzoum Bachri''', known as '''Tardji''',<ref name="agusta">{{Cite news|first=Leon |last=Agusta |publisher=Jakarta Post |date=24 December 2007 |url=http://www.seasite.niu.edu/flin/sutardji.htm |title=Sutardji Calzoum Bachri: from bottle-poet to Sufism }}</ref> (born 1941 in [[Rengat]], [[Riau]]) is a well-known [[Indonesia]]n [[poet]]. A native [[Malay language|Malay]] speaker,<ref name="lindsay"/> he successfully launched a [[Creed|credo]] of 'freeing words of their meanings'.<ref name="lindsay">{{Cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=M6zs7VN9PnEC&pg=PA52&dq=%22sutardji+calzoum+bachri%22&cd=2#v=onepage&q=%22sutardji%20calzoum%20bachri%22&f=false |title=Babel or behemoth: language trends in Asia |editor=Jennifer Lindsay |page=52 |publisher=NUS |year=2003 |isbn=978-981-04-9075-1 }}</ref><ref>{{Cite book|first=Hal |last=Hill |publisher=University of Hawaii Press |year=1994 |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=rRlwAAAAMAAJ&q=%22sutardji+calzoum+bachri%22&dq=%22sutardji+calzoum+bachri%22&cd=3 |page=246 |title=Indonesia's new order: the dynamics of socio-economic transformation |isbn=978-0-8248-1660-5 }}</ref>

He was nicknamed the "bottle poet" for a preference, early in his career, for accompanying readings of his work with bottles of alcohol.<ref name="agusta"/> He was also known once as 'The President of Indonesian Poets'<ref name="agusta"/><ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.thejakartapost.com/news/2009/10/30/medy-loekito-silent-poetry-lonely-poetry.html |title=Medy Loekito: silent poetry, lonely poetry |date=30 October 2009 |publisher=Jakarta Post |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20101029005102/http://www.thejakartapost.com/news/2009/10/30/medy-loekito-silent-poetry-lonely-poetry.html |archive-date=29 October 2010 }}</ref> His style of reading has been compared to the [[incantation]]-like quality of the old Indonesian ''dukun'', chants stemming from Indonesian pre-Islamic [[Shamanism|shamanistic]] practice, still used today.<ref>{{Cite journal|author=Muhammad Haji Salleh |doi=10.1080/03062849108729752 |journal=Indonesia and the Malay World |volume=19 |issue=54 |date = March 1991|pages=3â€“18 |title=Oral elements in contemporary Indonesian poetry }}</ref>

The style of Tardji's [[poetry]] has been described as that of a ''[[mantra]]''.<ref name="lindsay"/> He has been quoted as saying that the ''mantra'' is the true use of words.
