'''Robert Zend''' (1929 - 1985) was a [[Hungarian-Canadian]] poet,<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://rogueembryo.com/2016/05/04/robertzend/ |title=Robert Zend, a poet without borders |access-date=2019-04-06 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190406165408/https://rogueembryo.com/2016/05/04/robertzend/ |archive-date=2019-04-06 |url-status=live}}</ref> fiction writer and a multimedia artist.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.robertzend.ca/esperanto.html |title=Robert Zend translated into Esperanto |access-date=2019-04-06 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190406164953/http://www.robertzend.ca/esperanto.html |archive-date=2019-04-06 |url-status=live }}</ref><ref name="canenc">[https://www.thecanadianencyclopedia.ca/en/article/robert-zend Robert Zend] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190406170745/https://www.thecanadianencyclopedia.ca/en/article/robert-zend |date=2019-04-06 }} at [[The Canadian Encyclopedia]].</ref>

Born in [[Budapest]], Zend fled to Canada after the failed [[Hungarian Uprising of 1956]].<ref name=canenc /> He took a master's degree in [[Italian literature]] from the [[University of Toronto]] in 1969, and worked as a producer of [[Ideas]] documentaries for [[CBC Radio]].<ref name=canenc />

His poetry collections included ''From Zero to One'' (1973), ''Beyond Labels'' (1982) and ''Arbormundi'' (1982); ''OƒÅb'', was published in two separate volumes in 1983 and 1985.<ref name=canenc /> A short story collection, ''Daymares: Selected Fictions on Dreams and Time'', was published posthumously in 1991.<ref name=canenc />

Zend was part of a trilogy of Toronto poets who published with HMS Press et al. ''The Three Roberts'' with [[Robert Sward]] and [[Robert Priest]] in 1984.
