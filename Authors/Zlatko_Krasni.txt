'''Zlatko Krasni''' ({{lang-sr|Златко Красни}}, (born 1951, [[Sarajevo]], [[Yugoslavia]]—died 31 October 2008) was a Serbian [[poet]] of [[Czechs|Czech]] origin who resided in [[Belgrade]] for most of his life. He held a BA and an MA in Germanic Languages from the Philology College of the [[University of Belgrade]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ffu.uni-freiburg.de/fobawi/awi/fornet/fornet_sopron2001.pdf|title=Proceedings of the opening conference in Sopron, Hungary 18 - 20 April 2001|publisher=Albert-Ludwig-Universität Freiburg|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20070611011245/http://www.ffu.uni-freiburg.de/fobawi/awi/fornet/fornet_sopron2001.pdf|archive-date=2007-06-11}}</ref>

==Works==
Krasni has won the prize of the [[Serbian Academy of Sciences and Arts]]<ref>http://www.festivaldepoesiademedellin.org/pub/es/Revista/ultimas_ediciones/59_60/krasni.html{{dead link|date=July 2016|bot=InternetArchiveBot|fix-attempted=yes}}</ref> and was a guest at the [[:de:Literarisches Colloquium Berlin|Literarisches Colloquium Berlin]] in 2005.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.lcb.de/gaeste/2005.htm|title=Gäste: Autoren und Übersetzungen im Haus 2005|publisher=Literarisches Colloquium Berlin|language=German|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170606033111/http://www.lcb.de/gaeste/2005.htm|archive-date=2017-06-06}}</ref> One of Krasni's poems is quoted by defenders of the former Serbian dictator [[Slobodan Milosevic]]'s admirers in Serbia.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.forumaugsburg.de/s_3themen/Europa/060329_milosevic/index.htm|title=Der Tod von Slobodan Milosevic und die Lügen über die Balkankriege|language=German|last=Ruf|first=Hans-Georg|publisher=Forum Augsburg|access-date=April 17, 2007|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20070928103922/http://www.forumaugsburg.de/s_3themen/Europa/060329_milosevic/index.htm|archive-date=September 28, 2007}}</ref>
