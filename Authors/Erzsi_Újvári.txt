'''Erzsi Újvári''' (born '''Erzsébet Kassák''') (14 July 1899, [[Érsekújvár]]-11 August 1940, [[Moscow]]) was a [[Hungarian people|Hungarian]] poet.

Her parents were Istvan Kassák and Erzsébet Istenes, and [[Lajos Kassák]] was her brother. In 1912 she moved to [[Budapest]] and got work in a textiles factory.<ref name="OB">{{cite web |last1=Dokumentation |first1=Österreichisches Biographisches Lexikon und biographische |title=Ujvári, Erzsi; eigentl. Kassák Erzsébet, verheiratete Barta Erzsébet |url=http://www.biographien.ac.at/oebl/oebl_U/Ujvari_Erzsi_1899_1940.xml |website=www.biographien.ac.at |accessdate=29 November 2018 |language=de |date=2003}}</ref> Influenced by her brother she became involved in literature, and joined him in founding the avant-garde journals ''[[A Tett]]'' (1915–16) and ''[[MA (journal)|MA]]''.<ref name="OB"/> She married the poet [[Sándor Barta]].<ref name="CPN">{{cite book |last1=Cornis-Pope |first1=Marcel |last2=Neubauer |first2=John |title=History of the Literary Cultures of East-Central Europe: Junctures and disjunctures in the 19th and 20th centuries. Volume III: The making and remaking of literary institutions |date=2007 |publisher=John Benjamins Publishing |location=Amsterdam/Philadelphia |isbn=9789027292353 |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=taw6AAAAQBAJ&q=%22Erzsi+%C3%9Ajv%C3%A1ri%22+%22sandor+Barta%22&pg=PA27 |language=en}}</ref>{{rp|27}}

Along with many other [[avant-garde]] writers, she left Hungary after the fall of the [[Hungarian Soviet Republic]].
