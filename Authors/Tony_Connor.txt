'''John Anthony Connor''' (born 1930) is an [[English people|English]] poet and playwright.

==Biography==
Tony Connor was born in [[Manchester]], England. After leaving school at 14, he served in the British Army as a tank gunner, and worked as a textile designer between 1944 and 1960,<ref>[http://www.jrank.org/literature/pages/3661/Tony-Connor-(Tony-John-Anthony-Connor).html "Tony Connor (Tony John Anthony Connor) Biography"], jrank.org.</ref> and in radio and television in Manchester in the 1960s. He was a founder member of The Peterloo Group. He earned an MA at the [[University of Manchester]]<ref>[https://www.poetryfoundation.org/poems-and-poets/poets/detail/tony-connor "Tony Connor"], Poetry Foundation.</ref> in 1967 and in 1968 visiting writer at [[Amherst College]] in [[Massachusetts]].<ref name=AAP>[http://www.poets.org/poet.php/prmPID/1578 "Tony Connor"], Academy of American Poets.</ref>

In 1961, he married the speech therapist, [[Frances MacCurtain|Frances Foad]]. They had three children: two sons, Samuel and Simon, and a daughter Rebecca. They divorced in 1979.<ref name="Hayes">{{cite book|last1=Hayes|first1=Cathy|title=[[Dictionary of Irish Biography]]|date=2009|publisher=Cambridge University Press|editor1-last=McGuire|editor1-first=James|location=Cambridge|chapter=MacCurtain (n√©e Foad), Frances|editor2-last=Quinn|editor2-first=James}}</ref> 

From 1971 until he retired in 1999 Connor was professor of [[English language|English]] at [[Wesleyan University]] in [[Middletown, Connecticut]]. 

He lives in Middletown and London. He was a close friend of the English writer [[J. G. Ballard]] and remains close friends with [[Michael de Larrabeiti]]. One section of Connor's 2006 anthology ''[[Things Unsaid: Selected Poems 1960-2005|Things Unsaid]]'' is dedicated to de Larrabeiti; de Larrabeiti's 1992 book [[Journal of a Sad Hermaphrodite]] is dedicated to Connor, and includes one of his poems.

Connor has published nine volumes of poetry. His work is anthologized in ''[[British Poetry since 1945]]''.  He has been a Fellow of the [[Royal Society of Literature]] since 1974.<ref name=AAP />
