'''Jeffrey M. "Jeff" Shaara''' (born February 21, 1952) is an American [[novelist]], the son of [[Pulitzer Prize]] winner [[Michael Shaara]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.cnn.com/books/dialogue/9807/shaara/index.html|title=CNN - Like father, like son - July 14, 1998|website=www.cnn.com|access-date=2018-11-27}}</ref>

==Biography==
Jeffrey Shaara was born in [[New Brunswick, New Jersey]], and grew up in [[Tallahassee, Florida]]. He graduated from [[Florida State University]] in 1974 with a degree in [[Criminology]]<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.clarionledger.com/story/life/2015/06/06/author-qa-jeff-shaara/28611349/|title=Author Q&A: Jeff Shaara|work=The Clarion Ledger|access-date=2018-11-27|language=en}}</ref> and lives in [[Gettysburg, Pennsylvania|Gettysburg]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.penguinrandomhouse.com/authors/71683/jeff-shaara|title=Jeff Shaara {{!}} Penguin Random House|website=www.penguinrandomhouse.com|language=en-US|access-date=2018-11-27}}</ref>

He wrote ''[[Gods and Generals (novel)|Gods and Generals]]'' and ''[[The Last Full Measure (novel)|The Last Full Measure]]'', which are the prequel and sequel, respectively, to his father Michael's award-winning novel ''[[The Killer Angels]]''.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.salisburypost.com/2017/05/28/bookmarks-presents-jeff-shaara-best-selling-military-author/|title=Bookmarks Presents Jeff Shaara, Best-Selling Military Author - Salisbury Post|date=2017-05-28|work=Salisbury Post|access-date=2018-11-27|language=en-US}}</ref> Jeff followed his father's footsteps upon the latter's death, writing [[historical fiction]] and documenting the American wars and their most historically relevant characters. In total, Jeff has written fifteen [[The New York Times Best Seller list|''New York Times'' bestselling]] novels.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://jeffshaara.com/about|title=Jeff's Bio {{!}} Jeff Shaara|website=jeffshaara.com|access-date=2018-11-27}}</ref>

Jeff delivered the commencement speech at University of Delaware's 2005 undergraduate ceremony. Jeff has deemed this "one of the most important moments in his life."<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www1.udel.edu/PR/UDaily/2005/mar/shaara063005.html |title=	Commencement speaker felt ‘part of UD family’ | publisher=University of Delaware | date=June 30, 2005}}</ref>

He completed a trilogy in 2010 about [[World War II]] in the [[European Theatre of World War II|European]] and [[North African Campaign|North African]] theaters. A fourth World War II novel, titled ''[[The Final Storm (Jeff Shaara novel)|The Final Storm]]'', covers the end of the war in the [[Pacific War|Pacific]], and was released on May 17, 2011.<ref name="goodreads">{{cite web|url=http://www.goodreads.com/book/show/9866451-the-final-storm |title=The Final Storm: A Novel of World War II in the Pacific (World War II: 1939-1945 #4) |publisher=[[Goodreads]] |access-date=2012-01-07}}</ref>

==Awards==

Shaara has received the [[W.Y. Boyd Literary Award for Excellence in Military Fiction]] from the [[American Library Association]] in 2018 for ''The Frozen Hours'',<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ala.org/news/press-releases/2018/05/frozen-hours-novel-korean-war-jeff-shaara-wins-2018-w-y-boyd-literary-award|title='Frozen Hours: A Novel of Korean War' by Jeff Shaara wins 2018 W. Y. Boyd Literary Award for Excellence in Military Fiction|last=CMALDEN|date=8 May 2018|website=News and Press Center}}</ref> in 2005 for [[To the Last Man (Jeff Shaara novel)|''To The Last Man: A Novel of The First World War'']],<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.ala.org/awardsgrants/awards/2/winners/2005|title=W.Y. Boyd Literary Award for Excellence in Military Fiction {{!}} Awards & Grants|website=www.ala.org|language=en|access-date=2018-11-27}}</ref> and in 1997 for [[Gods and Generals (novel)|''Gods and Generals'']].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.ala.org/awardsgrants/awards/2/winners/1997|title=W.Y. Boyd Literary Award for Excellence in Military Fiction {{!}} Awards & Grants|website=www.ala.org|language=en|access-date=2018-11-27}}</ref> The American Library Association's Reference and Users Services Association recognized [[The Steel Wave]] as a 2009 Notable Book.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ala.org/aboutala/governance/annualreport/annualreport/annualreportarch/awardsandhonorsa/awards|title=Awards and Honors|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141221214558/http://www.ala.org/aboutala/governance/annualreport/annualreport/annualreportarch/awardsandhonorsa/awards|archive-date=2014-12-21}}</ref>

He has also been awarded the Lincoln Forum's "Richard Nelson Current Award",<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.thelincolnforum.org/richard-nelson-current-award|title=lincolnforum {{!}} Richard Nelson Current Award|website=lincolnforum|language=en|access-date=2018-11-27}}</ref> New York Civil War Round Table's "Bell I. Wiley Award",<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.cwrtnyc.org/bell-i-wiley-award.html|title=Civil War Round Table of New York {{!}} Bell I. Wiley Award|website=www.cwrtnyc.org|access-date=2018-11-27}}</ref> and Florida State University's "Artes Award" as a Distinguished Alumnus.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://vam.fsu.edu|title=Vires, Artes, Mores {{!}} A university-wide donor recognition society|website=vam.fsu.edu|language=en|access-date=2018-11-27}}</ref> In 2011, Shaara was inducted into the FSU College of Criminology Hall of Fame  and awarded FSU's first annual Distinguished Writer's Award.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.cassidyandfishman.com/speakers/jeff-shaara/|title=JEFF SHAARA}}</ref>

==Film adaptations==

In 2003, [[Warner Brothers]] made the major motion picture ''[[Gods and Generals (film)|Gods and Generals]]'', which was based upon his book of the same title.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.warnerbros.com/gods-and-generals|title=Gods and Generals|website=www.warnerbros.com|language=en|access-date=2018-11-27}}</ref>
