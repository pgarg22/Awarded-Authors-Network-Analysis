'''Helen Lowe''' is a New Zealand novelist. Her first novel, ''Thornspell'', was published in 2008. She has since published the first two books in ''The Wall of Night'' quartet, ''The Heir of Night'' and ''The Gathering of the Lost''. Lowe is a three-time winner of the [[Sir Julius Vogel Award]],<ref>{{cite web|title=SFFANZ Sir Julius Vogel Awards|url=http://www.sffanz.org.nz/sjv/sjvAwards.shtml}}</ref> and won the [[David Gemmell Legend Awards|David Gemmell Morningstar Award]]<ref>{{cite web|title=David Gemell Morningstar Award Winners |url=http://gemmellaward.com/profiles/blogs/david-gemmell-legend-award-winners-2012-announced |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20130921061056/http://gemmellaward.com/profiles/blogs/david-gemmell-legend-award-winners-2012-announced |archivedate=2013-09-21 }}</ref> for Best Fantasy Newcomer in 2012.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.hums.canterbury.ac.nz/rss/news/?feed=news&articleId=428|title=Writer in Residence wins international award|date=21 June 2012|publisher=University of Canterbury}}</ref>

==Biography==
Lowe was born in [[Wellington]] in 1961. 
She attended schools in New Zealand and Singapore. She has a Bachelor of Arts in English and Geography from the [[University of Waikato]].
Lowe continued her education at the [[Stockholm University]] and in 1984 earned a Postgraduate Diploma in Social Science.
