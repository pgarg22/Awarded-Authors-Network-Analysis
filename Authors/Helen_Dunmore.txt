'''Helen Dunmore''' [[FRSL]] (12 December 1952 – 5 June 2017<ref name=Kellaway />) was a British poet, novelist, and short story and children's writer.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://literature.britishcouncil.org/writer/helen-dunmore|title=Helen Dunmore – Literature|publisher=British Council Literature|access-date=6 June 2017|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180104013748/https://literature.britishcouncil.org/writer/helen-dunmore|archive-date=4 January 2018}}</ref> 

Her best known works include the novels ''[[Zennor in Darkness]]'', ''[[A Spell of Winter]]'' and ''[[The Siege (Dunmore novel) |The Siege]]'', and her last book of poetry ''[[Inside the Wave]]''. She won the inaugural [[Orange Prize for Fiction]], the [[National Poetry Competition]], and posthumously the [[Costa Book Award]].<ref name="Cain">{{cite news |last1=Cain |first1=Sian |title=Helen Dunmore wins Costa book of the year for Inside the Wave |url=https://www.theguardian.com/books/2018/jan/30/helen-dunmore-wins-costa-book-of-the-year-for-inside-the-wave |access-date=23 June 2021 |work=The Guardian |date=30 January 2018}}</ref>

==Biography==
Dunmore was born in [[Beverley]], Yorkshire, in 1952, the second of four children of Betty (''née'' Smith) and Maurice Dunmore.
She attended (briefly) [[Nottingham Girls' High School]], then a [[direct grant grammar school]].

She studied English at the [[University of York]], and lived in [[Finland]] for two years (1973–75) and worked as a teacher. She lived after that in [[Bristol]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.bloodaxebooks.com/news?articleid=712|title=News And Publicity {{!}} Bloodaxe Books|website=www.bloodaxebooks.com|language=en|access-date=5 June 2017|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170611135501/http://www.bloodaxebooks.com/news?articleid=712|archive-date=11 June 2017}}</ref><ref name=Kellaway>{{cite web|url=https://www.theguardian.com/books/2017/jun/05/helen-dunmore-obituary|title=Helen Dunmore obituary|author=Kate Kellaway|work=The Guardian|date=5 June 2017|access-date=9 June 2017|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170609075655/https://www.theguardian.com/books/2017/jun/05/helen-dunmore-obituary|archive-date=9 June 2017}}</ref> Dunmore was a Fellow of the [[Royal Society of Literature]] (FRSL). Some of Dunmore's children's books are included in reading schemes for use in schools.

== Personal life ==
Dunmore's husband Frank Charnley, whom she married in 1980, is a lawyer.<ref name=HDperMM>{{cite news|url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/culture/books/3603057/A-writers-life-Helen-Dunmore.html|title=A writer's life: Helen Dunmore|author=Marianne Macdonald|date=21 September 2003|work=[[The Daily Telegraph]]|access-date=12 March 2017|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170313124236/http://www.telegraph.co.uk/culture/books/3603057/A-writers-life-Helen-Dunmore.html|archive-date=13 March 2017}}</ref> Dunmore had a son, daughter and stepson, and three grandchildren at the time of her death.<ref name=Kellaway />
