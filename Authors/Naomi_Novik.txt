'''Naomi Novik''' (born April 30, 1973) is an American author of [[speculative fiction]]. She is best known for the [[Temeraire (series)|''Temeraire'' series]], an [[alternate history]] of the [[Napoleonic Wars]] involving dragons, and the standalone fantasy novels ''[[Uprooted (novel)|Uprooted]]'' and ''[[Spinning Silver]]'', which were inspired by Polish folklore and the [[Rumpelstiltskin]] fairy tale, respectively. Novik has won many awards for her work, including the [[Alex Awards|Alex]], [[Audie Award|Audie]], [[British Fantasy Award|British Fantasy]], [[Locus Award|Locus]], [[Mythopoeic Award|Mythopoeic]] and [[Nebula Awards]]. Film rights to her novels ''Uprooted'' and ''[[A Deadly Education]]'' have been purchased by [[Warner Bros.]] and [[Universal Pictures]].

==Early life==
Novik grew up in [[Roslyn Heights]] on [[Long Island]]. She is a second-generation American; her father's family were [[Lithuanian Jews]], and her mother's family were [[Poland|Polish]] [[Catholics]].<ref name="Paste">{{Cite web |url=https://www.pastemagazine.com/books/naomi-novik/naomi-novik-talks-spinning-silver/|title=Naomi Novik Talks ''Spinning Silver'', Her Rumpelstiltskin-Inspired Novel|last=Jackson |first=Frannie |work=[[Paste (magazine)|Paste]] |date=July 10, 2018 |access-date=March 9, 2020}}</ref> Displaying an interest in reading at a young age, she read ''[[The Lord of the Rings]]'' at age six, and developed a love for [[Jane Austen]] soon afterward.<ref name="NewYorkTimes" />

She received a [[Bachelor of Arts|bachelor's degree]] in [[English literature]] at [[Brown University]], and holds a [[Master of Science|master's degree]] in [[computer science]] from [[Columbia University]].<ref>{{cite web |last1=Fox |first1=Rose |title=Interview: Naomi Novik |url=http://strangehorizons.com/non-fiction/articles/interview-naomi-novik/ |website=[[Strange Horizons]] |access-date=November 11, 2021 |date=August 14, 2006}}</ref> She participated in the design and development of the computer game ''[[Neverwinter Nights: Shadows of Undrentide]]'', until she discovered that she preferred writing to game design.<ref name="NewYorkTimes">{{cite news|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2006/10/11/books/11novi.html|title=A New Writer Is Soaring on the Wings of a Dragon|date=2006-10-11|work=[[The New York Times]]|first=Julie|last=Bosman|access-date=2010-05-19}}</ref>

==Career==
Novik's first novel, ''[[His Majesty's Dragon]]'' (''Temeraire'' in the UK), which commences the [[Temeraire (series)|''Temeraire'' series]], is an [[alternate history]] of the [[Napoleonic Wars]] in a "Flintlock Fantasy" world in which dragons are abundant and are used in aerial combat. ''His Majesty's Dragon'' won the [[Compton Crook Award]] in 2007,<ref name="Compton2007" /> and was nominated for the [[Hugo Award for Best Novel]].<ref name="HugoAward2007" /> ''Temeraire: In the Service of the King'', an [[Omnibus edition|omnibus]] volume collecting the first three books of the series (''His Majesty's Dragon'', ''[[Throne of Jade]]'', and ''[[Black Powder War]]''), won the [[Locus Award for Best First Novel]] in 2007.<ref name="LocusAward2007" />

In September 2006, [[Peter Jackson]] optioned the rights to the ''Temeraire'' series,<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.theguardian.com/film/2006/sep/12/books.news |title=Peter Jackson enters dragons' den |date=12 September 2006 |work=[[The Guardian]]}}</ref> intending to produce three or more live-action films,<ref>{{cite web |last1=Creen |first1=Willow |title=Peter Jackson Options Temeraire |url=https://www.empireonline.com/movies/news/peter-jackson-options-temeraire/ |website=[[Empire (film magazine)|Empire]] |access-date=May 22, 2021 |date=September 11, 2006}}</ref> but the rights have since reverted to Novik.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.reddit.com/r/YAwriters/comments/476y90/ama_naomi_novik_author_of_uprooted_the_temeraire/|title=r/YAwriters AMA: Naomi Novik, author of ''Uprooted'' & the ''Temeraire'' series|website=Reddit|access-date=December 14, 2020}}</ref>

The ''Temeraire'' series has also been released in [[audiobook]] format. The first five audiobooks were released by [[Books on Tape (company)|Books on Tape]], beginning in 2007, and read by [[Simon Vance]].<ref>{{cite web|url= http://www.booksontape.com/search.cfm?author=60490&|title= Books by Naomi Novik|website=[[Books on Tape (company)|Books on Tape]]|access-date= 2011-12-02|archive-url= https://web.archive.org/web/20120405125644/http://www.booksontape.com/search.cfm?author=60490&|archive-date= 2012-04-05|url-status= dead}}</ref> The sixth audiobook was released by [[Tantor Audio]] in September 2010, also read by Vance.<ref>{{cite web|url= http://www.tantor.com/BookDetail.asp?Product=1933_TonguesSerpents|title=''Tongues of Serpents'': Book 6 in the ''Temeraire'' series|website=[[Tantor Media]]|access-date=May 23, 2021}}</ref>

In September 2007, Novik was awarded the [[John W. Campbell Award for Best New Writer]] for best new science fiction writer of 2006.<ref name="JohnCampbellAward" />

In 2011, Novik wrote ''Will Supervillains Be on the Final?'', a [[graphic novel]] about the next generation of high-flying costumed crusaders. [[Yishan Li]] illustrated the [[comic]] with "[[manga]]-styled art".<ref>{{cite web |title=''Will Supervillains Be on the Final?'' Liberty Vocational, Vol. 1 |url=https://www.publishersweekly.com/978-0-345-51656-5 |website=[[Publishers Weekly]] |access-date=May 23, 2021 |date=March 7, 2011}}</ref>

In 2015, Novik published ''[[Uprooted (novel)|Uprooted]]'', a standalone novel "set in a fantasy world inspired by the Kingdom of Poland",<ref>{{cite web|url=http://sf-fantasy.suvudu.com/2013/08/new-release-interview-blood-of-tyrants-by-naomi-novik.html|title=New Release Interview: ''Blood of Tyrants'' by Naomi Novik|first=Shawn|last=Speakman|date=13 August 2013|website=[[Suvudu]]|publisher=[[Random House]]|access-date=23 December 2013|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131224094504/http://sf-fantasy.suvudu.com/2013/08/new-release-interview-blood-of-tyrants-by-naomi-novik.html|archive-date=24 December 2013|url-status=dead}}</ref> which won the [[Nebula Award for Best Novel]],<ref name="Nebula2016" /> the [[British Fantasy Award]] for Best Fantasy Novel,<ref name="BritishFantasyAward2016" /> the [[Locus Award for Best Fantasy Novel]],<ref name="LocusAward2016" />  and the [[Mythopoeic Awards|Mythopoeic Fantasy Award]].<ref name="MythopoeicAwards2016" /> [[Warner Bros.]] purchased the film rights to ''Uprooted''; [[Ellen DeGeneres]] and Jeff Kleeman signed on to produce the film through their production company, [[A Very Good Production]].<ref>{{cite web |last1=Kit |first1=Borys |title=Ellen DeGeneres to Produce Adaptation of Naomi Novik's Fantasy Novel 'Uprooted' (Exclusive) |url=https://www.hollywoodreporter.com/tv/tv-features/ellen-degeneres-produce-adaptation-naomi-800931/ |website=[[The Hollywood Reporter]] |access-date=May 23, 2021 |date=June 8, 2015}}</ref>

In 2016, Novik published "Spinning Silver", a short story retelling the [[Rumpelstiltskin]] fairy tale, in the fantasy anthology ''The Starlit Wood: New Fairy Tales''.<ref name="Paste" /><ref>{{cite web |last1=Speakman |first1=Shawn |title=Spinning Silver â€“ A New Naomi Novik Short Story |url=https://www.naominovik.com/2016/10/spinning-silver-a-new-naomi-novik-short-story/ |website=NaomiNovik.com |access-date=May 22, 2021 |date=October 6, 2016}}</ref> Two years later, she expanded the story into her second standalone novel, ''[[Spinning Silver]]'',<ref name="Paste" /> which won the 2019 Locus Award for Best Fantasy Novel,<ref name="LocusAward2019" /> the 2019 [[Alex Award]],<ref name="AlexAward2019" /> and the 2019 [[Audie Award]] for Fantasy.<ref name="AudieAward2019" />

In 2020, Novik published ''[[A Deadly Education]]'', the first in a trilogy set in the [[Scholomance]], a school of [[black magic]] from folklore. The main character, Galadriel "El" Higgins, a half-Welsh, half-Indian sorceress, must survive to graduation while controlling her destructive abilities.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Canfield |first1=David |title=''Spinning Silver'' author Naomi Novik to launch epic new trilogy this fall |url=https://ew.com/books/2020/02/07/naomi-novik-deadly-education-announcement-excerpt-cover/ |website=[[Entertainment Weekly]] |access-date=May 22, 2021 |date=February 7, 2020}}</ref> [[Universal Pictures]] purchased the film rights to the ''Scholomance'' series in advance, assigning [[Todd Lieberman]] and [[David Hoberman]] of [[Mandeville Films]] to develop and produce the films.<ref>{{cite web |last1=D'Alessandro |first1=Anthony |title=Universal & Mandeville Films Partner On Naomi Novik's 'Scholomance' Series |url=https://deadline.com/2020/05/universal-mandeville-films-partner-on-naomi-noviks-scholomance-series-1202943726/ |website=[[Deadline Hollywood|Deadline]] |access-date=May 23, 2021 |date=May 26, 2020}}</ref> Upon its release, ''A Deadly Education'' was criticized for a passage where the hairstyle known as [[dreadlocks]] is described as susceptible to an infestation of bug-like magical creatures.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Irankunda |first1=Larissa |title=Racism vs. Representation: The Missteps of Naomi Novik's ''A Deadly Education'' |url=https://www.themarysue.com/racism-vs-representation-the-missteps-of-naomi-noviks-a-deadly-education/ |website=[[The Mary Sue]] |access-date=May 23, 2021 |date=October 16, 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |last1=Tanjeem |first1=Namera |title=A Response to Claims of Racism in Naomi Novik's ''A Deadly Education'' |url=https://bookriot.com/racism-in-naomi-noviks-a-deadly-education/ |website=Book Riot |access-date=May 23, 2021 |date=December 3, 2020}}</ref> Novik later apologized for "evok[ing] a racist stereotype" about [[Afro-textured hair]]. She revised the passage for future reprints, and promised that the sequel novels will not be sent to reviewers and publishers before "revisions are fully complete and a final sensitivity read has happened."<ref>{{cite web |last1=Novik |first1=Naomi |title=Apology for ''A Deadly Education'' |url=http://www.naominovik.com/apology/ |website=NaomiNovik.com |access-date=May 23, 2021}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Novik is married to the entrepreneur and writer [[Charles Ardai]]. They live in Manhattan.<ref name="NewYorkTimes" /> They have one child, a daughter named Evidence Novik Ardai, who was born in 2010.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Novik|first=Naomi|url=http://www.naominovik.com/2011/04/temeraire-fanart-contest-results/|title=Temeraire Fanart Contest Results!|website=NaomiNovik.com|date=April 8, 2011|access-date=December 14, 2020}}</ref>
