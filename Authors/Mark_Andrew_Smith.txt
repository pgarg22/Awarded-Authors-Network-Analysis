'''Mark Andrew Smith''' (born July 24th, 1979) is an American [[comic book]] [[Comic book creator|author]] and graphic novelist.

== Early life and education ==
Smith attended the [[University of California, Santa Barbara]], where he studied film.<ref name="booksmuggle">{{Cite web |url=http://thebooksmugglers.com/2010/03/guest-author-mark-andrew-smith-on-inspirations-and-influences.html |title=Guest Author: Mark Andrew Smith on Inspirations and Influences |last=James |first=Thea |date=March 25, 2010 |website=TheBookSmugglers.com |access-date=June 14, 2016}}</ref>  He graduated in 2002.<ref name="booksmuggle"/><ref name="coastlines">{{Cite magazine |date=Winter 2011 |title=Profileâ€”Mark Andrew Smith, '02 |url=http://www.ucsbalum.com/Coastlines/2011/Winter/milestones_win2011.html |magazine=Coastlines |access-date=June 14, 2016 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160304122937/http://www.ucsbalum.com/Coastlines/2011/Winter/milestones_win2011.html |archive-date=March 4, 2016 |url-status=dead }}</ref> Smith received his Master's Degree in TESOL from [[SIT Graduate Institute]] in Brattleboro Vermont in 2017.

== Career ==
Out of college, Smith's first job was as a production assistant on season one of [[American Idol]].<ref name="coastlines"/>  His first entry into the comic book world came in 2004 when Smith and Dan Hipp were picked up by [[Image Comics]].<ref name="coastlines"/><ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.filmandmedia.ucsb.edu/people/Alumni/alumni-pages/smith-mark-and.html |title=Mark Andrew Smith |website=filmandmedia.ucsb.edu |access-date=June 14, 2016}}</ref>  Image Comics would go on to publish their work, ''[[The Amazing Joy Buzzards]]''.<ref name="coastlines"/>  Smith is also the co-creator and editor of the [[Popgun (comics)|Popgun]] [[Comics anthology]].

Smith wrote the original graphic novel Aqua Leung, published in April 2008.  Aqua Leung was nominated for a [[Yalsa]] award for best graphic novel for teens.<ref>[http://www.comicbookresources.com/?page=article&id=12748 "Aqua Leung," My Friend], [[Comic Book Resources]], March 3, 2008</ref><ref>[http://forum.newsarama.com/showthread.php?t=153210 Mark Andrew Smith on ''Aqua Leung'']{{dead link|date=January 2018 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}, [[Newsarama]], April 10, 2008</ref> Also in 2008 Smith wrote Kill All Parents, a 38 page one shot about superheroes whose parents are killed by a secret government program, drawn by Marcelo Di Chiara, also for Image Comics.<ref>[http://forum.newsarama.com/showthread.php?t=155337 Mark Andrew Smith on ''Kill All Parents'']{{dead link|date=January 2018 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}, [[Newsarama]], April 29, 2008</ref><ref>[http://www.comicbookresources.com/?page=article&id=16237 Mark Andrew Smith Plans to "Kill All Parents"], [[Comic Book Resources]], April 30, 2008</ref>

In 2009 Smith released [[The New Brighton Archeological Society]] stemming from a series of short stories in Popgun with artist Matthew Weldon.<ref>[http://www.newsarama.com/comics/100822-New-Brighton-Archeological-Society.html Smith on New Brighton Archaeological Society], [[Newsarama]], October 22, 2008</ref><ref>[http://www.comicbookresources.com/?page=article&id=18551 Smith & Weldon Dig Up New Brighton Archeological Society], [[Comic Book Resources]], October 23, 2008</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.newsarama.com/comics/030930-Matthew-Weldon.html|title = Newsarama &#124; GamesRadar+}}</ref>  The New Brighton Archeological Society follows a group of children whose parents are the greatest archeologists in the world.  When their parents are lost on an expedition, the children must finish their parents' work, collect the books of magic, and defeat their parents' nemesis.  The New Brighton Archeological Society was selected as "Best All-Ages Comic" of 2009 by [[MTV]] Splash Page.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://splashpage.mtv.com/2009/12/15/best-of-2009-comics/ |title=Archived copy |access-date=2010-04-11 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://www.webcitation.org/6AYt2Aogq?url=http://splashpage.mtv.com/2009/12/15/best-of-2009-comics/ |archive-date=2012-09-10 }}</ref> The New Brighton Archeological Society received two Harvey Award Nominations in 2010.<ref>[http://www.comicbookresources.com/?page=article&id=27117/],</ref>

Smith has been published in a number of anthologies such as the [[Belle & Sebastian]] Anthology, 24Seven Vol. 1, Space Doubles, Paper Museum Vol. 3, and Popgun.

2011 saw the release of Smith's Gladstone's School for World Conquerors  by Image Comics with artist  and co-creator Armand Villavert which centers on a super-villain academy. Gladstone's School for World Conquerors won the 2013 American Library Association Award for 'Great Graphic Novels for Teens'.

Smith released Sullivan's Sluggers, a baseball horror graphic novel drawn by James Stokoe, in 2012. Sullivan's Sluggers was published by [[Dark Horse Comics]] in 2018.

In 2015 New Brighton Archeological Society Volume 1 was published as a hardcover by 'Maneki Neko Books' along with volume 2 titled 'The Sword of Azure', both were drawn by Matthew Weldon.

In 2016 Gladstone's School for World Conquerors Volume 1 was published as a hardcover by 'Maneki Neko Books' along with Gladstone's School for World Conquerors Volume 2 which is drawn by frequent collaborator Matthew Weldon who was filling in for co-creator Armand Villavert on art duties.

In 2019 Smith released Gladstone's School for World Conquerors Book 3 with artist and co-creator Armand Villavert.

== Honors and awards ==
Smith has received recognition for his work on ''[[Popgun (comics)|Popgun]]''.  Volume 1 won the 2008 [[Harvey Award]] for Best Anthology.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.comicsbeat.com/2008-harvey-award-winners/ |title=2008 Harvey Award winners |last=MacDonald |first=Heidi |author-link=Heidi MacDonald |date=September 28, 2008 |website=ComicsBeat.com |access-date=June 14, 2016}}</ref>  This was followed by Volume 3 winning the 2010 [[Eisner Award]] for Best Anthology.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.comicsreporter.com/index.php/your_2010_eisner_award_winners/ |title=Your 2010 Eisner Award Winners |date=July 24, 2010 |website=ComicsReporter.com |access-date=June 14, 2016}}</ref> Gladstone's School for World Conquerors won the 2013 ALA Awards for 'Great Graphic Novels for Teens'.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.ala.org/awardsgrants/gladstone%E2%80%99s-school-world-conquerors/ |title=Gladstone's School for World Conquerors |date=2013 |website=www.ala.org}}</ref>
