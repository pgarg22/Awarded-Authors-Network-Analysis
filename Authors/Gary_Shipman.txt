'''Gary Lee Shipman''' (born March 16, 1966) is an American artist, comic book illustrator and writer of ''[[Pakkins' Land]]''. He is father to three children.

==Biography==
Shipman grew up in [[Salinas, California]] and is a self-taught artist. His interest in comics and art first began at a young age, while in the sixth grade.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://lubbockonline.com/stories/081499/ent_0814990017.shtml#.VgYQ3LSDcoU |title=Archived copy |access-date=September 26, 2015 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150927150703/http://lubbockonline.com/stories/081499/ent_0814990017.shtml#.VgYQ3LSDcoU#.VgYQ3LSDcoU |archive-date=September 27, 2015 |url-status=dead |df=mdy-all }}</ref>

Shipman, with wife [[Rhoda Shipman]], first created the independently produced comic book series ''[[Pakkins' Land]]'' in 1995, both plotting out the stories together and Gary scripting, lettering, penciling, and inking the illustrations for the series. ''Pakkins’ Land'' tells the adventuresome and engaging tale of Paul, a young boy who finds himself in a magical world filled with talking animals, mystery and excitement. ''Pakkins’ Land'' has been heralded for excellence by both critics and peers alike, accumulating an international following and numerous award nominations.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.hahnlibrary.net/comics/awards/manning.php|title = Russ Manning Most Promising Newcomer Award}}</ref><ref>[http://www.hahnlibrary.net/comics/awards/eisner98.php 1998 Will Eisner Comic Industry Award Nominees], the Comic Book Awards Almanac</ref>

Certain characters in the ''Pakkins' Land'' stories are named after Shipman's children.

Shipman resides in Illinois with his family and, at times, makes appearances as a guest artist at comic conventions in the region.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.wizardworld.com/garyshipman.html |title=Archived copy |access-date=September 26, 2015 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150926192724/http://www.wizardworld.com/garyshipman.html |archive-date=September 26, 2015 |url-status=dead |df=mdy-all }}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |url=http://chi-fi.org/artists.html |title=Archived copy |access-date=September 26, 2015 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150815044000/http://chi-fi.org/artists.html |archive-date=August 15, 2015 |url-status=dead |df=mdy-all }}</ref>

Shipman has [[dyslexia]] and has shared about coping with it, at times, on his youtube live streams.<ref>https://www.youtube.com/user/MrGship/featured</ref>

In late 2013, a 46-page color collection of his artistry: ''The Gary Shipman Sketchbook Volume #1'', {{ISBN|1600392016}} was published by Lamp Post, Inc., released as both a perfect-bound paperback edition and also as a [[Amazon Kindle|Kindle]] e-book.<ref>{{Cite book|isbn = 978-1600392016|title = The Gary Shipman Sketchbook Volume 1|last1 = Shipman|first1 = Gary|date = February 2013}}</ref>

In 2016, the ''[https://www.garyshipmanart.com/store/p170/Gary_Shipman_Sketchbook_2016.html Gary Shipman Sketchbook 2016]'', a 45-page paperback collection of Shipman's artwork, was self-published and offered online.

in 2018, Shipman started a [https://www.patreon.com/GaryShipman Patreon account] and began regularly live streaming drawing sessions on his [https://www.youtube.com/user/mrgship YouTube channel] where he chats and interacts with fans from around the world.

In late 2018, ''Gary Shipman 2018 Sketchbook'', A full-color 70 page collection of his art, was crowdfunded in an [[Indiegogo]] campaign. The campaign was fully funded in minutes and raised $8745.00 (411% funded). The campaign offered incentives such as original art, a republished ''[[Pakkins Land]]'' issue 1 & 2, and collectible ''[[Pakkins Land]]'' trading cards.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.indiegogo.com/projects/gary-shipman-sketchbook-2018#/|title = Gary Shipman Sketchbook 2018}}</ref><ref>https://indiecron.com</ref>

== Other works ==

Shipman's artistic talents can also be found elsewhere. In 1987, he co-created the superhero character Edge for [[Silverwolf Comics]], and illustrated the first 3 issues of that series.<ref>{{Cite book|url=https://www.amazon.com/Edge-2-1-Gary-Shipman/dp/B000WHSN1O|title = Edge #2|date = January 1987}}</ref>

In 1997, Shipman illustrated the cover of Issue #19 of the comic series ''OZ'', based on the books and characters of [[L. Frank Baum]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.mycomicshop.com/search?TID=283301|title=Oz (1994 Caliber) comic books}}</ref>

In 1998, Shipman illustrated the cover of ''Parables & Proverbs'' {{ISBN|0-9665118-0-8}} published by New Creation Publications.

In 2003, Shipman illustrated ''Amazing True Life Stories'' Issue # 2: ''Spymaster'' for [https://web.archive.org/web/20040411020254/http://www.crosswindcomics.com/ Crosswind Comics].

In 2008, Shipman illustrated ''Kingdoms: A Biblical Epic, Vol. 4 - Valley of Dry Bones'', and ''Kingdoms: A Biblical Epic, Vol. 5 - The Writing on the Wall''.<ref>{{Cite book|isbn=978-0310713562|title=Valley of Dry Bones|last1=Avery|first1=Ben|last2=Rogers|first2=Bud|last3=Burner|first3=Brett|year=2008}}</ref><ref>{{Cite book|isbn = 978-0310713579|title = The Writing on the Wall|last1 = Avery|first1 = Ben|last2 = Rogers|first2 = Bud|year = 2008}}</ref>

In 2011, Shipman was invited to join the [[DreamWorks Animation]] Fine Art program,<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.dreamworksanimationfineart.com |title=Archived copy |access-date=2019-02-08 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130228225213/http://www.dreamworksanimationfineart.com/servlet/StoreFront |archive-date=February 28, 2013 |df=mdy-all }}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.awn.com/articles/art/dreamworks-animation-goes-fine-art|title=DreamWorks Animation Goes Fine Art}}</ref> with his first offering being an interpretative piece based on ''[[How To Train Your Dragon]]'', made available as the original painting <ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.dreamworksanimationfineart.com/servlet/the-181/How-To-Train-Your/Detail |title=Archived copy |access-date=2013-02-23 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20121213061827/http://www.dreamworksanimationfineart.com/servlet/the-181/How-To-Train-Your/Detail |archive-date=December 13, 2012 |df=mdy-all }}</ref> or as prints.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.dreamworksanimationfineart.com/servlet/the-182/How-To-Train-Your/Detail |title=Archived copy |access-date=2013-02-23 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20121213061832/http://www.dreamworksanimationfineart.com/servlet/the-182/How-To-Train-Your/Detail |archive-date=December 13, 2012 |df=mdy-all }}</ref>

In 2012, Shipman was one of 80 different contributing writers and artists from around the globe for the third and final book in the ''Fables for Japan'' digital anthology to benefit the victims of the [[2011 Tōhoku earthquake and tsunami]].<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.fables4japan.com/ |title=Archived copy |access-date=December 15, 2018 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150926222600/http://www.fables4japan.com/ |archive-date=September 26, 2015 |url-status=dead |df=mdy-all }}</ref>

Released in 2013, Shipman was a contributing artist for both the [[DC Comics]] ''[[Batman]]: The Legend'' [[trading cards]] and the ''[[Superman]]: The Legend'' [[trading cards]] sets produced by [[Cryptozoic Entertainment]].<ref>http://www.cryptozoic.com/tradingcards/dc-comics-batman™-legend-trading-cards</ref><ref>https://www.cryptozoic.com/trading-cards/dc-comics-superman™-legend-trading-cards</ref>

Released in 2014, Shipman illustrated ''Fun with Kirk and Spock (Star Trek: A Parody)'' {{ISBN|1604334762}} written by author Robb Pearlman.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.trektoday.com/content/2014/06/fun-with-kirk-and-spock-book/|title = Fun with Kirk and Spock Book – TrekToday}}</ref>

Also in 2014, Shipman was a contributing artist for the [[Adventure Time]] [[trading cards]] set produced by [[Cryptozoic Entertainment]].<ref>https://www.cryptozoic.com/trading-cards/adventure-time-trading-cards</ref>

In 2014, Shipman started to see his artwork distributed by art distributor Acme Archives, doing licensed pieces in his distinctive style from [[Disney]],<ref>http://acmelimited.com/news/new-disney-artwork-acme-direct/</ref> [[Marvel Entertainment]],<ref>http://acmelimited.com/news/new-marvel-artwork-acme-direct/</ref> and [[Lucasfilm]]<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://jediinsider.com/index.php?catid=8&itemid=15797|title = Acme Archives "Determined Diplomat" Print by Gary Shipman}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=http://filmschoolrejects.com/features/posters-of-comic-con-2014.php/3 |title=Archived copy |access-date=2014-09-22 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150317195255/http://filmschoolrejects.com/features/posters-of-comic-con-2014.php/3 |archive-date=March 17, 2015 |df=mdy-all }}</ref>

In 2015, Shipman illustrated a label for the Rot Berry (strawberry) flavor of [[Deadworld]] Zombie Soda, and the associated trading card series.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://store.capricebrands.com/deadworld-soda-cards/rotters-20brby-gary-shipman |title=Archived copy |access-date=2015-09-26 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150926213915/http://store.capricebrands.com/deadworld-soda-cards/rotters-20brby-gary-shipman |archive-date=September 26, 2015 |df=mdy-all }}</ref>

In 2018, Shipman was a contributing artist to the [[Rick and Morty]] [[trading cards]] season 1 set produced by [[Cryptozoic Entertainment]].<ref>https://www.cryptozoic.com/blogs/rick-and-morty-trading-cards-season-1-sketch-card-previews-part-13</ref>

* In 1997 Shipman and his wife were nominated for the [[Russ Manning Award|Russ Manning Most Promising Newcomer Award]].<ref>[http://www.hahnlibrary.net/comics/awards/manning.php The Russ Manning Most Promising Newcomer Award], the Comic Book Awards Almanac</ref>
* In 1998 Shipman and his wife were nominated for an [[Eisner Award]] in the "Talent Deserving of Wider Recognition" category.<ref>[http://www.hahnlibrary.net/comics/awards/eisner98.php 1998 Will Eisner Comic Industry Award Nominees], the Comic Book Awards Almanac</ref>
