'''Anne Wignall''', known as '''Lady Ebury''', née Acland-Troyte; (12 June 1912 – 23 June 1982), was an English socialite and author as '''Alice Acland''' and '''Anne Marreco'''.

==Family life==
Anne Wignall was born Anne Acland-Troyte in the [[Metropolitan Borough of Kensington|London Borough of Kensington]], the daughter of Herbert Walter Acland-Troyte and Marjorie Florence Pym. She had one younger brother, John Acland-Troyte.<ref name = ThePeerage>{{cite web |url=http://www.thepeerage.com/p3143.htm#i31426 |title=ThePeerage – Anne Acland-Troyte |accessdate=21 January 2019}}</ref>

She married:
*On 1 July 1933, [[Robert Grosvenor, 5th Baron Ebury|Baron Ebury]] (1914-1957). They had two sons, [[Francis Grosvenor, 8th Earl of Wilton|Francis Egerton Grosvenor, 8th Earl of Wilton]] (born 1934) and the Hon. Robert Victor Grosvenor (1936–1993). They were divorced in 1941. During their marriage they lived at Redheath (now [[Croxley Green#York House School|York House School]]), Croxley Green, Watford,<ref>{{cite magazine |title=The Sketch |page=383 |date=29 August 1934}}</ref> Kingston Bagpuize House, North Berkshire (now in Oxfordshire)<ref>{{cite magazine |title=The Bystander |issue=1707 |page=1 |date=2 September 1936}}</ref> and Day's House (now Philberd's Manor<ref>{{cite book |title=Traditional Buildings in the Oxford Region |author=John Steane & James Ayres |publisher=Oxbow Books |year=2013}}</ref>), East Hanney, Berkshire.<ref>{{cite magazine |title=The Tatler |issue=1996 |page=553 |date=27 September 1939}}</ref>
*On 23 December 1941, Henry Peregrine Rennie Hoare (1901–1981). They were divorced in 1947.
*On 13 November 1947, Lt.-Col. Frederick Wignall (1906–1956). They had one daughter, Caroline Louisa Wignall (born 1948). She was widowed in 1956.<ref>{{cite book |title=Debrett's Peerage and Baronetage |author=Patrick Montague-Smith |year=1976}}</ref>
*On 25 September 1961, [[Anthony Marreco]] (1915–2006), a [[junior counsel]] at the [[Nuremberg Trials]], and later a founding director of [[Amnesty International]].

She changed her name back to Wignall by deed poll in 1969 and died in 1982 in Tiverton, Devon.<ref>{{cite book |title=Debrett's Peerage and Baronetage |author=Patrick Montague-Smith |year=1976}}</ref> She is buried in the churchyard at [[All Saints Church, Huntsham]] close to her father's ancestral seat, [[Huntsham Court]].<ref name = FindaGrave.com>{{cite web |url=https://www.findagrave.com/memorial/191238819/anne-wignall |title=FindaGrave - Anne Wignall |accessdate=21 January 2019}}</ref>

==Bibliography==
Anne Wignall wrote 11 books under two different names:<ref>{{cite book |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=phhhHT64kIMC&q=who%27s+who+of+authors+and+writers+2004+wignall&pg=PA578 |title=International Who's Who of Authors and Writers 2004 |author=Europa Publications |publisher=Psychology Press |year=2003 |page=578 |isbn=9781857431797}}</ref>

As '''Alice Acland''':
*''[[Caroline Norton]]'' ([[Constable & Robinson|Constable]], 1948) {{ASIN|B0007G4CKG}}, a biography of the English social reformer and author
*''Templeford Park'' (Constable, 1954) {{ASIN|B000SEMHL8}}, a tale of country life in contemporary 1950s Britain
*''A Stormy Spring'' (Constable, 1955) {{ASIN|B002EO5JTW}}, a novel set in Sussex, Bruges and Paris, about the life of a fictional young lady, Emily Satersham
*''A Second Choice'' (Constable, 1956) {{ASIN|B0016RQZJQ}}, a novel about a young girl in love with a philandering middle-aged married man
*''A Person of Discretion'' ([[William Collins, Sons|Collins]], 1958) {{ASIN|B002JV9856}}, story of three sisters from Brussels becoming entangled in the [[black market]] and the resistance during the closing stages of the Second World War
*''The Corsican Ladies'' ([[Peter Llewelyn Davies|Peter Davies Ltd]], 1974) {{ISBN|9780432004104}}, historical novel based on the extensive autobiographical writings of [[Laure Junot, Duchess of Abrantès|Laure Junot]]
*''The Secret Wife'' (Peter Davies Ltd, 1975) {{ISBN|9780432004111}}, a historical novel based on the life of [[Françoise d'Aubigné, Marquise de Maintenon|Françoise d'Aubigné]]
*''The Ruling Passion'' (Peter Davies Ltd, 1976) {{ISBN|9780432004128}}, historical novel based on the life of [[Diane de Poitiers]], a mistress of [[Henry IV of France]]

As '''Anne Marreco''':
*''The Charmer and the Charmed'' ([[Weidenfeld & Nicolson]], 1963) {{ASIN|B001N8MF2E}}, a comedy of manners, in which the wife of a publisher falls in love with one of her husband's authors
*''The Boat Boy'' (Weidenfeld & Nicolson, 1964) {{ASIN|B0000CMAPN}}, a novel in which the anti-hero brings chaos to rural Ireland
*''The Rebel Countess – The Life and Times of [[Constance Markievicz]]'' (Weidenfeld & Nicolson, 1967) {{ASIN|B0006D8I46}}, a biography of the Irish revolutionary and politician
