'''Roxann Dawson''' (née '''Caballero''', born September 11, 1958), also credited as '''Roxann Biggs''' and '''Roxann Biggs-Dawson''', is an American actress and director who played [[B'Elanna Torres]] on the television series ''[[Star Trek: Voyager]]''.

==Early life==
Roxann Dawson was born in [[Los Angeles]], [[California]].<ref name=TVGuide>McDonagh, Maitland (January 2, 2003). [http://movies.tvguide.com/movie-news/question-rosario-dawson-60865.aspx "Question: Are Rosario Dawson ..."] ''[[TV Guide]]''. {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130926101046/http://movies.tvguide.com/movie-news/question-rosario-dawson-60865.aspx |date=September 26, 2013 }}</ref> She graduated from [[University of California, Berkeley]] in 1980.<ref>{{cite web |url= http://www.berkeley.edu/tour/students/famous_alumni2.html |title= Famous Berkeley Alumni |publisher= [[University of California, Berkeley]] |access-date=May 10, 2013|quote= Roxann Dawson, '80, actress |url-status= dead |archive-url= https://web.archive.org/web/20100527100153/http://www.berkeley.edu/tour/students/famous_alumni2.html |archive-date=May 27, 2010 }}</ref>

==Career==
===Acting, directing and producing===
Dawson's first professional acting job was in a [[Broadway theatre|Broadway]] production of ''[[A Chorus Line]]''.<ref name="bio">[http://www.roxanndawson.net/bio.html "Biography"]. The Official Roxann Dawson Website. Retrieved January 19, 2013.</ref>

In 1989 Dawson worked on the [[NBC]] drama ''[[Nightingales (U.S. TV series)|Nightingales]]''. During the course of her work on that show, she met casting director Eric Dawson, who would later become her second husband. After the show was canceled, Eric's company cast her in guest roles on ''[[Matlock (TV series)|Matlock]]'' and ''[[Jake and the Fat Man]]''.<ref name=LincolnJournalStar>Korbelik, Jeff (September 10, 2005). [https://journalstar.com/lifestyles/former-lincoln-man-gets-emmy-nomination-for-casting-nip-tuck/article_fef34988-61c6-5fe6-92aa-34b9f0132e1b.html "Former Lincoln man gets Emmy nomination for casting 'Nip/Tuck'"]. ''[[Lincoln Journal-Star]]''. Retrieved February 7, 2019.</ref>

[[File:DawsonMulgrewLien1995.jpg|334px|thumb|left|Roxann Dawson with ''Voyager'' actresses [[Kate Mulgrew]] and [[Jennifer Lien]] (1995)]]
In 1994, Dawson began her role as the half Human/half-[[Klingon]] engineer [[B'Elanna Torres]] on ''[[Star Trek: Voyager]]'', which lasted for all seven seasons of the show. While working on ''Voyager'', Dawson made her directorial debut on the episode "[[Riddles (Star Trek: Voyager)|Riddles]]", which aired in September 1999. She later directed the second part of the two-part episode "[[Workforce (Star Trek: Voyager)|Workforce]]" and directed 10 episodes of ''[[Star Trek: Enterprise]]''. In 2002, she provided the voice of the repair station computer in [[Dead Stop|one]] of the ''Star Trek: Enterprise'' episodes she directed.<ref>{{Cite episode |title=Dead Stop|episode-link=Dead Stop |series=Star Trek: Enterprise|series-link=Star Trek: Enterprise|credits=Sussman, Michael; Strong, Phyllis|network=[[UPN]]|date=October 9, 2002|season=2 |number=4 }}</ref>

Her other television credits include appearances on ''[[The Hat Squad]]'', ''[[Baywatch]]'', ''[[The Closer]]'', ''[[Matlock (TV series)|Matlock]]'', ''[[The Untouchables (1993 TV series)|The Untouchables]]'', ''[[Any Day Now (TV series)|Any Day Now]]'', ''[[Without a Trace]]'', ''[[The Lyon's Den]]'', ''[[The Division]]'', the U.S. version of ''[[Coupling (U.S. TV series)|Coupling]]'' and the science fiction television series ''[[Seven Days (TV series)|Seven Days]]''.

Dawson has directed episodes of ''[[Charmed]], [[The O.C.]], [[Close to Home (2005 TV series)|Close to Home]]'', ''[[Lost (TV series)|Lost]], [[Heroes (U.S. TV series)|Heroes]], [[Hawthorne (TV series)|Hawthorne]], The Closer, [[Cold Case]], [[Caprica (TV series)|Caprica]]'', ''[[The Mentalist]]'' and ''[[Treme (TV series)|Treme]]''. In 2010, she directed the season two episode "[[List of Lie to Me episodes#Season 2: 2009-2010|Teacher and Pupils]]" of ''[[Lie to Me]]'' and the season two episode "[[The Good Wife (season 2)#Episodes|On Tap]]" of ''[[The Good Wife]]''. In 2013, she directed the March 22 episode "Reunions" of ''[[Touch (American TV series)|Touch]]'', and the October 15 episode "Eye-Spy" of ''[[Agents of S.H.I.E.L.D.]]''.

In 2014 she directed the March 5 episode "Dreamcatcher" of ''[[Revolution (TV series)|Revolution]]'' and the October 22 episode "Phobia" of ''[[Stalker (TV series)|Stalker]]''. For the 2015 season of the Amazon Video series ''[[Bosch (TV series)|Bosch]]'', she directed "Chapter Six: Donkey's Years").<ref>{{Cite episode |title=Chapter Six: Donkey's Years|series=Bosch (TV series)|series-link=Bosch (TV series)|credits=Connelly, Michael; Overmyer, Eric|network=[[Amazon Studios]]|date=February 12, 2015|season=1 |number=6 }}</ref> She directed early episodes of ''[[Mercy Street (TV series)|Mercy Street]]'', a series released on PBS in January 2016. In 2016, she also directed the season one episodes "Broussard" and "Zero Day" of the TV series ''[[Colony (TV series)|Colony]]''. She's also directed the episode "Crossbreed" of [[The Americans (season 5)|season five]] of ''[[The Americans]]''. She also directed episode 8 ("Chapter 60") of the fifth season of ''[[House of Cards (American TV series)|House of Cards]]''.

Dawson was a producer on ''[[Scandal (TV series)|Scandal]], [[Crossing Jordan]]'' and ''Cold Case''.<ref>[http://www.tvguide.com/celebrities/roxann-dawson/credits/190858 "Roxann Dawson: Credits"]. ''TV Guide''. Retrieved June 4, 2013.</ref>

She then went onto direct two episodes of the first season of ''[[Foundation (TV series)]]''.

===Writing===
Dawson's first play ''Desire to Fall'' was produced by the Circle Repertory Company workshop in 1986.{{citation needed|date=June 2019}} Dawson's second play, ''Passage Through the Heart'' debuted in 1997 at the [[University of Minnesota Duluth]].<ref>Smith, Maureen (January 8, 1997; Editor) [http://conservancy.umn.edu/bitstream/97656/1/Brief_1997.pdf ''Brief'']{{dead link|date=December 2017 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}. [[University of Minnesota]]. Vol. XXVII No. 1, Retrieved June 4, 2013. (PDF file)</ref> From 2000 to 2001, Dawson co-wrote with Daniel Graham a trilogy of science fiction novels, ''Entering Tenebrea'' ({{ISBN|0-671-03607-6}}), ''Tenebrea's Hope'' ({{ISBN|0-671-03609-2}}) and ''Tenebrea Rising'' ({{ISBN|0-671-03611-4}}).

==Personal life==
Dawson was married to actor [[Casey Biggs]], but they eventually divorced. He later became a recurring cast member on the sister series ''[[Star Trek: Deep Space Nine]]'', playing the Cardassian [[Damar (Star Trek)|Damar]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.startrek.com/article/catching-up-with-casey-biggs-part-2|title=Star Trek Catching Up With Casey Biggs, Part 2|publisher=[[Startrek.com]]|date=April 7, 2012|access-date=May 10, 2019|archive-date=July 15, 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180715093752/https://www.startrek.com/article/catching-up-with-casey-biggs-part-2}}</ref>

In May 1994 Dawson married casting director Eric Dawson, whom she met while working on the series ''Nightingales''. They have two daughters, Emma and Mia; Mia was adopted from China.<ref name="LincolnJournalStar"/> Dawson converted to [[Catholic Church|Catholicism]] at the time of her second marriage in 1994 despite having been raised in "an atheist household", and was eventually inspired by her faith to direct the 2019 Christian film ''[[Breakthrough (2019 film)|Breakthrough]]''.<ref>{{cite web|last=Burkepile|first=Jacqueline|title=Atheist-to-Catholic Hollywood Director Reveals How "Breakthrough" Strengthened Her Faith: "It Gives You Chills"|url=https://churchpop.com/2019/07/21/atheist-to-catholic-hollywood-director-shares-how-breakthrough-strengthened-her-faith-it-gives-you-chills/|website=ChurchPOP|publisher=ChurchPOP|access-date=July 21, 2019}}</ref>
