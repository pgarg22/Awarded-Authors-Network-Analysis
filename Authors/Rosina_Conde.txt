'''Rosina Conde''' ({{nee|'''Hilda Rosina Conde Zambada'''}}; born February 10, 1954), is a Mexican narrator, playwright and poet.<ref name="1-">{{cite web |title=Rosina Conde - Detalle del autor - Enciclopedia de la Literatura en México - FLM - CONACULTA |url=http://www.elem.mx/autor/datos/252 |website=www.elem.mx |access-date=4 September 2021 |date=1 January 2021}}</ref>

==Biography==
Hilda Rosina Conde Zambada was born in [[Mexicali]], [[Baja California]], February 10, 1954. She is the daughter of musicians and composers Guillermo Conde and Laura Mabel Zambada Valdez. At the age of four, she moved to [[Tijuana]] where poems and songs were part of her education from an early age:—{{quote|text="I started with oral tradition. My parents were musicians, composers, they taught us all to speak with poems and songs; So I started with rhymes. My father used to compose songs and verses for us to declare, that is, since we were little, they had us doing antics with the visitors every time they came to the house, who were generally artists."<ref name="2and5-">{{cite news |title=Rosina Conde, escritora en su tierra |url=https://zetatijuana.com/2018/11/rosina-conde-escritora-en-su-tierra/ |access-date=4 September 2021 |work=ZETA |date=12 November 2018 |language=en}}</ref><ref name="3-">{{cite news |last1=Ponce |first1=Roberto |title=Medalla Leona Vicario a la poeta Rosina Conde |url=https://www.proceso.com.mx/cultura/2020/12/26/medalla-leona-vicario-la-poeta-rosina-conde-255188.html |access-date=4 September 2021 |work=www.proceso.com.mx |date=26 December 2020 |language=spanish}}</ref>}}

At the age of six, Conde starting writing songs, and at the age of nine, she began writing stories. During her [[secondary education]], she developed a taste for reading authors such as [[Jean-Paul Sartre]], [[Honoré de Balzac]], [[Leo Tolstoy]], and [[Anton Chekhov]].

Conde began to write poetry as well as creating, acting and staging plays. In 1976, she decided to return to Tijuana, where she began to publish poems for the magazine ''Hojas'', during workshops of the [[Autonomous University of Baja California]].<ref name="2and5-" /> She studied Hispanic Language and Literature at the [[School of Philosophy and Letters, UNAM|Faculty of Philosophy and Letters of the National Autonomous University of Mexico]] (UNAM). Later, she obtained a master's degree in Spanish literature from this same university.<ref name="1-" />

She is a co-founder of the degree program in [[Universidad Autónoma de la Ciudad de México#Arts|Creación Literaria]] (literature creation) at the [[Universidad Autónoma de la Ciudad de México]] and is a member of the [[Sistema Nacional de Creadores de Arte]] (national system of art creators).<ref name="4-">{{cite web |title=Nombre:  Conde Zambada Hilda Rosina  - Academia: Creación Literaria |url=https://portalweb.uacm.edu.mx/uacm/Portals/14/Documentos/tarjetas/Conde%20Zambada%20Hilda%20Rosina.pdf |website=portalweb.uacm.edu.mx |language=es}}</ref><ref name="2and5-" />

Conde has published 22 books of various literary genres, including short stories, dramaturgy, essays, novels and poetry.<ref name="2and5-" /><ref name="6-">{{cite web |title=Literatura de Baja California: Autores: Rosina Conde |url=https://larcmaterials.sdsu.edu/material/Baja-mp4/autores/conde.html |website=larcmaterials.sdsu.edu |access-date=4 September 2021}}</ref> There are also works of "Arte de acción" that include a script, costumes and scenery, which have been translated into English and German.<ref name="7-">{{cite web |title=Rosina Conde - Conaculta en los Estados - Conaculta |url=https://www.cultura.gob.mx/estados/actividades_detalle.php?id=205127#.YF-UdK9KjIW |website=www.cultura.gob.mx |access-date=4 September 2021 |language=es}}</ref>

==Awards and honours==
* Río Rita 1990 in Literatura (awarded by the XII City Council of Tijuana, Baja California, 1989).
* Premio Nacional de Literatura “[[Carlos Monsiváis]]” (awarded by the XVI Hispano-American Meeting of Writers "Horas de Junio", 2010).
* Medalla al Mérito Literario “[[Abigael Bohórquez]]” (XXVI Binational Literature Conference Abigael Bohórquez, 2017).
* Premio "[[Gilberto Owen]]" (in the short story category, 1996).
* Tribute at the Feria del Libro del [[Palacio de Minería]] (for 40 years of experience as an editor, writer and teacher).
* Scholarship from the Sistema Nacional de Creadores de Arte.
* Tribute at the Festival de Literatura en el Norte (Felino).
