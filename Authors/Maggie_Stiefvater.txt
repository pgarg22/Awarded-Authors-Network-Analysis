'''Margaret Stiefvater''' ({{IPAc-en|ˈ|s|t|iː|v|ɑː|t|ər}} {{respell|STEE|vah|tər}}; born November 18, 1981) is an American writer of [[Young Adult fiction]], known mainly for her series of [[Fantasy literature|fantasy]] novels ''[[The Wolves of Mercy Falls]]'' and ''[[The Raven Cycle]]''. She currently lives in [[Virginia]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www2.scholastic.com/browse/contributor.jsp?id=3747725 |title=Maggie Stiefvater's Biography |publisher=Scholastic.com |access-date=2014-02-10 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110811205518/http://www2.scholastic.com/browse/contributor.jsp?id=3747725 |archive-date=2011-08-11 }}</ref>

== Life and career ==

=== Early life ===

Stiefvater was born in [[Harrisonburg, Virginia]]. She described herself as an "anxious child with many phobias."<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://twitter.com/mstiefvater/status/659794269931225088|title=Maggie Stiefvater on Twitter|website=Twitter|access-date=2016-04-26}}</ref> As a child, she wanted to be a fighter pilot and race-car driver<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.thedailybeast.com/articles/2012/09/28/maggie-stiefvater-talks-new-novel-the-raven-boys-fast-cars-and-ya-fiction.html|title=Maggie Stiefvater Talks New Novel 'The Raven Boys,' Fast Cars, and YA Fiction|last=Stanton|first=Doug|website=The Daily Beast|access-date=2016-04-26}}</ref> and was a voracious reader who enjoyed writing.<ref name=":0">{{Cite book|title=Maggie Stiefvater|last=Staley|first=Erin}}</ref> By age 16, she was submitting manuscripts to publishers.<ref name=":0" /> After being home-schooled from sixth grade on,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.schoollibraryjournal.com/slj/articles/interviews/893044-338/the_mane_event_maggie_stiefvater.html.csp |title=School Library Journal |publisher=Schoollibraryjournal.com |access-date=2014-02-10}}</ref> Stiefvater attended [[University of Mary Washington|Mary Washington College]], graduating with a B.A. in history.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.iesb.net/index.php?option=com_content&view=article&id=7666:exclusive-interview-maggie-stiefvaters-shiver-may-turn-out-to-be-another-twilight-or-harry-potter-franchise&catid=43:exclusive-fea |title=Whois Lookup |publisher=Iesb.net |access-date=2014-02-10}}</ref> By the time she had entered college, she had already written over 30 novels, including four thrillers about the [[Irish Republican Army (1922–69)|Irish Republican Army]], a historical blockade runner novel, and a high-fantasy novel about "impassioned enchanters fighting among civil unrest."<ref name=":0" /> At 16, she legally changed her first name from Heidi to Margaret.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://m-stiefvater.livejournal.com/189746.html |archive-url=https://archive.today/20120710122025/http://m-stiefvater.livejournal.com/189746.html |url-status=dead |archive-date=2012-07-10 |title=Words on Words by Maggie Stiefvater – In Which Maggie Helps With Homework |publisher=M-stiefvater.livejournal.com |date=2011-01-25 |access-date=2014-02-10 }}</ref> Her maiden name was Hummel.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://maggie-stiefvater.tumblr.com/post/124398175731/hi-are-you-an-anarchist-or-a-democrat-or |title= What is Stiefvater's political stance?|publisher=Tumbrl |date=2015-07-18 |access-date=2017-10-25 }}</ref> After graduating, she worked as a portrait artist, specializing in equestrian art.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.chasengalleries.com |title=Fine Art and Glass Gallery Richmond Virginia |publisher=Chasen Galleries |access-date=2014-02-10}}</ref> In 2010, she gave a [[TEDx talk|TEDx Talk]] for NASA entitled "How Bad Teens Become Famous People",<ref>{{Citation|title=TEDxNASA - Maggie Stiefvater - How Bad Teens Become Famous People|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gIJPg3wfJ1c|language=en|access-date=2020-02-26}}</ref> in which she reflects on her youth as a "Bad Teen" and how those years have impacted her.

=== Writing career ===

Stiefvater published her first novel, ''Lament'', in 2008.<ref>{{cite web|last=Leitich |first=Cynthia |url=http://cynthialeitichsmith.blogspot.com/2008/10/author-interview-maggie-stiefvater-on.html |title=Cynsations: Author Interview: Maggie Stiefvater on Lament: The Faerie Queen's Deception |publisher=Cynthialeitichsmith.blogspot.com |date=2008-10-14 |access-date=2014-02-10}}</ref> Before ''Lament'' had been released, she sold the rights to ''Ballad,'' the sequel to ''Lament,'' and to ''Shiver,'' the first book in the trilogy ''[[The Wolves of Mercy Falls]]''.<ref>{{cite web|last=Thornton |first=Matthew |url=http://www.publishersweekly.com/pw/by-topic/industry-news/book-deals/article/8189-deals-elements-of-graphic-style.html |title=Deals: Elements of Graphic Style |publisher=Publishersweekly.com |date=2008-09-15 |access-date=2014-02-10}}</ref> ''Shiver'' spent more than 40 weeks on [[The New York Times Best Seller list|''The New York Times'' Best Seller list]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://nationalwritersseries.org/fantasy-writer-makes-real-impact-on-fiction-genre/ |title=Fantasy writer makes real impact on fiction genre |publisher=National Writers Series |access-date=2014-02-10 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140222152735/http://nationalwritersseries.org/fantasy-writer-makes-real-impact-on-fiction-genre/ |archive-date=2014-02-22 |url-status=dead }}</ref> There are over 1.7 million copies of ''The Wolves of Mercy Falls'' series in print and more than thirty-six foreign editions have been licensed.<ref>{{cite web|title = Maggie Stiefvater's Biography|url = http://www.scholastic.com/teachers/contributor/maggie-stiefvater|website = Scholastic Teachers|access-date = 2015-11-06}}</ref>

In 2011, Stiefvater published ''The Scorpio Races'', which received 5 starred reviews and was named a [[Michael L. Printz Award]] Honor Book.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Bartel|first1=Julie|title=One Thing Leads to Another: An Interview with Maggie Stiefvater|url=http://www.yalsa.ala.org/thehub/2014/01/09/one-thing-leads-to-another-an-interview-with-maggie-stiefvater/|website=YALSA The Hub|publisher=American Library Association|access-date=6 July 2015}}</ref>

Stiefvater has been very open about sharing her techniques and methods when writing. She has a series of blog posts entitled "[http://maggiestiefvater.com/tag/how-i-write/ how i write]" describing her different approaches and sharing advice.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://maggiestiefvater.com/|title=how i write|website=Maggie Stiefvater|language=en-US|access-date=2020-02-26}}</ref> In 2018 and 2019, Maggie Stiefvater gave writing seminars entitled Portraits & Dreams: Writing with Maggie Stiefvater.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://maggiestiefvater.com/portraits-dreams-writing-with-maggie-stiefvater-in-edinburgh/|title=Portraits & Dreams: Writing with Maggie Stiefvater in Edinburgh|language=en-US|access-date=2020-02-26}}</ref> It included a lecture and a Q&A. She gave this lecture in [[Edinburgh]], [[New York City]], [[Seattle]], [[Austin, Texas|Austin]], [[Los Angeles]], [[Brooklyn]], [[Toronto]], and [[Vancouver]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://maggiestiefvater.com/|title=Search results for|website=Maggie Stiefvater|language=en-US|access-date=2020-02-26}}</ref>

=== Music ===
Stiefvater plays various musical instruments.<ref>{{cite web|last=Lodge |first=Sally |url=http://www.publishersweekly.com/pw/by-topic/authors/interviews/article/52295-pw-talks-with-maggie-stiefvater.html |title=PW Talks with Maggie Stiefvater |publisher=Publishersweekly.com |date=2012-06-05 |access-date=2014-02-10}}</ref> She recorded original compositions for the audio books of ''The Scorpio Races''<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.audiobooks.com/audiobook/scorpio-races/136248|title=Scorpio Races|website=Audiobooks.com|language=en|access-date=2020-02-26}}</ref> and ''[[The Raven Cycle]]''.<ref>{{Cite book|url=https://www.audible.com/|title=Audiobooks & Original Audio Shows - Get More from Audible|language=en}}</ref>  She has a SoundCloud account where she releases her original tracks.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://soundcloud.com/maggie_stiefvater/sets/maggie-stiefvater|title=Maggie Stiefvater|website=SoundCloud|language=en|access-date=2020-02-26}}</ref> Stiefvater is very connected to music and has released playlists for some of her novels of songs she listened to while writing.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://maggiestiefvater.com/call-down-the-hawk-playlist/|title=Call Down the Hawk playlist|language=en-US|access-date=2020-02-26}}</ref>

=== Art ===

[[File:Cartoon character maggie.png|thumb|Self portrait by the author.]]
Before turning to writing full-time, Stiefvater was a professional portrait artist, specializing in colored pencil.<ref>[https://web.archive.org/web/20130104082834/http://www.cbcbooks.org/sub-news.php?id=499 "Maggie Stiefvater on Creating an Animated Book Trailer for ''The Raven Boys''"]. Children's Book Council (cbcbooks.org). August 6, 2012. Archived 2013-01-04. Retrieved 2014-02-10.</ref> She currently has her own Etsy page and Society6 page where she sells her original art.

Stiefvater also created a Tarot card deck, The Raven's Prophecy Tarot Cards in September 2015.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://maggiestiefvater.com/tarot-deck-release/|title=Tarot Deck Release|language=en-US|access-date=2020-02-24}}</ref>

She was asked to create a poster for the [[American Library Association]] to promote reading.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.alastore.ala.org/content/raven-cycle-poster|title=Raven Cycle Poster {{!}} ALA Store|website=www.alastore.ala.org|access-date=2020-02-26}}</ref> The poster includes characters from ''[[The Raven Cycle]]'' and the phrase "The future belongs to those who read."

=== Cars ===
Stiefvater greatly enjoys cars, especially fast ones.<ref name=":1"/> She has completed a stunt driving class.<ref name="A Bullet-Point Decade">{{Cite web|url=http://maggiestiefvater.com/a-bullet-point-decade/|title=A Bullet-Point Decade|language=en-US|access-date=2020-02-26}}</ref> She has worked as an automotive journalist.<ref name=":1">{{Cite web|url=https://www.roadandtrack.com/new-cars/first-drives/a23894949/rolls-royce-cullinen-luxury-suv-first-drive-review/|title=The Rolls-Royce Cullinan Is Spectacular|last=Stiefvater|first=Maggie|date=2018-10-19|website=Road & Track|language=en-US|access-date=2020-02-26}}</ref><ref name=":2">{{Cite web|url=https://www.roadandtrack.com/car-culture/a25635381/driving-the-last-brand-new-mitsubishi-evo-to-its-grave/|title=Driving the Last Brand-New Mitsubishi Evo to its Grave|last=Stiefvater|first=Maggie|date=2019-01-10|website=Road & Track|language=en-US|access-date=2020-02-26}}</ref>

Stiefvater has frequently used her passion for vehicles to promote her novels. To promote the second book of ''[[The Raven Cycle]]'', ''The Dream Thieves'', Stiefvater spray-painted her own car. She later allowed fans to also spray-paint the vehicle at the book's launch in Kansas City on September 18, 2013.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://maggiestiefvater.com/in-which-readers-become-hooligans-briefly/|title=In Which Readers Become Hooligans (Briefly)|language=en-US|access-date=2020-02-26}}</ref>  She repeated this for another event in October 2016, where she let fans paint her [[Mitsubishi Lancer Evolution X]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://twitter.com/mstiefvater/status/787991940293885952|title=So, readers painted my car yesterday.pic.twitter.com/Pl9ne7hm3r|last=Stiefvater|first=Maggie|date=2016-10-17|website=@mstiefvater|language=en|access-date=2020-02-26}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://jalopnik.com/why-you-should-give-your-car-a-shitty-paint-job-1795704984|title=Why You Should Give Your Car A Shitty Paint Job|last=Stiefvater|first=Maggie|website=Jalopnik|language=en-us|access-date=2020-02-26}}</ref> In 2013 Stiefvater went [[Rallying|rally racing]] in a race car printed with the cover of ''The Raven Boys''.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://maggiestiefvater.com/band-name-alert-damage-waiver/|title=Band Name Alert: Damage Waiver|language=en-US|access-date=2020-02-26}}</ref><ref>{{Citation|last=Stiefvater|first=Maggie|title=SnoDrift 2013|date=2013-01-30|url=https://www.flickr.com/photos/maggiestiefvater/8430030599/|access-date=2020-02-26}}</ref>

In 2015, Stiefvater drove her [[Mitsubishi Lancer Evolution X]] in a race against fellow author [[John Green (author)|John Green]] at the Princeton Speedway.<ref>{{Citation|title=Maggie Stiefvater vs. John Green at the Princeton Speedway|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=fKokQ-Anx4g|language=en|access-date=2020-02-26}}</ref><ref name="jalopnik.com">{{Cite web|url=https://jalopnik.com/what-its-like-to-destroy-a-fire-breathing-mitsubishi-ev-1745775434|title=What It's Like To Destroy A Fire-Breathing Mitsubishi Evolution|website=Jalopnik|language=en-us|access-date=2020-02-26}}</ref> Both their vehicles caught fire.<ref name="jalopnik.com"/><ref name="A Bullet-Point Decade"/>

=== Personal life ===

Stiefvater has a personal blog where she shares her life events.<ref name="A Bullet-Point Decade"/> Stiefvater is married to a "straight-laced husband"<ref name="A Bullet-Point Decade"/> and has two children.<ref>[https://web.archive.org/web/20120311140758/http://www.wydaily.com/arts/2503-cw-and-werewolves-shiver-has-worldwide-buzz-to-launch-at-wm-bookstore.html "CW and Werewolves? ''Shiver'' Has Worldwide Buzz, to Launch at WM Bookstore"]. ''Williamsburg Yorktown Daily''. August 2, 2009. Archived 2012-03-11. Retrieved 2014-02-10.</ref> She has four dogs named Winnie, Parsifal, Jane, and Rose.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://twitter.com/mstiefvater/status/1197926434460913669|title=(from upper left, clockwise: Winnie, Parsifal, Jane, Rose, a stuffed hedgehog)|last=Stiefvater|first=Maggie|date=2019-11-22|website=@mstiefvater|language=en|access-date=2020-02-26}}</ref> She also has nine goats<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://maggiestiefvater.com/things-fall-down/|title=Things Fall Down|language=en-US|access-date=2020-02-26}}</ref> and a horse.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.facebook.com/MaggieStiefvaterAuthorPage/posts/10157765112539466|title=Maggie Stiefvater (Really, it's me)|website=www.facebook.com|language=en|access-date=2020-02-26}}</ref>

In 2019, Stiefvater was diagnosed with [[Addison's disease]] after she collapsed on a book tour.<ref name="The Years Without Words">{{Cite web|url=http://maggiestiefvater.com/the-years-without-words/|title=The Years Without Words|language=en-US|access-date=2020-02-26}}</ref> She has been very public about her health condition in the hope that it would raise awareness for others.<ref name="The Years Without Words"/>

== Film adaptations ==
Unique Features, in association with Warner Bros., optioned ''Shiver'''s film rights shortly after the book was released.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.southportreporter.com/526/526-10.shtml |title=Mersey Reporter & Southport Reporter – News page |publisher=Southportreporter.com |access-date=2014-02-10}}</ref> A screenplay was written by [[Nick Pustay]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://novelnovice.com/2010/02/12/screenwriter-hired-for-movie-of-shiver-by-maggie-stiefvater/ |title=Screenwriter hired for movie of Shiver by Maggie Stiefvater |publisher=Novel Novice |date=2010-02-12 |access-date=2014-02-10}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.variety.com/article/VR1118009345.html?categoryid=13&cs=1 |title=Unique sparks to 'Shiver' |publisher=Variety |date=2009-09-29 |access-date=2014-02-10}}</ref>

It was reported in 2011 that David Katzenberg and Seth Grahame-Smith’s KatzSmith Productions would produce a film of ''Scorpio Races''.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.hollywoodreporter.com/heat-vision/warner-bros-scorpio-races-254345 |title=Warner Bros. Developing 'Scorpio Races' Fantasy (Exclusive) |publisher=Hollywoodreporter.com |date=2011-10-27 |access-date=2014-02-10}}</ref> New Line Cinema, in conjunction with Weed Road, optioned the film rights for ''The Raven Boys'' shortly before the book's release in September 2012.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.hollywoodreporter.com/heat-vision/new-line-raven-boys-maggie-stiefvater-370454 |title=New Line Picks Up Maggie Stiefvater's YA Novel 'The Raven Boys' |publisher=Hollywoodreporter.com |date=2012-09-14 |access-date=2014-02-10}}</ref>

In 2019, Stiefvater wrote the pilot for a TV show of ''[[The Raven Cycle]]''.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://twitter.com/mstiefvater/status/1167867232044503043|title=Oh right, I read chapter 14 really recently, though, when I wrote the pilot for the TV show (yes, it's still in development, KEEP BEING PATIENT)pic.twitter.com/jydKJ98so1|last=Stiefvater|first=Maggie|date=2019-08-31|website=@mstiefvater|language=en|access-date=2020-02-26}}</ref>
