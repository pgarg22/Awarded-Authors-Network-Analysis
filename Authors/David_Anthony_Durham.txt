'''David Anthony Durham''' (born March 23, 1969) is an American novelist, author of historical fiction and fantasy.

Durham's first novel, ''Gabriel's Story'', centered on [[African American]] settlers in the [[American Old West|American West]]. ''Walk Through Darkness'' followed a runaway slave during the tense times leading up to the [[American Civil War]]. ''Pride of Carthage'' focused on [[Hannibal Barca]] of [[Carthage|Ancient Carthage]] and his war with the [[Roman Republic]]. His novels have twice been ''[[New York Times]]'' Notable Books, won two awards from the [[American Library Association]], and been translated into eight foreign languages. [[Gabriel's Story]], [[Walk Through Darkness]] and [[Acacia: The War with the Mein]] are all in development as feature films. A third book, [[Acacia: The Sacred Band]], concludes his [[epic fantasy]] Acacia Trilogy. In 2016, Durham returned to historical fiction with the publication of The Risen: A Novel of Spartacus.

Born to parents of [[Caribbean]] ancestry, Durham has lived in Scotland for a number of years. He has worked as an Outward Bound Instructor, and as a whitewater raft guide and [[kayak]] instructor. After receiving an [[Master of Fine Arts|MFA]] from the [[University of Maryland, College Park]] in 1996, he taught at the [[University of Maryland, College Park|University of Maryland]] and [[University of Massachusetts Amherst]]. He was the MacLean Distinguished Visiting Writer at [[Colorado College|The Colorado College]] and was an associate professor at Cal State University, Fresno and an adjunct professor at [[Hampshire College]]. He won the [[Hurston-Wright Legacy Award|Zora Neale Hurston/Richard Wright Fiction Award]] in 1992, the 2002 Legacy Award for Debut Fiction and was a Finalist for the 2006 Legacy Award for Fiction. In 2009, he won the [[John W. Campbell Award for Best New Writer]]. He currently teaches for the [[Stonecoast MFA Program in Creative Writing]] and is an assistant professor of creative writing at the University of Nevada, Reno.
