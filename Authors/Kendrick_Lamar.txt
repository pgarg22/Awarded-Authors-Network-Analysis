'''Kendrick Lamar Duckworth''' (born June 17, 1987)<!-- Please do not include "Kendrick Lamar" separately. --> is an American rapper, songwriter and record producer. Since his mainstream debut in 2012 with ''[[Good Kid, M.A.A.D City]]'', Lamar has been regarded as one of the most influential rappers of his generation.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Tylt|first=The|title=Greatest rapper of this generation: Kendrick Lamar or J. Cole?|url=https://thetylt.com/entertainment/j-cole-kendrick-lamar-goat-rappers|access-date=August 18, 2020|website=The Tylt|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite magazine|date=December 22, 2017|title=Is Kendrick Lamar the Greatest Rapper of Our Generation? Kehlani, LeBron James & More Weigh In|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/events/year-in-music-2017/8078455/kendrick-lamar-greatest-rapper-of-generation-debate-interview|access-date=August 18, 2020|magazine=Billboard}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|last=Braboy|first=Mark|title=Kendrick Lamar is the artist of the decade|url=https://www.insider.com/kendrick-lamar-artist-of-the-decade-2010s-2019-12|access-date=August 18, 2020|website=Insider}}</ref> Aside from his solo career, he is also known as a member of the hip hop supergroup [[Black Hippy]] alongside his [[Top Dawg Entertainment]] (TDE) label-mates [[Ab-Soul]], [[Jay Rock]], and [[Schoolboy Q]].

Raised in [[Compton, California]], Lamar embarked on his musical career as a teenager under the stage name '''K.Dot''', releasing a mixtape that garnered local attention and led to his signing with indie record label Top Dawg Entertainment. He began to gain recognition in 2010 after his first retail release, ''[[Overly Dedicated]]''. The following year, he independently released his first studio album, ''[[Section.80]]'', which included his debut single "[[HiiiPoWeR]]". By that time, he had amassed a large online following and collaborated with several prominent [[Hip hop music|hip hop]] artists.

Lamar's major-label debut album, ''[[Good Kid, M.A.A.D City]]'', was released in 2012 to critical acclaim. It would go on to be certified [[Platinum certification|triple platinum]] by the [[Recording Industry Association of America]] (RIAA). His third album ''[[To Pimp a Butterfly]]'' (2015) incorporated elements of [[funk]], [[soul (music)|soul]], [[jazz]], and [[spoken word]]. It became his first number one album on the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]], and was the most acclaimed album of the 2010s, according to statistics from [[Metacritic]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.metacritic.com/feature/best-albums-of-the-decade-2010s?fbclid=IwAR3HsIfiNV2zQt8HM3dKFiJeAhQhLwZDjFLv-btOYSKMQf2TuvRQFWXI79A|title=Best Albums of the Decade|website=Metacritic|language=en|access-date=April 1, 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190406225640/https://www.metacritic.com/browse/albums/score/metascore/all/filtered|archive-date=April 6, 2019|url-status=live}}</ref> It was followed by ''[[Untitled Unmastered]]'' (2016), a collection of unreleased [[Demo (music)|demos]] that originated during the recording sessions for ''To Pimp a Butterfly''. He released his fourth album, ''[[Damn (Kendrick Lamar album)|Damn]]'' (2017) to further acclaim; its lead single "[[Humble (song)|Humble]]" topped the US [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]], while the album became the first non-classical and non-jazz album to be awarded the [[Pulitzer Prize for Music]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.bbc.com/news/business-43789936|title=Kendrick Lamar wins Pulitzer music prize|last=Johnston|first=Chris|date=April 17, 2018|work=BBC News|access-date=May 31, 2018|language=en-GB|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180530000200/http://www.bbc.com/news/business-43789936|archive-date=May 30, 2018|url-status=live}}</ref> In 2018, he wrote and produced 14 songs for the [[Black Panther (soundtrack)|soundtrack]] to the superhero film [[Black Panther (film)|''Black Panther'']], which also received critical acclaim.

Lamar has received [[List of awards and nominations received by Kendrick Lamar|many accolades]] over the course of his career, including 13 [[Grammy Award]]s, two [[American Music Awards]], six [[Billboard Music Award|''Billboard'' Music Awards]], a [[Brit Awards|Brit Award]], 11 [[MTV Video Music Award]]s, a [[Pulitzer Prize]], and an [[Academy Awards|Academy Award]] nomination. In 2012, [[MTV]] named him the [[MTV's Hottest MCs in the Game#2012|Hottest MC in the Game]] on their annual list.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.mtv.com/news/articles/1703255/hottest-mcs-2013-kendrick-lamar.jhtml |title=Kendrick Lamar Brings Crown To Compton As 'Hottest MC in the Game |publisher=MTV |date=March 7, 2013 |access-date=March 7, 2013 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140312012725/http://www.mtv.com/news/articles/1703255/hottest-mcs-2013-kendrick-lamar.jhtml |archive-date=March 12, 2014 |url-status=live }}</ref> ''[[Time (magazine)|Time]]'' named him one of the [[Time 100|100 most influential people in the world]] in 2016.<ref name="time 100" /> In 2015, he received the [[California State Senate]]'s Generational Icon Award. Three of his studio albums have been listed in ''[[Rolling Stone]]'''s [[Rolling Stone's 500 Greatest Albums of All Time|500 Greatest Albums of All Time]] (2020).

== Controversies ==
=== Lyrics ===
Lamar's 2015 song "The Blacker the Berry" gathered controversy following the lines, "So why did I weep when [[Trayvon Martin]] was in the street, when gang-banging make me kill a nigga blacker than me? Hypocrite!" Some fans perceived the line to be Lamar judging the black community.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/music/articles/de986537-03ab-49ed-a0a0-165577dfbdd4|title=8 song lyrics that caused a storm of controversy - BBC Music|date=July 21, 2016|language=en-GB|access-date=February 8, 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180220061508/https://www.bbc.co.uk/music/articles/de986537-03ab-49ed-a0a0-165577dfbdd4|archive-date=February 20, 2018|url-status=live}}</ref> Lamar later spoke on the lyrics in a NPR interview, saying, "It's not me pointing at my community; it's me pointing at myself, I don't talk about these things if I haven't lived them, and I've hurt people in my life. It's something I still have to think about when I sleep at night."<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.npr.org/2015/12/29/461129966/kendrick-lamar-i-cant-change-the-world-until-i-change-myself-first|title=Kendrick Lamar: 'I Can't Change The World Until I Change Myself First'|work=NPR.org|access-date=February 8, 2018|language=en|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180209063402/https://www.npr.org/2015/12/29/461129966/kendrick-lamar-i-cant-change-the-world-until-i-change-myself-first|archive-date=February 9, 2018|url-status=live}}</ref>

Following the release of Lamar's 2017 song "[[Humble (song)|Humble]]", he faced backlash for the lines, "I'm so fucking sick and tired of the [[Photoshop]] / Show me something natural like afro on [[Richard Pryor]] / Show me something natural like ass with some stretch marks." He was accused of putting down sections of women who enjoy makeup in an attempt to be uplifting.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2017/03/kendrick-lamars-humble-sparks-feminist-backlash/|title=Kendrick Lamar's 'Humble' Song Sparks Feminist Backlash - XXL|website=XXL Mag|access-date=February 8, 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180208092022/http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2017/03/kendrick-lamars-humble-sparks-feminist-backlash/|archive-date=February 8, 2018|url-status=live}}</ref> Female labelmate [[SZA (singer)|SZA]] later defended Lamar.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2017/07/sza-defends-controversial-kendrick-lamar-lyrics-humble/|title=SZA Defends Controversial Kendrick Lamar Lyrics on 'Humble' - XXL|website=XXL Mag|access-date=February 8, 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180208102056/http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2017/07/sza-defends-controversial-kendrick-lamar-lyrics-humble/|archive-date=February 8, 2018|url-status=live}}</ref> The model who appeared in the music video for "Humble" was also attacked on social media due to her role in the video.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.newsweek.com/kendrick-lamar-humble-damn-album-song-584606|title=Kendrick Lamar's praise of "natural" beauty sparked more misogyny between women than his lyrics did|date=April 14, 2017|work=Newsweek|access-date=February 8, 2018|language=en|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180208182635/http://www.newsweek.com/kendrick-lamar-humble-damn-album-song-584606|archive-date=February 8, 2018|url-status=live}}</ref>
