William '''Vaughan Wilkins''' (March 6, 1890 – February 1959) was an English [[historical novelist]] and journalist. <ref name="kunitz">[[Stanley Kunitz]] and H. W. Wilson ''Twentieth Century Authors: A Biographical Dictionary of Modern Literature. Supplement, Volume 1''. New York, 1955. (p. 1083)</ref> Wilkins was born and raised in England. Wilkins was interested in Welsh history, and some of his stories have Welsh settings, causing some writers to mistakenly describe Wilkins as Welsh.<ref name="kunitz" />

==Biography==
{{Expand section|date=January 2008}}
Vaughan Wilkins was born in Camberwell, London. His father, William Henry Wilkins, was a clergyman (born in Nottingham in 1857)  and his mother, Charlotte Wilkins, née Law, a voice teacher (born in London).<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://reading19001950.wordpress.com/2013/11/13/vaughan-wilkins-1890-1959/|title = Vaughan Wilkins (1890-1959)|date = 13 November 2013}}</ref> He married Mary Isabel Stanistreet and had two children. He spent some time working as a journalist for the ''[[Daily Express]]''. Wilkins was noted for his novel ''And So - Victoria'' about [[Queen Victoria]], which became a surprise bestseller in the United States.<ref name="kunitz" /><ref>[https://web.archive.org/web/20121105172543/http://www.time.com/time/magazine/article/0,9171,770768-1,00.html TIME magazine review of "And So-Victoria"]</ref> ''Fanfare for a Witch'' focuses on intrigue in the court of [[George II of Great Britain|George II]].<ref>"Have You Read..."Fanfare for a Witch" by Vaughan Wilkins". ''[[Record-Journal|Meridan Record]]'', August 5th, 1954  (p.4).</ref> Wilkins also wrote two [[Lost World (genre)|"lost world"]] [[fantasy]] novels inspired by [[Celts|Celtic]] mythology, ''The City of Frozen Fire'' (1950) and ''Valley Beyond Time'' (1955). <ref>[[Brian Stableford]], "Wilkins, Vaughan (William)" in ''St. James Guide To Fantasy Writers'', ed. [[David Pringle]], St. James Press, 1996, {{ISBN|1-55862-205-5}}, p. 611-12.</ref>
His grave is in the churchyard in Farnsfield, Notts and states that his birthplace was Ross-on-Wye.
