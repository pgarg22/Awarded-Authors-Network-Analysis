'''Sarah Klassen''' (born 1932) is a Canadian writer.<ref>{{cite book |title=A cappella: Mennonite voices in poetry |last=Hostetler |first=Ann Elizabeth |year=2003 |publisher=University of Iowa Press |location=Iowa City |isbn=0-87745-874-X |page=16 |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=8nYK17LyKWYC&pg=PA16&lpg=PA16 |accessdate=19 May 2011}}</ref> She is the author of ''A Feast of Longing'' and one other short fiction collection, ''The Peony Season'', and five books of poetry. A novel, ''The Wittenbergs'', was published in 2013. Klassen's first volume of poetry, ''Journey to Yalta'', was awarded the [[Gerald Lampert Award|Gerald Lampert Memorial Award]] in 1989. Klassen is the recipient of Canadian Authors Association Award for Poetry<ref>{{Cite news|last=Unrau-Poetker|first=Emily|date=13 October 2020|title=Sarah Klassen — ‘The Tree of Life’|work=The Manitoban|url=http://www.themanitoban.com/2020/10/sarah-klassen-the-tree-of-life/40503/|url-status=live|access-date=18 March 2021}}</ref> and Klassen's novel, ''The Wittenbergs'', was awarded the [[Margaret McWilliams Award]] for popular history.<ref name=":0" /> 

She holds a Bachelor of Arts degree and a Bachelor of Education degree from the [[University of Winnipeg]]. Aside from writing, she has taught English in the public school system in Winnipeg and at summer institutes in Lithuania and Ukraine. Klassen was born in [[Winnipeg]], [[Manitoba]], and currently resides there.
