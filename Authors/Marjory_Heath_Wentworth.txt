'''Marjory Heath Wentworth''' (born June 3, 1958) is an American poet. She was named by [[Governor of South Carolina|Governor]] [[Mark Sanford]] as the sixth [[South Carolina Poet Laureate]] in 2003.

==Biography==

===Early life and education===
Wentworth was born Marjory Heath on June 3, 1958, in [[Lynn, Massachusetts]], and raised in nearby [[Swampscott, Massachusetts|Swampscott]].<ref name="High Profile">{{cite news|last=Watts|first=Judy|title=Hard times have inspired new poet laureate of South Carolina to become advocate for writing|url=https://news.google.com/newspapers?id=lttQAAAAIBAJ&pg=4014%2C6636561|accessdate=December 20, 2012|newspaper=The Charleston Post and Courier|date=August 30, 2003}}</ref><ref name=SCCftB>{{cite web|title=Marjory Wentworth|url=http://faculty.libsci.sc.edu/literarymap/authors/wentw.htm|publisher=South Carolina Center for the Book|accessdate=December 20, 2012|url-status=dead|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20140127113503/http://faculty.libsci.sc.edu/literarymap/authors/wentw.htm|archivedate=January 27, 2014}}</ref> Her parents were John and Mary (Tully) Heath.<ref name="SC Encyclopedia">{{cite book|title=The South Carolina Encyclopedia|year=2006|publisher=University of South Carolina Press|location=Columbia, South Carolina|isbn=978-1-57003-598-2|chapter=Wentworth, Marjory Heath|page=1014}}</ref> As a child, she spent many years in and out of hospitals to correct some congenital organ anomalies. Adding further hardship was the fact that her father, John, a purchasing agent for [[Parker Brothers]], died of [[leukemia]] when she was just 14 years old.<ref name="High Profile" /><ref name=Abedon>{{cite news|last=Abedon|first=Emily Perlman|title=Charleston Profile: Marjory Wentworth|url=http://charlestonmag.com/charleston_magazine/feature/marjory_wentworth|accessdate=December 20, 2012|newspaper=Charleston Magazine|date=January 2009|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130826215859/http://charlestonmag.com/charleston_magazine/feature/marjory_wentworth|archive-date=August 26, 2013|url-status=dead}}</ref>

She graduated from [[Mount Holyoke College]] (where she majored in anthropology, political science, and dance)<ref name="High Profile" /> and went on to receive her [[Master of Arts|M.A.]] in Writing from [[New York University]] (NYU). While at NYU, she studied under [[Galway Kinnell]], [[Philip Levine (poet)|Phil Levine]], [[Joseph Brodsky]], and [[Carolyn Forché]].<ref name=Abedon /> After Mount Holyoke, she did some studies at [[Oxford University]].<ref name="High Profile" />

===Career===
While still a graduate student at New York University, Wentworth worked in refugee resettlement with the [[United Nations]] High Commission on Refugees.<ref name=Abedon /> She went on after graduating to work as a book publicist with Readers International, a branch of [[Amnesty International]], interviewing with Brodsky for the job.<ref name="High Profile" />

After moving to South Carolina with her husband in 1989, Wentworth began teaching both children and adults in the area. In 1993, she started teaching as an adjunct instructor at [[Trident Technical College]] in [[North Charleston, South Carolina]].<ref name="SC Encyclopedia" /> For many years she has conducted the "Expressions of Healing" class at Roper Hospital in Charleston. The class focuses on those affected by cancer.<ref name=LCLmag>{{cite news|last=Williams|first=Stephanie Burt|title=It's What I Can Do|url=http://video.theeagle.com/news/2008/sep/05/featu/|accessdate=December 20, 2012|newspaper=Low Country Living Magazine|date=September 5, 2008|url-status=dead|archiveurl=https://archive.is/20130204090245/http://video.theeagle.com/news/2008/sep/05/featu/|archivedate=February 4, 2013}}</ref> She also teaches at the Charleston County School of the Arts<ref name=Abedon /> and the creative writing class at [[The Art Institute of Charleston]].<ref name=About>{{cite web|title=About|url=http://www.marjorywentworth.net/wp/about/|publisher=Marjory Heath Wentworth|accessdate=December 20, 2012|url-status=dead|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20130204000208/http://www.marjorywentworth.net/wp/about/|archivedate=February 4, 2013}}</ref> She is also president of the Lowcountry Initiative for the Literary Arts.<ref>{{cite web|title=LILA Board Members|url=http://lilaconnects.com/board-members|publisher=Lowcountry Initiative for the Literary Arts|accessdate=December 20, 2012}}</ref>

Wentworth and her husband Peter run Wentworth PR which manages [[public relations]] for publishers and authors such as Dottie Frank, [[Mary Alice Monroe]], and Gary Smith.<ref name="High Profile" />

===Poet laureateship===
Wentworth read the inaugural poem at [[Mark Sanford]]'s first inauguration as Governor of South Carolina.<ref name="High Profile" /> Shortly thereafter, in 2003, he appointed her as the sixth South Carolina Poet Laureate.<ref>{{cite web|title=Poet Laureate|url=http://www.southcarolinaarts.com/arts/poet.shtml|publisher=South Carolina Arts Commission|accessdate=December 8, 2012}}</ref> Usually given a small [[honorarium]] ($1,200) as poet laureate, this honorarium was cut by Sanford in 2003 when Wentworth agreed to serve without the pay.<ref>{{cite news|last=Stanton|first=David|title=Gov. Sanford releases budget vetoes|url=http://www.wistv.com/Global/story.asp?S=1326348&nav=0RaPGRl8|accessdate=December 20, 2012|newspaper=WIS TV channel 10|date=June 18, 2003}}</ref> As poet laureate, Wentworth is on the board of directors of The Poetry Society of South Carolina.<ref>{{cite web|title=Board of Directors|url=http://www.poetrysocietysc.org/board.html|publisher=The Poetry Society of South Carolina|accessdate=December 20, 2012}}</ref>

===Personal life===
She is married to filmmaker Peter Wentworth and they have three sons. They were introduced by her brother, Jack, and were married soon after graduating from college,<ref name="High Profile" /> on June 27, 1981.<ref name="SC Encyclopedia" /> The Wentworth family moved to [[Sullivan's Island, South Carolina]], and lived there for many years, beginning in 1989. Shortly after relocating, though, [[Hurricane Hugo]] hit the area hard and they were unable to live in their house for nearly a year. It was during that time that their third child was born.<ref name="High Profile" /> Later, the family moved to nearby [[Mount Pleasant, South Carolina|Mount Pleasant]] in 2004.<ref>{{cite journal|last=Tibbetts|first=John H.|title=The Coast's Great Leap|journal=Coastal Heritage|date=Fall 2004|volume=19|issue=2|url=http://www.scseagrant.org/content/?cid=176|publisher=South Carolina Sea Grant Consortium}}</ref>

Wentworth is a close friend of former South Carolina first lady [[Jenny Sanford]].<ref>{{cite news|last=Hankla|first=Kristen|title=Marjory Wentworth, South Carolina's poet laureate|url=http://www.postandcourier.com/article/20091211/ARCHIVES/312119918|accessdate=December 20, 2012|newspaper=The Charleston Post and Courier|date=December 11, 2009}}</ref>
