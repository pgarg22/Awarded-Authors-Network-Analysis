'''Harold Harley''' (20 June 1860 – 11 August 1937), known by his [[pen name]] '''Mark Ambient''',  was an English actor and dramatist. He is particularly noted as a writer of the  musical comedy ''[[The Arcadians (musical)|The Arcadians]]'', first produced in 1909.<ref name=alumni/><ref>[https://www.imdb.com/name/nm1158304/bio Mark Ambient] [[IMDb]], accessed 19 August 2017.</ref>

He was born in [[Rastrick]], Yorkshire, son of [[Robert Harley (mathematician)|Robert Harley]], a Congregational minister and mathematician. He was educated at [[Mill Hill School]] in London, where his father was vice-principal, and [[King's College, Cambridge]], graduating BA in 1884.<ref name=alumni>[https://books.google.co.uk/books?id=Abx6EqTRfqEC&pg=PA246&lpg=PA246 ''Alumni Cantabrigienses: A Biographical List of All Known Students, Graduates and Holders of Office at the University of Cambridge, from the Earliest Times to 1900''] Volume 2, page 246.</ref>

==Works==
* ''Oh! Susannah!'' "A Farcical Comedy" in three acts: first produced at the Eden Theatre, [[Brighton]], opening on 6 September 1897, afterwards at the [[Royalty Theatre]], London.<ref>Introduction to the script published by Samuel French, 1905</ref>
* ''A Little Ray of Sunshine'', a play in three acts by Ambient and Wilton Heriot, was first performed in the Assembly Rooms, [[Yeovil]], opening on 3 May 1898. The first London performance was at the Royalty Theatre, opening on 31 December 1898; it featured [[W. S. Penley]] as Lord Markham. In New York it opened at [[Wallack's Theatre]] on 28 August 1899, running for 22 performances. It was the inaugural production of the [[Novelty Theatre|Great Queen Street Theatre]], London, after its reconstruction; it ran from 24 May to 6 July 1900 (44 performances).<ref>[https://books.google.co.uk/books?id=o5JWAgAAQBAJ&pg=PA20&lpg=PA20 J. P. Wearing. ''The London Stage 1900-1909: A Calendar of Productions, Performers, and Personnel''. Page 20.] [[Scarecrow Press]] 2013.</ref><ref>[https://www.ibdb.com/broadway-production/a-little-ray-of-sunshine-4978 ''A Little Ray of Sunshine''] [[IBDB]], accessed 19 August 2017.</ref>
* ''A Snug Little Kingdom'', "A Comedy of Bohemia" in three acts, was performed at the Royalty Theatre, running from 31 January to 28 February 1903 (28 performances). It featured [[Charles Warner]], [[Lyn Harding]] and [[H. B. Warner]].<ref>[https://books.google.co.uk/books?id=o5JWAgAAQBAJ&pg=PA130 J. P. Wearing. ''The London Stage 1900-1909: A Calendar of Productions, Performers, and Personnel''. Page 130.] [[Scarecrow Press]] 2013.</ref>
* ''[[The Arcadians (musical)|The Arcadians]]'', "A Fantastic Musical Play" in three acts: book by Ambient and [[Alexander M. Thompson]], lyrics by [[Arthur Wimperis]], music by [[Lionel Monckton]] and [[Howard Talbot]]. It was first produced at the [[Original Shaftesbury Theatre|Shaftesbury Theatre]], London, opening on 29 April 1909 and running for 809 performances. In New York it opened at the [[Liberty Theatre]] on 17 January 1910, running for 136 performances.<ref>[http://gsarchive.net/british/arcadians/index.html The Arcadians] Gilbert and Sullivan Archive, accessed 19 August 2017.</ref><ref>[http://www.guidetomusicaltheatre.com/shows_a/arcadians.htm The Arcadians] The Guide to Musical Theatre, accessed 19 August 2017.</ref>
* ''The Light Blues'', a musical comedy: book by Ambient and [[Jack Hulbert]], lyrics by [[Adrian Ross]], music by Howard Talbot and [[Herman Finck]]. It was first seen on a provincial tour in 1915. At the Shaftesbury Theatre in London it ran from 14 September to 30 September 1916 (20 performances).<ref>[http://www.overthefootlights.co.uk/London%20Musicals%201915-1919.pub.pdf London Musicals 1915–1919] Over the Footlights, accessed 19 August 2017.</ref>
