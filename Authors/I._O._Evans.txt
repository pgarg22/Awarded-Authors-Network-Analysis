'''Idrisyn Oliver Evans''' (1894–1977) was a [[South African people|South African]]-born [[editor]] and [[author]].  He lived in the UK from an early age, and was a UK [[civil servant]] from 1912.  He retired in 1956, but continued working as an editor.<ref>''[[The Encyclopedia of Science Fiction]]'', 1993 edition.</ref>

Evans was noted as a [[translator]] of the works of [[Jules Verne]].<ref>[http://www.verniana.org/volumes/04/HTML/IOEvans.html ''“Verne's Best Friend and his Worst Enemy”: I.O. Evans and the Fitzroy Edition of Jules Verne.'']</ref> He also wrote about [[invention]]s, and penned [[historical novel]]s featuring inventions.  In 1966, Evans edited a work called ''[[Science Fiction]] through the Ages''.<ref>[http://www.sf-encyclopedia.com/entry/evans_i_o ''The Encyclopedia of Science Fiction'', on-line.  Entryfor I. O. Evans]</ref>
