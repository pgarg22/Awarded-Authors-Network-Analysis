'''Józef Rufin Wybicki''' ({{IPA-pl|ˈjuzɛv vɨˈbit͡skʲi}}; 29 September 1747 – 19 March 1822) was a [[Poland|Polish]] [[szlachta|nobleman]], jurist, [[Polish poet|poet]], political and military activist of [[Kashubians|Kashubian]] descent.<ref>{{cite web |title=JÓZEF WYBICKI |url=http://www.jozefwybicki.pl/pl/muzeum-hymnu-narodowego/jozef-wybicki/ |website=www.jozefwybicki.pl |publisher=Towarzystwo Przyjaciół Muzeum Hymnu Narodowego |access-date=22 November 2020}}</ref> He is best remembered as the author of "[[Mazurek Dabrowskiego|Mazurek Dąbrowskiego]]" ({{Lang-en|"Dąbrowski's [[Mazurka]]"}}), which in 1927 was adopted as the Polish [[national anthem]].

==Life==
[[File:Manor_House_of_Józef_Wybicki_in_Będomin.JPG|thumb|left|200px|Manor house ([[Manor houses of Polish nobility|dwór]]) of Józef Wybicki in the village of [[Będomin]]]]
Wybicki was born in [[Będomin]], in the region of [[Pomerania]] in the [[Polish–Lithuanian Commonwealth]].<ref name="unigd"/> His family was Pomeranian [[szlachta|nobility]].<ref name="Bartov2007"/>

He finished a [[Jesuit]] school, and in his youth was a junior court official.<ref name="Bartov2007"/> Wybicki was elected a deputy to the [[Repnin Sejm]], the session of Polish parliament in 1767, on the eve of the [[First Partition of Poland]].<ref name=unigd/> Subsequently he joined the insurgency known as the [[Confederation of Bar]] (1768–1772), aimed at opposing the Russian influence and king [[Stanisław August Poniatowski]].<ref name=unigd/><ref name="Bartov2007"/> He was one of the advisors (''konsyliarz'') of the Confederacy, acting as a diplomat.<ref name="Michalika1994"/> After the failure of the uprising, he spent some time in the Netherlands, studying law at [[Leiden University]].<ref name="Bartov2007"/>

[[Image:DabrowskiWybickiNapoleon.jpg|thumb|left|200px|[[Jan Henryk Dąbrowski]] and Wybicki met [[Napoleon]] in Berlin in 1806]]
Returning to Poland, in the 1770s and 1780s he was associated with the [[Commission of National Education]].<ref name=unigd/> He supported King [[Stanisław August Poniatowski]] and his proposed reforms.<ref name=unigd/><ref name="Bartov2007"/> He helped draft the liberal [[Zamoyski Codex]] of laws of the late 1770s.<ref name="Butterwick2011"/> He was a [[Patriotic Party]] activist during the [[Great Sejm]] (1788–92) – though he was not one of its first deputies, during much of that time staying at his estate, writing and staging operas.<ref name=unigd/><ref name="Bartov2007"/> He did, however, participate in the Great Sejm's deliberations, beginning in 1791.<ref name="Butterwick2011"/> In 1792, in the aftermath of the [[Polish–Russian War of 1792]], like many of Poniatowski's supporters, he joined the [[Targowica Confederation]].<ref name="Zajewski1989"/>

He participated in the [[Kościuszko Uprising]] (1794)<ref name=unigd/> and was a member of the Military Section of the [[Provisional Council of the Duchy of Masovia]].<ref name="Kociszewski1982"/> After the failure of this insurrection he moved to France.<ref name="Bartov2007"/>

[[Image:Wybicki.jpg|thumb|right|150px|Józef Wybicki]]
He was a close friend of both [[Tadeusz Kościuszko]] and [[Jan Henryk Dąbrowski]].<ref name="Nance2008"/> With Dąbrowski he organized the [[Polish Legions in Italy]], serving under [[Napoleon Bonaparte]].<ref name=unigd/> In 1797, while in [[Reggio Emilia]], Italy, he wrote ''[[Mazurek Dabrowskiego|Mazurek Dąbrowskiego]]'' (Dąbrowski's [[Mazurka|Mazurek]]).<ref name=unigd/> In 1806 he helped Dąbrowski organize the [[Greater Poland Uprising (1806)|Greater Poland Uprising]].<ref name="Bartov2007"/>

After the creation of the [[Duchy of Warsaw]] in 1807, he held a number of positions in its Department of Justice, and continued working for it after the Duchy's transformation into [[Congress Poland]].<ref name="Bartov2007"/> In 1817 he became president of the Supreme Court of Congress Poland.<ref name="Lechicka1962"/>

He died on 10 March 1822 in [[Manieczki]], in [[Śrem|Schrimm]], then part of the [[Grand Duchy of Posen]], Prussia.<ref name=unigd/>

==Works==
Wybicki was a writer, journalist and a poet.<ref name=unigd/> He wrote political-themed poems, plays and political treaties advocating reforms in Poland in the 1770s and 1780s.<ref name=unigd/><ref name="Bartov2007"/> His works of that time analyzed the Polish political system, the concepts of liberty, and advocated for more rights for the peasantry.<ref name="Grzeskowiak-Krwawicz2012"/> He would also publish more political brochures in the 1800s, advocating for liberal reforms in the Duchy of Warsaw.<ref name="Bartov2007"/>

''[[Mazurek Dabrowskiego|Mazurek Dąbrowskiego]]'' (Dąbrowski's [[Mazurka]]) remains Wybicki's most famous creation.<ref name="Bartov2007"/> It has been regarded as an unofficial [[national anthem]] since the [[November Uprising]] of 1831.<ref name=unigd/> In 1927 the Mazurka was officially adopted as the [[Poland|Polish]] [[national anthem]] by the Polish parliament ([[Sejm]]).<ref name="Bartov2007"/><ref name="ministerstwa"/>
