'''Kristin Hannah''' (born September 25, 1960) is an [[Americans|American]] [[writer]]. Her most notable works include ''Winter Garden'', ''[[The Nightingale (Hannah novel)|The Nightingale]]'', ''[[Firefly Lane (novel)|Firefly Lane]]'', ''[[The Great Alone]]'', and ''The Four Winds''.

==Biography==
Kristin Hannah was born in [[California]]. After graduating with a degree in communication from the [[University of Washington]], she worked at an advertising agency in [[Seattle]]. She graduated from the [[University of Puget Sound]] law school and practiced law in Seattle before becoming a full-time writer. Hannah wrote her first novel with her mother, who was dying of cancer at the time; the book was never published.<ref>{{Cite news|last=Egan|first=Elisabeth|date=2021-01-29|title=Kristin Hannah Reinvented Herself. She Thinks America Can Do the Same.|language=en-US|work=The New York Times|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2021/01/29/books/kristin-hannah-four-winds.html|access-date=2021-02-01|issn=0362-4331}}</ref>

Hannah's best-selling work is ''The Nightingale'', which has sold over 4.5 million copies worldwide and has been published in 45 languages.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|last=McNary|first=Dave|date=2018-02-05|title=Sony Buys 'Nightingale' Author's Alaska Novel 'The Great Alone' (EXCLUSIVE)|url=https://variety.com/2018/film/news/great-alone-movie-kristin-hannah-sony-1202686468/|access-date=2020-08-11|website=Variety|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=https://time.com/5935255/firefly-lane-book-netflix/ |title=What to Know About the Book Behind Netflix's Firefly Lane |last=Gutterman |first=Annabel |date=February 3, 2021 |work=[[Time (magazine)|Time]]] |access-date=March 26, 2021}}</ref>

Hannah lives on [[Bainbridge Island, Washington]],<ref>{{citation|author=Mary Ann Gwinn|title=10 top-selling Washington state authors|publisher=[[Seattle Times]]|date=April 10, 2011|url=http://seattletimes.nwsource.com/html/books/2014724474_litlife11.html|accessdate=2012-01-25}}</ref> with her husband and their son.
