'''William Missouri Downs''' is an American comedy writer, playwright, screenwriter, stage director, and author

== Life & Writing ==
{{BLP unsourced section|date=February 2020}}
William Missouri Downs started as an actor earning an MFA in acting from the University of Illinois, but a bout with viral encephalitis left him with a slight stammer and ended his acting career.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.kansascity.com/entertainment/performing-arts/article57009133.html|title=A life in theater is not for the weak of heart, says playwright William Missouri Downs}}</ref> So he gave up acting, moved to New York and lived in a transient hotel on the corner of 77th and Broadway.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.uwbrandingiron.com/2015/11/20/william-downs-not-your-typical-professor/|title=William Downs: 'not your typical professor'|last=Branding Iron|date=2015-11-20|language=en-US|access-date=2020-04-26}}</ref> There he studied playwriting at the [[Circle Repertory Company|Circle Rep Theatre]]{{citation needed|date=May 2020}} under [[Lanford Wilson]] and [[Milan Stitt]]. Early in his career, he became a writer because he wasn't good at anything else. He was fired by the big band singer Peggy Lee (“Is that all there is”) for being a rotten spotlight runner<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://no-strings.org/event.php?ID=155|title=NSTC - Headset: A view from the Light Booth|website=no-strings.org|access-date=2020-04-26}}</ref> and fired from the Carlyle Hotel in New York for being a bad waiter.

He then moved to Los Angeles and lived at 10733 Ohio in a bungalow once owned by the writer [[John O'Hara]]<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://planning.lacity.org/StaffRpt/CHC/06-18-15/CHC-2015-1979-HCM.pdf|title=Los Angeles Department of City Planning}}</ref> while he earned an MFA in screenwriting from UCLA.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://littlevillagemag.com/the-stage-interview-william-missouri-downs/|title=The Stage: Interview: William Missouri Downs|last=Crossett|first=Laura|date=2013-01-31|website=Little Village|language=en-US|access-date=2020-04-26}}</ref> While a student at [[UCLA School of Theater, Film and Television|UCLA film school]], he won numerous screenwriting awards, including the Jack Nicholson prize.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.tft.ucla.edu/scholarships-2/|title=Scholarships and Fellowships|website=UCLA School of TFT|access-date=2020-04-26}}</ref>

In Hollywood, he started as a script secretary on NBC's [[Moonlighting (TV series)|Moonlighting]] (Bruce Willis and Cybill Shepherd). He studied sitcom writing at the  Warner Brothers Sitcom Writing Workshop. He was a staff writer on NBC's <u>[[imdbname:6303953|My Two Dads]]</u> (Paul Reiser).<ref>{{Citation|title=My Two Dads (TV Series 1987–1990) - IMDb|url=http://www.imdb.com/title/tt0092410/fullcredits|access-date=2020-04-26}}</ref> He also wrote episodes for the NBC shows <u>[[imdbname:6303953|Amen]]</u> (Sherman Hemsley)<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.imdb.com/name/nm6303953/|title=William Missouri Downs|website=IMDb|access-date=2020-04-26}}</ref> (He wrote the 100th episode of this show that starred Kareem Abdul-Jabbar)<ref>{{Citation|title=The Deacon's Slam Dunk|date=9 February 1991|url=http://www.imdb.com/title/tt0511237/|access-date=2020-04-26}}</ref> and <u>[[imdbname:6303953|Fresh Prince Of Bel Air]]</u> (Will Smith).<ref>{{Citation|title="The Fresh Prince of Bel-Air" Something for Nothing (TV Episode 1991) - IMDb|url=http://www.imdb.com/title/tt0583029/fullcredits|access-date=2020-04-26}}</ref> In addition, he sold a movie to Ron Howard's [[Imagine Film Company|Imagine Films]] and optioned another to [[Filmways]]. Recently he worked with Jim Parsons (Big Bang Theory) developing a TV pilot that was optioned by Hollywood producer [[Meryl Poster]].

His plays include [https://www.samuelfrench.com/p/9804/the-exit-interview ''The Exit Interview''] (Premiere at the Orlando Shakespeare) winner of a rolling premiere from NNPN ([[National New Play Network - (NNPN)|National New Play Network]]), published by [[Samuel French, Inc.|Samuel French]]''.''<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.concordtheatricals.com/p/2249/cockeyed|title=Cockeyed|website=Concord Theatricals|language=en|access-date=2020-04-26}}</ref>  ''[http://nnpn.org/about/news/unicorn-theatre-launches-nnpn-rolling-world-premiere-0f-women-playing-hamlet-on-march-4th Women Playing Hamlet]'' (Premiere Unicorn Theatre, MO), winner of a rolling premiere from NNPN, published by Playscripts.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.playscripts.com/play/2794#searchterm=Women%20Playing%20H|title=Women Playing Hamlet by William Missouri Downs {{!}} Playscripts Inc.|website=www.playscripts.com|access-date=2020-04-26}}</ref>  ''Mad Gravity'', a finalist at the Eugene O'Neill, winner of the Reva Shiner Comedy Award<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.newplays.org/opportunities/submissions/reva-shiner-comedy-award/|title=Reva Shiner Comedy Award|website=Bloomington Playwrights Project|language=en-US|access-date=2020-04-26}}</ref> (Premiere Bloomington Playwrights Project), published by Playscripts.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.playscripts.com/play/2793#searchterm=Mad%20gravity|title=Mad Gravity by William Missouri Downs {{!}} Playscripts Inc.|website=www.playscripts.com|access-date=2020-04-26}}</ref>  ''Cockeyed''<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.concordtheatricals.com/p/2249/cockeyed|title=Cockeyed|website=Concord Theatricals|language=en|access-date=2020-04-26}}</ref> winner of The Greenhouse Festival Of New Plays, published by Samuel French. ''Mr. Perfect''<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.playscripts.com/play/2795|title=Mr. Perfect by William Missouri Downs {{!}} Playscripts Inc.|website=www.playscripts.com|access-date=2020-04-26}}</ref> published by Playscripts. ''Headset, A view from the light booth''<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.hitplays.com/default.aspx?pg=fr-ta|title=Search for plays by title and author name|website=www.hitplays.com|access-date=2020-04-26}}</ref> published by Heuer Publishing, ''Kosher Lutherans''<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.concordtheatricals.com/p/2252/kosher-lutherans|title=Kosher Lutherans|website=Concord Theatricals|language=en|access-date=2020-04-26}}</ref> published by Samuel French, ''Dead White Males''<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.playscripts.com/find-a-play?keyword=Dead+white+males&sort=popularity|title=Find a Play {{!}} Playscripts, Inc.|website=www.playscripts.com|access-date=2020-04-26}}</ref> published by Playscripts. ''How To Steal A Picasso'' a finalist at the Eugene O'Neill.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://sites.google.com/a/theoneill.org/2015-national-playwrights-conference-finalists/how-to-steal-a-picasso-by-william-missouri-downs|title=How To Steal A Picasso by William Missouri Downs - 2015 National Playwrights Conference Finalists|website=sites.google.com|access-date=2020-04-26}}</ref> His other plays include Innocent Thoughts published by Next Stage Press,<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.nextstagepress.net/Innocent_Thoughts.html|title=Innocent Thoughts|website=www.nextstagepress|access-date=2020-04-26}}</ref> ''A Doll House'' (adaptation) published by Next Stage Press. ''[https://www.washingtonpost.com/archive/lifestyle/1985/07/19/kabuki-medea-furious-fusion/19c6a3bd-d4a8-478a-b0d2-287860c2bd1d/?noredirect=on Kabuki Medea]'', winner of the Bay Area Critics Award for Best Production in San Francisco, [https://www.chicagotribune.com/news/ct-xpm-1986-03-21-8601210354-story.html ''Kabuki Faust''], ''Forgiving John Lennon and Fascism The Musical.''

He has had over 250 productions of his plays. Including productions at The Orlando Shakespeare Theatre,<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.orlandosentinel.com/news/os-xpm-2012-09-26os-exit-interview-shakespeare-theater-20120926-story.html|title='The Exit Interview' makes world premiere at Shakespeare Theater|last=Writer|first=Matthew J. Palm, Orlando Sentinel Arts|website=OrlandoSentinel.com|language=en-US|access-date=2020-04-26}}</ref> The InterAct Theatre in Philadelphia,<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.interacttheatre.org/world-premieres|title=World Premieres|website=InterAct Theatre Company|language=en-US|access-date=2020-04-26}}</ref> The San Diego Rep,<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.sdrep.org/production-history|title=San Diego Repertory Theatre Production History|website=www.sdrep.org|access-date=2020-04-26}}</ref> The Berkeley Repertory Theatre, the Salt Lake City Acting Company, the Actors Theatre of Charlotte,<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://clclt.com/charlotte/the-exit-interview-is-theater-and-pop-culture-on-prozac/Content?oid=3066381|title=The Exit Interview is theater and pop culture on Prozac {{!}} Performing Arts {{!}} Creative Loafing Charlotte|website=clclt.com|access-date=2020-04-26}}</ref> the Jewish Theatre of Toronto,<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.cjnews.com/perspectives/opinions/teatron-stage-kosher-lutherans|title=Teatron to stage Kosher Lutherans|last=thecjnadmin|date=2010-10-28|website=The Canadian Jewish News|language=en-US|access-date=2020-04-26}}</ref> The Bloomington Playwright's Project, the Detroit Rep and the New York City Fringe Festival.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://alumnae.smith.edu/spotlight/from-the-fringe/|title=From the Fringe « - Smith College Office of Alumnae Relations Smith College Office of Alumnae Relations|website=alumnae.smith.edu|language=en-US|access-date=2020-04-26}}</ref>

His plays have been produced in Spain (''Fuera de órbita)'',<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://danimantero.wixsite.com/fueradeorbita|title=Fuera de Órbita, de William Missouri Downs|website=fueradeorbita|language=es|access-date=2020-04-26}}</ref> Canada, South Africa ( Durban Performing Arts Center) , Russia (Хороший парень - [http://teatrserov.ru/affiche The Serov Theatre Drama After Chekhov], Serov), Singapore (The Hexis Theatre), Switzerland (Franklin University, Lugano,), Austria (stadt Theater walfischgasse, Vienna), Israel (The International Theatre Festival), India (Alliance Francaise de Bangalore, Bangalore),{{citation needed|date=May 2020}} and South Korea (피카소를 훔치는 법 & 실제 게임 at Theatre in Daehangno & Daehakro Theatre,  Seoul).

Bill has an extensive publication record including articles,  plays, and [https://www.amazon.com/William-Missouri-Downs/e/B001IOBP60?ref=sr_ntt_srch_lnk_1&qid=1554116086&sr=8-1 books]. He has co-authored four books, including <u>Naked Playwritin</u>''g''<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.silmanjamespress.com/shop/screenwriting/naked-playwriting/|title=Naked Playwriting|website=Silman-James Press, Inc.|language=en-US|access-date=2020-04-26}}</ref> (Silman/James), <u>Playwriting: From Formula To Form</u><ref>{{Cite book|isbn = 0155038613|title = Playwriting from Formula to Form: A Guide to Writing a Play|last1 = Downs|first1 = William Missouri|last2 = Wright|first2 = Lou Anne|year = 1998}}</ref> (Harcourt Brace), <u>Screenplay: Writing The Picture</u><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.silmanjamespress.com/shop/screenwriting/screenplay-2nd-edition/|title=Screenplay, 2nd Edition|website=Silman-James Press, Inc.|language=en-US|access-date=2020-04-26}}</ref> (Silman/James), and <u>The Art of Theatre</u><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.cengage.com/s/?q=The%20art%20of%20theatre|title=Search|website=www.cengage.com|language=en|access-date=2020-04-26}}</ref> (Wadsworth/Cenage). <u>The Art of Theatre</u> is now in its 4th edition and has been used by tens of thousands of college student in the US. [https://www.silmanjamespress.com/shop/screenwriting/screenplay-2nd-edition/ <u>Screenplay: Writing the Picture</u>]  (Silman/James), is in its 3rd edition and was published in Warsaw, Poland, <u>Jak Napisac Scenariusz Filmowy</u> .<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://lubimyczytac.pl/ksiazka/14922/jak-napisac-scenariusz-filmowy|title=Jak napisać scenariusz filmowy|website=Lubimyczytać.pl|access-date=2020-04-26}}</ref>

He lives in a log cabin near the [[Shambhala Mountain Center|Shambhala]]  2350 meters up in the Rockies of Colorado.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.williammissouridowns.com/copy-of-bio-1|title=William Missouri Downs - Biography|website=playwright|language=en|access-date=2020-04-26}}</ref>
