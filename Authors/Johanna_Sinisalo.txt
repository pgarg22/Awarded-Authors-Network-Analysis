'''Aila Johanna Sinisalo''' is a Finnish [[science fiction]] and [[fantasy]] writer.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.finlit.fi/fili/en/authors/sinisalo-johanna/|title=Johanna Sinisalo / FILI|language=en-US|access-date=2019-04-09}}</ref> She studied comparative literature and drama, amongst other subjects, at the [[University of Tampere]].<ref name=":0" /> Professionally she worked in the advertising business, rising to the level of marketing designer.

An important figure in the Finnish science fiction scene in the late 1980s and early '90s (winning a rare back-to-back collection of [[Atorox Award]]s for short fiction in the genre), she was also the first Finnish science fiction writer to make a mainstream breakthrough by breaking genre barriers.

==Fiction==
Sinisalo was awarded the [[Finlandia Prize]] for literature in 2000 for her first novel, ''[[Not Before Sundown|Ennen päivänlaskua ei voi]]'' (translated as ''Not Before Sundown'' in 2003 and again as ''Troll — A Love Story'' in 2004 for the American market).<ref>Burns, John (2004) "[https://www.straight.com/article/troll-by-johanna-sinisalo-translated-by-herbert-lomas Troll, by Johanna Sinisalo, translated by Herbert Lomas]", Straight.com, 13 May 2004, retrieved 2010-01-20</ref><ref>Lehmann, Chris (2004) "[https://www.washingtonpost.com/wp-dyn/articles/A34887-2004May17.html After He Was Out of the Woods ]", ''[[Washington Post]]'', 18 May 2004, retrieved 2010-01-20</ref> The novel has been translated into several languages. Movie rights were acquired by [[Carter Smith]] in 2006.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.mtvuutiset.fi/artikkeli/ennen-paivanlaskua-ei-voi-romaanista-elokuva/2025046|title=Ennen päivänlaskua ei voi -romaanista elokuva|date=2006-05-29|website=mtvuutiset.fi|language=fi|access-date=2019-04-09}}</ref>

An English translation of ''Linnunaivot'' was published in 2010 by Peter Owen Publishers under the title ''Birdbrain'' (translated by David Hackston).

She has named the 1967 novel ''[[Friday, or, The Other Island]]'' by French writer [[Michel Tournier]] as a major influence on her career.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|url=https://www.study.eu/article/popular-finnish-authors-reveal-the-books-that-shaped-their-student-years|title=Popular Finnish authors reveal the books that shaped their student years|website=www.study.eu|language=en|access-date=2019-04-09}}</ref>

==Screenwriting==

She is also the screenwriter of [[Energia Productions]] ''[[Iron Sky]]'' movie, a sci-fi comedy set in 2018.<ref>Woerner, Meredith (2009) "[http://io9.com/5437264/space-nazi-movie-finally-launches-into-production Space Nazi Movie Finally Launches Into Production]", i09.com, December 30, 2009, retrieved 2010-01-20</ref>

==Awards==
*Sinisalo was awarded the [[Finlandia Prize]] for literature in 2000 for ''Ennen päivänlaskua ei voi'' (''Not Before Sundown'', also known as ''Troll — A Love Story'').  The novel was also awarded [[James Tiptree, Jr. Award|The James Tiptree Jr. award]] in 2004.
*She was nominated for a [[Nebula Award]] in 2009 for the short story "Baby Doll".<ref>Payne, Marshall (2009) "[https://www.webcitation.org/6AR8lGwqg?url=http://web.archive.org/web/20101017190344/http://www.nebulaawards.com/index.php/interview/johanna_sinisalo_2009/ Johanna Sinisalo 2009 Interview]", nebulaawards.com, retrieved 20 January 2010</ref> 
*She has won the [[Atorox Award]] for best Finnish science fiction short story in 1986, 1989, 1993, 1994, 1997, and 2001
*''[[The Core of the Sun]]'' won the 2017 [[Prometheus Award]] for Best Novel.
