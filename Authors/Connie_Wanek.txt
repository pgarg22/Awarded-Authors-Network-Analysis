'''Connie Wanek''' (born June 1, 1952) is an American [[poet]].

==Life==
She was born in [[Madison, Wisconsin]], and grew up in [[Las Cruces, New Mexico]]. In 1989, she moved with her family to [[Duluth, Minnesota]]. She now divides her time between Minnesota and New Mexico.<ref>{{Cite web|url = http://www.conniewanek.com/|title = Connie Wanek|website = www.conniewanek.com|access-date = 2016-03-24}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url = http://www.americanpoems.com/poets/Connie-Wanek|title = Connie Wanek Poems, Biography and Quotes - by American Poems|website = www.americanpoems.com|access-date = 2016-03-24}}</ref>

Her work appeared in ''Poetry'', ''The Atlantic Monthly'', ''The Virginia Quarterly Review'', ''Quarterly West'', ''Poetry East'', ''Prairie Schooner'',<ref>{{Cite book|url = https://books.google.com/books?id=tDexAAAAIAAJ|title = The Prairie Schooner|date = 1979-01-01|language = en}}</ref> and ''Missouri Review''.<ref>{{Cite book|url = https://books.google.com/books?id=sTSxAAAAIAAJ|title = The Missouri Review|date = 2000-01-01|publisher = Department of English of University of Missouri--Columbia|language = en}}</ref>

She has published four books of poetry, one book of short prose, and served as co-editor (with [[Joyce Sutphen]] and [[Thom Tammaro]]) of the comprehensive historical anthology of Minnesota women poets, called ''To Sing Along the Way'' (New Rivers Press, 2006). [[Ted Kooser]], Poet Laureate of the United States (2004–2006), named her a Witter Bynner Fellow of the Library of Congress for 2006.

==Awards==
* Willow Poetry Prize
* Jane Kenyon Poetry Prize.
* 2006 [[Witter Bynner Fellowship]] of the Library of Congress by United States Poet Laureate [[Ted Kooser]].
* 2009 George Morrison Artist of the Year
