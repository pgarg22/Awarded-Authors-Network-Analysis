{{short description|American poet (born 1940)}}
'''Hugh Seidman''' (born 1940 in [[Brooklyn, New York]]) is an American [[poet]].

==Life==
He is a graduate of [[Polytechnic Institute of New York University]].<ref>http://cable.poly.edu/sites/default/files/download/2002fall.pdf</ref>

He has taught writing at the [[University of Wisconsin]], [[Yale University]], [[Columbia University]], the [[College of William and Mary]], [[The New School]].<ref>http://www.newschool.edu/writing/</ref>

His work appeared in ''The Brooklyn Rail'',<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.brooklynrail.org/2004/11/poetry/hugh-seidman|title = 3 Sections from TRUE TUNES|date = November 2004}}</ref> ''Harper's'',<ref>http://www.harpers.org/subjects/HughSeidman</ref> ''The Paris Review'',<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.theparisreview.org/viewissue.php/prmIID/106 |title=Archived copy |access-date=2009-12-14 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100115060010/http://theparisreview.org/viewissue.php/prmIID/106 |archive-date=2010-01-15 |url-status=dead }}</ref> ''Virginia Quarterly Review''.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.vqronline.org/articles/2006/spring/seidman-will-change/ |title=Archived copy |access-date=2009-12-14 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20091010133931/http://www.vqronline.org/articles/2006/spring/seidman-will-change/ |archive-date=2009-10-10 |url-status=dead }}</ref>

He lives in [[New York City]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.pw.org/content/hugh_seidman_2|title = Hugh Seidman}}</ref>
