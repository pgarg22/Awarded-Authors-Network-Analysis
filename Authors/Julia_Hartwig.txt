'''Julia Hartwig-Międzyrzecka''' (14 August 1921 – 14 July 2017) was a Polish [[writer]], [[poet]] and [[translator]], considered to be one of Poland's most important poets.<ref name=columbia>{{cite book|url=https://archive.org/details/columbiaguidetol00sege|url-access=registration|title=The Columbia Guide to the Literatures of Eastern Europe Since 1945|publisher=Columbia University Press|page=[https://archive.org/details/columbiaguidetol00sege/page/207 207]|last=Segel|first=Harold B.|year=2003|isbn=0231114044}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://culture.pl/pl/tworca/julia-hartwig|title=Julia Hartwig|work=culture.pl|publisher=Adam Mickiewicz Institute|language=pl}}</ref><ref name=":0">{{Cite web|url=http://ic.galegroup.com/ic/bic1/BiographiesDetailsPage/BiographiesDetailsWindow?failOverType=&query=&prodId=BIC1&windowstate=normal&contentModules=&display-query=&mode=view&displayGroupName=Biographies&limiter=&currPage=&disableHighlighting=false&displayGroups=&sortBy=&search_within_results=&p=BIC1&action=e&catId=&activityType=&scanId=&documentId=GALE%7CH1000194862&source=Bookmark&u=mlin_n_umass&jsid=c2bbd64ca742b646dd49bedd0259f001|title=Contemporary Authors Online|year=2010|accessdate=28 December 2015|website=Biography in Context|publisher=Gale}}</ref>

==Life and career==
She was born and raised in [[Lublin]]. She studied Polish and French literature at [[University of Warsaw|Warsaw University]] and continued her studies at the [[John Paul II Catholic University of Lublin|Catholic University of Lublin]].<ref name=miller>{{cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=Y-TMhtk5AUYC&pg=PA136|title=Who's who in Contemporary Women's Writing|page=136|last=Miller|first=Jane Eldridge|year=2001|isbn=0415159806}}</ref> Her first poems appeared in the journal ''Odrodzenie'' in 1944. Hartwig lived in Paris from 1947-50. In 1954, she published ''Z niedalekich podróży'' (''From Nearby Places''), a collection of articles. She published her first collection of poetry ''Pożegnania'' (''Farewells'') in 1956.<ref name=columbia/>

She lived in the United States from 1970 to 1974, later returning to [[Warsaw]].<ref name=miller/> During her time in America, Hartwig took part in the [[International Writing Program]] at the [[University of Iowa]] and also taught at several universities.<ref name=slavic>{{cite journal|url=https://www.lsa.umich.edu/UMICH/slavic/Home/About/Our%20Newsletter/May2008web.pdf|title=Julia Hartwig's in Praise of the Unfinished translated by John and Bogdana Carpenter|last=Carpenter|first=Bogdana|page=10|journal=Slavic Scene|date=May 2008|volume=16|issue=1|url-status=dead|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20151030101317/http://www.lsa.umich.edu/UMICH/slavic/Home/About/Our%20Newsletter/May2008web.pdf|archivedate=30 October 2015}}</ref>

She published translations of French poetry by [[Guillaume Apollinaire]], [[Blaise Cendrars]], [[Max Jacob]], [[Henri Michaux]], and [[Pierre Reverdy]] and wrote books on Apollinaire and [[Gérard de Nerval]].<ref name=grol/> She also published translations of American poets such as [[Robert Bly]] and [[Marianne Moore]].<ref name=columbia/> Hartwig's poetry has been translated into English, French, Italian, Russian, Lithuanian, Serbian, Greek and German.<ref name=slavic/>

{{external media|width=210px|headerimage=[[File:Julia Hartwig 2011.jpg|210px]]|float=right|video1=[https://www.webofstories.com/playAll/julia.hartwig?sId=53075 Born at the meeting point of diagonals, Julia Hartwig, poet], [[Web of Stories]]|accessdate=29 March 2017}}
Hartwig was awarded the [[Jurzykowski Prize]], the Thornton Wilder Prize from [[Columbia University]]'s Translation Center and the [[Georg Trakl]] Poetry Prize.<ref name=grol>{{cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=3F_jtt0uuZ8C&pg=PA432|title=Ambers aglow: an anthology of Polish women's poetry (1981–1995)|page=432|last=Grol|first=Regina|year=1996|isbn=0924047151}}</ref> She received six nominations for the prestigious [[Nike Award]]. She is the winner of the 2014 [[Wisława Szymborska Award]] for her book of poetry ''Zapisane''.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://newzar.wordpress.com/2014/10/28/hartwig-wins-poetry-prize/ |title=Hartwig Wins Poetry Prize |access-date=24 December 2017}}</ref>

==Personal life==
In 1954,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://bookhaven.stanford.edu/2011/08/happy-90th-birthday-julia-hartwig|title=Happy 90th birthday, Julia Hartwig! Poland's late-blooming poet is still in glorious flower|work=The Book Haven|publisher=Stanford University|date=3 August 2011}}</ref> Hartwig married poet {{Interlanguage link multi|Artur Międzyrzecki|pl}}; he died in 1996.<ref name=":0"/><ref name=miller/> She died on 14 July 2017 in [[Pennsylvania]] at the age of 95.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://kultura.gazeta.pl/kultura/7,114628,22098069,julia-hartwig-nie-zyje-odeszla-jedna-z-najwybitniejszych-polskich.html|title=Julia Hartwig nie żyje. She was the sister of the prominent photographer [[Edward Hartwig]].
