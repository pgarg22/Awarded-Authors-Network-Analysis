'''Lenore Montanaro''' (born May 30, 1990) is a Rhode Island and Massachusetts attorney, American poet, above-the-knee amputee, three-time [[cancer survivor, and [[Animal shelter|animal rescue]] attorney and writer.

== Early life ==

Montanaro was born on May 30, 1990, in [[Rhode Island]], United States. She was diagnosed in September 1995, with [[Rhabdomyosarcoma]] at the age of five and subsequently battled the disease until the loss of her right leg above-the-knee on May 10, 2002.<ref>{{cite news|last=Church|first=Chris|title=Poetry helps NKHS grad deal with loss|url=http://www.independentri.com/independents/north_east/article_9e41dbc0-74e3-5b7e-90e8-766d1adf296a.html|accessdate=January 4, 2013|newspaper=North East Independent|date=November 15, 2012}}</ref> She wears a [[prosthetic]] leg and is the first leg [[amputee]] to attend and graduate from [[College of the Holy Cross]] in [[Worcester, Massachusetts]].<ref>{{cite news|last=Turner|first=Samantha|title=21-Year-Old Overcomes Cancer, Family Tragedy to Keep Brother's Dream Alive|url=http://northkingstown.patch.com/articles/challenge-accepted|accessdate=January 4, 2013|newspaper=North Kingstown Patch|date=January 28, 2012}}</ref><ref name="turnto10">{{cite news|last=Silva|first=Barbara Morse|title=Health Check: Cancer survivor inspires with poetry|url=http://www2.turnto10.com/lifestyles/2012/nov/13/health-check-cancer-survivor-inspires-poetry-ar-1239252/|accessdate=January 4, 2013|newspaper=WJAR NBC10 website|date=November 13, 2012|archive-url=https://archive.is/20130205030213/http://www2.turnto10.com/lifestyles/2012/nov/13/health-check-cancer-survivor-inspires-poetry-ar-1239252/|archive-date=February 5, 2013|url-status=dead}}</ref> She received her [[Juris Doctor]] from [[Western New England University]].  While in law school, Montanaro was one of two recipients of a national scholarship competition sponsored by the Defense Research Institute (DRI). Her winning essay contained her detailed opinion of two of the proposed amendments to the Federal Rules of Civil Procedure.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www1.wne.edu/assets/23/Perspectives_Spring_2015.pdf |title=Archived copy |accessdate=June 10, 2015 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20150610030828/http://www1.wne.edu/assets/23/Perspectives_Spring_2015.pdf |archivedate=June 10, 2015 }}</ref> On April 26, 2011, Montanaro's younger brother, John F. Montanaro III., died from [[Acute Lymphoblastic Leukemia]].<ref>{{cite news|title=John Frank Montanaro III obituary|url=http://www.legacy.com/obituaries/providence/obituary.aspx?n=john-frank-montanaro&pid=150621173|accessdate=January 4, 2013|newspaper=Providence Journal|date=April 28, 2011}}</ref>

==Legal career==
Montanaro passed the [[Bar Exam]] in [[Rhode Island]] and [[Massachusetts]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.courts.ri.gov/PDF/February2016RhodeIslandBarExaminationPassList.pdf |title=Archived copy |accessdate=May 23, 2016 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20160604192310/https://www.courts.ri.gov/PDF/February2016RhodeIslandBarExaminationPassList.pdf |archivedate=June 4, 2016 }}</ref><ref>http://www.mass.gov/courts/docs/bbe/bbefeb2016passl-z.pdf</ref> She is the Director of Advocacy for the Animal Rescue League of Boston, one of the oldest animal protection organizations in the United States.<ref>https://www.arlboston.org/crueltymanual/</ref>

==Literary career==
Montanaro began writing poetry after the loss of her leg. Her [[literary]] influences include [[Ralph Waldo Emerson]], [[Henry David Thoreau]], [[Elizabeth Bishop]], Robert K. Cording, and [[Rainer Maria Rilke]]. She has published her first book of poetry, ''The Morning within the Dark''.<ref name="Montanaro 2012">Montanaro, Lenore M., (2012), ''The Morning Within the Dark'', Montanaro Press, {{ISBN|978-0-9858894-5-6}}</ref> A vision of understanding of death and dying is the subject of her poems.<ref>[https://www.amazon.com/Morning-Within-Dark-Lenore-Montanaro/dp/0985889454 The Morning Within the Dark.] Amazon.com page</ref>

==Writing style ==
Montanaro often writes about suffering and [[Grief|loss]]. Many of her poems detail one's experience with cancer and dying as viewed in an excerpt to her poem, titled, ''Sunglasses'':<ref name="Montanaro 2012" />{{rp|page=29}}

{{quote|Perhaps, death is the taking off of the sunglasses,<br/> the awareness of no longer needing<br/> to be protected from the suffering existence<br/> that is so visible to us here.}}

Her poem, "This Camp," was selected for the anthology ''The Cancer Poetry Project 2'' that was released in 2013.<ref>{{cite web|last=Miller|first=Karin B.|title=The Cancer Poetry Project 2|url=http://www.cancerpoetryproject.com/cpp2.pdf|publisher=The Cancer Poetry Project|accessdate=January 4, 2013}}</ref> Montanaro is working on her next book of poems.
