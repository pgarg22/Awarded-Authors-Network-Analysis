'''Jennifer Lynette Pattrick''' {{post-nominals|country=NZL|OBE}} (née '''Priestley''', born 1936) is a New Zealand novelist, known primarily for her historical fiction.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|url=https://www.bookcouncil.org.nz/writer/pattrick-jenny/|title={{!}} New Zealand Book Council|website=www.bookcouncil.org.nz|access-date=8 June 2019}}</ref> Her first novel, ''[[The Denniston Rose]]'' (2003) and its sequel ''Heart of Coal'' (2004) became two of New Zealand's best-selling novels.<ref name=":0" /> She has published nine novels, and also writes and publishes songbooks for children.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.noted.co.nz/culture/books/a-way-with-words-jenny-pattrick/|title=A Way with Words: Jenny Pattrick – The Listener|last=Noted|website=Noted|language=en|access-date=8 June 2019}}</ref> ''The Denniston Rose'' has been optioned by Bohemia Group Originals.<ref name=":1">{{Cite web|url=http://www.scoop.co.nz/stories/CU1804/S00128/bohemia-originals-snaps-up-the-denniston-rose.htm|title=Bohemia Originals Snaps up "the Denniston Rose" {{!}} Scoop News|last1=Wednesday|last2=April 2018|first2=11|website=www.scoop.co.nz|access-date=8 June 2019|last3=Release|first3=3:56 pm Press Release: Joint Press}}</ref>   

Pattrick is a graduate of the [[International Institute of Modern Letters]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.stuff.co.nz/dominion-post/news/local-papers/the-wellingtonian/745990/Wellingtonian-interview-Jenny-Pattrick|title=Wellingtonian interview: Jenny Pattrick|website=Stuff|language=en|access-date=8 June 2019}}</ref> In 2009, she was awarded the [[Katherine Mansfield Menton Fellowship]],<ref name=":0" /> during which she wrote her sixth novel ''Inheritance'' (2010).<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.nzherald.co.nz/entertainment/news/article.cfm?c_id=1501119&objectid=10636865|title=Review: Inheritance|last=Hill|first=David|newspaper=The New Zealand Herald|date=5 April 2010|access-date=8 June 2019|language=en-NZ|issn=1170-0777}}</ref> She was appointed an [[Officer of the Order of the British Empire]], for services to the arts, in the [[1989 Birthday Honours (New Zealand)|1989 Queen's Birthday Honours]].<ref name=":1" /><ref>{{London Gazette |date=17 June 1989 |supp=3 |issue=51774 |page=32}}</ref>

Pattrick's novels are often set in New Zealand locations. ''The Denniston Rose'' and ''Heart of Coal'' centre on coalmining communities on the [[Denniston, New Zealand|Denniston]] plateau from the late 1800s to early 1900s.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.nzherald.co.nz/|title=Jenny Pattrick: Heart of coal|newspaper=The New Zealand Herald|date=7 March 2004|access-date=8 June 2019|language=en-NZ|issn=1170-0777}}</ref> ''Landings'' (2008) is about the [[Whanganui River]] at the turn of the twentieth century.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.odt.co.nz/entertainment/books/whanganui-woven-historical-novel|title=Whanganui woven into historical novel|date=27 May 2008|website=Otago Daily Times Online News|language=en|access-date=8 June 2019}}</ref> ''Heartland'' (2014) is set in a fictional town based on the Central Plateau settlement of [[Rangataua]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.nzherald.co.nz/lifestyle/news/article.cfm?c_id=6&objectid=11257546|title=Small town, caring hearts|newspaper=The New Zealand Herald|date=17 May 2014|access-date=8 June 2019|language=en-NZ|issn=1170-0777}}</ref>  

Pattrick was a jeweller for 35 years and has had pieces gifted to overseas dignitaries by the New Zealand government.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.stuff.co.nz/dominion-post/news/local-papers/the-wellingtonian/745990/Wellingtonian-interview-Jenny-Pattrick|title=Wellingtonian interview: Jenny Pattrick|website=Stuff|language=en|access-date=8 June 2019}}</ref>
