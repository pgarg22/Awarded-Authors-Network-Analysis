'''Chad Beguelin''' (born September 24, 1969) is an American [[playwright]] and [[lyricist]]. He wrote the lyrics and co-wrote the book for ''[[The Prom (musical)|The Prom]]''. He also wrote the book for Disney's ''[[Aladdin (2011 musical)|Aladdin]]'', as well as additional lyrics for the score. He was nominated for Best Original Book and Best Original Score for ''Aladdin''. He is also known for his collaborations with composer [[Matthew Sklar]], having written the [[lyrics]] and co-written the book for the [[Broadway theatre|Broadway]] [[musical theatre|musical]] ''[[The Wedding Singer (musical)|The Wedding Singer]]'' and the lyrics for the Broadway musical ''[[Elf the Musical]]''. Beguelin was nominated for two [[Tony Awards]] for his work on ''The Wedding Singer'', as well as a [[Drama Desk Award]] for Outstanding Lyrics.

==Biography==
Beguelin, a native of [[Centralia, Illinois]], received an [[Master of Fine Arts|MFA]] from the graduate writing program at [[New York University]]'s [[Tisch School of the Arts]].

Beguelin wrote the book and lyrics for ''The Rhythm Club'' which was produced at the [[Signature Theatre (Arlington, Virginia)|
Signature Theatre]], [[Arlington, Virginia]] in 2000<ref>Jones, Kenneth; Ehren, Christine. [http://www.playbill.com/news/article/71236-Whos-Got-Rhythm-Musical-Rhythm-Club-Still-Strummin "Who's Got Rhythm? Musical 'Rhythm Club' Still Strummin'"] {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20121019153829/http://www.playbill.com/news/article/71236-Whos-Got-Rhythm-Musical-Rhythm-Club-Still-Strummin |date=October 19, 2012 }} playbill.com, August 2, 2002</ref> and was nominated for a [[Helen Hayes]] Award. He wrote the book and lyrics for ''[[Wicked City (musical)|Wicked City]]'', which was produced at the American Stage Company and the Mason Street Warehouse, Saugatuck, Michigan, in 2004.<ref>[http://www.masonstreetwarehouse.org/WickCity.pdf "Saugatuck’s Mason Street Warehouse Presents World Premiere of 'Wicked City'"] masonstreetwarehouse.org, August 4, 2004</ref> He wrote the books for [[The Walt Disney Company|Disney]]'s ''[[Disney's Aladdin: A Musical Spectacular|Aladdin]]'' and ''[[On the Record (musical)|On the Record]]''.

As a [[screenwriter]], Beguelin sold a script to [[Grammnet Productions]] and also worked as a staff writer for [[Disney]]'s live action film department in [[California]].

[[Casey Nicholaw]] directed and choreographed the stage musical ''[[Aladdin (2011 musical)|Aladdin]]'' which ran at the [[5th Avenue Theatre]], [[Seattle]], Washington, July 7–31, 2011.<ref>[http://broadwayworld.com/article/Seattles_5th_Avenue_Theatre_Premieres_ALADDIN_7731_20110113 "Seattle's 5th Avenue Theatre Premieres ''Aladdin'', 7/7-31"], BroadwayWorld.com, January 13, 2011</ref> The musical uses songs from the 1992 film ''[[Aladdin (1992 Disney film)|Aladdin]]'', with a new book by Beguelin and new lyrics by Beguelin and [[Alan Menken]]. The musical opened on Broadway on March 20, 2014 at the [[New Amsterdam Theatre]].<ref>Hetrick, Adam. [http://www.playbill.com/news/article/181617-Disneys-Aladdin-Will-Arrive-on-Broadway-in-February-2014 "Disney's 'Aladdin' Will Arrive on Broadway in February 2014"] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130901002722/http://www.playbill.com/news/article/181617-Disneys-Aladdin-Will-Arrive-on-Broadway-in-February-2014 |date=2013-09-01 }}, Playbill.com, August 29, 2013</ref><ref>[http://www.playbillvault.com/Show/Detail/14037/Aladdin " ''Aladdin'' Broadway"] Playbill Vault, accessed October 15, 2015</ref>

Beguelin made his [[Off-Broadway]] playwrighting debut with ''Harbor'', directed by [[Mark Lamos]], presented by [[Primary Stages]], from August 6, 2013 to September 8, 2013.<ref>Propst, Andy. [http://www.theatermania.com/connecticut-theater/news/01-2013/chad-beguelins-harbor-to-dock-in-new-york_64114.html "Chad Beguelin's Harbor to Dock in New York"] theatremania.com, January 10, 2013</ref><ref>[http://www.lortel.org/LLA_archive/index.cfm?search_by=show&id=6162 ''Harbor''] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20151222102850/http://www.lortel.org/LLA_archive/index.cfm?search_by=show&id=6162 |date=2015-12-22 }} lortel.org, accessed December 15, 2015</ref> ''Harbor'' had its world premiere at the [[Westport Country Playhouse]], Westport, Connecticut, from August 2012 to September 15, 2012, directed by Mark Lamos.<ref>Hennessy, Christina. [http://www.ctpost.com/news/article/World-premiere-comedy-sails-into-Westport-Country-3805169.php "World premiere comedy sails into Westport Country Playhouse"] ctpost.com, August 21, 2012</ref>

Beguelin and [[Bob Martin (comedian)|Bob Martin]] wrote the book for a new musical based on the film ''[[Gotta Dance]]'', titled ''Half Time'', with music by [[Matthew Sklar]] and lyrics by Nell Benjamin, with additional music by [[Marvin Hamlisch]]. Directed and choreographed by [[Jerry Mitchell]], the musical premiered at the [[Bank of America Theatre]] in [[Chicago]] on December 13, 2015, running through January 17, 2016. It had originally been expected to open on Broadway in the Fall of 2016,<ref>{{cite news|last=Gans|first=Andrew| url=http://www.playbill.com/news/article/gotta-dance-helmed-by-jerry-mitchell-extends-chicago-run-broadway-debut-set-for-fall-375512#| title=Gotta Dance, Helmed by Jerry Mitchell, Extends Chicago Run; Broadway Debut Set for Fall| work=Playbill| date=December 14, 2015}}</ref> and then for the Spring of 2017.<ref>Gans, Andrew. [http://www.playbill.com/article/broadway-bound-gotta-dance-gets-a-new-title-and-music-collaborator# "Broadway-Bound 'Gotta Dance' Gets a New Title and Music Collaborator"] Playbill, July 13, 2016</ref> The musical instead opened at the [[Paper Mill Playhouse]], Millburn, New Jersey on May 31, 2018. The cast stars [[André De Shields]], [[Georgia Engel]], [[Donna McKechnie]], and [[Lillias White]].<ref>Hetrick, Adam. [http://www.playbill.com/article/new-musical-half-time-starring-andre-de-shields-georgia-engel-donna-mckechnie-and-lillias-white-begins-paper-mill-run# "New Musical 'Half Time', Starring André De Shields, Georgia Engel, Donna McKechnie, and Lillias White, Begins Paper Mill Run"] Playbill, May 31, 2018</ref>

He wrote the book with Bob Martin and lyrics for a new musical ''[[The Prom (musical)|The Prom]]'', with music by Matthew Sklar based on an original concept by Jack Viertel. The musical premiered at the Alliance Theatre, Atlanta, Georgia on August 18, 2016 in previews, directed by Casey Nicholaw.<ref>Gans, Andrew. [http://www.playbill.com/article/the-prom-from-drowsy-chaperone-and-wedding-singer-creators-begins-previews-tonight# " 'The Prom', from Drowsy Chaperone and Wedding Singer Creators, Begins Previews Tonight"] Playbill, August 18, 2016</ref> The musical opened on Broadway at the [[Longacre Theatre]] on November 15, 2018, with previews starting on October 23, 2018.<ref>McPhee, Ryan. [http://www.playbill.com/article/full-cast-set-for-broadways-the-prom# "Full Cast Set for Broadway’s 'The Prom'"] ''Playbill'', May 3, 2018</ref><ref>McPhee, Ryan. [http://www.playbill.com/article/the-prom-sets-new-broadway-start-date# " 'The Prom' Sets New Broadway Start Date"] ''Playbill'', June 13, 2018</ref><ref>McPhee, Ryan. [http://www.playbill.com/article/musical-comedy-the-prom-switches-theatres-ahead-of-broadway-premiere# "Musical Comedy 'The Prom' Switches Theatres Ahead of Broadway Premiere"] Playbill, July 12, 2018</ref>

===Awards and honors===
He has received six [[Tony Award]] nominations: 2019 Best Book of a Musical and Best Original Score for ''The Prom''; 2014 Best Book of a Musical and Best Original Score Written for the Theatre for ''Aladdin''; 2006 Best Book of a Musical and Best Original Score for ''The Wedding Singer''.<ref name=awards>[https://www.ibdb.com/broadway-cast-staff/chad-beguelin-402892#tabs-Awards "Beguelin Awards"] ibdb.com, accessed July 15, 2016</ref>

He received three [[Drama Desk Award]] nominations: 2014 Outstanding Book of a Musical and Outstanding Lyrics for ''Aladdin''; 2006 Drama Desk Award Outstanding Lyrics for ''The Wedding Singer''.<ref name=awards/>

Beguelin has received the [[Jonathan Larson]] Performing Arts Foundation Award, the [[Gilman & Gonzalez-Falla Theatre Foundation]] Award,<ref>Hernandez, Ernio. [http://www.playbill.com/news/article/the-rhythm-club-team-win-gonzalez-falla-musical-theatre-award-dec.-8-116791# "'The Rhythm Club' Team Win Gonzalez-Falla Musical Theatre Award, Dec. 8"] playbill.com, December 8, 2003</ref> and the [[Edward Kleban]] Award for Outstanding Lyric Writing.<ref>[http://www.newdramatists.org/kleban_award.htm Kleban Award] newdramatists.org</ref> He was named to the Centralia High School Distinguished Alumni Hall of Fame in November 2016.<ref>http://www.wjbdradio.com/local-news/2016/11/06/three-enter-centralia-high-school-hall-of-fame</ref>

===Personal life===
Beguelin currently lives in [[Manhattan]] and [[Bridgehampton]] with his husband Tom and their dog Tucker.<ref>Beguelin, Chad. [http://www.chadbeguelin.com/ Official website for Chad Beguelin]. Retrieved March 2009.</ref>
