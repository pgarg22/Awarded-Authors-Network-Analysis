'''Baltasar del Alcázar''' (1530 in [[Seville, Spain]]–16 February 1606 in [[Ronda]]) was a [[Spain|Spanish]] [[poet]].<ref name="Fitzmaurice-Kelly">{{cite book|last=Fitzmaurice-Kelly|first=James|title=Cambridge Readings In Spanish literature|publisher=The University press|year=1920|url=https://archive.org/details/cambridgereadin00kelgoog|quote=Baltasar del Alcázar 1530.|page=[https://archive.org/details/cambridgereadin00kelgoog/page/n71 49]}}</ref> He was the sixth child of [[Luis del Alcázar]]. His poetry was about life and love, most of it spiced with a keen sense of humor.

==Works==

A short example of his poetry is ''Tres Cosas'' (''Three Things''):
{{Verse translation|
{{lang|es|Tres cosas me tienen preso
de amores el corafzón.
La bella Inés, el jamón
y berenjenas con queso.}}
|
Three things have caught my heart:

The pretty Inés, [[cured ham]]
and [[aubergine]]s with [[cheese]].}}
