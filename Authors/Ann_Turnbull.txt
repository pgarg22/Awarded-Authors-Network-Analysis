'''Ann Turnbull''' (born 1943) is a [[UK|British]] writer of fiction for children and young adults.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://literature.britishcouncil.org/writer/ann-turnbull|title=Ann Turnbull - Literature|website=literature.britishcouncil.org|language=en|access-date=2018-01-16}}</ref> Her work includes a [[picture book]], set in a Shropshire mining town during the [[Great Depression]] of the 1930s which is about a young girl named Mary Dyer, and ''[[No Shame, No Fear]]'', a novel for young adults that depicts the persecution of [[Religious Society of Friends|Quaker]]s during the 1660s, and is set in both Shropshire and [[London]] and was shortlisted for the [[Guardian Children's Fiction Prize]] .<ref>{{cite news|title=2004 Guardian Children's Fiction prize|url=https://www.theguardian.com/books/childrensfictionprize2004/0,14765,1263070,00.html|accessdate=26 May 2018}}</ref> 
''Pigeon Summer'' was nominated for the [[Nestle Smarties Book Prize]] and ''No Shame, No Fear'' was nominated for the [[Whitbread Book Awards|Whitbread Book Award]]. She has written a number of picture books but the best known is ''[[The Sand Horse]]'' which is illustrated by [[Michael Foreman (author/illustrator)|Michael Foreman]].
