'''Susan Wiggs''' (born May 17, 1958)<ref>[https://www.fantasticfiction.com/w/susan-wiggs/ Fantastic fiction]</ref><ref>[https://annecarolinedrake.com/2010/05/17/happy-birthday-susan-wiggs/ Happy birhday Susan Wiggs by Anne Caroline Drake]</ref> is an American [[author]] of [[historical romance|historical]] and contemporary [[romance novels]].

==Early years==
Wiggs began writing as a child, finishing her first novel, ''A Book About Some Bad Kids'', when she was eight.  She temporarily abandoned her dream of being a novelist after graduating from [[Harvard University]], instead becoming a math teacher.  She continued to read, especially reveling in [[romance novels]].  After running out of reading material one evening in 1983, she began writing again, using the working title ''A Book About Some Bad Adults.''<ref name="rock">{{cite web|last=Rock|first=Bonnie|title=Writerspace talks with Romance Author Susan Wiggs|publisher=Writerspace|url=http://www.writerspace.com/interviews/wiggs0202.html|date=February 2002|access-date=2007-07-25|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20071006180638/http://www.writerspace.com/interviews/wiggs0202.html|archive-date=2007-10-06}}</ref><ref name="housley">{{cite web|last=Housely|first=Suzie|title=Her Pen is Mightier Than A Sword|publisher=Beneath the Covers Past|url=http://www.myshelf.com/beneaththecovers/02/wiggs.htm|date=June 2002|access-date=2007-07-25}}</ref>

==Career==
For three years Wiggs continued to write, and in 1987 [[Zebra Books]] published her first novel, a Western [[historical romance]] named ''Texas Wildflower''.<ref name="rock"/>  Her subsequent historical and contemporary romances have been set in a wide range of settings and time periods.   Many of her novels are set in areas where she's lived or visited.<ref name="arr"/>  She gave up teaching in 1992 to write full-time, and has since completed an average of two books per year.<ref name="ward">{{cite web|last=Ward|first=Jean Marie|author2=Teri Smith |title=Susan Wiggs: Historical Polish, Organizational Savvy|publisher=Crescent Blues|year=2000|url=http://www.crescentblues.com/3_4issue/wiggs.shtml|access-date=2007-07-25}}</ref>

In 2000, Wiggs began writing single-title women's fiction stories in addition to historical romance novels.  The first, ''The You I Never Knew'', was published in 2001.<ref name="ward"/> After writing [[mass-market paperback|mass-market]] original novels for several years, Wiggs made her [[hardcover]] debut in 2003 with ''Home Before Dark''.<ref name="arr">{{cite web|title=Susan Wiggs - And Now (as usual), Something New|publisher=All About Romance Novels|url=http://www.likesbooks.com/susanwiggs.html|date=May 25, 2003|access-date=2007-07-25|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20070928010904/http://www.likesbooks.com/susanwiggs.html|archive-date=September 28, 2007}}</ref>

Many of her novels are connected, allowing Wiggs to revisit established characters.<ref name="rock"/>

Her books have been published in many languages, including French, German, [[Dutch language|Dutch]], [[Latvian language|Latvian]], [[Japanese language|Japanese]], [[Hungarian language|Hungarian]] and [[Russian language|Russian]].<ref name="housley"/>

==Awards==
Wiggs's books are frequently named finalists for the [[RITA Award]], the highest honor given in the genre. She received the [[Romance Writers of America]] RITA Award for Best Romance of the year in 1993 for ''Lord of the Night''.  She won a second RITA in 2000 when ''The Charm School'' was named "Favorite Book of the Year." She has also won the RITA in 2001 for Best Short Historical for ''The Mistress.''<ref name="arr"/>  and in 2006 for ''Lakeside Cottage''. She has also been the recipient of the Holt Medallion, the Colorado Award of Excellence, and the Peninsula Romance Writers of America Blue Boa Award.<ref name="rock"/>  ''Romantic Times'' has twice named her a Career Achievement Award winner.<ref name="ward"/>

==Personal==
Wiggs lives on [[Bainbridge Island, Washington]].<ref name="ward"/><ref name="bexte">{{cite web|last=Bexte|first=Martina|title=Interview: Susan Wiggs|publisher=BookLoons|date=September 2003|url=http://www.bookloons.com/cgi-bin/Columns.asp?type=Interview&name=Susan%20Wiggs|access-date=2007-07-25}}</ref><ref>{{citation|url=http://www.bainbridgepubliclibrary.org/local-authors.aspx|title=Local authors|publisher=Bainbridge Public Library|date=March 26, 2011|access-date=2012-01-25|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120119213338/http://www.bainbridgepubliclibrary.org/local-authors.aspx|archive-date=January 19, 2012}}</ref>  Her mother maintains her web page.<ref name="housley"/>
