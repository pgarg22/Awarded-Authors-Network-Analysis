'''Mona Mansour''' is an American [[playwright]] of Middle Eastern descent. She has been a member of the [[Public Theater]]'s Emerging Writers Group and a [[The Playwrights' Center|Playwrights' Center]] Core Writer. She is currently a resident playwright at [[New Dramatists]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://newdramatists.org/mona-mansour|title=Mona Mansour|work=New Dramatists}}</ref> Mansour often writes about the Middle East, and she has frequently collaborated with English director [[Mark Wing-Davey]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://aszym.blogspot.com/2011/04/i-interview-playwrights-part-345-mona.html|title=Adam Szymkowicz|author=Adam Szymkowicz|work=aszym.blogspot.com}}</ref> In addition to her theater work, Mansour has written for the television shows [[Queens Supreme]] and [[Dead Like Me]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://juniortheatre.com/artists/mona-mansour/|title=Mona Mansour|work=juniortheatre.com}}</ref>

==Produced works==
* ''Urge for Going'' - [[Public Theater]] (New York, 2011) (Public LAB production)
* ''The Hour of Feeling'' - [[Humana Festival]] at [[Actors Theatre of Louisville]] (2012)
* ''The Way West'' - [[Steppenwolf Theatre]] (Chicago, 2014); [[Marin Theatre Company]] (San Francisco, 2015); [[LAByrinth Theater Company]] (New York, 2016)

==Awards and honors==
* 2012 [[Whiting Awards|Whiting Award]]
* 2013 Sky Cooper New American Play Prize for ''The Way West''
* 2014 Middle East America Playwright Award
