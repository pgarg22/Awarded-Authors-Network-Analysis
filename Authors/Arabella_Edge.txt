'''Arabella Edge''' (born in London, England) is a  writer and novelist whose first work, ''The Company'', received a 2001 [[Commonwealth Writers Prize]] and was shortlisted for the [[Miles Franklin Award]].<ref>[http://www.austlit.edu.au/austlit/page/A11623 Austlit - Arabella Edge]</ref>

==Early life ==
Edge graduated with an English Literature degree from [[Bristol University]].  She moved to [[Sydney]], Australia in 1992 and worked as an editor for several consumer illustrated magazines. Her short tales appeared in the literary illustrated magazines ''Westerly'' and ''Ulitarra.''<ref name="Middlemiss">{{cite web| title =''The Company'' | publisher =Perry Middlemiss | year =2006 | url =http://www.middlemiss.org/lit/australian/thecompany.html | access-date =2008-11-26 }}</ref> In 2002 Edge relocated from Sydney to the small township of [[Bicheno, Tasmania]] to work on her second novel.<ref>{{cite news| last =Edge |first =Arabella |title =Arabella Edge: My Tasmania | work =The Independent (UK) | publisher =Independent News & media plc | date =2006-04-16 | url =https://www.independent.co.uk/travel/ausandpacific/arabella-edge-my-tasmania-474428.html | access-date =2008-11-26 }}</ref>

== Major works ==
Her first novel, ''The Company'', was published in 2000 and was a work of [[historical fiction]] based around the wreck of the [[Batavia (1628 ship)|''Batavia'']] on its [[maiden voyage]] off the [[Western Australia]]n coast. The book was shortlisted for the 2001 Miles Franklin Award, and won the 2001 Commonwealth Writers' Prize for Best First Book in the Southeast Asia/South Pacific region.<ref>{{cite web|title=Arabella Edge |publisher=Australia Council for the Arts |year=2007 |url=http://www.australiacouncil.gov.au/the_arts/artists_and_orgs/artists/arabella_edge |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080630082527/http://www.australiacouncil.gov.au/the_arts/artists_and_orgs/artists/arabella_edge |url-status=dead |archive-date=2008-06-30 |access-date=2008-11-26 }}</ref>

A second novel, ''The God of Spring'' was published in 2005. Also a work of historical fiction, the novel is set during the [[French Revolution]] and based on the life of artist [[Théodore Géricault]] as he researches and completes his controversial painting, ''[[The Raft of the Medusa]]''.<ref>{{cite news| last =Hughes |first =Juliette |title =The God of Spring | work =The Sydney Morning Herald | publisher =Fairfax Ltd | date =2005-12-13 | url =http://www.smh.com.au/news/book-reviews/the-god-of-spring/2005/12/13/1134236049517.html | access-date =2008-11-26 }}</ref>

Her third novel, also historical fiction, is called ''Fields of Ice'' and was published in 2011. It follows [[Lady Jane Franklin]] and her attempts to raise money to fund a rescue mission for her husband, [[Sir John Franklin]], whose ships [[HMS Erebus (1826)|HMS ''Erebus'']] and [[HMS Terror (1813)|HMS ''Terror'']] were lost while trying to find the [[North West Passage]].<ref>{{cite web |last1=Edge |first1=Arabella |title=Fields of Ice |url=https://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B005WTORN0?ref=dbs_p2d_P_R_popup_yes_pony_T1 |website=Amazon |publisher=Picador |access-date=6 March 2019}}</ref>
