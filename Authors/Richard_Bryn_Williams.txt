'''Richard Bryn Williams''', or Bryn Williams (1902&ndash;1981), was a [[Welsh people|Welsh]] writer, poet, playwright and historian. From 1875 to 1978 he was [[Archdruid]] of the National Eisteddfod of Wales.<ref name="Stephens">{{cite book|author1=Former Literature Director Meic Stephens|author2=Welsh Academy|title=The Oxford Companion to the Literature of Wales|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=P51iAAAAMAAJ|year=1986|publisher=Oxford University Press|isbn=978-0-19-211586-7|page=220}}</ref>

== Biography ==
Williams was born in [[Blaenau Ffestiniog]], [[Gwynedd]], [[Wales]].  When he was seven years old, his family moved to [[Trelew]], [[Chubut Province|Chubut]], [[Patagonia]], [[Argentina]]. He returned to Wales in 1923 and studied at the University College of North Wales (now [[Bangor University]]). He became an expert on Patagonian history and was a major contributor to the province's literature.<ref name=Miles/>

He was a supporter of the [[National Eisteddfod]], as well as competing in the cultural festival itself, and won the chair both in 1964 and 1968, and from 1975 to 1978 he was [[archdruid]], using the bardic name ''Bryn''.<ref name="Stephens"/><ref name=Miles>{{cite book|author=Dillwyn Miles|title=The Secret of the Bards of the Isle of Britain|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=DJZiAAAAMAAJ|year=1992|publisher=Gwasg Dinefwr Press|isbn=978-0-9519926-0-9|page=193}}</ref> Almost all of his numerous works reflect the life of Patagonia and its history.
