'''Kazimierz Wierzyński''' ([[Drohobycz]], [[Kingdom of Galicia and Lodomeria]], 27 August 1894 &ndash; 13 February 1969, [[London]]) was a [[Poles|Polish]] [[poet]] and [[journalist]]; an elected member of the prestigious [[Polish Academy of Literature]] in the [[Second Polish Republic]].<ref name="portalwiedzy">{{cite web | url=http://portalwiedzy.onet.pl/29184,,,,polska_akademia_literatury,haslo.html | title=Polska Akademia Literatury | publisher=Encyklopedia [[Onet.pl]], Grupa Onet | year=2011 | accessdate=December 12, 2011}}</ref>

==Life==
Kazimierz Wierzyński was born in [[Drohobycz]] (Drohobych), [[Kingdom of Galicia and Lodomeria]].<ref>[http://culture.pl/pl/tworca/kazimierz-wierzynski Kazimierz Wierzyński.]</ref> He was a co-founder, with [[Julian Tuwim]] and three other poets, of the [[Skamander]] group of experimental poets.<ref>[http://culture.pl/pl/tworca/kazimierz-wierzynski Kazimierz Wierzyński.]</ref> His work ''Olympic Laurel'' ([[Polish language|Polish]]: ''Laur olimpijski'', 1927),<ref>[http://culture.pl/pl/tworca/kazimierz-wierzynski Kazimierz Wierzyński.]</ref> which idealizes the grace and fitness of [[sportsperson|athlete]]s, won the gold medal for poetry at the [[1928 Summer Olympics|1928 Olympic Games]] in [[Amsterdam]],<ref name="databaseolympics">[http://www.databaseolympics.com/players/playerpage.htm?ilkid=WIERZKAZ01 Kazimierz Wierzyński profile] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20121013043740/http://www.databaseolympics.com/players/playerpage.htm?ilkid=WIERZKAZ01 |date=2012-10-13 }} at www.databaseolympics.com</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.olympedia.org/athletes/921250 |title=Kazimierz Wierzyński |work=Olympedia |accessdate=25 July 2020}}</ref> and his other early poems also celebrate the joy of living. In September 1939 after the invasion of Poland by Nazi Germany, Wierzyński and his wife, Helena, escaped from Poland and through Romania, Yugoslavia, Italy, France, eventually reached USA where they stayed for almost twenty years.<ref>ANDRES, ZBIGNIEW, and Agnieszka Maria Gernand. "KAZIMIERZ WIERZYŃSKI — AN EMIGRANT'S FATE." The Polish Review 55, no. 1 (2010): 35-48. Accessed July 28, 2020. www.jstor.org/stable/25779860.</ref>

His later works, written in exile, are more somber and socially conscious. ''The Bitter Crop'' (1933) includes poems about the [[United States]]. His ''Forgotten Battlefield'' (1944) contains narratives of [[World War II]]. He died in [[London]], England.<ref>[http://culture.pl/pl/tworca/kazimierz-wierzynski Kazimierz Wierzyński.]</ref>
