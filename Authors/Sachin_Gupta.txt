'''Sachin Gupta''' (born 9 March 1978) is an [[Indian film]] producer, writer and director.<ref name=dream>{{Cite news|url=https://www.thehindu.com/todays-paper/tp-features/tp-fridayreview/a-man-with-a-dream/article3218047.ece|title=A man with a dream...|date=2006-05-05|work=The Hindu|access-date=2020-02-19|language=en-IN|issn=0971-751X}}</ref> He produces films under Chilsag Motion Pictures and is also an artistic director of Chilsag Chillies Theatre Company.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.thehindu.com/todays-paper/tp-features/tp-fridayreview/a-theatre-meal-with-chillies/article3230301.ece|title=A theatre meal with chillies|date=2006-08-11|work=The Hindu|access-date=2020-02-19|language=en-IN|issn=0971-751X}}</ref> He made his writing and directorial debut with the critically acclaimed film ''[[Paranthe Wali Gali]]'' (2014).<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://timesofindia.indiatimes.com/entertainment/hindi/bollywood/news/Sachin-Gupta-Anuj-Neha-Pawar-in-Delhi/articleshow/18447576.cms|title=Sachin Gupta, Anuj & Neha Pawar in Delhi - Times of India|website=The Times of India|language=en|access-date=2020-02-19}}</ref> He also shot to fame with ''[[Pakhi (film)|Pakhi]]'' (2018).  Gupta's sojourn into theatre started when he was 12 years old, and to date he has staged more than 100 shows around the world, working as an actor, director and playwright.<ref name="Life on stage">{{Cite news|url=https://www.thehindu.com/todays-paper/tp-features/tp-metroplus/life-on-stage/article28151455.ece|title=Life on stage|date=2005-02-17|work=The Hindu|access-date=2020-02-19|language=en-IN|issn=0971-751X}}</ref> His award-winning Off Broadway plays include ''[[Celebration of Life]]'', ''[[Handicapped City]]'',<ref name="Playbill Aug 17">{{Cite magazine |url=http://www.playbill.com/news/article/131890-Handicapped-City-to-Play-SoHo-Playhouse-Aug-17 |archive-url=https://archive.today/20130104232223/http://www.playbill.com/news/article/131890-Handicapped-City-to-Play-SoHo-Playhouse-Aug-17 |archive-date=2013-01-04 |url-status=dead |title=Handicapped City to Play SoHo Playhouse Aug. 17 |date=2013-01-04|magazine=[[Playbill]]|access-date=2020-02-19}}</ref> and ''Kailashnath weds Madhumati''. He staged these in New York and in Toronto, Canada for which he got huge appreciation amongst theatre lovers in North America.<ref name=dream/><ref>{{cite news |title=Out of Box: Engineered to act:Sachin Gupta gave up software engineering to pursue theatre|url=http://www.tribuneindia.com/2005/20050409/saturday/main1.htm |work=[[The Tribune (Chandigarh)|The Tribune]] |date=9 April 2005 }}</ref> Sachin Gupta was also the recipient of the Natya Bhushan award in 2012.<ref name=dream/><ref name=Extra>{{Cite web|url=https://www.tribuneindia.com/2005/20050409/saturday/main1.htm|title=The Tribune - Magazine section - Saturday Extra|website=www.tribuneindia.com|access-date=2020-02-19}}</ref><ref name="Life on stage"/>

==Education==
Sachin Gupta was born in New Delhi, India on 9 March 1978. He did his schooling at Summer Fields School, Kailash Colony New Delhi,<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.hinduonnet.com/thehindu/fr/2005/08/05/stories/2005080501790300.htm|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20071106054835/http://www.hinduonnet.com/thehindu/fr/2005/08/05/stories/2005080501790300.htm|url-status=usurped|archive-date=6 November 2007|title=The Hindu : Entertainment Delhi / Theatre : In a world of fantasy|date=2007-11-06|access-date=2020-02-19}}</ref> and then attended Engineering College in Noida UP, India from where he received his bachelor's degree in computer engineering.<ref name=Extra/> Later he completed his MBA Degree from Symbiosis Pune. After this he went on to [[Stratford-upon-Avon]] in London to study and research [[William Shakespeare]]. This inspired him to then go to [[Fanshawe College]] in Canada to study [[theatre]]. While studying in [[Ontario]] he started the Canadian chapter of his theatre company, which immediately became a huge success. He went on to study management programs at [[Harvard University]] and studied acting at [[Lee Strasberg Theatre and Film Institute|Lee Strasberg Theatre Institute]], [[New York City|New York]]. He has also learned nuances of Shakespearean acting at the [[Emerson College]] in the US, and did a case study on Harvard University's [[American Repertory Theater|American Repertory Theatre]]. Even while studying management programs, his end goal was theatre acting and filmmaking. He then used everything he learnt at Harvard to fuel his passion into the entertainment industry.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.tribuneindia.com/2004/20040102/ncr2.htm|title=The Tribune, Chandigarh, India - NCR stories|website=www.tribuneindia.com|access-date=2020-02-19}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.financialexpress.com/news/life-in-technicolour/1082325 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140115051547/http://www.financialexpress.com/news/life-in-technicolour/1082325 |archive-date=2014-01-15 |url-status=dead |title=Life in technicolour - Financial Express|date=2014-01-15|access-date=2020-02-19}}</ref>

==Theatre career==
Sachin's sojourn into theatre started when he was 12 years old, and to date he has staged seventy-five shows around the world working as an actor, director and playwright. His award-winning [[Off Broadway]] plays include ''[[Celebration of Life]]'', ''[[Handicapped City]]'' and ''Kailashnath weds Madhumati''. He staged these in New York and in Toronto, Canada for which he got huge appreciation amongst theatre lovers in North America. His acting career started in his school days where he starred in and produced plays. This continued in college as well, as he slowly developed a passion for theatre and performing arts.<ref>{{cite news |title=Listening to the alarm|url=http://www.thehindu.com/todays-paper/tp-features/tp-fridayreview/listening-to-the-alarm/article2273634.ece |work=[[The Hindu]] |date=14 September 2007 }}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|last=Anima|first=P.|url=https://www.thehindu.com/features/friday-review/theatre/An-alarm-to-action/article16145870.ece|title=An alarm to action|date=2010-08-26|work=The Hindu|access-date=2020-02-19|language=en-IN|issn=0971-751X}}</ref><ref name="web.archive.org">{{Cite web|url=http://www.hinduonnet.com/thehindu/fr/2005/08/05/stories/2005080501790300.htm|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20071106054835/http://www.hinduonnet.com/thehindu/fr/2005/08/05/stories/2005080501790300.htm|url-status=usurped|archive-date=2007-11-06|title=The Hindu : Entertainment Delhi / Theatre : In a world of fantasy|date=2007-11-06|access-date=2020-02-19}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://hindu.com/thehindu/thscrip/print.pl?file=2007091450120300.htm&date=2007/09/14/&prd=fr&|archive-url=https://archive.today/20130104040711/http://hindu.com/thehindu/thscrip/print.pl?file=2007091450120300.htm&date=2007/09/14/&prd=fr&|url-status=dead|archive-date=2013-01-04|title=http://hindu.com/thehindu/thscrip/print.pl?file=2007091450120300.htm&â€¦|date=2013-01-04|website=archive.ph|access-date=2020-02-19}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.hinduonnet.com/thehindu/fr/2006/08/11/stories/2006081101330300.htm|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20071106050228/http://www.hinduonnet.com/thehindu/fr/2006/08/11/stories/2006081101330300.htm|url-status=usurped|archive-date=2007-11-06|title=The Hindu : Friday Review Delhi / Theatre : A theatre meal with chillies|date=2007-11-06|access-date=2020-02-19}}</ref>

In December 2002 he started his [[Chilsag Chillies Theatre Company]], which has produced thirteen original theatre productions written and directed by him addressing various social issues. He has trained more than 300 actors in various acting programs under the acting school he began in 2004.<ref name="Playbill Aug 17"/><ref>{{cite news |title='Celebration of Life' a moving play|url=http://www.tribuneindia.com/2004/20040102/ncr2.htm |work=[[The Tribune (Chandigarh)|The Tribune]] |date=2 January 2010 }}</ref> The company's very first production, ''Celebration of Life'',<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.tribuneindia.com/news/archive/breaking-the-shackles-629258|title=Breaking the shackles|last=Service|first=Tribune News|website=Tribuneindia News Service|language=en|access-date=2020-02-19}}</ref> was felicitated by the chief minister of Delhi, [[Sheila Dikshit]], and was also appreciated internationally when it was performed at the Factory Theatre, Toronto, Canada and Off-Broadway Theare, New York. Chilsag tied up with the Actor's Experimental Lab in the United States and established Salgane School of Acting in 2003. In 2004, Chilsag Chillies received letters of appreciation from [[Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam]] (former President of India), [[Shivraj Patil]] (former Home Minister, India) and the Italian cultural centre and Department of Art and Culture, Government of India for the contribution to theatre.<ref>{{cite news |title=The play's the thing|url=http://www.thehindu.com/todays-paper/tp-features/tp-fridayreview/the-plays-the-thing/article2272283.ece |work=[[The Hindu]] |date=27 April 2007 }}</ref> In late 2004, Chilsag laid the foundation of a new venture operationalising corporate theatre across its units. ''[[Theatre Pasta]]'',<ref>{{cite news |title=A theatre meal with chillies|url=http://www.thehindu.com/todays-paper/tp-features/tp-fridayreview/a-theatre-meal-with-chillies/article3230301.ece |work=[[The Hindu]] |date=11 August 2006 }}</ref> an international theatre magazine, was launched in 2005. Chilsag also stages two of its productions in locations across London in three days with its pack of English actors.

In 2005, Gupta conducted the Three Days Chilsag Chillies Theatre Festival,<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.telegraphindia.com/states/odisha/individuals-shine-at-theatre-festival/cid/350897|title=Individuals shine at theatre festival|website=www.telegraphindia.com|language=en|access-date=2020-02-19}}</ref> where he directed eight plays including four full-length plays over three days. Mid-2006 witnessed the initiation of Chilsag Children's Theatre Company and Theatre-in-Education projects. Chilsag Theatre Pasta International Awards successfully started its journey in beginning of 2007. The company staged the premier of its plays ''A Rollercoaster Ride'', ''Wake Up Call'',<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.tellychakkar.com/tv/tv-news/wakeup-call-time-gauri-karnik-anjum-farooki-rubina-dilaik-praneeta-sahu-kashmira-irani|title='Wakeup Call' time for Gauri Karnik, Anjum Farooki, Rubina Dilaik, Praneeta Sahu, Kashmira Irani, Nupur Joshi and Prerna Wanvari|last=Team|first=Tellychakkar|website=Tellychakkar.com|language=en|access-date=2020-02-19}}</ref> and ''Kailashnath Weds Madhumati'' at one of the premier performing arts location in the capital city, New Delhi.<ref>{{cite news |title=Laugh a while|url=http://www.thehindu.com/todays-paper/tp-features/tp-fridayreview/laugh-a-while/article2273063.ece |work=[[The Hindu]] |date=20 July 2007 }}</ref> Chilsag took its theatre productions ''Celebration of Life'' and ''Handicapped City''<ref name="Playbill Aug 17"/> to the international forum through shows Off-Broadway (New York City), Factory Theatre (Canada), locations across [[Boston]], [[West Virginia]], and other cities. This year marked the launch of International Repertory for Chilsag with American actors.

In 2009, Chilsag started its own Drama Therapy Centre. Late 2010 witnessed a collaboration with [[Indian Council for Cultural Relations]] and production of the play ''Wake Up Call''. In 2011, director Sachin Gupta received the Natya Bhushan, the highest ranking award for his contribution to Hindi theatre. 2015 also witnessed the conceptualisation and production of the biggest musical to be staged in the capital city, ''Chota Bheem The Musical'',<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://tennews.in/chhota-bheema-and-chutki-in-le-meridien-new-delhi/|title=Sachin Gupta introduces the first ever musical theatre featuring Chhota Bheema and Chutki in Le Meridien, New Delhi.|date=2015-07-20|website=tennews.in - National News Portal, Breaking, Latest, Top and Trending, News|language=en-US|access-date=2020-02-19}}</ref> which was attended by over 18,000 in the [[Siri Fort Auditorium]]. In 2016, Chilsag signed a six-film deal with United States-based production house for the production of meaningful cinema and short films. A new production, ''Kafan'', was also staged in 2017 with collaboration with the Indo-American Friendship Association and supported by the [[Ministry of Culture (India)|Ministry of Culture]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://paranthewaligalithefilm.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/03/dainikbhaskar.jpg|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140115043627/http://paranthewaligalithefilm.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/03/dainikbhaskar.jpg|url-status=dead|archive-date=2014-01-15|date=2014-01-15|access-date=2020-02-19}}</ref>

Internationally, he marked his debut in 2005 as he moved to North America to expand his film and theatre work. Since then he has performed the world over, including major cities like New York, Toronto, London, Birmingham, Boston, Philadelphia, Mumbai, New Delhi, Kolkata, Ontario, Orissa, Bangalore, and Goa. He has been invited to perform at some of the most prestigious international festivals. In 2011 he staged his theatre production ''The Play Begins @8pm'' in [[Orissa, India|Orissa]] while working as an actor along with film actor and playback singer [[Vasundhara Das]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://timesofindia.indiatimes.com/A-dramatic-idea/articleshow/7863701.cms|title=A dramatic idea - Times of India|website=The Times of India|access-date=2020-02-19}}</ref>

Film and television actors who have worked with Gupta's theatre company include [[Huma Qureshi (actress)|Huma Qureshi]], [[Jitin Gulati]], [[Deepak Wadhwa]], Vasundhara Das, [[Gauri Karnik]], Anjum Farooki, [[Nausheen Ali Sardar]],<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.dnaindia.com/entertainment/report-i-m-foot-in-mouth-says-nausheen-ali-sardar-1528489|title=I'm foot in mouth, says Nausheen Ali Sardar|last=Mazumder|first=Jayeeta|date=2011-04-05|website=DNA India|language=en|access-date=2020-02-19}}</ref> [[Anuj Saxena]], [[Prerna Wanvari]], Kashmira Irani, [[Shraddha Musale]], [[Rubina Dilaik]] and Neha Pawar.

He also acted in the play ''Kailash Nath Weds Madhumati'', an Off Broadway musical comedy production in New York in 2009.
