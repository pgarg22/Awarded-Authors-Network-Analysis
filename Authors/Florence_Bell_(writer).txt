'''Dame Florence Eveleen Eleanore Bell''', {{post-nominals|country=GBR|DBE|JP|sep=,|size=100%}} (née Olliffe; 9 September 1851 - 16 May 1930)<ref name=obit>{{cite news | title = Lady Bell | newspaper = The Times | location = London, England | date = 17 May 1930 | page = 14}}</ref> was a [[United Kingdom|British]] writer and playwright.

==Biography==
Bell was born in [[Paris]], [[France]] in 1851,<ref>The National Archives, RG33/71/page4,  Foreign Registers & Returns, Baptisms, Consular/Overseas: Paris, France. 
Florence Eveline Eleanor Olliffe.
Baptism date: 17 Apr 1851.
Father: Joseph Olliffe.
Mother: Laura.
Father's occupation Physician</ref> the youngest of four children of Irish-born [[Joseph Francis Olliffe]] (later Sir Joseph Olliffe) and Laura Cubitt.<ref>{{cite web |first=Angela V. |last=John |url=http://www.oxforddnb.com/view/article/41106 |title=Bell, Florence Eveleen Eleanore, Lady Bell (1851–1930) |publisher=Oxford Dictionary of National Biography, Oxford University Press |date=2004}}</ref> She was the second wife of [[Sir Hugh Bell, 2nd Baronet|Sir Hugh Bell, 2nd Baronet Bell of Rounton Grange]], and was styled Lady Florence Bell. The couple had three children:

* Hugh Lowthian Bell (21 October 1878 – 2 February 1926)
* Florence Elsa Bell (c. 1880 – 3 May 1971, married [[Herbert Richmond|Admiral Sir Herbert Richmond]])
* [[Mary Trevelyan (Lady)|Mary Katherine Bell]], OBE (c. 1882 - 8 October 1966, married [[Charles Philips Trevelyan]])<ref>{{Cite ODNB|title=Trevelyan [née Bell], Mary Katharine [Molly], Lady Trevelyan (1881–1966), political hostess and voluntary worker|url=https://www.oxforddnb.com/view/10.1093/ref:odnb/9780198614128.001.0001/odnb-9780198614128-e-53939|access-date=2021-05-04|year = 2004|language=en|doi=10.1093/ref:odnb/53939}}</ref>

Bell was the stepmother to her husband's children from his first marriage: [[United Kingdom|British]] explorer and key political figure in the [[Middle East]], [[Gertrude Bell]] (1868–1926) and [[Sir Maurice Hugh Lowthian Bell, 3rd Baronet|Maurice, 3rd Baronet]] (1871–1944).

In 1918 she was appointed as Dame Commander of the [[Order of the British Empire]] (DBE). She died on 16 May 1930 at her home, 95 Sloane Street, [[Chelsea, London|Chelsea]].
