'''Sherryl Jordan''' (born 8 June 1949) is a [[New Zealand]] writer for children and young adults, specialising in fantasy and historical fiction. She has written a number of children's and young adult works published in New Zealand and overseas. She is best known for her books ''[[The Juniper Game]]'' and ''[[The Raging Quiet]]''. She received the [[Margaret Mahy Medal and Lecture Award|Margaret Mahy Medal]] for her contribution to children's literature, publishing and literacy in 2001.

==Biography==
Jordan was born on 8 June 1949 in Hawera, New Zealand, and grew up in Normanby, near [[Mount Taranaki]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://tauranga.kete.net.nz/tauranga_local_history/topics/show/1467|title=Sherryl Rose Jordan (1949– )|last=Libraries)|first=Debbie McCauley (Tauranga City|website=tauranga.kete.net.nz|access-date=29 May 2016}}</ref> Her adult life has been spent in [[Tauranga]]. Her early work in children's literature was as an illustrator, and she has written picture books, middle grade fiction, and young adult fiction. Her knowledge of sign language and her experience working as a teacher aide with deaf children is a clear influence on her historical fiction, ''The Raging Quiet''.
