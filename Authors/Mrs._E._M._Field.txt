'''Louise Frances Field''' (née Story, 1856–1940)<ref name="Oxford reference">{{cite web |url=http://www.oxfordreference.com/view/10.1093/oi/authority.20110803095816915 |title=Mrs. E. M. Field - Oxford Reference |newspaper=Oxford Reference|date=  |author= |accessdate= November 7, 2016}}</ref> was an [[Irish people|Irish]] novelist and literary critic who wrote an early historical study of children's literature in England.<ref name="Bradley2007">{{cite book|author=Johanna Bradley|title=From Chapbooks to Plumb Cake: The History of Children's Literature|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=65KJo_XpLoYC&pg=PA10|year=2007|isbn=978-0-549-34070-6|pages=10–}}</ref><ref name="Butler2006">{{cite book|author=C. ==Life and work==
Born Louise Frances Story in 1856 to a Justice of the Peace in Bingfield, [[Crossdoney]], Co. Cavan, Field wrote under the name Mrs Field or Mrs E. M. Field, and is occasionally listed as Louisa.<ref name="Spectator">{{cite web |url=http://archive.spectator.co.uk/article/10th-november-1888/24/bryda-by-mrs-e-m-field-wells-gardner-darton-and |title=Bryda. By Mrs. E. M. Field. (Wells Gardner, Darton, and » 10 Nov 1888|newspaper=The Spectator Archive  |date= 10 November 1888 |author= |accessdate= November 7, 2016}}</ref><ref name="Ricorso">{{cite web |url=http://www.ricorso.net/rx/az-data/authors/f/Field_EM/life.htm |title=E. M. Field |newspaper=Ricorso|accessdate= November 7, 2016}}</ref> She wrote stories for and about children and was known for her writings on the Sepoy [[Indian Rebellion of 1857]] and the [[Great Famine (Ireland)|Irish Famine]].<ref name="Rupkatha">{{cite web |url=http://rupkatha.com/the-mutiny-novels-a-series/ |title=Book Review: The Mutiny Novels: A Series |newspaper=Rupkatha.com |date=  |author= |accessdate= November 6, 2016}}</ref><ref name="Bourke2002">{{cite book|author=Angela Bourke|title=The Field Day Anthology of Irish Writing|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=qZ6W1LiIyYYC&pg=PA924|year=2002|publisher=NYU Press|isbn=978-0-8147-9907-9|pages=924–}}</ref><ref name="MyUser_Madrasmusings.com_November_6_2016c">{{cite web |url=http://madrasmusings.com/Vol%2023%20No%2013/six-novels-the-great-revolt-their-theme.html |title=Madras Musings - We care for Madras that is Chennai |newspaper=Madras musings|accessdate= November 6, 2016}}</ref><ref name="Victorian">{{cite web |url=http://www.victorianresearch.org/atcl/show_author.php?aid=2573 |title=Author Information At the Circulating Library |newspaper=Victorian research |accessdate= November 6, 2016}}</ref><ref name="Lovett2005">{{cite book|author=[[Charlie Lovett]]|title=Lewis Carroll Among His Books: A Descriptive Catalogue of the Private Library of Charles L.
