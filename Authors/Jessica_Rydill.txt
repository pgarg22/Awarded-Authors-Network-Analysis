'''Jessica Rydill''' is a British [[fantasy]] author from the [[West Country]]. She was born in 1959. She studied at [[King's College, Cambridge]] and the College of Law, working as a solicitor for 13 years. Her travels in [[Israel]], [[France]], [[Eastern Europe]] and [[Southern Africa]] have provided some of the inspiration for her writing. She was a founder member of the Write Fantastic writers' group together with Fantasy authors [[Sarah Ash]], [[Chaz Brenchley]], [[Mark Chadbourn]], [[Juliet E. McKenna]], the late Deborah J. Miller, [[Stan Nicholls]], Kari Sperring, [[Freda Warrington]] and [[Ian Whates]].

Her interests include collecting Asian [[ball-jointed doll]]s, [[Richard III]], [[Sasha dolls]], [[mythology|myth]], and [[East European]] music. Her short story ''My Brother Jonathan'' was short-listed for the Ian St James award in 1999 and she appears in ''The New Writer'' magazine Roll of Honour.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.thenewwriter.com/RollOfHonour.htm|title = NameBright - Coming Soon}}</ref><ref>https://zemres.org</ref>

Her first novel, ''Children of the Shaman'', was published by Orbit in 2001, and was short-listed for the Locus magazine best first novel in 2002.<ref>[http://www.pirandot.de/Pirandot/SF-Awards/Major/Locus/?Year=2002 Locus Awards 2002] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110718205819/http://www.pirandot.de/Pirandot/SF-Awards/Major/Locus/?Year=2002 |date=18 July 2011 }}</ref> A sequel, ''The Glass Mountain'', appeared in October 2002.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.books-by-isbn.com/authors/jessica/rydill/|title = ✎ Books by Jessica Rydill}}</ref> A short story, ''The Anniversary'', was published in an anthology printed by [http://newconpress.co.uk/ NewCon Press] in 2010 to celebrate the fifth anniversary of [https://web.archive.org/web/20110209194335/http://thewritefantastic.com/ The Write Fantastic]. 

Both books have been reissued by small press [http://kristell-ink.com/ Kristell Ink Publishing], together with sequels ''Malarat'' and ''Winterbloom''. They have cover art by award-winning artist [http://www.multigrade.it/ Daniele Serra] and ''Children of the Shaman'' also has internal illustrations by [https://web.archive.org/web/20170516021808/http://enoksen-art.com/ Evelinn Enoksen], who designed the maps. After publication of ''Children of the Shaman'',<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.sffworld.com/2016/07/children-of-the-shaman-cover-reveal/|title=Children of the Shaman Cover Reveal – SFFWorld|website=www.sffworld.com|language=en-US|access-date=2017-05-09}}</ref> the three remaining books were released through Amazon in February 2019 and are available from many booksellers.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://shamansland.com/2019/02/28/winterbloom-day/|title=Winterbloom day|date=2019-02-28|website=shamansland|language=en|access-date=2019-03-18}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.fantasticfiction.com/r/jessica-rydill/|title=Jessica Rydill|website=www.fantasticfiction.com|access-date=2019-03-18}}</ref>  

Rydill's first three books are set in the alternate world of Mir, with elements of [[Slavic fantasy|Slavic Fantasy]], but ''Winterbloom'' is the first in which part of the action takes place in the real world, in her home-town, the city of [[Bath, Somerset|Bath]], and features a number of historical figures from Dr [[John Dee]] to [[Aleister Crowley]]. 

Her sister [[Sarah Ash]], author of the [[Tears of Artamon]] trilogy, the ''Tide Dragons''<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.facebook.com/tidedragons/|title=Tide Dragons|website=www.facebook.com|language=en|access-date=2019-03-18}}</ref> series and many other works,<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.isfdb.org/cgi-bin/ea.cgi?Sarah_Ash|title=Summary Bibliography: Sarah Ash|website=www.isfdb.org|access-date=2019-03-18}}</ref> is also a fantasy novelist.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.sarah-ash.com/|title=Sarah Ash – Fantasy Author|website=www.sarah-ash.com|access-date=2019-03-18}}</ref>
