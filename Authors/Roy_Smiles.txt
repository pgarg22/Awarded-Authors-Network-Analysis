'''Roy Smiles''' (born 1966) is a singer-songwriter & playwright from Ealing, West London. He is also an occasional actor.

Smiles has written twenty seven theatre plays & six radio plays, the best known of which is [[Kurt and Sid]], a 2009 London West End production about the fictional meeting of [[Sid Vicious]] and [[Kurt Cobain]].<ref>{{cite web|last=Spencer |first=Charles |url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/culture/theatre/theatre-reviews/6198258/Kurt-and-Sid-at-the-Trafalgar-Studios-review.html |title=Kurt and Sid at the Trafalgar Studios, review |publisher=Telegraph |access-date=21 January 2017}}</ref> He has released eight albums of songs:<ref>{{cite web|url=https://roysmiles.bandcamp.com/ |title=Music &#124; Roy Smiles |website=Roysmiles.bandcamp.com |date=1 June 2013|access-date=21 January 2017}}</ref> ''Drunks & Dreamers,<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://audiomack.com/roy-smiles/album/drunks-dreamers|title=Audiomack &#124; Free Music Sharing and Discovery}}</ref> Time's Moving On, Seize The Day, Autumn Song, Bremen, The Trains & The Rain, Northern Angels ''&'' Lost Souls.''<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://audiomack.com/roy-smiles|title = Audiomack &#124; Free Music Sharing and Discovery}}</ref><ref>https://www.jamendo.com/artist/461369/roy-smiles</ref>

==Career==
His first play staged in 1992 at the [[Battersea Arts Centre]], ''Schmucks'' <ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.bloomsbury.com/uk/schmucks-9781786825162|title = Schmucks}}</ref>was about a fictitious meeting between Groucho Marx and Lenny Bruce.{{citation needed|date=March 2018}}

In 2002 he played the role of Itzak Heller, a Jewish collaborator, in Roman Polanski's ''The Pianist''.{{citation needed|date=October 2016}}

Smiles's stage and radio plays focus largely on biographical subjects such as Albert Camus (''The Weight Of Days''),<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.bloomsbury.com/uk/the-weight-of-days-9781786825254/|title=The Weight of Days}}</ref> John Garfield (''Ballad Of John Garfield''), Terry Thomas (''Memories Of A Cad''),<ref>https://www.bbc.co.uk/programmes/b04vkjsm</ref> Richard Burton (''The VIP''), Evelyn Waugh (''Waugh in Winter''),<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.bloomsbury.com/uk/waugh-in-winter-9781786825223/|title=Waugh in Winter}}</ref> the Beyond The Fringe team (''Good Evening''),<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.benedictcumberbatch.co.uk/2013/02/02/cumberbatchweb-review-of-good-evening-a-tribute-to-beyond-the-fringe/ |title=Cumberbatchweb – Review of Good Evening – A Tribute to Beyond the Fringe |website=Benedictcumberbatch.co.uk |date=2 February 2013 |access-date=21 January 2017}}</ref> Arthur Miller/Marilyn Monroe (''Reno''),<ref>{{cite web|url=http://brighton.fringeguru.com/reviews/brighton-2015/reno |title=Reno &#124; Fringe Guru |website=Brighton.fringeguru.com |access-date=21 January 2017}}</ref> George Orwell (''Year of the Rat''),<ref>{{cite news|author=Alfred Hickling |url=https://www.theguardian.com/stage/2008/mar/14/theatre |title=Theatre review: Year of the Rat / West Yorkshire Playhouse, Leeds &#124; Stage |newspaper=[[The Guardian]] |access-date=21 January 2017}}</ref> Tony Hancock (''The Lad Himself''),<ref>{{cite web|last=Cavendish |first=Dominic |url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/culture/theatre/edinburgh-festival-reviews/9494725/Edinburgh-2012-The-Lad-Himself-Gilded-Balloon-review.html |title=Edinburgh 2012: The Lad Himself, Gilded Balloon, review |publisher=Telegraph |access-date=21 January 2017}}</ref> Robert F. Kennedy (''The Last Pilgrim''),<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.timeout.com/london/theatre/the-last-pilgrim |title=The Last Pilgrim at White Bear Theatre – Fringe – Time Out London |website=Timeout.com |date=6 October 2010 |access-date=21 January 2017}}</ref> PG Woodhouse (''Plum'')<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.theatreview.org.nz/reviews/review.php?id=7284 |title=PLUM – A regular wheeze plus facts and irony |website=Theatreview.org.nz |access-date=21 January 2017}}</ref> and the Enola Gay (''Bombing People'').

A number of his plays have debuted or transferred to theatres around the world, such as [[Pythonesque]],<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/culture/theatre/edinburgh-festival-reviews/6017048/Edinburgh-Festival-2009-Pythonesque-review.html|title=Edinburgh Festival 2009: Pythonesque at the Cowbarn, E4's Udderbelly, review}}</ref> the story of the Monty Python team, which opened in South Africa before moving to Edinburgh as part of the Fringe Festival.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.scotsman.com/lifestyle/theatre-review-pythonesque-1-766204 |title=Theatre review: Pythonesque |newspaper=[[The Scotsman]] |date=2 August 2009|access-date=21 January 2017}}</ref> ''Ying Tong – A Walk with the Goons'', the story of Spike Milligan's nervous breakdown whilst writing The Goon Show, was staged at West Yorkshire Playhouse in 2004, transferring the following year to the New Ambassadors Theatre in London's West End before touring the US, Australia, New Zealand and South Africa.<ref>{{cite news|author=Michael Billington |url=https://www.theguardian.com/stage/2005/feb/15/theatre1 |title=Ying Tong, New Ambassadors, London &#124; Stage |newspaper=[[The Guardian]] |access-date=21 January 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|author=Charles Spencer |url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/culture/theatre/3637241/The-genius-and-the-torment-of-Spike-Milligan.html |title=The genius – and the torment – of Spike Milligan |publisher=Telegraph |access-date=21 January 2017}}</ref> 

Six of his plays have been broadcast on BBC Radio 4.  

''Year of the Rat'',<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.britishtheatreguide.info/reviews/yearrat-rev|title = Theatre review: The Year of the Rat at West Yorkshire Playhouse}}</ref> ''Ying Tong '', ''Pythonesque'' and ''Kurt & Sid'' are published by Oberon Books.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.oberonbooks.com/roy-smiles.html |title=Roy Smiles |website=Oberonbooks.com |access-date=21 January 2017}}</ref> 

''Funny People'', his book on his comic influences, was published by Oberon Books in 2016.<ref>https://www.amazon.com.au/Funny-People-Roy-Smiles/dp/1849430349</ref>

''Ten Plays'' by Roy Smiles was published by Oberon Books in 2018.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.oberonbooks.com/roy-smiles-10-plays.html|title=Roy Smiles: 10 Plays|website=Oberon Books|access-date=18 July 2020}}</ref>

Two compilations of songs: ''Go Gently (Best Of)'' <ref>https://open.spotify.com/album/0752JMhnJo27bQaFiFfTM6&</ref> ''A Land Called Home (Best Of II)''<ref>https://open.spotify.com/album/1ezEeL8WSHJarWGlb1rVzD</ref> were released on Spotify in 2021. 

''The Funny Girls'', his play about Barbra Streisand & Joan Rivers younger years, was first staged at Wimbledon Theatre in September 2021.<ref>https://www.timeandleisure.co.uk/the-funny-girls/</ref>
