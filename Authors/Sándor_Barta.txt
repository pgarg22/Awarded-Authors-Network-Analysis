'''Sándor Barta''' (7 October 1897, [[Budapest]] – 3 June, 1938) was a [[Hungarian people|Hungarian]] poet active in various [[avant-garde]] movements. After emigrating to the [[Soviet Union]], he remained active amongst [[German language|German]]-speaking cultural groups. He was an active agent in carrying out the [[Stalinist purges]] amongst the literary intelligentsia, but was himself arrested by the Soviet authorities and shot in 1938.<ref>{{cite journal |last1=Forgács |first1=Éva |last2=Miller |first2=Tyrus |title=The Avant-Garde in Budapest and in Exile in Vienna: A Tett (1915-6), Ma (Budapest 1916-9; Vienna 1920-6), Egység (1922-4), Akasztott Ember (1922), 2x2 (1922), Ék (1923-4), Is (1924), 365 (1925), Dokumentum (1926-7), and Munka (1928-39) |journal=Modernist Magazines |date=2013 |volume=III |issue=Part II |pages=1128–1156 |url=https://monoskop.org/images/6/61/Forgacs_Eva_Miller_Tyrus_2013_The_Avant-Garde_in_Budapest_and_in_Exile_in_Vienna.pdf |accessdate=25 September 2019}}</ref>

==Early life==
His schooling stopped at secondary school and he went to work in his father's workshop. However he suffered from [[tuberculosis]] as a teenager. Then he got a job as a bookshop assistant and became involved with the [[Galileo Circle]].<ref name="HEL">{{cite web |title=Magyar Életrajzi Lexikon 1000-1990 |url=http://mek.oszk.hu/00300/00355/html/ABC00523/01135.htm |website=mek.oszk.hu |publisher=Hungarian Electronic Library |accessdate=28 November 2018}}</ref> He started writing poetry, and his first poem was published in ''[[MA (journal)|MA]]''. He married the poet [[Erzsi Újvári]] the sister of [[Lajos Kassák]] who edited ''MA''.<ref name="CPN">{{cite book |last1=Cornis-Pope |first1=Marcel |last2=Neubauer |first2=John |title=History of the Literary Cultures of East-Central Europe: Junctures and disjunctures in the 19th and 20th centuries. Volume III: The making and remaking of literary institutions |date=2007 |publisher=John Benjamins Publishing |location=Amsterdam/Philadelphia |isbn=9789027292353 |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=taw6AAAAQBAJ&q=%22Erzsi+%C3%9Ajv%C3%A1ri%22+%22sandor+Barta%22&pg=PA27 |language=en}}</ref>{{rp|27}}

==In Vienna==
===MA period===
Barta wrote the manifesto "A zöldfejü ember" (The Green Headed Man) appeared in the January 1921 issue of ''MA'' in January. This indicates an interest in [[Dadaism]], but Lassák was to later distance himself from Dadaism and Barta claiming that it was only published to appease Barta.<ref name="Mazzone">{{cite journal |last1=Mazzone |first1=Marian |title=Dadaist Text/Constructivist Image: Kassák's Képarchitektúra |journal=Hungarian Studies Review |date=2004 |volume=XXXI |issue=1–2 |pages=15–46 |url=https://www.academia.edu/6083011 |accessdate=29 November 2018 |language=en}}</ref>

===''Akasztott Ember''===
Barta launched the journal ''[[Akasztott Ember]]'' ("Hanged Man") in November 1922. In 1923 it was renamed ''Ék'' ("Wedge").<ref name="Cardullo">{{cite book |last1=Cardullo |first1=Bert |title=Theories of the Avant-garde Theatre: A Casebook from Kleist to Camus |date=2013 |publisher=Rowman & Littlefield |isbn=9780810887046 |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=7qE53ri6EbYC&q=S%C3%A1ndor+Barta+Uitz&pg=PR63 |language=en}}</ref> He explained the title thus: "As people, we now feel ourselves to be hanged. And if someone doesn't feel hanged, he belongs among those who hang and kick us".<ref name="AG to PA">{{cite journal |last1=Botar |first1=Oliver A. I. |title=From the Avant-Garde to "Proletarian Art" The Emigre Hungarian Journals Egyseg and Akasztott Ember, 1922-23 |date=1993 |volume=52|url=https://monoskop.org/images/b/b8/Botar_Oliver_AI_1993_From_the_Avant-Garde_to_Proletarian_Art.pdf |accessdate=26 June 2018}}</ref>
