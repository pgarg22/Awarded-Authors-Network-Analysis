'''Mirko Petrović-Njegoš''' ({{lang-sr-cyr|Мирко Петровић-Његош}}; 19 August 1820 – 1 August 1867) was a Montenegrin military commander, politician and poet, belonging to the [[House of Petrović-Njegoš]]. He was given the title of ''veliki vojvoda'' (Grand Duke) of [[Grahovo, Nikšić|Grahovo]], and is thus also known as '''Vojvoda Mirko''' (Duke Mirko). He was the father of [[Nikola I Petrović-Njegoš|Nikola]], the future ruler of Montenegro.

==Life==
Mirko was born on 19 August 1820, in [[Njeguši]],<ref name="Paunović1998">{{cite book|author=Marinko Paunović|title=Srbi: biografije znamenitih : A-Š|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=9qE-AQAAIAAJ|year=1998|publisher=Emka|page=195|isbn=9788685205040}}</ref> the son of Stanko Petrović-Njegoš and wife Krstinja Vrbica. He was the older brother of [[Danilo I, Prince of Montenegro|Prince Danilo I]], while his uncle was [[Petar II Petrović-Njegoš]]. He is famous for winning the [[Battle of Grahovac]] on 1 May 1858, leading the Montenegrin army against the [[Ottoman Empire|Ottomans]].

In 1862, after the [[Convention of Scutari]], he was deported because he had fought against the Ottomans.

His epic corpus ''Junački spomenik'' (''Heroic Monument''), published in the Montenegrin capital of [[Cetinje]] in 1864, glorifies Montenegro and Montenegrins, and tells of the great victories over the Ottoman Empire.

He was married in Njeguši on {{OldStyleDate|November 7|1840|October 26}} to Anastasia (Stana) Martinović ([[Bajice]], {{OldStyleDate|June 27|1824|June 15}} – [[Cetinje]], {{OldStyleDate|December 23|1894|January 12}}), daughter of [[Vojevoda]] Drago Martinović and wife Stana Martinović.<ref>Burke's Royal Families of the World; Europe and Latin-America</ref>

They had three children: 
* [[Nikola I Petrović-Njegoš|Nikola]] ({{OldStyleDate|October 7|1841|September 25}} – March 1, 1921)
* Princess Anastasia (d. March 29, 1879), married to Sava Plamenac
* Princess Maria, married to Capt. Y. Gopcević.

In February 1857, vojvoda Mirko Petrović-Njegoš replaced his cousin [[Đorđije Petrović-Njegoš]] as the President of the [[Government of Montenegro|Governing Senate of Montenegro and the Highlands]], he served during the reign of his brother prince [[Danilo II Petrović-Njegoš|Danilo II]] and later during the reign of his son prince [[Nicholas I of Montenegro|Nicholas]]. He held the position of president until his death in August 1867.
