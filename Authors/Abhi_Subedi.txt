'''Abhi Subedi''' ({{lang-ne|अभि सुवेदी}}; born on June 30, 1945) is a Nepali [[poet]], [[playwright]], [[linguist]], columnist, translator and critic, who writes in [[Nepali language|Nepali]] and [[English language|English]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.foundationsaarcwriters.com/literature/saarc-writers-intellectual/poets/nepal/196-abhi-subedi-nepal |title=Archived copy |access-date=2014-12-17 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141217064335/http://www.foundationsaarcwriters.com/literature/saarc-writers-intellectual/poets/nepal/196-abhi-subedi-nepal |archive-date=2014-12-17 }}</ref>

== Biography ==
Abhi Subedi was born in Sabla village of Tehrathum district in eastern Nepal. He was the 21st child of his father and the 7th child of his mother. He recognized [[Bengali alphabet|Bengali]] letters before [[Devnagari]] scripts after seeing his mother read Bengali epic [[Kashiram Das]]'s [[Mahabharata]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.dcnepalonline.com/video/int_video.php?nid=2936|title=DC Nepal Main Page}}</ref>

== Education ==
Abhi Subedi was offered a British Council scholarship to the University of Edinburgh in 1978 and completed his post-graduate degree. He wrote about his struggle for education viz a viz the Thatcher government's cut on foreign scholarships and grants and the bureaucratic malice of his university back home.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Subedi |first1=Abhi |title=Struggle for Education |url=https://kathmandupost.com/columns/2021/05/08/struggle-for-education-in-britain |publisher=Kathmandu Post}}</ref> 

He earned a [[Ph.D]] on the pragmatics of poetry from [[Tribhuvan University]], Kirtipur and did postgraduate work in [[Stylistics (field of study)|stylistics]] and applied linguistics at the [[University of Edinburgh]] in [[Scotland]]. 

[[File:South Asian Writers.jpg|thumb|250px|Abhi Subedi with [[Suman Pokhrel]], [[Sitakanta Mahapatra]] and [[Pratibha Ray]] during South Asian Literature Festival in [[New Delhi]] in February 2017]].

== Career ==
Subedi started as a teacher of English at [[Tribhuvan University]] and after returning from Europe taught stylistics there. He taught for 40years at the Central Department of English in [[Tribhuvan University]], and headed the department for more than a decade.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://ecs.com.np/features/abhi-subedi|title=Abhi Subedi}}</ref> Subedi is vice-President of the Nepali Folklore Society of Nepal. He is the founding former President of the [[International Theatre Institute]] (ITI) UNESCO from 2000 to 2008 and member of International Playwright's Forum from 2000 to 2011. Subedi became President (1990-1992) and two times General Secretary of the Linguistic Society of Nepal. He was also President of the Literary Association of Nepal. He is a member of the International Association of Theatre Critics. He has been involved in a number of interdisciplinary study groups and is a prolific writer on issues of freedom, culture, literature, arts and social transformation. His essays and seminar papers have been published in Nepal and abroad.<ref>http://lifeandlegends.com/suman-pokhrel-translated-dr-abhi-subedi/</ref>

== Works ==
Subedi has published over two dozen books on different subjects. Several of his plays have been performed by well-known theatre groups in Nepal and abroad.<ref>http://lifeandlegends.com/suman-pokhrel-translated-dr-abhi-subedi/</ref>

==Ecology and Conservation==
His keynote speech at the Nepal Year of Conservation 2009 UK Celebration hosted by the Zoological Society of London was published in
the Kathmandu Post on 25 November 2009. In his address he stated that the plentifulness of animals and the perennial existence of opulent nature was a myth and gave examples of visual dramatisation of animals in relation with hegemonic power structures.
