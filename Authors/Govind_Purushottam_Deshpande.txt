'''Govind Purushottam Deshpande''' ([[Devanagari]]: गोविंद पुरुषोत्तम देशपांडे; 1938 – 16 October 2013<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.business-standard.com/article/pti-stories/marathi-playwright-g-p-deshpande-passes-away-113101600967_1.html|title=Marathi playwright G P Deshpande passes away|date=2013-10-16|work=Business Standard India|access-date=2019-11-09}}</ref>) was a [[Marathi people|Marathi]] playwright and academic from [[Maharashtra]], India.

==Biography==
===Education and career===
{{Unreferenced section|date=May 2021}}
Born in [[Nashik]], Deshpande grew up in [[Rahimatpur]], where he went to school. He completed an MA in Ancient Indian History from [[Maharaja Sayajirao University of Baroda]], and enrolled for a Ph.D. at the School of International Affairs, New Delhi. This school subsequently became part of the Jawaharlal Nehru University. Deshpande completed his Ph.D. and later taught at the Centre for East Asian Studies at the [[Jawaharlal Nehru University, Delhi|Jawaharlal Nehru University]] in [[New Delhi]]. He lived in [[Pune]] after retirement. He suffered a brain hemorrhage in July 2013 and was hospitalized. He died at home on 16&nbsp;October&nbsp;2013.

===Writing===
Deshpande wrote a column in the ''Economic and Political Weekly'' for four decades.{{Citation needed|date=May 2021}} His collection of essays on culture and politics, ''Dialectics of Defeat: Problems of Culture in Post-Colonial India'' (Seagull, Kolkata), was published in 2006, and he also issued a collection of poems, ''Ityadi Ityadi Kavita'' (Etc. etc. poems).{{Citation needed|date=May 2021}}
He was the editor of the anthology of Indian plays in translation, ''Modern Indian Drama'',<ref>[http://www.cse.iitk.ac.in/~amit/books/deshpande-2004-modern-indian-drama.html Modern Indian Drama], ed. G.P. Deshpande, [[Sahitya Akademi]], 2004</ref> published by [[Sahitya Akademi]], 2004.

==Awards and recognition==
{{Unreferenced section|date=May 2021}}
Deshpande received the Maharashtra State Award for his collective work in 1977 and the [[Sangeet Natak Akademi Award]] for playwrighting in 1996.

==Family==
{{Unreferenced section|date=May 2021}}
Deshpande's wife, Kalindi, is a women's rights activist. His daughter, Ashwini, is an economist at the [[Delhi School of Economics]], and his son, Sudhanva, is a publisher with [[LeftWord Books]] and a theatre activist with [[Jana Natya Manch]], Delhi. Marathi stage and film actor [[Jyoti Subhash]] is his sister, and her daughter [[Amruta Subhash|Amruta]], also an actor, is his niece.

==Notable plays==
* ''Udhwastha Dharmashala'' (published in English as ''A Man in Dark Times''), directed by Dr Shreeram Lagoo (Marathi), Om Puri (Hindi), Rajinder Nath (Hindi), [[Shyamanand Jalan]] ([[Padatik (Indian theatre group)|Padatik]]) in 1982.{{Citation needed|date=May 2021}}
* ''Andhar Yatra'' (A Journey in Darkness), directed in Marathi by Satyadev Dubey and in Hindi by Rajinder Nath.{{Citation needed|date=May 2021}}
* ''Satyashodhak'' (The Truth Seeker) on the life and times of the 19th-century social reformer [[Jotiba Phule]], directed by Sudhanva Deshpande and performed by Jana Natya Manch.<ref>{{cite web |title=Life As Message|url=http://www.tehelka.com/story_main53.asp?filename=hub160612LIFE.asp |date=16 June 2012 |publisher=[[Tehelka]] Magazine, Vol 9, Issue 24 }}</ref> The Marathi productions were directed by Sharad Bhuthadia (Kolhapur) and Atul Pethe (Pune), who also directed a Kannada production in Heggodu.{{Citation needed|date=May 2021}}
* ''Antim Divas'' directed by G.P. Deshpande for Padatik in Kolkata (in Hindi) and by Jyoti Subhash in Marathi.{{Citation needed|date=May 2021}}
* ''Chanakya Vishnugupta'' directed by Satyadev Dubey for the National School of Drama in Hindi with Ashish Vidyarthi and Baharul Islam in the title roles, and by Dr Shreeram Lagoo in Marathi with himself in the title role.{{Citation needed|date=May 2021}}
* ''Music System'' directed by Vijay Kenkre in Marathi.<ref name=":0">{{Cite news|url=https://www.thehindu.com/entertainment/theatre/what-ails-theatre/article29910352.ece|title=What ails theatre?|last=Gahlot|first=Deepa|date=2019-11-07|work=The Hindu|access-date=2019-11-09|language=en-IN|issn=0971-751X}}</ref>
* ''Raastey'' directed in Marathi by Vijay Kenkre,<ref name=":0" /> and Hindi by [[Jyoti Subhash]], directed by Satyadev Dubey for the National School of Drama Repertory Company, and by [[Arvind Gaur]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.thehindu.com/features/friday-review/theatre/a-journey-of-questions/article3839818.ece|title=A journey of questions
