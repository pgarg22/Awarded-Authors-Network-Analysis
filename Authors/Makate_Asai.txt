{{nihongo|'''Makate Asai'''|朝井 まかて|Asai Makate}} is a [[List of Japanese women writers|Japanese writer]] of [[historical fiction]]. She has won the [[Naoki Prize]] and the Oda Sakunosuke Prize, and two of her novels have been adapted for television by [[NHK]].

== Early life and education ==
Asai was born in 1959 in [[Habikino, Osaka]], Japan. After graduating from [[Konan Women's University]] she took a job writing copy for advertising.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.zakzak.co.jp/entertainment/ent-news/news/20140202/enn1402020741003-n1.htm|language=japanese|title=直木賞受賞の朝井まかてさん独占インタビュー|work=Zakzak|date=February 2, 2014|access-date=August 16, 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180818084006/https://www.zakzak.co.jp/entertainment/ent-news/news/20140202/enn1402020741003-n1.htm|archive-date=August 18, 2018|url-status=live|df=mdy-all}}</ref> 

== Career ==
Asai made her literary debut in 2008 with ''{{nihongo||実さえ花さえ|Mi sae hana sae}}'', which won the ''Shōsetsu Gendai'' Novel Newcomer Encouragement Prize from [[Kodansha]].<ref name=renzaburo>{{cite web|url=http://renzaburo.jp/interview/007/index.html|language=japanese|title=作家　朝井まかてさん|work=Renzaburo|access-date=August 16, 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180818084218/http://renzaburo.jp/interview/007/index.html|archive-date=August 18, 2018|url-status=live|df=mdy-all}}</ref> She chose the [[pen name]] "Makate" to honor her Okinawan grandmother.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.sankei.com/life/news/140116/lif1401160003-n1.html|language=japanese|title=朝井まかてさん「まさかまさかまさか」|work=Sankei News|date=January 16, 2014|access-date=August 16, 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180818084056/https://www.sankei.com/life/news/140116/lif1401160003-n1.html|archive-date=August 18, 2018|url-status=live|df=mdy-all}}</ref> More novels followed, including the 2010 novel ''{{nihongo||ちゃんちゃら|Chanchara}}'' and the 2012 novel ''{{nihongo||ぬけまいる|Nukemairu}}'', which [[NHK]] later adapted into a television series starring [[Rena Tanaka]], [[Rie Tomosaka]], and [[Eriko Sato]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.oricon.co.jp/news/2116731/full/|language=japanese|title=田中麗奈・ともさかりえ・佐藤江梨子、江戸から伊勢へ女子旅時代劇で共演|work=Oricon News|date=August 1, 2018|access-date=August 16, 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180818083807/https://www.oricon.co.jp/news/2116731/full/|archive-date=August 18, 2018|url-status=live|df=mdy-all}}</ref>

In 2014 Asai won both the [[Naoki Prize]] and the Oda Sakunosuke Prize, but for different books. Her 2013 novel ''{{nihongo3|Love Song|恋歌|Renka}}'', a story based on the life of the poet [[Nakajima Utako]], won the 150th [[Naoki Prize]], which she shared with [[Kaoruko Himeno]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://ryukyushimpo.jp/news/prentry-218906.html|language=japanese|title=「４分の１は沖縄の血」　直木賞作家・朝井まかてさん|work=[[Ryūkyū Shimpō]]|date=February 5, 2014|access-date=August 17, 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180818085501/https://ryukyushimpo.jp/news/prentry-218906.html|archive-date=August 18, 2018|url-status=live|df=mdy-all}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.japantimes.co.jp/opinion/2014/01/25/editorials/literature-prizes-elevate-women/|title=Literature prizes elevate women|work=[[The Japan Times]]|date=January 25, 2014|access-date=August 16, 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180714053807/https://www.japantimes.co.jp/opinion/2014/01/25/editorials/literature-prizes-elevate-women/|archive-date=July 14, 2018|url-status=live|df=mdy-all}}</ref> Her book ''{{nihongo||阿蘭陀西鶴|Oranda Saikaku}}'', a story based on the life of the poet [[Ihara Saikaku]], won the 31st Oda Sakunosuke Prize.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.sankei.com/life/news/141005/lif1410050010-n1.html|language=japanese|title=『阿蘭陀西鶴』朝井まかて著|work=Sankei News|last=大矢|first=博子|date=October 5, 2014|access-date=August 16, 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180818084119/https://www.sankei.com/life/news/141005/lif1410050010-n1.html|archive-date=August 18, 2018|url-status=live|df=mdy-all}}</ref>

Her novel ''{{nihongo||眩|Kurara}}'', about the relationship between the painter [[Katsushika Ōi]] and her father, the painter [[Hokusai]], was published in 2016.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.yomiuri.co.jp/life/book/news/20160523-OYT8T50136.html|language=japanese|title=北斎の娘描いた『眩』刊行…朝井まかてさん|work=[[Yomiuri Shimbun]]|last=西田|first=朋子|date=June 2, 2016|access-date=August 16, 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180818120646/https://www.yomiuri.co.jp/life/book/news/20160523-OYT8T50136.html|archive-date=August 18, 2018|url-status=live|df=mdy-all}}</ref> ''Kurara'' won the 22nd [[Gishū Nakayama]] Literature Prize, and was adapted into the 2017 [[NHK]] television movie ''{{nihongo|Kurara: The Dazzling Life of Hokusai's Daughter|眩 ～北斎の娘～|Kurara ~Hokusai no Musume~}}'' starring [[Aoi Miyazaki]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://mainichi.jp/articles/20161115/k00/00m/040/030000c|language=japanese|title=中山義秀文学賞 朝井まかてさんの「眩」に|work=[[Mainichi Shimbun]]|date=November 14, 2016|access-date=August 16, 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180818083904/https://mainichi.jp/articles/20161115/k00/00m/040/030000c|archive-date=August 18, 2018|url-status=live|df=mdy-all}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=https://mainichi.jp/articles/20170914/dde/018/200/021000c|language=japanese|title=ドラマ「眩～北斎の娘～」制作統括・佐野元彦さん　温めた思い結実|work=[[Mainichi Shimbun]]|date=September 14, 2017|access-date=August 16, 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180818083845/https://mainichi.jp/articles/20170914/dde/018/200/021000c|archive-date=August 18, 2018|url-status=live|df=mdy-all}}</ref>
