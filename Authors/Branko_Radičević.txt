'''Aleksije "Branko" Radičević''' ({{lang-sr-cyr|Алексије Бранко Радичевић}}, {{IPA-sh|brǎːŋko radǐːtʃeʋitɕ|sr}}; 28 March 1824 – 1 July 1853) was a Serbian [[poet]] who wrote in the period of [[Romanticism]].

== Biography==
Branko Radičević was born in [[Slavonski Brod]] on 15 March 1824. Aleksije was his baptismal name before he changed it to Branko, a more common Serbian name.<ref>{{Cite book|last=Stefanović|first=Dragutin A. |others=Stanisavljević, Vukašin |title=Pregled jugoslovenske književnosti |year=1962|publisher= Zavod za izdavanje udžbenika |location = Beograd}}</ref> He finished high school in [[Sremski Karlovci]], the setting of his best poems.<ref>[[Večernje novosti]]: [http://novosti.rs/vesti/kultura.71.html:324441-Sve-adrese-Branka-Radicevica ''Sve adrese Branka Radičevića'' (All Addresses of Branko Radičević)], 26 Mar 2011 {{in lang|sr}}</ref> He then studied in [[Vienna]].<ref name="ks">{{Cite web|last=Mitić|first=Marija Stojičić|date=2020-04-01|title=Branko Radičević – prvi srpski romantičarski pesnik|url=https://kultivisise.rs/branko-radicevic-prvi-srpski-romanticarski-pesnik/|access-date=2021-01-29|website=Kultiviši se|language=sr-RS}}</ref> In 1847 Radičević's first book of poetry appeared, launching a new era in Serbian poetry.<ref name="pesnik">{{Cite book|last=Grujičić|first=Nenad|title=Branko - pesnik mladosti|publisher=Književna zajednica Novog Sada|year=1984|isbn=|location=Novi Sad|pages=}}</ref> He went to Serbia but soon returned to Vienna to study medicine, where he was surrounded by Serb intellectuals, either living in the city or passing through, including his lifelong friend Bogoboj Atanacković, Vuk Karadžić, [[Đuro Daničić]], [[Milica Stojadinović-Srpkinja]] and [[Petar II Petrović-Njegoš]].<ref name="pesnik"/> Radičević's second collection of poetry is considered weaker than his first.<ref name="rastko">{{Cite web|url=https://www.rastko.rs/knjizevnost/jderetic_knjiz/jderetic-knjiz_07.html|title=Jovan Deretic: Kratka istorija srpske knjizevnosti|website=www.rastko.rs|access-date=2020-01-05}}</ref> Near his death he wrote a notable poem titled ''Kad mlidijah umreti'' (As I Thought of Dying).<ref name="ks"/>

He was one of the first authors to write poetry in the simple language of the common folk.<ref>{{Cite journal|last=Bogert|first=Ralph|date=1996|title=The burden of Vuk's philological reform on the lyrics of Branko Radičević|url=http://scindeks.ceon.rs/article.aspx?artid=0354-47029603157B|journal=Facta universitatis - series: Linguistics and Literature|volume=1|issue=3|pages=157–168|issn=0354-4702}}</ref> He attempted to recreate the rhythm of the folk song, thus supporting the belief of Vuk Karadžić that even poetry can be written in the newly reformed Serbian language.<ref name="ks"/> Radičević proved to be very important to Vuk Karadžić's victory because he gathered his generation of young writers and poets around the cause of language reform.<ref name="ks"/>

Skerlić writes in his ''History of New Serbian Literature'' that Branko Radičević had a significant influence on [[Jovan Jovanović Zmaj]], Bogoboj Atanacković, Kosta Ruvarac, Jovan Grčić Milenko, and other Serbian poets and writers of the period.<ref>http://biblioteka-np.org.rs/wp-content/uploads/2020/05/Istorija_nove_srpske_knjizevnosti.pdf</ref>

Radičević died of tuberculosis in Vienna 1853 and in 1883 his remains were buried in [[Stražilovo]].<ref name="pesnik"/>

==Poetry==
[[File:House of Branko Radičević in Sremski Karlovci.jpg|thumb|House of Branko Radičević in [[Sremski Karlovci]]]]
''Đački rastanak'' (The Parting of Schoolfriends) is the most popular of his works. In it, Radičević portrayed the life of college students. For a time he gives himself up to the fleeting impressions of the moment. 

In ''Put'' (A Journey), a magnanimous allegory, Radičević shows a skill in satirical nomenclature by stigmatizing [[Vuk Stefanović Karadžić]]'s adversaries who disapproved the reforms of language and orthography.<ref>{{Cite journal|last=Bogert|first=Ralph|date=1996|title=The burden of Vuk's philological reform on the lyrics of Branko Radičević|url=http://scindeks.ceon.rs/article.aspx?artid=0354-47029603157B|journal=Facta universitatis - series: Linguistics and Literature|volume=1|issue=3|pages=157–168|issn=0354-4702}}</ref>

With ''Bezimena'' he changed his style, from the influences by Byron and Romanticism, towards [[Pushkin]]'s ''[[Eugene Onegin]]'' and [[Vissarion Belinsky]]'s essay of 1843 about Pushkin and [[Literary realism|Realism]].<ref name="rastko"/>

His work gave an important contribution to Vuk's victory in the cultural battle for language reform.<ref name="ks"/>

==Legacy==
[[File:Monument of Branko Radičević 01.jpg|thumb|right|Monument of Branko Radičević in Sremski Karlovci]]
[[File:Glasses with potraits of Serbian poets, Sarajevo, Despić House.jpg|thumb|Drinking glass with portrait of Radičević, [[Despić House]]]]
Radičević left some unfinished work. He left at least one text meriting close attention in any inquiry into Slavic Romantic irony, and the ambitious unfinished poem of 1477 lines, composed in 1849 which featured two titles: ''Ludi Branko'' (Branko the Fool) and ''Bezimena'' (Unnamed). Seventy-four years later, literary critics [[Pavle Popović]] and his brother [[Bogdan Popović]] found that in this poetic fragment "there is no poetry whatsoever" and that it "merits no compliment of any kind."<ref>{{Cite journal|last=Petrović|first=Predrag Ž.|date=2019|title=Pustite me kako ja hoću: o "trećem dobu" u antologiji Bogdana Popovića|url=|journal=Književnik i jezik|volume=LXVI/3–4|pages=271|via=}}</ref> 

He is included in [[The 100 most prominent Serbs]]. He influenced future generations of Serbian poets, including [[Đura Jakšić]], [[Jovan Milenko Grčić]] and [[Miloš Crnjanski]].

His statue made by [[Jovan Soldatović]] and a plaque on his birth house were removed during the 90s from [[Slavonski Brod]]. There are ongoing talks to rebuild the statue.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Hrvati neće Branka Radičevića|url=https://www.novosti.rs/vesti/kultura.71.html:716792-Hrvati-nece-Brankovo-kolo|access-date=2021-01-29|website=NOVOSTI|language=sr}}</ref>

A number of his poems were turned into music.<ref name="ks"/>

Branko's Award<ref>{{Cite web|title=BIBLIOTEKA MATICE SRPSKE|url=http://www.bms.ns.ac.rs/bmslat109.htm|access-date=2021-01-29|website=www.bms.ns.ac.rs}}</ref> was founded in 1954 in his honour. It is awarded for the best book of poetry in Serbian, published by authors under the age of 30.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2018-03-20|title=БРАНКОВА награда|url=http://www.nbpo.rs/2018/03/20/brankova-nagrada/|access-date=2021-01-29|website=Народна библиотека "Илија М. Петровић" Пожаревац|language=sr-RS}}</ref>

Squares in [[Zemun]] and [[Sremski Karlovci]] were named after him.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Trg Branka Radičevića u Sremskim Karlovcima: Pokrajinski zavod za zaštitu spomenika kulture|url=http://www.pzzzsk.rs/sremski-karlovci/trg-branka-radicevica.html|access-date=2021-01-29|website=www.pzzzsk.rs}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=Trg Branka Radičevića Zemun, ulica i objekti na mapi|url=https://www.planplus.rs/zemun/ulica/trg-branka-radicevica/24443|access-date=2021-01-29|website=PlanPlus.rs|language=sr-Latn}}</ref>

Schools in [[Batajnica]],<ref>{{Cite web|title=ОШ "Бранко Радичевић" у Батајници|url=http://osbrankoradicevic.edu.rs/|access-date=2021-01-29|website=ОШ „Бранко Радичевић“ у Батајници|language=sr-RS}}</ref> [[Velika Moštanica]],<ref>{{Cite web|title=Почетна - ОШ "Бранко Радичевић"|url=http://www.osbr.edu.rs/|access-date=2021-01-29|website=www.osbr.edu.rs}}</ref> [[Novi Beograd]],<ref>{{Cite web|title=OШ "БРАНКО РАДИЧЕВИЋ" – Јурија Гагарина 195, Нови Београд|url=http://brankoradicevic-nbgd.edu.rs/|access-date=2021-01-29|language=sr-RS}}</ref> [[Novi Sad]],<ref>{{Cite web|title=Бранкови основци {{!}} ОШ "Бранко Радичевић" Нови Сад|url=http://brankoviosnovci.edu.rs/|access-date=2021-01-29|language=en-US}}</ref> [[Banja Luka]],<ref>{{Cite web|title=JU Osnovna škola "Branko Radičević" Banjaluka|url=https://www.brankoradicevic.com/|access-date=2021-01-29|website=Основна школа "Бранко Радичевић" Бањалука|language=en}}</ref> [[Negotin]],<ref>{{Cite web|title=ОШ "БРАНКО РАДИЧЕВИЋ" - Неготин|url=http://www.osbradicevicnegotin.nasaskola.rs/|access-date=2021-01-29|website=www.osbradicevicnegotin.nasaskola.rs}}</ref> [[Smederevo]] and other cities are named after him.
