'''Will Shetterly''' (born 1955) is an American writer of fantasy and science fiction best known for his novel ''[[Dogland (novel)|Dogland]]'' (1997).<ref>{{Cite news |last=Ohlheiser |first=Abby |date=October 7, 2015 |title=Why 'social justice warrior,' a Gamergate insult, is now a dictionary entry |language=en-US |work=Washington Post |url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/news/the-intersect/wp/2015/10/07/why-social-justice-warrior-a-gamergate-insult-is-now-a-dictionary-entry/ |access-date=March 30, 2018 |issn=0190-8286}}</ref> The novel is inspired by his childhood at the [[tourist attraction]] Dog Land owned by his parents.<ref name="blogbio">{{Cite web |last=Shetterly |first=Will |title=A short autobiography by Will Shetterly |url=http://shetterly.blogspot.com/p/a-short-autobiography-by-will-shetterly.html |access-date=June 14, 2014}}</ref> He won the [[Minnesota Book Award]] for Fantasy & Science Fiction for his novel ''Elsewhere'' (1991),<ref name="MBA_1992">{{Cite news |date=1992 |title=Minnesota Book Awards 1992 Finalists and Winners |publisher=The Friends of the Saint Paul Library |url=http://www.thefriends.org/mba_winners_1992.html |access-date=September 19, 2008}}</ref> and was a finalist with ''Nevernever'' (1993);<ref name="MBA_1994">{{Cite news |date=1994 |title=Minnesota Book Awards 1994 Finalists and Winners |publisher=The Friends of the Saint Paul Library |url=http://www.thefriends.org/mba_winners_1994.html |access-date=September 19, 2008}}</ref> both books are set in [[Terri Windling]]'s [[The Borderland Series]] [[shared universe]]. He has also written short stories for various Borderland [[anthology|anthologies]].

==Biography==
Shetterly is married to the author [[Emma Bull]]. The couple lives in Minneapolis, Minnesota, and were both members of the writing group [[The Scribblies]], which also included, [[Pamela Dean]], [[Kara Dalkey]], Nate Bucklin, [[Patricia Wrede]], and [[Steven Brust]]. Shetterly and Bull created and edited the [[Liavek]] [[shared universe]] anthologies.

Shetterly created the comic book character [[Captain Confederacy]], played a small role in the film ''Toxic Zombies'', and ran for [[Minnesota gubernatorial election, 1994|governor of Minnesota]] in 1994 on the [[Grassroots Party]] ticket.<ref name="blogbio" />

In 2009, he donated his archive to the department of Rare Books and Special Collections at [[Northern Illinois University]].<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.ulib.niu.edu/rarebooks/sciencefiction.cfm |title=Science Fiction and Fantasy Writers of America (SFWA) Collection |archive-url=https://archive.today/20110725202207/http://www.ulib.niu.edu/rarebooks/sciencefiction.cfm |archive-date=July 25, 2011}}, Northern Illinois University</ref>
