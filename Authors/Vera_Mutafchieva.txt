'''Vera Mutafchieva''' ({{lang-bg|Вера Мутафчиева}}; March 28, 1929 &ndash; June 9, 2009) was a [[Bulgaria]]n writer and historian.<ref name=detrez>{{cite book |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=hywaBgAAQBAJ&pg=PA339 |title=Historical Dictionary of Bulgaria |page=339 |last=Detrez |first=Raymond |year=2014 |isbn=978-1442241800}}</ref>

==Biography==
Vera Mutafchieva was born in [[Sofia]], March 28, 1929. Her parents were the historian prof. [[Petar Mutafchiev]] and Nadia Triphonova

Mutafchieva was educated at [[Sofia University]]. She obtained her PhD (1958) and DSci (1978) degrees at the [[Bulgarian Academy of Sciences]] (BAS) in Sofia.

She was a senior researcher at various institutes of the BAS (Institute of History; Institute of Balkan Studies; Institute of Demographic Studies; Institute of Literature), and was elected vice-president of BAS (1993-1996).<ref>{{cite book |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=6J8xDWDqOkEC&pg=PA395 |title=The International Who's Who of Women 2002 |page=395 |last=Sleeman |first=Elizabeth |year=2001 |isbn=1857431227}}</ref> In 2004 she was elected a member of the Bulgarian Academy of Sciences. Her research focused on the [[Ottoman Empire|Ottoman]] period on which she published dozens of studies in Bulgarian and European journals. Some of her monographies were published in the USA, Turkey and Greece.<ref>{{cite book |title= Agrarian Relations in the Ottoman Empire in the 15th and 16th Centuries |last=Mutafchieva |first=Vera P. |year=1988 |isbn= 0880331488}}
</ref><ref>{{cite book |url= http://helios-eie.ekt.gr/EIE/handle/10442/4291 |title= Αγροτικές σχέσεις στην Οθωμανική αυτοκρατορία (15ος - 16ος αι.) (Agrotikes Scheseis stin Othomaniki Avtokratoria (15os – 16os ai)) |last=Moutaftchieva |first=Vera |year=1990 |isbn= 960-7043-01-4}}</ref><ref>{{cite book |title= L'anarchie dans les Balkans a la fin du XVIIIe siècle |last= Moutaftchieva |first=Vera |year=2005 |isbn= 975-428-295-1 }}</ref>

Mutafchieva is the author of historical novels which were translated into 11 languages.<ref name="Official site of Vera Mutafchieva">{{cite news |url=http://veramutafchieva.net/ |title= Official site of Vera Mutafchieva }}</ref> She was also script writer of the 1981 film ''[[Aszparuh|Khan Asparuh]]''<ref name=novinite/> (4th position in the Most Viewed Movies in Bulgaria of All Times Chart and Official submission of Bulgaria for the 'Best Foreign Language Film' category of the 55th Academy Awards in 1983;<ref name="IMDB name">{{cite news |url=https://www.imdb.com/title/tt0275942/trivia?ref_=tt_trv_trv
|title=Aszparuh (1981) Trivia}}</ref> international title ''681 AD: The Glory of Khan''<ref>{{cite news |url= https://www.imdb.com/title/tt0444982/?ref_=nm_knf_t2|title=681 AD: The Glory of Khan}}</ref> ) and of 2 other films on contemporary subjects,<ref>{{cite news |url= https://www.imdb.com/name/nm1052952/?ref_=tt_ov_wr
|title= Vera Mutafchieva (1929–2009), Writer, at IMDb}}</ref> as well as of the drama on the second Bulgarian Kingdom "The Road".

From 1997 to 1998, Mutafchieva was head of the State Agency for Bulgarians Abroad. In 2008, it was revealed that she had collaborated with the secret police in communist Bulgaria.<ref name=novinite/> However, she is also known for her defence of women's rights in Bulgaria.<ref name=detrez/>

Mutafchieva died at the [[Lozenetz Hospital]] in Sofia at the age of 80.<ref name=novinite>{{cite news |url=http://www.novinite.com/articles/104529/Renown+Bulgarian+Historian,+Writer+Vera+Mutafchieva+Dies+at+80 |title=Renown Bulgarian Historian, Writer Vera Mutafchieva Dies at 80 |work=novinite.com |date=June 9, 2009}}</ref> Her ashes were scattered in the [[Aegean sea]] near the Cape [[Sounion]].

==Awards==
Mutafchieva was awarded with numerous national prizes and with the International [[Herder Prize]] (1980).
