'''Julie Victoria Jones''' (born 1963) is a British author of [[fantasy]].

She was born 1963<ref>{{cite web|url=http://jvj.com/nsjan-feb99.html|title=J.V. Jones...News!|website=Jvj.com|accessdate=23 September 2017}}</ref> in [[Liverpool]], England, as the daughter of a pub owner. In her youth she worked as a bar tender and for a record label in Liverpool. She lives in [[San Diego]], California, where she initially ran an export business and later occupied a position as marketing director.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://archive.today/20130125025227/http://www.hachettebookgroup.com/authors_J-V-Jones-(1013110).htm|title = J. V. Jones - Hachette Book Group}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.iblist.com/author325.htm|title=Internet Book List :: Author Information: J. V. Jones|first=IBList Group -|last=iblistgroup@iblist.com|website=Iblist.com|access-date=23 September 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170924092909/http://www.iblist.com/author325.htm|archive-date=2017-09-24|url-status=dead}}</ref>  The current publishers of J. V. Jones are [[Tor books]] in the US and [[Orbit Books]] in the UK.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://us.macmillan.com/author/jvjones|title=J. V. Jones - Authors - Macmillan|website=Us.macmillan.com|accessdate=23 September 2017}}</ref>

The manuscript that was to become the first novel in the ''Book of Words'' [[trilogy]] was submitted as ''Immortal Longings'' to [[Warner Books]] in 1993. In a joint essay, ''The Road to a First Novel'', J. V. Jones and Betsy Mitchell describe the editing process that followed the arrival of the manuscript in the publisher's [[slush pile]].<ref>J. V. Jones and Betsy Mitchell, {{isfdb title|id=124050|title=The road to a first novel}}</ref> The novel was published in 1995 as ''[[The Baker's Boy]]''. The ''Book of Words'' trilogy was completed in 1997 and followed by a standalone novel, ''[[The Barbed Coil]]'', in the same year. Her ''Sword of Shadows'' series was launched in 1999 and is to contain five books.
