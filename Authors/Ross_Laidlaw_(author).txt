'''Ross Laidlaw''' is a [[Scottish people|Scottish]] writer of [[Historical fiction|historical]], [[Thriller (genre)|thriller]] and [[spy fiction]].

==Biography==
Laidlaw was born in [[Aberdeen]] and now lives in [[East Lothian]]. He attended the [[University of Cambridge]] and has spent time working and traveling in [[southern Africa]].<ref name="FF RL B">{{cite web | url = http://www.fantasticfiction.co.uk/l/ross-laidlaw/ | title = Ross Laidlaw | publisher = FantasticFiction | date = | accessdate = 2009-09-21}}</ref> In 1979, while working as a geography and history teacher at [[Belhaven Hill School]] near [[Dunbar]], Laidlaw's first book was released, ''The Lion is Rampant'', receiving significant praise.<ref name="BL RL A2">{{cite web | url = http://www.birlinn.co.uk/book/details/Attila-9781846970122/ | title = Attila | publisher = [[Birlinn Limited]] | date = | accessdate = 2009-09-30}}</ref> He has since released five more books with the most recent being ''Justinian: The Sleepless One'' which was released in 2010.

==Bibliography==
*'''''The Lion is Rampant''''' (1979) ({{ISBN|9780904002270}}) - originally published by Molendinar Press, it is a political thriller set in Scotland during the 1980s.<ref name="HB RL TLIR">{{cite web | url = http://www.hessaybooks.co.uk/si/632.html | title = The Lion is Rampant A Political Thriller | publisher = Hessay Books | date = | accessdate = 2009-09-30}}</ref>
*'''''The Linton Porcupine''''' (1984) ({{ISBN|9780862410636}}) - originally published by Littlehampton Book Services, set in 16th century Scotland it is written as the journal of Nicholas Wainwright who has been sent to find a weapon that is being created by the Scots.<ref name="MBS RL TLP">{{cite web | url = http://www.medievalbookshop.co.uk/abitems/ABK0145.shtml | title = The Linton Porcupine: The Journal of One Nicholas Wainwright | publisher = Medieval Bookshop | date = | accessdate = 2009-09-30}}</ref>
*'''''Aphra Behn: Dispatch'd from Athole''''' (1992) ({{ISBN|9781872557175}}) - originally published by Balnain Books, set in 17th century [[Great Britain|Britain]], the story tells of [[Aphra Behn]]'s journal which details her mission to Scotland as a spy.<ref name="A RL AB">{{cite web | url = https://www.amazon.co.uk/dp/1872557171 | title = Aphra Behn: Dispatch'd from Athole | publisher = Amazon.co.uk | date = | accessdate = 2009-09-30}}</ref>
*'''''Attila: The Scourge of God''''' (2004) ({{ISBN|9781904598084}}) - originally published by [[Polygon Books]], set in fifth century Europe, the story centers around [[Attila the Hun]] and [[Flavius Aetius|Aetius]] who are in battle with each other for the fate of their empires.<ref name="PB B A">{{cite web | url = http://polygon.birlinn.co.uk/author/titles/Ross-Laidlaw-893/ | title = Books | publisher = [[Polygon Books]] | accessdate = 2009-09-21 | url-status = dead | archiveurl = https://archive.is/20081013005347/http://polygon.birlinn.co.uk/author/titles/Ross-Laidlaw-893/ | archivedate = 13 October 2008 | df = dmy-all }}</ref>
*'''''[[Theoderic (novel)|Theoderic]]''''' (2008) ({{ISBN|9781846970825}}) - originally published by Polygon Books, set in fifth century Europe during a time when the [[Western Roman Empire]] has been left in ruins, the story centers around [[Theodoric the Great|Theodoric the Goth]] who rises to a status equal of [[Roman Emperor]]s.<ref name="FF RL T">{{cite web | url = http://www.fantasticfiction.co.uk/l/ross-laidlaw/theoderic.htm | title = Theodoric by Ross Laidlaw | publisher = FantasticFiction | date = | accessdate = 2009-09-21}}</ref>
*'''''Justinian''''' (2010) ({{ISBN|9781846971587}}) - originally published by Polygon Books, it follows the life of [[Justinian I]], an emperor of the [[Byzantine Empire]].<ref name="JUSTINIAN">{{cite web | url = http://www.birlinn.co.uk/book/details/Justinian-9781846971587/ | title = ''Justinian'' | publisher = [[Birlinn Limited]] | date = | accessdate = 2010-06-08 }}</ref>
