'''Joe DiPietro''' (born 1961<ref name="thevillager">{{cite journal|url=https://www.thevillager.com/villager_218/elevenyearsrunning.html|date=July 4–10, 1998|title=Eleven years running, just perfect, why change?|first=Jerry|last=Tallmer|journal=[[The Villager (Manhattan)|The Villager]]|access-date=February 16, 2008}}</ref>) is an American [[playwright]],  [[lyricist]] and [[author]].<ref>[https://goliath.ecnext.com/coms2/gi_0199-1245493/Giving-Allegro-a-heart-transplant.html "Giving ''Allegro'' a heart transplant"], ''The Washington Times''</ref> He is best known for the Tony Award-winning musical ''[[Memphis (musical)|Memphis]]'', for which he won the Tony Awards for [[Tony Award for Best Book of a Musical|Best Book of a Musical]] and [[Tony Award for Best Original Score|Best Original Score]] as well as for writing the book and lyrics for the long-running [[off-Broadway]] show ''[[I Love You, You're Perfect, Now Change]]''.<ref name = "FFFF" />

==Biography==
Born in [[Teaneck, New Jersey]],<ref>Ouzounian, Richard. [https://www.thestar.com/entertainment/theatre/article/734739--toxic-avenger-found-his-bard-in-bon-jovi "Toxic Avenger found his bard in Bon Jovi: Band's David Bryan just wants people to sing the songs he writes"], ''[[Toronto Star]]'', December 6, 2009. Accessed December 6, 2009. "He got a crash course in how to do it when he met up with Joe DiPietro, another Jersey boy (this one from Teaneck)."</ref> DiPietro grew up in nearby [[Oradell, New Jersey|Oradell]],<ref>[https://query.nytimes.com/gst/fullpage.html?res=990CE0DB113BF931A25751C0A963958260&sec=&spon=&pagewanted=all Theater; New Musical Headed For American Stage], ''The New York Times''</ref><ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.playbill.com/news/article/110254.html# |title=Playbill News: A Perfect Love |access-date=February 24, 2017 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080706190751/http://www.playbill.com/news/article/110254.html# |archive-date=July 6, 2008 |url-status=dead }}</ref> the son of Lou and Jean DiPietro.  He attended Oradell Public School and [[River Dell Regional High School]],<ref name="FFFF">{{cite journal|url=https://query.nytimes.com/gst/fullpage.html?res=9507EEDF133BF932A25753C1A96E958260|date=October 11, 1998|title=In Person; Family, Faith, Food and Fun|first=Alvin|last=Klein|journal=[[The New York Times]]|access-date=February 16, 2008}}</ref> before graduating [[Phi Beta Kappa Society|Phi Beta Kappa]] from [[Rutgers University]] in 1984 with a bachelor's degree in English.<ref>[http://www.thefreelibrary.com/DOUBLING+DOWN+WITH+JOE+DIPIETRO%3B+2+OF+HIS+SHOWS+OPEN+IN+AREA.(L.A....-a083393166 Doubling Down With Joe DiPietro; 2 of his shows open in area], ''Daily News''</ref>

DiPietro is openly [[gay]].<ref>[http://www.dallasvoice.com/memphis-belle-10109003.html "''Memphis'' belle"]. ''[[Dallas Voice]]'', May 10, 2012.</ref>

==Work==
Following a run of ''[[Love Lemmings]]'' at the Top of the Village Gate in 1991, DiPietro's first produced work was ''[[I Love You, You're Perfect, Now Change]]'', written with composer [[Jimmy Roberts (composer)|Jimmy Roberts]], which ran for twelve years (5,003 performances) off-Broadway at the [[Westside Theatre]].<ref name="playbill">{{cite journal|url=https://www.playbill.com/news/article/119798-I-Love-You-Youre-Perfect-Now-Change-Ends-NYC-Run-After-More-Than-a-Decade-July-27|date=July 27, 2008|title=I Love You, You're Perfect, Now Change Ends NYC Run After More Than a Decade|first=Andrew|last=Gans|journal=[[Playbill]]|access-date=March 8, 2013|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130614041924/http://www.playbill.com/news/article/119798-I-Love-You-Youre-Perfect-Now-Change-Ends-NYC-Run-After-More-Than-a-Decade-July-27#|archive-date=June 14, 2013|url-status=dead}}</ref>  He followed that up with 1998's comedy ''[[Over the River and Through the Woods]]'', which played the John Houseman Theatre for 800 performances over two years.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.lortel.org/LLA_archive/index.cfm?search_by=show&title=Over%20the%20River%20and%20Through%20the%20Woods# |title=Archived copy |access-date=February 24, 2017 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20151119153443/http://www.lortel.org/LLA_archive/index.cfm?search_by=show&title=Over%20the%20River%20and%20Through%20the%20Woods# |archive-date=November 19, 2015 |url-status=dead }}</ref>

DiPietro's work made its [[Broadway theatre|Broadway]] debut in 2005 with the [[Elvis Presley]] jukebox musical ''[[All Shook Up (musical)|All Shook Up]]''.<ref>{{Cite web | url=http://www.ibdb.com/production.php?id=383114 |title = All Shook up – Broadway Musical – Original &#124; IBDB}}</ref>  ''[[The Toxic Avenger (musical)|The Toxic Avenger]]'', written with [[Bon Jovi]]'s [[David Bryan]], debuted off-Broadway on April 6, 2009, and won the Outer Critics Circle Award for Outstanding New Off-Broadway Musical.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.lortel.org/LLA_archive/index.cfm?search_by=show&id=5324# |title=Archived copy |access-date=March 8, 2013 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140513062805/http://www.lortel.org/LLA_ARCHIVE/index.cfm?search_by=show&id=5324# |archive-date=May 13, 2014 |url-status=dead }}</ref>

DiPietro and Bryan's ''[[Memphis (musical)|Memphis]]'' won the 2010 Tony Award for Best Musical.  It ran at the Shubert Theatre for three years (1,165 performances) and continues in touring production all over the country.  DiPietro won Tony Awards for Best Book and Best Score, and the show also received the Tony, Drama Desk, and Outer Critics Circle Awards for Best Musical.<ref>{{Cite web | url=http://www.ibdb.com/production.php?id=484158 |title = Memphis – Broadway Musical – Original &#124; IBDB}}</ref>

''[[Nice Work If You Can Get It (musical)|Nice Work If You Can Get It]]'', a re-imagining of a Gershwin musical, starring [[Matthew Broderick]] and [[Kelli O'Hara]], opened at Broadway's [[Imperial Theatre]] in 2012.  The show, and DiPietro's book, were nominated for Tony Awards in 2012, and DiPietro won the Drama Desk Award for Best Book of a Musical.<ref>{{Cite web | url=http://www.ibdb.com/production.php?id=491374 |title = Nice Work if You Can Get It – Broadway Musical – Original &#124; IBDB}}</ref>

''Clever Little Lies'' ran in late 2013 at [[George Street Playhouse]] and debuted in New York's Westside Theatre in 2015 with [[Marlo Thomas]] and [[Greg Mullavey]].<ref>[http://observer.com/2015/10/marlo-thomas-stars-in-off-broadway-marriage-comedy-clever-little-lies/ "Marlo Thomas Stars in Off-Broadway Marriage Comedy ''Clever Little Lies''] by Harry Haun, ''[[The New York Observer]]'', October 1, 2015</ref>

The musical ''Chasing the Song,'' which reunited DiPietro with Bryan, was workshopped at [[La Jolla Playhouse]] in the summer of 2014. 2014 also saw DiPietro reworking the [[Garson Kanin]] play ''Peccadillo'' into ''Living on Love,'' which starred the opera star [[Renée Fleming]] and was a highlight of the 2014 [[Williamstown Theatre Festival]].<ref>{{Cite news | url=https://www.nytimes.com/2014/07/21/theater/in-living-on-love-renee-fleming-moves-beyond-opera.html?_r=0 |title = Out of Tune, and Seeking New Muses|newspaper = The New York Times|date = July 20, 2014|last1 = Soloski|first1 = Alexis}}</ref>

Other recent plays include ''Creating Claire'', which debuted at George Street Playhouse in 2010,<ref name="newyorktimes">{{cite journal|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2010/05/30/nyregion/30theatnj.html?_r=0|date=May 28, 2010|title=Old Battle, New Play: Religion vs. Science|first=Anita|last=Gates|journal=[[New York Times]]|access-date=February 16, 2008}}</ref> and ''The Last Romance'', which played the Old Globe in 2010<ref name="TheaterMania">{{cite journal|url=http://www.theatermania.com/san-diego-theater/news/08-2010/review-roundup-joe-dipietros-the-last-romance-with_29615.html|date=August 11, 2010|title=Review Roundup: Joe DiPietro's ''The Last Romance'', With Marion Ross, Paul Michael, Opens at the Old Globe|first=Andy|last=Propst|journal=TheaterMania|access-date=February 16, 2008}}</ref> after debuting at Kansas City's New Theatre in 2008.<ref name="entertainmentspectrum">{{cite journal|url=http://www.entertainmentspectrum.com/index/theatre/794/thenewtheatrerestaurantlastromancereview.html|date=September 23, 2008|title=The New Theatre Restaurant: Last Romance Review|first=Marieann|last=Koehler|journal=Entertainment Spectrum|access-date=February 16, 2008}}</ref> and ''Ernest Shackleton Loves Me'' (with the score written by [[Val Vigoda]] and Brendan Milburn of ''[[GrooveLily]]'' fame.) ''[http://www.playbill.com/article/off-broadways-ernest-shackleton-loves-me-broadcast-on-thirteen-november-26 Shackleton]'' was shown as part of the PBS series ''Broadway on HD,'' on November 26, 2018.
