'''Roger Williams''' (born 1974) is a [[Wales|Welsh]] [[playwright]]<ref name=filmdrama>{{cite book|title=Film, drama and the break-up of Britain|year=2007|publisher=Intellect Books|isbn=978-1-84150-150-5|pages=171|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=Op6ouZ2AMv8C&q=%22roger+williams%22+gulp+gay&pg=PA171|author=Steven Blandford|access-date=24 December 2010|quote=Typical of this was Gulp by a young Cardiff writer, Roger Williams: It was generally considered to be Cardiff's first professionally produced young, out gay play, referred to by the press as a 'cultural milestone'. ...}}</ref> and [[screenwriter]] working in both [[English language|English]] and [[Welsh language|Welsh]].  His work often examines aspects of modern Welsh life, such as the place of minority languages, the plight of declining industrial communities and the Cardiff gay scene.<ref>{{Cite web | url=http://www.parthianbooks.co.uk/RogerWilliams.htm | title=Parthian Books}}</ref>

He was born at [[Newport, Wales|Newport]], [[Wales]], and brought up in [[Carmarthen]].  He graduated in 1995 from the [[University of Warwick]] with a degree in English and American Literature.

His theatre work includes:
* ''Surfing, Carmarthen Bay'' (1995)
* ''Love in Aberdare'' (1997)
* ''Gulp'' (1997)<ref name=filmdrama/>
* ''Calon Lân'' (1997)
* ''Saturday Night Forever'' (1998)
* ''Killing Kangaroos'' (1999)
* ''Pop'' (2000)
* ''Y Byd (A'i Brawd)'' (2004)
* ''Me, a Giant'' (2005)
* ''Mother Tongue'' (2005)
* "Kapow!" (2006)
* "Tir Sir Gâr" (2013)

In 2002, his work ''[[Tales from the Pleasure Beach]]'', screened on [[BBC Two]], was nominated for a [[BAFTA Award]] in the [[British Academy Television Award for Best Drama Series|Best Drama Series]] category.  He has also written episodes of "Hollyoaks" (Channel 4), "The Story of Tracy Beaker" (BBC), "The Bench" (BBC Wales), "Citizens!" (BBC Wales) and many episodes of the daily Welsh-language soap opera ''[[Pobol y Cwm]]'' which is broadcast on [[S4C]].  In 2006, he became the lead writer of S4C's popular new drama series "Caerdydd",<ref name=caerdydd>{{citation |title=S4C 'has lack of hunger for great programming' |date=7 December 2010 |first=Martin |last=Shipton |url=http://www.walesonline.co.uk/news/welsh-politics/welsh-politics-news/2010/12/07/s4c-has-lack-of-hunger-for-great-programming-91466-27779108/ |periodical=Wales Online |access-date=24 December 2010}}</ref> for which he won a Bafta in 2011.<ref name=caerdydd_bafta_2011>{{citation |title=British Academy Cymru Awards - Winners in 2011 |url=http://www.bafta.org/wales/awards/2011-bafta-cymru-awards,1770,BA.html#jump220|date=29 May 2011}}</ref>

In 2012 he established the production company Joio.  The company's first production was the film "Tir" for S4C.  The film was adapted from Roger Williams's original theatre play "Tir Sir Gâr" for which he won the Best Welsh language Playwright award at the Wales Theatre Critics Awards 2014.<ref>{{Cite web | url=http://www.theatre-wales.co.uk/news/newsdetail.asp?newsID=5080 |title = Theatre Critics of Wales Awards 2014 - News and latest information on Theatre Dance and Performance in Wales - news, reviews, commentary, features and discussion}}</ref>
