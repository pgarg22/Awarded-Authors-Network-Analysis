'''Catherine 'Kate' Charlotte Maberly''' (1805 – 7 February 1875) was an [[United Kingdom of Great Britain and Ireland|Irish]] writer.

==Biography==
Born Catherine Charlotte Prittie in November 1805 in Corville, [[County Tipperary|Tipperary]], Ireland, Maberly was the daughter of Hon. Francis Aldborough Prittie and Elizabeth Ponsonby. Her brother Henry Prittie became the 3rd [[Baron Dunalley]] of Kilboy when their uncle, [[Henry Prittie, 2nd Baron Dunalley|Henry Sadleir Prittie]], died childless.  She married [[William Leader Maberly]] on 11 November 1830.<ref name="Main Page 2009">{{cite web | title=Person Page | website=Main Page | date=2009-10-26 | url=http://www.thepeerage.com/p39194.htm#i391931 | access-date=2019-10-24}}</ref>

Maberly was a novelist who wrote predominately historical fiction though she also wrote some non fiction.<ref name=Cannadine>{{cite book|editor1-last=Cannadine|editor1-first=David |title=Oxford Dictionary of National Biography|date=2019|publisher=Oxford University Press|location=Oxford |chapter=Maberly, William Leader }}</ref> Maberley's  1851 novel ''The Lady and the Priest'' is about the life of King [[Henry II of England]], and his relationships with his mistress [[Rosamund Clifford]], and his antagonist, [[Thomas Becket|Thomas à Becket]].<ref name="Sutherland 2014 p. 396">{{cite book | last=Sutherland | first=J. | title=The Longman Companion to Victorian Fiction | publisher=Taylor & Francis | year=2014 | isbn=978-1-317-86333-5 | url=https://books.google.com/books?id=X2KuBAAAQBAJ&pg=PA396 | access-date=2019-10-24 | page=396}}</ref>

She is buried in [[Cowes]] on the [[Isle of Wight]] with her only son, William Anson Robert Maberly.<ref name="Friends of Northwood Cemetery 2013">{{cite web | title=MABERLY Catherine Charlotte | website=Friends of Northwood Cemetery | date=2013-08-21 | url=http://www.friendsofnorthwoodcemetery.org.uk/burial-record/maberley-catherine-charlotte/ | access-date=2019-10-24}}</ref><ref name="WorldCat.org">{{cite web | title=Maberly Mrs 1805-1875 (Catherine Charlotte) &#91;WorldCat Identities&#93; | website=WorldCat.org | url=http://worldcat.org/identities/lccn-no89013172/ | access-date=2019-10-24}}</ref><ref name="Victoria Research Web 2019">{{cite web | title=At the Circulating Library Author Information: Catherine Charlotte Maberly | website=Victoria Research Web | date=2019-07-09 | url=https://www.victorianresearch.org/atcl/show_author.php?aid=1361 | access-date=2019-10-24}}</ref><ref name="Maberly 1848">{{cite book | last=Maberly | first=C.C. | title=Fashion and its votaries | series=Fashion and its votaries | year=1848 | url=https://archive.org/details/fashionanditsvo00mabegoog | access-date=2019-10-24 | page=[https://archive.org/details/fashionanditsvo00mabegoog/page/n9 3]}}</ref><ref name="Sutherland 2014 p. 396" />
