'''Marya Zaturenska''' (September 12, 1902 &ndash; January 19, 1982) was an [[United States|American]] [[Lyric poetry|lyric poet]], winner of the [[Pulitzer Prize for Poetry]] in 1938.<ref name="nytimes.com">{{cite news| url=https://www.nytimes.com/1982/01/21/obituaries/marya-zaturenska-lyric-poet-received-pulitzer-prize-in-38.html| title=MARYA ZATURENSKA, LYRIC POET RECEIVED PULITZER PRIZE IN '38| date=January 21, 1982| work=The New York Times}}</ref>

==Life==
She was born in [[Kyiv]] and her family emigrated to the United States, when she was eight and lived in New York. Like many immigrants, she worked in a clothing factory during the day, but was able to attend night high school. She was an outstanding student and won a scholarship to [[Valparaiso University]];<ref>http://www.jpress.nli.org.il/Olive/APA/NLI_heb/SharedView.Article.aspx?href=FRW%2F1926%2F02%2F14&id=Ar00300&sk=5658D096</ref><ref>http://www.jpress.nli.org.il/Olive/APA/NLI_heb/SharedView.Article.aspx?href=FRW%2F1926%2F02%2F14&id=Ar01503&sk=2E4805F6</ref> she later transferred to the [[University of Wisconsin–Madison]], receiving a degree in library science.<ref>{{cite book| chapter-url=https://books.google.com/books?id=18J48gmmwrAC&q=Marya+Zaturenska&pg=PA122| chapter=Marya Zaturenska| title=The tenement saga: the Lower East Side and early Jewish American writers| author=Sanford V. Sternlicht| publisher=Terrace Books| year=2004| isbn=978-0-299-20484-6 }}</ref> She met her husband, the prize-winning poet [[Horace Gregory]] there; they married in 1925.<ref name="nytimes.com"/>  Her two children were Patrick and Joanna Gregory. She wrote eight volumes of poetry, including the [[Pulitzer Prize]]-winning ''Cold Morning Sky'', and she edited six anthologies of poetry.

Her work appeared in ''The New York Times'',<ref>{{cite news| url=https://query.nytimes.com/search/query?frow=0&n=10&srcht=s&query=Marya+Zaturenska&srchst=p&submit.x=36&submit.y=7&submit=sub&hdlquery=&bylquery=Marya+Zaturenska&daterange=full&mon1=01&day1=01&year1=1981&mon2=06&day2=19&year2=2009 | work=The New York Times | first=Marya | last=Zaturenska | accessdate=May 12, 2010}}</ref> ''Poetry Magazine'',<ref>http://www.poetryfoundation.org/search.html?q=Marya%20Zaturenska&refinement=poetry_magazine&disp_type=Poetry%20Magazine{{dead link|date=December 2017 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref>
