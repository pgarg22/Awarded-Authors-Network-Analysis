'''Kelly Stuart''' is an American [[playwright]].

==Life==
She lived in [[Los Angeles]].<ref name="thevillager">{{cite web|url=http://www.thevillager.com/villager_48/spidersandbalzac.html|title=Spiders and Balzac|publisher=thevillager.com|accessdate=2016-06-20|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130902014350/http://thevillager.com/villager_48/spidersandbalzac.html|archive-date=2013-09-02|url-status=dead}}</ref>
She has been a New Dramatists writer in residence at The [[Royal National Theatre]]â€™s Studio in London.
She lives in New York, and teaches in the Theatre Department at [[Columbia University]].<ref name="columbia">{{cite web|url=https://directory.columbia.edu/people/uni;jsessionid=548850E280C9573731C6A6EFE56BDC46?code=ks792|title=Columbia University: Directory|publisher=directory.columbia.edu|accessdate=2016-06-20}}</ref><ref name="playscripts">{{cite web|url=http://www.playscripts.com/author.php3?authorid=188|title=Kelly Stuart &#124; Playscripts, Inc.|publisher=playscripts.com|accessdate=2016-06-20}}</ref>

==Works==
* ''Ball and Chain'', produced at the [[Padua Hills Playwrights Festival]] (1990)
* ''The Interpreter of Horror'', produced at the [[Padua Hills Playwrights Festival]] (1991)
* ''Demonology'', produced at the [[Padua Hills Playwrights Festival]] (1995) and at [[Playwrights Horizons]] (1996)
* ''The Square Root of Terrible'', produced at [[The Mark Taper Forum]], Los Angeles (1998) (theater for young audiences)
* ''Furious Blood'', produced at [[Sledgehammer Theatre]], San Diego (2001)
* ''The New New'', [[Guthrie Theater]] (2002) (one-act play)<ref name="playscripts2">{{cite web|url=http://www.playscripts.com/productions.php3?playid=311|title=The New New by Kelly Stuart &#124; Playscripts Inc.|publisher=playscripts.com|accessdate=2016-06-20}}</ref>
* ''Mayhem'', produced at [[The Evidence Room]], Los Angeles (2003)
* ''The Life of Spiders'', produced by Holderness Theatre Company, New York City (2004)
* ''Homewrecker'', produced at [[The Evidence Room]], Los Angeles (2004)
* ''Bill of (W)Rights'', [[Mixed Blood Theatre]], Minneapolis (2004) (one-act as part of evening of short plays)
* ''Shadow Language'', produced at [[Theatre503]], London (2008)
* ''A Shoe Is Not a Question'', presented by the A.S.K. Theater Projects, Los Angeles
