'''Alix E. Harrow''' (born November 9, 1989) is a [[Hugo Award]]-winning American science fiction and fantasy writer. Her short fiction has been nominated for the [[Nebula Award]], [[World Fantasy Award]], and [[Locus Award]], and in 2019 she won a [[Hugo Award for Best Short Story|Hugo Award]] for her story "A Witch's Guide to Escape: A Practical Compendium of Portal Fantasies". She has published under the name Alix Heintzman.

==Life and career==

Alix E. Harrow was born in 1989 in the United States and grew up in [[Kentucky]]. She enrolled at [[Berea College]] at age sixteen, where she completed a bachelor's degree in history in three years.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2020-10-02|title=Alix Harrow ’09 Makes Literary History|url=https://www.berea.edu/give/2020/10/02/alix-harrow/|access-date=2021-02-01|website=Give to Berea|language=en-US}}</ref> She then went on to earn a master's degree in history from the [[University of Vermont]]. Harrow lives in Virginia<ref>{{cite web| website=Tor.com |title=Return to the Multiverse of Fairy Tales: Revealing a Mirror Mended by Alix E. Harrow | url= https://www.tor.com/2021/09/08/cover-reveals-a-mirror-mended-by-alix-e-harrow/#more-660018 | date=September 8, 2021}}</ref> with her husband, Nick Stiner, and two children.

Her first novel, ''[[The Ten Thousand Doors of January]]'', was critically received and nominated for multiple awards, including the [[Hugo Award for Best Novel|Hugo Award]], [[Nebula Award for Best Novel|Nebula Award]], and [[World Fantasy Award—Novel|World Fantasy Award]] for best novel.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2020-04-07|title=2020 Hugo Awards|url=http://www.thehugoawards.org/hugo-history/2020-hugo-awards/|access-date=2021-02-01|website=The Hugo Awards|language=en-US}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|last=|first=|last2=|first2=|last3=|first3=|last4=|first4=|last5=|first5=|last6=|first6=|date=|title=Nebula Awards® Nominees and Winners: Best Novel Nebula Awards®|url=https://nebulas.sfwa.org/award/best-novel/|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=2021-02-01|website=The Nebula Awards®|language=en-US}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=World Fantasy Awards℠ 2020, World Fantasy Convention|url=http://www.worldfantasy.org/world-fantasy-awards%e2%84%a0-2020/|access-date=2021-02-01|website=World Fantasy Convention|language=en-US}}</ref> Harrow has also written short fiction for ''Shimmer'', ''[[Strange Horizons]]'', [[Tor.com]], and Apex.
