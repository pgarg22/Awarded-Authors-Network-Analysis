'''Lisa Gluskin Stonestreet''' (born January 31, 1968) is an [[United States|American]] [[poet]]. Stonestreet's second book, ''The Greenhouse,'' was awarded the 2014 [[Frost Place]] Chapbook Prize and published by Bull City Press in August 2014. Her first book, ''Tulips, Water, Ash'', was published by [[Northeastern University Press]], and chosen by [[Jean Valentine]] as the last [[Morse Poetry Prize]], before its suspension in 2009 .<ref>{{cite web|title=Northeastern University Press|url=http://www.upne.com/1555537081.html|accessdate=February 1, 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Poets & Writers Magazine|url=http://www.pw.org/content/samuel_french_morse_poetry_prize_suspended|accessdate=September 16, 2014}}</ref>

==Education and career==
Stonestreet is a graduate of [[Yale University]], and she received an [[Master of Fine Arts|MFA]] in Creative Writing from the [[Warren Wilson College MFA Program for Writers|MFA Program for Writers]] at [[Warren Wilson College]] where she received the [[Jacob K. Javits Fellowship]]<ref>{{cite web|title=FY 2004 JAVITS FELLOWSHIP CANDIDATES|url=http://www2.ed.gov/programs/jacobjavits/jjfellowsbyfield2004.pdf|accessdate=February 5, 2014}}</ref> in Creative Writing. Her poems have been anthologized in ''[[Best New Poets]] 2005'' and ''Best New Poets 2006'' (Samovar Press), and they have appeared in literary journals and magazines, including ''[[The Iowa Review]]'', ''[[Bellingham Review]]'', ''[[Blackbird (journal)|Blackbird]]'',<ref>{{cite web|last=Blackbird|title=Two Poems by Lisa Gluskin Stonestreet|url=http://www.blackbird.vcu.edu/v8n1/poetry/stonestreet_l/index.shtml|accessdate=February 4, 2014}}</ref>  and ''[[Third Coast]]''.<ref>{{cite web|last=Lisa Gluskin Stonestreet Website|title=Bio|url=http://lisagluskinstonestreet.com/about/}}</ref> Her honors include fellowships from [[Millay Colony for the Arts]] and [[Vermont Studio Center]]. She lives with her husband and son in Portland, Oregon, where she works as a writer, teacher, and editor.
