'''Kristen Britain''' is an American author. She wrote ''[[Green Rider]]'' (which was nominated for the [[Crawford Award]]),<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.locusmag.com/SFAwards/Db/Crawford1999.html |title=Archived copy |access-date=2013-08-27 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20121018035423/http://www.locusmag.com/SFAwards/Db/Crawford1999.html |archive-date=2012-10-18 }}</ref> ''[[First Rider's Call]]'', ''[[The High King's Tomb]]'', ''[[Blackveil]]'' (which was nominated for the [[David Gemmell Legend Award]]),<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://gemmellaward.com/page/nominees-to-date |title=Archived copy |access-date=2013-08-27 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141030101200/http://gemmellaward.com/page/nominees-to-date |archive-date=2014-10-30 |url-status=dead }}</ref> and ''Mirror Sight''.  The sixth book in the Green Rider series, ''Firebrand'', was released February 28, 2017. The seventh book "Winterlight" was released September 2021.

==Early life and education==
Britain grew up in the [[Finger Lakes]] region of [[New York State]], where she started her first novel — an undersea fantasy featuring herself and her friends — at the age of nine. She published her first book, a cartoon collection called ''Horses and Horsepeople'', at the age of thirteen.<ref name="bio">[http://www.kristenbritain.com/bio.html Kristen Britain Biography] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20091016201824/http://www.kristenbritain.com/bio.html |date=2009-10-16 }}</ref>

After completing her degree in film production, with a minor in writing,<ref name="ithacan">"[http://theithacan.org/am/publish/accent/200904_Fantasy-writing_alumna_to_give_storytelling_tips.shtml Fantasy-writing alumna to give storytelling tips] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100613154901/http://theithacan.org/am/publish/accent/200904_Fantasy-writing_alumna_to_give_storytelling_tips.shtml |date=2010-06-13 }}," ''The Ithacan'', 2009-04-16</ref> at [[Ithaca College]] in 1987, she went to work for the [[National Park Service]] in 1988<ref name="ithacan"/> after a conversation with a park ranger during a visit to [[Women's Rights National Historical Park]].

==Career==
Her first ranger job was a seasonal position at [[Clara Barton National Historic Site]] in Maryland.<ref name="futint">[http://www.futurefiction.com/kristen_britain_interview.htm Kristen Britain Interview], Future Fiction</ref> 
At the time of the publication of her first novel, ''[[Green Rider]]'', she was working full-time as a park ranger at [[Acadia National Park]], and she drew much of the inspiration for the landscape of Sacoridia from the park.<ref>"[https://www.amazon.co.uk/exec/obidos/tg/feature/-/26081/026-2991312-0073265 Into the Woods: An Interview with Kristen Britain]," Amazon UK</ref> Her many years as a park ranger enabled her to work in a variety of natural and historical settings, from 300 feet below the surface of the Earth to 13,000 feet above sea level on the [[Continental Divide]]; and from the textile mills of the [[American Industrial Revolution]] to the homes of Americans who changed the course of history.<ref name="bio"/>

==Personal life==
She lives in [[Maine]].<ref>{{Cite web | url=http://kristenbritain.com/author/author.html |title = Kristen Britain's Official Site}}</ref>

==Literary career==
Kristen Britain's first novel, "[[Green Rider]]", was published by [[DAW Books]] in November 1998.  It was nominated in 1999 for both the [[Locus Award for Best First Novel]]<ref>{{cite web|title=The Locus Index to SF Awards |url=http://www.locusmag.com/SFAwards/Db/Locus1999.html#nvl1 |access-date=19 September 2015 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131023183112/http://www.locusmag.com/SFAwards/Db/Locus1999.html |archive-date=23 October 2013 }}</ref> and the William L. Crawford - IAFA Fantasy Award, short [[Crawford Award]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Science Fiction Awards Database|url=http://www.sfadb.com/Kristen_Britain}}</ref> The fourth novel in her Green Rider series, "[[Blackveil]]", was nominated for the 2011 [[Goodreads Choice Awards|Goodreads Fantasy Award]].<ref name="2011 Goodreads Fantasy Award">{{cite web |title=Best Fantasy |url=https://www.goodreads.com/choiceawards/best-fantasy-books-2011 |website=Goodreads |publisher=Goodreads Inc |access-date=21 March 2021}}</ref>
