'''Christine Feehan''' (born '''Christine King''' in [[Ukiah, California]]) is an American author of [[paranormal romance]], paranormal [[military thriller]]s, and [[fantasy]].  She is a #1 ''New York Times'',<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.penguin.com/newsroom/christine-feehans-earth-bound-debuts-1|title=Christine Feehan's Earth Bound Debuts at #1 - News About Penguin Books USA|language=en-US|access-date=2019-10-21}}</ref> #1 ''Publishers Weekly'',<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.digitalpw.com/digitalpw/20180205?pg=1&lm=1555516104000|title=Publishers Weekly - February 5, 2018 - page1|website=www.digitalpw.com|language=en|access-date=2019-10-21}}</ref> and International bestselling author of seven series; ''Carpathian'' (aka ''Dark Series''), ''GhostWalker Series'', ''Drake Sisters'', ''Sister of the Heart'' (''Sea Haven'') ''Series'', ''Shadow Riders Series'', ''Leopard Series'' and ''Torpedo Ink Series''.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.christinefeehan.com/books.php|title=Books|website=www.christinefeehan.com|access-date=2019-10-21}}</ref> Six of the seven series have made #1 on the ''New York Times'' bestseller list. As of January 2020 she has 80 published [[novels]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Printable Book Catalogue|url=http://www.christinefeehan.com/catalog/printlist.html}} ChristineFeehan.com Retrieved 2/2/2013</ref> The first in her ''Torpedo Ink Series'', ''Judgment Road'', debuted at #1 on the ''New York Times'' bestsellers list.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.nytimes.com/books/best-sellers/2018/02/11/combined-print-and-e-book-fiction/|title=Combined Print & E-Book Fiction - Best Sellers - Feb. 11, 2018 - The New York Times|work=The New York Times|access-date=2019-10-21|language=en-US|issn=0362-4331}}</ref>

== Biography ==
Christine Feehan was born in [[Ukiah, California]]. She grew up with three brothers and ten sisters. She spent a lot of time getting in trouble at school for writing instead of doing the things she was supposed to do. Later she forced her ten sisters to read every word.{{citation needed|date=January 2020}}

Feehan's first job was working in a library, then she went on to take journalism classes and worked at the ''Ukiah Journal''.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.readbrightly.com/the-library-the-place-where-my-love-for-books-began/|title=Romancing the Library: The Place Where My Love for Books Began|date=2017-04-11|website=Brightly|language=en-US|access-date=2019-10-21}}</ref>

Feehan trained in martial arts, earning a 3rd degree [[Black belt (martial arts)|black belt]] in [[Tang Soo Do]], going on to acquire her instructor's license.&nbsp; She taught martial arts for over twenty years, specializing in women's self-defense.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2014/01/26/books/review/inside-the-list.html|title=Inside the List|last=Cowles|first=Gregory|date=2014-01-17|work=The New York Times|access-date=2019-10-21|language=en-US|issn=0362-4331}}</ref>

== Career ==
''Time'' magazine said, "After [[Bram Stoker]], [[Anne Rice]], and [[Joss Whedon]] (who created the venerated ''[[Buffy the Vampire Slayer]]''), Christine Feehan is the person most credited with popularizing the neck gripper." ''USA Today'' called her "The Queen of paranormal romance."<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://content.time.com/time/magazine/article/0,9171,1161234,00.html|title=Books: Well, Hello, Suckers|last=Luscombe|first=Belinda|date=2006-02-19|work=Time|access-date=2019-10-22|language=en-US|issn=0040-781X}}</ref>

Feehan has been published in multiple languages and in many formats, including audio book, e-book, hardcover and large print.&nbsp; In October 2007 her first manga comic, ''Dark Hunger'', was released in stores.&nbsp; This was the first manga comic released by [[Berkley Publishing]] and it made #11 on ''Publishers Weekly'' Bestseller's List.{{citation needed|date=January 2020}}

Feehan's ground-breaking book trailer commercials have been shown on TV and in movie theaters.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.theguardian.com/books/booksblog/2010/jun/16/novel-trailers-moby-awards|title=Following the trailers|last=Barnett|first=David|date=2010-06-16|work=The Guardian|access-date=2019-10-22|language=en-GB|issn=0261-3077}}</ref> She has been featured on local TV, and appeared on ''The Montel Williams Show''. ''Dark Legend'' was featured on the cover of ''Romantic Times Magazine'', ''Dark Challenge'' was seen in the movie ''[[Kate & Leopold]]'' starring [[Meg Ryan]] and [[Hugh Jackman]], and several of her books appeared in the 2017 movie ''[[Happy Death Day]]''.

Feehan has appeared as an honored guest at numerous writers’ conventions, including ''Romantic Times Convention'', ''Get Caught Reading at Sea Cruise'', ''Celebrate Romance Conference'', ''Emerald City Conference'', and numerous ''Romance Writers of America Conferences''. She was also a special guest at the 2013 [[San Diego Comic-Con]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.comic-con.org/guest/christine-feehan|title=Christine Feehan|date=2012-09-10|website=Comic-Con International: San Diego|language=en|access-date=2019-10-22}}</ref>

== Personal life ==
Feehan has eleven children and several grandchildren, as well as fourteen brothers and sisters.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.christinefeehan.com/about.php|title=Meet Christine|website=www.christinefeehan.com|access-date=2020-01-08}}</ref>&nbsp;Her son, Calvert, passed away in the 1990s following a motorcycle accident.

In 2015 her family home in [[Cobb, California]], burned down in the [[Valley Fire]].{{citation needed|date=January 2020}}

Feehan lives in [[Mendocino, California]], near the ocean in the [[Redwoods]].&nbsp;She has several dogs, including two large black Russian terriers.{{citation needed|date=January 2020}}&nbsp;

== Awards ==
Feehan is a #1 ''[[The New York Times|New York Times]]'' best selling author of seven series. She has won multiple Paranormal Excellence Awards for Romantic Literature (PEARL) and ''[[Romantic Times]]'' awards. She has been on  bestsellers list, including those of ''Publishers Weekly'' <ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.publishersweekly.com/pw/nielsen/xromance.html|title=Publishers Weekly Bestseller Lists|work=PublishersWeekly.com|access-date=2018-03-10|language=en}}</ref>  ''and [[USA Today]]''.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.usatoday.com/life/books/best-selling/|title=Book Reviews and Best Selling Lists - USATODAY.com|website=USA TODAY|access-date=Sep 14, 2020}}</ref> Feehan has also received a Career Achievement Award from ''Romantic Times'' and has been nominated for a RITA award by the [[Romance Writers of America]].<ref name=":13">{{Cite web|url=http://www.mormonsf.org/awards.html|title=Authors' Awards|last=Parkin|first=Marny K.|website=Mormon SF|access-date=December 29, 2017}}</ref>
