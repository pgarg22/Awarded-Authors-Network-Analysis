'''Brian Lee Durfee''' is an [[United States of America|American]] [[Animal painter|wildlife]], [[Landscape painting|landscape]], and fantasy painter, and a [[fantasy]] and [[Horror fiction|horror]] writer. His paintings have appeared in various genre and other magazines, games, and books. One of his wildlife paintings is in the permanent collection at the [[Grand Canyon National Park]] visitors center.

[[Saga Press]] published his epic grimdark fantasy series, beginning with ''[[The Forgetting Moon]]''. He also has a horror alternate history series.

==Biography==
Durfee grew up in [[Fairbanks, Alaska]] and [[Monroe, Utah]].<ref name="raymond bolton article 20171106"/><ref name="ogtimes 19931013"/> He played high school football,<ref name="richfield reaper 19850918"/><ref name="richfield reaper 19931006"/> then served a mission for [[The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints]] in [[Connecticut]],<ref name="richfield reaper 19931006"/> though he is no longer a practicing member of that church.<ref name="south severe review"/> He graduated from [[Brigham Young University]] with a degree in illustration and fine arts.<ref name="richfield reaper 19931006"/><ref name="south severe review"/>

In 1992, he won second place in the second quarter [[Writers of the Future#Illustrators of the Future|Illustrators of the Future]] contest and was chosen to illustrate "The Monitor" by John Richard DeRose and "Lady's Portrait, Executed in Archaic Colors" by Charles M. Saplak in ''Writers of the Future, Volume IX''.<ref name="richfield reaper 19931006"/><ref name="isfdb wotf v9"/> He was a member of the [[Society of Illustrators]] in New York.<ref name="springville herald 19970319"/> Durfee cites works by [[Robert Jordan]], [[Stephen King]], [[George R. R. Martin]], [[Larry McMurtry]], [[Mötley Crüe]], the [[Las Vegas Raiders|Oakland Raiders]], and [[Tad Williams]] as being influential on his life and his works.<ref name="raymond bolton article 20171106"/>

His oil painting, "Grand Canyon Cliffs–Peregrine Falcons", won the 1999 Bird Award of Merit and the Grand Canyon National Park Association Purchase Award.<ref name="sltrib arts for parks 19991024"/> One of his paintings is in the permanent collection at the Kolb Gallery at the visitors center at [[Grand Canyon National Park]].<ref name="raymond bolton article 20171106"/> He was the artist guest of honor at [[Conduit (convention)|CONduit 10]] in 2000.<ref name="conduit 16 prog"/><ref name="sltrib conduit 20000519"/>

Durfee works as a prison guard at the [[Utah State Prison]], where he has worked in the mental health unit, the gang unit, processing new inmates, and overseeing the six libraries within the prison.<ref name="raymond bolton article 20171106"/><ref name="fantasy faction"/> He initially got the job because he wanted to be a police officer, but then decided to stay in that job.<ref name="fantasy faction"/> He worked with author [[James Dashner]] to put on a convention for the inmates at the prison,<ref name="fantasy faction"/> and he has taught creative writing classes to some of the inmates.<ref name="raymond bolton article 20171106"/>

====Collectible card games====
Durfee illustrated several cards for ''[[Magic: The Gathering]]'' [[collectible card game]], including four mountain cards,<ref name="mtg mountain 1"/><ref name="mtg mountain 2"/><ref name="mtg mountain 3"/><ref name="mtg mountain 4"/><ref name="art of mtg portal"/> "Blossoming Wreath",<ref name="mtg blossoming wreath"/> and "Sylvan Hierophant".<ref name="mtg sylvan hierophant"/> He also illustrated three cards for the ''[[Middle-earth Collectible Card Game]]'': "Black Númenóreans", "Returned Exiles", and "Hey, come merry dol!".
