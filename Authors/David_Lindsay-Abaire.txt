'''David Lindsay-Abaire''' (born November 14, 1969) is an American [[playwright]], [[lyricist]] and [[screenwriter]]. He received the [[Pulitzer Prize for Drama]] in 2007 for his play ''[[Rabbit Hole]]'', which also earned several [[Tony Award]] nominations.

==Early life and education==
David Lindsay-Abaire was born '''David Abaire''' in Boston, Massachusetts and grew up in [[South Boston]]. He attended [[Milton Academy]] and concentrated in theatre at [[Sarah Lawrence College]], from which he graduated in 1992.<ref>{{cite web | url = http://alum.slc.edu/page.aspx?pid=305 | title = Alumni News and Announcements | date = 2007–2008 | publisher = [[Sarah Lawrence College]]}}</ref> He was accepted into the [[Lila Bell Wallace|Lila Acheson Wallace]] American Playwrights Program at the [[Juilliard School]],<ref>{{ cite web | url = http://www.juilliard.edu/alumni/news/news_decades/2011-2012/1109/index.php | title = Alumni News | publisher = [[The Juilliard School]] | date = September 2011 | archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20111111120416/http://juilliard.edu/alumni/news/news_decades/2011-2012/1109/index.php | archive-date = 2011-11-11 | quote = David Lindsay-Abaire (Playwrights '97)}}</ref> where he wrote under the tutelage of playwrights [[Marsha Norman]] and [[Christopher Durang]] from 1996 to 1998.<ref name="NYT 2000">{{cite news | title = Finding the Humor and the Hope in Fractured Lives | url = https://query.nytimes.com/gst/fullpage.html?res=9F04EFDC1038F931A25750C0A9669C8B63 | work = [[The New York Times]] | first = Peter | last = Marks | date = March 12, 2000 | access-date = December 25, 2008}}</ref>

==Career==
Lindsay-Abaire had his first theatrical success with ''[[Fuddy Meers]],'' which was workshopped as part of the National Playwrights Conference at the [[Eugene O'Neill Theatre Center]] in 1998 under Artistic Director [[Lloyd Richards]].<ref>David Lindsay-Abaire, ''Fuddy Meers'', Dramatists Play Service Inc,, 2000, {{ISBN|0822217511}}, p. 2</ref><ref>Lefkowitz, David. [http://www.playbill.com/news/article/oneill-center-playwrights-conference-in-full-swing-through-aug.-1-76525/ "O'Neill Center Playwrights Conference in Full Swing Through Aug. 1"] Playbill, July 17, 1998</ref> The play premiered [[Off-Broadway]] at the [[Manhattan Theatre Club]], running from November 2, 1999 to January 2000<ref>Jones, Kenneth. [http://www.playbill.com/news/article/lindsay-abaires-new-comedy-fuddy-meers-opens-nov.-2-at-mtc-85170# "Lindsay-Abaire's New Comedy, 'Fuddy Meers', Opens Nov. 2 at MTC"] Playbill, November 2, 1999</ref> and transferred to the Minetta Lane Theatre on January 27, 2000, closing in April 2000 after 16 previews and 78 performances there.<ref>Jones, Kenneth. [http://www.playbill.com/news/article/off-broadways-fuddy-meers-folds-april-16-88538# "Off-Broadway's 'Fuddy Meers' Folds, April 16"] Playbill, April 16, 2000</ref><ref>[http://www.lortel.org/LLA_archive/index.cfm?id=231&search_by=show "'Fuddy Meers' Listing"] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140201162458/http://www.lortel.org/lla_archive/index.cfm?search_by=show&id=231 |date=2014-02-01 }} lortel.org, accessed September 1, 2015</ref> He returned to the Manhattan Theatre Club in 2001 with ''[[Wonder of the World (play)|Wonder of the World]]'', starring [[Sarah Jessica Parker]], about a wife who suddenly leaves her husband and hops a bus to [[Niagara Falls]] in search of freedom, enlightenment, and the meaning of life.<ref>[http://www.lortel.org/lla_archive/index.cfm?search_by=show&id=49 "''Wonder of the World'' Listing"] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150405004932/http://www.lortel.org/lla_archive/index.cfm?search_by=show&id=49 |date=2015-04-05 }} lortel.org, accessed September 1, 2015</ref><ref>Jones, Kenneth and [[Simonson, Robert]]. [http://www.playbill.com/news/article/sarah-jessica-parker-beholds-wonder-of-the-world-opening-in-nyc-nov.-1-99489# "Sarah Jessica Parker Beholds 'Wonder of the World', Opening in NYC Nov. 1"] Playbill, November 1, 2001</ref>

Lindsay-Abaire also wrote ''[[Kimberly Akimbo]]'' (2000),<ref>{{cite web|work=[[Los Angeles Times]]|title=How a Teen Copes in a World Thrown 'Akimbo'|date=April 16, 2001|first=Don|last=Shirley|url=http://articles.latimes.com/2001/apr/16/entertainment/ca-51637}}</ref> ''Dotting and Dashing'' (1999), ''[[Snow Angel (play)|Snow Angel]]'' (1999),<ref name='SnowAngel'>{{cite book | last = Lindsay-Abaire | first = David | author-link = David Lindsay-Abaire | title = Snow Angel | publisher = Playscripts, Inc.  | year = 2003 | url = http://www.playscripts.com/play.php3?playid=245}}</ref> and ''A Devil Inside'' (Off-Broadway, 1997).<ref>Bruckner, D. J. R. [https://www.nytimes.com/1997/01/23/theater/theater-in-review-306690.html?pagewanted= "Theater in Review"] ''The New York Times'', January 23, 1997</ref> Among his early short plays, he wrote ''The Li'l Plays'' (1997-1999) which are five comedic plays, each 10–15 minutes in length.<ref>Bryer, Jackson R. and Hartig, Mary C.  "Lindsay-Abaire, David (1969-)", ''The Facts on File Companion to American Drama'', Infobase Publishing, 2010, {{ISBN|1438129661}}, p. 313</ref>

His play ''[[Rabbit Hole]]'' premiered in 2006 on Broadway with [[Cynthia Nixon]], [[Tyne Daly]], and [[John Slattery]],<ref>[http://www.ibdb.com/production.php?id=398476 " 'Rabbit Hole' Listing"] ibdb, accessed September 1, 2015</ref> and won the 2007 Pulitzer Prize for Drama.<ref>[http://www.pulitzer.org/citation/2007-Drama "Pulitzer Prize Winner, Drama, 2007"] pulitzer.org, accessed September 1, 2015</ref> It was nominated for a [[Tony Award]] for Best Play, as well as other Tony awards, and Cynthia Nixon won the 2006 Tony Award as Best Actress.<ref>[http://www.playbill.com/news/article/just-the-facts-list-of-2006-tony-award-winners-and-nominees-133125# "Just the Facts: List of 2006 Tony Award Winners and Nominees"] Playbill. June 11, 2006</ref>

He wrote the book for the [[musical theatre|musical]] ''[[High Fidelity (musical)|High Fidelity]]'', which ran on Broadway in December 2006.<ref>{{Ibdb title|id=446878|title=High Fidelity}}</ref><ref>[http://www.playbillvault.com/Show/Detail/6030/High-Fidelity "'High Fidelity' Broadway Production"] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150530004916/http://www.playbillvault.com/Show/Detail/6030/High-Fidelity |date=2015-05-30 }} playbillvault.com, accessed October 20, 2015</ref>

He wrote the book and lyrics for the musical ''[[Shrek the Musical]]'' which ran on Broadway from November 8, 2008 (previews) to January 3, 2010, with Lindsay-Abaire receiving a 2009 [[Tony Award]] nomination for Book of a Musical<ref>[http://www.playbillvault.com/Show/Detail/12248/Shrek-the-Musical '"Shrek the Musical" Broadway Production"] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20151026074400/http://www.playbillvault.com/Show/Detail/12248/Shrek-the-Musical |date=2015-10-26 }} playbillvault.com, accessed October 20, 2015</ref> and in the [[West End theatre|West End]] in May 2011.<ref>Shenton, Mark. [http://www.playbill.com/news/article/150539-Shrek-the-Musical-Begins-Performances-at-West-Ends-Theatre-Royal-Drury-Lane-May-6 "'Shrek the Musical' Begins Performances at West End's Theatre Royal, Drury Lane May 6"] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140812205330/http://www.playbill.com/news/article/150539-Shrek-the-Musical-Begins-Performances-at-West-Ends-Theatre-Royal-Drury-Lane-May-6 |date=2014-08-12 }}, Playbill, May 6, 2011</ref> The musical ran for 441 performances on Broadway.<ref>{{cite web|work=[[The New York Times]]|date=2009-10-21|author=Healy, Patrick|title=Shrek the Musical to Close January 3|url=http://artsbeat.blogs.nytimes.com/2009/10/21/shrek-the-musical-to-close-jan-3/}}</ref>

''[[Good People (play)|Good People]]'' officially opened on Broadway on March 3, 2011, with [[Frances McDormand]] and [[Tate Donovan]] in the lead roles.<ref name="jones">{{cite web | last = Jones | first = Kenenth | url = http://www.playbill.com/news/article/145354-Becky-Ann-Baker-Estelle-Parsons-Rene-Goldsberry-Join-World-Premiere-Cast-of-Broadways-Good-People | title = Becky Ann Baker, Estelle Parsons, Renée Goldsberry Join World-Premiere Cast of Broadway's ''Good People'' | publisher = [[Playbill]].com | date = November 9, 2010 | url-status = dead | archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20110204092737/http://www.playbill.com/news/article/145354-Becky-Ann-Baker-Estelle-Parsons-Rene-Goldsberry-Join-World-Premiere-Cast-of-Broadways-Good-People | archive-date = February 4, 2011 }}</ref> The play was nominated for the 2011 [[Tony Award]], Best Play and won the 2011 Tony Award, Actress in a Play for McDormand.<ref>[http://www.playbillvault.com/Show/Detail/13720/Good-People '"Good PeopleLisa. " Broadway Production"] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20151029024138/http://www.playbillvault.com/Show/Detail/13720/Good-People |date=2015-10-29 }}  playbillvault.com, accessed October 20, 2015</ref>

His play ''Ripcord'' opened Off-Broadway on October 20, 2015 at the Manhattan Theatre Club in a limited engagement. Directed by [[David Hyde Pierce]], the cast features [[Marylouise Burke]], [[Rachel Dratch]], [[Glenn Fitzgerald]], and [[Holland Taylor]]. The play focuses on two roommates in a retirement home, who according to ''Variety'' "devise dirty tricks...to torment one another."<ref>[http://www.broadwayworld.com/article/Review-Roundup-David-Lindsay-Abaires-RIPCORD-Opens-Off-Broadway-20151020 "Review Roundup: David Lindsay-Abaire's RIPCORD Opens Off-Broadway"] broadwayworld.com, October 20, 2015</ref>

Among his influences, Lindsay-Abaire said: "I love Chris's [Durang] work. And I don't think there's been a piece written about me that hasn't mentioned the fact that he and I live in the same world. But I think I've also been influenced by [[John Guare]] and [[Tina Howe]] and older folks like Feydeau and [[Eugene Ionesco|Ionesco]] and [[Joe Orton]]."<ref>Wren, Celia. [http://www.tcg.org/publications/at/playindex/?fuddy "'Fuddy Meers'. Lost in the Funhouse. An Interview with the playwright"] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150924113602/http://www.tcg.org/publications/at/playindex/?fuddy |date=2015-09-24 }} Originally published in American Theatre magazine (July/August 2000), accessed September 1, 2015</ref>

Lindsay-Abaire has received commissions from [[Dance Theater Workshop]] and the Jerome Foundation.<ref name=bw2>{{cite news|url=http://www.broadwayworld.com/bwwtv/article/David-Lindsay-Abaire-to-Reimagine-POLTERGEIST-for-MGM-20110607|title=David Lindsay-Abaire to Reimagine POLTERGEIST for MGM? |work=Broadway World|date=7 June 2011|access-date=18 June 2016}}</ref> He has received awards from the Berilla Kerr Foundation, the [[Lincoln Center]] LeComte du Nuoy Fund, [[Mixed Blood Theater]], [[Primary Stages]], the [[Eugene O'Neill Theatre Center]], the [[Tennessee Williams/ New Orleans Literary Festival]], and the South Carolina Playwrights Festival.{{citation needed|date=October 2015}}

===Film===
Lindsay-Abaire wrote the screenplay of the 2010 [[Rabbit Hole (2010 film)|film adaptation]] of his play ''Rabbit Hole'', which starred [[Nicole Kidman]].<ref>Kilday, Greg. [http://www.hollywoodreporter.com/hr/content_display/news/e3i776b1668d474ee36e352bd643641d7b3 "Lionsgate takes trip down 'Rabbit Hole'"] ''[[The Hollywood Reporter]]'', September 16, 2010</ref> His other screenplays have tended to be in the children's fantasy and science fiction genres, including the animated film ''[[Robots (2005 film)|Robots]]'' (2005),<ref>{{Allmovie title|289664|Robots}}</ref> written with [[Lowell Ganz]] and [[Babaloo Mandel]], ''[[Inkheart (film)|Inkheart]]'' (2008), based on the [[Inkheart|novel of the same name]],<ref>{{Amg movie|359300|Inkheart}}</ref> the animated film ''[[Rise of the Guardians]]'' (2012),<ref>{{Rotten Tomatoes|rise_of_the_guardians_2012|Rise of the Guardians}}</ref> based on a story by co-director [[William Joyce (writer)|William Joyce]], and ''[[Oz the Great and Powerful]]'' (2013), written with Mitchell Kapner.<ref>Kennedy, Lisa. [http://www.denverpost.com/ci_22733035/movie-review-oz-great-and-powerful-more-amusing "Movie review: 'Oz the Great and Powerful' more amusing than great"] ''Denver Post'', March 8, 2013</ref> He also wrote the screenplay for the 2015 horror remake ''[[Poltergeist (2015 film)|Poltergeist]]''.

==Personal life==
Lindsay-Abaire and his wife [[Christine Lindsay-Abaire|Christine]] live in [[Brooklyn]], New York.<ref name="NYT 2000"/> In 2016, Lindsay-Abaire was named co-director of [[Juilliard School|Juilliard's]] [[Lila Bell Wallace|Lila Acheson Wallace]] American Playwrights Program.<ref name=bw>{{cite news|url=http://www.broadwayworld.com/article/Pulitzer-Prize-Winner-David-Lindsay-Abaire-to-Join-Juilliard-as-Co-Director-of-Lila-Acheson-Wallace-American-Playwrights-Program-20160317|title=Pulitzer Prize Winner David Lindsay-Abaire to Join Juilliard as Co-Director of Lila Acheson Wallace American Playwrights Program |work=Broadway World|date=17 March 2016|access-date=18 June 2016}}</ref>
