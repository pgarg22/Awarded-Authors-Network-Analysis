'''Brent Weeks''' (born March 7, 1977) is an American fantasy writer. His [[debut novel]], ''The Way of Shadows'', was a [[New York Times Best Seller]] in April 2009.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Not An April Fools Joke.|url=http://www.brentweeks.com/2009/04/not-an-april-fools-joke/|website=Brent Weeks}}</ref> Each of the five books in his Lightbringer Series made the NYT list as well, starting with ''[[The Black Prism]]'' in 2010. He lives and works near Portland, Oregon with his wife, Kristi, and their two daughters.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Brent Weeks Bio|url=http://www.brentweeks.com/bio/|website=Brent Weeks}}</ref>

==Early life==

Weeks was born in Whitefish, Montana on {{Birth date|1977|3|7}}. He attended Whitefish High School, and graduated from [[Hillsdale College]] in 2000 with a degree in English. He has said that he decided to try writing novels during a semester abroad at Oxford College, an experience that was influential to him personally and professionally. He briefly worked as a teacher at Salem Academy in Oregon and as a bartender before moving to writing full-time.<ref>{{cite web|title=Bio|url=http://www.brentweeks.com/bio/|publisher=Brent Weeks|access-date=10 November 2012}}</ref>

==Writing==
Weeks has published two complete series, The Night Angel Trilogy and The Lightbringer Series. All eight novels are [[Orbit Books|published by Orbit Books]],<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.hachettebookgroup.com/contributor/brent-weeks/|title=Brent Weeks|date=2017-06-27|website=Hachette Book Group|language=en-US|access-date=2019-08-09}}</ref> a division of [[Hachette Book Group]]. His writing has been heavily influenced by the Classics (including ''The Odyssey''<ref>{{Cite web|title=Author's Shelf Series feat. Brent Weeks|url=https://www.podbean.com/media/share/pb-b9vpf-c4aae8?utm_campaign=w_share_ep&utm_medium=dlink&utm_source=w_share|website=Legendarium Podcast}}</ref> and ''Dante's Inferno''<ref>{{Cite book|last=Weeks|first=Brent|title=The Burning White|publisher=Orbit|year=2019|isbn=978-0-316-25130-3|pages=916–917}}</ref>), Shakespeare, William Butler Yeats, and JRR Tolkien.<ref>{{Cite book|last=Weeks|first=Brent|title=The Burning White|publisher=Orbit|year=2019|isbn=978-0-316-25128-0|pages=430}}</ref>

The entire ''[[The Night Angel Trilogy|Night Angel Trilogy]]'',<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.fantasy.fr/articles/view/8326|title=Bragelonne donne de la Voie|last=Bastos|first=Cesar|publisher=Fantasy.fr|access-date=10 November 2012}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.publishersweekly.com/pw/by-topic/industry-news/publisher-news/article/44226-orbit-makes-a-third-rotation.html|title=Orbit Makes a Third Rotation|publisher=Publishers Weekly|access-date=10 November 2012}}</ref>--''The Way of Shadows, Shadow's Edge,'' and ''Beyond the Shadows''--was published as mass market paperback volumes in October 2008. Since its debut, the trilogy has been printed in more than 14 languages, and has more than one million copies in print. ''The Way of Shadows'' was also published as a graphic novel by [[Yen Press]],<ref>{{cite web|last=Green|first=Scott|title=Yen Press Licenses "Another" and "BTOOOM!" Manga|url=http://www.crunchyroll.com/anime-news/2012/07/14-1/yen-press-licenses-another-and-btooom-manga|publisher=Crunchyroll|access-date=10 November 2012}}</ref> adapted by Andrew McDonald and Ivan Brandon. Orbit Books published a hardcover 10th Anniversary Edition of the trilogy in November 2018.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.orbitbooks.net/night-angel-trilogy-10th-anniversary/|title=THE NIGHT ANGEL TRILOGY – LIMITED EDITION HARDCOVER|website=www.orbitbooks.net|access-date=2020-01-22}}</ref> ''Perfect Shadow'', a novella set in the Night Angel universe, was published by [[Subterranean Press]] as a lettered & numbered hardcover edition in 2011, and is currently available from Orbit as an ebook. There are more than 4 million copies of his books in print. 

The first novel in his Lightbringer Series'', [[The Black Prism]],'' was released in 2010.<ref>{{cite web|title=Fiction Book Review: The Black Prism|url=https://www.publishersweekly.com/978-0-316-07555-8|publisher=Publisher's Weekly}}</ref> The series was originally intended to be a trilogy, but his publishers announced in 2012 (to coincide with the release of book 2, ''[[The Blinding Knife]]'') that it would span four books,<ref>{{cite web|last=Fitzgerald|first=Laura|title=THE BLINDING KNIFE by Brent Weeks releases this week!|url=http://www.orbitbooks.net/2012/09/11/the-blinding-knife-by-brent-weeks-releases-this-week/|publisher=Orbit Books blog|access-date=17 December 2012}}</ref> the third of which, ''[[The Broken Eye]]'', was released in August 2014.

On May 11, 2016, Brent Weeks announced via email newsletter and his website that Lightbringer #4, ''[[The Blood Mirror]],'' would be published on October 25/27 of 2016 (US/UK release dates, respectively). He also announced that "There will be five books in the Lightbringer Series, not four..."  Weeks did not, however, indicate a release date.<ref>{{cite web|title=The Blood Mirror Release Date|url=http://www.brentweeks.com/2016/05/the-blood-mirror-release-date/|website=Brent Weeks, the Official Site|access-date=11 May 2016}}</ref> 

The fifth and final book in the Lightbringer Series, ''[https://www.goodreads.com/book/show/30169100-the-burning-white The Burning White]'', was published on October 22, 2019. Weeks wrote a longform essay for the promotional tour of the book, titled, "[http://www.brentweeks.com/2019/11/on-ending-well/ On Ending Well]."

Each book in the Lightbringer Series appeared on the ''New York Times'' Bestseller List, with ''The Burning White'' reaching #4 on the Combined Print & E-book list.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.nytimes.com/books/best-sellers/2019/11/10/|title=Best Sellers - Nov. 10, 2019 - The New York Times|work=The New York Times|access-date=2020-01-22|language=en-US|issn=0362-4331}}</ref> ''The Black Prism'' was recently listed among the [https://www.bookbub.com/blog/best-sff-books best Sci-Fi & Fantasy Novels of the 2010s by BookBub].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.bookbub.com/blog/best-sff-books|title=The Best Sci-Fi and Fantasy Books of the Decade|website=www.bookbub.com|language=en-US|access-date=2020-01-22}}</ref> 

Weeks is represented by the [http://maassagency.com/clients-science-fiction-fantasy/ Donald Maass Literary Agency].
