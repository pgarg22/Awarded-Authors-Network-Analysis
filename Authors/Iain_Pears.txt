'''Iain George Pears''' (born 8 August 1955<ref name="Who's Who 2016" />) is an English [[art historian]], [[novelist]] and journalist.<ref>[http://www.barnesandnoble.com/writers/writerdetails.asp?cid=883549 Iain Pears biography]</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2009/05/21/books/21newl.html|title=Newly Released|last=Virshup|first=Amy|date=21 May 2009|work=The New York Times|page=7|access-date=28 September 2011}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Pears was born on 8 August 1955 in [[Coventry]], England. He was educated at [[Warwick School]], an all-boys [[Public school (United Kingdom)|public school]] in [[Warwick]]. He studied at [[Wadham College, Oxford]], graduating with a [[Bachelor of Arts]] (BA) degree, and at [[Wolfson College, Oxford]], graduating with a [[Doctor of Philosophy]] (DPhil) degree.<ref name="Who's Who 2016"/><ref>{{cite web|last1=Iain|first1=Pears|title=The growth of interest in painting in England, 1680-1768|url=http://ethos.bl.uk/OrderDetails.do?did=1&uin=uk.bl.ethos.353523|website=EThOS|publisher=[[The British Library]]|access-date=19 May 2017|date=1984}}</ref>

In 1985, Pears married [[Ruth Harris (historian)|Ruth Harris]], a historian and academic.<ref name="Who's Who 2016" /> Together they have two sons.<ref name="Who's Who 2016" /> He currently lives with his wife and children in Oxford.

==Career==
Before writing, he worked as a reporter for the BBC, Channel 4 (UK) and ZDF (Germany) and correspondent for [[Reuters]] from 1982 to 1990 in Italy, France, UK and US. In 1987 he became a Getty Fellow in the Arts and Humanities at [[Yale University]]. 

Pears first came to international prominence with his best selling book ''[[An Instance of the Fingerpost]]'' (1997), which was translated into several languages.  He is known for experimenting with different narrative structures, presenting four consecutive versions of the same events in ''An Instance of the Fingerpost'', three stories interleaved in ''The Dream of Scipio'' (2002), three stories told in reverse chronological order in ''Stone's Fall'' (2009), and allowing the reader to switch between multiple narratives in the electronic book version of ''Arcadia'' (2015).<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.theguardian.com/books/2015/aug/20/novel-use-for-app-iain-pears-arcadia|title=Why you need an app to understand my novel|date=20 August 2015|last=Pears|first=Iain}}</ref>  He has also written a novel series featuring Jonathan Argyll, art historian.  

==Bibliography==
===Jonathan Argyll series (art history mysteries)===
This series presents detective art historian Jonathan Argyll who works with two members of the (fictitious) Italian Art Squad: Flavia di Stefano (deputy) and General Bottando (head of the squad).
Pears published seven books in the series:
* ''[[The Raphael Affair]]'' (1991)
* ''[[The Titian Committee]]'' (1992)
* ''[[The Bernini Bust]]'' (1993)
* ''[[The Last Judgement (1994 book)|The Last Judgement]]'' (1994)
* ''[[Giotto's Hand]]'' (1995)
* ''[[Death and Restoration]]'' (1996)
* ''[[The Immaculate Deception]]'' (2000)

===Novels===

* ''[[An Instance of the Fingerpost]]'' (1997)
* ''[[The Dream of Scipio (novel)|The Dream of Scipio]]'' (2002)
* ''[[The Portrait (novel)|The Portrait]]'' (2005)
* ''[[Stone's Fall]]'' (2009)
* ''Arcadia'' (2015)
