}}'''Jordan Ifueko''' (born August 16, 1993) is a [[Nigerian Americans|Nigerian American]] writer of fantasy and [[young adult fiction]].<ref>{{Cite web|date=2020-08-14|title=This San Fernando Valley author was just 13 when she started plotting her YA fantasy debut|url=https://www.ocregister.com/2020/08/14/this-san-fernando-valley-author-was-just-13-when-she-started-plotting-her-ya-fantasy-debut|access-date=2021-09-29|website=Orange County Register|language=en-US}}</ref> She is best known for her novel ''[[Raybearer]]'', which became a New York Times best seller, and its sequel, ''Redemptor.''<ref>{{Cite book|last=author.|first=Ifueko, Jordan|url=http://worldcat.org/oclc/1152281861|title=Raybearer|date=18 August 2020|isbn=978-1-68335-719-3|oclc=1152281861}}</ref> She also writes short stories, which have been published in ''[[Strange Horizons]]'' magazine<ref>{{Cite web|title=Short Stories|url=https://www.jordanifueko.com/short-stories|access-date=2021-09-29|website=Jordan Ifueko|language=en}}</ref>

== Early life ==
Jordan Ifueko was born in [[Southern California]] to two Nigerian parents who had who migrated to the United States;<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|last=Sutton|first=Roger|title=Jordan Ifueko Talks with Roger|url=https://www.hbook.com/?detailStory=jordan-ifueko-talks-with-roger|access-date=2021-09-29|website=The Horn Book}}</ref> her mother is from the [[Yoruba people|Yoruba]] tribe while her father is from the [[Edo people|Bini]] tribe. Ifueko stated she grew up listening to [[West African mythology|West African folktales]] which were narrated by her mother.<ref name=":0" /> She was home schooled by her parents and attended [[George Fox University]] in [[Oregon]]. She is married and lives in [[Los Angeles]] with her family.<ref>{{cite web |title=Meet Jordan |url=https://www.jordanifueko.com/about |website=jordanifueko.com |access-date=7 November 2021}}</ref>

== Career ==
Ifueko's debut novel ''[[Raybearer]]'', inspired partly by her West African heritage and other world cultures, was published in August 18, 2020 by [[Abrams Books|Abrams books]],<ref>{{Cite web|title=Get a first look at 'Raybearer,' a dazzling West African-inspired YA fantasy|url=https://ew.com/books/2019/08/30/raybearer-first-look-fantasy/|access-date=2021-09-29|website=EW.com|language=en}}</ref> and became a [[The New York Times Best Seller list|New York bestseller]].<ref>{{Cite web|date=2020-08-30|title=Jordan Ifueko on her stunning debut West African inspired YA fantasy, Raybearer|url=https://www.unitedbypop.com/young-adult-books/interviews-young-adult-books/jordan-ifueko-raybearer/|access-date=2021-09-29|website=United By Pop|language=en-US}}</ref> It was nominated for the [[Andre Norton Award]]<ref>{{Cite web|last1=Fictions|first1=© 2021 Science|last2=America|first2=Fantasy Writers of|last3=SFWA®|first3=Inc|last4=Fiction|first4=Nebula Awards® are registered trademarks of Science|last5=America|first5=Fantasy Writers of|last6=SFWA|first6=Inc Opinions expressed on this web site are not necessarily those of|title=Nebula Awards® Nominees and Winners: Andre Norton Nebula Award for Middle Grade and Young Adult Fiction Nebula Awards®|url=https://nebulas.sfwa.org/award/andre-norton-award/|access-date=2021-09-29|website=The Nebula Awards®|language=en-US}}</ref> and the [[Goodreads Choice Awards|Goodreads Choice Award]] for best Debut Novel and best Young Adult Fantasy & Science Fiction.<ref>{{cite web |title=Announcing the Goodreads Choice Winner in Best Debut Novel! |url=https://www.goodreads.com/choiceawards/best-debut-novel-2020 |website=Goodreads |access-date=September 30, 2021}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |title=Announcing the Goodreads Choice Winner in Best Young Adult Fantasy & Science Fiction! |url=https://www.goodreads.com/choiceawards/best-young-adult-fantasy-books-2020 |website=Goodreads |access-date=September 30, 2021}}</ref> It was followed by a sequel, ''Redemptor'', published in 2021.<ref>{{Cite book|last=author.|first=Ifueko, Jordan|url=http://worldcat.org/oclc/1262873440|title=Redemptor|date=17 August 2021|isbn=978-1-4197-3984-2|oclc=1262873440}}</ref>

In September 2021, it was announced that [[Netflix]] would be adapting ''Raybearer'' into a television series, under a new overall deal with Gina Atwater. The project will be produced by Suger23 and Macro Television Studios with Atwater as writer, director and producer.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Petski|first=Denise|date=2021-09-28|title=Gina Atwater Inks Overall Deal With Netflix; Sets 'Raybearer' Adaptation As First Project|url=https://deadline.com/2021/09/gina-atwater-overall-deal-netflix-raybearer-adaptation-first-project-1234845641/|access-date=2021-09-29|website=Deadline|language=en-US}}</ref>
