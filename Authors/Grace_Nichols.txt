'''Grace Nichols''' [[Royal Society of Literature|FRSL]] (born 1950) is a [[Guyana|Guyanese]] [[poet]] who moved to Britain in 1977, before which she worked as a teacher and journalist in Guyana. Her first collection, ''I is a Long-Memoried Woman'' (1983), won the [[Commonwealth Poetry Prize]].

==Biography==
Grace Nichols [[Royal Society of Literature|FRSL]] was born in [[Georgetown, Guyana]], and lived in a small village on the country's coast<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://literature.britishcouncil.org/grace-nichols |title="Grace Nichols", Literature, British Council. |access-date=2012-11-17 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20151115204253/http://literature.britishcouncil.org/grace-nichols |archive-date=2015-11-15 |url-status=dead }}</ref> until her family moved to the city when she was eight years old.<ref>[http://www.curtisbrown.co.uk/grace-nichols/ Grace Nichols profile at Curtis Brown.]</ref> She took a Diploma in Communications from the [[University of Guyana]], and subsequently worked as a [[teacher]] (1967–70), as a [[journalist]] and in government information services,<ref name=Busby>[[Margaret Busby]] (ed.), ''[[Daughters of Africa: An International Anthology of Words and Writings by Women of African Descent from the Ancient Egyptian to the Present]]'', London: Vintage, 1993, p. 796.</ref> before she immigrated to the United Kingdom in 1977.<ref name="dawes"/> Much of her poetry is characterised by [[Caribbean]] rhythms and culture, and influenced by Guyanese and [[Amerindian]] folklore.

Her first collection of poetry, ''I is a Long-Memoried Woman'' won the 1983 [[Commonwealth Poetry Prize]]. She has written several further books of poetry and a novel for adults, ''Whole of a Morning Sky'', 1986. Her books for children include collections of short stories and poetry anthologies. Her latest work, of new and selected poems, is ''Startling the Flying Fish'', 2006. Her poetry is featured in the [[Assessment and Qualifications Alliance|AQA]], [[WJEC (exam board)|WJEC]] (Welsh Joint Education Committee), and [[Edexcel]] English/English Literature [[IGCSE]] anthologies – meaning that many IGCSE students in the UK have studied her work. Her religion is Christianity after she was influenced by the UK's many religions and multi-cultural society.

She lives in [[Lewes]], [[East Sussex]],<ref name=Busby /> with her partner, the Guyanese poet [[John Agard]].<ref name="dawes">{{cite book |title=Talk yuh talk: interviews with Anglophone Caribbean poets |last=Dawes |first=Kwame Senu Neville |author-link= Kwame Dawes|year=2001 |publisher=University of Virginia Press |isbn= 9780813919461 |page=135 |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=YBkWXcsoN5sC&q=%22John+Agard%22+%22grace+nichols%22&pg=PA135 |access-date=2010-06-06}}</ref>

==Anthologise — annual poetry competition for schools==

In 2011 Nichols was a member of the first ever judging panel for a new schools poetry competition named "Anthologise", spearheaded by Poet Laureate [[Carol-Ann Duffy]].<ref name=Anthologise>[http://www.picador.com/blog/july-2011/anthologise-a-national-poetry-anthology-competitio "Anthologise - A national poetry anthology competition"], Picador, 14 July 2011.</ref> School students aged 11–18 from around the UK were invited to create and submit their own anthologies of published poetry. The first ever winners of Anthologise were the sixth form pupils of [[Monkton Combe School]], [[Bath, Somerset|Bath]], with their anthology titled ''The Poetry of Earth is Never Dead''.<ref name=Anthologise />
