'''Josef Alois Gleich'''  (also '''Joseph Alois Gleich'''; pseudonyms Adolph Blum, Ludwig Dellarosa, Heinrich Walden; 14 September 1772 – 10 February 1841)<ref name=lexikon>[https://www.musiklexikon.ac.at/ml/musik_G/Gleich_Familie.xml "Gleich, Familie"] ''Oesterreiches Musiklexikon Online''. Retrieved 9 March 2021.</ref><ref name=obl/> was an Austrian civil servant, and a prolific dramatist and novelist.

==Life==
Gleich was born in Vienna in 1772, son of a clerk. At the [[University of Vienna]] he studied philosophy, languages and public accounting, and entered the civil service. From 1790 to 1831 he was employed in provincial accounting of [[Lower Austria]].<ref name=obl>[https://www.biographien.ac.at/oebl/oebl_G/Gleich_Josef-Alois_1772_1841.xml "Gleich, Josef Alois; Ps. Adolph Blum, Ludwig Dellarosa, H. Walden (1772-1841), Schriftsteller"] ''Österreichisches Biographisches Lexikon''. Retrieved 9 March 2021.</ref><ref name=db>[https://www.deutsche-biographie.de/pnd119559994.html "Gleich, Josef Alois (Pseudonym Adolph Blum, Ludwig Dellarosa, H. Walden)"] ''[[Neue Deutsche Biographie]]'' (1964) in ''[[Deutsche Biographie]]''. Retrieved 9 March 2021.</ref>

===Plays===
From 1791 he was active in Vienna as a writer. He was a dramatist, and wrote about 220 plays from 1804 to 1833; they were staged at the [[Theater in der Josefstadt]], where he was assistant director from 1814 to 1816, and at the [[Theater in der Leopoldstadt]]. Like his contemporaries [[Karl Meisl]] and [[Adolf Bäuerle]], his plays developed the tradition of {{ill|Old Viennese folk theatre|de|Alt-Wiener Volkstheater}}.<ref name=obl/><ref name=db/>

The actor and dramatist [[Ferdinand Raimund]]'s first acting success was in Gleich's play ''Die Musikanten am Hohen Markt'', and Gleich wrote further plays for him. Raimund, who became Gleich's son-in-law, continued the development of Viennese folk theatre.<ref name=obl/><ref name=db/>

===Novels and periodical===
Gleich also wrote about 100 popular novels about knights, robbers and ghosts, modelled on the stories of [[Christian Heinrich Spiess]].<ref name=db/>

In 1832 he founded a periodical for popular consumption, the "Comic letters of Hansjörgel von Gumpoldskirchen to his brother-in-law Maxel in Feselau with his accounts of daily events in Vienna". They were in the style of the ''{{ill|Eipeldauer-Briefe|de}}'' of {{ill|Joseph Richter|de|Joseph Richter (Schriftsteller)}}. He continued to produce it until his death in 1841.<ref name=db/>
