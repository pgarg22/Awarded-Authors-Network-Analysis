'''Jonathan Anthony Stroud''' (born 27 October 1970) is a British writer of [[fantasy fiction]], best known for the [[Bartimaeus Sequence|''Bartimaeus'']] young adult sequence and ''[[Lockwood & Co.]]'' children's series. His books are typically set in an [[alternate history]] London with fantasy elements, and have received note for his satire, and use of magic to reflect themes of class struggle. The ''Bartimaeus'' sequence is the recipient of the [[Grand Prix de l'Imaginaire]] and [[Mythopoeic Fantasy Award]]s. Stroud's works have also been featured on [[ALA Notable lists]] of books for children and young adults.  In 2020, Netflix announced a TV series based on ''Lockwood & Co.'', with filming initiated in July 2021.

==Biography==
Born in 1970 in [[Bedford, England|Bedford]], England, Stroud began to write stories at a very young age.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.jonathanstroud.com/about_author.html|title = About Jonathan Stroud}}</ref> He grew up in [[St Albans]] where he attended Wheatfields Junior School and [[St Albans School, Hertfordshire|St Albans Boys' School]].<ref>{{cite news| title=St Albans gave inspiration to bestselling fantasy writer Jonathan Stroud| url=http://www.stalbansreview.co.uk/leisure/cfufeatures/8901856.St_Albans_gave_inspiration_to_bestselling_fantasy_writer_Jonathan_Stroud/  |website=St Albans & Harpenden Review| date=10 March 2011| first=Melanie |last=Anglesey}}</ref> He enjoyed reading books, drawing pictures, and writing stories. He attributes his love of reading and writing to being ill between the ages of seven and nine. To escape boredom, he would occupy himself with books and stories.<ref name = about>{{cite web |url=http://jonathanstroud.com/about_author.html |title=About Jonathan Stroud |publisher=jonathanstroud.com |access-date=7 March 2010 }}</ref> After he completed his studies of [[English literature]] at the [[University of York]], he worked in London as an editor for the Walker Books store. During the 1990s, he started publishing his own works and quickly gained success.<ref name = about/>

Among his works are the best-selling<ref name = NYTimes>{{cite news |url=https://query.nytimes.com/gst/fullpage.html?res=9D01E0D71F3FF931A25751C0A9609C8B63 |title=New York Times Best Sellers (Children's) February 12, 2006 |work=The New York Times|access-date=19 February 2011 |date=12 February 2006}}</ref> ''[[Bartimaeus Sequence|Bartimaeus Trilogy]]'', which has received note for its satire and use of magic to explore themes of class struggle. By narrating the story from the perspective of [[Bartimaeus (Bartimaeus trilogy)|Bartimaeus]], a sarcastic and slightly egomaniacal [[djinni]], Stroud examines the stereotypes and ethics of the magician class and the enslaved demons.<ref name=independent-2013>{{cite web |url=https://www.independent.co.uk/arts-entertainment/books/reviews/ptolemy-s-gate-by-jonathan-stroud-317146.html |title=Ptolemy's Gate, by Jonathan Stroud |last=Feay |first=Suzi |work=[[The Independent]] |date=29 April 2013}}</ref><ref name=bustle>{{cite web |url=https://www.bustle.com/articles/144237-how-the-bartimaeus-trilogy-gave-me-my-sense-of-humor |title=How The Bartimaeus Trilogy Made Me Love Comedy | last=Ahlin |first=Charlotte |work=[[Bustle (magazine)|Bustle]] |date=26 February 2016}}</ref> The books in this series, his first to be published in the United States, are ''[[The Amulet of Samarkand]]'', ''[[The Golem's Eye]]'', ''[[Ptolemy's Gate]]'', and prequel ''[[The Ring of Solomon]]''.

In 2013, ''[[The Screaming Staircase|Lockwood & Co.: The Screaming Staircase]]'' was released, and achieved critical acclaim, with [[Rick Riordan]] calling Stroud a "genius".<ref>{{cite book|last=Stroud|first=Jonathan|title=The Screaming Staircase|url={{Google books|lfJ9AAAAQBAJ|plainurl=yes}}|year=2013}}</ref> The second book, ''[[The Whispering Skull (novel)|The Whispering Skull]]'', was released in September 2014. A third novel, titled ''The Hollow Boy'', was announced through a competition orchestrated by Stroud, asking readers to send in an idea for a ghost to feature in the third story.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.theguardian.com/childrens-books-site/2014/oct/31/ghost-competition-jonathan-strouds-lockwood-and-co|title=See your ghost in Jonathan Stroud's next book!|first=Jonathan|last=Stroud|date=31 October 2014|website=The Guardian}}</ref> The fourth book, ''The Creeping Shadow'', was published in 2016, and the last book in the series, ''The Empty Grave'', was published in 2017.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bookseriesinorder.com/jonathan-stroud/|title=Jonathan Stroud – Book Series in Order|date=16 January 2017}}</ref>

In late 2020, [[Netflix]] announced that Stroud's ''[[Lockwood & Co.]]'' series would be made into a television show produced by [[Nira Park]], Rachael Prior, and [[Joe Cornish]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://variety.com/2020/tv/global/netflix-uk-original-series-slate-1234852613/|last=Niu|first=Yinchen|title=Netflix Unveils New U.K. Projects With Sam Mendes, Rowan Atkinson, Andy Serkis|date=2020-12-13|access-date=2021-07-17}}</ref> Filming began in July 2021 in London.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://moviesr.net/p-netflix-supernatural-detective-series-lockwood-co-begins-filming|author=Bradley|title=Netflix Supernatural Detective Series 'Lockwood & Co.' Officially Begins Filming|date=2021-07-08|publisher=Midgard Times|access-date=2021-07-17}}</ref>

Stroud lives in [[St Albans]], Hertfordshire, with his three children and his wife Gina,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://jonathanstroud.com/about_me.html|title=About Jonathan Stroud|website=jonathanstroud.com}}</ref> an illustrator of children's books.<ref name = about/>
