'''Tommy Murphy''' (born 1979) is an [[Theatre of Australia|Australian playwright]], [[screenwriter]], adaptor and director . He is best known for his [[Holding the Man (play)|stage]] and [[Holding the Man (film)|screen adaptation]] of Timothy Conigrave's memoir ''[[Holding the Man]]''. His most recent plays are ''[[Mark Colvin's Kidney]]'' and ''[[Packer & Sons]]''.

== Early life ==
Murphy was born in [[Queanbeyan]], [[New South Wales]], Australia,<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://belvoir.com.au/wp-content/uploads/2012/08/Gwen_In_Purgatory_Interview_Tommy_Murphy.pdf|title=An interview with Tommy Murphy|last=|first=|date=|website=Belvoir Theatre|publisher=|access-date=8 January 2017}}</ref> the seventh of eight children<ref name=":1">{{Cite web|url=http://sydney.edu.au/arts/alumni_friends/profiles/tommy-murphy.shtml|title=Alumni in focus : Tommy Murphy 22 May 2015?|last=|first=|date=|website=University of Sydney|publisher=|access-date=8 January 2017}}</ref> in a Catholic family. He is a graduate of the [[University of Sydney]] (BA 2004)<ref name=":1" /> and of the [[National Institute of Dramatic Art]] (Director's course).<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|url=http://australianplays.org/playwright/ASC-424|title=Tommy Murphy [profile]|last=|first=|date=|website=Australian Plays org|publisher=|access-date=8 January 2017}}</ref>

== Career ==
He was a resident writer at [[Griffin Theatre Company]]<ref name=":0" /> 2004–06, for which he wrote ''[[Strangers in Between]]'' and ''[[Holding the Man]]''.  Both plays are published by [[Currency Press]], in one volume. ''Strangers in Between'' won the national 2006 NSW Premier's Literary Award for Best Play, and ''Holding the Man'' won the same Award in 2007. Murphy is the youngest recipient of the award, and the only playwright to win in successive years.<ref name=":0" />

''Holding the Man'' had an encore season at Griffin before transferring to Sydney Opera House, Belvoir, Melbourne Theatre Company and Brisbane's Powerhouse. It played London's West End from 23 April to 3 July 2010. [[Guy Edmonds]] and [[Matt Zeremes]] were joined by new cast members [[Jane Turner]] and [[Simon Burke]]. [[David Berthold]] directed and [[Brian Thomson (scenic designer)|Brian Thomson]] designed. The [[Trafalgar Studios]] season was produced by Daniel Sparrow and [[Mike Walsh (TV host)|Mike Walsh]]. The play has been produced every year since its premiere with new productions in San Francisco, Auckland, Adelaide and encore productions in Brisbane and Sydney. In 2014 the play was also mounted in Los Angeles by The Australian Theatre Company with [[Larry Moss (Acting Coach)|Larry Moss]] directing; Nate Jones, [[Adam J. Yeend]], [[Cameron Daddo]], and [[Roxanne Wilson]] were cast for the production.

''[[Saturn's Return]]'' was commissioned by [[Sydney Theatre Company]] co-artistic directors [[Andrew Upton]] and [[Cate Blanchett]] for STC's Wharf 2 season 2008. In 2009 it transferred to the STC main stage. ''Saturn's Return'' is published by [[Currency Press]].

Murphy's award-winning play, ''Gwen in Purgatory'', was co-produced by [[Belvoir (theatre company)|Belvoir]] and [[La Boite Theatre]] and directed by [[Neil Armfield]] in 2010. The cast for this premiere production were [[Nathaniel Dean]], [[Grant Dodwell]], Sue Ingleton, [[Melissa Jaffer]] and [[Pacharo Mzembe]]. This play is also published by [[Currency Press]].

He was writer-in-residence at [[Belvoir (theatre company)|Belvoir]] 2011–2012. His adaptation of ''Blood Wedding'' was commissioned for the London 2012 Olympiad<ref name=":1" /> and his reworking of ''Peter Pan'' for Belvoir transferred to New York in 2013.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2013/10/08/theater/reviews/peter-pan-from-belvoir-company-at-the-new-victory.html|title=A Magical, Flying Visit From a Magical, Flying Boy 7 Oct 2013|last=|first=|date=7 October 2013|website=New York Times|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=24 April 2017|last1=Saltz|first1=Rachel}}</ref>

After the multi-production international success of the stage play of ''Holding the Man'', Murphy initiated the screen project. He partnered with executive producer Cameron Huang, producer Kylie Du Fresne of [[Goalpost Pictures]], producers of ''[[The Sapphires (film)|The Sapphires]]'' and director [[Neil Armfield]] to see the film to fruition in 2015. It opened to strong domestic box office in Australia and is released globally via [[Netflix]] after an international cinema release. Murphy wrote the screenplay and worked as associate producer for the film.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.imdb.com/title/tt3671542/fullcredits?ref_=tt_ov_st_sm|title=Holding the Man|last=|first=|date=|website=IMDb|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=24 April 2017}}</ref>

Murphy's screen credits include teleplays for ''[[Offspring (TV series)|Offspring]]'', ''[[Spirited]]'', and Matchbox's 2014 Foxtel mini-series ''[[Devil's Playground (TV Series)|Devil's Playground]]''.<ref name=":1" />

Murphy is the recipient of a [[Centenary Medal]] and the British Council Realise Your Dream Award.<ref name=":4">{{Cite web|url=http://www.atyp.com.au/tommy-murphy|title=Tommy Murphy|last=|first=|date=|website=Australian Theatre for Young People|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=23 April 2017}}</ref> In 2007, he had the title of honorary associate conferred by the Faculty of Education & Social Work, University of Sydney.<ref name=":4" /> He also sat on the board of directors of the [[Australian Theatre for Young People]] 2005–2010.<ref name=":4" />

Murphy was commissioned to write a play for [[Black Swan State Theatre Company]] as recipient (joint) of the Richard Burton Award 2012 which carried $15,000 prizemoney .<ref name=":2">{{Cite web|url=https://awg.com.au/view/burtonaward2012/|title=Ingle Knight and Tommy Murphy Win the 2012 Richard Burton Award for Playwriting|last=|first=|date=|website=AWG|publisher=|access-date=8 January 2017}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.bsstc.com.au/blog/2012/october/winners-of-richard-burton-award-for-playwriting-2012-announced/#.WHIneVN97IU|title=Winners of Richard Burton Award for Playwriting 2012 Announced 31 October 2012|last=|first=|date=|website=Black Swan|publisher=|access-date=8 January 2017}}</ref> He is also currently writing for Belvoir Theatre as well as developing a number of screen projects.  Murphy is the 2016 [[University of Queensland]] Drama Creative Fellow.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://communication-arts.uq.edu.au/uq-drama-creative-fellowship-tommy-murphy-gubbins|title=School of Communication and Arts - The University of Queensland, Australia|website=communication-arts.uq.edu.au|date=April 2016|access-date=2016-04-24}}</ref>

The Sydney Theatre Company has awarded Murphy the 2015 Patrick White Fellow worth $25.000.<ref name=":5" /> The gives the playwright a commission and 12 months work at the company.  Each year the fellowship is awarded to an established playwright  and Murphy said of the fellowship that it, "offers a sense of belonging to a pursuit that is often solitary."<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.smh.com.au/entertainment/theatre/tommy-murphy-chosen-as-sydney-theatre-companys-new-patrick-white-fellow-20160521-gp0mql.html|title=Tommy Murphy chosen as Sydney Theatre Company's new Patrick White Fellow|last=|first=|date=23 May 2016|website=Sydney Morning Herald|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=2 August 2017}}</ref>
