'''Susan Tatum Woodhouse''' (born August 7, 1958, [[Suffolk, Virginia]]), known by her pseudonym '''Liz Carlyle''', is an [[United States|American]] writer of [[romance novel]]s since  1999, primarily of historical romances.

==Biography==

===Personal life===
Susan Tatum Woodhouse was born in 1958 in [[Suffolk, Virginia]].<ref name="highbeamn">{{Citation|title=Liz Carlyle at highbeam|url=http://www.highbeam.com/doc/1G2-2286000034.html|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160121090101/https://www.highbeam.com/doc/1G2-2286000034.html|url-status=dead|archive-date=2016-01-21|accessdate=2012-09-18}}</ref> She attended college on a [[Scripps Howard]] writing scholarship and majored in journalism. She is married to Edward Carlyle, and has two stepchildren.<ref name="highbeamn" />

===Career===
She spent much of her career working in [[Human Resources|human resources and labor relations]] in the chemical and automotive industries. She didn't begin writing until December 1996, when she was between jobs. She finished the novel within two months and attempted to find a publisher for it. Although that work did not sell, Pocket Books was interested in seeing more of her work. In 1998 they bought two novels from her, publishing the first, ''My False Heart'', in 1999.<ref name="sue">{{cite web|title=Liz Carlyle|publisher=Sue's Clues|year=2003|url=http://romancereaderatheart.com/clues/mysteryauthors/carlyle|accessdate=2007-07-25|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20070210102821/http://www.romancereaderatheart.com/clues/mysteryauthors/carlyle|archivedate=2007-02-10}}</ref> In 2003, she contributed a novella to the anthology ''Big Guns'', marking her first foray into contemporary romance.<ref name="sue"/>

Carlyle has been nominated for ''Romantic Times'' Reviewers' Choice Awards five times, winning in 1999 for ''My False Heart'' as well as having been nominated for a ''Romantic Times'' Career Achievement Award,<ref>{{cite web|publisher=Romantic Times|title=Author Profile: Liz Carlyle|url=http://www.romantictimes.com/authors_profile.php?author=8492|year=2007|accessdate=2007-07-25}} {{Dead link|date=October 2010|bot=H3llBot}}</ref> and won a [[Romance Writers of America]] RITA Award in 2006 for Best Long Historical Romance (''The Devil to Pay'').<ref>{{Cite web|title=RITA Award Past Winner |publisher=[[Romance Writers of America]] |url=http://www.rwanational.org/cs/contests_and_awards/rita_awards/past_winners |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20070927043913/http://www.rwanational.org/cs/contests_and_awards/rita_awards/past_winners |url-status=dead |archive-date=2007-09-27 |year=2006 |accessdate=2007-07-25 }}</ref>  Several of her books have become ''[[USA Today]]'' bestsellers.<ref name=buonfiglio>{{cite web|last=Buonfiglio|first=Michelle|title=AuthorView:Liz Carlyle|publisher=WNBC New York|url=http://www.wnbc.com/entertainment/9407338/detail.html|date=June 21, 2006|accessdate=2007-07-25}}{{Dead link|date=January 2020 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref>
