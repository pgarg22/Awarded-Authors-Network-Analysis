'''Michaël Arnoldus Slory''' (4 August 1935 in [[Totness, Suriname|Totness]], [[Surinam (Dutch colony)|Surinam]] – 19 December 2018 in [[Paramaribo]], [[Suriname]]) was a [[Surinam]]ese [[poet]]. He mainly wrote poetry in [[Sranan Tongo]], and is considered one of the most important poets in Sranan Tongo.<ref name="parool">{{cite news|url=https://www.parool.nl/nieuws/surinames-grootste-dichter-michael-slory-overleden~bc45ea56/|title=Surinames grootste dichter Michaël Slory overleden|website=[[Parool]]|access-date=17 May 2020|date=10 December 2018|language=nl}}</ref><ref name="anda"/><ref name="dichters"/><ref name="ons_erfdeel"/> He also published in [[Dutch language|Dutch]], English and [[Spanish language|Spanish]]<ref name="parool"/>

==Biography==
In 1947, Slory moved to Paramaribo from his birth place Totness, and went to the Algemene Middelbare School<ref name="75th_birthday"/> which is comparable to [[high school]], but does not qualify for university.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.wur.nl/en/Education-Programmes/Bachelor/Admission/Dutch-prior-education/Dutch-VWO-diploma-and-subject-requirements.htm|title=Dutch VWO diploma and subject requirements|website=[[Wageningen University and Research]]|language=en|access-date=18 May 2020|quote="Only [[Voorbereidend wetenschappelijk onderwijs|VWO]] qualifies for university in the Netherlands, unless you can demonstrate your knowledge"}}</ref> His teacher encouraged him to write not only to in Dutch, but also Sranan Tongo and Spanish. While in high school, he published his first three Dutch poems in the magazine ''Tongoni''.<ref name="75th_birthday">{{cite web|url=https://werkgroepcaraibischeletteren.nl/michael-slory-viert-zijn-75ste-verjaardag/|title=Michaël Slory viert zijn 75ste verjaardag|website=Werkgroep Caraïbische Letteren|access-date=18 May 2020}}</ref>

In 1958, Slory moved to [[Amsterdam]] to study a course in Spanish at a private school. After graduating, he enrolled in the [[University of Amsterdam]] where he joined Vereniging Ons Suriname. His poetry can be found in the [[yearbook]]s of the time.<ref name="zon">{{cite web|url=https://www.dbnl.nl/tekst/slor001ikza01_01/slor001ikza01_01_0001.php|title=Ik zal zingen om de zon te laten opkomen|website=Digital Library for Dutch Literature|access-date=17 May 2020|language=nl|date=1991}}</ref> Ons Suriname was during that period, the centre for the Surinamese nationalist movement.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.at5.nl/artikelen/190769/vereniging-ons-suriname-viert-honderdjarig-bestaan-met-expositie|title=Vereniging Ons Suriname viert honderdjarig bestaan met expositie|website=[[AT5]]|access-date=18 May 2020|language=nl}}</ref> Slory also gained access to the Dutch literary world, and befriended authors like [[Harry Mulisch]] and [[Hugo Claus]].<ref name="parool"/> 

In 1961, he published his first work ''Sarka / Bittere strijd'' under the [[pseudonym]] Asjantenoe Sangodare,<ref name="anda">{{cite web|url=http://www.andasuriname.com/303ges/januari29.html|title=Wat gebeurde er in Januari in Suriname|website=Anda Suriname|access-date=17 May 2020|language=nl}}</ref> with  the [[communist]] publisher Pegasus.<ref name="parool"/> ''Sarka / Bittere strijd'' was bilingual with the poems printed both in Dutch and Sranan Tongo, and expressed his sympathy for [[Fidel Castro]]. The work is dedicated to [[Patrice Lumumba]], and introduced by [[Theun de Vries]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://dpc.uba.uva.nl/cgi/i/image/image-idx?type=boolean;view=reslist;c=surinamica;;rgn1=surinamica_all;q1=Micha%EBl%20Arnoldus%20Slory|title=Suriname 1599-1975 - Sarka - Bittere strijd|website=University of Amsterdam|access-date=17 May 2020}}</ref> His [[scholarship]] was revoked in 1966,<ref name="zon"/> and Slory decided to focus on writing. He published ''Brieven aan de Guerrilla'' (1968), and ''Brieven aan [[Ho Chi Minh|Ho Tsji Minh]]'' (1968).<ref name="anda"/>

In 1970, Slory returned to Suriname and only wanted to write in Sranan Tongo.<ref name="dichters">{{cite web|url=https://www.dbnl.nl/tekst/kemp009suri02_01/kemp009suri02_01_0012.php|title=Surinaamse schrijvers en dichters|website=Digital Library for Dutch Literature|language=nl|access-date=17 May 2020|date=1989}}</ref> Slory started with ''Fraga mi wortoe'' (1970), and 20 more publications followed.<ref name="anda"/> His style started to change as well. The major themes are no longer nationalism and social equality, but love started to become a major theme in his poetry.<ref>{{cite web|title=De brug van Paramaribo naar Willemstad. Nederlands-Caribische en Caribisch-Nederlandse literatuur 1945-2005|url=https://www.dbnl.nl/tekst/rutg014brug01_01/rutg014brug01_01_0050.php|website=Digital Library for Dutch Literature|access-date=17 May 2020|language=nl|date=2007}}</ref> Slory started to become disenchanted with the events after the [[History of Suriname#Independence|independence of Suriname]].<ref name="international"/> In 1982, Slory had another change of mind, and started to write in Dutch and Spanish.<ref name="dichters"/> He released ''Poemas contra la agonía'' (1988),<ref name="anda"/> ''Een andere weg'' (1991) among others. During his lifetime, he published more than 30 works, and several thousand poems.<ref name="ons_erfdeel">{{cite web|url=https://www.dbnl.nl/tekst/_ons003199101_01/_ons003199101_01_0143.php|title=Ons Erfdeel. Jaargang 34|website=Digital Library for Dutch Literature|access-date=17 May 2020|date=1991}}</ref>

In 1985, Slory was awarded the Suriname State Price for Literature for his love poem ''Fresko en Efu na Kodyo''.<ref name="literatuurprijs">{{cite web|url=http://www.indeknipscheer.com/tag/literatuurprijs-van-suriname/|title="Dichtend tegen de klippen op."|website=In de Knipscheer|access-date=17 May 2020|language=nl}}</ref> In 1993, his poem ''Sarka'' was set to music by [[Gerda Geertens]].<ref name="75th_birthday"/> In 1995, [[Nederlandse Programma Stichting|NPS]] created a television program about Michaël Slory ''En nu de droom over is.. de Surinaamse dichter Michael Slory''.<ref name="international">{{cite web|url=https://www.idfa.nl/nl/film/3440fb37-6e9e-4356-8b9f-bb366e5ea7ce/en-nu-de-droom-over-is|title=En nu de droom over is...|website=International Documentary Filmfestival Amsterdam|access-date=17 May 2020|language=nl,en}}</ref> Even though Slory was lauded by literary critics, he always remained well below the [[Poverty threshold|poverty line]],<ref name="parbode"/> and often sold his books in the street.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.literairnederland.nl/2086/|title=Michael Slory Literair Nederland|website=Literair Nederland|access-date=18 May 2020|language=nl}}</ref> Slory was grateful that he received a monthly allowance from [[De Ware Tijd]] for his many contributions to ''DWT-Literair'' after retiring.<ref name="parbode">{{cite web|url=https://parbode.com/michaa-l-slory/|title=Michaël Slory|website=Parbode|access-date=18 May 2020|language=nl}}</ref>

On 19 December 2018, Slory died in Paramaribo,<ref name="parool"/> and on 28 December received a [[state funeral]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.gfcnieuws.com/michael-slory-met-staatseer-begraven/|title=Michael Slory met staatseer begraven|website=GFC Nieuws|access-date=17 May 2020|language=nl}}</ref>
