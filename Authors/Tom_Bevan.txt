'''Tom Bevan''' (1868–1938), who also wrote under the [[pseudonym]] '''Walter Bamfylde''', was a British writer of boys' adventure stories. Originally a schoolteacher, he also wrote non-fiction books for children.

==Life==
Born in [[Monmouthshire]], then still in England, Bevan was educated at [[Sir Thomas Rich's School]], [[Gloucester]], and St Paul's College, [[Cheltenham]], a teacher training college that awarded [[Bristol University]] qualifications.

Bevan began writing historical adventure stories while working as a schoolmaster. In the 1920s he was education editor for [[Sampson Low|Sampson Low and Marston]].<ref name="Watson1992">{{Cite book |author=Benjamin Watson |title=English Schoolboy Stories: An Annotated Bibliography of Hardcover Fiction |url=https://archive.org/details/englishschoolboy0000wats |url-access=registration |year=1992 |publisher=Scarecrow Press |isbn=978-0-8108-2572-7 |page=[https://archive.org/details/englishschoolboy0000wats/page/15 15]}}</ref> He contributed to several boys' magazines, including ''Everybody's Story Magazine'' and ''[[Boy's Own Paper]]''.<ref>Stanley Nichols, "Roving Thoughts on the 'B.O.P.'", ''Golden Hours'' 6:1 (December 1962), p. 81. For example, ''The Baymouth Scouts'' was serialised in the ''Boy's Own Paper'' in 1911–1912. [http://www.philsp.com/homeville/bjsp/s103.htm#A631 British Juvenile Story Papers and Pocket Libraries Index]</ref>

Bevan also wrote non-fiction books for children. His ''Stories from British History: B. C. 54 – A. D. 1485'' discusses, for example, the degree to which [[Shakespearean history|Shakespeare's History Plays]] present historical truth.<ref>Velma Bourgeois Richmond: ''Shakespeare as Children's Literature: Edwardian Retellings in Words and Pictures'' (Jefferson, NC: McFarland & Company Inc., 2008), p. 264 [https://books.google.com/books?id=syOfBQAAQBAJ&pg=PA266&lpg=PA266&dq=Tom+Bevan+writer&source=bl&ots=fB1wKFXsnz&sig=5Ts1kbWZX1OBGiKkKyenNIIcEXA&hl=en&sa=X&ved=0ahUKEwiqmMKruunWAhUGOsAKHdolAeAQ6AEITTAG#v=onepage&q=Tom%20Bevan%20writer&f=false Retrieved 11 October 2017.]</ref>
