'''William Gwenlyn Parry''' (8 June 1932 – 5 November 1991) was a [[Wales|Welsh]] dramatist,<ref name="WAEoW">{{cite book |editor1-first=John |editor1-last=Davies |editor1-link=John Davies (historian)|editor2-first=Nigel |editor2-last=Jenkins |editor2-link=Nigel Jenkins |editor3-first=Baines |editor3-last=Menna |editor4-first=Peredur I. |editor4-last=Lynch |title=The Welsh Academy Encyclopaedia of Wales |year=2008 |publisher=University of Wales Press |location=Cardiff |page=651|isbn=978-0-7083-1953-6}}</ref> the author of several plays in Welsh, including ''Saer Doliau'' (1966), ''Ty ar y Tywod'' (1968), ''Y Ffin (1973), ''Panto'', ''Sal'' and ''Y Tŵr'' (1978).

==Early life==
Parry was born in the Welsh slate-quarrying village of [[Deiniolen]], [[Caernarfonshire]] ([[Gwynedd]]).<ref name="WAEoW"/> He initially worked as a teacher, teaching mathematics at a school in London and later science through the medium of Welsh in [[Bethesda, Wales]].

==Drama==
Parry joined [[BBC Wales]] in 1966 and helped to establish the scripts department, where he worked on Welsh programmes such as ''[[Pobol y Cwm]]''.<ref name="WAEoW"/> He also has writing credits for the TV play ''[[Grand Slam (1978 film)|Grand Slam]]'' and the feature film ''Un Nos 'Ola Leuad'' (based on the book by [[Caradog Prichard]]).

Parry's early work has been called [[Theatre of the Absurd|Absurdist]].<ref>Roger Owen, ''Writers of Wales: Gwenlyn Parry'' (University of Wales Press, 2013) {{ISBN|9780708326626}}</ref> He created and co-wrote the comedy series ''Fo a Fe'' with Rhydderch Jones.<ref>In Welsh [https://www.bbc.co.uk/cymru/cymruaryrawyr/database/rhydderch.shtml Retrieved 2 May 2017.]</ref>

==Family==
Parry was married twice, first to Joy and then to Ann Beynon. He had two daughters from his first marriage and a son and a daughter from his second. He died in the [[Cardiff]] area of South Wales in 1991. He is buried in Macpela, [[Penygroes, Gwynedd]].
