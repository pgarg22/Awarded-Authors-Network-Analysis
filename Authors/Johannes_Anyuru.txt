'''Johannes Anyuru'''  (born 23 March 1979) is a Swedish poet and author.

==Biography==
Anyuru was born in [[Borås]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.svt.se/nyheter/regionalt/smalandsnytt/vaxjo-johannes-anyuru-sommarvard-i-p1|title = Växjö – Johannes Anyuru sommarvärd i P1|date = 8 June 2010}}</ref>  His father is from [[Uganda]] and his mother is Swedish.<ref>SVT 1: ''Babel'' 9 September 2010</ref>

He debuted in 2003 with ''Det är bara gudarna som är nya'' (Only The Gods Are New), a poetry collection. In this collection of poems Anyuru used [[Homer]]'s epic [[Iliad]] as a background and inspiration for the portrayal of [[immigrant]] neighborhoods. A place that is often mentioned in his poetry is the area around ''Mörners road'' in [[Växjö]], where Anyuru lived as a child.<ref>{{cite web|author=Fanny Söderbäck |url=http://www.dn.se/kultur-noje/gamla-gudar-och-nya-1.194714 |archive-url=https://archive.today/20120731182705/http://www.dn.se/kultur-noje/gamla-gudar-och-nya-1.194714 |url-status=dead |archive-date=31 July 2012 |title=Gamla gudar och nya |language=sv |publisher=DN.se |date=7 July 2003 |access-date=28 August 2010 }}</ref> Reviews of this book linked his style to both older contemporary Swedish poets as [[Göran Sonnevi]] or [[Tomas Tranströmer]], and hip hop band [[The Latin Kings (hip hop group)|The Latin Kings]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://tidningenkulturen.se/index.php/13-litteratur/litteratur-kritik/5079-litteratur-johannes-anyuru-staederna-inuti-hall |title= Litteratur: Johannes Anyuru, Städerna inuti Hall |last=Fries |first=Jonna |date=22 September 2009 |website=tidningenkulturen.se |language=sv |access-date=1 June 2015 }}</ref> Anyurus second poetry collection, ''Omega'', is a much more downbeat since it deals with the loss of a close friend to cancer.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.svd.se/kulturnoje/litteratur/varifran-och-varthan_31361.svd |title=Recension: Anyuru, Johannes - Omega (Övrigt) &#124; Litteratur &#124; SvD |newspaper=Svenska Dagbladet |date=18 August 2005 |language=sv |publisher=Svd.se |access-date=28 August 2010|last1=Lundberg |first1=Johan }}</ref> Anyurus third collection, ''Städerna inuti Hall'' (The Cities Inside Hall)<ref>{{cite web|author=Johannes Anyuru  |url=http://www.dn.se/dnbok/utdrag-ur-staderna-inuti-hall-s-27-30/ |title=Utdrag ur "Städerna inuti Hall" s. 27-30 |language=sv |publisher=DN.se |date=16 January 2010 |access-date=28 August 2010}}</ref> was published in 2009 and describes sad socio-political landscape.

His fourth book ''Skulle jag dö under andra himlar'' (If I Were to Die Under Other Skies)<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.bokus.com/bok/9789113022086/skulle-jag-do-under-andra-himlar/ |title=Skulle jag dö under andra himlar - Johannes Anyuru - Bokus bokhandel |publisher=Bokus.com |date=12 August 2010 |access-date=28 August 2010|language=sv}}</ref> is not a poetry book, but [[prose]].

He has also worked with [[spoken word]] as part of the group ''Broken Word'' and a touring performance with [[Riksteatern|National Theatre Company]] called Abstrakt rap. Both projects has been released as CD's.<ref>{{cite web|author=Andreas Ekström |url=http://www.dn.se/kultur-noje/poeten-som-rappar-orimmat-1.324767 |archive-url=https://archive.today/20120729082323/http://www.dn.se/kultur-noje/poeten-som-rappar-orimmat-1.324767 |url-status=dead |archive-date=29 July 2012 |title=Poeten som rappar orimmat |language=sv |publisher=DN.se |date=10 November 2004 |access-date=28 August 2010 }}</ref>

In December 2009 he debuted with his first play ''Förvaret'' (The Detention Centre), at Gothenburg City Theatre, co-written with Aleksander Motturi.<ref>{{cite web|author=Ingegärd Waaranperä ingegard.waaranpera@dn.se |url=http://www.dn.se/kultur-noje/scenrecensioner/forvaret-pa-nya-studion-goteborgs-stadsteater-1.1013619 |title="Förvaret" på Nya Studion Göteborgs Stadsteater |language=sv |publisher=DN.se |date=13 December 2009 |access-date=28 August 2010 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20091217012257/http://www.dn.se/kultur-noje/scenrecensioner/forvaret-pa-nya-studion-goteborgs-stadsteater-1.1013619 |archive-date=17 December 2009 }}</ref>

During the summer of 2010, he was one of several hosts of legendary radio program [[Sommar (radio program)|Sommar]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://sverigesradio.se/sida/avsnitt/126298?programid=2071 |title=Johannes Anyuru - Sommar i P1 &#124; Sveriges Radio |publisher=Sverigesradio.se |date=28 July 2010 |access-date=28 August 2010}}</ref>
