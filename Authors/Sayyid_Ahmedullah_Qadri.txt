'''Sayyid Ahmedullah Qadri''' ( 9 August 1909 – 5 October 1985), known as ''Lisan-ul-Mulk'', was a [[List of Indian writers#S|writer]],<ref>{{cite encyclopedia|title=SHAH TAHIR HUSSAIN|url=http://ismaili.net/heritage/node/10754|encyclopedia=Encyclopaedia of Ismailism|author=Mumtaz Ali Tajddin}}</ref><ref>{{cite book|title=Courtly Encounters: Translating Courtliness and Violence in Early Modern Eurasia.|date=30 October 2012|publisher=Sanjay Subrahmanyam|isbn=9780674067059|page=240|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=F-ub1MSr89QC&q=Courtly+Encounters:+Translating+Courtliness+and+Violence+in+Early+Modern+Eurasia.+sayyid+ahmedullah+qadri&pg=PA240}}</ref> author, critic, [[editor-in-chief]], [[Indian independence activist]], [[India]]n [[politician]] and an acclaimed figure of [[Hyderabad]], India. He was president of [[Lutfuddaulah Oriental Research Institute]]<ref>http://www.ebay.in/itm/DR-NEELAM-SANJIVA-REDDY-GOLDEN-JUBILEE-JULY1963-with-3-Rare-Signatures-of-MPs-/300949552764?pt=IN_Books_Magazines&hash=item4611fdbe7c</ref> Hyderabad, president of Hyderabad Journalist Association,<ref>[[:File:Andhra nama.jpg]]</ref> member of State Library Council.<ref>{{cite web|title=Andhra Nama|url=https://archive.org/details/20130813154002|publisher=Lutfuddaulah oreintal research institute|author=Sayyid Ahmedullah Qadri|location=Hyderabad|year=1958}}</ref>

Qadri was awarded civilian award [[Padma Shri]] in 1966 by the government of India for his work in literature and education.<ref>{{cite web|title=Padma Shri Awardess|url=http://www.archive.india.gov.in/myindia/padmashri_awards_list1.php?start=2170|publisher=india.gov.in}}</ref> He was also a member of Andhra Pradesh State Legislative Council<ref>{{cite web|title=Bahadur Nama|date=March 1967|url=https://archive.org/details/20130813155310|publisher=Lutfuddaulah oreintal research center}}</ref> and chairman of Andhra Pradesh state [[Hajj Committee of India|Hajj Committee]]. Further he was founder and editor-in-chief of the Urdu daily newspaper ''Saltanat''<ref>http://ahmedullahqadri.blogspot.com</ref> and Paisa Akhbar; [http://www.siasat.com/english/news/book-padmashree-ahmedullah-quadri-released] prior to that he was editor in Tarikh Publications, which was founded by his father since 1929.<ref name="lib.uchicago">{{Cite web|url=https://www.lib.uchicago.edu/e/su/southasia/urdujournals.html|title = Major Urdu Journals at the Urdu Research Center|date = 24 December 2008}}</ref>
[[File:Sayyid Ahmedullah qadri Indira Gandhi.jpg|thumb|Padamshri Sayyid AhmedUllah Qadri (M.L.C) With then Prime Minister [[Indira Gandhi]]]]

The Nation awarded him the title of ''Lisanul Mulk'' in 1965. 
Shri Qadri Published English Volume Title ''Jawaharlal the Great''. Prime Minister Shri [[Lal Bahadur Shastri]] Visited the residence of Ahmedullah Qadri to release this great Volume on 21 March 1965.

[[File:Qadri With Bahuguna.jpg|thumb|Qadri, With then Chief Minister of U.P [[Hemvati Nandan Bahuguna]] and other Notables]]

==Early life==

Qadri was born in [[Hyderabad State]] on 9 August 1909 to Shamsullah Qadri and Mahboob Begum Qadri in an academic family that was well known in literary circles. He was the eldest son. He had two younger brothers Imdadullah Qadri and Saadullah Qadri.
