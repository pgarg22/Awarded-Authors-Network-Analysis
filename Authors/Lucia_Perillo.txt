Accessed October 27, 2016. "A decade into her career, and after the publication of two more poetry volumes, she received a 2000 MacArthur fellowship, commonly known as a 'genius grant.'"</ref>

==Life and career==
Perillo was born in [[Manhattan]] on September 30, 1958<ref name="Doty2012">{{cite book|author=Mark Doty|title=The Best American Poetry 2012: Series Editor David Lehman|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=MiD66AN10mkC&q=Lucia+Perillo+1958&pg=PA192|date=18 September 2012|publisher=Simon and Schuster|isbn=978-1-4391-8154-6|page=192}}</ref> and grew up in [[Irvington, New York|Irvington]].<ref name=gates>Gates, Anita. [https://www.nytimes.com/2016/10/26/books/lucia-perillo-dead.html "Lucia Perillo, Whose Illness Shaped Her Poetry, Dies at 58"], ''[[The New York Times]]'', October 25, 2016. Accessed October 26, 2016. "Lucia Maria Perillo was born on Sept. 30, 1958, in Manhattan and grew up in suburban Irvington, N.Y."</ref>

Her work appeared in ''[[The New Yorker]]'',<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.newyorker.com/search|title=New Yorker Search|work=newyorker.com|access-date=24 October 2016}}</ref> ''[[The Atlantic]]'' and ''[[The Kenyon Review]]'',<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.kenyonreview.org/?s=lucia%20perillo|title=Search for "lucia perillo" - The Kenyon Review|work=kenyonreview.org|access-date=24 October 2016}}</ref> among other magazines. A traditional poet of mostly free-verse personal reflection, she wrote extensively about living with [[multiple sclerosis]] in her poems and essays.<ref>{{cite book|last=Perillo|first=Lucia|title=I've Heard the Vultures Singing|year=2007|publisher=Trinity University|location=San Antonio|isbn=978-1-59534-031-3|url-access=registration|url=https://archive.org/details/iveheardvultures0000peri}}</ref> ''Time Will Clean the Carcass Bones'' was her last book of poetry ([[Copper Canyon Press]], 2016). Her 2012 collection of short fiction, ''Happiness is a Chemical in the Brain,'' was shortlisted for the 2013 [[PEN/Robert W. Bingham Prize]]. She died on October 16, 2016 in [[Olympia, Washington]], aged 58.<ref name=gates /><ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.artesianews.com/1375337/poet-and-novelist-lucia-perillo-dies-at-58.html |title=Archived copy |access-date=2016-10-24 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20161024214030/http://www.artesianews.com/1375337/poet-and-novelist-lucia-perillo-dies-at-58.html |archive-date=2016-10-24 |url-status=dead }}</ref>
