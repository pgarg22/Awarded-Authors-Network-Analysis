{{short description|American poet}}
'''David Romtvedt''' is an American [[poet]].

==Life==
He graduated from [[Reed College]],<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.reed.edu/reed_magazine/spring2007/features/what_would_say/romtvedt.html |title=Reed Magazine: Memo to Self |publisher=Reed.edu |date= |access-date=2012-10-31}}</ref> and the [[Iowa Writers' Workshop]].
He teaches at [[University of Wyoming]].<ref>[http://www.uwyo.edu/creativewriting/displayfaculty.asp?facultyid=3948 ] {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100210022358/http://www.uwyo.edu/creativewriting/displayfaculty.asp?facultyid=3948 |date=February 10, 2010 }}</ref> He lives in [[Buffalo, Wyoming]],<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.pw.org/content/david_romtvedt_1 |title=David Romtvedt &#124; Directory of Writers &#124; Poets & Writers |publisher=Pw.org |date=2008-06-09 |access-date=2012-10-31}}</ref> with his wife, the potter [[Margo Brown]].  His daughter, Caitlin Belem, plays Brazilian and Latin music with the band Maracuja.

His work has appeared in ''The Sun Magazine'',<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.thesunmagazine.org/author/1822 |title=Selections by David Romtvedt |publisher=The Sun Magazine |date= |access-date=2012-10-31 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110927160927/http://www.thesunmagazine.org/author/1822 |archive-date=2011-09-27 |url-status=dead }}</ref> ''The American Poetry Review'', ''The Paris Review'', ''Ploughshares'', ''Prairie Schooner'',<ref>[http://muse.jhu.edu/login?uri=/journals/prairie_schooner/v078/78.4romtvedt.html]  {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110608140521/http://muse.jhu.edu/login?uri=%2Fjournals%2Fprairie_schooner%2Fv078%2F78.4romtvedt.html |date=June 8, 2011 }}</ref> ''The Missouri Review'',<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.missourireview.com/content/dynamic/author_detail.php?author_id=1240 |title=TMR: David Romtvedt |publisher=Missourireview.com |access-date=2012-10-31 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110928004609/http://www.missourireview.com/content/dynamic/author_detail.php?author_id=1240 |archive-date=2011-09-28 }}</ref> and the Basque cultural review ''Erle''.

He is a founder and current board member of Worlds of Music.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://wiki.wyomingauthors.org/David+Romtvedt |title=The Wyoming Authors Wiki / David Romtvedt |publisher=Wiki.wyomingauthors.org |access-date=2012-10-31 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20071015003411/http://wiki.wyomingauthors.org/David%20Romtvedt |archive-date=2007-10-15 }}</ref> Romtvedt plays button accordion with the band, The Fireants. They have recorded three CDs: ''Bury My Clothes'', ''Ants on Ice'' and ''It's Hot''. The band plays Latin and Cajun/Zydeco music as well as original music that David Romtvedt has written.

==Awards==
* 1991 [[National Poetry Series]], for ''A Flower Whose Name I Do Not Know''
* Pushcart Prize
* two [[National Endowment for the Arts]] Fellowships
* Wyoming Arts Council literature fellowship
* Wyoming Governor's Arts Award.
