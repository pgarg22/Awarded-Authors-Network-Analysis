'''Catherine Barnett''' (born 1960 in [[San Francisco]])<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.whiting.org/awards/winners/catherine-barnett#/|website=whiting.org|title=Catherine Barnett|accessdate=2020-03-11}}</ref> is an American poet and educator. She is the author of ''Human Hours'' ([[Graywolf Press]], 2018); ''The Game of Boxes'' ([[Graywolf Press]], 2012), winner of the [[James Laughlin Award]]; and ''Into Perfect Spheres Such Holes Are Pierced'' ([[Alice James Books]], 2004), winner of the [[Beatrice Hawley Award]]. Her honors include a [[Whiting Awards|Whiting Award]] and a [[Guggenheim Fellowship]]. She has published widely in journals and magazines including ''[[The American Poetry Review]]'', ''[[Barrow Street (magazine)|Barrow Street]]'', ''[[The Iowa Review]]'', ''[[The Kenyon Review]]'', ''[[The Massachusetts Review]]'', ''[[The New York Review of Books]]'', ''[[The New Yorker]]'', ''[[Pleiades (journal)|Pleiades]]'', ''[[Poetry (magazine)|Poetry]]'', the ''[[Virginia Quarterly Review]]'', and ''[[The Washington Post]]''. Her poetry was featured in ''[[The Best American Poetry]] 2016'', edited by Edward Hirsch.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://bestamericanpoetry.com/archive/?id=30 |title=The Best American Poetry 2016 Table of Contents |accessdate=2018-07-23 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190420071231/http://bestamericanpoetry.com/archive/?id=30 |archive-date=2019-04-20 |url-status=dead }}</ref> Barnett teaches in the graduate and undergraduate writing programs at [[New York University]] and is a distinguished lecturer at [[Hunter College]]. She has also taught at [[Princeton University]], [[The New School]], and [[Barnard College]], where she is a Visiting Poet. She also works as an independent editor. She received her [[Bachelor of Arts|B.A.]] from [[Princeton University]] and an [[Master of Fine Arts|M.F.A.]] from the [[Warren Wilson College]] MFA Program for Writers.
