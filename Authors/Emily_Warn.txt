'''Emily Warn''' is an [[United States|American]] [[poet]]. She was born in [[San Francisco]], grew up in [[Michigan]], and was educated at [[Kalamazoo College]], the [[University of Washington]], and [[Stanford University]]. She moved to the Pacific Northwest 1978 to work for [[North Cascades National Park]], and a year later moved to [[Seattle]] where she has lived, more or less ever since.<ref name="emilywarn.com">[http://emilywarn.com/?page_id=2 Emily Warn<!-- Bot generated title -->] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160303192821/http://emilywarn.com/?page_id=2|date=3 Mar 2016}}</ref>

Her essays and poems have appeared in ''Poetry'', ''[[Parabola (magazine)|Parabola]]'', ''[[The Seattle Times]]'', ''[[The Kenyon Review]]'', ''[[Blackbird (online journal)|Blackbird]]'', ''[[BookForum]]'', ''[[The Bloomsbury Review]]'', and ''[[The Writer's Almanac]]''.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.coppercanyonpress.org/authors/emily-warn/|title = Emily Warn}}</ref>

Her most recent book of poetry, ''Shadow Architect'', was published by [[Copper Canyon Press]] in 2008.

She currently divides her time between Seattle and [[Twisp, Washington]].
