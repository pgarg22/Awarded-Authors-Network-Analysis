'''Géza Röhrig''' ({{lang-hu|Röhrig Géza}}, {{IPA-hu|ˈrøːriɡ ˈɡeːzɒ|pron}}; born May 11, 1967) is a [[Hungary|Hungarian]] actor and poet. He is best known for his role in the 2015 film ''[[Son of Saul]]'',<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.france24.com/en/20150515-cannes-review-son-saul-holocaust-portman-tale-love-darkness-israel|title=Culture - Saul’s inferno: Searing Holocaust drama stuns Cannes festival|work=France 24}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.imdb.com/name/nm6574338/|title=Géza Röhrig|work=IMDb}}</ref> which won the [[Grand Prix (Cannes Film Festival)|Grand Prix]] at the [[2015 Cannes Film Festival]], the [[73rd Golden Globe Awards|Golden Globe]] for [[Golden Globe Award for Best Foreign Language Film|Best Foreign Language Film]] and the [[Academy Award]] for [[Academy Award for Best Foreign Language Film|Best Foreign Language Film]].

==Life and career==
Géza Röhrig was born on May 11, 1967, in [[Budapest]], [[Hungary]]. His mother left the family after he was born, and his father died when he was four, so Röhrig spent his childhood in foster care.<ref name="Híres örökbefogadottak">{{cite web |url=http://orokbe.hu/2016/02/26/orokbefogadas-rohrig-geza-saul-fia/ |title=Híres örökbefogadottak: Röhrig Géza |date=2016-02-29 |accessdate=2016-02-26|publisher=orokbe.hu}}</ref> From the age of 12 he was raised by a Jewish family. In the 1980s, he was the frontman of an [[underground art|underground music]] band called ''Huckleberry'' (also known as ''HuckRebelly''), whose concerts were almost always interrupted by the [[Hungarian People's Republic|communist authorities]]. At university he studied [[Hungarian language|Hungarian]] and [[Polish language|Polish]], and after a visit to [[Auschwitz]] during a study tour in [[Poland]], he decided to become an [[Orthodox Jew]] in [[Brooklyn]]. He portrayed poet [[Attila József]] in a film by [[József Madaras]].<ref name="Magyar Hírlap">{{cite web |url=http://www.jbsz.hu/rocktortenetikonyvek/52-kezdetek-/956-roehrig-geza-ugy-ereztem-udvari-bolondda-kezdek-valni.html |title=Röhrig Géza - Úgy éreztem, udvari bolonddá kezdek válni  |date=1991-04-12 |accessdate=2015-05-16 | publisher= [[Magyar Hírlap]] |work=jbsz.hu }}</ref> He studied filmmaking under [[István Szabó]].<ref name="Magyar Hírlap"/><ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.femcafe.hu/cikkek/vorosszonyeg/saul-fia-cannes-rohrig-geza |author=Brugós Bernadette |title=Röhrig Géza, a Saul fia sztárja: halottmosdatással foglalkozik New Yorkban |date=2016-02-29 |accessdate=2015-06-10|publisher=femcafe.hu}}</ref>

He published two collections of poems on the theme of the [[Shoah]], ''{{lang|hu|Hamvasztókönyv}}'' (literally "Book of Incineration", 1995) and ''{{lang|hu|Fogság}}'' ("Captivity", 1997).<ref>{{cite web|url=http://magyarnarancs.hu/film2/csak_ezt_az_egy_regenyt_kell_megirnom_rohrig_geza_kolto-57610|title=Magyar Narancs - Film - "Csak ezt az egy regényt kell megírnom" (Röhrig Géza költő)|work=magyarnarancs.hu}}</ref> He graduated from the [[Academy of Drama and Film in Budapest]] with a degree in [[filmmaking]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://nol.hu/kultura/20130213-eleszto_es_irodalom-1366367|title=Kultúra: Élesztő és irodalom - NOL.hu|author=Szemere Katalin|date=February 13, 2013|work=NOL.hu}}</ref> Since 2000, he has lived in the [[The Bronx|Bronx]] borough of [[New York City]],<ref>Antony Lane, "Deep and Dark," ''The New Yorker'', December 21, 2015: 227</ref> where he received a degree from the [[Jewish Theological Seminary of America|Jewish Theological Seminary]] and has been a kindergarten teacher at Hannah Senesh Community Day School in Brooklyn.<ref>{{Cite web|url = http://www.tabletmag.com/scroll/191325/the-kindergarten-teacher-who-won-cannes|title = The Kindergarten Teacher Who Won Cannes|date = May 29, 2015|accessdate = |website = Tablet|publisher = |last = Davidzon|first = Vladislav}}</ref> He is married, and has four children.<ref name="Híres örökbefogadottak"/> He has published many collections of poetry.<ref name="multesjovo.hu">{{cite web|url=http://www.multesjovo.hu/en/konyvkiado/tallozo/torveny.html|title=Múlt és Jövő Kiadó -  Törvény - All Categories - Publishing House|work=multesjovo.hu}}</ref>
