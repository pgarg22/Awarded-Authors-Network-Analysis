'''Walter Robert McDonald''' (born July 18, 1934 in [[Lubbock, Texas|Lubbock]], [[Texas]]) is an [[United States|American]] [[poet]] and former [[professor]].<ref>[http://www.ttup.ttu.edu/BookPages/0896724875.html Texas Tech University Press - The Waltz He Was Born For: An Introduction to the Writing of Walt McDonald<!-- Bot generated title -->]</ref><ref>[http://www.lib.utexas.edu/taro/ttusw/00204/tsw-00204.html Walter McDonald: An Inventory of His Papers, 1945-2003 and undated, at the Southwest Collection/Special Collections Library<!-- Bot generated title -->]</ref><ref>[http://www.faculty.english.ttu.edu/mcdonald/ McDonald Home Page<!-- Bot generated title -->]</ref><ref>[http://www.acu.edu/campusoffices/acupress/books/authors/walt_mcdonald.html Walt McDonald - Abilene Christian University<!-- Bot generated title -->]</ref> He served as [[Poet Laureate of Texas]] in 2001. In May 2002, he retired from [[Texas Tech University]] as "Paul Whitfield Horn Professor of [[English literature|English]]" and "Poet in Residence".

==Career==
After graduating from [[Texas Technological College]] and service in the [[United States Air Force]] in Vietnam, McDonald received a [[Doctor of philosophy|PhD]] from the [[University of Iowa]].

McDonald's 23 books of [[poetry]] -- published by Harper & Row (now HarperCollins) and university presses such as Ohio State; Notre Dame; Massachusetts; Pittsburgh; Texas Christian; Texas Tech -- include ''Faith Is A Radical Master,'' 'Night Landing,' and ''A Thousand Miles of Stars''.<ref>[http://www.albany.edu/offcourse/summer02/wmcdonald.html Two Poems by Walter McDonald<!-- Bot generated title -->]</ref> Before joining the faculty at Texas Tech University, he was a [[Aviator|pilot]] in the Air Force and also taught at the [[United States Air Force Academy|Air Force Academy]].

Some of his experiences as a pilot are reflected in his verses.
