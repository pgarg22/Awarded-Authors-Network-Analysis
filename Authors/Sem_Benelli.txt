'''Sem Benelli''' (August 10, 1877 â€“ December 18, 1949) was an [[Italy|Italian]] playwright, essayist and [[libretto|librettist]].  He provided the texts for several noted Italian operas, including [[Italo Montemezzi]]'s ''[[L'amore dei tre re]]'' and ''[[L'incantesimo]]'', and [[Umberto Giordano]]'s ''[[La cena delle beffe]]'', based on Benelli's own [[The Jester's Supper (play)|play of the same title]].  He was a native of [[Prato]].

The play ''[[The Jester's Supper (play)|The Jester's Supper]]'' was a great New York theatre success in 1919 under the title ''The Jest'', starring [[Lionel Barrymore|Lionel]] and [[John Barrymore]]. In Italy [[Paola Pezzaglia]] was considered the best male protagonist of ''La cena delle beffe'', after an incident in 1913 when the leading man collapsed before playing the character of Giannetto; she took his place achieving resounding success. Ever since Pezzaglia carried on doing the same role again and again.

He wrote the screenplay for the 1942 film ''[[The Jester's Supper (film)|The Jester's Supper]]'' based on his own famous play.
