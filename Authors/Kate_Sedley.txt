'''Kate Sedley''' is the pen-name of '''Brenda Margaret Lilian Clarke (n√©e Honeyman)''', an English [[historical novelist]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://news.google.com/newspapers?id=L0IgAAAAIBAJ&sjid=vX8EAAAAIBAJ&pg=6311,2963791&dq=kate-sedley&hl=en|title=Impostor or not and tragedy resurrected|last=Philips|first=Helen|date=December 17, 2001|work=[[Sarasota Herald-Tribune]]|page=4B|accessdate=1 June 2011}}</ref> She was born in [[Bristol]] in 1926 and educated at [[The Red Maids' School]], [[Westbury-on-Trym]]. She is married and has a son and a daughter, and three grandchildren. Her medieval [[historical whodunnit]]s feature Roger the Chapman, who has given up a monk's cell for the freedom of peddling his wares on the road.
