'''Tim Waggoner''' is the [[author]] of numerous novels and short stories in the [[Fantasy]], [[horror fiction|Horror]], and [[Thriller (genre)|Thriller]] genres.

{{TOC limit|limit=3}}

==Education==
Waggoner graduated from [[Wright State University]] in 1989 with a [[Master of Arts]] in English<ref>Thrasher, Don (April 25, 2004). "An exciting new chapter: Area novelist to have a number of his works published in 2004", ''[[Dayton Daily News]]'', p. F3.</ref> with a [[Creative Writing]] Concentration.{{Citation needed|date=June 2013}}

He holds BS ed. and MA degrees from [[Wright State University]].<ref name="wizardsbio"/>

==Career==
Waggoner has written and published novels for both adult and young readers, including ''[[Temple of the Dragonslayer]]'' and ''[[Return of the Sorceress]]'' (both for [[Wizards of the Coast]]), ''Dark Ages: Gangrel'' and ''Exalted: A Shadow Over Heaven's Eye'' (both White Wolf), ''Necropolis'' (Five Star), and ''Defender: Hyperswarm'' (I-Books).<ref name="wizardsbio"/> He is also the author of the short story collection ''All Too Surreal'' (Prime Books).<ref name="wizardsbio"/> He has published numerous short stories in the fantasy and horror genres, and his articles on writing have appeared in ''[[Writer's Digest]]'', ''Writers' Journal'', ''New Writer's Magazine'', ''Ohio Writer'', ''[[Speculations (magazine)|Speculations]]'', and ''Teaching English in the Two-Year College''.<ref name="wizardsbio">{{cite web|url=http://ww2.wizards.com/Books/Wizards/Bios/default.aspx?doc=TimWaggoner|title=Tim Waggoner|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20090624123824/http://ww2.wizards.com/Books/Wizards/Bios/default.aspx?doc=TimWaggoner|archivedate=June 24, 2009}}</ref> He has also written the ''Nekropolis'' series of urban fantasies and the ''Ghost Trackers'' series written in collaboration with Jason Hawes and Grant Wilson of the ''Ghost Hunters'' television show. His books for writers include ''The Art of Writing Genre Fiction'', written in collaboration with Michael Knost, and ''Writing in the Dark,'' a guide to writing horror and dark fantasy fiction.

A number of his stories have received honorable mentions in various editions of the [[Year's Best Fantasy and Horror]].<ref name="wizardsbio"/> He won first place in the 1998 Authorlink! New Author Awards Competition and was a finalist for the [[Darrell Awards|Darrell Award]] for Best MidSouth Short Story in 1999.<ref name="wizardsbio"/> His novella ''The Men Upstairs'' was nominated for the 2011 [[Shirley Jackson Award]],<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.shirleyjacksonawards.org/award-winners/2011-shirley-jackson-awards-winners/|title=The Shirley Jackson Awards » 2011 Shirley Jackson Awards Winners|website=Shirleyjacksonawards.org|accessdate=5 January 2018}}</ref> and his short story "How to be a Horror Writer" was nominated for the 2018 Shirley Jackson Award.{{cn|date=September 2020}} His novella ''The Winter Box'' won the 2016 [[Bram Stoker Award]] for Superior Achievement in Long Fiction.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://horror.org/bram-stoker-award-winners-announced/|title=Bram Stoker Award® Winners Announced - Horror Writers Association BlogHorror Writers Association Blog|website=Horror.org|accessdate=5 January 2018}}</ref> His how-to-write-horror book ''Writing in the Dark'' won the 2020 [[Bram Stoker Award]] for Superior Achievement in Nonfiction, and his article "Speaking of Horror" won the 2020 Bram Stoker Award for Superior Achievement in Short Nonfiction.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Templeton |first1=Molly |title=Announcing the 2020 Bram Stoker Awards Winners |url=https://www.tor.com/2021/06/01/announcing-the-2020-bram-stoker-awards-winners/ |website=Tor.com |access-date=1 June 2021 |date=2021-06-01}}</ref>

==Teaching==
He serves as a professor of English and teaches composition and creative writing at [[Sinclair Community College]] in [[Dayton, Ohio]].<ref name="wizardsbio"/><ref name=Short>{{cite news|author=Short, Sharon|date=March 24, 2013|title=Local author Waggoner: Writing was in his script|newspaper=Dayton Daily News|page=D4}}</ref> Waggoner also taught creative writing for many years at [[Seton Hill University]], [[Pennsylvania]], in an innovative [[low-residency program|low-residency]] [[Master of Fine Arts]] degree program in Writing Popular Fiction.

==Personal life==
[[File:Tim Waggoner at lake.jpg|right|thumb|upright|Waggoner in 2018]]
Waggoner grew up in the [[Dayton, Ohio]], area.<ref name=Short /> In addition to writing fiction, Waggoner has worked as a newspaper reporter, magazine editor, and copy editor.<ref name="wizardsbio"/> He has two daughters from a previous marriage.<ref name="wizardsbio"/>
