'''William Benedict Nicholson''', [[Order of the British Empire|OBE]], [[Fellow of the Royal Society of Literature|FRSL]]<ref>{{cite web |date=30 December 2014 |newspaper=[[BBC News]] |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/entertainment-arts-30625196 |access-date=31 December 2014 |title=New Year Honours: John Hurt receives a knighthood}}</ref> (born 12 January 1948) is a [[British people|British]] screenwriter, playwright, and novelist who has been nominated twice for an [[Academy Award|Oscar]].<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.theguardian.com/books/2011/oct/09/william-nicholson-golden-hour-review |title=The Golden Hour by William Nicholson-review |date=8 October 2011 |newspaper=[[The Guardian]] |last=Groskop|first=Viv |author-link=Viv Groskop |access-date=25 March 2012}}</ref>

==Early life==
A native of [[Lewes]], Sussex, William Nicholson was raised in a [[Roman Catholic]] family in Gloucestershire. By the time he reached his tenth birthday he had decided to become a writer. He was educated at [[Downside School]], [[Somerset]], and [[Christ's College, Cambridge|Christ's College]], [[Cambridge]].<ref name="interviewqb">{{cite web|url=http://www.quercusbooks.co.uk/blog/2011/06/23/interview-william-nicholson/|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110718235057/http://www.quercusbooks.co.uk/blog/2011/06/23/interview-william-nicholson/|url-status=dead|archive-date=18 July 2011|title=Interview: William Nicholson|publisher=quercusbooks.co.uk|access-date=1 April 2012}}</ref>

==Career==
At the start of his career Nicholson worked for the [[BBC]] as a director of [[documentary film]]s with numerous works to his credit<ref name="all movie">{{cite web|url= http://www.allmovie.com/artist/william-nicholson-p167072|title=William Nicholson biography|publisher=[[Allmovie]]|last=Buchanan|first=Jason|access-date=25 March 2012}}</ref> between the mid-1970s and mid-1980s. He gained renown as a novelist and playwright when the first book of his popular ''[[Wind on Fire|Wind On Fire]]'' trilogy won the [[Blue Peter]] best book award and the Smarties Gold Award for best children's book. He has written several novels and fantasy books.

He married author [[Virginia Nicholson]] (née Bell) in 1988.{{citation needed|date=April 2020}}

===Screenplays and theatre===

He has twice been nominated for [[Tony Awards]] for best play, for ''[[Shadowlands (play)|Shadowlands]]'' and ''[[The Retreat from Moscow]]''. He also turned ''Shadowlands'', based on the relationship between [[C. S. Lewis]] and [[Joy Gresham]], into a [[BBC-TV]] play in 1985, and an acclaimed film in 1993. The latter starred [[Anthony Hopkins]] and [[Debra Winger]] and was directed by [[Richard Attenborough]]. His other screenplays include ''[[Nell (film)|Nell]]'' (1994), ''[[First Knight]]'' (1995) and ''[[Grey Owl (film)|Grey Owl]]'' (1999). He later worked as a writer on the [[Academy Awards|Academy Award]] winning epic ''[[Gladiator (2000 film)|Gladiator]]'' (2000), and wrote and directed the 1997 film ''[[Firelight]]''.

In 2007, Nicholson co-wrote ''[[Elizabeth: The Golden Age]]'', from an earlier script by [[Michael Hirst (writer)|Michael Hirst]].

In 2012, Nicholson adapted ''[[Les Misérables (musical)|Les Misérables]]'' as a [[Les Misérables (2012 film)|film]].

===Film and TV nominations and awards===
William Nicholson's first nomination came in 1989 when [[BAFTA]] TV Awards included the 1987 teleplay ''Sweet as You Are'', which he co-wrote with Ruth Caleb and Angela Pope, on its list of candidates for 'Best Single Drama'. His next nominations were for 1994's [[Shadowlands (1993 film)|''Shadowlands'']] when he was a contender for both a BAFTA and an Oscar for 'Best Adapted Screenplay'. 1997 was another successful year, with an 'Outstanding Writing for a Miniseries or a Special' [[Emmy]] nomination for the 1996 TV drama ''[[Crime of the Century (1996 film)|Crime of the Century]]''. He was also singled out at the [[San Sebastian International Film Festival]] for ''[[Firelight]]'', with a nomination for the 'Golden Seashell' Award and a win of the "Special Prize of the Jury."

2000 turned out to be Nicholson's most impressive year to date, with acclaim for the [[Academy Award for Best Picture|Best Picture]] Oscar winner [[Gladiator (2000 film)|''Gladiator'']]. He had nominations for the Sierra Award from the Las Vegas Film Critics Society Awards and the [[Saturn Award for Best Writing|Saturn Award]] from the [[Academy of Science Fiction, Fantasy & Horror Films]], followed by 'Best Screenplay' nominations from both BAFTA and Oscar.

He was appointed [[Officer of the Order of the British Empire]] (OBE) in the 2015 New Year Honours for services to drama and literature.<ref>{{London Gazette|issue=61092 |supp=y|page=N14|date=31 December 2014}}</ref><ref>[https://www.gov.uk/government/uploads/system/uploads/attachment_data/file/391413/New_Year_Honours_List_2015.pdf 2015 New Year Honours List]</ref>
