'''Sheri Holman''' (born 1966) is an American novelist and screenwriter.

==Biography==
Holman was born in [[Hanover County, Virginia]]. Following graduation from [[The College of William & Mary]] in 1988 with a degree in theatre, she moved to [[New York City|New York]]. After transitioning from acting to various positions in the publishing industry, including several years as a temp at [[Penguin Books]], Holman became the assistant to literary agent Molly Friedrich.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://davidabramsbooks.blogspot.com//2011/03/my-first-time-sheri-holman.html/|title=The Quivering Pen|accessdate=6 February 2017|url-status=dead|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20151022123532/http://davidabramsbooks.blogspot.com/2011/03/my-first-time-sheri-holman.html|archivedate=22 October 2015}}</ref> It was during this time that she began writing her first novel, ''A Stolen Tongue'', a mystery set along the route of a fifteenth-century religious pilgrimage. The debut novel was published by [[Grove/Atlantic, Inc.|Grove/Atlantic]] in 1997 and subsequently translated into thirteen languages. This was followed by the bestselling<ref>[https://www.publishersweekly.com/pw/print/20030728/19229-sheri-holman-guarding-perfection-flaws-and-all.html Publishers Weekly profile]</ref> ''The Dress Lodger'', named a [[New York Times Notable Book|''New York Times'' Notable Book of the Year]] in 2000, and a nominee for the International IMPAC Dublin Literary Award.

After publishing a young adult title in 2002, Holman returned with ''The Mammoth Cheese'' in 2003, which was a finalist for the UK [[Orange Prize for Fiction]]. Her most recent novel is ''Witches on the Road Tonight'', named a ''New York Times'' Editors' Choice<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2011/04/03/books/review/EdChoice-t.html|title=Editors’ Choice|date=3 April 2011|work=The New York Times|accessdate=6 February 2017}}</ref> and to the best fiction of 2011 lists of the ''[[Boston Globe]]''<ref>[https://www.bostonglobe.com/arts/books/2011/12/18/best-year-fiction/GHddsyAduqJ4psFVW4jgcJ/story.html/ Best fiction books of 2011] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20151012105406/https://www.bostonglobe.com/arts/books/2011/12/18/best-year-fiction/GHddsyAduqJ4psFVW4jgcJ/story.html |date=2015-10-12 }}</ref> and ''[[The Globe and Mail]].''<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.theglobeandmail.com/arts/books-and-media/the-globe-100-the-very-best-books-of-2011/article4179464/|title=The Globe 100: The very best books of 2011|publisher=|accessdate=6 February 2017}}</ref> She is at work on a new novel involving a pediatric health worker in Eldoret, Kenya.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.sheriholman.com/blog/sally-test-pediatric-center-a-glimpse-into-the-new-novel/|title=Sally Test Pediatric Center -- a glimpse into the new novel|accessdate=6 February 2017|url-status=dead|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20160305085624/http://www.sheriholman.com/blog/sally-test-pediatric-center-a-glimpse-into-the-new-novel|archivedate=5 March 2016}}</ref>

Holman is a writer and producer on ''[[Filthy Rich (2020 TV series)|''Filthy Rich'']]'', premiering on [[Fox Broadcasting Company|Fox]] in spring 2020, as well as the upcoming [[Fox 21 Television Studios]]/[[National Geographic (TV network)|National Geographic Channel]] series ''[[Barkskins (TV series)|''Barkskins'']]'', based on the [[Annie Proulx]] [[Barkskins|novel]].<ref>[http://www.sheriholman.com/television.html Sheri Holman: Television]</ref> She spent three seasons writing on [[Longmire (TV series)|''Longmire'']] for [[Warner Horizon Television]], which premiered on [[Netflix]] in Fall, 2015.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://powderkegwriters.wordpress.com/2011/11/14/hello-world/|title=PowderKeg Writers|date=14 November 2011|publisher=|accessdate=6 February 2017}}</ref> She served as a staff writer on [[Emerald City (TV series)|''Emerald City'']] on [[NBC]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.hollywood.com/tv/6858249/emerald-city#credits/|title=Emerald City - TV Series - 2016|first=Hollywood.com|last=Staff|date=5 February 2015|publisher=|accessdate=6 February 2017}}</ref> ''The Crooked Road'', her television adaptation of ''Witches on the Road Tonight'', is in development by [[Universal Television]]. Holman teaches in the Goldberg Department of Dramatic Writing at [[NYU Tisch]]<ref>{{cite web|url=https://tisch.nyu.edu/about/directory/dramatic-writing/905053467|title=Tisch Directory}}</ref>

She is a founding member<ref>[http://themoth.org/posts/15-years-of-the-moth/ Celebrating 15 Years of The Moth] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130117154118/http://themoth.org/posts/15-years-of-the-moth |date=2013-01-17 }}</ref> of the storytelling collective [[The Moth]] and serves on its curatorial board. Her stories have been featured on The Moth Podcast and the [[Peabody Award]]-winning Moth Radio Hour.

Holman lives with her family in [[Brooklyn]], New York.<ref>[http://www.hercircleezine.com/2011/09/15/qa-with-sheri-holman—author-of-witches-on-the-road-tonight/ Fear & Its Monsters: An Interview with Sheri Holman]</ref>
