'''Patrick James Rothfuss''' (born June 6, 1973) is an American writer of [[epic fantasy]]. He is best known for his projected trilogy ''[[The Kingkiller Chronicle]]'', which has won him several awards, including the 2007 [[Quill Award]] for his [[debut novel]], ''[[The Name of the Wind]]''. Its sequel, ''[[The Wise Man's Fear]]'', topped [[The New York Times Best Seller list|''The New York Times'' Best Seller list]].

== Early life ==
Patrick Rothfuss was born in [[Madison, Wisconsin]], and received his B.A. in English from the [[University of Wisconsin–Stevens Point]] in 1999.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://locusmag.com/2010/08/patrick-rothfuss-worldbuilder/ |title=Patrick Rothfuss: Worldbuilder |work=[[Locus (magazine)|Locus]] |date=August 12, 2010 |access-date=December 13, 2017}}</ref> He contributed to ''The Pointer'', the campus paper,<ref name="CSG"/> and produced a widely circulated parody warning about the [[Goodtimes Virus]].<ref>"Legend/AntiLegend: Humor as an Integral Part of the Contemporary Legend Process", in ''Rumor Mills: The Social Impact of Rumor and Legend'', ed. Gary Alan Fine, Veronique Campion-Vincent, and Chip Heath, pp. 131-33. New York: [[Aldine de Gruyter]]. {{ISBN|978-0-202-30747-3}}</ref> He taught part-time at Stevens Point.<ref name="PR_Bio"/> In 2002, he received a master's degree in arts and English from [[Washington State University]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=WSU alum nails two fantasy bestsellers; third coming|url=https://news.wsu.edu/news/2013/02/27/wsu-alum-nails-two-fantasy-bestsellers-third-coming/|access-date=2021-10-24|website=WSU Insider|language=en-US}}</ref> He won the [[Writers of the Future]] 2002 Second Quarter competition with "The Road to Levenshir," an excerpt from his then-unpublished novel ''[[The Wise Man's Fear]]''.<ref name="WoF"/>

== Career ==

=== Writing ===
In 2006, Rothfuss sold his novel ''[[The Name of the Wind]]'' to [[DAW Books]], which was released in 2007. It won a Quill Award (for Science Fiction, Fantasy and Horror)<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.wnbc.com/thequills/14078987/detail.html |website=WNBC.com ([[Internet Archive]]) |title=2007 Quill Award Winners |archive-date=20 February 2008 |access-date=29 October 2019 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080220114638/http://www.wnbc.com/thequills/14078987/detail.html}}</ref> and was listed among ''[[Publishers Weekly]]''{{'}}s Books of the Year. It also won an [[Alex Award]] in 2008.<ref>{{Cite web |url= http://www.ala.org/yalsa/booklistsawards/bookawards/alexawards/alex08 |website= ala.org |title= 2008 Alex Awards |publisher=Young Adult Library Services Association, American Library Association |date=14 January 2008 |access-date=29 October 2019}}</ref> An illustrated tenth anniversary edition was published in 2017.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Rocket|first=Stubby the|date=2017-05-11|title=The Name of the Wind Celebrates Its 10th Anniversary with a Gorgeous Illustrated Edition|url=https://www.tor.com/2017/05/11/patrick-rothfuss-the-name-of-the-wind-illustrated-10th-anniversary-edition-art-by-dan-dos-santos/|access-date=2021-03-29|website=Tor.com|language=en-US}}</ref> Its sequel, ''[[The Wise Man's Fear]]'', was published in March 2011 and reached No. 1 on the New York Times Hardback Fiction Best Seller List.<ref>{{Cite web |url= https://www.nytimes.com/books/best-sellers/2011/03/20/hardcover-fiction/ |title= Hardcover Fiction Books - Best Sellers |date= March 20, 2011 |work= The New York Times| via= NYTimes.com}}</ref>

''[[The Slow Regard of Silent Things]],'' an illustrated novella, was published in October 2014 as a companion story for ''[[The Kingkiller Chronicle]]'', centring on the character Auri.

Rothfuss has also released two stories set in the same world as ''The Kingkiller Chronicle'' in anthologies. The first was "How Old Holly Came To Be", published in ''[[Unfettered (anthology)|Unfettered]]'' in June 2013. The second was the novella ''The Lightning Tree'', released in ''[[Rogues (anthology)|Rogues]]'' in June 2014, featuring the character Bast. The whole anthology was nominated for the 2015 [[World Fantasy Award for Best Anthology]].<ref>{{cite web| url= http://www.sfadb.com/World_Fantasy_Awards_2015|title=World Fantasy Awards 2015|date=November 8, 2015|website=[[Science Fiction Awards Database]]|publisher=Locus Science Fiction Foundation|access-date=March 2, 2016}}</ref>

In July 2020, Rothfuss's editor and publisher [[Betsy Wollheim]] responded publicly on her Facebook account to an article speculating on reasons why ''The Doors of Stone'', the concluding volume of the trilogy, had not been published,<ref name=newsweek-2020>{{Cite web|last=Whalen|first=Andrew|date=2020-07-27|title="Kingkiller Chronicle" editor believes author hasn't written anything for years|url=https://www.newsweek.com/kingkiller-chronicle-editor-believes-author-hasnt-written-anything-years-1520812|url-status=live|access-date=2021-03-29|website=Newsweek|language=en}}</ref> saying she had "never seen a word of book three" and that she didn't think Rothfuss had written anything since 2014.<ref name=newsweek-2020/> The post has since been deleted.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Flood|first=Alison|title=First George RR Martin, now Patrick Rothfuss: the curse of sequel-hungry fans|url=https://www.theguardian.com/books/booksblog/2020/jul/29/first-george-rr-martin-now-patrick-rothfuss-the-curse-of-sequel-hungry-fans|url-status=live|website=The Guardian|date=July 29, 2020|access-date=October 1, 2021}}</ref>

=== Charity ===
Rothfuss organizes the charity Worldbuilders, which, since 2008, has raised over $11.5 million, primarily for [[Heifer International]], a charity that provides livestock, clean water, education, and training for communities in the developing world.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://worldbuilders.org/our-story/history/|title= Our Story - History| website= worldbuilders.org}} Retrieved May 15, 2020.</ref><ref>{{cite web |url= https://worldbuilders.org/|title= Geeks Doing Good| website= worldbuilders.org}} Retrieved May 15, 2020.</ref> By 2020, Worldbuilders had raised over $10 million in support of Heifer.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Author Pat Rothfuss on Fundraising for Heifer International via Worldbuilders, Joy in a Dark Time and What It Means to Be a Geek|url=https://www.heifer.org/blog/culture/author-pat-rothfuss-on-fundraising-for-heifer-international-via-worldbuilders.html|access-date=2021-03-29|website=Heifer International|language=en}}</ref>

=== Podcasts ===
In August 2012, Rothfuss began a monthly podcast, ''The Story Board'', on fantasy, featuring authors such as [[Terry Brooks]] and [[Brandon Sanderson]].<ref>{{cite web|url= https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PL7atuZxmT957LDLBa4uMnJc8k6m5I1ZBz|title= The Story Board|publisher= Geek & Sundry|url-status= dead|archive-url= https://web.archive.org/web/20130814082719/http://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PL7atuZxmT957LDLBa4uMnJc8k6m5I1ZBz|archive-date= August 14, 2013|via= YouTube|access-date= January 3, 2019}}</ref> ''The Story Board'' ran for eight episodes.

In June 2015, he and Max Temkin started a podcast, ''Unattended Consequences'', then named ''Untitled Patrick Rothfuss''.<ref>{{Cite web| url= https://www.stevenspointjournal.com/story/life/2016/05/18/rothfuss-podcast-starts-second-season/84534036/|title=Rothfuss podcast starts second season| website= StevensPointJournal.com |publisher= Stevens Point Journal Media|language=en|access-date=April 24, 2019}}</ref> The podcast concluded in 2018.

=== Roleplaying and games ===
In 2014, Rothfuss began collaborating with [[James Ernest]] to create an abstract strategy game called [[Tak (game)|Tak]] based on the game featured in his book ''[[The Wise Man's Fear]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url= http://cheapass.com/node/215|title= Tak Abstract Strategy Game |archive-url= https://web.archive.org/web/20160515123650/http://cheapass.com/node/215 |archive-date=May 15, 2016|url-status=dead|access-date=May 30, 2016}}</ref>

Rothfuss has played a character named Viari in the [[Penny Arcade]]'s live [[Dungeons & Dragons]] games known as Acquisitions Inc. from Season 7 onward, as well as a guest role in its spin-off show "The 'C' Team". He also role-played as guest character Kerrek in [[Geek and Sundry]]'s show ''[[Critical Role]]'' episode 56, "Hope," and again in episodes 81–84.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://geekandsundry.com/critical-role-episode-56/|title=Critical Role: Episode 56 - Hope|website=Geek and Sundry|access-date=September 27, 2016}}</ref> He also recorded a letter his character wrote which was heard in episode 69, "Passed Through Fire".<ref>{{cite web| title=Critical Role: Episode 69 - Passed Through Fire|url=http://geekandsundry.com/critical-role-episode-69/|website=Geek and Sundry|access-date=September 30, 2016}}</ref>

Rothfuss was a guest on [[Wil Wheaton]]'s ''[[TableTop (web series)|Tabletop]]'', playing ''[[Lords of Waterdeep]]'' on Episode 10 of Season 2, which he won.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=D7DvEIfrP84|title=Lords of Waterdeep: Felicia Day, Pat Rothfuss, and Brandon Laatsch Join Wil on TableTop SE2E10 | publisher= Geek & Sundry| access-date=January 3, 2019|via=YouTube}}</ref>

He was a member of the Story Design team for [[inXile]]'s ''[[Torment: Tides of Numenera]]'' game.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.pcgamer.com/torment-tides-of-numenera-interview-with-colin-mccomb-and-patrick-rothfuss/ |title=Torment: Tides of Numenera interview with Colin McComb and Patrick Rothfuss|last=Hafer|first=T. J.| date=April 4, 2013|website=PC Gamer|language=en-US|access-date=April 24, 2019}}</ref>
