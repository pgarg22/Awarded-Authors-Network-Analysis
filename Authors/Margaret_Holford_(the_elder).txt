'''Margaret Holford''' (1757–1834, sometimes known as "the elder")<ref name=orlando/> was an English novelist, playwright, and poet of the late 18th century. Both she and her daughter, likewise [[Margaret Holford]], were accomplished authors.

==Life and family==
Born Margaret Wrench in [[Chester]] in 1757, she later married Allen Holford of [[Davenham]].<ref>{{Cite book |last=Slagle |first=Judith Bailey |title=Romantic Appropriations of History |year=2012 |publisher=Fairleigh Dickinson University Press |location=Maryland |page=8}}</ref> She died in Chester in November 1834.<ref name=orlando>{{Cite web |last=Brown |first=Susan |title=Orlando Project |url=http://orlando.cambridge.org/public/svPeople?person_id=holfma |accessdate=19 June 2013}}</ref>

Her daughter, likewise [[Margaret Holford]] (1778–1852), was also an accomplished author.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Radcliffe |first=David |title=ENGLISH POETRY 1579–1830: SPENSER AND THE TRADITION |url=http://spenserians.cath.vt.edu/authorrecord.php?action=GET&recordid=33367 |accessdate=19 June 2013 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20140108094450/http://spenserians.cath.vt.edu/authorrecord.php?action=GET&recordid=33367 |archivedate=8 January 2014}}</ref> Their works are said to be "often confused in catalogues and dictionary entries".<ref name=odnb>{{Cite encyclopedia |first=Kathryn |last=Sutherland |title=Holford , Margaret (bap. 1778, d. 1852) |work= Oxford Dictionary of National Biography |publisher=Oxford University Press |year=2004 |url=http://www.oxforddnb.com/view/article/13450 |accessdate=20 June 2013 |url-access=subscription}}</ref>
