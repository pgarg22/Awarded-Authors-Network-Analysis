'''Scott McPherson''' (October 13, 1959 [[Columbus, Ohio]] â€“ November 7, 1992 [[Chicago]]) was an American [[playwright]].<ref>{{cite news| url=https://www.nytimes.com/1992/11/08/obituaries/scott-mcpherson-actor-33-author-of-a-hit-stage-play.html?pagewanted=1| title=Scott McPherson; Actor, 33, Author of a Hit Stage Play| author= BRUCE LAMBERT| date=November 8, 1992| work=The New York Times}}</ref>

==Life==
He graduated from [[Ohio University]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.glhalloffame.org/index.pl?item=22&todo=view_item |title=Archived copy |accessdate=2010-01-02 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20101005080405/http://glhalloffame.org/index.pl?todo=view_item&item=22 |archivedate=2010-10-05 }}</ref>
In 1981, he moved to [[Chicago]], where he acted in ''The Normal Heart'', and ''The House of Blue Leaves''.  He joined the play writing group Chicago New Plays.
His partner was activist and cartoonist Daniel Sotomayor.<ref>{{Citation
  | last = Smith
  | first = Sid

</ref>
He died of complications from [[HIV/AIDS|AIDS]] on November 7, 1992, aged 33.<ref>{{cite book|editor-last1=Nelson|editor-first1=Emmanuel Sampath|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=tzuahdj0Q9EC&lpg=PA313|title=Contemporary gay American poets and playwrights: an A-to-Z guide|location=Westport, CT & London|publisher=Greenwood Publishing Group| year= 2003| isbn= 978-0-313-32232-7 }}</ref>

Ohio University named a new theater space for him. 
The [[Goodman Theatre]] and the [[Victory Gardens Theater]] have established an annual playwriting award in his name.<ref>{{cite book| url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/wp-srv/style/longterm/movies/review97/fmarvinsroom.htm| title= A Door Left Ajar in 'Marvin's Room'| author= David Richards| work=The Washington Post | date=January 5, 1997 }}</ref>

==Family==
The son of Leo McPherson and Peggy Sansbury, he had the following siblings: Bret and Mark McPherson, Steve Sansbury, Hugh Sansbury, Susan MacDowell, Ellen Oatney and Cathy Hargett.{{citation needed|date=February 2020}}
