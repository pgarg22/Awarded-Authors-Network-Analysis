'''Barbu Ștefănescu Delavrancea''' ; pen name of '''Barbu Ștefan'''; April 11, 1858 in [[Bucharest]] &ndash; April 29, 1918 in [[Iași]]) was a [[Romania]]n writer and poet, considered one of the greatest figures in the [[National awakening of Romania]].

==Early life and studies==
Barbu Ștefănescu Delavrancea was born on April 11, 1858 in the village of Delea Nouă, now a suburb of [[Bucharest]].<ref>{{cite book |title=Delavrancea |last=Săndulescu |first=Al. |publisher=Albatros |location=Bucharest |date=1975 |language=Romanian}}</ref> He was the ninth child of Ștefan Tudorică Albu and Iana (Ioana). His father originates in [[Vrancea County|Vrancea]].<ref name="tititudorancea">{{cite web |url=http://www.tititudorancea.ro/z/biografie_barbu_stefanescu_delavrancea.htm |title=Barbu Ștefănescu-Delavrancea |last=Tudorancea |first=Titi |language=Romanian}}</ref> Assigned to [[Sohatu]], [[Ilfov]], he leaves Vrancea for [[Bucharest]] and becomes guildmaster of carters transporting grain from the scaffolds of [[Giurgiu]] and [[Oltenița]]. Barbu's mother was the daughter of widow Stana from Postovari, on the Filipescu estate.<ref name="tititudorancea" />

He spent the first years of life with his father, then learned to read and write with deacon Ion Pestreanu from St. George the New Church.<ref>{{cite book |title=Dicționar biografic de istorie a României |last=Stan |first=Stoica |publisher=Meronia |location=Bucharest |date=2008 |language=Romanian}}</ref> In 1866, Barbu is enrolled in the School of boys no. 4 directly in the second grade. Educator Spirache Dănilescu add the father's surname suffix "-escu", and thus the future writer bears the name Barbu Ștefănescu.<ref name="enciclopedia">{{cite web |url=http://enciclopediaromaniei.ro/wiki/Barbu_%C5%9Etef%C4%83nescu_Delavrancea |title=Barbu Ștefănescu Delavrancea |work=Enciclopedia României |language=Romanian}}</ref> In 1867 he transfers to the Royal School, where will follow the third and fourth classes. He follows high school at Gheorghe Lazăr, first class, and the other seven at St. Sava.<ref>{{cite book |title=Pe urmele lui Delavrancea |last=Milicescu |first=Șt. Emilia |publisher=Sport-Turism |location=Bucharest |date=1986 |language=Romanian}}</ref> In 1878 is enrolled in the Faculty of Law in Bucharest. After sustaining license (1882) goes to specialization in Paris, but fails to get his doctorate.<ref name="enciclopedia" />

==Literary activity== 
[[File:Delavrancea, Musca şi Piciorul d-luĭ Filipescu, Foaia Populară, 14 mai 1900.JPG|thumb|left|Cartoon targeting Barbu Ştefănescu Delavrancea.]]

Due to his extremely laborious work, in 1912 he became a titular member of the [[Romanian Academy]]. Barbu Ștefănescu Delavrancea unfolds a varied activity: substitute teacher at the Faculty of Letters of the [[University of Bucharest]], journalist, lawyer (is famous the ''Caion'' trial, filed to [[Ion Luca Caragiale]] in conjunction with the paternity of drama ''The Scourge'', when in the courtroom, to listen to the arguments of lawyers, was entered only upon invitation), writer (novelist and playwright).<ref name="crispedia">{{cite web|url=http://www.crispedia.ro/Barbu_Stefanescu_Delavrancea_-_biografia__viata__activitatea_si_opera_literara |title=Barbu Ștefănescu Delavrancea - biografia, viața, activitatea și opera literară |work=crispedia.ro |language=Romanian |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20150402145531/http://www.crispedia.ro/Barbu_Stefanescu_Delavrancea_-_biografia__viata__activitatea_si_opera_literara |archivedate=2015-04-02 }}</ref>

His publicistic activity consists in the collaboration with newspapers ''[[România Liberă]]'' and ''Epoca'' (since 1884), whose editor was;<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.ro.biography.name/scriitori/8-romania/22-barbu-s-delavrancea-1858-1918 |title=Barbu S. Delavrancea (1858-1918) |work=Biography.name |language=Romanian}}</ref> in 1887 leads, for a short period, ''Lupta Literară'', and the following year becomes editor of [[Bogdan Petriceicu Hasdeu]]'s magazine ''Revista Nouă'' and collaborator to ''Democrația'' and ''Voința națională''; since 1893 starts working with ''Literatura și știința'' of [[Constantin Dobrogeanu-Gherea]]. Other publications that worked over time were: ''Revista Literară'', ''Familia'', ''Românul''.<ref name="crispedia" />

In literature debuts in 1877 with patriotic poem ''Stante'', part of volume ''Poiana lungă'', signed Barbu, and in 1883 debuts as novelist with ''Sultănica'', follows ''Bunicul'', ''Bunica'', ''Domnul Vucea'' and, especially, ''Hagi Tudose'' (1903). In the following year publishes under the pseudonym "Delavrancea". Drawing on folk, he published several tales: ''Neghiniță'', ''Palatul de cleștar'', ''Dăparte, dăparte'', ''Moș Crăciun'', etc.<ref name="crispedia" />

Barbu Ștefănescu Delavrancea is widely known especially for his historical trilogy: ''Apus de soare'' (1909), ''Viforul'' (1910), ''Luceafărul'' (1910), full of romantic breath.

==As politician==
As a politician, is distinguished by holding several dignities:
*Mayor of Bucharest (1899–1901),
*Minister of Public Works (December 29, 1910 – March 27, 1912),
*Minister of Industry and Trade (since July 10, 1917), deputy.<ref>{{cite book |title=Dicționarul scriitorilor români |last1=Zaciu |first1=Mircea |last2=Papahagi |first2=Marian |last3=Sasu |first3=Aurel |publisher=Albatros |volume=II |location=Bucharest |date=1998 |language=Romanian}}</ref>
