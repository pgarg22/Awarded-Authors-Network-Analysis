'''Guillaume de Lorris''' (c. 1200{{spaced ndash}}c. 1240) was a [[France|French]] scholar and [[poet]] from [[Lorris]]. He was the author of the first section of the ''[[Roman de la Rose]]''.<ref name="Lorris)Meun)1970">{{cite book |author1=Guillaume (de Lorris)|author2=Jean (de Meun)|title=Roman de la Rose|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=QI35TgEACAAJ|accessdate=6 January 2011|year=1970|publisher=Librairie Honor√© Champion}}</ref> Little is known about him, other than that he wrote the earlier section of the poem around 1230, and that the work was completed forty years later by [[Jean de Meun]].<ref>{{EB1911|volume=12|wstitle=Guillaume de Lorris}}</ref> He is only known by mention of Jean de Meun, (de Meung in French), in <nowiki>''Roman de la Rose''</nowiki>. <ref>{{Cite web|url=https://dlmm.library.jhu.edu/en/digital-library-of-medieval-manuscripts/|title=Digital Library of Medieval Manuscripts|language=en-US|access-date=2020-02-17}}</ref> 

Lorris' poem was translated into Middle English verse by [[Geoffrey Chaucer|Chaucer]], and into Modern English verse by [[Frederick Startridge Ellis|F. S. Ellis]].
