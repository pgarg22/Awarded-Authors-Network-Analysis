{{nihongo|'''Nobori Kiuchi'''|木内 昇|Kiuchi Nobori}} is a [[List of Japanese women writers|Japanese writer]] of historical fiction. She has won the Shibata Renzaburo Prize, the [[Chūōkōron]] Literary Prize, and the [[Naoki Prize]]. 

==Early life==
Kiuchi was born in 1967 in [[Tokyo]], Japan. She attended [[Chuo University]], and upon graduation took a publishing job editing various magazines, including the Japanese version of the American teen magazine ''[[Sassy (magazine)|Sassy]]''.<ref name=webdoku /> She started her own magazine, then quit the publishing job to work as a freelance writer and editor.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://life.oricon.co.jp/news/83968/|language=japanese|title=「第144回芥川賞・直木賞」は共に2作品　7年ぶり4名選出|work=Oricon Life|publisher=[[Oricon]]|date=January 17, 2011|access-date=August 4, 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180808135936/https://life.oricon.co.jp/news/83968/|archive-date=August 8, 2018|url-status=live|df=mdy-all}}</ref><ref name=bfj />

from the [[Edo period]] to the [[Shōwa period]], was published.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://ddnavi.com/review/23018/a/|language=japanese|title=2008年12月号 『茗荷谷の猫』木内昇|work=Da Vinci News|date=November 6,
