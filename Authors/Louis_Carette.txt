'''Félicien Marceau''' (16 September 1913 – 7 March 2012) was a French novelist, playwright and essayist originally from Belgium. His real name was '''Louis Carette'''. He was close to the [[Hussards (literary movement)|Hussards]] right-wing literary movement, which in turn was close to the monarchist movement .<ref name=belgtour /><ref name=acadfr>{{cite web |url=http://www.academie-francaise.fr/immortels/base/academiciens/fiche.asp?param=653 |title=Félicien Marceau |work=academie-francaise.fr |year=2011 |access-date=14 August 2011 |language=fr |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110916163312/http://www.academie-francaise.fr/immortels/base/academiciens/fiche.asp?param=653 |archive-date=16 September 2011}}</ref> He was born in [[Kortenberg]], [[Flemish Brabant]].<ref name=belgtour>{{cite web |url= http://www.belgiumtheplaceto.be/happy_famous_belgians_introduction.php?idCat=50&idFamous=84 |title=Famous Belgians |work=belgiumtheplaceto.be |year=2011 |access-date=14 August 2011}}</ref>

Marceau received the [[Prix Goncourt]] for his book ''[[Creezy]]'' ({{ISBN|0714507083}}) in 1969. On 27 November 1975 he was elected to the [[Académie française]], succeeding [[Marcel Achard]].<ref name=acadfr /><ref>{{cite web |url= http://www.evene.fr/celebre/biographie/felicien-marceau-352.php |title=Félicien Marceau  |work=evene.fr |year=2011 |access-date=14 August 2011|language=fr}}</ref> In 1974, [[Goudji]] created the academician's sword for Félicien Marceau.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://exposureroom.com/members/mawi/d7e0ccfb23ae4959ad9ab5a665aba0a4/ |title=L'epee d'academicien de Felicien Marceau, par Goudji By Marc Wittmer on ExposureRoom |work=exposureroom.com |year=2011 |access-date=14 August 2011 |language=fr |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20111005205044/http://exposureroom.com/members/mawi/d7e0ccfb23ae4959ad9ab5a665aba0a4/ |archive-date=5 October 2011}}</ref>
