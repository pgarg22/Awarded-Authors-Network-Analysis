'''John F. Deane''' (born 1943 on [[Achill Island]]) is an [[Ireland|Irish]] [[poet]] and [[novelist]].<ref>[http://www.writerscentre.ie/centre/anthology/deane.html Anthology of Irish Writing<!-- Bot generated title -->] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20071118201640/http://www.writerscentre.ie/centre/anthology/deane.html |date=2007-11-18 }}</ref> He founded ''Poetry Ireland'' and ''The Poetry Ireland Review'' in 1979.<ref>[http://www.carcanet.co.uk/cgi-bin/indexer?owner_id=170 Carcanet Press - John F. Deane<!-- Bot generated title -->]</ref><ref>{{Cite web |url=http://irishliterature.library.emory.edu/content.php?id=deane_10016191 |title=Finding Aid : John F. Deane papers, 1971-2000 : Irish Literary Collections<!-- Bot generated title --> |access-date=2007-10-26 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110719192101/http://irishliterature.library.emory.edu/content.php?id=deane_10016191 |archive-date=2011-07-19 |url-status=dead }}</ref>

==Personal life==
Deane was educated at the Jesuit run boarding school [[Mungret College]], Limerick, and [[University College Dublin]] studying English and French, he had been training to become a Spiritan priest, in Killshane, Tipperary and Holy Ghost College, [[Kimmage Manor]] but became a teacher.<ref>[http://www.ricorso.net/rx/az-data/authors/d/Deane_JF/life.htm John F Deane - Life] www.ricorso.net</ref> Deane married Barbara Sheridan. Barbara was the daughter of music hall performer [[Cecil Sheridan]], they had two children, Barbara died of Lupus aged 37.<ref>[https://www.independent.ie/entertainment/books/book-reviews/earnest-memoir-intrigues-but-lacks-an-occasional-indiscreet-aside-31115267.html Earnest memoir intrigues but lacks an occasional indiscreet aside] Entertainment, Irish Independent, 5 December 2019.</ref> Deane was married again in 1984 to Ursula Foran of Drumkeelanmore, Drumshanbo Co Leitrim, and they have one daughter, Mary, born 1985.{{fact|date=November 2021}}

==Career==
Deane published several collections of [[poetry]] and some fiction.<ref>[http://www.irishwriters-online.com/johnfdeane.html John F. Deane<!-- Bot generated title -->] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20071017232908/http://www.irishwriters-online.com/johnfdeane.html |date=2007-10-17 }}</ref>  He won the [[O’Shaughnessy Award]] for Irish Poetry, the [[Marten Toonder Award for Literature]] and poetry prizes from [[Italy]] and [[Romania]]. Deane was elected Secretary-General of the European Academy of Poetry in 1996. Shortlisted for both the [[T. S. Eliot Prize]] and the [[Irish Times Poetry Now Award]], he won residencies in [[Bavaria]], [[Monaco]] and [[Paris]]. He is a member of [[Aosdána]], the body established by the [[Arts council|Arts Council]] to honour artists "whose work had made an outstanding contribution to the arts in Ireland". In 2007, the French Government honoured him by making him [[Ordre des Arts et des Lettres|''Chevalier, Ordre des Arts et des Lettres'']].
