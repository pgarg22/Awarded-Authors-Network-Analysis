'''Andrea Cheng''' (September 19, 1957 – December 26, 2015)<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.happybirthdayauthor.com/2012/09/happy-birthday-andrea-cheng-september-19.html|title=Happy Birthday Andrea Cheng – September 19|last=VanRaepenbusch|first=Eric|access-date=April 11, 2019}}</ref><ref name=":0">{{Cite web|url=https://blog.leeandlow.com/2016/01/14/remembering-childrens-author-andrea-cheng/|title=Remembering Children's Author Andrea Cheng|last=hannahehrlich|date=January 14, 2016|website=Lee & Low Blog|access-date=April 11, 2019}}</ref> was a Hungarian-American author of [[Children’s books|children's books]] and poet, best known for her Anna Wang Series of [[middle grade]] novels and ''Shanghai Messenger''. She has written over 25 books, spanning genres like Picture books, [[Young adult fiction|young adult]], [[Chapter book]]s, non-fiction, and poetry.

== Personal life ==
Cheng was born in [[El Paso, Texas|El Paso]], Texas.<ref name=":0" /> She was raised in [[Cincinnati]], Ohio, as the child of Hungarian immigrant parents who originally came from Australia and emigrated in 1954.<ref name=":1">{{Cite web|url=http://www.andreacheng.com/about/school-and-family|title=School and Family {{!}} Andrea Cheng|website=www.andreacheng.com|access-date=April 11, 2019}}</ref><ref name=":2">{{Cite web|url=http://www.andreacheng.com/about/about-andrea|title=About Andrea {{!}} Andrea Cheng|website=www.andreacheng.com|access-date=April 11, 2019}}</ref> Cheng was raised in a predominantly African-American neighborhood and was the youngest of three siblings.<ref name=":2" /><ref name=":1" /> She grew up speaking both English and Hungarian.<ref name=":2" />

She graduated with a bachelor's degree in Teaching English as a Second language from [[Cornell University]] in 1979.<ref name=":2" /><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.publishersweekly.com/pw/by-topic/childrens/childrens-authors/article/69065-obituary-andrea-cheng.html|website=Publishers Weekly|access-date=April 11, 2019 | title=Obituary Andrea Cheng}}</ref> After graduation, she apprenticed to become a bookbinder and studied French and taught English in Switzerland.<ref name=":2" /> After she returned from Switzerland, she studied at Cornell University to receive her master's degree in Linguistics.<ref name=":2" />

In addition to writing books, she taught English as a Second Language as well as Children's Literature at [[Cincinnati State Technical and Community College]].<ref name=":2" />

== Death ==
Cheng died of breast cancer on December 26, 2015 at the age of 58, after a long illness.<ref name=":0" /><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://conniewooldridge.com/a-bittersweet-interview-connie-wooldridge-and-the-late-andrea-cheng/|title=A Bittersweet Interview – Listen to an Interview with Connie Wooldridge and the Late Andrea Cheng {{!}} Connie Wooldridge – Author|last=Wooldridge|first=Connie Nordhielm|date=January 31, 2017|website=Connie Nordhielm Wooldridge|access-date=April 11, 2019}}</ref> Two of her manuscripts were published posthumously.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.andreacheng.com/news|title=News {{!}} Andrea Cheng|website=www.andreacheng.com|access-date=April 11, 2019}}</ref>
