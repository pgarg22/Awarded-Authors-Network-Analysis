'''Christine James''' (born 2 February 1954) is a Welsh poet and academic. She served as the first female [[Archdruid]] of Wales from June 2013 until June 2016.<ref name="BBC">[https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-wales-18563191 Eisteddfod names Christine James first woman archdruid, BBC News, 23 June 2012]</ref> She first presided over the ceremonies at the National Eisteddfod in the 2013 Eisteddfod in [[Denbigh]].<ref name="BBC"/> Like all holders, she qualified by winning a major literary prize at a previous National Eisteddfod. She was elected Cofiadur (Recorder) of the Gorsedd of Bards at the National Eisteddfod in 2017, again as the first woman to hold the post. She is a recipient of the [[Wales Book of the Year]].

==Life and academic career==
Christine Mumford was born 2 February 1954,<ref>{{Cite web |title=E-mail from Christine James, 5 Sep. 2001 :‡b (b. 2 Feb. 1954; maiden name: Christine Mumford) |url=https://viaf.org/processed/LC%7Cno2001070899 |website=viaf.org |accessdate=1 January 2019}}</ref> in [[Tonypandy]], [[Rhondda]], and educated at [[Porth]] County Grammar School. Later at [[Aberystwyth University]] she gained a first-class honours degree in Welsh and was subsequently awarded a PhD for a thesis on the [[Laws of Hywel Dda]].

Mumford currently lives in [[Cardiff]] and is an emeritus professor in the Department of Welsh, [[Academi Hywel Teifi]], [[Swansea University]].<ref name="BBC"/> She is an elected [[Fellow of the Learned Society of Wales]] and of the [[Academi Gymreig]] (the [[Welsh Academy]]).

==Poetry==
She won the Crown at the 2005 National Eisteddfod for a poetry collection inspired by works of art, including a number in the [[National Museum Cardiff]].<ref name="Williams2010">{{Cite book |author=Daniel G Williams |title=Aneurin Bevan and Paul Robeson: socialism, class and identity |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=WNzPIk7UydUC&pg=PA8 |accessdate=13 October 2012 |date=20 August 2010 |publisher=Institute of Welsh Affairs |isbn=978-1-904773-53-5 |pages=8–}}</ref>

Her first volume of poetry, ''Rhwng y Llinellau'', was published by Cyhoeddiadau Barddas in 2013 and launched at the National Eisteddfod in Denbigh. It was awarded the prize for the best volume in the Poetry category in the Wales Book of the Year 2014 competition.
