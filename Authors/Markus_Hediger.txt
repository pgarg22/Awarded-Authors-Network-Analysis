'''Markus Hediger''' (born 31 March 1959) is a Swiss writer and translator.

==Life==
Markus Hediger was born in Zürich and brought up in [[Reinach, Aargau]]. From 1980 to 1990 he studied [[French literature]], [[literary criticism]] and [[Italian literature]] at [[University of Zurich]].

At the age of 16 he went to Paris for the first time. In 1979 he met there the Lebanese poet and playwright [[Georges Schehadé]] who opened to him new poetical horizons and whom he visited regularly until his death in 1989. In Switzerland he became friends with the writers [[Erika Burkart]], [[Hugo Loetscher]], [[Alice Rivaz]] and [[Walter Vogt (writer)|Walter Vogt]].

In 1996 Markus Hediger published his first book of poetry, ''Ne retournez pas la pierre''. This was followed by ''En deçà de la lumière'' in 2009.

In 2011 he was invited to the [[International Poetry Festival of Rosario]]<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://fipr.com.ar/2011/edicionesanteriores/2011/p_markus-hediger.html |title=Archived copy |access-date=March 11, 2016 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170311091731/http://fipr.com.ar/2011/edicionesanteriores/2011/p_markus-hediger.html |archive-date=March 11, 2017 |url-status=dead }}</ref> ([[Argentina]]), in 2014 to the [[International Poetry Festival of Medellín]]<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://poesiamedellin.tumblr.com/post/90859659067/markus-hediger-suiza|title = Markus Hediger (Suiza)}}</ref><ref>https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Q4_9i-vT_x0</ref> ([[Colombia]]) and in 2016 to the International Poetry Festival of Lima.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://fiplima.com/poeta/hediger/ |title=Archived copy |access-date=May 31, 2016 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160920044003/http://fiplima.com/poeta/hediger/ |archive-date=September 20, 2016 |url-status=dead }}</ref><ref>https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Ofzw-ECh0MU</ref>
