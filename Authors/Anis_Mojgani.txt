'''Anis Mojgani''' ([[Persian language|Persian]]: انیس مژگانی) (born June 13, 1977) is an American [[spoken word]] poet, visual artist and musician based in Portland, Oregon. Mojgani has been characterized as a "geek genius" with "fiercely hopeful word arias."<ref name="beat">{{cite episode

== Early life and education ==
Mojgani was born and raised in [[New Orleans]], Louisiana. He later moved to [[Georgia (U.S. state)|Georgia]] and graduated from the [[Savannah College of Art and Design]] with a [[Bachelor of Fine Arts]] in [[Sequential Art|sequential art]] and a [[Master of Fine Arts]] in performing arts.<ref name="seattle">{{cite web  |author=SeattlePoetrySlam.org  | title = Apr 16- 3rd SemiFinal Slam & Featured Poet Anis Mojgani  | work = Events  | publisher = SeattlePoetrySlam.org  | url = http://www.seattlepoetryslam.org/2007/09/11/apr-16-3rd-semifinal-slam-featured-poet-tbd/  | access-date = April 8, 2008  | archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20080723111213/http://www.seattlepoetryslam.org/2007/09/11/apr-16-3rd-semifinal-slam-featured-poet-tbd/| archive-date = July 2008}}</ref><ref>{{cite book

==Career==
Over the course of his career, Mojgani has been a member of several poetry slam teams. He was also a member of 2007's Solomon Sparrow's Electric Whale Revival and 2008's Junkyard Ghost Revival, both of which featured poets [[Buddy Wakefield]] and [[Derrick C. Brown]] as actively-touring members.

Mojgani cites several influences: "[[Jeffrey McDaniel]], [[Richard Brautigan]], [[Charles Bukowski]], [[Gregory Corso]], [[Kerouac]], [[MF DOOM]], [[Aesop Rock]], [[Robert Rauschenberg]], [[Jean-Michel Basquiat|Basquiat]], [[Chris Ware]], [[Frank Miller (comics)|Frank Miller]], [[Saul Williams]], [[Walt Whitman]], [[Savannah, Georgia]], New York City, [[New Orleans]], being broke, being a [[hermit]], ''[[Shoot the Piano Player]]'', the [[Baháʼí literature#Authoritative|Baháʼí writings]] and [[Baháʼí history|history]]."<ref name="interview">{{Cite news

Mojgani was named in the book ''Words in Your Face: A Guided Tour Through Twenty Years of the New York City Poetry Slam'' as one of the poets who "saw success" after leaving New York City's notoriously competitive [[poetry slam]] community.

His book ''The Feather Room'' was a 2011 [[National Book Award]] nominee.<ref>{{cite web| title =ANIS MOJGANI • Full CV| publisher = Anis Mojgani | url=http://g.virbcdn.com/_f/files/3f/FileItem-103169-mojganicombinedcv.pdf | access-date = May 20, 2012}}</ref> Mojgani is featured performing a live poem in "Heartbreak Dreamer," the first song on [[Mat Kearney]]'s 2015 album ''[[Just Kids (album)|Just Kids]]''.

On April 27, 2020, it was announced that Oregon Governor [[Kate Brown]] had appointed Mojgani as the 10th [[Oregon Poet Laureate|Oregon poet laureate]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://pamplinmedia.com/pt/9-news/464468-376649-portland-author-anis-mojgani-named-oregon-poet-laureate|title=Portland author Anis Mojgani named Oregon Poet Laureate|last=Vondersmith|first=Jason|website=[[Pamplin Media Group]]|language=en-gb|access-date=April 28, 2020}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.lagrandeobserver.com/news/state/news-around-the-state/article_13e6587e-88b0-11ea-8a60-8b5f245f6722.html|title=News around the state|website=The Observer|language=en|access-date=April 28, 2020}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.opb.org/news/article/anis-mojgani-oregon-poet-laureate/|title=Anis Mojgani named Oregon's 10th Poet Laureate|last=Tonthat|first=Steven|website=opb.org|language=en|access-date=April 28, 2020}}</ref>
