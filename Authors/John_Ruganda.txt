'''John Ruganda''' (30 May 1941 to 8 December 2007) was [[Uganda|Uganda's]] best known [[playwright]]. Beyond his work as a playwright, Ruganda was also a professor at University of North, South Africa, [[University of Nairobi]], and [[Makerere University]].<ref>{{cite web|last=African Books Collective|title=John Ruganda|url=http://www.africanbookscollective.com/authors-editors/john-ruganda|accessdate=3 May 2011}}</ref>

He was born in [[Fort Portal]] and died in Uganda's capital [[Kampala]]. 

Ruganda's plays "reflect the reality of the East African sociopolitical situation after independence."<ref>{{cite web|last=Imbuga|first=Francis|title=John Ruganda|url=http://galenet.galegroup.com/servlet/GLD/hits?r=d&origSearch=true&o=DataType&n=10&l=d&c=5&locID=mtlib_4_1051&secondary=false&u=CA&u=CLC&u=DLB&t=KW&s=1&NT=%22African%22|work=Gale Database: Dictionary of Literary Biography|accessdate=3 May 2011}}</ref>  He was considered a shaping force of East African theater.<ref>{{cite web|last=Ssenkaaba|first=Stephen|title=Ruganda: The passing of a literary giant|url=http://www.newvision.co.ug/D/8/25/602615|work=New Vision Online|accessdate=3 May 2011|date=18 December 2007|url-status=dead|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20120922010943/http://www.newvision.co.ug/D/8/25/602615|archivedate=22 September 2012}}</ref> ''The Burdens'' (1972) and ''The Floods'' (1980) have become a regular part of curriculum in literature classes.<ref>{{cite web|last=East African Educational Publishers Ltd.|title=John Ruganda Passes On|url=http://www.eastafricanpublishers.com/News/John%20Ruganda.htm|accessdate=3 May 2011|url-status=dead|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20101129095319/http://www.eastafricanpublishers.com/News/John%20Ruganda.htm|archivedate=29 November 2010}}</ref>
