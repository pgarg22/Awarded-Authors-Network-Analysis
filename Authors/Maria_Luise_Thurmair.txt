'''Maria Luise Thurmair''' née Mumelter (27 September 1912 – 24 October 2005) was a German [[Catholic Church|Catholic]] theologian, [[hymnodist]] and writer. She contributed the lyrics of many hymns when the Catholic hymnal ''[[Gotteslob]]'' was first published in 1975.

== Career ==
Maria Luise Mumelter was born in [[Bolzano|Bozen]], [[South Tyrol]], then in the [[Austro-Hungarian Empire]],<ref name="BMLO" /> the daughter of the towns's last Austrian {{ill|Bezirkshauptmann|de}}. Due to political pressure, the family moved to [[Innsbruck]], where she earned her [[Matura]].<ref name="Negel-Täuber" /> She studied philosophy at the [[University of Innsbruck]] and attended classes in [[Liturgics]] with [[Josef Andreas Jungmann]] [[Society of Jesus|SJ]]. She wrote her doctoral thesis on [[Irene Angelina]] of Byzanz in 1936.<ref name="Negel-Täuber" /> She married [[Georg Thurmair]], a poet in [[Munich]], in 1941.

Thurmair and her husband were active in the {{ill|Catholic Youth Movement|de|Katholische Jugendbewegung}} and especially in the [[Liturgical Movement]].<ref name="Gallegos Sánchez" /> She began during World War II to work for the Innsbruck hymnal ''Gotteslob'', followed by contributions to several hymnals of German dioceses, the songbooks ''Singende Gemeinde'' (Singing congregation) and ''[[Kirchenlied]]'' (Church song), a precursor of the later ''Gotteslob''.<ref name="Gallegos Sánchez" /> She translated Latin [[hymn]]s and several books. She published during the war ''Liebesgespräche im Krieg'' (Love talks during the war), a lyrical dialogue of letters including poems which she exchanged with her husband who was a soldier.<ref name="Negel-Täuber" /> It was reprinted in 1981 by Aventinus. She lectured on subjects such as faith, liturgical year, first communion and religious education. The couple had six children and lived in Munich.<ref name="Gallegos Sánchez" />

From 1963, Thurmair worked on the first Catholic hymnal for German-speaking countries, ''[[Gotteslob (1975)|Gotteslob]]'' (GL), as the only woman in the commission.<ref name="Negel-Täuber" /><ref name="Kathnews" /> The first edition of 1975 included 38 of her songs, which was the highest number for one hymnwriter. Some of her hymns (or single stanzas) have also been included in the Protestant hymnal ''[[Evangelisches Gesangbuch]]'' (EG). The current ''[[Gotteslob]]'' includes 28 of her songs.

She received the Papal order ''[[Pro Ecclesia et Pontifice]]''. She died in [[Germering]], near [[Munich]].<ref name="BMLO" />

=== Hymns in ''Gotteslob'' ===
Several of her hymns appear in the current Catholic German hymnal ''[[Gotteslob]]'', and even more in its first [[Gotteslob (1975)|1975 edition]]. She wrote several lyrics for the parts of the [[Mass (liturgy)|mass]] [[Kyrie]], [[Gloria in excelsis Deo|Gloria]], [[Credo]], and many psalm songs, often on traditional melodies. Several songs are related to the occasions of the liturgical year, such as [[Advent]], [[Christmas]] and [[Pentecost]]. Other songs relate to events such as the [[Stations of the Cross]]. She wrote many ''Psalmlieder'', rephrasing [[psalms]].
