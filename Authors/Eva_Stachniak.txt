'''Eva Stachniak''' (born 1952 in [[Wrocław]], [[Poland]]) is a Polish-Canadian novelist.<ref name="roncy">{{cite news |last1=Warzecha |first1=Monika |title=New book puts Roncesvalles Avenue on Toronto's literary map |url=https://www.theglobeandmail.com/news/toronto/new-book-puts-roncesvalles-avenue-on-torontos-literary-map/article576566/ |access-date=30 March 2021 |work=[[The Globe and Mail]] |date=15 April 2011}}</ref>

==Biography==
Stachniak came to Canada in 1981 to study at [[McGill University]],<ref name=roncy/> and remained in the country after the imposition of [[martial law in Poland]] prevented her from returning home.<ref>"Stachniak wins first novel award". ''[[Kingston Whig-Standard]]'', September 27, 2001.</ref> She worked for [[Radio Canada International]] from 1984 to 1986, and then moved to [[Toronto]] to teach at [[Sheridan College]].

Her [[debut novel]] ''Necessary Lies'', published in 2000, won the [[Books in Canada First Novel Award]].<ref>"Necessary Lies wins first-novel award". ''[[The Globe and Mail]]'', September 26, 2001.</ref> Her second novel, ''Garden of Venus'', followed in 2005.<ref>"Final journey culmination of trip from poverty to luxury". ''[[Edmonton Journal]]'', July 24, 2005.</ref> The book was also published under the title ''Dancing with Kings'' in the [[United Kingdom]].

Her third novel, ''The Winter Palace'', was published in 2012.<ref name=catherine>[https://www.theglobeandmail.com/arts/books-and-media/eva-stachniak-in-search-of-catherines-greatness/article4241735/ "Eva Stachniak: In search of Catherine's greatness"]. ''[[The Globe and Mail]]'', January 20, 2012.</ref> A historical novel about [[Catherine the Great]],<ref name=catherine /> ''The Winter Palace'' became Stachniak's first Canadian and international bestseller.<ref name=catherine /> She followed with a sequel novel, ''Empress of the Night'', in 2014.<ref>Ruth Myles, [https://calgaryherald.com/entertainment/ruth-myles-eva-stachniak-returns-to-the-court-of-catherine-the-great-with-empress-of-the-night "Eva Stachniak returns to the court of Catherine the Great with Empress of the Night"]. ''[[Calgary Herald]]'', March 31, 2014.</ref>

She has also published short stories in literary magazines and anthologies.<ref name=roncy/>
