'''Chay Yew''' ({{zh|s=谢耀|zh|t=謝耀|p=Xiè Yào}}) is a [[playwright]] and [[theatre director|stage director]] who was born in [[Singapore]]. He was [[Artistic Director]] of [[Victory Gardens Theater]], [[Chicago]] from 2011 to 2020.<ref>{{Cite news |last=Clement |first=Olivia |date=December 6, 2019 |title=Chay Yew to Depart Victory Gardens |language=en |url=http://www.playbill.com/article/chay-yew-to-depart-victory-gardens |access-date=2020-06-07}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news |last=Pierce |first=Jerald Raymond |date=2020-05-27 |title=Chay Yew: Break the Table and Build a New One |language=en-US |work=American Theater |url=https://www.americantheatre.org/2020/05/27/chay-yew-break-the-table-and-build-a-new-one/ |access-date=2020-06-07}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |title=Victory Gardens Media Room |url=http://victorygardens.org/media/vg-pr-3may11.php |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120325052551/http://victorygardens.org/media/vg-pr-3may11.php |archive-date=2012-03-25 |access-date=2013-12-04 |publisher=Victorygardens.org}}</ref>

==Career==
Yew's breakthrough work came from his early plays ''Porcelain''<ref>{{Cite web |title=Porcelain Reviews - Theatre In Chicago - Play Reviews |url=https://www.theatreinchicago.com/porcelain/reviews/7757/ |access-date=2021-01-02 |website=www.theatreinchicago.com}}</ref> and ''A Language of Their Own'',<ref>{{Cite web |date=2017-03-31 |title=More Than Words: A Review of A Language of Their Own |url=https://www.criticsrepublic.com/2017/03/31/more-than-words-a-review-of-a-language-of-their-own/ |access-date=2021-01-02 |website=Critics Republic |language=en-GB}}</ref> which, along with ''Wonderland'',<ref>{{Cite web |title=Calculated Cacophonies: The Queer Asian American Family and the Nonmusical Musical in Chay Yew's Wonderland |url=https://jadtjournal.org/2016/12/17/calculated-cacophonies-the-queer-asian-american-family-and-the-nonmusical-musical-in-chay-yews-wonderland/ |access-date=2021-01-02 |website=jadtjournal.org}}</ref> make up what Yew calls the Whitelands Trilogy.<ref>{{Cite web |last1=Sep. 19 |first1=JAN BRESLAUER |last2=Am |first2=1999 12 |date=1999-09-19 |title=Hitting Close to Home |url=https://www.latimes.com/archives/la-xpm-1999-sep-19-ca-11750-story.html |access-date=2021-01-07 |website=Los Angeles Times |language=en-US}}</ref> Other plays include ''As if He Hears'';<ref>{{Cite web |last=mghangaa@st |date=2015-02-24 |title=Grappling with sexual politics and love |url=https://www.straitstimes.com/lifestyle/arts/grappling-with-sexual-politics-and-love |access-date=2021-01-07 |website=The Straits Times |language=en}}</ref> ''Red'';<ref>{{Cite news |last=Swarns |first=Rachel L. |date=1999-03-21 |title=THEATER; An Outsider Determined Not to Be Someone He's Not (Published 1999) |language=en-US |work=The New York Times |url=https://www.nytimes.com/1999/03/21/theater/theater-an-outsider-determined-not-to-be-someone-he-s-not.html |access-date=2021-01-02 |issn=0362-4331}}</ref> ''A Beautiful Country'';<ref>{{Cite web |last=Molina |first=Nina |title='A Beautiful Country' blooms virtually |url=https://www.michigandaily.com/section/arts/beautiful-country-blooms-virtually |access-date=2021-01-02 |website=The Michigan Daily |language=en}}</ref> ''Question 27, Question 28'';<ref>{{Cite web |date=2004-02-20 |title=Repeating the history |url=https://www.latimes.com/archives/la-xpm-2004-feb-20-et-boehm20-story.html |access-date=2020-12-30 |website=Los Angeles Times |language=en-US}}</ref> ''A Distant Shore'';<ref>{{Cite web |title=A Distant Shore Will Bring You in Close |url=https://international.ucla.edu/institute/article/25462 |access-date=2021-01-07 |website=international.ucla.edu}}</ref> ''Vivien and the Shadows''<ref>{{Cite web |title=Vivien and The Shadows |url=http://charlotteengelkes.com/vivien-and-the-shadows/ |access-date=2021-01-02 |website=Charlotte Engelkes |language=en-US}}</ref>'';'' and ''Visible Cities''.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Mad World |url=http://www.inkpotreviews.com/2009reviews/0523,visi,kk.xml |access-date=2020-12-30 |website=www.inkpotreviews.com}}</ref> His adaptations include ''A Winter People''<ref>{{Cite web |title=European Cultural Review |url=http://www.c3.hu/~eufuzetek/en/eng/bookshelf/index.php?mit=13stefanova |access-date=2021-01-02 |website=www.c3.hu}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |title=QLRS - Extra Media : Cherry Blossoms In China {{!}} Vol. 1 No. 4 Jul 2002 |url=http://www.qlrs.com/emedia.asp?id=235 |access-date=2021-01-02 |website=www.qlrs.com}}</ref> (based on [[Anton Chekhov]]'s ''[[The Cherry Orchard]]''); and [[Federico García Lorca]]'s ''[[The House of Bernarda Alba]]''.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Oxman |first=Steven |date=2002-07-25 |title=The House of Bernarda Alba |url=https://variety.com/2002/legit/reviews/the-house-of-bernarda-alba-5-1200547177/ |access-date=2021-01-02 |website=Variety |language=en-US}}</ref>

In 1989, the government in Singapore banned his first play ''As If He Hears'' because the [[homosexuality|gay]] character acted "too sympathetic and too straight-looking".<ref name="auto">{{Cite news |last=Healy |first=Patrick |date=2012-11-21 |title='Opening Doors' Means Rattling Some Cages (Published 2012) |language=en-US |work=The New York Times |url=https://www.nytimes.com/2012/11/22/theater/victory-gardens-theater-in-chicago-in-new-era-with-chay-yew.html |access-date=2020-12-30 |issn=0362-4331}}</ref> Chay Yew's plays appear in numerous anthologies, and two collections of his plays<ref>{{Cite book |url=https://groveatlantic.com/book/porcelain-and-a-language-of-their-own/ |title=Porcelain and A Language of Their Own {{!}} Grove Atlantic |language=en}}</ref> have been published by [[Grove Press]]. Yew also edited an anthology of contemporary Asian American plays, "Version 3.0," for Theatre Communications Group Publications.<ref>{{Cite news |last=Chai |first=Barbara |date=2011-08-02 |title='Version 3.0′ Gives Platform to Asian American Playwrights |language=en-US |work=Wall Street Journal |url=https://www.wsj.com/articles/BL-SEB-66467 |access-date=2021-01-06 |issn=0099-9660}}</ref>

Yew's plays have been produced by many theaters, including the [[New York Shakespeare Festival]]/[[Public Theater]] in New York City, [[Mark Taper Forum]], [[Manhattan Theatre Club]], Wilma Theatre, [[Long Wharf Theatre]], [[La Jolla Playhouse]], [[Intiman Theatre]], Portland Center Stage, [[East West Players]], [[Cornerstone Theatre Company]], amongst others. Overseas, his work has been produced by the [[Royal Court Theatre]] (London, UK); Fattore K and Napoli Teatro Festival (Naples, Italy); La Mama (Melbourne, Australia); Shanghai Dramatic Arts Center (Shanghai, China); Four Arts (Kuala Lumpur, Malaysia); and Wild Rice, [[Singapore Repertory Theatre]], Toy Factory, Checkpoint Theatre, and TheatreWorks (Singapore).<ref>{{Cite web |title=About Me {{!}} Chay Yew {{!}} resume and bio |url=https://www.chayyew.com/resume |access-date=2021-01-07 |website=chayyew.com |language=en}}</ref>

For his plays, He is the recipient of the London Fringe Award for Best Playwright and Best Play, George and Elisabeth Marton Playwriting Award, GLAAD Media Award, APGF Community Visibility Award, Made in America Award, AEA/SAG/AFTRA 2004 Diversity Honor, and [[Robert Chesley Award]];<ref>{{Cite web |title=The Robert Chesley Award for Lesbian and Gay Playwriting |url=https://publishingtriangle.org/awards/robert-chesley-award/ |access-date=2020-12-30 |website=The Publishing Triangle |language=en-US}}</ref> he has also received grants from the Rockefeller MAP, McKnight Foundation and the TCG/Pew National Residency Program.

As a director, Chay Yew has directed productions at the Public Theater, New York Theatre Workshop, Signature Theatre, Playwrights Horizons, American Conservatory Theatre, Kennedy Center, Long Wharf Theatre, Mark Taper Forum, East West Players, [[Actors Theatre of Louisville]], [[Goodman Theatre]], [[Oregon Shakespeare Festival]], Portland Center Stage, [[La Jolla Playhouse]], Empty Space, National Asian American Theatre Company, [[Berkeley Rep]], [[Seattle Rep]], Gala Hispanic Theatre, Singapore Repertory Theatre, Cornerstone Theatre Company, [[Huntington Theatre]], [[South Coast Repertory]] Theatre, and [[Smithsonian Institution]].

He also directed the world premieres of [[David Henry Hwang]]'s and [[Osvaldo Golijov]]'s ''Ainadamar'' at the Tanglewood Festival of Contemporary Music and Rob Zuidam's ''Rage D'Amors'' (Tanglewood).

In 2006, Yew participated in The Collision Project at The Alliance Theatre in Atlanta, Georgia. He wrote a documentary play ''17'' based on the actual lives of Atlanta’s racially diverse teenagers.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Palefsky Collision Project {{!}} Alliance Theatre |url=https://alliancetheatre.org/content/palefsky-collision-project |url-status=live |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190327135952/https://alliancetheatre.org/content/palefsky-collision-project |archive-date=2019-03-27 |access-date=2019-08-21 |website=alliancetheatre.org}}</ref>

Chay Yew was the Artistic Director of Victory Gardens Theater in Chicago from 2011 to 2020.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Artistic director Chay Yew: 'Audiences come here wanting a dialogue about America' |url=https://www.thestage.co.uk/features/artistic-director-chay-yew-audiences-come-here-wanting-a-dialogue-about-america |access-date=2020-12-30 |website=The Stage |language=En}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |title=Meaningful Dialogue |url=https://howlround.com/meaningful-dialogue |access-date=2020-12-30 |website=HowlRound Theatre Commons |language=en}}</ref><ref name="auto" /><ref>{{Cite web |last=Reid |first=Kerry |title=How Chay Yew Made Victory Gardens Feel New Again |url=http://www.chicagomag.com/Chicago-Magazine/November-2013/Staging-a-Second-Act/ |access-date=2020-12-30 |website=Chicago magazine |language=en}}</ref> During his tenure, out of 43 productions, 18 plays received world premieres of which one went to Broadway, four were produced off-Broadway at the Public, [[Soho Rep]], and [[Signature Theatre Company|Signature Theatre]], while others were presented regionally, and abroad at [[Donmar Warehouse]] and [[Bush Theatre]] in London.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Victory Gardens Theater {{!}} Chay Yew {{!}} artistic director {{!}} Chicago |url=https://www.chayyew.com/victory-gardens |access-date=2021-01-07 |website=chayyew.com |language=en}}</ref> For his leadership, he was awarded the Iris Award for Outstanding Commitment to Connecting Chicago Communities and the Arts,<ref>{{Cite web |title=Night of Flight 2016 {{!}} Halcyon Theatre |url=https://halcyontheatre.org/nightofflight2016 |access-date=2020-12-30 |website=halcyontheatre.org}}</ref> and the Impact Award for Bold and Inclusive Artistic Leadership.<ref>{{Cite web |last=<!--Staff writer(s); no by-line.--> |title=Sideshow Theatre to Honor Arts & Business Council of Chicago and Chay Yew with 2017 Impact Awards |url=https://www.broadwayworld.com/chicago/article/Sideshow-Theatre-to-Honor-Arts-Business-Council-of-Chicago-and-Chay-Yew-with-2017-Impact-Awards-20170607 |access-date=2020-12-30 |website=BroadwayWorld.com |language=en}}</ref>

For direction, Chay Yew is the recipient of the DramaLogue Award, 2020 Craig Noel Award;<ref>{{Cite web |title=2019 Awards |url=https://sdcriticscircle.org/2019-awards/ |access-date=2020-12-30 |website=The San Diego Theatre Critics Circle |language=en-US}}</ref> and OBIE Award for Direction.<ref>{{Cite news |date=2007-05-15 |title=2006–2007 Obie Award Winners |url=http://www.villagevoice.com/2007-05-15/theater/2006-2007-obie-award-winners/ |url-status=dead |access-date=2013-12-04 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131202222002/http://www.villagevoice.com/2007-05-15/theater/2006-2007-obie-award-winners/ |archive-date=December 2, 2013}}</ref>

Yew was also the Founder and Director of the [[Mark Taper Forum]]'s Asian Theatre Workshop for ten years.<ref>{{Cite web |date=1995-12-17 |title=Asians Get a Proponent at the Taper |url=https://www.latimes.com/archives/la-xpm-1995-12-17-ca-14907-story.html |access-date=2020-12-30 |website=Los Angeles Times |language=en-US}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news |last=Jefferson |first=Margo |date=2005-08-07 |title=Will Theater in Los Angeles Fade to White? (Published 2005) |language=en-US |work=The New York Times |url=https://www.nytimes.com/2005/08/07/theater/newsandfeatures/will-theater-in-los-angeles-fade-to-white.html |access-date=2020-12-30 |issn=0362-4331}}</ref> An alumnus of [[New Dramatists]], he has served on the Executive Board of the [[Stage Directors and Choreographers Society]], [[Theatre Communications Group]], Chicago’s Department of Cultural Affairs and Special Events’ Cultural Advisory Council, the League of Chicago Theatres, and the Executive Board of the Consortium of Asian American Theatre and Artists.
