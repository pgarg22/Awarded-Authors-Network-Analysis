Lieutenant-Colonel '''Frederick Sadleir Brereton''', [[Order of the British Empire|CBE]] (5 August 1872 – 12 August 1957) was a [[United Kingdom|British]] [[British Army|Army]] medical officer and an author of children's books on heroic deeds conducted in the name of the British Empire.<ref>Paris, Michael (2004). "Over The Top: The Great War and Juvenile Literature in Britain". Praeger. 
p. 39.</ref> Brereton was a prolific author. By the time he died he had written over 40 books.

==Early career==
Brereton was commissioned into the [[Royal Army Medical Corps]] (RAMC) as a [[surgeon-lieutenant]] on 29 January 1896, and was promoted to [[Captain (BARM)|captain]] on 29 January 1899. During the [[Second Boer War]] he was attached as a medical officer to the [[Scots Guards]].<ref>Hart′s Army list, 1902</ref> He retired his commission on 22 November 1902, after the end of the war in South Africa.<ref>{{London Gazette |issue=27497 |date=21 November 1902 |page=7537}}</ref>

== First World War ==

Brereton served again in the RAMC during the [[First World War]] with the rank of Brevet Lieutenant Colonel. In 1919 he was appointed as a Commander of the Portuguese [[Order of Aviz]] and a [[Order of the British Empire|CBE]].<ref>{{Cite web|url = http://www.thepeerage.com/p35365.htm|title = Lt.-Col. Frederick Sadleir Brereton|date = |accessdate = |website = |publisher = |last = |first = }}</ref>

== Personal life ==

Brereton married Ethel Lamb in 1898 and Isobel Murdoch in 1953.
