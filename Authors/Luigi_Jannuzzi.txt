'''Luigi Jannuzzi''' (born November 12, 1952) is a contemporary American comedic playwright.<ref>{{cite web|title=Luigi Jannuzzi|url=http://www.goodreads.com/author/show/308356.Luigi_Jannuzzi|publisher=Goodreads Inc|accessdate=15 October 2012}}</ref> A native of New Jersey, Jannuzzi attended [[Bound Brook High School]], [[Raritan Valley Community College]], [[Salem University]], WV & [[The University of Notre Dame]], where in 1977, he received a master's degree.  From 1978 - 2012 at Immaculata High School, Somerville, NJ, he taught English, Drama and Creative Writing.

His play ''A Bench At The Edge''<ref name="samuelfrench1">{{cite web|author=Luigi Jannuzzi |url=http://www.samuelfrench.com/p/564/bench-at-the-edge-a |title=Bench at the Edge, A &#124; Samuel French, Inc |publisher=Samuelfrench.com |date= |accessdate=2012-10-16}}</ref> won best one act in Ireland in 1999 and best one act in Scotland and the United Kingdom in 2001.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.audf.org.uk/british2.html |title=BRITISH ONE-ACT DRAMA FINALS IN NORTHERN IRELAND 2001 |publisher=Audf.org.uk |date=2001-06-30 |accessdate=2012-10-16}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|last=A Bench At The Edge|first=Jannuzzi|title=United Kingdom Trophy|url=http://www.audf.org.uk/british2.html|accessdate=15 October 2012}}</ref> He also wrote the screen play for the movie.<ref>{{cite web|last=Jannuzzi|first=Luigi|title=A Bench At The Edge|url=https://www.imdb.com/title/tt0398752/|work=movie|publisher=imdb|accessdate=15 October 2012}}</ref> He is a recipient of two New Jersey State Council on the Art Fellowships, two Geraldine R. Dodge Grants, three National Endowments for the Humanities (2000 at Rutgers U,. 1998 at Columbia U., and 1995 at The U of Vermont), the 1986 Goshen Peace Prize, a 1998 and 2000 Finalist in the Eugene O'Neill National Playwriting Conference, and a 2007-2009 James Madison Grant at [[Princeton University]].  He is listed in Contemporary Authors and in Who's Who in America.

==Works==
Jannuzzi's first play in New York was ''A Bench At The Edge'',<ref name="samuelfrench1"/> staged at St Clement Theatre in May 1981, followed by ''The Barbarians Are Coming''<ref>{{cite web|author=Luigi Jannuzzi |url=http://www.samuelfrench.com/p/574/barbarians-are-coming-the |title=Barbarians Are Coming, The &#124; Samuel French, Inc |publisher=Samuelfrench.com |date=1983-05-22 |accessdate=2012-10-16}}</ref> staged at the Double Image Theatre in May 1983. ''Bench'' won the Samuel French short play competition, ''Barbarians'' won the 1986 Goshen Peace Prize and both were subsequently published. ''The Appointment''<ref>{{cite web|author=Luigi Jannuzzi |url=http://www.samuelfrench.com/p/589/appointment-the |title=Appointment, The &#124; Samuel French, Inc |publisher=Samuelfrench.com |date= |accessdate=2012-10-16}}</ref> produced by Love Creek Productions at the Nat Horne Theatre in December 1994 also won the Samuel French short play competition as did ''With Or Without You'',<ref>{{cite web|author=Luigi Jannuzzi |url=http://www.samuelfrench.com/p/532/with-or-without-you |title=With or Without You &#124; Samuel French, Inc |publisher=Samuelfrench.com |date= |accessdate=2012-10-16}}</ref> produced by Love Creek Productions at the Harold Clurman Theatre in May 1996 and are published by [[Samuel French]].

''Night Of The Foolish Moon''<ref>{{cite web|author=Luigi Jannuzzi |url=http://www.samuelfrench.com/p/507/night-of-the-foolish-moon |title=Night of the Foolish Moon &#124; Samuel French, Inc |publisher=Samuelfrench.com |date=1998-05-29 |accessdate=2012-10-16}}</ref> premiered in 1998, was a Eugene O'Neill Playwrighting Conference Finalist and is published by [[Samuel French]] as was "For The Love Of Juliet,"<ref>{{cite web|author=Luigi Jannuzzi |url=http://www.samuelfrench.com/p/510/for-the-love-of-juliet |title=For the Love of Juliet &#124; Samuel French, Inc |publisher=Samuelfrench.com |date= |accessdate=2012-10-16}}</ref> also a finalist in the O'Neill National Theatre Conference and at Florida's Orlando Shakespeare Festival.

''Exhibit This! The Museum Comedies'',<ref>{{cite web|author=Luigi Jannuzzi |url=http://www.samuelfrench.com/p/536/exhibit-this-the-museum-comedies |title=Exhibit This! The Museum Comedies &#124; Samuel French, Inc |publisher=Samuelfrench.com |date= |accessdate=2012-10-16}}</ref> a collection of one acts and monologues about [[The Metropolitan Museum of Art]] that comes to life one night, was produced by the Metropolitan Theatre Company at the Midtown International Festival, NYC in July 2007 winning  for outstanding production,<ref>{{cite web|last=Hetrick|first=Adam|title=Playbill News:  Midtown Theatre Festival Award Winners Announced|url=http://www.playbill.com/news/article/110990-Midtown-International-Theatre-Festival-Awards-Winners-Announced|work=Exhibit This! The Museum Comedies|publisher=Playbill News|accessdate=15 October 2012}}</ref> was the number one pick by New York Magazine in their July 16, 2007 issue of New York Magazine and received positive reviews from the New York Theatre Critics.<ref>{{cite web|last=Zlokower|first=Roberta|title=Review:  Exhibit This! The Museum Comedies at the Midtown Theatre Festival|url=http://www.robertaonthearts.com/id942.html|publisher=Roberta On The Arts|accessdate=15 October 2012}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|last=Murg|first=Stephanie|title=Theatre Review:  "Pictures worth 1,000 words, and a handful of actors|url=http://www.chelseanow.com/articles/2007/07/27/chelseanow_archives/20070727-archive15.txt|publisher=Now Chelsea News, July 27, 2007}}</ref>   It also won the Perry Award for Best Original Comedy.  It is published by [[Samuel French]].

''All The King's Women'',<ref>{{cite web|author=Luigi Jannuzzi |url=http://www.samuelfrench.com/p/533/all-the-kings-women |title=All the King's Women &#124; Samuel French, Inc |publisher=Samuelfrench.com |date=2007-07-18 |accessdate=2012-10-16}}</ref> a collection of one acts and monologues about the life of Elvis Presley, was produced by the Metropolitan Theatre Company at the Midtown International Festival, NYC in July 2007 received positive reviews from the New York Critics <ref>{{cite web|last=Kim|first=Nancy|title=NYTheatre.com Review|url=http://www.nytheatre.com/Show/Review/2006477|work=All The King's Women|accessdate=15 October 2012}}</ref> and is published by [[Samuel French]].
