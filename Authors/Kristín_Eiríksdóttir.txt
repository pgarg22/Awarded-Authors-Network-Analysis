{{short description|Icelandic poet and writer}}
'''Kristín Eiríksdóttir''' (born 1981)<ref>{{cite web |title=Kristín Eiríksdóttir |url=https://www.norden.org/en/nominee/kristin-eiriksdottir |website=Nordic cooperation |accessdate=27 September 2019 |language=en}}</ref> is an Icelandic poet and author.

Eiríksdótter's debut novel, ''Kjötbærinn'' (''Meat Town'') was published in 2004. This was followed by ''Húðlit auðnin'' (''Skin-coloured Wasteland'') in 2006 and ''Annarskonar sæla'' (''A Different Sort of Bliss'') in 2008. Her first two books were a mixture of poetry and prose while her third was entirely poetry.<ref>{{cite web |title=Kristín Eiríksdóttir |url=https://www.islit.is/en/promotion-and-translations/icelandic-literature/interviews-with-icelandic-authors/nr/1294 |website=Miðstöð íslenskra bókmennta |accessdate=27 September 2019 |language=en}}</ref>

She was one of the winners of the [[Bókmenntaverðlaun starfsfólks bókaverslana]] with her 2014 book ''Kok'' (''Cook'').<ref>{{cite web |title=Bókmenntaverðlaun starfsfólks bókaverslana 2014 |url=https://www.islit.is/frettir/nr/3761 |website=Miðstöð íslenskra bókmennta |accessdate=27 September 2019 |language=is}}</ref>

Her novel, ''Elín, ýmislegt'' (''A Fist or a Heart''), won the [[Icelandic Literary Prize]]<ref>{{cite web |title=Icelandic Literary Prize |url=http://www.fibut.is/english/icelandic-literary-prize |website=www.fibut.is |accessdate=27 September 2019}}</ref> and was shortlisted for the 2019 [[Nordic Council Literature Prize]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Kristín Eiríksdóttir and Kristín Ómarsdóttir Nominated for Nordic Council Literature Prize |url=https://www.icelandreview.com/news/kristin-eiriksdottir-and-kristin-omarsdottir-nominated-for-nordic-council-literature-prize/ |website=Iceland Review |accessdate=27 September 2019}}</ref>
