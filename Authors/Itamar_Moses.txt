'''Itamar Moses''' (born 1977) is an American [[playwright]], [[author]], and [[television writer]].

==Biography==
Moses grew up in a [[American Jews|Jewish]] family<ref>{{Cite web|last=Bloom|first=Nate|title=The Jews go to the Tonys, 2018 â€” including Berkeley's own Ari'el Stachel |publisher=[[J. The Jewish News of Northern California]]|date= June 7, 2018|url=https://www.jweekly.com/2018/06/07/jews-go-tonys-2018-including-berkeleys-ariel-stachel/ }}</ref> in [[Berkeley, California|Berkeley]], [[California]], earned his bachelor's degree at [[Yale University]], and his [[Master of Fine Arts]] degree in dramatic writing from [[New York University]]. He has taught playwriting at both Yale and New York University, and he has written for ''[[Men of a Certain Age]]'' and ''[[Boardwalk Empire]]''.

His most prominent work, the musical [[The Band's Visit (musical)|''The Band's Visit'']], opened on December 8, 2016 at the [[Atlantic Theater Company]].<ref>Atlantic Theater Company, https://atlantictheater.org</ref> That production won the 2017 [[Obie Award]] for Musical Theatre Off-Broadway.<ref>http://www.obieawards.com/events/year-2017/</ref> After closing on January 9, 2017, the musical moved to Broadway.<ref>Pipenberg, Erik [https://www.nytimes.com/2017/05/24/theater/the-bands-visit-to-open-on-broadway.html "The Bands Visit to Open on Broadway"], The New York Times, May 24th, 2017</ref> It began previews on October 7, 2017 and officially opened on November 9, 2017 at the [[Ethel Barrymore Theatre]]. For his work on ''[[The Band's Visit (musical)|The Band's Visit]]'', Moses won the 2018 [[Tony Award for Best Book of a Musical]].
