'''Francesca Sanders''' is a [[playwright]] from [[Portland, Oregon]].

She has been the recipient of the Oregon Literary Fellowship for Drama<ref>[http://www.literaryarts.org literaryarts.org]</ref> and the Portland Civic Theatre Guild Fellowship For Theatrical Excellence.<ref>[http://www.drammyinfo.com/pctawards.html]{{Dead link|date=December 2019 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref> She has also been a finalist for the Rosenthal New Play Prize<ref>[http://www.cincyplay.com Cincinnati Playhouse in the Park]</ref> and PlayLabs (Playwright's Center Minneapolis).<ref>[http://www.pwcenter.org The Playwrights Center]  {{webarchive|url=https://www.webcitation.org/5v0VDXZzR?url=http://www.pwcenter.org/ |date=December 16, 2010 }}</ref> She was the winner of The Lakewood Theatre New Play Competition<ref>[http://www.lakewoodcenter.org La Amistad Foundation]  {{webarchive|url=https://www.webcitation.org/5v0VDswu6?url=http://www.lakewoodcenter.org/ |date=December 16, 2010 }}</ref> and the Mercury Rising Playwriting Competition.<ref>[http://www.mercuryplayerstheatre.com Mercury Players Theater]  {{webarchive|url=https://www.webcitation.org/5v0VEZge7?url=http://www.mercuryplayerstheatre.com/ |date=December 16, 2010 }}</ref>

Sanders won a women's work residency at New Perspectives Theatre in New York and has been commissioned by such places as [[Portland Center Stage]]<ref>[http://www.pcs.org Portland Center Stage]  {{webarchive|url=https://www.webcitation.org/5v0VFsdpt?url=http://www.pcs.org/ |date=December 16, 2010 }}</ref> Proving Ground (Salt Lake City) and Integrity Productions.<ref>[http://www.integrityproductions.org Integrity Productions]  {{webarchive|url=https://www.webcitation.org/5v0VGLLwY?url=http://www.integrityproductions.org/ |date=December 16, 2010 }}</ref> She has been selected to participate in the Seven Devilâ€™s Playwriting Conference<ref>[http://www.sevendevils.org Seven Devils Playwright Conference]  {{webarchive|url=https://www.webcitation.org/5v0VGaAF0?url=http://www.sevendevils.org/ |date=December 16, 2010 }}</ref> and the Kennedy Center Summer Playwriting Intensive.

Her plays ''Celeste and Starla Save Todd and Win Back the Day'' and ''I Become A Guitar'' were performed at the Bartell Theatre in [[Madison, Wisconsin]] and the Kitchen Theatre in [[Ithaca, New York]], respectively.
