'''Harivansh Rai Bachchan''' ({{ne|'''Srivastava'''}}; 27 November 1907 {{endash}} 18 January 2003) was an Indian poet and writer of the Nayi Kavita literary movement (romantic upsurge) of early 20th century [[Hindi literature]]. He was also a poet of the [[Kavi sammelan|Hindi Kavi Sammelan]]. He is best known for his early work ''[[Madhushala]]''.<ref name=obituary>[https://web.archive.org/web/20100822142158/http://www.hinduonnet.com/fline/fl2003/stories/20030214007511800.htm Harivanshrai Bachchan, 1907–2003] Obituary, Frontline, (''[[The Hindu]]''), 1–14 February 2003.</ref> He is also the husband of social activist, [[Teji Bachchan]], father of [[Amitabh Bachchan]] and Ajitabh Bachchan, and grandfather of [[Abhishek Bachchan]]. In 1976, he received the [[Padma Bhushan]] for his service to Hindi literature.<ref name="Padma Awards">{{cite web |url=http://mha.nic.in/sites/upload_files/mha/files/LST-PDAWD-2013.pdf |title=Padma Awards |publisher=Ministry of Home Affairs, Government of India |date=2015 |access-date=21 July 2015 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://www.webcitation.org/6U68ulwpb?url=http://mha.nic.in/sites/upload_files/mha/files/LST-PDAWD-2013.pdf |archive-date=15 November 2014 }}</ref>

== Early life ==
Bachchan was born in the village of [[Babupatti]] in the [[United Provinces of Agra and Oudh]] in [[British Raj|British India]] on 27 November 1907 into an [[Awadhi people|Awadhi]] [[Hindu]] [[Kayastha]] family.<ref>{{Cite web|date=25 November 2016|title=खुली किताब सा जीवन : हरिवंश राय बच्चन की जीवनी के चार खंड!|url=https://hindi.thebetterindia.com/2951/harivanshrai-bachchan-autobiography-snippets/|access-date=5 August 2020|website=The Better India - Hindi|language=en-US}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |title=Harivansh Rai Bachchan {{!}} Indian poet |url=https://www.britannica.com/biography/Harivansh-Rai-Bachchan |website=Encyclopedia Britannica |access-date=17 October 2020 |language=en}}</ref> From 1941 to 1957, he taught in the English Department at the [[Allahabad University]] and after that, he spent the next two years at [[St Catharine's College, Cambridge]], [[Cambridge University]] completing a PhD on [[W.B. Yeats]].<ref name=obituary /> He began using the pen name "Bachchan" (meaning child) instead of Shrivastava when he wrote Hindi poetry.

==Writing career==
Bachchan was fluent in several [[Hindi languages]] ([[Hindustani language|Hindustani]], [[Awadhi language|Awadhi]]).<ref name="West-Pavlov">{{cite book |last=West-Pavlov |first=Russell |title=The Global South and Literature |date=2018 |publisher=[[Cambridge University Press]] |isbn=9781108246316 |page=167 |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=yMdJDwAAQBAJ&pg=PA167}}</ref> He incorporated a broadly Hindustani vocabulary,<ref>{{cite book |last1=Williams |first1=Mukesh |last2=Wanchoo |first2=Rohit |title=Representing India: Literatures, Politics, and Identities |date=2008 |publisher=[[Oxford University Press]] |isbn=9780195692266 |page=73 |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=3uYTAQAAIAAJ |quote=Harivansh Rai Bachchan recalled how some of the Urdu vocabulary used by audiences in appreciating poetic recitals in Hindi kavi sammelans was consciously changed to Sanskritized Hindi creating an artificial Hindi idiom.}}</ref> written in [[Devanagari script]].<ref name="West-Pavlov"/> While he could not read [[Persian script]],<ref name="West-Pavlov"/> he was influenced by [[Persian poetry|Persian]] and [[Urdu poetry]], particularly [[Omar Khayyam]].<ref>{{cite book |last=Gopal |first=Madan |title=Origin and development of Hindi/Urdu literature |date=1996 |publisher=Deep & Deep Publications |page=204 |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=D8djAAAAMAAJ |quote=He was influenced by Persian and Urdu poetry, especially by Omar Khayyam and started versifying in the Bachchalian style.}}</ref>

==Works used in movies==
Bachchan's work has been used in movies and music. For example, couplets of his work "Agneepath" are used throughout the 1990 movie ''[[Agneepath (1990 film)|Agneepath]]'', starring [[Amitabh Bachchan]], and later in the 2012 remake ''[[Agneepath (2012 film)|Agneepath]]'', starring [[Hrithik Roshan]].<ref>[https://www.imdb.com/name/nm0045394/ Harivansh Rai Bachchan (1907–2003)].
