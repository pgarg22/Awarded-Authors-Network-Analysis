'''Katherine Arden Burdine''' (born 1987) is an American author known primarily for her [[Winternight trilogy|''Winternight'' trilogy]] of [[fantasy]] novels set in medieval Russia.<ref>{{Cite news|title=Hardcover Fiction Books - Best Sellers - Books - Jan. 27, 2019 - The New York Times|language=en-US|work=The New York Times|url=https://www.nytimes.com/books/best-sellers/2019/01/27/hardcover-fiction/|access-date=2021-05-06|issn=0362-4331}}</ref> She has been nominated for the [[Hugo Award|Hugo]] and [[Locus Award|Locus]] Awards. The first novel of the ''Winternight'' trilogy, ''[[The Bear and the Nightingale]]'', was published in 2017, followed by ''[[The Girl in the Tower]]'' in 2018 and ''[[The Winter of the Witch]]'' in 2019. Arden has also written a series of horror novels for [[Middle grade fiction|middle grade]] children, which includes ''Small Spaces'' in 2018, ''Dead Voices'' in 2019, ''Dark Waters'' in 2021, and ''Empty Smiles'' in 2022. ''Small Spaces'' won the [[Vermont Golden Dome Book Award]] in 2020.

== Biography ==
Arden was born in [[Austin, Texas]], and currently resides in [[Vermont]]. She spent a year in [[Moscow]] after high school before returning to Vermont.<ref>{{Cite news|date=2017-01-16|title=Katherine Arden Debuts A Different Kind Of Coming Winter|language=en-US|work=Unbound Worlds|url=http://www.unboundworlds.com/2017/01/katherine-arden-debuts-different-kind-coming-winter/| url-status=dead |archive-url= https://web.archive.org/web/20170126053928/http://www.unboundworlds.com/2017/01/katherine-arden-debuts-different-kind-coming-winter/ |archive-date=January 26, 2017 |last=Speakman |first=Shawn}}</ref> She attended [[Middlebury College]], graduating with a degree in Russian and French in 2011.<ref name="addison" />

Arden's writing is influenced by [[J. R. R. Tolkien|J.R.R Tolkien]], [[Mary Renault]], [[Naomi Novik]], [[Patrick O'Brian|Patrick O'Brien]], [[Dorothy Dunnett]], [[Diana Gabaldon]], and [[Robin McKinley]].<ref>{{cite web |last1=Tufnell |first1=Nicholas |title=Katherine Arden: It's a great time for female fantasy writers |date=January 24, 2018 |url=https://www.cnet.com/news/katherine-arden-the-girl-in-the-tower-female-fantasy-writers/ |website=[[CNET]]}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=Katherine Arden|url=https://www.goodreads.com/author/show/13922215.Katherine_Arden|access-date=2021-05-28|website=[[Goodreads]]}}</ref>
