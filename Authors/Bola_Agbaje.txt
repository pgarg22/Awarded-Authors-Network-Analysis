'''Bola Agbaje''' {{post-nominals|country=GBR|FRSL}} is a [[playwright]] of [[Nigerian]] origin.<ref name=WhatsOnStage>[https://web.archive.org/web/20080501163405/http://www.whatsonstage.com/index.php?pg=207&story=E8821209377116 "The Write Stuff: Stenham & Other Courtiers"], ''What's On Stage'', 28 April 2008.</ref>

==Career==
Agbaje's first play ''[[Gone Too Far!]]'' was produced at the [[Royal Court Theatre]] in [[London]], where it won the [[2008 Laurence Olivier Awards|2008]] [[Laurence Olivier Award for Outstanding Achievement in an Affiliate Theatre]].<ref name=OnTour>[https://royalcourttheatre.com/whats-on/gone-too-far-on-tour-2/ "Gone Too Far! on Tour"], Royal Court, 2008.</ref><ref name=WhatsOnStage /> It was later revived for very brief runs at the [[Albany Theatre]] and [[Hackney Empire]].<ref name=OnTour />

Agjabe also wrote the plays ''The Burial'' and ''[[Belong (play)|Belong]]''.<ref>Bayes, Honour (7 May 2013), [https://www.timeout.com/london/theatre/the-burial-2 "The Burial"] (review), ''[[Time Out London|Time Out]]''.</ref><ref>Reinelt, Janelle. "Forum: Creative Ambivalence And Precarious Futures: Women In British Theatre." vol. 62, no. 4 (Dec 2010), p. 553.(n.d.): Web. 14 March. 2016.</ref>

In 2018, Agbaje was elected as a Fellow of the [[Royal Society of Literature]] (RSL), one of 40 under the age of 40 elected in an RSL initiative designed "to address historical biases".<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.theguardian.com/books/2018/jun/28/royal-society-of-literature-40-under-40-fellows|title=Royal Society of Literature admits 40 new fellows to address historical biases|last=Flood|first=Alison|date=2018-06-28|website=[[The Guardian]]|language=en|access-date=2018-07-03}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Agbaje is a Laurence Olivier Award-winning playwright of Nigerian origin. Born in London, Agbaje has spent almost her entire life in England, living in Nigeria between the ages of six and eight. Formerly an actress, Agbaje's works explore the African condition both in England and abroad, mainly exploring the assimilation and social dynamics in African communities.<ref>Young, Carlene. "Assimilation And Social Change Dynamics In African And African American Communities." vol. 27, no. 3 (Fall 2003), p. 164-175.(n.d.): Web. 13 March. 2016</ref> Works such as [[Gone Too Far!]], and ''Belong'' examine these elements alongside the tensions and conflicts between racial and ethnic identities.<ref>Reinelt, Janelle. "Forum: Creative Ambivalence And Precarious Futures: Women In British Theatre." vol. 62, no. 4 (Dec 2010), p. 553.(n.d.): Web. 14 March. 2016.</ref>
