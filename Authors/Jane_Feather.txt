'''Jane Feather''' (born '''Jane Robotham''' 1945 in [[Cairo]], [[Egypt]]) is a popular British–American writer of [[historical romance|historical]] [[romance novels]]. In 1984 she wrote five contemporary romances under the pseudonym '''Claudia Bishop'''. She is a ''New York Times''-bestselling, award–winning writer, and has more than ten&nbsp;million [[romance novels]] in print.<ref name="RH">{{cite web|title=Author Spotlight – Jane Feather |url=http://www.randomhouse.com/author/results.pperl?authorid=8466&view=full_sptlght |publisher=Random House |accessdate=2007-12-27}}</ref>

==Biography==
Jane Robotham was born on 1945 in [[Cairo]], ([[Egypt]]), and grew up in New Forest, in the south of England.<ref name="RH" /> She has a master's degree in applied social studies from [[Oxford University]].{{Citation needed|date=December 2007}} She is married and has three children.{{Citation needed|date=December 2007}}

In 1978 she moved with her husband and children to [[New Jersey]], where she worked as a psychiatric social worker.{{Citation needed|date=December 2007}}

In 1981 she began her writing career after she and her family moved to Washington D.C.<ref name="RH" /> Since printing in 1984 she wrote five contemporary [[romance novel]]s under the pen name Claudia Bishop and since 1986 has written historical romances under her name, Jane Feather.
