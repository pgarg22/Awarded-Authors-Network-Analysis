{{short description|American poet and author (born 1932)}}
'''Peter Meinke''' (born 1932 in Brooklyn, New York) is an [[United States|American]] [[poet]] and [[author]]. He has published 18 books of poems and short stories. ''The Piano Tuner'', won the 1986 [[Flannery O'Connor Award for Short Fiction]]. His poetry has received many awards, including two [[National Endowment for the Arts|NEA Fellowship]]s and three prizes from the [[Poetry Society of America]].  His work has appeared in ''[[The New Yorker]]'', ''[[Atlantic Monthly]]'', ''[[Poetry Magazine|Poetry]]'', and other magazines.

==Biography==
Raised in [[Mountain Lakes, New Jersey]], Meinke graduated in 1950 from [[Mountain Lakes High School]] and was inducted into its hall of fame in 2016.<ref>[https://hs.mlschools.org/our_school/alumni_hall_of_fame/2016_inductees/peter_meinke Alumni Hall of Fame: Peter Meinke Class of 1950], Mountain Lakes High School. Accessed September 8, 2019.</ref>

Meinke and his wife, the artist Jeanne Clark, have lived in [[St. Petersburg, Florida]] since 1966. For 27 years, Meinke was a professor at [[Eckerd College]], where he was a director of the EC Writing Workshop. In February 2004, he was inducted as a foundation member into the Eckerd College [[Phi Beta Kappa]] chapter, Zeta of Florida. He also served on the faculty of the Eckerd College's Third Annual Writers in Paradise Conference in January 2007. From 2003 through 2005, he held the Darden Chair in Creative Writing at [[Old Dominion University]].<ref>[http://www.eckerd.edu/news/index.php?f=detail&id=1924 "Writers in Paradise Conference Readings Held Nightly Through Jan. 27"], Eckerd College News Center website, published January 21, 2007.</ref>
