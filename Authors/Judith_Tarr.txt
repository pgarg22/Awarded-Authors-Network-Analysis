'''Judith Tarr''' (born January 30, 1955)<ref name=bio>{{Cite web|title = Judith Tarr - Summary Bibliography|url = http://www.isfdb.org/cgi-bin/ea.cgi?1420|publisher = [[Internet Speculative Fiction Database]] |access-date = October 18, 2015}}</ref> is an American [[fantasy]] and [[science fiction]] author.

== Life ==
Tarr was born in [[Augusta, Maine]] on January 30, 1955.<ref name=bio/> She is the daughter of Earle A. Tarr, Jr. (a waterworks manager and salesman of real estate), and Regina (a teacher).<ref name="Judith Tarr's Bibliography">{{Cite web|title = Judith Tarr's Bibliography|url = http://www.sff.net/people/judith-tarr/biblio.html|website = www.sff.net|access-date = September 16, 2015|url-status = dead|archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20160303175701/http://www.sff.net/people/judith-tarr/biblio.html|archive-date = March 3, 2016|df = mdy-all}}</ref><ref name="bs">"Tarr, Judith" in [[Brian Stableford|Stableford, Brian M.]] ''The A to Z of Fantasy Literature''.Lanham (Md.) : Scarecrow Press, 2009. {{ISBN|9780810868298}} (p. 397)</ref><ref name="as">Sawyer, Andy. "Tarr, Judith", in the ''St. James Guide To Fantasy Writers'', ed. [[David Pringle]]. London, St. James Press, 1996, {{ISBN|1-55862-205-5}},(pp. 551-2).</ref> She received her B.A. in Latin and English from [[Mount Holyoke College]] in 1976, and has an M.A. in Classics from [[Cambridge University]], and an M.A. and PhD in Medieval Studies from [[Yale University]].<ref name="amazon">{{cite web|url=https://www.amazon.com/Judith-Tarr/e/B000AQ8RZE|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20091119100223/http://www.amazon.com/Judith-Tarr/e/B000AQ8RZE|url-status=dead|archive-date=November 19, 2009|title=Amazon.com: Judith Tarr: Books, Biography, Blog |access-date=November 27, 2016}}</ref><ref name=":2">{{cite web|title = Judith Tarr|url = http://us.macmillan.com/author/judithtarr|access-date = October 2, 2014|publisher = [[Macmillan Publishers]]}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title = Judith Tarr (1955-) Biography - Writings, Sidelights - Personal, Addresses, Career, Member, Honors Awards|url = http://biography.jrank.org/pages/368/Tarr-Judith-1955.html|website = biography.jrank.org|access-date = September 20, 2015}}</ref> She taught Latin at [[Wesleyan University]] from 1990 to 1993.<ref>{{Cite journal|url = http://www.wesleyan.edu/classics/newsletters/Juno2.pdf|title = Where are they now?|date = July 1996|journal = Juno's Peacock|access-date = September 20, 2015|publisher = Wesleyan University, Department of Classical Studies|issue = 2|page =8}}</ref>

She breeds [[Lipizzan]] horses at ''Dancing Horse Farm'', her home in [[Vail, Arizona]].<ref name=":2" /> The romantic fantasies that she writes under the name ''Caitlin Brennan''<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|title = Judith Tarr {{!}} BVC Authors {{!}} Book View Cafe|url = http://bookviewcafe.com/bookstore/bvc-author/judith-tarr/|website = bookviewcafe.com|access-date = September 15, 2015}}</ref> feature "dancing horses" modeled on those that she raises.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://us.macmillan.com/author/|title=Caitlin Brennan {{!}} Authors {{!}} Macmillan|website=US Macmillan|language=en-US|access-date=2019-01-28}}</ref>

==''The Hound and the Falcon'' Trilogy ==
Tarr's ''[[The Hound and the Falcon]]'' trilogy  (''[[The Isle of Glass]]'', 1985; ''[[The Golden Horn (novel)|The Golden Horn]]'', 1985; ''[[The Hounds of God]]'', 1986) is a fantasy trilogy set in twelfth and thirteenth century Europe. The trilogy focuses on a race of [[Elves]] with supernatural powers, secretly living in medieval society.<ref name="sk">Kelso, Sylvia. "The God in the Pentagram: Religion and Spirituality in Modern Fantasy".''[[International Association for the Fantastic in the Arts|Journal of the Fantastic in the Arts]]''. 18(1) (pgs. 61-82). 2007.</ref> The trilogy's main character is Alf, a young monk who is also an Elf. The trilogy features historical personages such as [[Francis of Assisi]] and King [[Richard I of England|Richard I]] as characters.<ref name="as" /><ref name="sk" />

==''Alamut''==
In an interview, Tarr stated that she became interested in the period of the Crusades after hearing the 1971 record album, ''Music of the Crusades'' by [[David Munrow]] and the [[Early Music Consort of London]].<ref name="bg">Mah, Emily. [https://www.blackgate.com/2012/04/28/the-best-of-modern-arabian-fantasy-judith-tarr-and-alamut/ "The Best of Modern Arabian Fantasy, Part II: Judith Tarr and Alamut"] ''Blackgate.com''. 28th April 2012. Retrieved 9th April, 2020.</ref> This inspired her to write her novel set in the period of the Crusades, ''Alamut''.<ref name="bg" /> Tarr consulted the history books ''[[The Crusades Through Arab Eyes]]'' by [[Amin Maalouf]] and ''[[The Assassins: A Radical Sect in Islam]]'' by [[Bernard Lewis]], as part of her research for ''Alamut''.<ref name="bg" />

==''Epona'' series==
Tarr's ''Epona'' series of novels (''White Mare's Daughter'', 1998; ''The Shepherd Kings'', 1999; ''Lady of Horses'', 2000; ''Daughter of Lir'', 2001) is set in prehistoric Europe. The ''Epona'' series dramatizes the ideas of archaeologist [[Marija Gimbutas]] about a matriarchal society existing in Paleolithic Europe.<ref>Sperring, Kari. [http://strangehorizons.com/non-fiction/columns/matrilines-fire-from-heaven-judith-tarr/ "Matrilines: Fire From Heaven - Judith Tarr"]. ''[[Strange Horizons]]'', 27 June 2016. Retrieved 10 April 2020.</ref>
