'''George F. Walker''' (born August 23, 1947) is a [[Canadians|Canadian]] [[playwright]] and [[screenwriter]]. He is one of Canada's most prolific playwrights, and also one of the most widely produced Canadian dramatists both in Canada and internationally.

==Early years==
Walker was born in [[Toronto]], [[Ontario]] on August 23, 1947 to Florence and Malcolm Walker.<ref name=cca>{{cite encyclopedia |year=1996 |title =volume 1 |encyclopedia=contemporary canadian authors |publisher=gale canada |location=444 front st. west toronto}}</ref> He was raised in the east end of Toronto, which was considered a working-class neighbourhood.<ref name=dlb>{{cite encyclopedia|year=1987|title =volume 60:canadian writers since 1960 second series|encyclopedia=dictionary of literary biography|publisher=gale research |location=835 pendoscot building detroit}}</ref> This neighbourhood is what made him loyal to the city lifestyle, which is shown in his work later on in life.

He attended [[Riverdale Collegiate Institute]] but did not graduate from high school. After he dropped out of high school he worked a variety of different jobs, and while doing these jobs he had been writing many stories and poems.<ref name=dlb /> When working at a job as a taxi driver he saw a pamphlet from the [[Factory Theatre]] Lab inviting scripts to be submitted.<ref name=ce>[http://www.thecanadianencyclopedia.com/index.cfm?PgNm=TCE&Params=A1ARTA0009707.html "Craig Walker"]</ref>

==Career==
Walker has written over 30 stage scripts and has also written many television shows, radio shows, and also some movies. Some of the television shows that he had written for were ''[[Due South]]'', ''[[The Newsroom (Canadian TV series)|The Newsroom]]'', ''[[This is Wonderland]]'', ''[[The Line (TV series)|The Line]]'' and ''[[Living in Your Car]]'',<ref name=tm>http://this.org/magazine/2010/08/16/george-f-walker-hbo-canada/.html{{dead link|date=December 2017 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }} "Emily Landau"</ref> and the film ''[[Niagara Motel]]''. Most of his screen projects are cowritten with [[Dani Romain]].

Walker was first known for his playwriting.<ref name=hl>{{Citation| last=Lane| first=H.| title=George F. Walker| publisher=Playwrights Canada Press| place=Toronto, Ontario| year=2006}}</ref> In 1997, he published a cycle of six new plays, all of which took place in the same suburban motel room. He wrote plays for most of his career but since 2000 he has worked more in television and film.

In 2006, he was made a Member of the [[Order of Canada]]. In 2009, he received the [[Governor General's Performing Arts Award]] for Lifetime Artistic Achievement, Canada's highest honour in the performing arts.<ref>{{cite web|title=George F. Walker biography|url=http://ggpaa.ca/award-recipients/2009/walker-george-f.aspx|publisher=Governor General's Performing Arts Awards Foundation|access-date=11 February 2015}}</ref>
