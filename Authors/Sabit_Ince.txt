'''Sabit İnce''' (born 27 August 1954 in [[Nevşehir]]) is a Turkish poet, writer and Islamic scholar.

== Early life ==

He graduated from the Political Sciences Faculty of [[Marmara University]].

== Career==

He was the editor-in-chief of the ''[[Papirus]]'' [[literary magazine]]. Sabit Ince is a member of the [[Turkish literature#Poetry|Second New Generation]] of [[Turkish poetry]], an abstract and [[postmodern]] movement created as a backlash against the more popular-based [[Anadolu Hececileri]] movement. Love, mainly through its erotic entity, is a popular theme of ince's works. Sabit's poems and articles were published in magazines including ''Tore'', ''Devlet'', ''Deniz Postası'', ''Bizim Anadolu '', ''Tercüman'', ''Hergün'', ''Ortadogu'', ''[[Kayseri]] Hakimiyet'' and ''Yeni Kayseri ''.
