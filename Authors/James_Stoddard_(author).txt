'''James Stoddard''' is an American [[fantasy]] [[author]].  He lives in [[West Texas]], United States, where he is also a music recording and engineering instructor.  Stoddard's first published short story, ''The Perfect Day'', was penned under the name James Turpin and appeared in ''[[Amazing Stories]]'' in 1985.

Stoddard won the 1999 [[Compton Crook Award]] for his novel ''The High House''.<ref name="compcrk">{{cite web |url=http://www.bsfs.org/bsfsccw.htm |title=Compton Crook Award Winners |publisher=[[Baltimore Science Fiction Society]] |accessdate=September 5, 2012}}</ref> The book was also nominated for the 1999 Mythopoeic Fantasy Award,<ref>[http://www.mythsoc.org/awards/awards-1999.htm]</ref> placed in the top ten of the Locus Best First Novels of 1999,<ref>https://web.archive.org/web/20070903125611/http://www.locusmag.com/SFAwards/Db/Locus1999.html</ref> and was nominated for the Crawford Award by the International Association of the Fantastic in the Arts.<ref>https://web.archive.org/web/20070807201903/http://www.locusmag.com/SFAwards/Db/Crawford1999.html</ref> In 2018 he was one of five judges of the prestigious Phillip K. Dick awards.<ref>https://www.sfadb.com/Philip_K_Dick_Award_2018</ref>
