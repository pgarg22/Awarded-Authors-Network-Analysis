'''Onyeka Nubia''' {{Post-nominals|country=GBR|FRHistS}}<ref>{{Cite web|title=Fellows – N|url=https://files.royalhistsoc.org/wp-content/uploads/2020/06/22162907/RHS-Fellows-N.pdf|access-date=1 September 2020|publisher=[[Royal Historical Society]]}}</ref> is a British historian, writer and lecturer. Writing under the [[pen name]] '''Onyeka''', his works explore the history of [[Black British people]], and [[Multiculturalism#United_Kingdom|multiculturalism in the United Kingdom]]. In 2013, he published the non-fiction work ''[[Blackamoores|Blackamoores: Africans in Tudor England, their Presence, Status and Origins]]'', which detailed the history of [[Black people]] in [[Tudor period|Tudor England]].<ref>"Theory of WAR", ''Voice'', August, 1998 p. 40</ref><ref>[http://www.voxafrica.com/modx/en/ShootTheMessenger/the-genetic-map-of-africa/ Study of African genes]</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Onyeka Nubia|url=https://www.nottingham.ac.uk/History/News-Events/Onyeka-Nubia.aspx|website=University of Nottingham|access-date=9 August 2017|ref=Nottingham}}</ref>

==Career==
Onyeka's third novel, ''The Phoenix'', was awarded the 2009 African Achievers award for Communication and Media for the psychological portrayal of the Black British experience.<ref>{{cite web|title=2009 Award Winners|url=http://africanachievers.webs.com/2009awardwinners.htm|website=African Achievers International|access-date=24 December 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090804193549/http://africanachievers.webs.com/2009awardwinners.htm|archive-date=August 4, 2009}}</ref> 

In 2009 Onyeka was featured on the television programme ''Shoot the Messenger'' on the TV channel ''[[VoxAfrica]]'', discussing the experience of the [[African diaspora]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Study of African genes: who stands to gain most from it?|url=http://www.voxafrica.com/modx/en/ShootTheMessenger/the-genetic-map-of-africa/|website=Voxafrica.com|access-date=24 December 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090515180702/http://www.voxafrica.com/modx/en/ShootTheMessenger/the-genetic-map-of-africa/|archive-date=May 15, 2009|date=November 5, 2009}}</ref>
