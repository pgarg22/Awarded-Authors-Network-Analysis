'''Pamela Collins Dean Dyer-Bennet''' (born 1953), better known as '''Pamela Dean''', is an American fantasy author whose best-known book is ''[[Tam Lin (novel)|Tam Lin]]'', based on the [[Child Ballads|Child Ballad]] of the [[Tam Lin|same name]], in which the Scottish [[fairy]] story is set on a midwestern college campus loosely based on her alma mater, [[Carleton College]] in [[Minnesota]].<ref name=OfftheShelf2016>{{cite web |url=https://tbplofftheshelf.com/2016/02/03/interview-with-pamela-dean/ |title=Interview with Pamela Dean |last= |first= |date=February 3, 2016 |website=Thunder Bay Public Library |publisher= |access-date=2021-01-26}}</ref>

==Career==
Dean has published six novels and a number of short stories. ''Tam Lin'' and ''The Dubious Hills'' were both nominated for the [[Mythopoeic Fantasy Award for Adult Literature]], in 1992 and 1995 respectively.

She was a member of the writing group [[The Scribblies]], along with [[Emma Bull]], [[Will Shetterly]], [[Kara Dalkey]], Nate Bucklin, [[Patricia Wrede]] and [[Steven Brust]], and was a contributor to the [[Liavek]] [[shared world|shared-world]] anthologies. She is a member of the [[Pre-Joycean Fellowship]].

As of 2012, Dean reports that ''Going North,'' the future "joint sequel to ''The Dubious Hills'' and ''The Whim of the Dragon'', has been rejected by [[Viking Press]], leaving her to make further revisions and seek alternative methods for publication.<ref>[http://pameladean.livejournal.com/148001.html Going South] at Dean's blog, dated 2012-09-11.</ref><ref>[http://dd-b.net/pddb/ Official website]</ref>

== Personal life ==
Dean graduated from Carleton College in 1975.<ref name=OfftheShelf2016/>

Charles Mosley, Burke's Peerage Ltd, p. 1254</ref> Dean practices [[polyamory]].<ref>[http://www.strangehorizons.com/2001/20010101/pamela_dean.shtml Interview: Pamela Dean], by [[Mary Anne Mohanraj]]; at ''[[Strange Horizons]]''; published January 1, 2001; retrieved October 9, 2013</ref>
