'''Geeta Tripathee''' ({{lang-ne|[[:ne:गीता त्रिपाठी|गीता त्रिपाठी]]}}; born, [[June 28]], [[1972]]) is a [[Nepali people|Nepali]] [[poet]], [[lyricist]], [[essayist]], [[literary critic]] and [[scholar]]. An eminent writer in Nepali, Geeta Tripathee has two volumes of poetry collection, one of lyrical poems and seven books in other literary genre to her credit. She also writes for newspapers on issues concerning women, environment and societal injustice.<ref name="lila1">{{cite book |author=Lila Luitel|title=नेपाली महिला साहित्यकार|trans-title=Nepali Women Literati |chapter=Women Poets in Nepali Poetry|location= Kathmandu|page=215|date=2012|publisher=Shabdartha Prakashan|isbn=978-9937248051}}</ref><ref name="nayapatrika"/><ref name="kriti">{{citation|title=कृति विश्लेषण: प्रायोगिक आयाम|first=Geeta|last=Tripathee|date=2008|publisher=[[Ratna Pustak Bhandar]] ISBN 978999338836}}</ref><ref name="gt1">{{cite journal |last=Geeta|first=Tripathee |date=2018 |title=गहन पर्यावरणको सैद्धान्तिक अवधारणा Gahan Paryavaranko Saiddhantik Awadharana|url= https://www.nepjol.info/index.php/TUJ/article/view/24724 |journal= Tribhuvan University Journal |volume=32 |issue=2 |pages= 281–294|doi= 10.3126/tuj.v32i2.24724|doi-access=free }}</ref><ref name="GT2">{{cite web|url=https://nagariknews.nagariknetwork.com/news/122620

Geeta Tripathee is the recipient of 'Padmakanya Gold Medal - 2000', conferred by the [[Government of Nepal]]. She received 'Best Lyricist Award' in 2008 from 'Sanskritik Sansthan', the major cultural adjunct of [[Government of Nepal|Nepal Government]].<ref name="jagadamba"/>

== Early life ==
Geeta Tripathee was born on June 28, 1972 in [[Kharelthok]], [[Kavrepalanchok district|Kavrepalanchok]] to an educator Bedraj Thapaliya and Ramadevi Thapaliya. She completed her [[School Leaving Certificate (Nepal)|high school education]] in Kharelthok in 1988, and moved to Kathmandu for further education. Tripathee joined [[:ne:पद्मकन्या बहुमुखी क्याम्पस|Padmakanya Campus]] in Kathmandu and graduated in 1993. She married to Yadavraj Tripathee in 1989 while studying in Padmakanya college. Tripathee continued her study and completed her [[Master's degree]] in Nepali literature in 1998 with a gold medal by becoming the University topper. After completing her master's degree, she started teaching the subject of her interest and choice in different colleges under Tribhuvan University and [[Purbanchal University]]. Tripathee kept her writing carrier continued during her college days and afterwards. Later, she earned [[PhD]] in Nepali literature from Tribhuvan University in 2017.<ref name="samalochakkosh"/><ref name="nayapatrika"/><ref name="lila1"/>

== Career ==
Geeta Tripathee is a multi-genre writer. She has written two books of poetry, one book of lyrical poems, one book of essays and several books on [[literary criticism]].<ref name="lila2">{{cite book |author=Lila Luitel|title=नेपाली महिला साहित्यकार|trans-title=Nepali Women Literati |chapter= Women Writers in Nepali Literary Criticism |location= Kathmandu|page=215|date=2012|publisher=Shabdartha Prakashan|isbn=978-9937248051}}</ref>

Geeta Tripathee's [[Creative work|works]] are translated into other languages like [[English language|English]], [[Hindi]], [[Japanese language|Japanese]] and [[Korean language|Korean]]; and are published in notable [[literary journal]]s abroad. Tripathee has taken part in numbers of national and international literary events as a poet, presenter and [[Public speaking|speaker]]. Tripathee participated in South Asian Literature Festivals organized by [[Foundation of SAARC Writers and Literature]] in [[New Delhi]] in 2010 and 2017 as [[Nepali Literature|Nepali]] delegate poet.<ref name="foswal1">{{cite web|url=http://www.foundationsaarcwriters.com/add_peot_table_des.php?table_id=8&poet_id=1&des_id=133|title=Geeta Tipathee|publisher=Foundation of SAARC Writers and Literature|access-date=2020-04-17}}</ref><ref name="lifeandlegends1">{{cite web|url=http://lifeandlegends.com/geeta-tripathee-translated-by-mahesh-paudyal/?fbclid=IwAR1HX9Po88M9rrBxLN34FcC3TQ6gXAYGsM6jaFTwPAerMH-y-PB7_VpyC2Y|title=Geeta Tripathee: Translated by Mahesh Paudyal|first=Geeta|last=Tripathee|translator=Mahesh Poudyal|editor=Kalpna Singh-Chitnis|publisher=Life and Legends|access-date=2020-04-09}}</ref><ref>{{Citation|last=Tripathee|first=Geeta|title =Mid-Life Thoughts|translator=Bindesh Dhakal|journal= Snow Jowel|issue=6|page=13|publisher=Grey Sparrow Press|editor=Daine Smith|location= USA |date=1 March 2015|issn=2157-3646 }}</ref><ref name="satchidanandna1">{{citation|title=The Songs We Share|editor=[[K.
