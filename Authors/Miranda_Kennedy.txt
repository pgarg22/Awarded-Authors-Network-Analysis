'''Miranda Kennedy''' (born 1975) is an American journalist and writer. Her first book, ''Sideways on a Scooter: Life and Love in India,'' was published by Random House in 2011.<ref>{{cite book|url=http://www.randomhouse.com/book/91595/sideways-on-a-scooter-by-miranda-kennedy|title=Sideways on a Scooter by Miranda Kennedy {{!}} PenguinRandomHouse.com|accessdate=September 6, 2021}}</ref> Part memoir, part reported nonfiction, it tells the story of several Indian women to describe the slow pace of social and cultural change in India.<ref>{{citation|publisher=International Reporting Project|title=Relationships Between Tradition and Modernity|date=June 21, 2011|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QHtH7eRY56I|accessdate=September 6, 2021}}</ref> The book received praise from ''[[The Washington Post]]'',<ref>{{cite news|last=Bonos|first=Lisa|url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/entertainment/books/sideways-on-a-scooter-is-a-memoir-by-miranda-kennedy/2011/06/29/gIQA1qcpEI_story.html |title="Sideways on a Scooter" is a memoir by Miranda Kennedy|newspaper=[[The Washington Post]]|date=July 14, 2011|accessdate=September 6, 2021}}</ref> ''Macleans''<ref>Dafna Izenberg, [http://www.macleans.ca/2011/05/03/sideways-on-a-scooter-life-and-love-in-india/ 'Sideways on a Scooter: Life and Love in India'], Maclean's, May 3, 2011. Retrieved September 6, 2021.</ref> and ''[[Kirkus Reviews]]'' among others.<ref>[https://www.kirkusreviews.com/book-reviews/miranda-kennedy/sideways-scooter/ 'Sideways on a Scooter'], ''[[Kirkus Reviews]]'', January 15, 2011. Retrieved September 6, 2021.</ref>

Kennedy lived in New Delhi, India for five years, as a reporter covering South Asia for [[National Public Radio]] and American Public Media's Marketplace Radio.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://internationalreportingproject.org/fellows-editors/profile/kennedy-miranda|title=Kennedy, Miranda â€” International Reporting Project|website=internationalreportingproject.org|accessdate=September 6, 2021}}</ref>

Kennedy is the senior editor at National Public Radio's [[Morning Edition]]. She holds an MFA in creative nonfiction from [[Bennington College]] in Vermont and a BA degree from [[Trinity College Dublin]] in Ireland.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.mirandakennedy.com/#!the-author|title=Miranda Kennedy / Author & Journalist|website=Miranda Kennedy / Author & Journalist|accessdate=September 6, 2021}}</ref>
