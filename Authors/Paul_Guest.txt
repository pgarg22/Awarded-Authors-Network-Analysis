'''Paul Guest''' (born in [[Chattanooga]], [[Tennessee]]) is an American [[poet]] and [[memoirist]].

When he was twelve Paul broke the third and fourth [[vertebrae]] in his neck in a bicycle accident, bruising his [[spinal cord]] and paralyzing him from the neck down.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://thestory.org/archive/the_story_762_Poet.mp3/view |title=Archived copy |accessdate=2009-08-23 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20090821191927/http://thestory.org/archive/the_story_762_Poet.mp3/view |archivedate=2009-08-21 }}</ref>  He is a [[quadriplegic]]. He graduated from [[University of Tennessee at Chattanooga]] and from [[Southern Illinois University]] with an M.F.A. in 1999.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.versedaily.org/aboutpaulguest2.shtml|title=Verse Daily:  Paul Guest|website=www.versedaily.org|access-date=2016-04-13}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://mfacarbondale.blogspot.com/2011/04/mfa-graduate-paul-guest-awarded.html|title=Bulletin Board: MFA Carbondale: MFA Graduate Paul Guest Awarded Guggenheim Fellowship!!|date=2011-04-07|website=Graduate Program in Creative Writing at Southern Illinois University Carbondale|access-date=2016-04-13}}</ref> He lives in [[Charlottesville, Virginia]].

His poems appear in ''[[Harper's]]'', ''[[The Paris Review]]'', ''[[Tin House]]'', ''[[The Kenyon Review]]'', ''[[The Missouri Review]]'', ''[[Slate (magazine)|Slate]]'' and elsewhere.
