'''Maoilios Caimbeul''' (''Myles Campbell''; born 23 March 1944) is a [[Scottish people|Scottish]] writer of [[poetry]], [[prose]] and [[children's literature]]. He writes in [[Scottish Gaelic]].

Caimbeul, whose forename 'Maoilios' means "servant of Jesus" in Scottish Gaelic,<ref>[http://www.behindthename.com/name/maoilios Meaning, origin and history of the name Maoilios]</ref>{{unreliable source?|date=February 2017}} was born in [[Staffin]] on the [[Isle of Skye]] to a [[Free Church of Scotland (post 1900)|Free Church of Scotland]] [[missionary]] father and spent much of his childhood travelling between the isles of [[Isle of Skye|Skye]] and [[Isle of Lewis|Lewis]].

He joined the [[Merchant Navy]] at the age of sixteen and consequently experienced a wide variety of nations and cultures. It was not until he was 26 that he became fully literate in [[Scottish Gaelic|Gaelic]] (though he had always been a fluent speaker and capable of reading) and at 30 he graduated from the [[University of Edinburgh]] and [[Jordanhill College]] before embarking upon a career teaching [[Scottish Gaelic|Gaelic]]. He spent time teaching in [[Tobermory, Mull|Tobermory]], [[Isle of Mull|Mull]] and [[Gairloch]].

As well as being a prominent poet, Maoilios is also noted as a writer of [[prose]] and [[children's literature]]. He has won numerous prizes for his writing and is known for his writing both within Scotland and internationally. He was crowned the Bard at the [[Royal National Mòd]] in 2002 and he was the [[writer in residence]] at [[Sabhal Mòr Ostaig]] for 2008.
