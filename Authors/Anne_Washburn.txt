'''Anne Washburn''' is an American playwright.

==Life==
Washburn graduated from [[Reed College]]<ref>{{Cite web|title = The Electric Mr. Burns|url = http://www.reed.edu/reed_magazine/december2013/articles/features/washburn.html|website = Reed Magazine|accessdate = 2015-08-19}}</ref> and from [[New York University]], with an M.F.A.<ref name=whiting>{{Cite web|url=https://www.whiting.org/awards/winners/anne-washburn|title=Anne Washburn|website=www.whiting.org|accessdate=Jul 18, 2019}}</ref>

Her plays have been produced in [[New York City]] by [[Cherry Lane Theatre]], [[Clubbed Thumb]], [[The Civilians]], [[Vineyard Theatre]], [[Dixon Place]], and [[Soho Repertory Theatre]]—and elsewhere by [[American Repertory Theater]], [[Woolly Mammoth Theatre Company]], [[Two River Theater Company|New Jersey's Two River Theater Company]], [[Studio Theatre (Washington, D.C.)|Washington DC's Studio Theater]], and [[London|London's]] [[Gate Theatre (London)|Gate Theatre]] and [[Almeida Theatre]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://actorstheatre.org/cast-crew/anne-washburn/|title=Actors Theater of Louisville: Anne Washburn|accessdate=Jul 18, 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110123023720/http://actorstheatre.org/cast-crew/anne-washburn/|archive-date=January 23, 2011|url-status=dead}}</ref><ref name=newdram>{{Cite web|url=https://newdramatists.org/anne-washburn|title=Anne Washburn|website=New Dramatists|accessdate=Jul 18, 2019}}</ref>

Her 2012 play ''[[Mr. Burns, a Post-Electric Play]]'' received a [[Drama League Award]] nomination for Outstanding Production and was praised by ''[[The New York Times]]'' as "downright brilliant."<ref>{{Cite news|title = 'Mr. Burns, a Post-Electric Play,' at Playwrights Horizons|url = https://www.nytimes.com/2013/09/16/theater/reviews/mr-burns-a-post-electric-play-at-playwrights-horizons.html|newspaper = The New York Times|date = 2013-09-15|access-date = 2015-08-19|issn = 0362-4331|first = Ben|last = Brantley}}</ref> Her play ''A Devil at Noon'' was featured at the 2011 [[Humana Festival|Humana Festival of New American Plays]] and the play ''Sleep Rock Thy Brain''—written with [[Rinne Groff]] and Lucas Hnath—was featured at the 2013  Festival.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://actorstheatre.org/2012/09/19/actors-theatre-is-flying-high-with-sleep-rock-thy-brain-slated-to-premiere-during-the-37th-annual-humana-festival-of-new-american-plays/|title=Actors Theatre of Louisville: ''Sleep Rock Thy Brain'', slated to Premiere during the 37th Annual Humana Festival|accessdate=Jul 18, 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120927042243/http://actorstheatre.org/2012/09/19/actors-theatre-is-flying-high-with-sleep-rock-thy-brain-slated-to-premiere-during-the-37th-annual-humana-festival-of-new-american-plays|archive-date=September 27, 2012|url-status=dead}}</ref> In 2015,  ''10 Out of 12'' played at the Soho Rep theater.<ref>{{Cite news|title = Anne Washburn Brings Backstage Drama to Center Stage in '10 Out of 12'|url = https://www.nytimes.com/2015/05/31/theater/anne-washburn-brings-backstage-drama-to-center-stage-in-10-out-of-12.html|newspaper = The New York Times|date = 2015-05-29|access-date = 2015-08-19|issn = 0362-4331|first = Alexis|last = Soloski}}</ref>

Washburn is a member of 13P,<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://13p.org/|title=HOME|website=13p.org|accessdate=Jul 18, 2019}}</ref> an associated artist with The Civilians and New Georges, and an alumna of New Dramatists.<ref name=newdram/>  Her work has been published in [[Theatre Communications Group|American Theatre magazine]].
