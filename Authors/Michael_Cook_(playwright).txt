'''Michael Cook''' (14 February 1933 &ndash; 1 July 1994) was a Canadian playwright known for his plays set in [[Newfoundland and Labrador|Newfoundland]].

==Early life==
Cook was born in [[Fulham]], London, England. He attended boarding schools until age fifteen.<ref name="Leon1985">Lisa De Leon. ''[https://books.google.com/books?id=rJAfAQAAIAAJ Writers of Newfoundland and Labrador: Twentieth Century]''. Jesperson Press; 1 January 1985. {{ISBN|978-0-920502-58-7}}. p. 267.</ref> He served a number of years in the [[British Army]], mostly in Asia, and attended the [[University of Nottingham]], earning teaching qualifications.<ref>[https://www.thecanadianencyclopedia.ca/en/article/michael-cook/ Michael Cook entry] at ''The Canadian Encyclopedia''</ref>  He then settled in Newfoundland in 1965.<ref name="Colombo1984">John Robert Colombo. ''[https://books.google.com/books?id=WdPnEvbEKgQC&pg=PA22 Canadian Literary Landmarks]''. Dundurn; 1 January 1984. {{ISBN|978-1-4597-1798-5}}. p. 22–.</ref>

==Career==
Soon after arriving in Newfoundland, Cook wrote scripts for several radio dramas which were produced in St. John's;<ref name="NewBerger1990">''[https://books.google.com/books?id=FjU5DwAAQBAJ&pg=PT199 Literary History of Canada: Canadian Literature in English, Volume IV (Second Edition)]''. University of Toronto Press, Scholarly Publishing Division; 15 December 1990. {{ISBN|978-1-4875-9116-8}}. p. 199–.</ref> Cook taught theatre arts at [[Memorial University of Newfoundland]];<ref name="Lerena2015">María Jesús Hernáez Lerena. ''[https://books.google.com/books?id=SJDWCgAAQBAJ&pg=PA193 Pathways of Creativity in Contemporary Newfoundland and Labrador]''. Cambridge Scholars Publishing; 18 September 2015. {{ISBN|978-1-4438-8333-7}}. p. 193–.</ref> he reviewed plays and wrote articles about the importance of theatre in the ''St. John's Evening Telegram'' and in the ''Canadian Theatrical Review''.<ref>[https://asc.ucalgary.ca/node/827 "Michael Cook - Biocritical Essay"], by Malcolm Page, University of Calgary. 1994</ref> In the Fall and Spring semesters of 1974/75, he taught a first year English course at Memorial University of Newfoundland.

Cook wrote a number of plays set in Newfoundland, beginning with was ''Tiln'', written in 1971.<ref name="Lerena2015" />  His best-known works were ''Jacob's Wake'' and ''The Head, Guts and Soundbone Dance'', in which Newfoundland provides a sometimes realistic and sometimes symbolic backdrop for his poetic rendering of lives in continual conflict with natural elements.<ref name="Walker2001">Craig Stewart Walker. ''[https://books.google.com/books?id=ym0epMx87McC&pg=PA83 The Buried Astrolabe: Canadian Dramatic Imagination and Western Tradition]''. McGill-Queen's Press - MQUP; 2001. {{ISBN|978-0-7735-2075-2}}. p. 83–.</ref>

Many of Cook's plays include dialogue written in the Newfoundland dialect;<ref name="GilbertTompkins2002">''[https://books.google.com/books?id=1CmIAgAAQBAJ&pg=PA179 Post-Colonial Drama: Theory, Practice, Politics]''. Routledge; 11 September 2002. {{ISBN|978-1-134-87700-3}}. p. 179–.</ref> in spite of this, ''The Head, Guts and Soundbone Dance'' was restaged as far away as Regina, Saskatchewan in 1980.<ref name="WagnerAssociation1985">''[https://books.google.com/books?id=b6EoAAAAMAAJ Contemporary Canadian theatre, new world visions: a collection of essays]''. Simon & Pierre; 1985. {{ISBN|978-0-88924-152-7}}. p. 77.</ref>

==Personal life==
Cook married three times, and fathered twelve children, including actor [[Sebastian Spence]] by his wife, Janis.<ref>[http://www.cbc.ca/news/canada/newfoundland-labrador/director-actor-janis-spence-dead-at-61-1.714350 "Director, actor Janis Spence dead at 61"]. ''CBC News'', Feb 07, 2008</ref>
Cook retained a residence in [[Stratford, Ontario]]. While passing through [[St. John's, Newfoundland and Labrador|St Johns]] on a trip to his summer home on [[Random Island]], Cook became ill and died.<ref>[http://www.heritage.nf.ca/articles/arts/michael-cook.php "Michael Cook"]. ''Heritage Newfoundland'', www.heritage.nf.ca</ref>

==Plays==
* ''Tiln'', 1971.
* ''Colour The Flesh the Colour of Dust'', 1972.
* ''The Head, Guts and Sound Bone Dance'', 1973.
* ''Jacob's Wake'', 1974.
* ''Quiller'', 1975.
* ''Therese's Creed'', 1976.
* ''The Fisherman's Revenge'', 1976. (children's play)
* ''On The Rim of the Curve'', 1977.
* ''The Gayden Chronicles'', 1980.
