'''Geoffrey Brock''' (born October 19, 1964) is an American [[poet]] and translator.

==Education==
He received a master's degree in Fine Arts from the [[University of Florida]] in 1998.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.english.ufl.edu/crw/publications.html |title=Some Alumni & Alumnae of MFA@FLA |publisher=English.ufl.edu |date=2005-05-29 |access-date=2013-08-25 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180430041511/http://www.english.ufl.edu/crw/publications.html |archive-date=2018-04-30 |url-status=dead }}</ref>  He also holds a PhD in Comparative Literature from the [[University of Pennsylvania]].

His poems have been published in ''[[Poetry Magazine]], [[Paris Review]], [[Poetry Nation Review|PN Review]], [[New England Review]], [[The Hudson Review]]'', and ''[[The Best American Poetry 2007]]''.

Since 2006 he has taught Creative Writing & Translation at The [[University of Arkansas]], Fayetteville, where he lives with his wife, the novelist [[Padma Viswanathan]], their two children, and a variable number of animals.

==Awards==
His translations have received a number of prizes and fellowships, including the [[Academy of American Poets]]' [[Raiziss/de Palchi Translation Awards|Raiziss/de Palchi Fellowship]] and a [[Guggenheim Fellowship]]. He received a poetry fellowship from the [[National Endowment for the Arts]] and a [[Stegner Fellowship]] from [[Stanford University]].
