'''Laura Kasischke''' (born 1961) is an American fiction writer and [[American poet|poet]].  She is best known for writing the novels ''[[Suspicious River]]'', ''[[The Life Before Her Eyes]]'' and ''[[White Bird in a Blizzard]]'', all of which have been adapted to film.

==Life and work==
She was born in [[Grand Rapids, Michigan]].
Kasischke attended the [[University of Michigan]] (MFA 1987) and Columbia University.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.alicejamesbooks.org/fire_flower.html|title=Alice James Books > Author Page > Laura Kasischke}}</ref> She lives in [[Chelsea, Michigan]], with her husband and son.

She is the Theodore Roethke Distinguished University Professor of English Language and Literature,<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.lsa.umich.edu/english/grad/mfa/mfaFacDetail.asp?ID=964|title=Laura Kasischke|publisher =University of Michigan|access-date=2013-05-29}}</ref> and of the Residential College at the University of Michigan in Ann Arbor, Michigan.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.lsa.umich.edu/english/grad/mfa/mfaFacDetail.asp?ID=964|title=U-M English MFA Program: Faculty Profile: Laura Kasischke}}</ref>

Kasischke's literary works have been recognized and highlighted at Michigan State University in their Michigan Writers Series.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.lib.msu.edu/branches/vvl/writers/index.jsp  |title=Michigan Writers Series |publisher=Michigan State University Libraries |access-date=2012-07-15}}</ref>

Her novel ''The Life Before Her Eyes'' is the basis for the [[The Life Before Her Eyes|film of the same name]], directed by Vadim Perelman, and starring Uma Thurman and Evan Rachel Wood. Kasischke's work is particularly well received in France, where she is widely read in translation. Her novel ''A moi pour toujours'' (''Be Mine'') was published by Christian Bourgois, and was a national best seller.

Her most recent book of poetry, ''Where Now - New and Selected Poems'',<ref name="auto">{{Cite web|url=https://www.coppercanyonpress.org/books/where-now-new-and-selected-poems-by-laura-kasischke/|title=Where Now: New and Selected Poems by Laura Kasischke}}</ref> was published in 2017 by [[Copper Canyon Press]].

==Awards==
Kasischke was awarded the 2011 [[National Book Critics Circle Award]] in poetry for ''Space, In Chains''.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.coppercanyonpress.org/books/space-in-chains-by-laura-kasischke/|title=Space, in Chains by Laura Kasischke}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.poetryfoundation.org/harriet/2012/03/laura-kasischke-wins-nbcc-award|title=Laura Kasischke Wins NBCC Award by Harriet Staff|first=Poetry|last=Foundation|date=December 26, 2020|website=Poetry Foundation}}</ref> Her work has received the Juniper Prize, the Alice Fay di Castagnola Award from the [[Poetry Society of America]], the [[Pushcart Prize]], the Elmer Holmes Bobst Award for Emerging Writers, and the [[Beatrice Hawley Award]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.alicejamesbooks.org/winners.html|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20080709040335/http://www.alicejamesbooks.org/winners.html|url-status=dead|title=Alice James Books > Beatrice Hawley Award Winners List|archivedate=July 9, 2008}}</ref>  She is the recipient of two fellowships from the National Endowment for the Arts, as well as several Pushcart Prizes. In 2005 she was [[The Frost Place]] poet in residence and in 2009 she was awarded a [[Guggenheim Fellowship]] in Creative Arts - Poetry<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.gf.org/fellows/16467-laura-kasischke|title=Laura Kasischke - John Simon Guggenheim Memorial Foundation}}</ref>
