'''Lyndon Mauriece Hardy''' is an American physicist, fantasy author, and business owner.

==Biography==
Hardy is a member of the [[Science Fiction Writers of America]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.sfwa.org/member-links/member-list/|title=Science Fiction Writers of America Member Directory|publisher=Science Fiction Writers of America|accessdate=6 March 2010}}</ref>  He attended [[California Institute of Technology]] as an undergraduate and the [[University of California Berkeley]] for his Ph.D.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.linkedin.com/pub/lyndon-hardy/5/779/426|title=LinkedIn entry for Lyndon Hardy |publisher=LinkedIn|accessdate=6 March 2010}}</ref>

In his college years, he became fascinated with fantasy literature. In addition to being a fantasy author, he worked for 30 years at the aerospace company [[TRW Inc.|TRW]], and started a computer consulting company named Alodar Systems Inc. that, among other things, helped develop intelligence tools for medical research and enterprise resource planning software for small businesses. He is married and has two daughters.

In 1961, Hardy masterminded the [[Great Rose Bowl Hoax]], in which Caltech students rearranged the cards used by Washington to spell out words during halftime.<ref>{{Citation | last = Crowe | first = Jerry | title = Fifty Years Ago, Caltech Pulled Off a Prank for the Memory Banks at Rose Bowl | newspaper = Los Angeles Times | date = 2010-12-26 | url = http://articles.latimes.com/2010/dec/26/sports/la-sp-crowe-20101227 | accessdate = 2010-12-27}}</ref>
