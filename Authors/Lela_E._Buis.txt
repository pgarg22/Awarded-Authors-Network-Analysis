'''Lela E. Buis''' is a speculative fiction writer, playwright, poet and artist who was born in [[Middlesboro, KY]]. She graduated from [[Florida State University]] (FSU) and the [[Florida Institute of Technology]], and worked in engineering for a number of years at [[Kennedy Space Center]] in [[Merritt Island, Florida]]. She currently resides in Tennessee.

==Background==
Lela Buis was born the elder of two daughters of Joseph N. and Lena Grace Buis. When Joe was discharged from the army after [[World War II]], the couple took ownership of a [[Century Farm]] in [[Claiborne County, Tennessee]]. Lela Buis attended Claiborne County High School,<ref>{{citation |url=http://www.classmates.com/yearbooks/Claiborne-County-High-School/4182722925/?page=42|title=Claiborne County High School|accessdate=4 June 2016}}</ref> and as a child was active in farm related organizations such as 4-H clubs.<ref>{{citation |url=http://news-from-mtsu.blogspot.com/2007/05/386-claiborne-county-farm-joins-states.html|title=Claiborne County farm joins state's Century Farms Program: 110-Year-Old Buis Ridge Farm Recognized for Agricultural Contributions|date=25 May 2007|accessdate=24 April 2015}}</ref> At FSU she worked in research on [[E-learning|Computer Assisted Instruction]]<ref>{{citation |url=http://files.eric.ed.gov/fulltext/ED063790.pdf|title=CAI Center Annual Progress Report|date=1 April 1972|accessdate=24 April 2015}}</ref> was a member of the [[Alpha Lambda Delta]]<ref>{{citation |url=https://archive.org/stream/tallyho211968flor/tallyho211968flor_djvu.txt|title=Full text of "Tally-Ho"|date=1968|accessdate=24 April 2015}}</ref> and [[Phi Kappa Phi]]<ref>{{citation |url=http://www.e-yearbook.com/yearbooks/Florida_State_University_Renegade_Yearbook/1970/Page_221.html|title=Phi Kappa Phi|date=1970|accessdate=6 May 2015}}</ref> honor societies and was active in [[Yoshukai Karate]].<ref>{{citation |title=Who's Who Among Students in American Universities and Colleges|page=188|author=Randall, H.P.|date=1991}}</ref> She performed with the Florida State University Dance Theatre,<ref>{{citation |url=http://fsu.digital.flvc.org/islandora/object/fsu%3A154551/datastream/OBJ/view|title=Dance Theatre Idioms|accessdate=24 April 2015}}</ref> and from 1983 served as president of the [[Titusville, Florida]], non-profit Community Dance Theater.<ref>{{citation |url=https://opencorporates.com/companies/us_fl/N00353|title=Community Dance Theatre, Inc.|accessdate=24 April 2015}}</ref> From 2001-2003, she was active in the Brevard Playwrights Workshop.<ref>{{citation |url=http://www.playwrightsworkshopbrevard.org/uploads/3/1/6/1/3161503/pwb-production_history_feb_2014.pdf|title=Production History of Playwrights Workshop (1985-2014)|date=2014|accessdate=25 April 2015}}</ref>

==Writing career==
Buis' first listed short story sale in the science fiction and fantasy genre was in 1991.<ref name=DB /> She often writes fiction inclusive of minorities. From 1992 to 2002, she served as director of The Best of Soft Science Fiction Contest.<ref>{{Cite book |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=Ve00M5R0adMC&q=Lela+E.+Buis|title=Science Fiction and Fantasy Writer's Sourcebook: Where to Sell Your Manuscripts|author=Borcherding, D. H.|year=1996|isbn=9780898797626|accessdate=23 June 2013}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.oocities.org/soho/den/5752/index.html|title=The Best of Soft Science Fiction Contest|accessdate=23 June 2013}}</ref> From 2002-2003 she served as editor of the online literary and arts magazine ''Proteus Review'', published by [[Brevard Community College]].<ref>{{citation|url=http://www.brevardcc.edu/proteus/Titlepage.htm |title=Proteus Review |date=2002 |accessdate=24 April 2015 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20021015150901/http://www.brevardcc.edu/proteus/Titlepage.htm |archivedate=October 15, 2002 }}</ref> Her short stories, poems and articles have appeared in science fiction and fantasy publications including [[Galaxy magazine|Galaxy]], [[Thirteenth Moon]], [[Science Fiction Poetry Association|Star*line]] and others.<ref>{{cite book |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=9h6eZ_9v-DYC&q=Heldreth,+Leonard;+Pharr,+Mary+%281999%29.+The+Blood+Is+the+Life:+Vampires+in+Literature.+Bowling+Green+State+University+Press.&pg=PR4|title=The Blood Is the Life: Vampires in Literature|first1=Leonard|last1=Heldreth|first2=Mary|last2=Pharr|year=1999|publisher=Bowling Green State University Press|isbn=9780879728038}}</ref> That Ridge Press has recently released collections of her short stories and poetry.<ref name= DB>{{cite web |url=http://www.isfdb.org/cgi-bin/ea.cgi?11939|title=Lela E. Buis - Summary Bibliography|publisher=Internet Speculative Fiction Database|accessdate=23 June 2013}}</ref>

Buis is a member of the Knoxville Writers Guild, the Tennessee Mountain Writers, the [[Science Fiction and Fantasy Writers of America]] and the [[Science Fiction Poetry Association]].
