'''Margo Taft Stever''' is an American poet, whose poetry collections include ''Cracked Piano''<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.theexaminernews.com/sleepy-hollow-poet-to-release-collection-on-a-longtime-family-secret/|title=Sleepy Hollow Poet to Release Collection on a Longtime Family Secret|website=www.theexaminernews.com|access-date=2019-06-05}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://cavankerrypress.org/product/cracked-piano/|title=Cracked Piano|website=CavanKerry Press|language=en-US|access-date=2019-06-05}}</ref> (CavanKerry Press, 2019); ''Ghost Moose'' (Kattywompus Press, 2019); ''The Lunatic Ball'' (Kattywompus Press, 2015); The ''Hudson Line'' (Main Street Rag, 2012); ''Frozen Spring'' (Mid-List Press First Series Award, 2002) and ''Reading the Night Sky'' (Riverstone Press Poetry Chapbook Competition, 1996).<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.goodreads.com/author/show/7152864.Margo_Taft_Stever|title=Margo Taft Stever|website=www.goodreads.com|access-date=2019-06-05}}</ref>

==Biography==
Stever is a graduate of [[Harvard University]], and is a recipient of an [[Master of Education|Ed.M]] from the [[Harvard Graduate School of Education]], and an [[Master of Fine Arts|M.F.A.]] in Poetry from [[Sarah Lawrence College]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://margotaftstever.com/curriculum-vita|title=Curriculum Vitae|date=2018-11-18|website=MARGO TAFT STEVER|language=en-US|access-date=2019-06-05}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://alum.slc.edu/sslpage.aspx?pid=273|title=News and Announcements from Alums - Sarah Lawrence College|website=alum.slc.edu|access-date=2019-06-05}}</ref>

Stever co-authored the book ''Looking East: William Howard Taft and the 1905 U.S. Diplomatic Mission to China'' (Zhejiang University Press, 2012).<ref>{{Cite book|url=https://www.press.uchicago.edu/ucp/books/book/distributed/L/bo41498363.html|title=Looking East}}</ref>

Her poems, essays, and reviews have appeared widely in magazines and anthologies, including ''Verse Daily''; ''[[Prairie Schooner]]''; ''[[Connecticut Review]]''; “poem-a-day” on poets.org, [[Academy of American Poets]]; [[The Cincinnati Review|''Cincinnati Review'']]; ''upstreet''; ''Plume''; and ''Salamander''.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://margotaftstever.com/reviews|title=Reviews|date=2018-11-27|website=MARGO TAFT STEVER|language=en-US|access-date=2019-06-05}}</ref>

She is founder of the Hudson Valley Writers Center<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.nytimes.com/1994/04/24/realestate/in-the-region-westchester-transforming-a-waterfront-eyesore-into-an-asset.html|title=In the Region/Westchester; Transforming a Waterfront Eyesore Into an Asset|last=Vizard|first=Mary McAleer|date=1994-04-24|work=The New York Times|access-date=2019-06-05|language=en-US|issn=0362-4331}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.nytimes.com/1994/06/19/nyregion/writers-center-turning-philipse-manor-station-into-its-office.html|title=Writers' Center Turning Philipse Manor Station Into Its Office|last=Hershenson|first=Roberta|date=1994-06-19|work=The New York Times|access-date=2019-06-05|language=en-US|issn=0362-4331}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.nytimes.com/1989/07/30/nyregion/an-abandoned-station-inspires-a-literary-quest.html|title=An Abandoned Station Inspires a Literary Quest|last=Hadad|first=Herbert|date=1989-07-30|work=The New York Times|access-date=2019-06-10|language=en-US|issn=0362-4331}}</ref> and founding and current co-editor of Slapering Hol Press.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://thechapbookinterview.com/2013/08/05/margo-taft-stever/|title=Margo Taft Stever on publishing and mentoring chapbook poets|last=lauramadelinewiseman|date=2013-08-05|website=The Chapbook Interview|language=en-US|access-date=2019-06-05}}</ref> She lives in [[Sleepy Hollow, New York]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.westchestermagazine.com/Westchester-Magazine/October-2012/A-Profile-of-Poet-and-Hudson-Valley-Writers-Center-Founder-Margo-Taft-Stever-of-Sleepy-Hollow/|title=A Profile of Poet and Hudson Valley Writers' Center Founder Margo Taft Stever of Sleepy Hollow|website=www.westchestermagazine.com|language=en|access-date=2019-06-05}}</ref>
