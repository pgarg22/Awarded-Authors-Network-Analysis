'''Linda Lael Miller''' (born 1949 as Linda Lael), is a [[best-seller|best-selling]] American author of more than 100 contemporary and [[historical romance|historical]] [[romance novels]].  She has also written under the [[pen name]] '''Lael St. James.'''

==Personal life==
Linda Lael was born in 1949 in [[Washington (U.S. state)|Washington]].  Her father was a [[Marshal#State and local marshals|town marshal]], although he, along with her uncle [[Jiggs Lael|Jake "Jiggs" Lael]] also competed on the rodeo circuit.<ref name=bexte/> A voracious reader, Linda began writing her own stories when she was 10.<ref name=rhodes>{{citation|last=Rhodes|first=Cindy|date=February 2007|publisher=Sidewalks TV|url=http://sidewalkstv.com/webclips/m/lindalaelmiller.html|accessdate=January 21, 2010|title=The best selling author talks about 'McKettrick's Luck'|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110716071916/http://sidewalkstv.com/webclips/m/lindalaelmiller.html|archive-date=July 16, 2011|url-status=dead}}</ref>  As a child, Linda often visited with an elderly neighbor and listened to stories of her neighbor's experiences meeting outlaw [[Jesse James]] and witnessing a gunfight involving the [[Dalton Brothers]].<ref name=bexte>{{citation|last=Bexte|first=Martina|title=Linda Lael Miller Comes Full Circle in Life and in Storytelling|date=July 2006|url=http://bookloons.com/cgi-bin/Columns.asp?name=Linda%20Lael%20Miller&type=Interview|accessdate=January 21, 2010|publisher=Bookloons}}</ref>  Lael graduated from high school in [[Northport, WA]] but did not attend college.<ref name=rhodes/>   After her marriage, Miller spent 10 years living in Spokane before moving away. She has lived in Italy and in [[London]]. After five years living in Arizona, Miller moved back to Washington in 2006.<ref name=bexte/>

Miller has established a foundation, the Linda Lael Miller Scholarships for Women.  These scholarships are intended to allow women who are struggling to further their education, either through a trade school or a traditional college or university.  The scholarships are funded by the fees Miller commands at speaking engagements, and winners are allowed to spend the proceeds on any expense (including childcare and transportation) that will allow her to attend school.<ref name=bexte/><ref name=rhodes/>

==Works==
Her first manuscripts were rejected, but publishers finally purchased ''Fletcher's Woman'', a [[historical romance]].<ref name=arr/>  Since then, she has published over 77 novels, in a variety of [[romance novel]] subgenres, including contemporary, suspense, time-travel, and paranormal.<ref name=bexte/>  The historical romances have been set in a range of time frames, from the Medieval period to the American West.<ref name=arr/>

Miller is best known for her romances set in the [[Western United States]].  One of these, ''McKettrick's Choice'', reached number 15 on the [[New York Times Bestseller List]].<ref name=bexte/> In an interview, Miller stated that: "I love westerns best of all.  To me, the cowboy is the great North American myth, the ideal of honor, courage and persistence we need to live up to."<ref name=bexte/>  She has also described her western settings as "naturally romantic, from the big sky to the lonely prairie, and it's the perfect backdrop for adventure, danger, grand passion, joy, and sorrow—the whole gamut of human emotions".<ref name=arr>{{citation|title=ARR Interview with Linda Lael Miller|publisher=A Romance Review|url=http://www.aromancereview.com/interviews/lindalaelmiller.phtml|accessdate=January 21, 2010}}</ref>

The heroines of Miller's novels are known for being strong, independent women.  Miller has stated that she wants her heroines to be good examples, proving that they can take care of themselves.<ref name=bexte/>

She has been nominated six times for the [[Romance Writers of America]] RITA Award, the highest honor given to a romance author.<ref name=rhodes/>  She has won the Silver Pen Award as well as the ''Romantic Times'' award for Most Sensual Historical Romance.<ref name=rhodes/>
