'''Ernest Thompson''' (born '''Richard Ernest Thompson'''; November 6, 1949) is an American writer, actor, and director. He won an [[Academy Award for Best Adapted Screenplay]] for ''[[On Golden Pond (1981 film)|On Golden Pond]]'', an adaptation of his own [[On Golden Pond (play)|play of the same name]].

==Early life==
Thompson was born as Richard Ernest Thompson in Bellows Falls, Vermont. He spent his early years in Massachusetts, New Hampshire, and Maine, moving to Maryland as a junior high school student. He attended the [[University of Maryland]] and [[The Catholic University of America]], before ultimately graduating cum laude from [[American University]] in 1971.

==Career==
Thompson is known as the author of the play ''[[On Golden Pond (play)|On Golden Pond]]'', which he wrote at the age of 28. The play opened off-Broadway in 1978, starring [[Tom Aldredge]] and [[Frances Sternhagen]]. A great success at the [[John F. Kennedy Center for the Performing Arts|Kennedy Center]], it opened at the New Apollo Theater on Broadway February 28, 1979. Revived the following season at the Century Theatre, ''On Golden Pond'' ran for more than 400 performances. It became a hit [[On Golden Pond (1981 film)|1981 film]], starring [[Katharine Hepburn]] and [[Henry Fonda]]. Thompson won the [[Academy Award]] for [[Academy Award for Best Writing (Adapted Screenplay)|Best Adapted Screenplay]] in 1981 as well as awards from the [[Golden Globe Award|Golden Globes]] and the [[Writers Guild of America]].

''[[The West Side Waltz]]'', Thompson’s second popular play, opened on Broadway, starring Katharine Hepburn on November 19, 1981. Thompson is quoted as saying that ''The West Side Waltz'' came about after a telephone call he received on behalf of screenwriter [[George Seaton]], creator of movies such as ''[[Miracle on 34th Street]]'', offering Thompson "the only George Seaton grant" to write a new play for the Ahmanson Theatre in Los Angeles. Thompson wrote and directed a television version based on the play, premiering on Thanksgiving night 1995, starring [[Shirley MacLaine]], [[Liza Minnelli]], [[Kathy Bates]], and [[Jennifer Grey]].

[[File:Westside medical 1977.JPG|thumb|left|upright|Thompson (left) on ''Westside Medical'' in 1977]]
As an actor, Thompson's only Broadway appearance was as drifter Hal Carter in ''[[Summer Brave]]'', [[William Inge]]'s revised version of his play ''[[Picnic (play)|Picnic]]''. He portrayed Ranger Matt Harper on NBC’s 1974 series ''[[Sierra (TV series)|Sierra]]'' and Dr. Phil Parker on ABC's ''[[Westside Medical]]''. He appeared on the NBC soap opera ''[[Somerset (TV series)|Somerset]]'' as Tony Cooper and in the television films ''[[The Rimers of Eldritch]]'' and ''F. Scott Fitzgerald and The Last of the Belles''. Other acting credits include roles in the [[Bob Fosse]] movie ''[[Star 80]]'' and ''[[Next Stop Wonderland]]'', directed by [[Brad Anderson (director)|Brad Anderson]].

Thompson wrote the screenplay for the feature film ''[[Sweet Hearts Dance]]'', directed by [[Robert Greenwald]] and starring [[Susan Sarandon]] and [[Don Johnson]]. He directed ''[[1969 (film)|1969]]'', starring [[Kiefer Sutherland]], [[Robert Downey, Jr.]], and [[Winona Ryder]].

During the 1990s, Thompson wrote the television film ''[[Take Me Home Again]]'' (released on DVD as ''The Lies Boys Tell'') in 1994, based on the novel by [[Lamar Herrin]], starring [[Kirk Douglas]] and [[Craig T. Nelson]], in which Thompson also appeared. He directed and acted opposite [[Shirley MacLaine]] and [[Jennifer Grey]] in ''[[The West Side Waltz (film)|The West Side Waltz]]'' and co-wrote and directed the Emmy-nominated movie ''[[Out of Time (2000 film)|Out of Time]]'', starring [[James McDaniel]] and [[Mel Harris]].

In 2000, Thompson directed ''The Penis Responds'', with [[Richard Gilliland]]. In 2001, he directed his own live TV version of ''[[On Golden Pond (2001 film)|On Golden Pond]]'', starring [[Julie Andrews]] and [[Christopher Plummer]]. His play ''White People Christmas'' played at the Zephyr Theatre in Los Angeles, directed by Thompson.

==Personal life==
In 1991 his son August was born. In 1993, Thompson married Kristie Lanier at his home in New Hampshire, where he raised daughters Heather and Danielle. Thompson and Lanier later divorced. On Christmas Day in 2012, he married Kerrin (Rocha) Adrian.

Thompson lives most of the year in New Hampton, New Hampshire<ref>[http://www.nhmagazine.com/June-2016/Music-Review-Ernest-Thompson-and-Ray-Porcell/ Music Review: Ernest Thompson and Ray Porcell - New Hampshire Magazine - June 2016] Retrieved 2017-09-07.</ref> (very close to where ''[[On Golden Pond (1981 film)|On Golden Pond]]'' was filmed). In 2008, Thompson co-founded Whitebridge Farm Productions with partners Morgan Murphy and Lori Gigliotti-Murphy. He wrote, directed and starred in the company's first two movies, ''Heavenly Angle'' and ''Time and Charges'', both of which were primarily filmed in the Granite State and offered by Whitebridge Farm Productions.
