'''Susan King''',<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.goodreads.com/author/show/958212.Susan_Fraser_King|title=Susan Fraser King|website=www.goodreads.com|access-date=2018-05-15}}</ref> née '''Longhi''' (born 1951 in [[New York City]], United States) is a writer of [[historical romance|historical]] [[romance novels]] as '''Susan King''', '''Sarah Gabriel''' and '''Susan Fraser King'''.  King's work has been translated into five languages, and she has received awards from ''Romantic Times Magazine'' as well as a RITA Award nomination from the [[Romance Writers of America]].

==Biography==
Susan Longhi was born in 1951 in [[New York City, New York]], United States, and lived there whilst growing up. She attended the [[University of Maryland, College Park|University of Maryland]], where she earned a [[Bachelor of Arts|B.A.]] in Studio Art and an [[Master's degree|M.A.]] in Art History.<ref name="about">{{cite web| last =| first =| title =Susan King| pages =| language =| publisher =Susan King Official Website| year =2004| url =http://www.susanking.net/| access-date =2007-03-30| url-status =dead| archive-url =https://web.archive.org/web/20070410102433/http://www.susanking.net/| archive-date =2007-04-10}}</ref> After completing her studies, King lectured in art history and art theory and pursued a doctoral degree in medieval art history, again at the University of Maryland.  Following her M.A., King wrote her first novel, ''The Black Thorne's Rose'', which was published by Penguin/Topaz in September 1994.<ref name="about"/> Many of her novels, which have been published in several foreign languages,<ref name="about"/> feature Celtic legends and myths. Her passion for art and history lend her books a high level of historical accuracy and detail that set King's work apart from many others in the genre.

King has been nominated for the [[Romance Writers of America]] RITA Award for Best Long Historical.<ref name="about"/>  She has been the recipient of the ''Romantic Times'' Reviewers' Choice Award for Best Medieval Novel and the ''Romantic Times'' Reviewers' Choice Award for Career Achievement in Medieval Historical Romance.<ref name="rt">{{cite web  | last =   | first =   | title = Susan King  | pages =  | language =  | publisher = Romantic Times  | date =   | url =http://www.romantictimes.com/books.php?search=1&authtitle=susan+king  | accessdate = 2007-03-30}} {{Dead link|date=October 2010|bot=H3llBot}}</ref> She is a member of the [[Romance Writers of America]] and [[Novelists, Inc]], and has served as a member of the Board of Directors of the Washington Romance Writers. she holds a graduate degree in medieval art history.  King currently resides in Maryland with her family <ref>{{Cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=1uCIn4gOym8C&q=susan+king+residence+and+family+life&pg=PA60|title=African Americans in the Nineteenth Century: People and Perspectives: People and Perspectives|last=Haggard|first=Dixie Ray|date=2010-03-11|publisher=ABC-CLIO|isbn=9781598841244|language=en}}</ref>
