'''Duane Ackerson''' (October 17, 1942 – April 19, 2020)<ref>"United States Public Records, 1970-2009," database, FamilySearch (May 23, 2014), Duane W Ackerson, Residence, Salem, Oregon, United States; a third party aggregator of publicly available information.</ref> was an [[American literature|American writer]] of [[speculative poetry]] and [[speculative fiction|fiction]].<ref>[http://www.ravennapress.com/alba/issue_10/ackerson10a.html Alba]</ref>

Не taught at the [[University of Oregon]], then headed the creative program at [[Idaho State University]]. He lived in [[Salem, Oregon]], where he died on April 19, 2020.<ref>{{cite news|url = http://oregonpoeticvoices.org/poet/219/|title = Duane Ackerson|website = Oregon Poetic Voices|accessdate = February 19, 2021}}</ref>

Duane Ackerson's work has appeared in [[anthologies]] that include ''The Year's Best SF 1974'', ''100 Great Science Fiction Short Short Stories'', ''Future Pastimes'', and the textbook ''Writing Poetry''. He has won the [[Rhysling Award]] for Best Short Poem twice, in 1978 and 1979.

Ackerson's poems are translated into Russian by [[Dmitry Kuzmin]].<ref>[http://textonly.ru/mood/?issue=44&article=38930 TextOnly]</ref>
