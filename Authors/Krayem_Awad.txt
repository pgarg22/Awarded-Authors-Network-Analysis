'''Krayem Maria Awad''' (born 1948) is a [[Vienna, Austria|Vienna]]-based [[Painting|painter]], [[sculptor]] and [[poet]] of [[Syria]]n origin.

He was born in Basir, and later moved to [[Austria]], where he studied from 1968 telecommunications engineering at the [[Vienna University of Technology|Vienna Technical College]], and was also enrolled at the Vienna School of Economics.

He also attended the [[Academy of Fine Arts Vienna|Vienna Academy of Fine Arts]].

In the 1970-1980s, he received international recognition for his numerous [[Watercolor painting|aquarelle]] work.

In the 1970s he participated in lectures as part of the creative movement "North-South Dialogue". He was a member of the editorial board of the magazine "Treffpunkte", and author of the [[manifesto]] "The Position of Women in European Civilization".{{Citation needed|date=March 2012}}

In 1977 he joined the art cooperative "Arbeitsgemeinschaft Kunst", offering courses in printing graphics and ceramics, and teaching at a summer art school in Vienna.{{Citation needed|date=March 2012}}

In the 1990s he worked with video installations, [[photography]] and computer graphics.{{Citation needed|date=March 2012}}

Since 1994, he has been involved in art-workshops and art courses in [[Switzerland]].{{Citation needed|date=March 2012}}

Krayem uses mixed media when painting, mixing sand and ashes with oil-based [[pigment]]s, and often supplements artwork with his own poetry.

Krayem often creates large format paintings, like the one titled "The whole", which measured 3x10 meters, and was on display at Vienna’s Jugendstiltheater in September 2008 [[:de:Steinhof (Wien)]].{{Citation needed|date=March 2012}}

He is a member of the International Club of visual artists "Art/Diagonal" [http://www.artdiagonal.com/artdiagonal.html].

Krayem's art work has been shown, as part of one man shows, in many [[Europe]]an countries, as well as in [[Japan]], [[South Africa]], and the [[United States of America]]. His paintings are in public and private collections.{{Citation needed|date=March 2012}}
