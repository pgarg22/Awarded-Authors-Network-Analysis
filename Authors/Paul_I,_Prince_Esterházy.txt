'''Paul I, Prince Esterházy of Galántha'''<ref name=EuWeb>{{Cite web |last=Marek |first=Miroslav |url=http://genealogy.euweb.cz/hung/esterhazy1.html |title=Esterházy de Galántha family |accessdate=23 February 2010 |publisher=GENEALOGY.EU|url-status=live |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20100128084218/http://genealogy.euweb.cz/hung/esterhazy1.html |archivedate=28 January 2010 }}{{Self-published source|date=August 2012}}{{Better source|date=August 2012}}</ref><ref name=Esterhazy>{{Cite web | url=http://www.esterhazy.at/hu/kultur/FurstPaulI.htm | title=I. Pál herceg (1635–1713) | accessdate=23 February 2010 | publisher=Esterházy Betriebe GmbH| author=Esterházy Betriebe GmbH | url-status=dead | archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20090918134350/http://www.esterhazy.at/hu/kultur/FurstPaulI.htm | archivedate=18 September 2009 | df=dmy-all }}</ref><ref name=aeiou>{{Cite web | url=http://www.aeiou.at/aeiou.encyclop.e/e847780.htm;internal&action=_setlanguage.action?LANGUAGE=en | title=Esterházy, Paul I. Fürst | accessdate=23 February 2010 | publisher=AEIOU Project| author=Austrian Ministry of Education, Science, and Culture | url-status=live | archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20110605005828/http://www.aeiou.at/aeiou.encyclop.e/e847780.htm%3Binternal%26action%3D_setlanguage.action?LANGUAGE=en | archivedate=5 June 2011 | df=dmy-all }}</ref> (full [[German language|German]] name: ''Paul Fürst Esterházy von Galantha''; full [[Hungarian language|Hungarian]] name: ''galánthai herceg Esterházy Pál'') (8 September 1635<ref name=EuWeb/><ref name=aeiou/><ref name=Jrank>{{Cite web| url=http://arts.jrank.org/pages/2339/Esterházy.html | title=Pál [Paul] Esterházy | accessdate=23 February 2010 | publisher=The Arts: Fine Art, Contemporary Art & Music|author1=Wendy Thompson  |author2=Caryl Clark}}</ref>  – 26 March 1713) was the first [[House of Esterházy|Prince Esterházy of Galántha]] from 1687 to 1713, [[Palatine (Kingdom of Hungary)|Palatine of the Kingdom of Hungary]] from 1681 to 1713, and an [[Holy Roman Empire|Imperial]] [[Field Marshal]].<ref name=EuWeb/><ref name=Jrank/> Paul was also an accomplished [[poet]], [[harpsichord]]ist, and [[composer]].<ref name=Jrank/><ref name=NewOxford>{{Cite book |title=The New Oxford Companion to Music, Volume 1 |last=Arnold |first=Denis |year=1983 |publisher=[[Oxford University Press]] |isbn=0-19-311316-3 |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=QZAYAAAAIAAJ}}</ref> He actively participated in various battles against the [[Ottoman Turks]] during the [[Austro-Turkish War (1663-1664)|Fourth Austro-Turkish War]] (1663–1664) and the [[Great Turkish War]] (1683–1699). Paul is credited with establishing the wealth, power, and influence of the [[House of Esterházy|Princely House of Esterházy]].<ref name=aeiou/><ref name=Jrank/>

==Family and early life==
Born in [[Eisenstadt|Kismarton]] (Eisenstadt), [[Kingdom of Hungary]], Paul was the third son of [[Nicholas, Count Esterházy of Galántha]] and his second wife [[Krisztina Nyáry|Baroness Krisztina Nyáry of Bedegh]].<ref name=EuWeb/><ref name=Esterhazy/><ref name=Chisholm>{{Cite EB1911|wstitle=Esterházy of Galántha|display=Esterházy of Galántha s.v. Pál|volume=9|page=794|first=Robert Nisbet|last=Bain|authorlink=Robert Nisbet Bain}}</ref> His father, Nicholas, bore the office of the [[Palatine (Kingdom of Hungary)|Palatine of the Kingdom of Hungary]].<ref name=Esterhazy/>

Paul was raised in a deeply religious atmosphere and studied at [[Jesuit]] institutions in [[Graz]] and [[Trnava|Nagyszombat]].<ref name=aeiou/><ref name=tradicio>{{Cite web | url=http://www.tradicio.org/kvintesszencia/trad98esterhazy.htm | title=HERCEG ESTERHÁZY PÁL | accessdate=23 February 2010 | publisher=Kvintesszencia Kiadó| author=Kvintesszencia Kiadó | url-status=live | archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20090421063153/http://www.tradicio.org/kvintesszencia/trad98esterhazy.htm | archivedate=21 April 2009}}</ref> He displayed literary talents at an early age.<ref name=Thomas>{{Cite book|title=Universal Pronouncing Dictionary of Biography and Mythology, Volume 1|last=Thomas|first=Joseph|year=1901|publisher=J.B. Lippincott Company|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=QqkDAAAAYAAJ}}</ref>

On 16 August 1652, Paul's elder brother [[Ladislaus, Count Esterházy of Galántha|Ladislaus]] was killed in the [[Battle of Vezekény]] (Vezekey) against the [[Ottoman Turks|Turks]].<ref name=EuWeb/><ref name=Esterhazy/><ref name=aeiou/> Paul succeeded Ladislaus as [[House of Esterházy|Count Esterházy of Galántha]] and inherited the family's vast wealth and landholdings at the age of 17.<ref name=Esterhazy/>

==Military career==
Paul began his military career as early as 1663, and opposed the Turks in the battles of [[Austro-Turkish War (1663–1664)]] (e.g. [[Siege of Novi Zrin (1664)|siege of Novi Zrin in June 1664]] and [[Battle of Saint Gotthard (1664)|Battle of Saint Gotthard on 1 August 1664]]) under the leadership of [[Raimondo Montecuccoli]].<ref name=Chisholm/> Paul served with distinction under Montecuccoli alongside [[Miklós Zrínyi]].<ref name=Chisholm/> Paul served in the army with such distinction that he was appointed to the position of [[Field Marshal]] and [[Commander-in-chief]] of the [[Military Frontier]] of southern Kingdom of Hungary beginning in 1667 at the age of 30.<ref name=aeiou/><ref name=Chisholm/><ref name=Thomas/><ref name="eb">Encyclopædia Britannica, 1988 edition, article "Eszterházy"</ref><ref name=Hadden>{{Cite book |title=Haydn |last=Hadden |first=J.Cuthbert |year=2004 |publisher=Kessinger Publishing |isbn=1-4191-2295-9 |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=RrrCzDG289YC}}</ref> While serving as a commander-in-chief, Paul defeated [[malcontent]]s at [[Levoča|Leutschau]] and [[Győr]].<ref name=Chisholm/> Paul's troops were among the coalition that was raised for the [[Battle of Vienna]] in 1683.<ref name=aeiou/><ref name=Chisholm/> After the deliverance of Vienna from the Turks, Paul entered [[Buda]] in 1686 at the head of 20,000 men.<ref name=Chisholm/> Throughout his military career, Paul led the Hungarian side of the Habsburg army into battle against the Ottoman Turks a total of 16 times.<ref name=Esterhazy/><ref name=Chisholm/>

Paul throughout his life remained loyal to the [[House of Habsburg|Habsburgs]], and therefore was not involved in [[Wesselényi conspiracy|the conspiracy of the Hungarian nobility in the 1670s]].<ref name=Esterhazy/><ref name=Chisholm/> This loyalty was rewarded by the Habsburgs in 1681, when Paul was appointed to the position of [[Palatine (Kingdom of Hungary)|Palatine of the Kingdom of Hungary]].<ref name=Esterhazy/><ref name=Chisholm/><ref name=Thomas/> The wars with the Turks did not go unnoticed by the Esterházy family, because the advance of the Turks to Vienna devastated many of the family's lands and brought slaughter to many of the lands' residents.<ref name=aeiou/>

In 1681, Paul was made a [[Order of the Golden Fleece|Knight of the Austrian Order of the Golden Fleece]].<ref name=Esterhazy/><ref name=tradicio/> On 8 December 1687, [[Leopold I, Holy Roman Emperor]] issued a princely diploma elevating Paul to a [[Princes of the Holy Roman Empire|Prince of the Holy Roman Empire]] for his military successes against the Turks during the reconquest of Hungary and his loyalty to the [[House of Habsburg]].<ref name=aeiou/><ref name=Chisholm/><ref name=Thomas/><ref name=Hadden/><ref name=Dwight>{{Cite book|title=Dwight's journal of music, Volumes 35–36|last=Dwight|first=John Sullivan|year=1877|publisher=D.L. Balch|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=42gPAAAAYAAJ}}</ref> The title of Prince was extended to his male descendants in 1712.<ref name=aeiou/><ref name=Chisholm/> On 9 December 1687, in his role as Palatine, Paul placed the [[Crown of Saint Stephen]] on the head of [[Joseph I, Holy Roman Emperor|Archduke Joseph of Austria]] (later [[Joseph I, Holy Roman Emperor]]) as the first [[House of Habsburg|Habsburg]] hereditary [[List of rulers of Hungary|king of Hungary]].<ref name=Dwight/> As an ally of the Habsburgs, Paul had as one of his principal concerns the recatholicization of those areas of [[Central Europe]] "infected" by [[Protestantism]].<ref name=Jones>{{Cite book |title=Music in Eighteenth-Century Austria |last=Jones |first=David Wyn |year=2006 |publisher=[[Cambridge University Press]] |isbn=0-521-02859-0 |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=FLZuovg47LYC}}</ref> Paul's loyalty was also demonstrated in his cooperation with the Habsburg court in the subversion of [[civil liberty|civil]] and [[religious liberty|religious]] liberties.<ref name=Thomas/>

In 1703, Paul again fought with the Habsburgs in the [[Kuruc|wars with the Kuruc]], armed anti-Habsburg Hungarian rebels in [[Royal Hungary]].

From 1711 until his death, Paul served as [[Lord Lieutenant]] of [[Moson County]].<ref name=tradicio/>

==Building efforts==
[[File:Eisenstadt z01.JPG|thumb|left|[[Schloss Esterházy]]]]
Paul converted [[Schloss Esterházy]] in [[Eisenstadt]] from a [[medieval]] [[castle]] into a [[Baroque architecture|Baroque]] [[palace]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.schloss-esterhazy.at/en/liegenschaften/esterhazy-palace/history/history/ |title=General |work=Schloss Esterházy Official website |accessdate=24 May 2009 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20090625105914/http://www.schloss-esterhazy.at/en/liegenschaften/esterhazy-palace/history/history/ |archivedate=25 June 2009 }}</ref> Paul began his renovations of [[Schloss Esterházy]] in 1663.<ref name=aeiou/><ref name=Hadden/><ref name=Audiocom>{{Cite book|title=High fidelity, Volume 33, Issues 6–12|year=1983|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=Q1lLAAAAYAAJ}}</ref> The additions made by Paul took nearly ten years to complete and gave the palace the [[facade|façade]] it has retained to the present.

Paul further extended, ornamented, and fortified [[Forchtenstein Castle]] with architect Domenico Carlone.<ref name=Dwight/> At Forchtenstein Castle, Paul founded the Esterházy family's treasure house and created an extensive portrait gallery of Esterházy family [[ancestor]]s.<ref name=Dwight/>

==Musical career==
Paul was an accomplished musical amateur, [[composer]], and [[patron of the arts]].<ref name=NewOxford/><ref name=Chisholm/><ref name=Thomas/><ref name=Hadden/> He composed numerous [[cantatas]], the most notable of which are ''[[Harmonia Caelestis]]''.<ref name=Jrank/><ref name=Jones/> ''Harmonia Caelestis'' is a cycle of 55 [[Religious music|sacred]] cantatas composed in the [[Baroque music|Baroque]] style published in [[Vienna]] in 1711.<ref name=Jrank/><ref name=Jones/> Paul was one of the chief compilers of the ''Trophaeum Domus Inclytae Estorationae''.<ref name=Chisholm/> <!--Restorationae?-->

Paul used his great wealth in the liberal patronage of art and literature.<ref name=Thomas/> He founded the Esterházy private chapel, famous for its solo singers, chorus, and orchestra.<ref name=Hadden/>

==Marriage and issue==

[[File:Paul I, 1st Prince Esterházy of Galántha.jpg|thumb|250px|right|Paul shortly after his elevation to Prince Esterházy of Galántha.]]

*Prince Miklós Antal Esterházy of Galántha (1655–1695)<ref name=EuWeb/>
*Count Pál Esterházy of Galántha (1657–1664)<ref name=EuWeb/>

*Prince László Ignac Esterházy of Galántha (1662–1689)<ref name=EuWeb/>
*Princess Krisztina Esterházy of Galántha (1663–1732)<ref name=EuWeb/>

*Princess Orsolya Esterházy of Galántha (1670–after 1696)<ref name=EuWeb/>
*[[Michael I, 2nd Prince Esterházy of Galántha|Mihály I, 2nd Prince Esterházy of Galántha]] (1671–1721)<ref name=EuWeb/>
*Count György Bernat Esterházy of Galántha (1672–1672)<ref name=EuWeb/>

After the death of his first wife, Paul married Countess Éva Thököly of Késmárk, daughter of István, Count Thököly of Késmárk and his wife Mária Gyulaffy of Rátót.<ref name=EuWeb/> Paul and Éva had the following children:<ref name=EuWeb/>

*Princess Mária Terézia Anna Esterházy of Galántha (1684–1755)<ref name=EuWeb/>

*Prince Tamas Ignac Esterházy of Galántha (1689–1689)<ref name=EuWeb/>
*Prince Zsigmond Lipot Esterházy of Galántha (1692–1693)<ref name=EuWeb/>

==Charitable acts==
In 1671, he rescued some 3,000 [[Jews]] who had been expelled by [[Leopold I, Holy Roman Emperor]] from [[Vienna]]. This led to their resettlement as [[tenant farmers]] and the founding of the [[Siebengemeinden|Seven Municipalities]] ({{lang-de|Siebengemeinden}}) on Esterházy lands throughout [[Burgenland]].<ref name=GatesCoon>{{Cite book |title=The Landed Estates of the Esterházy Princes: Hungary During the Reforms of Maria Theresia and Joseph II |last=Gates-Coon |first=Rebecca |year=1994 |publisher=[[Johns Hopkins University Press]] |isbn=0-8018-4785-0 |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=yC14AAAAIAAJ}}</ref>

==Death and legacy==
[[File:Family Tree House Esterhazy.svg|thumb|300px|right|Family Tree of the Princes Esterházy of Galántha]]
Paul died in Eisenstadt on 26 March 1713.<ref name=EuWeb/><ref name=Esterhazy/><ref name=aeiou/> To secure his fortune, Paul founded the Austrian model of the [[fee tail]] and settled his successor in his [[Will (law)|will]] through the establishment of two primogeniture lines for his sons Michael and Joseph.<ref name=Esterhazy/> In his will, Paul left his sons Michael and Joseph two estates which were meant to remain separate, but were rejoined under Joseph's ownership in 1721.<ref name=Esterhazy/><ref name=aeiou/>

Paul was interred in the [[House of Esterházy|Esterházy]] family crypt at the [[Franziskanerkirche (Eisenstadt)|Franziskanerkirche]] in the Franciscan Monastery in Eisenstadt.<ref name=Esterhazy/>

Most of the Princely House of Esterházy's landholdings were acquired during Paul's reign as head of the family, but his lands incurred debts totaling 1,311,733 [[Florin (Italian coin)|florin]]s by 1711.<ref name=Hitchins>{{Cite book |title=Studies in East European social history, Volume 2 |last=Hitchins |first=Keith |year=1981 |publisher=Brill Archive |isbn=90-04-06386-2 |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=ZdcUAAAAIAAJ}}</ref>
