'''Dennis Schmitz''' (August 11, 1937 - September 12, 2019) was an American [[poet]].<ref>https://www.sacbee.com/news/local/obituaries/article235221732.html</ref>

==Life==
Dennis Schmitz grew up in [[Dubuque, Iowa]].<ref>{{cite news| url=http://www.highbeam.com/doc/1P2-11103453.html| archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20121025215453/http://www.highbeam.com/doc/1P2-11103453.html| url-status=dead| archive-date=October 25, 2012| title=Poet making trek back to his Dubuque roots; Schmitz will give a reading tonight at the Carnegie-Stout Library| newspaper=Dubuque Telegraph - Herald | date=September 18, 2003| first=Sandye |last=Voight}}</ref>  He graduated from [[Loras College]] and the [[University of Chicago]], where he met and married Loretta [[Nicholas D'Agostino, Sr.|D'Agostino]] in 1960; they have five children together.  He has taught at [[Illinois Institute of Technology]], [[University of Wisconsin&ndash;Milwaukee]]<ref>{{cite book| url=https://books.google.com/books?id=xTG2qvxxYNsC&pg=PA290 | title=International Who's Who in Poetry 2004| editor=Europa Publications| publisher=Taylor & Francis | year=2003| isbn=978-1-85743-178-0 }}</ref> and [[California State University, Sacramento]].<ref>http://www.csus.edu/pubaf/journal/fall2002/34facultyauthors.htm</ref>
His former students include [[Raymond Carver]],<ref>{{Cite book | url = https://archive.org/details/conversationswit00carv | url-access = registration | page = [https://archive.org/details/conversationswit00carv/page/180 180] | title = Conversations with Raymond Carver | publisher = Univ. Press of Mississippi | isbn = 978-0-87805-449-7 | last1 = Carver | first1 = Raymond | last2 = Gentry | first2 = Marshall Bruce | last3 = Stull | first3 = William L | date = January 1990}}</ref> [[Charlene Ungstad]],<ref>https://web.archive.org/web/20160304082518/http://www.blogtalkradio.com/the-jane-crown-show/blog/2008/12</ref> [[Western States Book Award#Poetry|Gary Short]],<ref>http://www.onlinenevada.org/Gary_Short</ref> and Gary Thompson.<ref name="ReferenceA">Sklenicka, Carol. ''Raymond Carver: A Writer's Life.'' New York: Scribner, 2009</ref>

He opposes the [[death penalty]] and protests executions in California every time they take place.<ref>http://www.lairdcarlson.com/celldoor/00302/Ains00302WrodsfromDR.htm</ref><ref name="sacramentopoetrycenter.blogspot.com">http://sacramentopoetrycenter.blogspot.com/2006/03/dennis-schmitz-reading-at-art-foundry.html</ref>

His work has appeared in the ''Alaska Quarterly Review'',<ref>http://www.uaa.alaska.edu/aqr/back-issues/17_1and2.cfm</ref> ''American Poetry Review'', ''The Nation'', ''Paris Review'',<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.parisreview.com/viewissue.php/prmIID/62 |title=Archived copy |access-date=2009-07-01 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20091003135812/http://www.parisreview.com/viewissue.php/prmIID/62 |archive-date=2009-10-03 |url-status=dead }}</ref> the ''Chicago Review'',<ref>http://humanities.uchicago.edu/orgs/review/archive1.shtml</ref> and ''Zyzzyva''.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.zyzzyva.org/published.poetry.htm |title=Archived copy |accessdate=2009-09-13 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20091014085422/http://www.zyzzyva.org/published.poetry.htm |archivedate=2009-10-14 }}</ref>

He resided in Sacramento.<ref>http://www.pw.org/content/dennis_schmitz_3</ref>
