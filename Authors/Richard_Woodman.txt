Captain '''Richard Martin Woodman''' [[Royal Victorian Order|LVO]] (born 1944) is an English novelist and naval historian who retired in 1997 from a 37-year nautical career, mainly working for [[Trinity House]], to write full-time.<ref name="fantasticfiction">{{cite web|url=http://www.fantasticfiction.co.uk/w/richard-woodman/|publisher=Fantastic Fiction|title=Richard Woodman|access-date=11 May 2011}}</ref>

==Writing==
His main work is 14 novels about the career of [[Nathaniel Drinkwater]],<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.leserglede.com/historical-fiction/richard-woodman-A.html|title=The Nathaniel Drinkwater series, by Richard Woodman|access-date=11 May 2011|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110411040752/http://www.leserglede.com/historical-fiction/richard-woodman-A.html|archive-date=11 April 2011|df=dmy-all}}</ref> and shorter series about James Dunbar and William Kite, but he also has written a range of factual books about 18th century and WW2 history. These include a trilogy of studies of convoys in the Second World War and a five volume history of the British Merchant Navy. Unlike many other modern naval historical novelists, such as [[C.S. Forester]] or [[Patrick O'Brian]], he has served afloat.  He went to sea at the age of sixteen as an indentured midshipman and has spent eleven years in command.<ref name="historypress">{{cite web|url=http://www.thehistorypress.co.uk/news/merchant-navy.aspx|title=The Surprising History of the British Merchant Navy|publisher=The History Press|access-date=11 May 2011}}</ref>  His experience ranges from [[cargo-liner]]s to [[ocean weather ship]]s and specialist support vessels as well as yachts, square-riggers, and trawlers.<ref name="historypress" />

Woodman is a regular correspondent for the shipping newspaper Lloyd's List and continues his close association with the sea as a keen yachtsman. He also serves on the Corporate Board of Trinity House.  He has won several awards including the Society for Nautical Research's Anderson Medal in 2005<ref name="historypress" /> and the Marine Society's Harmer Award in 1978.<ref name="fantasticfiction" />

==Honours==
Woodman was appointed [[Lieutenant of the Royal Victorian Order]] (LVO) in the 2014 New Year Honours for his services to Trinity House.<ref>{{London Gazette|issue=60728 |supp=y|page=4|date=31 December 2013}}</ref>
