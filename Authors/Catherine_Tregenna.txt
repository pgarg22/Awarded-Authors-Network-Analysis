'''Catherine Tregenna''' is a [[Wales|Welsh]] playwright, television screenwriter and actress. She has written episodes for ''[[EastEnders]]'', ''[[Casualty (TV series)|Casualty]]'' and for the first four series of [[ITV1]] crime drama ''[[Law & Order: UK]]''.

==Career==
Tregenna began as an actress. She played the character Kirsty McGurk, for several years, in ''[[Pobol Y Cwm]]'' and a doctor in [[Satellite City (sitcom)|Satellite City]].<ref name="Scity">{{cite web
  | title = Satellite City Credits & Complete Character Guide

In 2001, she wrote the comedy play ''Art and Guff''.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.theatre-wales.co.uk/plays/review_archive.asp?playname=Art%20and%20Guff&company=Sgript%20Cymru|title=Details of Art and Guff from the archive of the Theatre in Wales|website=theatre-wales.co.uk|access-date=30 October 2021}}</ref> After, she transitioned back into television as a writer, co-writing the series ''[[Cowbois ac Injans]]'' and four episodes of the [[BBC]]'s [[science fiction]] drama and ''Doctor Who'' spin-off ''[[Torchwood]]'': "[[Out of Time (Torchwood)|Out of Time]]", "[[Captain Jack Harkness (Torchwood episode)|Captain Jack Harkness]]", "[[Meat (Torchwood)|Meat]]" and "[[Adam (Torchwood)|Adam]]". As well as co-writing Meat, she also created the meat props shown in the episode. "Captain Jack Harkness" was nominated for the 2008 [[Hugo Award for Best Dramatic Presentation, Short Form]].<ref name="Denv">{{cite web

In November 2014, it was confirmed that she would be writing an episode for the [[Doctor Who (series 9)|ninth series]] of ''[[Doctor Who]]'', "[[The Woman Who Lived]]". She contributed episodes to the third and final series of ''[[Stan Lee's Lucky Man]]'', the third series of ''[[Riviera (TV series)|Riviera]]'' and the fifth episode of ''[[The Watch (TV series)|The Watch]]'', which is inspired by<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://discworldmonthly.co.uk/terrypratchett_thewatchseries.php|title = Terry Pratchett's Discworld the Watch TV Series|website=Discworldmonthly.co.uk}}</ref> the [[Ankh-Morpork City Watch]] from the ''[[Discworld]]'' series of fantasy novels by [[Terry Pratchett]].
