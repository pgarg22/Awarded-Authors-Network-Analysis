'''Francis Tolson''' (? – 1745) was an [[England|English]] [[poet]], [[playwright|dramatist]], and the [[author]] of several works. He was the [[Vicar]] of [[Easton Maudit]] and [[Chaplain]] to the Rt. Hon.ble the [[Talbot Yelverton, 1st Earl of Sussex|Earl of Sussex]].<ref>{{cite book| first=Mario| last=Praz| title=Studies in seventeenth-century imagery| url=https://books.google.com/books?isbn=8887114870| page=515| date=2001| publisher=Edizione di Storia e Letteratura| isbn=8887114870}}</ref>

==Works==
*''Octavius Prince of Syra: Or, a Lash for Levi. A Poem'' (1719)
*''The Earl of Warwick: Or, British Exile. A Tragedy. As it is Acted at the Theatre Royal in Drury-Lane'' (1719)
*''A Poem on His Majesty's Passing the South-Sea Bill'' (1720)
*''Proposals for Printing, Hermathenæ: Or, One Hundred and Twenty Moral Emblems, and Ethnick Tales. with Notes'' (1739)
*''Hermathenæ, Or Moral Emblems, and Ethnick Tales, with Explanatory Notes'' (1740)
