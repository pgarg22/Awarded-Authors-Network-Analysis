'''Bertha Hirsch Baruch''' was a German born American writer, social worker, and [[suffragist]].

Baruch was born in the [[Province of Posen]], [[Germany]]. She immigrated to [[New London, Connecticut]] with her father in 1876. Baruch wrote poetry in her teens and was encouraged by [[Rose Hawthorne Lathrop]] in her literary efforts. {{citation needed|date=June 2021}} Active in College Settlement and university extension work, she attended Pennsylvania University and [[Yale University|Yale]]. She later worked on the editorial staff for the ''[[Los Angeles Times]]''. {{citation needed|date=June 2021}} In 1906 she lived at 1168 W. 36th St., [[Los Angeles, California]].{{citation needed|date=June 2021}}

Baruch was active in the women's [[suffrage]] movement. She became the county president of the Los Angeles Suffrage Association in 1905 when two conventions were hosted:
*the Women’s Parliament, October 10–11, and
*the county convention of the Equal Suffrage League October 12. {{citation needed|date=June 2021}}

In 1908 Baruch became the treasurer of the Los Angeles Jewish Women’s Foreign Relief Association. She published ''Dress as a Social Factor''<ref>{{cite book |last1=Baruch |first1=Bertha Hirsch |title=Dress as a Social Factor |date=1912 |hdl=2027/uc1.$b260620 }}</ref> in 1912.{{citation needed|date=June 2021}}
