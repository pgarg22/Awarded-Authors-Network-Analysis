'''Herbert Achternbusch''' (born 23 November 1938 in [[Munich]]) is a German [[writer]], [[Painting|painter]] and [[filmmaker]]. His [[anarchism|anarchist]] [[surrealism|surrealistic]] [[film]]s are not known to a wide audience in Germany, although one of them, ''Das Gespenst (The Ghost)'', caused a scandal in 1983 because of its
alleged [[blasphemy|blasphemous]] content.<ref>[http://www.br-online.de/kultur-szene/thema/filmszene_bayern/achternbusch.shtml "Filmszene Bayern: Herbert Achternbusch"] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20070929155020/http://www.br-online.de/kultur-szene/thema/filmszene_bayern/achternbusch.shtml |date=29 September 2007 }}, ''[[Bayerischer Rundfunk]] Online'', 22 November 2006. Accessed 5 May 2007. (in German)</ref> [[Werner Herzog]], a director of the [[New German Cinema]], based his film ''[[Heart of Glass (film)|Heart of Glass]]'' on a story by Achternbusch.

In 1981, he directed the film ''[[Der Neger Erwin]]'', which was entered into the [[31st Berlin International Film Festival]].<ref name="imdb">{{cite web |url=https://www.imdb.com/title/tt0081221/awards |title=IMDB.com: Awards for Der Neger Erwin |accessdate=2010-08-29 |work=imdb.com}}</ref> The following year, he directed ''[[The Ghost (1982 film)|The Ghost]]'', which was entered into the [[33rd Berlin International Film Festival]].<ref name="Berlinale 1983">{{cite web |url=http://www.berlinale.de/en/archiv/jahresarchive/1983/02_programm_1983/02_Programm_1983.html |title=Berlinale: 1983 Programme |accessdate=2010-11-17 |work=berlinale.de}}</ref> In 1988, his film ''[[Wohin?]]'' was entered into the [[38th Berlin International Film Festival]].<ref name="Berlinale 1988">{{cite web |url=http://www.berlinale.de/en/archiv/jahresarchive/1988/02_programm_1988/02_Programm_1988.html |title=Berlinale: 1988 Programme |accessdate=2011-03-06 |work=berlinale.de}}</ref> In 1995, his film ''[[Hades (film)|Hades]]'' was entered into the [[45th Berlin International Film Festival]].<ref name="Berlinale1995">{{cite web |url=http://www.berlinale.de/en/archiv/jahresarchive/1995/02_programm_1995/02_Programm_1995.html |title=Berlinale: 1995 Programme |accessdate=2011-12-31 |work=berlinale.de}}</ref>

Herbert Achternbusch was awarded the German international literary [[Petrarca-Preis]] in [[1977 in literature|1977]], but he declined.{{citation needed|date=May 2012}}  In 2010, he was awarded the [[Kassel Literary Prize]]. He won the [[Mülheimer Dramatikerpreis]] in 1986 and 1994.
