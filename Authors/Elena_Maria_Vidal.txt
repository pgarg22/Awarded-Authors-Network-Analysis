'''Elena Maria Vidal''' (born 1962), the pen name of Mary-Eileen Russell, is a [[historical novelist]] and noted [[blog]]ger living in [[Easton, Maryland]]. She was born in [[Florence, Oregon]] and grew up in [[Frederick, Maryland]]. She is known for her defense of [[Louis XVI]] and [[Marie-Antoinette]] by means of her novels ''Trianon'' and ''Madame Royale'', her Tea at Trianon blog,<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://garethrussellcidevant.blogspot.com/2010/07/interview-with-elena-maria-vidal-author.html|title = Confessions of a Ci-Devant: An interview with Elena Maria Vidal, author of "Trianon"|date = 10 July 2010}}</ref> and her 2016 biography entitled ''Marie-Antoinette, Daughter of the Caesars''<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://planetrussell.net/emvidal2/?p=306|title = New Biography of Marie-Antoinette &#124; the Official Website of Elena Maria Vidal}}</ref>

==Education==
Vidal graduated in 1984 from [[Hood College]] in [[Frederick, Maryland]] with a [[Bachelor of Arts|BA]] in [[Psychology]], and in 1985 from the [[State University of New York at Albany]] with an [[Master of Arts|MA]] in Modern [[European History]].

==Publications==
In 1997 Vidal's first historical novel ''Trianon'' was published by St. Michaels Press. The [[historical novel]] tells of the life and death of [[Louis XVI]] and [[Marie-Antoinette]]. In 2000, the sequel ''Madame Royale'' was published, as well as the second edition of ''Trianon'', by The Neumann Press. ''Madame Royale'' chronicles the life of Marie-Antoinette's daughter, [[Marie-Thérèse-Charlotte]]. In 2010, the third edition of ''Trianon'' and the second edition of ''Madame Royale'' were released.<ref>http://books.emvidal.com</ref> In November 2009, ''The Night's Dark Shade'' was published by Mayapple Books. The new historical novel deals with the controversial [[Albigensian Crusade]] in thirteenth century France. Vidal has been a contributor to Canticle Magazine, [[Touchstone Magazine]], [[The American Conservative]], [[National Observer (Australia)|The National Observer]] and Parisienne Farmgirl Magazine. In May 2016 Vidal's biography of Marie-Antoinette was published.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.goodreads.com/book/show/30111575-marie-antoinette-daughter-of-the-caesars|title = Marie-Antoinette, Daughter of the Caesars}}</ref> She blogs at Tea at Trianon.

==Career==
In May 1984 Vidal was a student delegate at the [[Naval Academy Foreign Affairs Conference]] in [[Annapolis, Maryland]]. She has worked as an elementary school teacher <ref>{{Cite web|url=http://teaattrianon.blogspot.com/2008/04/gentle-witness.html|title=A Gentle Witness|date=8 April 2008}}</ref> and private tutor.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://fountainofelias.blogspot.com/2010/02/my-pilgrimage-to-lourdes-part-i.html|title = My Pilgrimage to Lourdes, Part I|date = 9 February 2011}}</ref> In 2006, Vidal was interviewed by Doug Keck on the [[EWTN]] Bookmark show.<ref>https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=N2TzcTKLUtY</ref> In April 2009 she was a speaker at the Eucharistic Convention in [[Auckland, New Zealand]]. In August 2010 Vidal spoke at the Catholic Writers Conference in [[Valley Forge, PA]]. In October 2010 she was presented to Archduke Imre of Austria and to [[Prince Bertrand of Orleans-Braganza]] in [[York, PA]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://teaattrianon.blogspot.com/2010/11/our-meeting-with-archduke-imre.html|title = Our Meeting with Archduke Imre|date = 3 November 2010}}</ref> Vidal is a member of the Catholic Writers Guild and the Eastern Shore Writers Association. In 2011, Vidal interviewed David Chambers, the grandson of ex-Communist spy [[Whittaker Chambers]], published in ''[[The American Conservative]]''<ref>http://www.amconmag.com/blog/historys-witness/</ref> (USA) and ''The [[National Observer (Australia)|National Observer]]''<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.nationalobserver.net/2011/84-2-david-chambers-interview.htm |title=Archived copy |access-date=2011-04-28 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120208120839/http://www.nationalobserver.net/2011/84-2-david-chambers-interview.htm |archive-date=2012-02-08 |url-status=dead }}</ref> (Australia). In August, 2011 Vidal became a reviewer for the Historical Novels Review, the journal of the Historical Novel Society.<ref>http://blog.catholicwritersguild.com/2011/08/cwg-news_26.html{{Dead link|date=August 2019 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref>

==Personal life and family==
"Elena Maria Vidal" is a [[nom-de-plume]] chosen to honor her Spanish grandmother; Vidal's [[legal name]] is Mary-Eileen Russell.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://garethrussellcidevant.blogspot.com/2010/07/interview-with-elena-maria-vidal-author.html|title = Confessions of a Ci-Devant: An interview with Elena Maria Vidal, author of "Trianon"|date = 10 July 2010}}</ref> She is the daughter of the late John Charles Laughland and of Alice Strong Laughland.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://teaattrianon.blogspot.com/2007/05/my-dad-rip.html|title = My Dad, R.I.P.|date = 6 May 2007}}</ref><ref>http://teaattrianon.blogspot.com/2008/09/memories-of-manila-1941-to-1945.html</ref> Among her cousins are British journalist [[John Laughland]], Canadian novelist [[Jennifer Lanthier]], and Canadian poet Kateri Lanthier.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://teaattrianon.blogspot.com/2006/11/john-laughland-my-cousin.html|title = John Laughland, my cousin|date = 27 November 2006}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://teaattrianon.blogspot.com/2008/04/mystery-of-martello-tower.html|title = The Mystery of the Martello Tower|date = 24 April 2008}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://teaattrianon.blogspot.com/2011/12/reporting-from-night.html|title=Reporting from Night|date=12 December 2011}}</ref> Vidal's great-grandfather was the [[socialist]] [[politician]] James Vint Laughland. She is also the cousin of actress and comedian [[Norma Jean (actress)|Norma Jean]].<ref>https://www.facebook.com/msnormajean</ref>

In 1986, Vidal joined the [[Secular Order of Discalced Carmelites]]. She briefly explored the [[religious life]] by entering a [[Carmelite]] [[monastery]] in the late 1980s.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://fountainofelias.blogspot.com/2010/02/my-pilgrimage-to-lourdes-part-i.html|title = My Pilgrimage to Lourdes, Part I|date = 9 February 2011}}</ref> She has described a trip to [[Lourdes, France]] in 1994 and a trip to Austria in 1995 as influencing her decision to write historical fiction.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://fountainofelias.blogspot.com/2010/02/my-pilgrimage-to-lourdes-part-ii.html|title=My Pilgrimage to Lourdes, Part II|date=10 February 2011}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |url=http://virginiebarbeau.wordpress.com/2010/01/09/celebrating-1000-comments-with-a-review-giveaway-and-guest-post/ |title=Archived copy |access-date=2011-03-06 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110718112334/http://virginiebarbeau.wordpress.com/2010/01/09/celebrating-1000-comments-with-a-review-giveaway-and-guest-post/ |archive-date=2011-07-18 |url-status=dead }}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://teaattrianon.blogspot.com/2008/03/empress-maria-theresa.html|title = Empress Maria Theresa|date = 13 March 2008}}</ref> In November 1996, Vidal married businessman Michael J. Russell.<ref>https://www.facebook.com/planetrussell</ref> She currently lives in Maryland with her family and has completed a historical novel called ''The Paradise Tree'' about her Irish ancestors which was published in October 2014 by Mayapple Books.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://blog.catherinedelors.com/an-interview-of-elena-maria-vidal-author-of-madame-royale/|title=An interview of Elena Maria Vidal, author of Madame Royale « Versailles and More}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://teaattrianon.blogspot.com/2014/05/the-past-and-present-mingle.html|title = "The Past and Present Mingle"|date = 25 May 2014}}</ref> ''The Paradise Tree'' has been listed by ''Kirkus Reviews'' as one of the top 20 indie books of 2014, and among the top 100 best books of the year, according to the December 2014 issue.<ref>http://teaattrianon.blogspot.com/2014/11/books-as-gifts.html</ref>
