'''Megan Whalen Turner''' (born November 21, 1965) is an [[American literature|American writer]] of [[fantasy]] fiction for young adults. She is best known for her novel ''[[The Thief (Turner novel)|The Thief]]'' and its five sequels. In 1997, ''The Thief'' was named a [[Newbery Medal|Newbery Honor]] book.<ref>{{cite book|last1=Horning|first1=Kathleen|title=The Newbery and Caldecott Awards: A Guide to the Medal and Honor Books|date=2009|publisher=American Library Association|isbn=978-0838935859|page=[https://archive.org/details/newberycaldecott00horn/page/30 30]|url=https://archive.org/details/newberycaldecott00horn/page/30}}</ref>

==Early life==

She received her BA with honors in English language and literature from the [[University of Chicago]] in 1987.<ref name="meganwhalenturner">{{cite web|url=http://meganwhalenturner.org/AbouttheAuthor.html|title=Megan Whalen Turner - About|publisher=meganwhalenturner.org|access-date=2014-01-25|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131224105738/http://meganwhalenturner.org/AbouttheAuthor.html|archive-date=2013-12-24}}</ref> Before becoming an author, she worked as a children's book buyer for bookstores in Chicago and Washington D.C.<ref name=alger>{{cite book|last1=Alger Drew|first1=Bernard|title=100 More Popular Young Adult Authors|date=2002|publisher=Libraries Unlimited|isbn=1563089203|page=[https://archive.org/details/100morepopularyo00drew/page/316 316]|url=https://archive.org/details/100morepopularyo00drew/page/316}}</ref>

==Career==
Turner began writing a collection of short fantasy stories after moving to [[California]] in 1989. She published the stories as ''[[Instead of Three Wishes: Magical Short Stories]]'' in 1995.

Turner is best known for her series of young adult novels primarily revolving around a character named Eugenides. Turner has no official name for the series herself, sometimes referring to it as "The Geniad",<ref name="firehouse">{{cite AV media |url=https://www.facebook.com/watch/live/?v=745615442687499 |title=Old Firehouse Books interview|time= 0:37 |date=27 October 2020 |access-date=2021-07-13}}</ref> but fans have coined it ''The Queen's Thief''. The first book in the series, ''[[The Thief (1996 novel)|The Thief]]'', won a [[Newbery Honor]] award.<ref name="ala">{{cite web|url=http://www.ala.org/ala/mgrps/divs/alsc/awardsgrants/bookmedia/newberymedal/newberyhonors/newberymedal.cfm|title=Newbery Medal and Honor Books, 1922-Present &#124; Association for Library Service to Children (ALSC)|publisher=ala.org|access-date=2014-01-25}}</ref>  The subsequent books in the series are ''[[The Queen of Attolia]]'', ''[[The King of Attolia]]'', ''[[A Conspiracy of Kings]]'', ''[[Thick as Thieves (Turner novel)|Thick as Thieves]]'', and ''Return of the Thief''.<ref name="meganwhalenturner2">{{cite web|url=http://meganwhalenturner.org/books/books.html|title=Megan Whalen Turner - Novels|last=|first=|date=|website=|publisher=meganwhalenturner.org|access-date=2014-08-03}}</ref>

Megan was the 2013 Literary Guest of Honor and Keynote Speaker at the [[Life, the Universe, & Everything]] professional science fiction and fantasy arts symposium.<ref name="LTUE program #31">{{cite web |title=Life, the Universe, & Everything 31: The Marion K. "Doc" Smith Symposium on Science Fiction and Fantasy |url=http://www.ltue.info/progbookpdfs/LTUEProgramBook2013.pdf |publisher=LTUE Press|date=February 1, 2013}}</ref>

In July 2018, Turner announced the March 2019 publication date of ''Return of the Thief''.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Turner |first1=Megan Whalen |title=Just in case you thought someone else was writing this book … |url=http://meganwhalenturner.tumblr.com/post/175117796992/by-megan-whalen-turner-just-in-case-you-thought |website=Tumblr |access-date=10 November 2018}}</ref>  This was first pushed back to spring 2020, before the book was finally published on October 6, 2020.

Her additional work includes a short story collection titled ''Instead of Three Wishes'', and the short story, "The Baby in the Night Deposit Box", published in a collection called ''[[Firebirds (anthology)|Firebirds]]'', edited by [[Sharyn November]]. "The Baby in the Night Deposit Box"  was selected for The Year's Best Fantasy and Horror. She has also written six uncollected short stories — "Thief!", "Destruction", "Eddis", "Knife Dance, "Wineshop", and "Alyta's Missing Earring" — which are set in the world of ''The Thief''.<ref name="meganwhalenturner3">{{cite web|url=http://meganwhalenturner.org/shortstories.html|title=Megan Whalen Turner - Short Stories|publisher=meganwhalenturner.org|access-date=2014-01-25|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140405051349/http://www.meganwhalenturner.org/shortstories.html|archive-date=2014-04-05}}</ref>

==Personal life==

Her husband is the [[cognitive scientist]] [[Mark Turner (cognitive scientist)|Mark Turner]]. The couple has three sons.
