'''Ad van Tiggelen''' ([[Maastricht]], 7 June 1958) is an [[author]] of [[Fantasy literature|fantasy novels]] using the [[pseudonym|pen name]] '''Adrian Stone'''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.depers.nl/cultuur/301821/Ik-beleg-en-ik-kan-ook-toveren.html |archive-url=https://archive.today/20120530172642/http://www.depers.nl/cultuur/301821/Ik-beleg-en-ik-kan-ook-toveren.html |url-status=dead |archive-date=2012-05-30 |title=Ik beleg en ik kan ook toveren |publisher=[[De Pers]] |date=2009-04-21 |language=nl }}</ref>

==Career==
Van Tiggelen studied [[business economics]] at the [[Erasmus University Rotterdam]] and in 1981 started his career at [[Nationale-Nederlanden]], where he became [[investment manager]] in 1988. 
Since then he moved through a series of investment jobs at ING Groep, including a long period as head of [[Europe]]an [[Equity (finance)|equities]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.morningstar.nl/nl/funds/article.aspx?lang=nl-NL&articleID=18161&categoryID=27|title = Groei noch waarde, maar GARP}}</ref> In the end, he worked as [[Investment strategy|investment strategist]], in which function he regularly appeared in national<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.volkskrant.nl/economie/article491288.ece/Kredietcrisis_maakt_voorspellen_beurskoersen_lastig|title=Cookiewall: Cookies op de Volkskrant - de Volkskrant|work=volkskrant.nl|accessdate=28 May 2015}}</ref> and international<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.reuters.com/article/managerMoves/idUSZWE12975020080711|title=Stocks close to capitulation - ING's van Tiggelen|work=Reuters|date=11 July 2008|accessdate=28 May 2015|last1=Robinson|first1=Blaise}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.faz.net/s/Rub09A305833E12405A808EF01024D15375/Doc~E7091EB6D6CB64D32BC87C8BE6B1B2D15~ATpl~Ecommon~Scontent.html?rss_aktuell|title = Aktuelle Nachrichten online}}</ref><ref>{{cite web | url = https://www.nytimes.com/2009/02/02/business/worldbusiness/02iht-col03.1.19860426.html?_r=1 | title = Crisis upends dividends | date = 2009-02-02 | work = [[The New York Times]]}}</ref><ref>{{cite web | url = https://www.wsj.com/articles/SB10001424052748704597704574486533633715604 | title = Telecoms Boost European Shares | date = 2009-10-22 | publisher = [[The Wall Street Journal]]}}</ref> media to comment on developments in the [[financial market]]s and to express and explain the vision of ING Investment Management.<ref>{{cite web | url = https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zaoTWOQpMaI | title = ING Investment Management: Webcast Equity Markets: Ad van Tiggelen | date = 2008-11-26 | publisher = [[YouTube]]}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.cnnstudentnews.cnn.com/TRANSCRIPTS/1008/03/qmb.01.html|title=CNN|work=cnn.com|accessdate=28 May 2015}}</ref><ref>http://www.ingim.com/file/IWP_029540{{dead link|date=October 2016 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref><ref>{{cite web | url = http://edition.cnn.com/video/?/video/business/2012/09/28/intv-wbt-europe-austerity-tiggelen.cnn | title = Global Economy: Spending vs. Saving | publisher = CNN | date = 2012-09-28}}</ref> He ended his career with ING on 30 June 2014.

==Columns==
Van Tiggelen wrote [[Column (periodical)|column]]s every month about a series of [[investment]] topics. These columns appeared in the [[Netherlands]] in [[Het Financieele Dagblad|FD.nl]]<ref>{{cite web | url = http://www.fd.nl/artikel/20129748/2010-verloren-jaar-aandelen | title = 2010: een verloren jaar voor aandelen? | publisher = [[Het Financieele Dagblad|FD.nl]] | date = 2010-08-27|language=nl}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.fd.nl/nieuws/dossiers/4672021/8190765/ |title=Dossier: Ad van Tiggelen |publisher=[[Het Financieele Dagblad]] |language=nl |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20080318224158/http://www.fd.nl/nieuws/dossiers/4672021/8190765/ |archivedate=March 18, 2008 }} (Internet Archive)</ref><ref>{{in lang|nl}} [http://www.fd.nl/zoeken?showResults=true&actie=shownewpage&bron_optie=&zoekterm=Ad+van+Tiggelen&periode=-1825&vandag=1&vanmaand=10&vanjaar=2004&totdag=30&totmaand=9&totjaar=2009&bedrijven=&sector=&auteurs=&rubriekenpaginas=&trefwoorden=&zoek.x=31&zoek.y=3 Ad van Tiggelen] in [[Het Financieele Dagblad|FD.nl]]</ref> and in various financial media in other countries: [[Belgium]], [[France]], [[Spain]], [[Italy]], [[Germany]], [[Greece]] and [[Poland]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.handelsblatt.com/finanzen/boerse-inside/geld-in-gefahr-anleger-fuerchten-inflation-doch-die-kommt-noch-nicht;2593078|title=Geld in Gefahr: Anleger fürchten Inflation – doch die kommt (noch) nicht - Märkte - Finanzen - Handelsblatt|work=handelsblatt.com|accessdate=28 May 2015}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://archives.lesechos.fr/archives/2011/LesEchos/20905-163-ECH.htm|title=Résultats recherche lesechos.fr|work=Les Echos.fr|date=5 April 2011|accessdate=28 May 2015}}</ref>

==Author==
Since his youth Van Tiggelen had a passion for [[fantasy literature]], which led him to write fantasy [[novel]]s himself.<ref>{{cite web | url = http://www.fd.nl/artikel/11467197/beleggingsstrateeg-fantasy-schrijver | title = Beleggingsstrateeg én fantasy-schrijver | publisher = [[Het Financieele Dagblad]] | date = 2009-04-23|language=nl}}</ref>
In 2006 his first book ''Devil's Prophet'' appeared in a limited edition at Gopher publishers. In 2008 he signed a contract with [[Luitingh-Sijthoff]], the Netherlands' largest publisher of speculative fiction and fantasy,<ref>{{cite web | url = http://www.luitinghsijthoff.nl/content/view/4/9/lang,en | title = About Luitingh-Sijthoff | publisher = [[Luitingh-Sijthoff]]}}</ref> on which basis Luitingh-Sijthoff re-published ''Devil's Prophet'' in April 2009, followed in June 2009 by ''Devil's Son''.
The third part of the Devil [[trilogy]], ''Devil's Soul'', appeared in April 2010. All books are published under the [[pseudonym]] '''Adrian Stone'''.<ref>{{cite web | url = http://www.dromen-demonen.nl/Auteurs/Adrian-Stone/index.html | title = Dromen & Demonen: Adrian Stone | publisher = [[Luitingh-Sijthoff]]|language=nl}}</ref>

After the Devil Trilogy Stone wrote the ''Rune'' Duology, which is situated in the same world. The first part ''The Eighth Rune'' was published in September 2011, the second part ''The First God'' in September 2012.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.adrianstone.nl/news.asp?id=26 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110524051958/http://www.adrianstone.nl/news.asp?id=26 |url-status=dead |archive-date=2011-05-24 |title=Nieuw tweeluik: Rune |publisher=www.adrianstone.nl |language=nl | access-date = 2021-07-07}}</ref>

After ''Rune'', Stone wrote a new series (title: ''Magycker''), which plays in a completely new world.<ref>{{cite web | url = https://www.facebook.com/adrianstoneofficial/posts/213583648780789 | title = Hierbij een wat uitgebreidere update ... | publisher = Facebook | date = 2013-02-14|language=nl}}</ref> The first part ''The Claw'' was published in 2016. The second and final part ''Dragon Heart'' will be published on October 26, 2021.<ref>{{cite book | title= Drakenhart Paperback – 26 oktober 2021 | id= {{ASIN|9024575273|country=nl}} }}</ref>

He is the Netherlands' best selling fantasy author.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bndestem.nl/breda/bredase-fantasy-bestseller-adrian-stone-noem-mij-gerust-een-nerd~a80fce20/  |title=Bredase fantasy-bestseller Adrian Stone: 'Noem mij gerust een nerd' | publisher=BN De Stem |language=nl | date = 2021-08-01 | access-date = 2021-08-01}}</ref>
