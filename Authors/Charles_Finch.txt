'''Charles Finch''' (born 1980) is an American [[author]] and [[literary critic]]. He has written a series of [[mystery novel]]s set in [[Victorian era]] England, as well as literary fiction and numerous essays and book reviews.

==Life and career==
Finch was born in [[New York City]].  He graduated from [[Phillips Academy]] and [[Yale University]], where he majored in English and History.  He also holds a [[master's degree]] in Renaissance English Literature from [[Merton College, Oxford]]. He is the grandson of American artist and writer [[Anne Truitt]].<ref>[http://www.aaa.si.edu/collections/oralhistories/transcripts/truitt02.htm Archives of American Art Interview with Anne Truitt] retrieved February 10, 2010</ref>

His first published novel, ''[[A Beautiful Blue Death]]'', introduced gentleman sleuth Charles Lenox. The book was named one of [[Library Journal]]’s Best Books of 2007<ref name="library">{{cite magazine | title=Best Books 2007|year=2007|magazine=Library Journal|url=http://www.libraryjournal.com/article/CA6510658.html}}</ref> and was nominated for the [[Agatha Award]] for best new mystery of 2007.<ref name="agatha">{{cite web| title=Agatha Awards| year=2008| publisher=Malice Domestic| url=http://www.malicedomestic.org/agathaawards.html| url-status=dead| archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20170121111008/http://www.malicedomestic.org/agathaawards.html| archivedate=January 21, 2017}}</ref>  ''[[The Fleet Street Murders]]'' came out in 2009 and was nominated for the [[Nero Award]].<ref name="Nero">{{cite web | title=Nominations for 2010 Nero Award Announced |year=2010|publisher=Mystery Books News|url=http://www.mystery-books.com/2010/06/nominations-for-2010-nero-award.html}}</ref> ''The Woman in the Water'', released in 2018, is a prequel presenting the beginning of Lenox’s career in detection. The series is published by St. Martin's Minotaur, a division of [[St. Martin's Press]].<ref name="minotaur">{{cite web|title=Charles Finch|year=2008|publisher=Macmillan Books|url=http://us.macmillan.com/Author/charlesfinch|access-date=August 8, 2008|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100110235900/http://us.macmillan.com/Author/charlesfinch|archive-date=January 10, 2010|url-status=dead}}</ref>

Finch's first contemporary novel, ''[[The Last Enchantments]]'', was published by St. Martin's Press in early 2014.<ref name="brown">{{cite web|title=Meet the Authors: Susanna Gregory Interviews Charles Finch|year=2012|publisher=Little Brown|url=http://www.littlebrown.co.uk/Sphere/susanna-gregory-interviews-charles-finch.page|access-date=January 23, 2013|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131014104704/http://www.littlebrown.co.uk/Sphere/susanna-gregory-interviews-charles-finch.page|archive-date=October 14, 2013|url-status=dead}}</ref><ref>[http://www.curtisbrown.co.uk/charles-finch/the-last-enchantments/ Curtis Brown - The Last Enchantments] retrieved January 23, 2012</ref>

He has written for ''[[The New York Times]]'' and ''[[Slate (magazine)|Slate]]''<ref name="Slate">{{cite magazine | title=Charles Finch |year=2014|magazine=Slate|url=http://www.slate.com/articles/arts/books/2014/06/stuart_dybek_s_short_story_collections_ecstatic_cahoots_and_paper_lantern.html}}</ref> and regularly writes essays and criticism for ''[[The New York Times]]'', ''[[The New York Times Book Review]]'', ''[[The New Yorker]]'', the ''[[The Guardian|Guardian]]'', the ''[[Chicago Tribune]]'' and ''[[USA Today]]''.<ref Finch serves on the curatorial board of the arts colony [[Ragdale]]<ref name="Ragdale.org">{{cite web |url=https://ragdale.org/about/board-staff/ |title=Curatorial Board |website=Ragdale |accessdate=August 19, 2018 }}</ref> and the board of the [[National Book Critics Circle]].<ref name="book critics.org">{{cite web |url=http://www.bookcritics.org/board/ |title=Board of Directors |website=National Book Critics Circle |accessdate=August 19, 2018 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180811060608/http://www.bookcritics.org/board |archive-date=August 11, 2018 |url-status=dead }}</ref>
