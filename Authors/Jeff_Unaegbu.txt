'''Jeff Unaegbu''' (born Elochukwu Jephthah Unaegbu on October 1, 1979) is a Nigerian writer, actor, artist and documentary film maker, the author of eight books.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://openlibrary.org/authors/OL6876805A/Jeff_Unaegbu|title=Jeff Unaegbu|last=OpenLibrary.org|website=Open Library|access-date=2018-01-03}}</ref><ref>Unaegbu, J. (2007). "I write to Put Right the Wrongs", Sunday Vanguard, April 8, P. 48.</ref>

==Life and career==

===Early years===
Unaegbu was born in Amankwo-Inyi village, [[Oji River]] LGA of [[Enugu State, Nigeria]], the first child of five surviving children to Mr. John Ejike Unaegbu and Mrs. Cecilia Enonanma Unaegbu. He attended the Government Secondary School, [[Minna]] in 1990, studying sciences and graduated in 1996.  He then attended Muhanatu Computer Institute, graduating in 2000 with distinctions in Certificate and Diploma in Computer Data Processing.  He then attended the [[University of Nigeria]], [[Nsukka]] from 2002 to 2006, graduating with second class in Mass Communication. As a student, Unaegbu served as the Editor of the university's ''Roar Magazine''. He also won faculty and poetry prizes.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://lurrenzinc.com/outofnigeria/2011/03/27/young-writer-that-is-making-nigeria-proud-jeff-unaegbu |title=An Online Magazine Reports an Interview of Jeff Unaegbu |access-date=2011-03-27 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110401193028/http://lurrenzinc.com/outofnigeria/2011/03/27/young-writer-that-is-making-nigeria-proud-jeff-unaegbu/ |archive-date=2011-04-01 }}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://nollywoodgossip.net/2011/03/06/young-writer-that-is-making-nigeria-proud-jeff-unaegbu-speaks-to-nollywoodgossip|title=An Online Magazine Interviews Jeff Unaegbu|access-date=2011-03-27}}</ref>

===Career===
Unaegbu became an Executive Officer of the Lagos State Council of Tradesmen and Artisans in the Ministry of Commerce at [[Alausa]], [[Ikeja]].  Based upon his experiences, he wrote his first book, ''This Lagos Na Wa'' (''Ode on Lagos'') ''and Other Poetic Portraits'' which was published in January 2006 by Prize Publishers. It is 700 lines long, with 668 lines in iambic pentameter, with [[rhyme scheme]] ABABCDCD.<ref>[https://books.google.com/books?id=bdS0PgAACAAJ&dq=%22Jeff+Unaegbu%22&hl=en&ei=iJ0dTYW-K4WClAeeqomgDA&sa=X&oi=book_result&ct=result&resnum=1&ved=0CCMQ6AEwAA]</ref>  The book was later said to contain a poem which was reviewed as "The longest poem by a Nigerian" in Nigeria's ''Newswatch'' magazine of April 2, page 57, 2007.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.newswatchngr.com/editorial/prime/bob/10326094437.htm|title=NewsWatchngr - Explore news that is trending now|website=NewsWatchngr|language=en-US|access-date=2018-01-03|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120504135846/http://www.newswatchngr.com/editorial/prime/bob/10326094437.htm|archive-date=2012-05-04|url-status=dead}}</ref>  Another magazine, ''The Roar'', declared that it is, "the longest metrical poem in Africa".<ref>[Ezendiokwele, S. (2005). "The Longest Metrical Poem in Africa!", The Roar, Vol. 6, No. 1, P.8. ]</ref> In 2010, a new edition of the book of poems was published, with an audio CD of the poems. Its new title was ''Ode on Lagos'' (''This Lagos Na Wa'') ''and Other Poetic Portraits''.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://openlibrary.org/works/OL15598340W/Ode_on_Lagos_and_other_poetic_Portraits|title = Ode on Lagos and other poetic Portraits (2006, 2010 edition) &#124; Open Library}}</ref>

While awaiting his undergraduate final year results, Unaegbu wrote his second book, the first-ever history of the Studentsâ€™ Union Government of the University of Nigeria, Nsukka from 1960 to 2004, ''Freedom in Our Bones''.<ref>Animalu, A.O.E., Njoku, F.I. and Ezekoye, B.A. (2010). ''Biography of Emeritus Professor James O.C. Ezeilo, FAS, CON, A World Class Mathematician and Administrator''. Abuja: Ucheakonam Foundation, p.34. {{ISBN|978-978-8417-91-0}}</ref><ref>[https://openlibrary.org/works/OL15598341W/Freedom_In_Our_Bones  ''Freedom in Our Bones'']</ref> The book was published the same month Jeff was posted to [[Bayelsa State]] for his National Youth Service Corps Programme, March 2007. The book won the 3rd position in the 2008 Nigerian Universities Research and Development Fair organized by the NUC.  This award helped the University of Nigeria to achieve second position overall during the Fair.<ref>[Edukugho, E. (2008). "UNILAG emerges overall winner of research fair", Vanguard, April 3, P.43. ]</ref>

While serving at Otu-oke in [[Ogbia]] LGA of [[Bayelsa State]], Unaegbu began work on the ''Origins of the NYSC'' and wrote a biography of the Professor Emeritus of Physics, Alexander Obiefoka Enukora Animalu, co-authored with Dr. Godfrey Akpojotor.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://physicsworld.com/cws/event/15271|title=Event details: Third International Seminar on Theoretical Physics and National Development (ISOTPAND2010) - physicsworld.com|website=physicsworld.com|language=en-GB|access-date=2018-01-03}}</ref><ref>{{Cite book|url=https://openlibrary.org/works/OL15598367W/Alexander_O._E._Animalu_A_Biography_of_the_Distinguished_Professor_of_Physics|title=Alexandetr O. Akpojotor and Jeff|date=2008|publisher=Ucheakonam Publishers|location=Abuja, Nigeria}}</ref>  The former Nigerian Minister of Science and Technology, Professor [[Bartholomew Nnaji]], remarked in his review of the book, that it described lucidly, life in Lagos in the late 1950s, especially the budding crime level.

In March 2011, Jeff Unaegbu became a contributing writer to ''NollywoodGossip'', an Online Magazine.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://lurrenzinc.com/outofnigeria/2011/03/27/jeff-unaegbu-nigerian-author-writes-for-nollywoodgossip |title=An Online Magazine Reports on Jeff Unaegbu's online writing contributions |access-date=2011-03-27 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110401193021/http://lurrenzinc.com/outofnigeria/2011/03/27/jeff-unaegbu-nigerian-author-writes-for-nollywoodgossip/ |archive-date=2011-04-01 }}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://nollywoodgossip.net/2011/03/25/jeff-unaegbu-nigerian-author-writes-for-nollywoodgossip|title=Another Online Magazine Reports on Jeff Unaegbu's online writing contributions|access-date=2011-03-27}}</ref> With Lawrence Onuzulike, another Nigerian Writer and actor, Jeff Unaegbu was also part of the development of a new website that is targeted at creating a platform for Nigerian writers to share thoughts together in the Nigerian literary, academic and art societies.<ref>[http://lurrenzinc.com/outofnigeria/about-us Lurrenzinc.com]</ref> In April 2011, Jeff Unaegbu's story, "Prey" was among the eight stories shortlisted out of 151 entries from sixteen countries in the inaugural edition of the Online African Writing Prize for Flash Fiction.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.african-writing.com/eleven/flashfiction1.htm|title=Results - Flash Fiction Competition, 2011;
