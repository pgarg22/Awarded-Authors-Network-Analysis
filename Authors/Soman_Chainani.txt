'''Soman Chainani''' is an American author and filmmaker, best known for writing the children's book series ''[[The School for Good and Evil]]''.<ref>{{cite web|title=Soman Chainani hits jackpot with novel 'School for Good and Evil'|url=http://www.miamiherald.com/2013/06/04/3433035/soman-chainani-hits-jackpot-with.html|publisher=Miami Herald|access-date=April 7, 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|last=Metallidis|first=Effie-Michelle|title='The characters drive your story'|date=October 16, 2008|url=http://www.thenational.ae/arts-culture/film/the-characters-drive-your-story|publisher=The National|access-date=April 7, 2014}}</ref>

Soman's first novel, ''[[The School for Good and Evil (novel)|The School for Good and Evil]]'', debuted on the [[New York Times Bestseller List]], has sold more than 3 million copies, been translated into 30 languages across 6 continents, and will soon be a film from [[Netflix]] directed by [[Paul Feig]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=Paul Feig to Direct 'School for Good and Evil' Adaptation for Netflix|url=https://www.hollywoodreporter.com/heat-vision/paul-feig-direct-school-good-evil-adaptation-netflix-1296168|website=The Hollywood Reporter|date=May 28, 2020|language=en|access-date=June 1, 2020}}</ref>

His other five books in the School for Good and Evil series – ''[[A World Without Princes]]'', ''The Last Ever After'', ''[[Quests for Glory]]'', ''A Crystal of Time'' and ''[[One True King (novel)|One True King]]'' – have all debuted on the New York Times Bestseller List as well. To date, these six books in the series have been on the print and extended New York Times Bestseller List for more than 35 weeks.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://schoolforgoodandevil.com/|title=School for Good and Evil|language=en-US|access-date=April 22, 2019}}</ref>

His most recent book, ''Beasts and Beauty,'' was released on September 21, 2021, to wide acclaim, with Kirkus Reviews calling the collection "expertly crafted... evoking the wonder, terror, and magic of the fantasy realms."<ref>{{Cite book|url=https://www.kirkusreviews.com/book-reviews/soman-chainani/beasts-and-beauty/|title=BEASTS AND BEAUTY {{!}} Kirkus Reviews|language=en}}</ref>

==Early life and personal life==
Chainani grew up in [[Key Biscayne, Florida]], where his family was one of the few of [[Indian Americans|Indian]] descent.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Hodara|first1=Susan|title=Soman Chainani's new fantasy-adventure novel is a fairy tale for today's world|url=https://harvardmagazine.com/2013/05/princess-not-so-charming|website=[[Harvard Magazine]]|date=2013}}</ref> He attended [[Harvard University]], where he graduated with a degree in English and American Literature in 2001. As of 2003 he resided in New York City.<ref>{{cite book |authors=Contributors |year=2003 |title=Marvels & Tales |url=https://muse.jhu.edu/article/46248 |publisher=Project MUSE |volume=17 |issue=2 |pages=294–296 |doi=10.1353/mat.2003.0024 }}</ref> He came out as gay in his senior year at college.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://tim.blog/2018/06/21/the-tim-ferriss-show-transcripts-soman-chainani/ |title=The Tim Ferriss Show Transcripts: Soman Chainani (#220) |author=<!--Not stated--> |date=2018-06-21 |website=The Tim Ferriss Show |access-date=2021-05-17}}</ref> After graduation, he went on to attend [[Columbia University]], where he participated in their MFA Film Program.
