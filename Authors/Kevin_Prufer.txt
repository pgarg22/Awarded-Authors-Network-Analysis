'''Kevin D. Prufer''' (born 1969 in [[Cleveland, Ohio]]) is an American [[poet]], [[academia|academic]], [[editing|editor]], and [[essayist]]. His most recent books are ''How He Loved Them'' ([[Four Way Books]], 2018),''Churches'' ([[Four Way Books]], 2014), ''In A Beautiful Country'' ([[Four Way Books]], 2011) and ''National Anthem'' ([[Four Way Books]], 2008). 

==Life==
Prufer graduated from [[Western Reserve Academy]] in 1988. He received a [[Bachelor of Arts|B.A.]] at [[Wesleyan University]] and an [[Master of Arts|M.A.]] at the [[Hollins University]] Writing Program. He went on to earn an [[Master of Fine Arts|MFA]] at [[Washington University in St. Louis]]. He is currently [[Professor]] of [[English studies|English]] in the Creative Writing Program at the [[University of Houston]] and Editor-at-Large of ''[[Pleiades (magazine)|Pleiades: A Journal of New Writing]]'',<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.cmsu.edu/englphil/pleiades/editors.html |title=Editors |accessdate=2006-12-15 |publisher=Pleiades & Pleiades Press |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20061209165800/http://www.cmsu.edu/englphil/pleiades/editors.html |archive-date=2006-12-09 |url-status=dead }}</ref> Associate Editor of ''[[American Book Review]]'',<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.litline.org/ABR/about.html |title=American Book Review |accessdate=2006-12-15 |publisher=LitLine |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20070105193635/http://www.litline.org/ABR/about.html |archive-date=2007-01-05 |url-status=dead }}</ref> Co-Curator of the Unsung Masters Series, and the former Vice President/Secretary of the [[National Book Critics Circle]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.bookcritics.org/?go=board |title=Board of Directors |accessdate=2006-12-15 |publisher=National Book Critics Circle}}</ref> Prufer currently resides in [[Houston, Texas]] with [[artist]] and [[literary criticism|critic]] Mary Hallab.<ref name="home">{{cite web |url=http://www.kevinprufer.com/ |title=Kevin Prufer |accessdate=2006-12-15 |last=Prufer |first=Kevin}}</ref>  He is Professor of English in the Creative Writing Program at the University of Houston.

==Career==
He has published poems, essays, and reviews  in literary journals and magazines including ''The Paris Review, American Poetry Review, Poetry, A Public Space, AGNI, The New Republic, The Kenyon Review'',<ref name="home"/> ''[[Boston Review]], [[Georgia Review]], '' and in ''[[The Best American Poetry]]'' (2003 & 2009). 

His honors include four [[Pushcart Prize]]s, and awards from the [[Poetry Society of America]], the [[Academy of American Poets]], [[The Lannan Foundation]] and other organizations. His first book, ''Strange Wood'', received the 1997 Lena-Miles Wever Todd Poetry Prize (formerly the Winthrop Prize).<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.poetrymagazine.com/archives/2001/October01/prufer.htm |title=Kevin D. Prufer |accessdate=2006-12-15 |publisher=PoetryMagazine.com |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20061116201949/http://www.poetrymagazine.com/archives/2001/October01/prufer.htm |archive-date=2006-11-16 |url-status=dead }}</ref> He has also been awarded a 2007 [[National Endowment for the Arts]] Literature Fellowship in Poetry.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.nea.gov/grants/recent/07grants/Lit.html |title=FY 2007 Grant Awards: Literature Fellowships in Poetry |accessdate=2006-12-20 |publisher=[[National Endowment for the Arts]] |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20070206092453/http://www.nea.gov/grants/recent/07grants/Lit.html |archivedate=2007-02-06 }}</ref>

Kevin is a featured faculty member at the 2018 Conference on Poetry at [[The Frost Place]] in Franconia, NH.
