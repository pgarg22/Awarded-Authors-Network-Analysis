[[File:Erno Polgar.jpg|Erno Polgar|thumb]]'''Ernő Polgár''' (born Ernö Csupity; 27 January 1954 in [[Bácsalmás]],<ref>Magyar Irodalom Evkonyve Budapest, 2003. 152. o.  {{ISBN|963 9373 64 8}}</ref> died 28 October 2018 in Borneo <ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.litera.hu/hirek/elhunyt-polgar-erno|title=Elhunyt Polgár Ernő – Litera – Az irodalmi portál|website=www.litera.hu}}</ref> is a Radnoti Prize-winning<ref>" http://www.demokrata.info/velemenyek-publicisztika/724-konyvegetes"</ref> Nagy Lajos Prize-winning In 2007 he won the one of the highest Hungarian prizes:(Knight’s Cross of the Order of the Republic of Hungary) He was nominated for [[Nobel Prize in Literature]] in 2017 for the year of 2018. World literary rank Hungarian-european<ref>"(International Who is who "(MTI ki kicsoda) 2009. Szerk. Hermann Péter. Budapest: Magyar Távirati Iroda. 2008. {{ISBN|978-963-1787-283}} Ki kicsoda Greger-Biograf Bp. 2000. 1298 oldal</ref> author, editor, littérateur and dramaturgist.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.szepiroktarsasaga.hu/tagjaink/Polg%C3%A1r_Ern%C3%B4 |title=Archived copy |access-date=2014-10-19 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140827030145/http://www.szepiroktarsasaga.hu/tagjaink/Polg%C3%A1r_Ern%C3%B4 |archive-date=2014-08-27 |url-status=dead }}</ref> From 2018 he was living in Borneo Island, Brunei Kingdom in his "writing house".

== Life ==
He attended the secondary grammar school Janos Hunyadi<ref>"A bacsalmasi Hunyadi Janos Gimnazium evfordulos evkonyve" 1953–1993. Bacsalmas, 1993. 35-36. o. {{ISBN|963 04 3027 4}}</ref> and did his final exams there in 1972. He worked as a librarian and graduated at the [[Academy of Drama and Film in Budapest|University of Theatre and Film Arts]] as a dramaturgist.<ref>"http://www.litera.hu/szemely/Polgar-Erno {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180212004956/http://www.litera.hu/szemely/Polgar-Erno |date=2018-02-12 }}"</ref>
Between 1972 and 1976 he worked as a librarian in the [[National Széchényi Library]] then became an editor for the [[Magyar Rádió|Hungarian Radio Corporation]].<ref>"Who´s who in Europe London, 2003. {{ISBN|92-890-1348-6}}"</ref> From 1979 he worked as a dramaturgist for the {{ill|Madach Theatre|hu|Madách Színház}}<ref>"Scenes of Hungarian Theatres International Theater Institute" Ed. György Lehel  Bp. 1981. {{ISBN|963 691 029 4}}</ref> then between 1997 and 1998 worked as Head of Registrars Department at the University of Theatre and Film Arts.<ref name="auto">"Hübners Who is Who" CH-6304Zug, 2005. {{ISBN|3-7290-0051-9}}</ref>

== Literary career ==
Ernő Polgár is a well-known writer. He received the [[Radnóti Anti-Racism Award]] 2014<ref>"Cserenyi Rita: Polgar Erno Eletpalya, muvei es repertorium  in  Zsidok, kotródjatok!
Jews, shove off! Exile to Babylon" Bp. 2010. 449-719. o. {{ISBN|978-963-9864-10-8}}</ref>

The readers can find the different manifestations of the [[anti-Semitism]] in his works<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ceo.hu/pr/CV/BT_CV.html|title=Barát Tamás CV|website=www.ceo.hu}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://mek.oszk.hu/12600/12682|title=Holocaust : Gettó a Délvidéken|first=Ernő|last=Polgár|date=13 December 2018|publisher=[Szerző]}}</ref> This sentence makes it sound like his writing is anti-semitic, which doesn't make sense given the preceding sentence noting that he won an award for not being racist. Ernő Polgar's philosophy is, “the measure of the civilization are the respect for ancestors and humanism.”<ref>"http://hegedus.bzsh.hu/web/index.php/magunkrol/koerzetuenk-mveszei.html"</ref><ref>Tom Barat (New York)</ref>
He started writing as a child for the journal ''The People of Petofi''.<ref>"Yearbook of the Hungarian Literature (A magyar irodalom evkonyve)" 2002. Bp. 2003. page no. 152. {{ISBN|963 9373 64 8}}</ref><ref>"Contemporary hungarian writers (Kortars magyar irok)" 1945–1997 Bp. Enciklopedia Edition, 1998. I. A-J II. K-Z {{ISBN|963 8477 31 8}}</ref>
His sociography titled “The Lodgers” was published in the magazine {{ill|Budapest (magazine)|hu|Budapest (folyóirat)|lt=''Budapest''}} in 1975. He won the scholarship of the [[Hungarian Academy of Sciences]]<ref name="auto1">"Who´s who in Europe" London, 2003. {{ISBN|92-890-1348-6}}</ref> in 1981, the Soros Fellowship<ref name="auto" /> in 1983 and the Scholarship of Pro Cultura Hungariae in 1998<ref>"Yearbook of the Hungarian Literature" (A magyar irodalom evkonyve) 2002. Bp. 2003. page no. 152. {{ISBN|963 9373 64 8}}</ref> He has been a free-lance writer since 1998. Between 2000 and 2003 he was the head of Blue Shop Online Gallery and Digital Publisher and chairman of the supervisory board of www.hun-info.hu online media agency. From 2002 he was the secretary and later chairman of the Prose Section of the [[Hungarian Writers' Union]].<ref>"http://www.irodalmijelen.hu/05242013-1001/keleti-izek-beszelgetes-polgar-ernovel"</ref>
Since 2004 he has been the member of the {{ill|Society of Belletrists|hu|Szépírók Társasága}},<ref>"http://www.spanyolnatha.hu/archivum/tata/17/szepirodalom/polgar-erno/924"</ref> managing literary programs: “Nights in Lipotvaros” (Club Gallery, Ujlipotvaros), “Encounters” and “Frankel Nights”. Member of the Hungarian P.E.N. Club<ref>"http://www.pen-hungary.eu/tagok/ (Members of the International PEN CLUB)"</ref> and the National Association of Hungarian Artists (MAOE), chairman of the Association of <ref>"Cserenyi Rita: Polgar Erno Eletpalya, muvei es repertorium  in  Zsidok, kotródjatok!
Jews, shove off! Exile to Babylon" Bp. 2010. 449-719. o. {{ISBN|978-963-9864-10-8}}</ref> Humanist Writers (HIT) and secretary general of the Barankovics Jewish Workshop In 2007 he won the one of the highest Hungarian prizes:<ref>"http://douglas.shopline.hu/search/authorProducts!page.action?author=polgar-erno&pageStart=20&pageLength=80"</ref> Magyar Köztarsasagi Erdemrend Lovagkereszt (Knight’s Cross of the Order of the Republic of Hungary) [[File:Erno Polgar is in Bali.jpg|Erno Polgar in Bali|thumb]]
