'''Marissa Meyer''' (born February 19, 1984)<ref>{{Cite web|title=Library of Congress LCCN Permalink n2011078235|url=https://lccn.loc.gov/n2011078235|access-date=2021-12-01|website=lccn.loc.gov}}</ref> is an American novelist. Her [[debut novel]], ''[[Cinder (novel)|Cinder]],'' was released on January 3, 2012. It is the first in her series ''[[The Lunar Chronicles]]''.

==Early life and education==
Meyer was born in [[Tacoma, Washington]] and attended [[Pacific Lutheran University]], where she received a Bachelors in Creative Writing and Children's Literature. She later attended Pace University and received a Master's in Publishing.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|title=About Marissa – Marissa Meyer|url=https://www.marissameyer.com/about-marissa-meyer/|access-date=2021-12-01|website=www.marissameyer.com}}</ref> Growing Meyer admits that she had a strong love for fairy tales and one of her favorite shows was Sailor Moon, both of which later impacted her creation of ''Cinder''.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Media|first=Mast|title=Author, alum Marissa Meyer talks ‘Sailor Moon’ and ‘Cinder’ {{!}} Mast Media|url=http://mastmedia.plu.edu/2014/author-alum-marissa-meyer-talks-sailor-moon-and-cinder/|access-date=2021-12-01|language=en-US}}</ref> Meyer also says that her love of superheroes helped lead to the creation of ''Renegades''.<ref name=":1">{{Cite web|date=2018-11-06|title=Author Marissa Meyer on Her Inspiration to Write a Teen Superhero Story|url=https://theportalist.com/marissa-meyer-renegades-interview|access-date=2021-12-02|website=theportalist.com|language=en}}</ref> 

== Career ==
Before writing ''Cinder'', wrote ''[[Sailor Moon]]'' [[fan fiction]] for ten years under the [[pen name]] of Alicia Blade.<ref name=":02">{{Cite web|title=About Marissa – Marissa Meyer|url=https://www.marissameyer.com/about-marissa-meyer/|access-date=2021-12-01|website=www.marissameyer.com}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=FAQ – Marissa Meyer|url=https://www.marissameyer.com/faq/|access-date=2021-12-02|website=www.marissameyer.com}}</ref> She also wrote a novelette titled ''The Phantom of Linkshire Manor'' under her pen name.<ref name=":02" /> In an interview with ''[[The News-Tribune]]'', she said that doing the fan fiction helped her learn the craft of writing, gave her instant feedback and taught her how to take criticism.<ref name="News Tribune2">{{cite web|last=Sailor|first=Craig|title=Tacoma novelist hits it big with her futuristic story "Cinder"|url=http://www.thenewstribune.com/2012/02/03/2010487/fairy-tale-meets-sci-fi.html|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120603021128/http://www.thenewstribune.com/2012/02/03/2010487/fairy-tale-meets-sci-fi.html|archive-date=2012-06-03|publisher=Tacoma News - McClatchy Company|newspaper=[[The News Tribune]]}}</ref> After graduating from college and prior to writing ''Cinder'', Meyer worked as a book editor and a freelance typesetter and proofreader.<ref name=":02" />

Meyer states that she was initially inspired to write ''Cinder'' after participating in the 2008 [[National Novel Writing Month]] contest where she wrote a story focusing on a futuristic version of ''[[Puss in Boots]]''.<ref name="News Tribune3">{{cite web|last=Sailor|first=Craig|title=Tacoma novelist hits it big with her futuristic story "Cinder"|url=http://www.thenewstribune.com/2012/02/03/2010487/fairy-tale-meets-sci-fi.html|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120603021128/http://www.thenewstribune.com/2012/02/03/2010487/fairy-tale-meets-sci-fi.html|archive-date=2012-06-03|publisher=Tacoma News - McClatchy Company|newspaper=[[The News Tribune]]}}</ref> The ''Lunar Chronicles'' is a [[Tetralogy|four book series]] with volumes based on the stories of [[Cinderella]], [[Little Red Riding Hood]], [[Rapunzel]], and [[Snow White]].<ref name="News Tribune3" /><ref>[http://books.usatoday.com/bookbuzz/post/2011-11-01/exclusive-excerpt-of-cinder-by-marissa-meyer/560252/1 'Cinder' by Marissa Meyer joins fleet of fairy tale-based books (Exclusive excerpt)] USA Today</ref> The first book, ''Cinder'', was a New York Times bestseller.<ref>{{cite news|author=Fowler, Tara|date=February 14, 2013|title=Marissa Meyer talks 'Scarlet,' her worries over 'Cinder,' and a potential movie -- EXCLUSIVE|work=Entertainment Weekly|url=http://shelf-life.ew.com/2013/02/14/marissa-meyer-talks-scarlet-her-worries-over-cinder-and-a-potential-movie-exclusive/|access-date=30 September 2013}}</ref>

In 2013 Meyer received a two-book deal from Feiwel & Friends for a young adult series featuring the Queen of Hearts from ''[[Alice in Wonderland]]''. The first book, ''Heartless'', was set for a Fall 2016 release. It is a stand-alone novel. ''Publishers Weekly'' announced that she would write a superhero series afterwards.<ref>{{cite news|author=Deahl, Rachel|date=September 30, 2013|title=Book Deals: Week of September 30, 2013|work=Publishers Weekly|url=http://www.publishersweekly.com/pw/by-topic/industry-news/book-deals/article/59305-book-deals-week-of-september-30-2013.html|access-date=30 September 2013}}</ref> The resulting book is called ''Renegades'', which was inspired by the misreading of a sign and impacted by her love of superheroes growing up.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2019-12-21|title=Q&A: Marissa Meyer, Author of 'Supernova'|url=https://www.thenerddaily.com/marissa-meyer-author-interview/|access-date=2021-12-01|website=The Nerd Daily|language=en-AU}}</ref><ref name=":12">{{Cite web|date=2018-11-06|title=Author Marissa Meyer on Her Inspiration to Write a Teen Superhero Story|url=https://theportalist.com/marissa-meyer-renegades-interview|access-date=2021-12-02|website=theportalist.com|language=en}}</ref> ''Renegades'' was released in the Fall of 2017, and became the number 2 book on the New York Times bestsellers in 

November.<ref>{{Cite news|title=Best Sellers - Books - Nov. 26, 2017 - The New York Times|language=en-US|work=The New York Times|url=https://www.nytimes.com/books/best-sellers/2017/11/26/|access-date=2021-12-02|issn=0362-4331}}</ref>

In 2020 she released ''Instant Karma'' and in February 2021 it was optioned for television by [[HBO Max]].<ref>{{Cite web|date=2021-02-26|title=HBO Max Options Marissa Meyer's 'Instant Karma' Novel for Series (Exclusive)|url=https://www.hollywoodreporter.com/news/hbo-max-options-marissa-meyers-instant-karma-novel-for-series-exclusive|access-date=2021-02-27|website=The Hollywood Reporter|language=en}}</ref>

In November 2021 she released ''Gilded'', a novel based on the story of [[Rumpelstiltskin]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=Fantasy Author Marissa Meyer Reveals Her Next Book Cover: Gilded|url=https://io9.gizmodo.com/marissa-meyer-reveals-her-next-book-cover-gilded-and-1846349606|access-date=2021-02-27|website=io9|language=en-us}}</ref>

Since 2020 she has hosted ''The Happy Writer with Marissa Meyer'', a podcast in which she interviews authors about their work.<ref>{{Cite web|title=The Happy Writer Podcast – Marissa Meyer|url=https://www.marissameyer.com/podcast/|access-date=2021-07-06|website=www.marissameyer.com}}</ref>

== Personal life ==
Meyer is married to Jesse Taylor.<ref name="News Tribune">{{cite web|last=Sailor|first=Craig|title=Tacoma novelist hits it big with her futuristic story "Cinder"|url=http://www.thenewstribune.com/2012/02/03/2010487/fairy-tale-meets-sci-fi.html|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120603021128/http://www.thenewstribune.com/2012/02/03/2010487/fairy-tale-meets-sci-fi.html|archive-date=2012-06-03|publisher=Tacoma News - McClatchy Company|newspaper=[[The News Tribune]]}}</ref> In 2015, she and her husband adopted their twin daughters, Sloane and Delaney.<ref name="ID">{{cite web|last1=Meyer|first1=Marissa|title=ID|url=http://www.marissameyer.com/id/|website=Marissa Meyer|access-date=March 1, 2017}}</ref>
