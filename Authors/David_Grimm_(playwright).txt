'''David Grimm''' (born {{circa|1965}}) is an American playwright.

==Background==
Grimm was born in 1965 in [[Oberlin, Ohio]], where both his parents were professors at [[Oberlin College]].<ref name="DAG">{{cite web |url=http://www.huntingtontheatre.org/articles/HISTORY-GAMES-The-Life-and-Plays-of-David-Grimm/ |title=History Games: The Life and Plays of David Grimm |last=Fitzgerald |first=Jason |website=huntingtontheatre.org |publisher=[[Huntington Theatre Company]] |access-date=October 21, 2016}}</ref> Upon their divorce when Grimm was age five, he moved with his mother and sister to Israel, until he was age 11. His family then continually relocated throughout Europe, as his mother's teaching job demanded, before Grimm ultimately returned to Oberlin to live with his father and finish high school.<ref name="DAG" /><ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.edgemedianetwork.com/entertainment/theatre/news//38546/david_grimm:_playwrighting_for_pleasure |title=David Grimm: Playwrighting for Pleasure |last=Gach |first=PJ |date=March 5, 2006 |website=edgemedianetwork.com |access-date=October 22, 2016}}</ref> In 1983, Grimm moved to New York to attend college at [[Sarah Lawrence]] and, later, graduate school at [[New York University]]'s [[Tisch School of the Arts]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.nytimes.com/2006/03/07/theater/newsandfeatures/the-comedy-is-restoration-but-the-sex-is-timeless.html |title=The Comedy Is Restoration, but the Sex Is Timeless |last=McElroy |first= Steven |date=March 7, 2006 |website=nytimes.com |access-date=October 22, 2016}}</ref> From there, he took residency in [[New Dramatists]].<ref name="DAG" />

==Plays==

''Tales of Red Vienna'' received its world premiere at The Manhattan Theatre Company on March 18, 2014. The production was directed by [[Kate Whoriskey]], starring [[Nina Arianda]], [[Tina Benko]], [[Kathleen Chalfant]], Michael Esper, Michael Goldsmith and Lucas Hall.<ref>[http://www.broadwayworld.com/article/MTCs-TALES-FROM-RED-VIENNA-with-Nina-Arianda-Opens-Tonight-20140318 MTC's ''Tales of Red Vienna'' with Nina Arianda Opens Tonight]</ref>

''The Miracle at Naples'' received its world premiere at The [[Huntington Theatre Company]] in Boston in April 2009. The production was directed by Huntington artistic director Peter Dubois, starring [[Dick Latessa]], [[Alma Cuervo]], and Lucy Devitto.<ref>[http://www.huntingtontheatre.org/season/production.aspx?id=5447 Huntington Theatre Company – Miracle at Naples]</ref>

''Steve & Idi'' was developed at the Sundance Theatre Lab and is the first play from Rattlestick's DirtyWorks reading series to have a full production. The play received its world premiere at Rattlestick Playwrights Theater in New York City in April 2008 in which Grimm played Steve to the Idi of [[Evan Parke]].<ref>[https://www.smarttix.com/show.aspx?showCode=STE20 Smarttix.com - Steve & Idi]</ref>

'' Chick'' is based on the book ''Magician of the Modern'' by Eugene R. Gaddis and was commissioned by Hartford Stage as part of the Hartford Heritage Project. The play received its world premiere at Hartford Stage in 2007 starring Robbie Sella and his wife, Enid Graham.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.hartfordstage.org/see_a_show/past_productions/chick_the_great_osram |title=Hartford Stage - Chick |access-date=2009-07-20 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20091119135925/http://www.hartfordstage.org/see_a_show/past_productions/chick_the_great_osram |archive-date=2009-11-19 |url-status=dead }}</ref><ref>[http://www.dramatists.com/cgi-bin/db/single.asp?key=3947 Dramatists - David Grimm/Chick]</ref>

''[[Measure for Pleasure]]'' received its world premiere in March 2006 at [[The Public Theater]] in New York City. The production was directed by Peter DuBois, starring [[Wayne Knight]], [[Michael Stuhlbarg]], [[Euan Morton]], and [[Suzanne Bertish]].<ref>[https://www.nytimes.com/2006/02/26/theater/newsandfeatures/26zino.html?pagewanted=print NY Times - News and Features]</ref><ref>[http://www.dramatists.com/cgi-bin/db/single.asp?key=3774 Dramatists.com - David Grimm/Measure for Pleasure]</ref>

''The Learned Ladies of Park Avenue'' received its world premiere at Hartford Stage in September 2005 directed by artistic director Michael Wilson.<ref>[http://www.dramatists.com/cgi-bin/db/single.asp?key=3726 Dramatists.com - David Grimm/Learned Ladies of Park Avenue]</ref>

''The Savages of Hartford'' was commissioned by The Public Theater/NY Shakespeare Festival.<ref>[http://www.newdramatists.org/david_grimm.htm NewDramatists.org - David Grimm]</ref>

''Kit Marlowe'' received its world premiere at The Public Theater in November 2000, starring [[Christian Camargo]], [[Sam Trammell]], and [[Keith David]].<ref>[http://www.dramatists.com/cgi-bin/db/single.asp?key=2987 Dramatists.com - David Grimm/Kit Marlowe]</ref>

''Sheridan, or Schooled in Scandal'' received its world premiere at the [[La Jolla Playhouse]] in San Diego, California, in July 2000. The production was directed by [[Mark Brokaw]].<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.lajollaplayhouse.org/about-the-playhouse/playhouse-history/world-premieres |title=La Jolla Playhouse, San Diego, CA |access-date=2009-07-20 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090912154422/http://www.lajollaplayhouse.org/about-the-playhouse/playhouse-history/world-premieres |archive-date=2009-09-12 |url-status=dead }}</ref><ref>[http://www.dramatists.com/cgi-bin/db/single.asp?key=2988 Dramatists.org - David Grimm/Sheridan]</ref>

''edgar'' was the winner of the 1996 Julie Harris Playwright's Award and the 1996 Panowski Award.<ref name="Dramatists.org - David Grimm/edgar">[http://www.newdramatists.org/david_grimm.htm Dramatists.org - David Grimm/edgar]</ref>

''Killing Hilda'' was produced by Blue Moon Productions in 1999.<ref>[http://www.newdramatists.org/david_grimm.htm#KILLING_HILDA,_a_love_story Dramatists.com - David Grimm/Killing Hilda]</ref>

==Appearances==

Originally taped in December 2007, Grimm joined a panel of playwrights on [[American Theatre Wing]]'s Working in the Theatre. Grimm, [[Carlyle Brown]], [[Quiara Alegría Hudes]], and [[Lucy Thurber]] talked about their differing styles of playwriting, what inspired them to start writing, how the world of the playwright has evolved and changed and what influences their writing and the challenges in getting their work produced.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://americantheatrewing.org/wit/detail/new_dramatists_12_07 |title=American Theare Wing TV Appearance |access-date=2009-07-19 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090618215407/http://americantheatrewing.org/wit/detail/new_dramatists_12_07 |archive-date=2009-06-18 |url-status=dead }}</ref>
