'''M. K. Asante''' (born November 3, 1982) is an American author, filmmaker, recording artist, and professor. He is the author of the 2013 best-selling memoir [[Buck: A Memoir|''Buck'']].<ref name="r1">{{cite web|url=http://articles.baltimoresun.com/2014-02-15/entertainment/bs-ae-asante-profile-20140214_1_buck-malo-naacp-image-award|title=Morgan State professor's memoir, Buck makes big splash|author=McCauley, Mary Carole|work=The Baltimore Sun|url-status=dead|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20141026105342/http://articles.baltimoresun.com/2014-02-15/entertainment/bs-ae-asante-profile-20140214_1_buck-malo-naacp-image-award|archivedate=October 26, 2014|df=mdy-all}}</ref><ref name="r2">[http://www.cnn.com/2011/WORLD/africa/04/05/mk.asante/ Master storyteller M.K. Asante keeps it real in the classroom]. CNN.com. Retrieved on June 23, 2017.</ref>

==Early life and education==
Born in [[Harare]], [[Zimbabwe]] and raised in [[Philadelphia, Pennsylvania]], Asante is the son of scholar [[Molefi Kete Asante]] and choreographer [[Kariamu Welsh]].

Asante is a graduate of [[The Crefeld School]].<ref>[http://www.chestnuthilllocal.com/issues/2005.12.15/locallife4.html Filmmaker from Hill thrills Crefeld School kids] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20070926233430/http://www.chestnuthilllocal.com/issues/2005.12.15/locallife4.html|date=September 26, 2007}}, ChestnutHillLocal.com, December 15, 2005.</ref> He studied film and literature at the [[University of London]], [[School of Oriental and African Studies]], earned a BA in Africana Studies and English from [[Lafayette College]], and an M.F.A. in Screenwriting from [[UCLA School of Theater Film and Television]].<ref>[http://www.admission.ucla.edu/OurPastMeetsYourFuture/Our_Past_Meets_Your_Future.pdf "Our Past Meeta Your Future"], UCLA.</ref>

== Career ==

===Books===
[[Image:BUCK-BY-MK-ASANTE.jpg|thumb|left|180px|Cover of ''Buck: A Memoir'' ([[Random House]]) by MK Asante]]
Asante is the author of four books, most notably ''[[Buck (book)|Buck]]'' (2013), a memoir about his troubled youth in Philadelphia. ''Buck'' was selected as a [[Barnes & Noble]] Discover Great New Writers pick and made the ''[[Washington Post]]'' bestseller list in 2014 and 2015.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.barnesandnoble.com/s/?category_id=2628740 |title=Barnes & Noble Discover Great New Writers 2013 |website=bn.com |accessdate=November 14, 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url= https://www.washingtonpost.com/opinions/2014/10/24/d5431af6-59f5-11e4-9d6c-756a229d8b18_story.html |title=The Washington Post Bestseller List |date=October 24, 2014 |website=washingtonpost.com |accessdate=October 24, 2014}}</ref> Poet [[Maya Angelou]], who mentored Asante, described ''Buck'' as "a story of surviving and thriving with passion, compassion, wit, and style."<ref>{{Cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=Q2eMDQAAQBAJ&q=a+story+of+surviving+and+thriving+with+passion%2C+compassion%2C+wit%2C+and+style.&pg=PP4|title=Buck: A Memoir|last=Asante|first=Molefi K.|date=2014|publisher=Spiegel & Grau|isbn=978-0-8129-8362-3|language=en}}</ref>

===Films===
Asante is a [[Sundance Institute]] Feature Film Fellow for the movie adaptation of his memoir ''Buck''. Asante wrote and produced the 2005 documentary ''[[500 Years Later]]'', a documentary about slavery which received the Breaking the Chains Award from [[UNESCO]]. Asante directed and produced ''[[The Black Candle]]'' (2012), a documentary about [[Kwanzaa]], co-written and narrated by [[Maya Angelou]].

===Lectures and essays===
Asante has delivered numerous lectures, including the [[Yale University]] Master's Tea; [[Vanderbilt University]] Walter R. Murray Jr. Lecture; [[Southern Methodist University]] Willis M. Tate Distinguished Lecture; and commencement addresses at [[UCLA]], [[University of Wisconsin]], [[Arizona State University]], [[Vassar College|Vassar]], and [[Harvard University]].

Asante is featured in ''Changing America: 1968 and Beyond'', a permanent exhibit at the Smithsonian [[National Museum of African American History and Culture]].

Asante has written essays on art, Hip Hop, technology, and culture for ''[[USA Today]]'',<ref>[http://usatoday30.usatoday.com/news/opinion/2004-10-25-rap-music_x.htm USATODAY.com – Enough disrespect: Return rap to its artistic roots]. Usatoday30.usatoday.com (October 25, 2004). Retrieved on 2017-06-23.</ref> ''[[Huffington Post]]'',<ref>[https://www.huffingtonpost.com/mk-asante-jr/celebrating-kwanzaa-with_b_390375.html Celebrating Kwanzaa With Maya Angelou (VIDEO) | HuffPost]. Huffingtonpost.com. Retrieved on June 23, 2017.</ref> ''[[San Francisco Chronicle]]'',<ref>{{cite web|last1=Asante|first1=MK|title=We are the post hip-hop generation|url=http://www.sfgate.com/opinion/openforum/article/We-are-the-post-hip-hop-generation-2504977.php|website=SFGate}}</ref> and the ''[[New York Times]]''.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Asante|first1=MK|title='Accidental Racist' and Lyrical Provocation|url=https://www.nytimes.com/roomfordebate/2013/04/10/accidental-racist-and-lyrical-provocation/racism-is-part-of-our-countrys-past-and-present-history-and-we-should-never-forget-it|website=The New York Times}}</ref>

===Music===
Asante is featured on the song "Bangers", along with Halo, from the album ''Indie 500'' by [[Talib Kweli]] and [[9th Wonder]].

Asante is the founder of Wonderful Sound Studios.

===Professor===
At 23, he joined the faculty of [[Morgan State University]]. He received [[tenure]] at 26<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.today.com/video/mk-asante-gets-candid-about-his-rise-struggles-and-gift-for-language-79529541739|title=MK Asante gets candid about his rise, struggles and gift for language|work=Today|date=February 27, 2020}}</ref> and is currently Associate Professor of creative writing and film in the Department of English and Language Arts.<ref name=":0">{{cite web|url=http://media.www.msuspokesman.com/media/storage/paper270/news/2007/10/13/WorldNews/Fear-The.Bear.The.Awakening.Of.A.Once.Powerful.Force-3030043-page2.shtml|title=Fear The Bear: The Awakening Of A Once Powerful Force|last=Gordon|first=Stanley|date=October 13, 2007|website=|url-status=dead|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20110714125723/http://media.www.msuspokesman.com/media/storage/paper270/news/2007/10/13/WorldNews/Fear-The.Bear.The.Awakening.Of.A.Once.Powerful.Force-3030043-page2.shtml|archivedate=July 14, 2011|accessdate=January 10, 2010}}</ref> In 2017, he was appointed to Distinguished Professor-in-Residence at the [[MICA (Institute of Strategic Marketing and Communication)]] in India.<ref>[https://www.mica.ac.in/press-release/mica-s-4th-international-conference-in-january-2017 MICA], 2017.</ref>

He is the recipient of the 2021 Morgan State University Distinguished Achievement Award.

===TV Shows===
Asante is the host and co-executive producer of ''[[While Black with MK Asante]]'', a docuseries produced by [[Snapchat]].<ref>Petski, Denise (April 4, 2019), [https://deadline.com/2019/04/snapchat-sets-10-new-original-series-from-buzzfeed-bunim-murray-new-form-more-1202588637/ "Snapchat Sets 10 New Original Series From Buzzfeed, Bunim/Murray, New Form, More"], ''Deadline Hollywood''.</ref> ''While Black with MK Asante'' takes the stories of America’'s black youth and gives them a platform in the smartphones of millions of America’s teens.<ref>[https://shortyawards.com/12th/while-black-with-mk-asante/ "While Black with MK Asante"], The Shorty Awards.</ref> ''While Black with MK Asante'' has nearly 17 million viewers.<ref>[https://variety.com/2021/digital/news/snap-50-percent-annual-revenue-growth-1234913390/ "Snap Projects 50% Annual Revenue Growth for Next Several Years, Stock Hits an All-Time High February 23, 2021. ]</ref>

Asante co-wrote the broadcast opens for the 2021 [[NBA Finals]] directed by [[Spike Lee]].

===TV Performances===

On November 30, 2020, he performed and debuted "We the Eagles" on [[ESPN]]'s [[NFL]] [[Monday Night Football]] for a live audience of 11.4 million viewers.<ref>[https://espnpressroom.com/us/press-releases/2020/12/espns-monday-night-football-experiences-year-over-year-viewership-gains-again-seahawks-eagles-generates-11-4-million-viewers/#:~:text=ESPN's%20Monday%20Night%20Football%20telecast,viewers%20watched%20the%20Seahawks'%20victory. "ESPN's Monday Night Football Experiences Year-over-Year Viewership Gains Again; Seahawks-Eagles Generates 11.4 Million Viewers]"</ref>

On May 29, 2021, he performed and debuted "Skate or D.I.E." at the 2021 [[Dew Tour]] Skateboarding Olympic qualifier on NBC.
