'''Mark Granier'''  born in [[London]], [[England]], is an [[Irish people|Irish]] poet and photographer based in Dublin, Ireland.<ref name=McAuliffe/>   [[Poetry Ireland Review]] describes Granier as, "a poet of individual poems," poems that are, "perfectly operating verbal machines, which are their own fulfillment, with everything concentrated on the final, sealing line."<ref name="O'Loughlin">{{cite journal|last1=O'Loughlin|first1=Michael|title=Review of Fade Street|journal=Poetry Ireland Review|date=April 2011|issue=103|page=121|jstor=41583378}}</ref>

== Biography ==
Mark Granier was born in [[London]] in 1957. He completed an MA in Poetry/Creative Writing with [[Lancaster University]] and has been teaching creative writing in [[University College Dublin]] for several years.  He lives in [[Dublin]] with his wife and son.

He has published five poetry collections: ''[http://www.salmonpoetry.com/details.php?ID=70&a=56 Airborne]'' ([http://www.salmonpoetry.com/ Salmon Poetry], 2001), ''[http://www.salmonpoetry.com/details.php?ID=56&a=56 The Sky Road]'' (Salmon Poetry, 2007), ''[http://www.isbnsearch.org/isbn/9781844717361 Fade Street]'' ([[Salt Publishing]], 2010), ''[http://www.salmonpoetry.com/details.php?ID=352&a=56 Haunt]'' (Salmon Poetry, 2015), and ''[http://www.salmonpoetry.com/details.php?ID=432&a=56 Ghostlight: New & Selected Poems]'' ([http://www.salmonpoetry.com/ Salmon Poetry], 2017). He received four [[Arts Council of Ireland|Arts Council]] bursaries, in 2002, 2008, 2013 and 2018. His awards include the [http://archive.uninews.unimelb.edu.au/view-48164.html Vincent Buckley Poetry Prize] in 2004 and [[Kavanagh Fellowship|The Patrick and Katherine Kavanagh Poetry Fellowship]] in 2011 and 2016.<ref name=Dempsey>{{cite news|last1=Dempsey|first1=Kate|title=Patrick Kavanagh Fellowship Readings|url=http://www.writing.ie/guest-blogs/patrick-kavanagh-fellowship-readings-2/|accessdate=16 June 2015|publisher=[[Writing.ie]]|date=15 October 2012}}</ref>

[[The Irish Times]] describes Granier's ''Fade Street'', in which the title poem is a reflection on a [[Victorian era]] photograph, as "[[Ekphrasis|ekphrastic]]."<ref name=McAuliffe>{{cite news|last1=McAuliffe|first1=John|title=Searing sketches of a suburban childhood|url=http://www.irishtimes.com/culture/books/searing-sketches-of-a-suburban-childhood-1.583325|accessdate=14 June 2015|publisher=[[The Irish Times]]|date=26 February 2011}}</ref> In its review of ''Fade Street,'' ''Poetry Ireland Review,'' describes Granier as, "well-known for his visual sense."<ref name="O'Loughlin"/>

Mark Granier's photography work includes portraits of some well-known writers and performers such as [[Allen Ginsberg]] and [[Jo Brand]]. Apart from furnishing the cover photography for his four poetry collections, he has done cover work for a number of publishers, including [[Faber & Faber]], [http://www.obrien.ie/ The O’Brien Press], Salmon Poetry, [[Poetry Ireland Review]] and [[The Stinging Fly]]. The literary/photographic journal [[Irish Pages]] published a portfolio of his work in 2011 and his photographs have regularly appeared in [https://www.theguardian.com/lifeandstyle/gallery/2013/feb/15/photography-guardian-readers-pictures-glamour The Guardian Weekend Magazine] and have been exhibited in two group shows in London (in [http://www.oxotower.co.uk/venue/galleryoxo/ The Oxo Gallery] on [[The South Bank]] and The Guardian Offices). In 2012 he was awarded the jury prize in The Open House Photographic competition run by the [http://www.architecturefoundation.ie/Irish Architecture Foundation]. In 2015 his work was selected by Mark St. John Ellis (of [http://www.nagallery.ie/ nag Gallery], Dublin) to appear in an open submission competition/exhibition, ''[http://www.dlrcoco.ie/newsevents/latestnews/title,11477,en.html Home]'', in the Municipal Gallery in [http://libraries.dlrcoco.ie/using-your-library/find-your-library/dlr-lexicon The Lexicon Library] in Dún Laoghaire. He was also awarded runner-up prize in this competition. His work has also been exhibited in the 2015 [http://www.rhagallery.ie/visit/annual-exhibition/ Royal Hibernian Academy annual exhibition] in [[Dublin]].
