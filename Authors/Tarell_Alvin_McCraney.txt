'''Tarell Alvin McCraney''' (born October 17, 1980) is an American playwright, screenwriter, and actor. He is the chair of playwriting at the [[Yale School of Drama]] and a member of the [[Steppenwolf Theatre]] Ensemble.

He co-wrote the 2016 film ''[[Moonlight (2016 film)|Moonlight]]'', based on his own play, for which he received an [[Academy Awards|Academy Award]] for [[Academy Award for Best Adapted Screenplay|Best Adapted Screenplay]]. He also wrote the screenplay for the 2019 film ''[[High Flying Bird]]'' and 2019 television series ''[[David Makes Man]]''.

==Early life and education==
[[File:Elliott Bay Books - author reading 01A.jpg|thumb|A reading at Elliott Bay Books, [[Seattle, Washington]], co-presented with the Seattle Repertory Theatre, in association with Seattle Rep's staging of ''The Breach'', a play based on [[Hurricane Katrina]] and its aftermath. At right, [[New Orleans]] ''[[The Times-Picayune|Times-Picayune]]'' columnist [[Chris Rose (journalist)|Chris Rose]], author of ''1 Dead in Attic''. To his right are Tarell Alvin McCraney, [[Catherine Filloux]], and [[Joe Sutton]], co-authors of ''The Breach''.]]
McCraney was born in Liberty City, Florida. He attended the [[New World School of the Arts]] (NWSA) in [[Miami, Florida]]. While attending NWSA, he also applied to and was awarded an honorable mention by the National [[YoungArts]] Foundation (1999, Theater). As a teenager, he was a member of an improv troupe directed by [[Teo Castellanos|Ted Castellanos]].<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|last=McNulty|first=Charles|date=2014-08-29|title=Rising playwright Tarell Alvin McCraney takes his own, wary path to L.A.|url=https://www.latimes.com/entertainment/arts/la-et-cm-ca-tarell-alvin-mccraney-20140828-column.html|url-status=live|access-date=2021-04-28|website=Los Angeles Times|language=en-US}}</ref>

He matriculated into [[The Theatre School at DePaul University]] and received his BFA in acting. In May 2007 he graduated from [[Yale School of Drama]]'s playwriting program,<ref name=":1">{{Cite web|last=Kalb|first=Peggy|date=2017|title=From Yale, to Yale: Moonlight author joins drama school|url=https://yalealumnimagazine.com/articles/4436-tarell-alvin-mccraney|url-status=live|access-date=2021-04-28|website=Yale Alumni Magazine|language=en}}</ref> receiving the Cole Porter Playwriting Award upon graduation. He also is an Honorary [[Warwick University]] Graduate.

== Career ==
As an actor, he has worked with directors such as [[Tina Landau]] of the Steppenwolf Theatre Company, [[Chicago, Illinois]], [[David Cromer]], and B. J. Jones, artistic director of the Northlight Theatre (where McCraney co-starred in the Chicago premiere of [[Joe Penhall]]'s ''[[Blue/Orange]]''), and developed a working relationship with [[Peter Brook]] and [[Marie-Hélène Estienne]] of the [[Bouffes du Nord]], [[Paris]].{{Citation needed|date=April 2021}} He is a member of the D Projects Theater Company in [[Miami]].<ref name=":0" />

While at Yale, McCraney wrote the [[The Brother/Sister Plays|''Brother/Sister'' trilogy]] of plays, which are set in the Louisiana [[Public housing|projects]] and explore [[Yoruba religion|Yoruba mythology]].<ref name=":0" />

From 2008 to 2010, he was the RSC/[[Warwick University|Warwick]] International Playwright in Residence at the [[Royal Shakespeare Company]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=Tarell Alvin McCraney|url=https://warwick.ac.uk/fac/cross_fac/capital/about/people/playwright/mccraney/|url-status=live|access-date=2021-04-28|website=Warwick: The Capital Centre}}</ref> In April 2010, McCraney became the 43rd member of the [[Steppenwolf Theatre]] Ensemble.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Steppenwolf Theatre Company|date=May 16, 2010|title=Tarell Alvin McCraney - The 43rd Member of Steppenwolf's Ensemble|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6LsdT95exf0|url-status=live|website=[[YouTube]]}}</ref> In July 2017, he became the chair of playwriting at the [[Yale School of Drama]].<ref name=":1" /><ref>{{Cite web|title=Tarell Alvin McCraney|url=https://www.drama.yale.edu/bios/tarell-mccraney-2/|access-date=2021-04-28|website=Yale School of Drama|language=en-US}}</ref>

In 2016, he co-wrote the screenplay for ''[[Moonlight (2016 film)|Moonlight]]'' with [[Barry Jenkins]], based on his own play ''In Moonlight Black Boys Look Blue''. The film was critically acclaimed, and McCraney and Jenkins won the [[Academy Award for Best Adapted Screenplay]]. In 2019, he wrote the screenplay for the film ''[[High Flying Bird]]'', which was directed by [[Steven Soderbergh]] and released by [[Netflix]]. He also wrote the screenplay for the television series ''[[David Makes Man]]'', which aired on the [[Oprah Winfrey Network]].

==== Other plays ====

*''Without/Sin''
* ''Run, Mourner, Run'' (adapted from Randall Kenan's short story), both of which premiered at Yale Cabaret. He directed ''Hamlet'' for the RSC's Young Shakespeare program for GableStage in Miami.

In the summer of 2006, McCraney, Catherine Filloux and Joe Sutton wrote ''The Breach'', a play on Katrina, the Gulf, and our nation, commissioned by [[Southern Rep]] in New Orleans, where it premiered in August 2007 to mark the second anniversary of the tragedy in New Orleans. ''The Breach'' also played at [[Seattle Rep]] in the winter of 2007.
