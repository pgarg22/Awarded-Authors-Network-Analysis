'''Aonghas MacNeacail''' (born 7 June 1942), nickname ''Aonghas dubh'' or ''Black Angus'') is a contemporary writer in the [[Scottish Gaelic]] language.

==Early life==
MacNeacail was born in [[Uig, Snizort|Uig]] on the [[Isle of Skye]] on 7 June 1942.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.scottishpoetrylibrary.org.uk/poetry/poets/aonghas-macneacail |title=Aonghas MacNeacail (b. 1942) |publisher=[[Scottish Poetry Library]] |access-date=6 April 2017}}</ref> He was raised in Idrigil, speaking Gaelic as a child.<ref name="SRB 9Jun2012">{{cite journal |url=https://www.scottishreviewofbooks.org/2012/06/black-angus-at-70/ |title="Black Angus" At 70 |first=Peter |last=Mackay |journal=[[Scottish Review of Books]] |date=9 June 2012 |access-date=6 April 2017}}</ref> He was registered at birth as Angus Nicolson, but later changed his official name to "Aonghas MacNeacail," the Scottish Gaelic version of his name. He attended Uig Primary School and [[Portree High School]], and from 1968 the [[University of Glasgow]]<ref name="SRB 9Jun2012"/> where he was one of a group of young writers who gathered around [[Philip Hobsbaum]] which also included [[James Kelman]], [[Tom Leonard (poet)|Tom Leonard]], [[Alasdair Gray]], [[Liz Lochhead]] and [[Jeff Torrington]].

==Career==
Besides drawing on Gaelic traditions, MacNeacail is influenced by the [[Black Mountain School]] of the US. He has held writing fellowships in Scotland, including residences at the Gaelic college of [[Sabhal Mòr Ostaig]], and reads his work at festivals around the world. He has collaborated with musicians and visual artists, and written drama. His poetry has been widely published throughout the English speaking world in journals such as ''Ploughshares'', ''Poetry Australia'', ''World Poetry Almanac'', and ''JuxtaProse Literary Magazine.'' He has also received wide recognition and critical acclaim for his screenwriting and songwriting.

MacNeacail won the Stakis Prize for Scottish Writer of the Year with his third collection, ''Oideachadh Ceart'' ('A Proper Schooling and other poems'), in 1997. His most recent collection ''Laoidh an Donais òig'' ('hymn to a young demon') was published by Polygon in 2007. His partner is the actor and writer [[Gerda Stevenson]].<ref>[http://www.bbc.co.uk/scotland/alba/foghlam/larachnambard/am_bard/aonghas_macneacail.shtml BBC biography - Làrach nam Bàrd]</ref>
