'''Dan O’Brien''' (born 1974) is an American [[playwright]], [[poet]], [[essayist]], and [[librettist]]. His most prominent works have been the play ''The Body of an American'' and the poetry collection ''War Reporter''. He was awarded a [[Guggenheim Fellowship]] for 2015–16. His most recent play, ''The House in Scarsdale: A Memoir for the Stage,'' is the winner of the 2018 [[PEN America]] Award for Drama.<ref>{{Cite web | url=http://www.latimes.com/books/la-ca-jc-pen-award-winners-20180927-story.html | title=PEN honors SoCal writers with 2018 literary awards| date=2018-09-27}}</ref>

==Career==
O'Brien's plays include ''The Three Christs of Ypsilanti'', ''The Cherry Sisters Revisited'', ''The Voyage of the Carcass'', ''The Dear Boy'', ''The House in Hydesville'', ''Moving Picture'', ''Key West'', ''Am Lit'', ''Lamarck'', ''The Last Supper Restoration'', ''The Angel in the Trees'', ''"Will You Please Shut Up?"'', and ''The Disappearance of Daniel Hand''. His work has been produced by [[Second Stage Theatre]], [[Ensemble Studio Theatre]], The [[Humana Festival of New American Plays]] at [[Actors Theatre of Louisville]], [[Williamstown Theatre Festival]], [[Geva Theatre Center]], Page 73 Productions, The Production Company, SoHo Playhouse, and elsewhere. He has served as a Hodder Fellow at [[Princeton University]], the Djerassi Fellow in Playwriting at the [[University of Wisconsin]] in Madison, and as the Tennessee Williams Fellow in Playwriting at [[The University of the South (Sewanee)]]. He has frequently served on the playwriting faculty at the [[Sewanee Writers' Conference]]. His work has been developed at the National Playwrights Conference at the [[Eugene O'Neill Theatre Center]], The New Harmony Project, and elsewhere. He is the recipient of a [[Rockefeller Foundation]] Bellagio Center Residency. In 1996-97 O'Brien received a [[Thomas J. Watson Fellowship]] for travel and independent study in Ireland and the U.K. Previous awards include the American Theatre Critics Association's [[M. Elizabeth Osborn|M. Elizabeth Osborn Award]] for Best New Play by an Emerging Playwright.

O'Brien's debut poetry collection entitled ''War Reporter'' was published in 2013 by Hanging Loose Press in Brooklyn and CB Editions in London, edited by [[Charles Boyle (poet)|Charles Boyle]]. ''War Reporter'' received the 2013 [http://www.thepoetrytrust.org/news/fenton-first-collection-prize-shortlist-announced/ Fenton Aldeburgh First Collection Prize],<ref>{{Cite web | url=http://www.thepoetrytrust.org/news/fenton-aldeburgh-first-collection-prize-2013-winner-announced/ |title = Mass Media Essay Examples &#124; Free Book Summary}}</ref> and was shortlisted for the 2013 [[Forward Prizes for Poetry|Forward Prize for Best First Collection]].<ref>{{cite news| url=https://www.theguardian.com/books/2013/jul/08/forward-poetry-prize-shortlists-2013?_r=0 | location=London | work=The Guardian | first=Liz | last=Bury | title=Forward poetry prizes highlight 'powerful year for poetry' | date=8 July 2013}}</ref> O'Brien's second collection of poetry, ''Scarsdale,'' was published in 2014 by CB Editions in London and in 2015 by Measure Press in the US. O'Brien was writer-in-residence at the [[James Merrill House]] in [[Stonington, Connecticut]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.jamesmerrillhouse.org/writers_in_residence.htm|title=The James Merrill House - Museum & Writer-in-Residence Program|website=www.jamesmerrillhouse.org|access-date=2013-07-06|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20091006062632/http://www.jamesmerrillhouse.org/writers_in_residence.htm|archive-date=2009-10-06|url-status=dead}}</ref> ''New Life'', O'Brien's third poetry collection, was published in 2016 by CB Editions in London and in 2017 by Hanging Loose Press in the US. Barbara Berman writes of ''New Life'' in The Rumpus that "an original voice speaks, on a plane with earlier masters."<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://therumpus.net/2016/01/new-life-by-dan-obrien/|title=New Life by Dan O'Brien|date=2016-01-29|work=The Rumpus.net|access-date=2018-08-31|language=en-US}}</ref> In 2014 O'Brien was the recipient of the Troubadour International Poetry Prize.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.coffeehousepoetry.org/poems/troubadour-poetry-prize-2014|title=Troubadour Poetry Prize 2014|date=2014-12-01|work=coffeehousepoetry.org|access-date=2018-09-13|language=en-US}}</ref>

[[Patrick McGuinness|Patrick McGuiness]] writes in ''[[The Guardian]]'' that ''War Reporter'' is "a masterpiece of truthfulness and feeling, and a completely sui generis addition not just to writing about war but to contemporary poetry.”<ref>{{cite news|last=McGuiness|first=Patrick|title=War Reporter by Dan O'Brien – review|url=https://www.theguardian.com/books/2013/nov/15/war-reporter-dan-obrien-review|access-date=21 November 2013|newspaper=Guardian|date=15 November 2013}}</ref> ''War Reporter'' was a staff pick for best books of 2013 at [[Slate.com]], where [[William J. Dobson]] hails it as an "incredible achievement. Anyone who cares about how we go to war—and how we return—must read it."<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.slate.com/articles/arts/books/2013/11/slate_staff_picks_for_best_books_of_2013.html|title=Best Books 2013: Slate Staff Picks|first=Slate|last=Staff|date=30 November 2013|publisher=|via=Slate}}</ref>

O'Brien was a 2015-16 Guggenheim Fellow.<ref>{{cite web|title=Fellowship Awards In The United States And Canada, 2015|url=http://www.gf.org/news/fellows-news/fellowship-awards-in-the-united-states-and-canada-2015/|website=John Simon Guggenheim Foundation|access-date=9 April 2015}}</ref>

O'Brien wrote the libretti for two one-act operas by composer [[Jonathan Berger]]. ''Theotokia'' and ''The War Reporter'', titled jointly as ''Visitations'', was commissioned by Stanford Live, the [[National Endowment for the Arts]], and the [[Mellon Foundation]], and premiered at the [[Bing Concert Hall]] at [[Stanford University]] in April 2013, directed by Rinde Eckert and performed by [[New York Polyphony]] and Mellissa Hughes.<ref>{{cite news| url=https://www.nytimes.com/2013/04/16/arts/music/jonathan-bergers-visitations-at-stanford.html?_r=0 | work=The New York Times | title=Jonathan Berger's 'Visitations,' at Stanford | date=15 April 2013}}</ref> This production of ''Visitations'' received a New York City premiere at the 2014 [[Prototype Festival]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://prototypefestival.org/show/visitations-theotokia-and-the-war-reporter/|title=Prototype|website=prototypefestival.org}}</ref> 
 
O'Brien's plays and poetry and prose have appeared internationally in newspapers, magazines, and literary journals including the [[Paris Review]], [[Times Literary Supplement]], [[The American Scholar (magazine)]], [[Sewanee Review]], the [[New England Review]], the [[Hopkins Review]], the London [[Sunday Times]], [[Yale Review]], [[Geist (magazine)|Geist]], [[Southwest Review]], [[Missouri Review]], [[32 poems]], [[Poetry Review]], [[Poetry Ireland Review]], [[The White Review]], Magma Poetry, Cyphers, The Moth, [[ZYZZYVA]], [[St. Petersburg Review]], [[Malahat Review]], [[Grain (magazine)|Grain]], Event, St. Ann's Review, 5 AM, 10TAL, [[StoryQuarterly]], [[Alaska Quarterly Review]], [[The Pinch]], [[Greensboro Review]], [[Crab Orchard Review]], [[Bellevue Literary Review]], Birmingham Poetry Review, and many others.

==Personal life==
Originally from [[Scarsdale, New York]], O'Brien currently lives in [[Los Angeles]] with his wife, actress, writer, comedian, and producer [[Jessica St. Clair]]. Their daughter, Isobel Kelly O'Brien, was born on October 1, 2013. He is a summa cum laude graduate of [[Middlebury College]], and received a master's degree in Creative Writing from [[Brown University]], graduating with high honors.<ref>http://www.danobrien.org/biography/{{Dead link|date=November 2019 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref>
