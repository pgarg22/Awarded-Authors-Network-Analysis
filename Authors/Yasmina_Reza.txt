'''Yasmina Reza''' (born 1 May 1959)<ref>http://catalogue.bnf.fr/ark:/12148/cb409560057/PUBLIC [archive] from the general catalogue of the BnF (Bibliothèque nationale de France).</ref> is a French playwright, actress, novelist and screenwriter best known for her plays ''[[Art (play)|'Art']]'' and ''[[God of Carnage]]''. Many of her brief satiric plays have reflected on contemporary middle-class issues.

==Life and career==
Reza's father was a Russian-born<ref>[https://www.independent.co.uk/arts-entertainment/theatre-dance/features/yasmina-reza-please-stop-laughing-at-me-795570.html Yasmina Reza: 'Please stop laughing at me']</ref><ref>[http://arlindo-correia.com/040203.html Yasmina Reza: Biography]</ref><ref>[https://www.theguardian.com/books/2000/sep/03/biography.features Papa knew best]</ref> [[Bukharan Jews|Bukharan Jewish]]<ref>[https://www.lexpress.fr/informations/yasmina-reza-ecrivain-d-art_627339.html Yasmina Reza, écrivain d' "Art"]: ''De son père, juif séfarade, mi-russe, mi-iranien, dont le grand-père jouait aux échecs dans les caravansérails de Samarkand''.</ref><ref>[https://web.archive.org/web/20160226164809/http://www.telegraph.co.uk/culture/4713302/The-art-of-a-second-success.html The art of a second success]</ref><ref name=":0">[https://www.timesofisrael.com/the-tears-and-laughter-of-yasmina-rezas-lost-babylon/ The tears and laughter of Yasmina Reza’s lost Babylon]</ref> engineer, businessman, and pianist and her mother was a Jewish Hungarian violinist from Budapest.<ref>{{cite book  | last =Pigeat  | first =Aurélien  | title ='Art', 1994: Yasmina Reza  | publisher =Hatier  | language=fr  | year =2005  | location =Paris  | isbn =2-218-75089-9}}</ref><ref>[http://www.arlindo-correia.org/040203.html The fragility and solitude of man]</ref><ref name=":1">{{Cite 2009|url=https://www.clevelandjewishnews.com/archives/jews-on-stage-broadway-bound/article_dc61b951-dfca-5945-8431-0d9a3aeadce9.html}}</ref> During the Nazi occupation, her father was deported from [[Nice]] to [[Drancy internment camp]].<ref>[https://www.haaretz.com/.premium-from-art-to-life-chatting-with-yasmina-reza-1.5275521 Between Sarkozy and Sarcasm: Playwright Yasmina Reza on What Makes a Person Powerful]</ref> At the beginning of her career, Reza acted in several new plays as well as in plays by [[Molière]] and [[Pierre de Marivaux|Marivaux]].

In 1987, she wrote ''Conversations after a Burial'', which won the [[Molière Award]], the French equivalent of the [[Tony Award]], for Best Author. The North American production premiered in February 2013 at Players by the Sea in Jacksonville Beach Florida. Holly Gutshall and Joe Schwarz directed; with Set Design by Anne Roberts. The cast for this US debut was Kevin Bodge, Paul Carelli, Karen Overstreet, Dave Gowan, Holly Gutshall and Olivia Gowan Snell. Reza translated [[Roman Polanski]]'s stage version of Kafka's ''[[The Metamorphosis|Metamorphosis]]'' in the late 1980s.<ref>{{cite news | url=https://www.theguardian.com/stage/2012/jan/22/yasmina-reza-interview-carnage-polanski | title=Yasmina Reza: 'There's no point in writing theatre if it's not accessible' | date=22 January 2012  | first=Elizabeth | last=Day | work=The Guardian | access-date=11 March 2016 }}</ref>
Her second play, ''Winter Crossing'', won the 1990 Molière Award for Best Fringe Production, and her next play, ''The Unexpected Man'', enjoyed successful productions in England, France, Scandinavia, Germany and New York.

In 1994, ''[[Art (play)|'Art']]'' premiered in Paris and went on to win the Molière Award for Best Author. Since then it has been produced internationally and translated and performed in over 30 languages.<ref name=":0" /> The London production, produced by [[David Pugh and Dafydd Rogers]], received the 1996–97 [[Laurence Olivier Award]] and [[Evening Standard Awards|''Evening Standard'' Award]], the former is the British equivalent of the Tony's. It also won the Tony Award for Best Play. {{nowrap|''Life X 3''}} has also been produced in Europe, North America and Australia.  Screenwriting credits include ''See You Tomorrow'', starring [[Jeanne Moreau]] and directed by Reza's then-partner Didier Martiny.

In September 1997, her first novel, ''Hammerklavier'', was published and another work of fiction, ''Une Désolation'', was published in 2001. Her 2007 work ''L'Aube le Soir ou la Nuit'' (''Dawn Evening or Night''), written after a year of following the campaign of [[Nicolas Sarkozy]] caused a sensation in France.<ref>{{cite news|first=Elaine|last=Sciolino|title=Portrait of President, Craving Power, Enthralls France|newspaper=[[The New York Times]]|date=24 August 2007|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2007/08/24/world/europe/24france.html}}</ref>

On 24 November 2007, her play ''Le Dieu du Carnage'' (''[[God of Carnage]]''), directed by [[Jürgen Gosch]] and performed first in [[Zürich]], received the Viennese [[Nestroy Theatre Prize]] for the best German-language performance of the season.<ref name=":1" /> It opened in London in March 2008, directed by [[Matthew Warchus]] in a translation by [[Christopher Hampton]] starring [[Ralph Fiennes]], [[Tamsin Greig]], [[Janet McTeer]] and [[Ken Stott]].<ref>{{cite web|last=Paddock|first=Terri|url=http://www.whatsonstage.com/index.php?pg=207&story=E8821198247213&title=Greig%2C+McTeer+%26+Stott+Join+Fiennes+God+of+Carnage|title=Greig, McTeer & Stott Join Fiennes God of Carnage|publisher=What's on Stage|date=24 December 2007|access-date=24 December 2007|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20071225131445/http://www.whatsonstage.com/index.php?pg=207&story=E8821198247213&title=Greig%2C+McTeer+%26+Stott+Join+Fiennes+God+of+Carnage|archive-date=25 December 2007}}</ref> It was produced once again by [[David Pugh and Dafydd Rogers]]. The London production won the [[Laurence Olivier Award for Best New Comedy]], which Hampton accepted on her behalf. Hampton told the audience that Reza would be thrilled by the win.<ref>{{cite web|author=Staff|date=8 March 2009|url=http://www.whatsonstage.com/index.php?pg=207&story=E8821236545161&title=Speeches%3A+And+the+Laurence+Olivier+Winners+Said|title=Speeches: And the Laurence Olivier Winners Said|publisher=WhatsonStage.com|access-date=8 March 2009|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090310130037/http://www.whatsonstage.com/index.php?pg=207&story=E8821236545161&title=Speeches%3A+And+the+Laurence+Olivier+Winners+Said|archive-date=10 March 2009}}</ref> The play premiered on Broadway with an opening night cast of [[James Gandolfini]], [[Jeff Daniels]], [[Marcia Gay Harden]], and [[Hope Davis]]. ''God of Carnage'' won Best Play at the 2009 Tony Awards.

In collaboration with Polanski, Reza wrote the screenplay adaptation of ''God of Carnage'' for the 2011 Polanski film [[Carnage (2011 film)|''Carnage'']]. She was nominated at the [[European Film Awards]] and won a [[César Award]] for her screenwriting and the film won a Little Gold Lion at the [[68th Venice International Film Festival]].

Her 2016 novel, ''Babylone'', received the [[Prix Renaudot]]. The English version, translated by Linda Asher, was published by [[Seven Stories Press]] in 2018.
