'''Graeme K Talboys''' is an English writer and teacher.

==Life==

Graeme Keith Talboys was born at [[Queen Charlotte's and Chelsea Hospital]] in [[Hammersmith]] on Thursday 26 November 1953.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.freebmd.org.uk/cgi/information.pl?cite=bl1JTXaN5aBmlBeeitrJMQ&scan=1|title=FreeBMD Entry Info|website=www.freebmd.org.uk|access-date=2016-04-04}}</ref>

==Writing==

He is represented by Leslie Gardner of Artellus.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.artellusltd.co.uk/authors/ |title=Authors |publisher=Artellus |accessdate=4 April 2016}}</ref>

==Reception==

Talboys was nominated for ''[[The Guardian]]''<!---->'s "Not the Booker Prize 2011."<ref>{{cite web|title=Not the Booker prize 2011 – the nominees|url=https://www.theguardian.com/books/2011/aug/10/not-booker-prize-2011-nominations|publisher=[[The Guardian]]|accessdate=3 April 2016|date=10 August 2011}}</ref>

In 2014, he was among 15 "mostly unagented writers" signed by HarperVoyager, the novels including his ''Exile and Pilgrim'' to be released digitally, followed by short-run paperback editions.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Farrington|first1=Joshua|title=HarperVoyager signs 15 in open submission fortnight|url=http://www.thebookseller.com/news/harpervoyager-signs-15-open-submission-fortnight|publisher=The Bookseller|accessdate=3 April 2016}}</ref>
