'''Tess Berry-Hart''' is a British playwright and novelist writing for adults, young adults and children.<ref>http://www.scholastic.co.uk/zone/authors_t-berry-hart_biog.htm</ref> Novels and theatre plays deal with themes such as the [[European migrant crisis]], [[LGBT]] rights, [[mental illness]], [[genetic engineering]], and the [[sex-positive movement]].  Berry-Hart has also written fiction and [[verbatim theatre]] pieces for stage to support human rights campaigns <ref>{{Cite web | url=https://www.ted.com/tedx/events/15510/ |title = TEDxSouthamptonUniversity &#124; TED}}</ref> and to raise funds for the refugee crisis.<ref>{{Cite web | url=http://exeuntmagazine.com/features/responsibilities-of-representation/ |title = Responsibilities of Representation}}</ref>

==Early life==
Tess Berry-Hart's father is painter and sculptor David Berry-Hart. Their grandmother, Alice Berry-Hart, was a journalist and author of children's books and aunt [[Marian Lines]] was a playwright and children's author. Great-grandfather [[David Berry Hart]] was a noted gynaecologist and one of the first male practitioners in the UK.

Berry-Hart was born in [[Warwickshire]], brought up in [[Oswestry]] on the [[Welsh Marches]], and from age 11 to 18, won a music scholarship to study at [[Howell's School, Denbigh]] in North Wales. After school, they spent a year travelling and working in Turkey and Pakistan's North West Frontier as a teacher of English. Berry-Hart graduated magna cum laude with a first-class degree in law from [[King's College London]].<ref>http://www5.scholastic.co.uk/zone/authors_t-berry-hart_biog.htm</ref>

==Career==
Berry-Hart studied at the [[Royal Court Theatre]] Young Writers’ Programme in [[London]] under the tutorship of [[Hanif Kureishi]] and [[Simon Stephens]]. Berry-Hart was selected to be the Royal Court young writing representative at the Interplay Young European Playwrights Festival in Warsaw, Poland, with their first play ''Legoland'' which dealt with post-traumatic stress and mental illness.

Berry-Hart's theatre work has been subsequently produced in London, New York, Los Angeles, Edinburgh, Cardiff and Ankara, and translated into German and Turkish. In 2019 Berry-Hart was shortlisted for the BBC Wales Writer in Residence Award in partnership with National Theatre Wales.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/blogs/writersroom/entries/2ac113c9-490b-49d6-a8fb-c83b4e9d51ee|title=Wales Writer in Residence - Shortlist Announced|date=18 September 2019}}</ref>

===Theatre===
In July 2016, Berry-Hart's play CARGO (inspired by experiences encountering child refugees in Calais) set in a cargo container following unaccompanied minors travelling to Europe, was produced by the [[Arcola Theatre]] in London.<ref>{{Cite web | url=https://www.standard.co.uk/goingout/theatre/refugee-play-cargo-puts-its-audience-in-mockedup-cargo-container-a3241726.html |title = Feel what it's like to be a refugee in a cargo container in new play|date = 6 May 2016}}</ref>

The Turkish translation premiere of Cargo ("Kargo") was produced and toured by the [[Turkish State Theatres]] in November 2018.<ref>{{Cite web | url=http://www.devtiyatro.gov.tr/programlar-sehirler-sivas-detay-bolum_sahneler-kargo.html/ | title=T.C. Kültür ve Turizm Bakanlığı Devlet Tiyatroları}}</ref>

In 2018, Welsh theatre The Other Room, [[Cardiff]] commissioned Berry-Hart to write a play for its 2019 Violence Series<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.otherroomtheatre.com/en/whats-on/seasons/lovesick-21/the-story/|title=The Story}}</ref> inspired by their experiences as a volunteer during the refugee crisis.<ref>http://getthechance.wales/2019/10/01/an-interview-with-writer-tess-berry-hart%EF%BB%BF</ref> "The Story" premiered in 2019 and subsequently toured Wales and London in Jan-Feb 2020. In October 2020 the Violence Series trilogy won an LPT 2020 theatre award for Best Transfer.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.londonpubtheatres.com/london-pub-theatres-awards|title=London Pub Theatre Awards 2020}}</ref> "The Story" is published by [[Oberon Books]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.bloomsbury.com/uk/the-story-9781786829344/|title=The Story}}</ref>

===Verbatim theatre commissions===
In 2012, Berry-Hart was commissioned to create a verbatim theatre piece, "Someone To Blame"<ref>{{Cite web | url=http://hackneycitizen.co.uk/2011/03/16/sam-hallam-campaigners-make-drama-of-hoxton-murder-case/ |title = Sam Hallam campaigners make drama of Hoxton murder case|date = 16 March 2011}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web | url=http://hackneycitizen.co.uk/2012/03/08/someone-to-blame-sam-hallam-play/ | title=Someone to Blame: Courtroom drama asks tough questions| date=8 March 2012}}</ref> based on the real-life case of [[Sam Hallam]],<ref>{{Cite news | url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/news/uknews/crime/9269413/Sam-Hallam-freed-from-murder-sentence-after-seven-years-as-court-hears-of-serious-miscarriage-of-justice.html |title = Sam Hallam freed from murder sentence after seven years as court hears of 'serious miscarriage of justice'|date = 16 May 2012|last1 = Evans|first1 = Martin}}</ref> a teenager wrongfully convicted of murder and imprisoned for 7 years.<ref>{{Cite news | url=https://www.theguardian.com/uk/2012/may/16/sam-hallam-released-seven-years/ | title=Sam Hallam released after seven years in prison| newspaper=The Guardian| date=17 May 2012| last1=Laville| first1=Sandra}}</ref> The play highlighted the inaccuracies in the case against Hallam and was performed by the [[King's Head Theatre]] in the weeks before his second appeal. Hallam was released after a second three-hour hearing at the Court of Appeal.<ref>[https://web.archive.org/web/20120516170822/http://www.telegraph.co.uk/news/uknews/crime/9269413/Sam-Hallam-freed-from-murder-sentence-after-seven-years-as-court-hears-of-serious-miscarriage-of-justice.html The Daily Telegraph]</ref>

An updated gala performance of Someone To Blame was staged at the King's Head after Hallam's release.<ref>{{Cite web | url=https://www.entertainment-focus.com/theatre-review/someone-to-blame-review/ | title=Someone to Blame review| date=4 June 2012}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web | url=http://www.hackneycitizen.co.uk/2012/06/01/someone-to-blame-sam-hallam-drama-returns/ |title = Someone to Blame: Sam Hallam courtroom drama returns to the stage|date = June 2012}}</ref>

In 2014, Berry-Hart was further commissioned by the [[King's Head Theatre]] in London to write a play about the lives of LGBT Russians following the passing of the anti-gay laws in Russia ahead of the Winter Olympics.<ref>{{Cite web | url=https://www.independent.co.uk/arts-entertainment/theatre-dance/news/gay-russia-finds-a-voice-in-london-play-8793026.html | title=Gay Russia finds a voice in London play| website=[[Independent.co.uk]]| date=September 2013}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news | url=https://www.theguardian.com/stage/2013/aug/13/london-theatre-russia-gay-kings-head |title = London theatre to stage protest play against Russia's anti-gay legislation|newspaper = The Guardian|date = 13 August 2013|last1 = Trueman|first1 = Matt}}</ref>

"Sochi 2014" was performed at the King's Head's new writing venue, The Hope Theatre London, in 2014, with other benefit performances occurring in New York (performed by [[Uzo Aduba]], [[Masha Gessen]] and [[Catherine Curtin]]) as well as Los Angeles that same year.<ref>https://www.theguardian.com/stage/2014/feb/09/sochi-2014-theatre-review}</ref>

An updated version of Sochi 2014 was produced and won an NSDF commendation award at the Edinburgh Festival, during August 2014.<ref>{{Cite web | url=http://www.nsdf.org.uk/sochi-2014-by-tess-berry-hart/ |title = Error &#124; National Student Drama Festival}}</ref>

In 2019, Berry-Hart was commissioned to curate the testimonies of women workers in the developing world to produce a short play for [[International Women's Day]] (#March4Women Rally) hosted by CARE International at [[Central Hall Westminster]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.eveningexpress.co.uk/lifestyle/entertainment/helena-bonham-carter-joins-rally-for-gender-equality/|title = Helena Bonham Carter joins rally for gender equality|date = 26 February 2019}}</ref> The play was performed live by [[Helena Bonham Carter]], [[Meera Syal]], [[Nicola Thorp]], [[Naana Agyei-Ampadu]] and [[Nina Sosanya]], directed by Berry-Hart.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.scoopnest.com/user/StylistMagazine/1102222956225286149-powerful-testimonies-from-women-in-bangladesh-cambodia-ecuador-and-nepal-are-now-being-read-out-on-s|title = Powerful testimonies women Bangladesh Cambodia Ecuador Nepal read stage Naana Agyei Ampadu Nina Sosanya Nicola Thorp Sue Vincent Helena Bonham Carter testimonies arranged edited Tess Berry Hart March Women}}</ref>

The next year, for IWD 2020, Berry-Hart produced and directed another verbatim play concerning the testimonies of refugees of climate change which was produced at [[The Royal Festival Hall]] and performed by [[Natalie Dormer]], [[Nicola Coughlan]], [[Sanjeev Bhaskar]] and [[Himesh Patel]].<ref>https://www.voice-online.co.uk/news/uk-news/2020/03/07/london-mayor-sadiq-khan-to-speak-at-march4women%E2%80%AF/</ref>

===Young adult novels===
In 2007, Berry-Hart's first novel, [[Escape from Genopolis]], was published by [[Scholastic Corporation]]. This was followed by the sequel, Fearless, in 2009.<ref>{{Cite web | url=http://www.convilleandwalsh.com/index.php/authors/author/tess-berry-hart/ |title = C&W Agency}}</ref>

===Refugee crisis===
In September 2015, during the [[European migrant crisis]], Berry-Hart visited the [[Calais Jungle]] refugee camp in France<ref>{{Cite web | url=http://exeuntmagazine.com/features/inside-the-calais-jungle/ | title=Inside the Calais Jungle}}</ref> and upon returning became aid and advocacy co-ordinator for [[Calais Action]], part of the UK [[Grassroots]] Aid movement, sending aid to Calais. Athens, and the islands of Samos, Chios, and Lesvos.

In 2016 Berry-Hart produced a gala weekend of new writing at the Southwark Playhouse featuring short pieces of refugee-related writing by Timberlake Wertenbaker, Anders Lustgarten, Barney Norris, Mediah Ahmed, as well as a collection of Syrian refugee poetry performed by [[Denise Gough]] and [[Russell Tovey]]. The event was compered by comedians [[Robin Ince]] and [[Adam Riches]].<ref>{{Cite web | url=http://southwarkplayhouse.co.uk/uncategorized/refugees-welcome/ | title=Refugees Welcome| date=20 February 2016}}</ref>

Berry-Hart has spoken at the [[United Nations High Commissioner for Refugees]] in Geneva and [[ALNAP]] in [[Stockholm]]<ref>{{Cite web | url=https://www.alnap.org/about/meet-our-team/tess-berry-hart/ |title = Tess Berry-Hart &#124; ALNAP}}</ref>  from a volunteer perspective, and appeared as commentator on refugee issues on the BBC, ITV, and various documentaries<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.huffingtonpost.co.uk/jamie-bell/calais-jungle_b_17475556.html|title=Eight Months On, A Reflection on the Calais Jungle|date=13 July 2017}}</ref> and campaigns for the rights of refugees and the need for safe and legal routes to asylum.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.independent.co.uk/news/world/europe/toddlers-babies-northern-france-sleeping-rough-calais-migrants-europe-eu-borders-a7824816.html|title=Refugee babies and toddlers are sleeping rough in France|website=[[Independent.co.uk]]|date=6 July 2017}}</ref>

Berry-Hart is also a founding member of the Citizens of the World Choir for refugees, migrants and friends.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://refugeeweek.org.uk/refugee-choir-start-summer-of-performances/|title = Refugee Choir Start Summer of Performances|date = 21 June 2017}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.platforma.org.uk/citizens-of-the-world-choir/|title=Citizens of the World Choir &#124; Platforma}}</ref>
