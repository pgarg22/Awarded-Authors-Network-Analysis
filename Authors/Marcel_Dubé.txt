'''Marcel Dubé''' {{post-nominals|country=CAN|OC|OQ}} (January 3, 1930 – April 7, 2016) was a Canadian playwright. <!-- Missing image removed: [[Image:dubem.jpg|right|Marcel Dubé (c. 1963)|150px]]  -->He produced over 300 works for radio, television, and stage. During his career he promoted the preservation and sanctity of the French language in [[Quebec]].
__TOC__

==Early life and education==
Dubé studied at [[Collège Sainte-Marie de Montréal|Collège Sainte-Marie]] where he first became interested in theatre, frequenting the school's auditorium, the historic Salle du Gésu. He attended Westminster School during his high school years.

==Career==
Dubé began writing plays as a young man, including ''Le Barrage'' which was staged by Theatre-club in 1955.<ref name="Nardocchio1986">Elaine Frances Nardocchio. ''[https://books.google.com/books?id=AOzcVm1n-48C&pg=PA31 Theatre and Politics in Modern Québec]''. University of Alberta; 1986. {{ISBN|978-0-88864-088-8}}. p. 31–.</ref> He was soon able to earn his living as a writer. He founded the group Jeune Scène, and at the [[Dominion Drama Festival]] in 1953 won several awards with his play, ''De l'autre côté du mur'' which later became ''Zone''.<ref>[http://ici.radio-canada.ca/nouvelle/1079533/marcel-dube-zone-dramaturgie-television-montreal-archives "Marcel Dubé, porte-parole de la vie ordinaire"]. ''Radio Canada'', 22 January 2018</ref> The play is still being performed sixty years later.<ref>[https://nowtoronto.com/stage/theatre/zone/ "Zone - 50s Quebec play about a street gang has an old-fashioned feel but a contemporary emotional hook"]. ''NOW'', by Jon Kaplan, February 10, 2012</ref> He was also considered a founder of contemporary Quebec dramaturgy.

Over the next five years [[Canadian Broadcasting Corporation|Radio-Canada]] presented, on radio and television, over 30 of his works (many of which he later adapted to the stage). He has produced over 300 works for radio, television and the stage.<ref>Sue Montgomery. [https://www.theglobeandmail.com/arts/theatre-and-performance/playwright-marcel-dube-captivated-quebeckers/article29767219/ "Playwright Marcel Dubé captivated Quebeckers"]. ''The Globe and Mail'', April 26, 2016</ref> He wrote mainly in French; some of his works have been translated or subtitled in English.<ref name="Miller2011">Mary Jane Miller. ''[https://books.google.com/books?id=_u49OJWr40UC&pg=PA199 Turn Up the Contrast: CBC Television Drama since 1952]''. UBC Press; 1 November 2011. {{ISBN|978-0-7748-4321-8}}. p. 199–.</ref>

His concerns for the preservation and sanctity of the French language in Quebec and around the world led him to participate in several organizations: He was first secretary, then president, of the [[Conseil de la langue française]],<ref>[https://www.theglobeandmail.com/arts/theatre-and-performance/quebec-playwright-marcel-dube-dead-at-86/article29568099/ "Quebec playwright Marcel Dube dead at 86"]. Antoine D'Esilets, ''The Canadian Press'',  April 8, 2016 in ''The Globe and Mail''.</ref> president of the [[Rencontres francophones du Québec]] and cofounder and director of the [[Sécretariat permanent des peuples francophones]].

In February 2001, he was named an Officer of the [[Order of Canada]].<ref>[http://www.canadiantheatre.com/dict.pl?term=Marcel%20Dub%E9 Canadian Theatre Encyclopedia<!-- Bot generated title -->]</ref> and in 1993 became an Officer of the [[Order of Quebec]].
