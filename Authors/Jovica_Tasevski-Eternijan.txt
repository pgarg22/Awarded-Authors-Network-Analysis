'''Jovica Tasevski-Eternijan''' ([[Macedonian language|Macedonian]]: '''Јовица Тасевски - Етернијан''', pronounced {{IPA-mk|jovitsa tasevski eternijan|}}; born 25 July 1976, Skopje) is a [[North Macedonia|Macedonian]] [[poetry|poet]], [[essay]]ist and [[literary critic]].

He won ''The Enchanting Poet Award'' for excellent contribution in poetry writing, given by [[The Enchanting Verses|The Enchanting Verses Literary Review]].<ref>[http://www.theenchantingverses.org/2010.html The Enchanting Poet Award Winners for 2010]</ref> <ref>[http://www.culture.in.mk/story.asp?id=31732&rub=56 Culture in MK, 16. 04. 2010] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120813091252/http://www.culture.in.mk/story.asp?id=31732&rub=56 |date=2012-08-13 }}</ref> His books were shortlisted twice for the most prestigious Macedonian poetry prize ''Miladinov Brothers'' and once for ''Dimitar Mitrev'' award given by the Macedonian Writers' Association for book of literary criticism and essays.<ref>[http://www.culture.in.mk/story.asp?id=17819&rub=58 Culture in MK, 26. 01. 2007] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110727035552/http://www.culture.in.mk/story.asp?id=17819&rub=58 |date=2011-07-27 }}</ref>

His poetry is published in a number of anthologies in Macedonia and abroad, it has appeared in many literary magazines and has been translated into several languages.<ref>[http://www.othervoicespoetry.org/vol43/tasevski/index.html The Other Voices International Project: Six poems of Jovica Tasevski-Eternijan translated into English] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110721073004/http://www.othervoicespoetry.org/vol43/tasevski/index.html |date=2011-07-21 }}</ref>

He is a member of the Editorial Board of the Macedonian literary magazine ''Stremež'' and a consultant for Macedonian poetry in ''The Other Voices International Project'', an anthology of world poetry endorsed by UNESCO.<ref>[https://web.archive.org/web/20120323051234/http://www.mia.com.mk/default.aspx?vId=69378807&lId=2&pmId=505 MIA, 26. 11. 2009]</ref><ref>[http://more.poetrysociety.org.uk/landmark/display.php?id=2122 Poetry Landmarks: The Other Voices International Project]</ref>

He graduated from the [[Comparative Literature]] Department, Faculty of Philology in [[Skopje]]. 

Works in National and University Library "St. Clement of Ohrid" (Macedonian: "Свети Климент Охридски").<ref>[http://www.culture.in.mk/story.asp?id=16659 Culture in MK, 03. 11. 2006] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110727035610/http://www.culture.in.mk/story.asp?id=16659 |date=2011-07-27 }}</ref>

He is a member of the Macedonian Writers' Association, The Poetry Society<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.poetrysociety.org.uk/content/membership/profiles/profiles/profilewording2#jtasevski |title=The Poetry Society: Profile of Jovica Tasevski-Eternijan |access-date=2009-07-17 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090926033549/http://www.poetrysociety.org.uk/content/membership/profiles/profiles/profilewording2#jtasevski |archive-date=2009-09-26 |url-status=dead }}</ref> and of the World Poets Society.

==Other literary works==
With [[Maja Apostoloska]] he selected and edited an anthology of Macedonian poetry with religious, biblical and apocryphal motifs, published as a special issue of magazine "Stremež".
Also he is compiler of anthology of youth Macedonian poetry, entitled "Unidentified Celestial Bosom" (2001).<ref>[http://www.culture.in.mk/story.asp?id=1346 Culture in MK, 26. 11. 2001] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110727035645/http://www.culture.in.mk/story.asp?id=1346 |date=2011-07-27 }}</ref>
