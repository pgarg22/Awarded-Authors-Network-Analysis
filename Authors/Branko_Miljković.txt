'''Branko Miljković''' ([[Serbian Cyrillic alphabet|Serbian Cyrillic]]: Бранко Миљковић; 29 January 1934 – 12 February 1961) was a Serbian poet.<ref name="Kako je stradao Branko Miljković">{{cite web |url=http://www.politika.rs/scc/clanak/166819/Kako-je-stradao-Branko-Miljkovic |author=Politika |author-link=Politika |title=Kako je stradao Branko Miljković |access-date=2017-07-24 |date=2011-10-02 |language=sr}}</ref>

==Biography==
Miljković was born in [[Niš]] to a [[Serbs|Serb]] father Gligorije Miljković, who hails from [[Gadžin Han]], and a [[Croats|Croat]] mother Marija Brailo, who hails from Trbounje near [[Drniš]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Branko Miljković – biografija |url=https://www.prelepapoezija.com/branko-miljkovic-biografija/ |website=Najlepša poezija – najbolji pesnici |access-date=8 August 2021 |language=sr-RS |date=27 November 2013}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |title=Branko Miljković: “Jedan nesretan čovjek ne može biti pjesnik” |url=https://www.bljesak.info/kultura/knjige/branko-miljkovic-jedan-nesretan-covjek-ne-moze-biti-pjesnik/337214 |website=Bljesak |access-date=8 August 2021 |language=hr-BA |date=29 January 2021}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |last1=Plavšić |first1=Goran |title=Princ poezije na prokletom tlu |url=https://arhiva.portalnovosti.com/2011/02/princ-poezije-na-prokletom-tlu/ |website=[[Novosti (Croatia)|Novosti]] |access-date=8 August 2021 |language=hr |date=5 February 2011}}</ref>

He was best known throughout [[Socialist Federal Republic of Yugoslavia|Yugoslavia]], the [[Soviet Union]] and other countries of the [[Eastern Bloc]] for his influential writings. At a time when no one could have foreseen anything but a bright future for the poet, he died prematurely in 1961 at the age of 27. He was found hanging from a tree in [[Zagreb]], today's [[Croatia]]. This controversial incident was officially recorded as a suicide.<ref name="Izložba o Branku Miljkoviću">{{cite web |url=http://www.politika.rs/scc/clanak/193201/Izlozba-o-Branku-Miljkovicu |author=Politika |author-link=Politika |title=Izložba o Branku Miljkoviću |access-date=2017-07-24 |date=2011-03-10 |language=sr}}</ref>

In his one-line poem "Epitaph", he writes "''Ubi me prejaka reč''" ("''I was killed by a word too strong''") almost sensing his premature end of life. During the last years of his life, he published five books of poetry (''I Wake Her in Vain'', ''Death against Death'', ''The Origin of Hope'', ''Fire and Nothing'', ''The Shining Blood'', criticism, and translations of the French Symbolists and Russian poet [[Osip Mandelstam]]. He continues to influence poets to this day.

==Legacy==
A biopic about Miljković's life and poetry ''Vatra i ništa'' ('Fire and Nothing') was produced in 1995. It was written and directed by {{ill|Marislav Radisavljević|sr}}, and produced by Ivan Zdravković. 
[[Goran Milev]] played the role of Branko Miljković.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.mod.gov.rs/lat/3495/filmsko-vece-posveceno-branku-miljkovicu-u-domu-vojske-3495 |title=Filmsko veče posvećeno Branku Miljkoviću u Domu vojske |date=2011-12-29 |access-date=2021-01-26 |website=Ministry of Defence of Serbia}}</ref>

He is sometimes called "the Serbian prince of poetry".<ref>{{Cite web|last=Serbia|first=RTS, Radio televizija Srbije, Radio Television of|title=Бранко Миљковић – принц поезије којег је убила прејака реч|url=http://www.rts.rs/page/magazine/ci/kulturno/story/3149/vest/4260753/branko-miljkovic-60.-godisnjica-od-smrti.html|access-date=2021-02-17|website=www.rts.rs}}</ref>
