'''Sergey Mitrofanovich Gorodetsky''' ({{Lang-rus|Серге́й Митрофа́нович Городе́цкий|p=sʲɪrˈɡʲej mʲɪtrɐˈfanəvʲɪdʑ ɡərɐˈdʲetskʲɪj|a=Syergyey Mitrofanovich Gorodyeczkiy.ru.vorb.oga}}; {{OldStyleDate|January 17|1884|January 5}} – June 8, 1967) was a [[Russians|Russian]] [[poet]], one of the founders (together with [[Nikolay Gumilev]]) of "Guild of Poets" ({{lang|ru|Цех поэтов}}). He was born in [[Saint Petersburg]], and died in [[Obninsk]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=Городецкий С. // Краткая литературная энциклопедия. Т. 2. — 1964 (текст)|url=http://feb-web.ru/feb/kle/KLE-abc/ke2/ke2-2821.htm|access-date=2020-10-28|website=feb-web.ru}}</ref>

Gorodetsky entered the literary scene as a [[Symbolism (arts)|Symbolist]], developing friendships with [[Alexander Blok]], [[Vyacheslav Ivanov (poet)|Vyacheslav Ivanov]], and [[Valery Bryusov|Valery Briusov]]. Following his brief stint with Symbolists, Gorodetsky began to associate with younger poets, forming the [[Acmeist poetry|Acmeist]] group with [[Nikolai Gumilev]], [[Anna Akhmatova]], and [[Osip Mandelshtam]]. Subsequently, abandoning yet another group, he welcomed the [[October Revolution|Bolshevik revolution]] as a Soviet poet.
