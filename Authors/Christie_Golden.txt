}}'''Christie Golden''' (born November 21, 1963) is an American author. She has written many novels and several short stories in fantasy, horror and science fiction.<ref name=about>{{cite web|url=http://www.christiegolden.com/about.htm|author=Golden, Christie|title=Christie Golden: About|access-date=September 27, 2012|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120720205632/http://www.christiegolden.com/about.htm|archive-date=July 20, 2012}}</ref>

==Career==
Golden has written many novels and several short stories in the fields of science fiction, fantasy and horror. Among her many projects are over a dozen ''Star Trek'' novels, over a dozen for gaming giant Blizzard's ''[[World of Warcraft]]'' and ''[[StarCraft]]'' novels, and three books for the now finished ''[[Fate of the Jedi]]'' series of ''[[Star Wars]]'' novels.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.starwars.com/vault/books/news20080912.html|title=Del Rey Star Wars Books Continue Through 2013|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080913160942/http://www.starwars.com/vault/books/news20080912.html|archive-date=2008-09-13}}</ref>  Her co-authors on that series were [[Troy Denning]] and [[Aaron Allston]].

She launched [[TSR, Inc.|TSR]]'s [[Ravenloft]] line of novels in 1991 with her first novel - ''[[Vampire of the Mists]]''.<ref name=contemp>{{cite journal | url=http://www.highbeam.com/doc/1G2-2591000055.html | archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140921212020/http://www.highbeam.com/doc/1G2-2591000055.html | url-status=dead | archive-date=September 21, 2014 | title=Golden, Christie 1963â€“ | journal=[[Contemporary Authors]] | date=January 1, 2007 | access-date=September 27, 2012}}{{subscription|via=[[HighBeam Research]]}}</ref> and is the creator of the elven vampire archetype in fantasy fiction. She followed up with ''[[Dance of the Dead (Ravenloft novel)|Dance of the Dead]]'' and ''[[The Enemy Within (novel)|The Enemy Within]]''.<ref name=contemp/> Golden has also written short stories set in the [[Forgotten Realms]], appearing in anthologies series in which she further explores the character of the [[Elf (Dungeons & Dragons)|elven]] [[Vampire (Dungeons & Dragons)|vampire]] [[Jander Sunstar]], whom she established in ''Vampire of the Mists''. Golden has written at a dozen ''[[Star Trek: Voyager]]'' novels including the popular ''Dark Matters'' trilogy, the ''Homecoming'' duology, and the ''Spirit Walk'' duology.<ref name=contemp/>

Golden is also the author of two original fantasy novels from [[Ace Books]], ''[[King's Man & Thief]]'' and ''[[Instrument of Fate]]'',<ref name=contemp/> which made the 1996 Nebula Preliminary Ballot.

Among her work in the gaming fantasy novels, she wrote ''[[Warcraft: Lord of the Clans]]'' (2001),<ref name=contemp/> and ''[[World of Warcraft: Rise of the Horde]]'' (December 2006).<ref name="wizardsbio"/> She also authored a trilogy titled ''StarCraft: the Dark Templar Saga''<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.simonsays.com/content/book.cfm?tab=23&pid=533882 | title=StarCraft: the Dark Templar Saga | access-date=September 27, 2012}}</ref> for Blizzard Entertainment and Simon and Schuster Pocketbooks. She also wrote ''[[World of Warcraft: Arthas: Rise of the Lich King]]'' which was released April 21, 2009. It was Golden's first New York Times bestseller.  Subsequent Warcraft novels are ''The Shattering: Prelude to Cataclysm'', ''[[Thrall (Warcraft)|Thrall: Twilight of the Aspects]]'', ''[[Jaina Proudmoore|Jaina Proudmoore: Tides of War]]'', and ''War Crimes''.

Under the name '''Jadrien Bell''' she wrote a fantasy thriller entitled ''[[A.D. 999]]'',<ref name=contemp/> which won her the Colorado Author's League Award for Best Genre Novel in 1999.<ref name=about/>

She is currently hoping to reissue her original fantasy series, the Dancers Pentad. The first book in the series ''On Fire's Wings'' follows the discovery of the first of five dancers, Kevla the flame dancer. The second book ''In Stone's Clasp'' records both the flame dancer, Kevla and the stone dancer, the element of earth, Jareth.  The book won her a second Colorado Author's League Award for Best Genre Novel in 2005.<ref name=about/> This series was released through [[Luna Books]].<ref name="wizardsbio">{{cite web|url=http://ww2.wizards.com/Books/Wizards/Bios/default.aspx?doc=ChristieGolden|title=Christie Golden|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090926081120/http://ww2.wizards.com/Books/Wizards/Bios/default.aspx?doc=ChristieGolden|archive-date=September 26, 2009}}</ref>

Golden has also written for ''[[Buffy the Vampire Slayer]]'' and ''Angel'' anthologies.<ref name="wizardsbio"/> She has written novels based on Steven Spielberg's ''[[Invasion America]]'' animated television show, and film producer and screenwriter [[Harve Bennett]] was so impressed with her ''Invasion America'' work that he invited her to Hollywood to write for the show - however, the show was not renewed for a second season.<ref name="wizardsbio"/>

Golden has authored four ''[[Assassin's Creed]]'' works. Her first, "Blackbeard: The Lost Journal", is a companion piece for the video game ''[[Assassin's Creed IV: Black Flag]]''. Another ''Assassin's Creed'' book, the in-universe "Abstergo Employee Handbook" hit shelves in December 2014. "The Accidental Knight," a novel set in the world of Cryptozoic's online card game, HEX, will be published later in 2014 as an online book, with a hard copy version to follow. Her ''[[Clone Wars (Star Wars)|Star Wars: The Clone Wars]]'' novel titled ''[[Star Wars: Dark Disciple]]'' was published in July 2015. And in 2017 she published the official movie [[novelization]] of ''[[Valerian and the City of a Thousand Planets]]'' through Titan Books - {{ISBN|978-1-7856-5384-1}}.

== Personal life ==
Golden was born on November 21, 1963 in [[Atlanta, Georgia]], USA.<ref name=contemp/>
