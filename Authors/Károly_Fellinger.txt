'''Károly Fellinger''' (born 1963) is a Hungarian poet, writer, [[local historian]] living in Slovakia.

== Life and works ==
Born in [[Bratislava]], Károly Fellinger lives in [[Jelka]] since his childhood. During his high school years in the Hungarian Academic Grammar School of Galanta, he was the editor in chief of the school journal and the founding editor of the quarterly review ''Words of Jelka'' (''Jelčianské slovo'') between 1993 and 2003.<ref>{{cite interview |first=Angyali |last=Sanyi |interviewer=Sanyi Angyali |title=Fellinger Károly kifogyhatatlan lírazsákja |url=https://issuu.com/klikkout/docs/klikkout_03_16/7?e=0 |journal=Klikk Out |pages=6–11. |access-date=21 March 2016}}</ref> He used to work as an agronomist, now he runs his own [[smallholding]]. He has published 20 books in [[Hungarian language|Hungarian]] so far. Most of them are collections of poems for adults and children, a village monograph and tales. As a mythographer, he collected the tales and legends of Mátyusföld (region of the Mátyus, Matúšova zem). His volumes of poetry have been published in English, German, Romanian, [[Serbian language|Serbian]], French, Russian, [[Slovak language|Slovak]] and [[Turkish language|Turkish]]. He has been awarded the Golden Opus Prize of the SZMÍT (Hungarian Writers' Association of Slovakia)<ref>{{cite news |first=.hu |last=Prae |title=Ismét Fellinger Károly kapta a szlovákiai Arany Opus-díjat |url=http://www.prae.hu/index.php?route=news%2Fnews&aid=13387 |publisher=Prae.hu |date=22 November 2011 |accessdate=21 March 2016 }}</ref><ref>{{cite news |first=Szabad |last=Újság  |title=Fellinger Károly kapta az Arany Opus-díjat |url=http://www.szabadujsag.com/fellinger-karoly-kapta-az-arany-opus-dijat/ |publisher=Szabad Újság |date=19 November 2011 |accessdate=21 March 2016 }}</ref> twice and the Imre Forbáth Prize for the best collection of poetry written in Hungarian in 2014.<ref>{{cite news |first=Tibor |last=Somogyi |title=Fellinger Károly Forbáth-, Pénzes Tímea Talamon-díjas |url=http://ujszo.com/online/kultura/2014/05/16/fellinger-karoly-forbath-penzes-timea-talamon-dijas |publisher=Új Szó |date=16 May 2014 |accessdate=21 March 2016 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170702163546/http://ujszo.com/online/kultura/2014/05/16/fellinger-karoly-forbath-penzes-timea-talamon-dijas |archive-date=2 July 2017 |url-status=dead }}</ref><ref>{{cite news |first=Ma |last=Felvidék  |title=Pénzes Tímea és Fellinger Károly irodalmi díjat kapott |url=http://felvidek.ma/2014/05/penzes-timea-es-fellinger-karoly-irodalmi-dijat-kapott/ |publisher=Felvidék Ma |date=16 May 2014 |accessdate=21 March 2016 }}</ref> In 2013, he was the winner of the Bóbita poetry contest of the Hungarian Writers' Association.<ref>{{cite news |first=Írók Társasága |last=Szlovákiai Magyar  |title=Fellinger Károly a Bóbita Költőverseny fődíjasa |url=http://www.szmit.sk/modules.php?name=News&file=article&sid=1563 |publisher=SZMÍT |accessdate=21 March 2016 }}</ref> He was a deputy of the municipal council for twenty years and the deputy mayor for years.

In 2014, his volume of selected poetry was translated in English and published in Canada.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.libroslibertad.ca/book.php?id=62 |title=Humility, poetry by Károly Fellinger |publisher=Libros Libertad |access-date=21 March 2016}}</ref> His poem was featured in the international anthology ''[[Poems for the Hazara]]'' in 2014.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.wpm2011.org/node/410 |title=Poems for the Hazara People |publisher=World Poetry Movement |access-date=21 March 2016}}</ref> In the same year, Károly Pallai devoted an English language analysis to the poetry of Fellinger.<ref>{{cite journal |last=Károly Sándor |first=Pallai |title=Metaphoricity and tropes of ontological condensation in Károly Fellinger's ''Humility'' |journal=Vents Alizés |issue=2014/2 |pages=145–152 |location=[[Victoria, Seychelles]] |date=2014 |language=English }}</ref> His first collection of poems in French was published by the prestigious Éditions du Cygne in Paris (''Bétonnière ivre'', 2015). In 2016, he received the prestigious Madách Award of the Slovakian Literary Fund for his collection of poems ''Különbejárat''.<ref>{{cite news |title=Slávnostné odovzdávanie Cien a Prémií LITERÁRNEHO FONDU za pôvodnú literárnu tvorbu za rok 2015 – v Zichyho paláci v Bratislave
|url=http://www.literarny-tyzdennik.sk/products/a14-6-slavnostne-odovzdavanie-cien-a-premii-za-povodnu-literarnu-tvorbu-za-rok-2015-v-zichyho-palaci-v-bratislave/ |publisher=Literárny týždenník |date=14 June 2016 |accessdate=19 June 2016 }}</ref> In 2016, he was the only Slovakian author to be published in the 2015 edition of the World Poetry Yearbook (China).<ref>{{cite book |last=Fellinger |first=Károly |editor=Zhang Zhi, Lai Tingjie |title=World Poetry Yearbook |edition=2015 |year=2016 |publisher=International Poetry and Translation Centre (IPTRC) – Earth Culture Press |location=[[Chongqing]], [[Kína]] |language=English|pages=300–301}}</ref> In 2016, his poems were selected and published in the yearly anthology of the Hungarian Writers' Association of Slovakia.<ref>{{cite book |last1=Fellinger |first1=Károly |title=SZÉP 2016 |language=Hungarian |location=Dunajská Streda |publisher=Szlovákiai Magyar Írók Társasága |year=2016 |isbn=978-80-89806-04-1 |pages=41–47}}</ref> In 2016, his poems were published in Spain (''Dios está ausente'') and in Canada (''Sieve of Light in the Pine Forest''), and a volume of his best poems (''Fellinger Károly legszebb versei'') was also released.

In 2017, his tales were published in a bilingual, Hungarian-English edition (''A Kincsesláda-The Treasure Chest''). His poems were featured in ''Empty Mirror'' and ''Setu'' ([[United States]]),<ref>{{cite magazine |last=Fellinger |first=Károly |date=3 February 2017 |title=Six poems by Károly Fellinger |translator=Károly Sándor Pallai |url=http://www.emptymirrorbooks.com/features/six-poems-by-karoly-fellinger.html |magazine=Empty Mirror |access-date=2017-02-17}}</ref> ''Sipay'' ([[Seychelles]]), ''A New Ulster'' ([[Northern Ireland]]), ''Ygdrasil'' ([[Canada]]) and in the prestigious Belgian review ''Traversées''.<ref>{{cite magazine |last=Fellinger |first=Károly |date=2016 |title=Quinze poèmes de Károly Fellinger |translator=Károly Sándor Pallai |url=https://traversees.wordpress.com/2017/02/12/traversees-numero-82-decembre-2016/ |magazine=Traversées |access-date=2017-02-17}}</ref> In 2017, he received the Tibor Simkó Prize of the Hungarian Writers' Association of Slovakia for his collection of tales ''Ilka vára''.<ref>{{cite news | date =2017-05-20 | title =Nem osztottak idén Talamon-díjat | publisher = Paraméter | url =http://parameter.sk/nem-osztottak-iden-talamon-dijat | accessdate =2017-05-20}}</ref> In the same year, he was awarded the Medal of the Chairman of the [[Trnava Region|Trnava]] Self-Governing Region for his contribution to culture.<ref>{{cite journal | date =2017-05-25 | title =Oceňovanie pracovníkov v oblasti kultúry | journal =SeredOnLine | url =http://www.seredonline.sk/clanky/ocenovanie-pracovnikov-v-oblasti-kultury/ | accessdate =2017-05-25 | archive-url =https://web.archive.org/web/20170525214054/http://www.seredonline.sk/clanky/ocenovanie-pracovnikov-v-oblasti-kultury/ | archive-date =25 May 2017 | url-status =dead }}</ref> His new poems were published under the title ''Köti a sötétséget'' by Media Nova and his second collection of poems in French appeared in Paris (''À l'affût de Dieu'', Éditions du Cygne). His poem "Elbocsátó" was translated in English by Károly Sándor Pallai and published in the international poetic anthology Amaravati Poetic Prism ([[India]]).

He is a member of the Hungarian Writers' Association of Slovakia and of the Hungarian Writers' Association.
