'''Ivan Vladislavić''' (born 17 September 1957) is a [[South Africa|South African]] author, editor and professor.<ref name="Stories">{{cite web |title=Ivan Vladislavić |url=https://www.andotherstories.org/authors/ivan-vladislavic/ |website=And Other Stories |accessdate=10 May 2019 |language=en}}</ref><ref name="Take Two">{{cite web |last1=MacKenzie |first1=Craig |title=Vladislavic: Take two |url=https://mg.co.za/article/2011-05-20-vladislavic-take-two |website=The M&G Online |accessdate=10 May 2019 |language=en |date=20 May 2011}}</ref> Vladislavić's style has been described as [[Postmodern literature|postmodern]], innovative, humorous and unpredictable.<ref name=":1">{{Cite book|last=Cornwell|first=Gareth|url=http://archive.org/details/columbiaguidetos0000corn|title=The Columbia guide to South African literature in English since 1945|date=2010|publisher=New York : Columbia University Press|others=|isbn=978-0-231-50381-5|pages=196-197}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|last=Kitamura|first=Katie|title=Ivan Vladislavić|url=https://bombmagazine.org/articles/ivan-vladislavić/|url-status=live|access-date=2021-11-15|website=[[Bomb Magazine]]}}</ref><ref>{{Cite book|last=Walder|first=Dennis|url=http://archive.org/details/essaysstudies0053unse|title=Reading the 'New' Literatures in a Postcolonial Era|date=2000|publisher=D. S. Brewer|others=|editor-last=Susheila|editor-first=Nasta|location=Cambridge|pages=157|chapter=The Necessity of Error: Memory and Representation in the New Literatures}}</ref> Despite receiving critical acclaim, his work is not well known outside his home county.<ref name=":2" />

== Biography ==
Vladislavić was born in Pretoria in 1957.<ref name=":1" /> His father was a mechanic of [[Croats|Croatian heritage]] and his mother was a housewife. He attended the [[University of the Witwatersrand]] and graduated in 1979.<ref name=":2">{{Cite web|last=Hoby|first=Hermione|date=2017-08-08|title=The South African Parables of Ivan Vladislavic|url=https://www.newyorker.com/books/page-turner/the-south-african-parables-of-ivan-vladislavic|url-status=live|access-date=2021-11-15|website=The New Yorker|language=en-US}}</ref> 

He has worked as Social Studies Editor for anti-apartheid publishing house [[Ravan Press]] and as an editor for ''[[Staffrider]]'' magazine.<ref>{{Cite journal|last=Reid|first=Katie|date=2021-05-04|title=Small and joined in print: Ivan Vladislavić, “Tsafendas’s Diary,” and Staffrider magazine (1988)|url=https://doi.org/10.1080/02533952.2021.1973335|journal=Social Dynamics|volume=47|issue=2|pages=264–287|doi=10.1080/02533952.2021.1973335|issn=0253-3952}}</ref> 

He lives in [[Johannesburg|Johannesburg, South Africa]] and is a Professor of Creative Writing at the [[University of the Witwatersrand]].<ref>{{Cite web|last=|first=|date=2020-10-02|title=Q&A: Words on the Times with Ivan Vladislavic on “The Distance”|url=https://africainwords.com/2020/10/02/qa-words-on-the-times-with-ivan-vladislavic/|url-status=live|access-date=2021-11-15|language=en}}</ref><ref name=":1" />
