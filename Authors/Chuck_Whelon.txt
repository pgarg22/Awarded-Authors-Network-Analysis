'''Chuck Whelon''' (born 1969){{citation needed|date=October 2014}} is a British, internationally published author and cartoonist, based in [[San Francisco]], [[California]], United States.<ref>Rosen-Molina, Mike (October, 2007). "You Call this Funny? | Since day one, the East Bay’s quirky independent cartoonists have been making us laugh—and think.", ''[[The East Bay Monthly]]''</ref>

==Career==
Chuck Whelon is the creator, artist and co-writer of the long-running humorous fantasy webcomic serial ''Pewfell'',<ref>Withrow, Steven (July 1, 2003), Toon Art: The Graphic Art of Digital Cartooning, [[Watson-Guptill]]</ref> which originally appeared online on [[Modern Tales]],<ref>O'Brien, Danny (February 26, 2006). "The tooniverse explodes: Interactive web comics are winning over legions of savvy young fans. Danny O'Brien assesses the coolest strips online", ''[[The Sunday Times]]'', p. 27.</ref> and later became available for free on [[Webcomics Nation]], [[DrunkDuck]], and [[ComicFury]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://comicfury.com/comicprofile.php?url=pewfell|title=Error! - Comic Fury Webcomic Hosting|website=Comicfury.com|accessdate=6 January 2018}}</ref>

Whelon has illustrated a series of search & find titles for Buster Books, including 'Where's the Penguin?', 'Where's Santa?' and 'The Great Fairy Tale Search',<ref>Where's Santa? by Bryony Jones and Chuck Whelon (Sep 22, 2015), [[Simon & Schuster]]</ref><ref>The Great Fairy Tale Search (Buster Activity) by Chuck Whelon (Oct 2, 2014), [[Buster Books]]</ref><ref>Where's Santa? "Bestsellers", ''[[Publishers Weekly]]'', p. 100., p135</ref> all of which have been translated into multiple languages.<ref>"Donde esta el pinguino?" (Spanish Edition) by Chuck Whelon and Sophie Schrey (Jan 30, 2014), [[Ediciones B]]</ref><ref>"Cache-cache avec les pingouins" (French Edition) by Chuck Whelon and Sophie Schrey  and Alice Delarbre (14 May 2014), [[Père Castor-Flammarion]]</ref><ref>„Gdzie jest pingwin?” (Polish Edition) by Chuck Whelon and Sophie Schrey,  (January 2014), [[IUVI]]</ref><ref>"Waar is de pinguïn?" Whelon designed and illustrated the comic boardgame "Legitimacy" published by Minion Games.<ref>"Legitimacy: The Game of Royal Bastards" by Chuck Whelon (2009), [[Minion Games]]</ref>
Whelon has also illustrated a number of other boardgames for Minion, including ''Those Pesky Humans'',<ref>"Those Pesky Humans" by James Mathe, illustrated by Chuck Whelon (2009), [[Minion Games]]</ref> ''Grave Business'',<ref>"Grave Business"  by Andy Van Zandt, illustrated by Chuck Whelon (2011), [[Minion Games]]</ref> 'Tahiti',<ref>"Tahiti"  by David E. Witcher, illustrated by Chuck Whelon (2012), [[Minion Games]]</ref> ''Nile'',<ref>"Nile"  by Daniel Callister, illustrated by Chuck Whelon (2009), [[Minion Games]]</ref><ref>"Nile Deluxor"  by Daniel Callister, illustrated by Chuck Whelon (2011), [[Minion Games]]</ref> and ''Battle Merchants''.<ref>"Battle Merchants"  by Gil Hova, illustrated by Chuck Whelon (2014), [[Minion Games]]</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://boardgamegeek.com/boardgamedesigner/14927/chuck-whelon|title=Chuck Whelon - Board Game Designer - BoardGameGeek|website=Boardgamegeek.com|accessdate=6 January 2018}}</ref>

Since 2012 Whelon has written and drawn the monthly "Dr. Dig" comic strip for Cricket Media Group's 'Dig' magazine.<ref>"Dr. Dig" by Chuck Whelon, [[Dig (magazine)|Dig]] Magazine, (Sept 2012 - continuing) [[Cricket Magazine Group]]</ref>

Whelon is the cartoonist for Goodman Game's '[[Dungeon Crawl Classics]]' Role Playing Game.<ref>Dungeon Crawl Classics RPG: Core Rulebook [DCC RPG], by Joseph Goodman et. al. (2012), [[Goodman Games]]</ref><ref>The Monsters Alphabet, by Jobe Bittman with Michael Curtis  (2014), [[Goodman Games]]</ref>

Whelon has also worked as an Art Director for [[Ogilvy & Mather]], an illustrator, Flash developer and production artist.<ref>[https://archive.is/20141008191012/https://www.linkedin.com/in/whelon]</ref>

Whelon was born and raised in [[Basingstoke]], England.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.comixology.com/Chuck-Whelon/comics-creator/8470 |title=Archived copy |accessdate=2014-10-08 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20141015195241/https://www.comixology.com/Chuck-Whelon/comics-creator/8470 |archivedate=2014-10-15 }}</ref> He is a member of the [[National Cartoonists Society]]<ref>Judgement Daze, [[Tom Richmond (illustrator)|Tom Richmond]], (March 23rd, 2013), [[The Cartoonist]], [[National Cartoonists Society]]</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.reuben.org/2013/03/judgment-daze/|title=Judgment Daze (UPDATED!)|website=Reuben.org|accessdate=6 January 2018}}</ref> and the [[Society of Children's Book Writers and Illustrators]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.scbwi.org/members-public/chuck-whelon|title=SCBWI - Public Profile|website=Scbwi.org|accessdate=6 January 2018}}</ref> He is represented by the Beehive Illustration Agency.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.beehiveillustration.co.uk/artists/whelon_chuck/collection.html |title=Illustrator: Chuck Whelon |accessdate=2014-10-08 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20141004061706/http://www.beehiveillustration.co.uk/artists/whelon_chuck/collection.html |archivedate=2014-10-04 }}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.childrensillustrators.com/agency/Beehive-Illustration/id=1732/pag=1/|title=Childrensillustrators.com - international directory of children's illustrators|last=GeniiWeb|website=Childrensillustrators.com|accessdate=6 January 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170807153701/http://www.childrensillustrators.com/agency/Beehive-Illustration/id=1732/pag=1/|archive-date=7 August 2017|url-status=dead}}</ref>

He is the brother to Emily Jordan.{{citation needed|date=October 2014}}

Whelon's current project is a crowd-funded puzzle book for kids called ''Wizard Pickles''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.patreon.com/cartoon|title=Chuck Whelon is creating Ferdinand the Frog comics - Patreon|website=Patreon.com|accessdate=6 January 2018}}</ref>
