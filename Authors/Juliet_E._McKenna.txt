'''Juliet E. McKenna''' (born 1965) is a British [[fantasy]] author with over fifteen epic fantasy novels.

==Biography==
McKenna was born in Lincolnshire in 1965, and studied Greek and Roman history and literature at [[St Hilda's College, Oxford]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.thewritefantastic.com/julietmckenna.html |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20051222033114/http://www.thewritefantastic.com/julietmckenna.html |url-status=dead |archive-date=22 December 2005 |title=Write Fantastic biography |date=30 May 2008 }}</ref> After college McKenna had a career in personnel management before a changing to work in book-selling. She also fitted in becoming a mother around her writing.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.solarisbooks.com/post/333|title=Juliet E. McKenna|publisher=Solaris Books}}</ref> McKenna is one of the British boom of fantasy writers.<ref>{{cite journal|author=Andrew M. Butler|title=Thirteen Ways of Looking at the British Boom|journal= Science Fiction Studies |volume=30 |issue=3|date= 2003|pages= 374–393|jstor=4241200}}</ref><ref>{{cite journal|author=Mark Bould|date=2002|title= Bould on the Boom|journal= Science Fiction Studies |volume=29 |issue=2|pages= 307–310|jstor=4241092}}</ref> As well as her various novel series McKenna writes articles and reviews for magazines.<ref name="Barbini2017">{{cite book|author=Francesca T Barbini|title=Gender Identity and Sexuality in Current Fantasy and Science Fiction|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=CscptAEACAAJ|year=2017|publisher=Luna Press Publishing|isbn=978-1-911143-24-6}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.newstatesman.com/culture/2014/02/i-don%E2%80%99t-want-be-rare-successful-female-writer-i-just-want-be-successful-writer|title=I don't want to be a rare successful female writer. I just want to be a successful writer|author=Sophia McDougall|date=27 February 2014|newspaper=The New Statesman}}</ref> She has worked as a judge for various awards such as the [[Arthur C. Clarke Award]] in 2013, the 2011 James White Award and the [[World Fantasy Awards]] in 2018.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.jameswhiteaward.com/award-history |title=James White Award History |newspaper=James White Award |accessdate= 2 June 2018|date=2010-10-20 }}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=https://locusmag.com/2017/12/2018-world-fantasy-awards-judges-announced/ |title=2018 World Fantasy Awards Judges Announced |newspaper=Locus Online |date= 4 December 2017  |author= |accessdate= 2 June 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|newspaper=The Guardian|author=Liz Williams|date=4 April 2013|url=https://www.theguardian.com/books/booksblog/2013/apr/04/feminist-all-male-clarke-prize-shortlist|title=Why this feminist chose an all-male Clarke prize shortlist}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.albedo1.com/juliet-e-mckenna-interview-i-was-once-patient-x-in-the-lancet-2012/|title= I was once patient X|newspaper=Albedo One|date=2012}}</ref> McKenna is also a contributing editor for the Irish anthology magazine [[Albedo One]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.albedo1.com/albedo-one-magazine/team/ |title=Albedo One Team |newspaper=Albedo One|accessdate= 1 June 2018}}</ref> In 2013 McKenna was the chair of the [[Eastercon|British National Science Fiction Convention]], EightSquaredCon.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.eightsquaredcon.org/web/Welcome.html |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130204072201/http://www.eightsquaredcon.org/web/Welcome.html |url-status=dead |archive-date=2013-02-04 |title=EightSquaredCon The 2013 Eastercon |date=  2013-02-04|accessdate= 2 June 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.thetelegraphandargus.co.uk/news/10280844.JB_Priestley_to_be__ghost_of_honour__at_Eastercon_science_fiction_convention/ |title=JB Priestley to be 'ghost of honour' at Eastercon science fiction convention |newspaper=The telegraph and argus |date=11 March 2013  |author= |accessdate= 2 June 2018}}</ref>

She was also one of the authors, along with others such as [[Sarah Ash]] and [[Mark Chadbourn]], behind  The Write Fantastic,<ref>https://web.archive.org/web/20110209194335/http://thewritefantastic.com/</ref> which was an initiative by a group of fantasy authors to promote the fantasy genre, and to display the scope of current fantasy writing.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.highbeam.com/doc/1P2-12569330.html|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180725122819/https://www.highbeam.com/doc/1P2-12569330.html|url-status=dead|archive-date=25 July 2018|title=Get write ideas from top authors CITY CENTRE|newspaper=Birmingham Evening Mail|date=9 August 2007}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.theguardian.com/books/2014/apr/18/genre-debate-science-fiction-speculative-literary|title=The genre debate: Science fiction travels farther than literary fiction|author=Juliet McKenna|date=18 April 2014}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.theguardian.com/books/booksblog/2014/feb/21/womens-fantasy-fiction-unknown-genre|newspaper=The Guardian|author=Alison Flood|date=21 February 2014|title=Women's fantasy fiction: join the quest for a world unknown to bookstores}}</ref>
McKenna joined forces with a group of micro business owners to  form EU VAT ACTION resolve the VAT issue caused by the EU VAT regulations which came into force on 1 January 2015. She spent considerable time working with businesses and experts in the UK and EU to create a way that small businesses online could work with the VAT regulations.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.internationaltaxreview.com/Article/3445165/VAT-MOSS-EU-to-review-B2C-digital-services-rules.html|title=EU to review B2C digital services rules|date=15 April 2015|author=Joe Stanley-Smith}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://euvataction.org/contact-us/about/|title=EU VAT Action}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.ft.com/content/65eca2c2-7bcd-11e4-b6ab-00144feabdc0|newspaper=Financial Times|author=Andrew Bounds, Enterprise Editor|title=Treasury listens to VAT fears of digital entrepreneurs|date = 4 December 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.theseus.fi/handle/10024/108645 |title=Influence of the recent changes in EU VAT regulations on the financial situation of British micro businesses providing digital services to end consumers. |publisher=Degree Programme in International Business University of Finland |date= 2016 |author=Nalewajk, Jolanta |accessdate= 2 June 2018}}</ref>

She regularly attends fantasy conventions and hosted FantasyCon 2015's awards night, gives talks, and teaches creative writing courses.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.novacon.org.uk/n46/|title=Guest of Honour|publisher=Novacon46}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.theoxfordtimes.net/lifestyle/thisweek/display.var.1262390.0.a_life_fuelled_by_fantasy.php |title=A Life Fuelled By Fantasy |date=15 March 2008 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20080516235607/http://www.theoxfordtimes.net/lifestyle/thisweek/display.var.1262390.0.a_life_fuelled_by_fantasy.php |archivedate=16 May 2008 }}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-scotland-glasgow-west-27073034|title=Sci-fi fans descend on Glasgow for Satellite 4 convention|publisher=BBC|date=18 April 2014}}</ref>

==Critical reception==
[[Financial Times]] reviewer [[James Lovegrove]] described McKenna's 2012 ''She-who-thinks-for-herself,'' as "a cunning, funny... feminist rewrite" of [[H. Rider Haggard]]'s ''[[She: A History of Adventure]]''.<ref name="LovegroveRomance">{{cite news |last1=Lovegrove |first1=James |title=Resurrection Engines: 16 Extraordinary Tales of Scientific Romance |publisher=Financial Times |date=19 January 2013|id={{ProQuest|1270927459}} }}</ref>
