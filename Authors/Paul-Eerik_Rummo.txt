'''Paul-Eerik Rummo''' (born January 19, 1942) is an Estonian poet, playwright, translator and politician who was the former Estonian Minister of Culture and Education, as well as the former Estonian Minister of Population Affairs.

Rummo was born in [[Tallinn]], the son of Estonian writer [[Paul Rummo]]. Paul-Eerik studied literature at the [[University of Tartu]], graduating in 1965. Rummo has worked in Estonian theatres.

==Personal life==
Paul-Eerik Rummo is married to an actress, poet, author, and translator [[Viiu Härm]]. The couple have three daughters.<ref name="Family">{{cite web |url=http://annestiil.delfi.ee/uudised/paar-viiu-harm-ja-paul-eerik-rummo?id=82300416|title=Paar: Viiu Härm ja Paul-Eerik Rummo|date=6 December 2005|periodical=Anne & Stiil|accessdate=5 January 2018|language=Estonian}}</ref>

==Legacy==
In October 1980, Rummo was a signatory of the [[Letter of 40 intellectuals|Letter of 40 Intellectuals]], a public letter in which forty prominent Estonian intellectuals defended the [[Estonian language]] and protested the [[Russification]] policies of the [[Kremlin]] in Estonia.<ref name="Kiri">{{cite web |url=https://www.postimees.ee/1506389/ajaleht-pravda-ja-40-keisri-hullu|title=Ajaleht Pravda ja 40 keisri hullu|first=Lauri|last=Vahtre|date=28 October 2005|work=[[Postimees]]|accessdate=25 August 2019|language=Estonian}}</ref>  The signatories also expressed their unease against Republic-level government in harshly dealing with youth protests in Tallinn that were sparked a week earlier due to the banning of a public performance of the [[punk rock]] band [[Propeller (band)|Propeller]].<ref name="Kiri" />

In the novel ''[[Purge (novel)|Purge]]'' (in [[Finnish language|Finnish]] ''Puhdistus'') by [[Sofi Oksanen]], Rummo's poetry becomes a symbol of resistance against [[Russification]] in Estonia, sprinkled throughout the narrative.<ref name = jaggi>{{cite news |title=Purge by Sofi Oksanen |first=
Maya |last=Jaggi |authorlink=Maya Jaggi|newspaper=[[The Guardian]] |date=21 August 2010 |url=https://www.theguardian.com/books/2010/aug/21/purge-sofi-oksanen |accessdate=2010-09-05}}</ref>
