'''Richard Cameron''' (born 16 June 1948) is an English [[playwright]] from [[Doncaster]] . His themes are Northern [[post-industrial society]], [[working class]] life, tough women and violent men.  Cameron's plays include ''Pond Life'' (1992), ''Not Fade Away'' (1993), ''The Mortal Ash'' (1994), ''Almost Grown'' (1994), ''All of You Mine'' (1996), ''The Glee Club'' (2002), ''Gong Donkeys'' (2004), ''Flower Girls'' (2007), and ''Can't Stand Up For Falling Down''.<ref>{{cite magazine|url=http://archive.tribunemagazine.co.uk/article/12th-june-1992/9/growing-pains|title= Growing pains|magazine=[[Tribune Magazine]]|date=12 June 1992|accessdate=30 August 2012}}</ref><ref name="Theatre record">{{cite book|title=Theatre record|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=QpgrAQAAIAAJ|accessdate=30 August 2012|year=1993|publisher=I. Herbert|page=228}}</ref><ref name="Plays and players">{{cite book|title=Plays and players|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=6clBAQAAIAAJ|accessdate=30 August 2012|year=1994|publisher=Hansom Books}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.cix.co.uk/~shutters/reviews/97003.htm|title=ALL OF YOU MINE |newspaper=[[Financial Times]]|accessdate=30 August 2012}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|author=Billington, Michael|url=https://www.theguardian.com/stage/2004/nov/08/theatre|title=Gong Donkeys|newspaper=[[The Guardian]]|location=London|date=9 November 2004}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/suffolk/content/articles/2007/10/09/flower_girls_review_feature.shtml|title=Flower Girls|publisher=[[BBC]]|date=9 October 2007|accessdate=30 August 2012}}</ref>

Cameron wrote ''[[Dear Nobody]]'' starring [[Sean Maguire]], and ''Stone, Scissors, Paper'' for BBC ([[1997 in television|1997]]).  He has also contributed to the popular TV series [[Midsomer Murders]], writing the script for the episode "Midsomer Rhapsody".
