'''Fiona McIntosh''' (born 1960) is an English-born Australian author of adult and children's books.<ref>{{cite web|last=Auden|first=Sandy|title=An Interview with Fiona McIntosh|url=http://www.sfsite.com/09a/fm207.htm|publisher=[[SF Site]]|access-date=30 April 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|last=Swart|first=Genevieve|title=Gruesome fantasy belies a gentle writer|url=http://www.smh.com.au/news/books/gruesome-fantasy-belies-a-gentle-writer/2007/01/14/1168709604476.html|publisher=SMH|access-date=30 April 2014}}</ref> She was born in [[Brighton]], England and between the ages of three and eight, travelled a lot to [[Africa]] due to her father's work. At the age of nineteen, she travelled first to [[Paris]] and later to Australia, where she has lived ever since. In 2007, she released a crime novel, ''Bye Bye Baby'', under the [[pen name]] of '''Lauren Crow''';<ref name="dates">{{cite web|url=http://www.laurencrow.com/ |title=November/December 2007 newsletter |publisher=Lauren Crow |access-date=2008-05-01 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080709035257/http://www.laurencrow.com/ |archive-date=9 July 2008 }}</ref> however, the pen name was dropped for the republished edition of ''Bye Bye Baby'' and for the sequel, ''Beautiful Death''.
