'''Miriam Alexander''', later '''Mrs Harold Stokes''', (born Sept. 1879) was a British-born author of historical novels with Irish settings.<ref>Stephen James Meredith Brown (ed), ''Ireland in Fiction: A Guide to Irish Novels, Tales, Romances, and Folk-lore'', Burt Franklin, NY, 1970 (Reprint of the 1919 ed.) pp.17-18 [https://books.google.com/books?id=JV96TFsdqH0C&lpg=PA17&dq=The%20House%20of%20Lisronan&hl=en&pg=PA17#v=onepage&q=The%20House%20of%20Lisronan&f=true]</ref>

She was born at [[Birkenhead]] and educated at home, except for a short period at [[Alexandra College]], Dublin. She was much interested in the [[Gaelic League]] but later became alienated from it.<ref>Brown</ref>

She made her literary debut with her novel ''The House of Lisronan'', a story of the [[Williamite War in Ireland|Williamite wars]]. The novel was awarded the 250-guinea [[Andrew Melrose Publisher|Melrose Prize]] for 1911. Six editions were sold in less than two months. Her other novels include ''The Port of Dreams'' (1912), ''The Ripple'' (1913, ''Miss O'Corra, M.F.H.'' (1915), and ''The Green Altar'' (1924).<ref>John Wilson Foster, Irish Novels, 1890-1940: New Bearings in Culture and Fiction, Oxford University Press, USA, 2008 {{ISBN|0-19-923283-0}} {{ISBN|978-0199232833}} pp26, [https://books.google.com/books?id=LWqLubr0m0IC&pg=PA26&dq=The+House+of+Lisronan&hl=en&cd=8#v=onepage&q=The%20House%20of%20Lisronan&f=false]</ref>
