'''Rossy Evelin Lima-Padilla''' ([[Veracruz]], [[Mexico]]), is a [[Mexican Americans|Mexican-American]] poet, professor and activist. She has published her work in numerous journals, magazines and anthologies in Europe, North America and South America.

==Biography==
Lima was born in [[Veracruz]], [[Mexico]] to [[Mexicans|Mexican]] parents on August 18, 1986.<ref>{{Cite book|url=https://muse.jhu.edu/book/65802|title=Writing Grandmothers: Africa Vs Latin America Vol 2|last1=Mwanaka|first1=Rinos|last2=Félix|first2=Rodríguez Ricardo|publisher=African Books Collective|year=2019|isbn=9781779065261|location=Oxford|pages=xvi-xv|language=en|url-access=subscription|via=Project MUSE}}</ref> At the age of thirteen her family immigrated into the [[United States]] at a river crossing.<ref name=":0">{{Cite journal|last=Mont'Ros|first=Dennis|date=2016|title=Aguacamino|url=http://search.ebscohost.com/login.aspx?direct=true&db=a9h&AN=121474348&site=ehost-live|journal=Texas Review|volume=37|issue=3|url-access=subscription|via=EBSCOhost}}</ref> Her family settled in [[Texas]] where she struggled with the new education system.<ref name="Struggled as Student">{{Cite web|url=http://larevistamujer.com/rossy-evelin-lima-en/|title=Struggled as Student|last=Davis|first=Lilia|website=Revista Mujer|language=en|access-date=2019-09-17}}</ref> Lima has stated, "School was a new system for me, and it made me feel lonely and scared. I struggled to learn a new language to communicate, but my brain and my lips seemed disconnected, I murmured trembling words that my classmates and teachers could not understand."<ref name="Struggled as Student"/> During her struggling teenage years, Lima excelled in school and found her passion for literature and poetry.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=eqVGvfUnkgM|title=TEDx McAllen|website=Youtube TEDx|language=en|access-date=2019-09-17}}</ref> She graduated from [[Pharr-San Juan-Alamo Memorial High School|PSJA Memorial High School]] in 2005.<ref name=":1">{{Cite web|url=https://www.kveo.com/news/local-news/psja-alumna-named-poet-of-the-year/|title=PSJA Alumna Named 'Poet of the Year'|date=2018-10-18|website=KVEO-TV|language=en-US|access-date=2019-10-15}}</ref> In 2009, Lima graduated with a bachelor's in Literature from the [[University of Texas–Pan American|University of Texas-Pan American]] and earned her masters's in literature the next year from the same university.<ref name=":1" /> Lima went on to earn a PhD in linguistics from the [[University of Texas Rio Grande Valley]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.poetryny.com/?p=1785|title=Mexican Ph.D. and DACA Recipient Rossy Evelin Lima Distinguished with 2018 Poet of the Year Award by The Americas Poetry Festival of New York.|date=2018|website=The Americas Poetry Festival of New York|access-date=2019-10-15}}</ref><ref name=":1" /> In 2012, she entered into the DACA ([[Deferred Action for Childhood Arrivals]]) program.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.newspapers.com/clip/37234317/|title=This is Who I Am|last=Rasmussen|first=Peter|date=22 September 2015|work=The Monitor|access-date=15 October 2019|via=Newspapers.com}}</ref>

In 2016, Rossy Lima co-founded Jade Publishing; a publishing company dedicated to the publication and promotion of emerging Latin American literary talent in both English and Spanish.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.jadepublishing.org/about-us.html|title=About Us|website=Jade Publishing|language=en|access-date=2019-04-29}}</ref>

Lima is currently the director of the Spanish Heritage Language Program at [[Texas Tech University]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.depts.ttu.edu/classic_modern/newsletter/issue19/faculty.php|title=Faculty Focus|date=Fall 2018|website=CMLL News|publisher=Texas Tech University|access-date=2019-10-15}}</ref>

== Writing ==
Lima was recognized by the 2014 [[International Latino Book Awards]] for her work on ''Ecos de barro''  (2013).<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.chicano.ucla.edu/files/news/2014%20Intl%20Latino%20Book%20Award%20Finalists.pdf|title=2014 Is An Amazing Year for Books for Latinos|last=Whisler|first=Kirk|date=2014|website=International Latino Book Awards|access-date=15 October 2019}}</ref> ''[[The Monitor (Texas)|The Monitor]]'' wrote that her poems in ''Ecos de barro'' were "vibrant" and that "Lima demonstrates with effortless art, words have the power to transform us for good or ill."<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.newspapers.com/clip/37239370/|title=New Books by Immigrant Authors|last=Bowles|first=David|date=2 January 2014|work=The Monitor|access-date=15 October 2019|via=Newspapers.com}}</ref>

''[[Southwest Review|Texas Review]]'' writes that Lima's personal journey as an immigrant and her own "roots" are central themes in the poetry of ''Aguacamino/Waterpath'' (2015).<ref name=":0" /> They write, "Lima's enlistment of her multifaceted identity allows her to actualize her immigrant and writing experiences, forming a visceral and critically needed prism."<ref name=":0" />

In 2015, she was recognized in [[Venice]] for her poem, ''Citlalicue'' with an International poetry award (P''remio Internazionale di Poesia Altino'').<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.lacartadialtino.it/premio-internazionale-di-poesia-altino-seconda-edizione-2015/|title=Premio Internazionale Di Poesia Altino – Seconda Edizone - 2015|date=23 March 2015|website=La Carta di Altino|language=it|access-date=2019-04-29}}</ref> She was awarded the ''Orgullo Fronterizo Mexicano'' award given by the [[Institute for Mexicans Abroad]] in 2016.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.elperiodicousa.com/entregan-el-premio-orgullo-fronterizo-mexicano/|title=Entregan el premio 'Orgullo Fronterizo Mexicano'|last=USA|date=18 October 2016|website=El Periódico USA|language=es|access-date=2019-04-29}}</ref> In 2017, she was awarded first place in the ''Concorso Internazionale di Poesia La Finestra Eterea'' in [[Milan]] in 2017.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.latinobookreview.com/south-texas-poet-rossy-evelin-lima-wins-italian-poetry-award--latino-book-review.html|title=South Texas Poet, Rossy Evelin Lima, Wins Italian Poetry Award|last=Padilla|first=Gerald A.|date=6 January 2018|website=Latino Book Review|language=en|access-date=2019-10-15}}</ref>

[[United States Poet Laureate|Poet Laureate of the United States]], [[Juan Felipe Herrera]], wrote that ''Migrare, mutare'' (2017) is "A magnificent set of poems, in a most appropriate time."<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.latinobookreview.com/us-poet-laureate-juan-felipe-herrera-on-rossy-lima.html|title=U.S Poet Laureate Juan Felipe Herrera on Rossy Lima|date=13 April 2017|website=Latino Book Review|language=en|access-date=2019-10-15}}</ref>
