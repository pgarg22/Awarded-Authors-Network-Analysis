'''Rajiv Joseph''' (born June 16, 1974) is an American [[playwright]]. He was named a finalist for the 2010 [[Pulitzer Prize for Drama]] for his play ''[[Bengal Tiger at the Baghdad Zoo]]'',<ref name=":0" /> and he won an [[Obie Award]] for Best New American Play for his play ''Describe the Night''.<ref name="offbroadway" />

==Early life==
Rajiv Joseph was born and raised in [[Cleveland, Ohio]]; his mother is Euroamerican of French and German ancestry and his father is of Indian ancestry and immigrated to the States from India.<ref name="Monji">{{cite news|last=Monji|first=Jana|title=Talking to the Man Behind the Tiger: Rajiv Joseph |newspaper=LA Examiner|date=June 13, 2009}}</ref> He attended [[Cleveland Heights High School]] and graduated from [[Miami University]] in [[Oxford, Ohio]] in 1996 with a [[Bachelor of Arts|B.A.]] in [[Creative Writing]]. While at Miami he was a member of the university's [[Miami University Glee Club|Men's Glee Club]] and its male acapella group, the Cheezies.

Following graduation Joseph joined the [[Peace Corps]], serving three formative years in the West African Republic of [[Senegal]],<ref name=autogenerated1>[http://www.broadwayworld.com/viewcolumn.cfm?colid=26573 Vineyard Presents Paula Vogel Award To Rajiv Joseph (BroadwayWorld.com)<!-- Bot generated title -->]</ref> including two years in Koular and the third in [[Kaolack]].<ref name="Monji"/> Joseph has stated about his time there: "Being in Senegal, more than anything else in my life, made me into a writer."<ref name="Monji"/> His time in Senegal helped him develop the discipline of daily writing and inspired his "fascination with the power of language"; as he has stated: "It felt a little like being a child again, because your language skills are on the level of a 4-year-old, so the adults kind of ignore you, but the children cluster around you telling you what everything is called".<ref name="D'Souza">{{cite news|last=D'Souza|first=Karen|title=Rajiv Joseph: A fresh and compelling voice in theater|url=http://www.mercurynews.com/ci_17523411?nclick_check=1|access-date=15 March 2011|newspaper=Silicon Valley Mercury News|date=5 March 2011}}</ref>

Joseph earned a [[Master of Fine Arts]] in Dramatic Writing from [[New York University]]'s [[Tisch School of the Arts]] in 2004.  He has taught Essay Writing at [[New York University]] and wrote for Seasons 3 and 4 of the Showtime series ''[[Nurse Jackie]]''.<ref>{{cite news| url=https://www.nytimes.com/2011/01/02/theater/02rajiv.html?sq=rajiv%20joseph&st=cse&adxnnl=1&scp=1&adxnnlx=1294974242-kHI+ZLbr4/dcjsQOCGCOVQ | work=The New York Times | first=Patrick | last=Healy | title=Rajiv Joseph's 'Gruesome Playground Injuries,' Second Stage | date=December 29, 2010}}</ref>

==Career==

===Theatre credits===
Joseph's first play, ''Huck & Holden'', debuted at the [[Cherry Lane Theatre]] in January 2006.<ref>Simmons, Paulanne. [http://www.nytheatre-wire.com/ps06021t.htm "Coming of Age in the 21st Century"] nytheatre-wire.com, January 24, 2006</ref> The play also had a West Coast run at the Black Dahlia Theater in [[Los Angeles]] the following year. Joseph has stated that the story, about an Indian college student arriving in the United States, is based on his father's experiences coming to the States.<ref name="Monji"/><ref name="D'Souza"/> His mixed-race background has given him what one critic has called a "fearlessness" about racial topics: "Being mixed-race has always been a part of my identity. You are never fully one thing or the other. You always feel a little apart, a little bit of an outsider, even when you are with your own family. That's an interesting perspective for looking at the world."<ref name="D'Souza"/>

''All This Intimacy'' premiered at [[New York City]]'s McGinn/Cazale Theater in 2006.<ref>{{cite news| url=http://theater2.nytimes.com/2006/07/28/theater/reviews/28inti.html?adxnnl=1&adxnnlx=1213326130-d8SmMAsp3LJFAIROU3N7Fg | work=The New York Times | first=Neil | last=Genzlinger | title='All This Intimacy' Shows a Louse's Animal Magnetism | date=July 28, 2006}}</ref>

Joseph's play ''The Leopard and the Fox'', concerning the overthrow of [[Zulfikar Ali Bhutto]], was produced Off-Off-Broadway in October to November 3, 2007 at the TBG Theatre at The Barrow Group.<ref>{{cite news| url=http://theater2.nytimes.com/2007/10/31/theater/reviews/31leop.html | work=The New York Times | first=Claudia | last=La Rocco | title=An Old Story, With No End in Sight | date=October 31, 2007}}</ref>

[[Second Stage Theatre]] presented the world premiere of Joseph's ''Animals Out of Paper'' which ran from July 14, 2008 (previews) to August 24, 2008 at the McGinn/Cazale Theatre.<ref>Gates, Anita. [https://www.nytimes.com/2008/08/05/theater/reviews/05anim.html?_r=0 "Works Well With Paper, Has Trouble With Life"]. New York Times, August 5, 2008</ref><ref>Hernandez, Ernio. [http://www.playbill.com/article/more-animals-out-of-paper-second-stage-extends-nyc-run-of-joseph-play-com-152068# "More 'Animals Out of Paper': Second Stage Extends NYC Run of Joseph Play"] Playbill, July 30, 2008</ref> ''Animals Out of Paper'' has been staged at [[Boise Contemporary Theater]] (2009),  at the [[Ensemble Theatre]] in [[Sydney, Australia]], [[San Francisco Playhouse]] in 2010,<ref>[http://www.sfplayhouse.org/] http://www.sfplayhouse.org/</ref> [[Portland, Maine]] (January 2011), and [[Fort Worth, Texas]] (March 2011). In September 2014, Joseph's ''Animals Out of Paper'' had its [[Los Angeles, California]] premiere at [[East West Players]] starring [[C.S. Lee]], Tess Lina and Kapil Talwalkar. ''Animals out of Paper'' was presented in [[Bangalore, India]] in September 2014 at Jagriti Theatre. It was produced by Jugaad Co.<ref>[https://www.facebook.com/JugaadCo]</ref> and got very good reviews. The production featured close to 75 pieces of origami.

Joseph's Pulitzer finalist production of ''[[Bengal Tiger at the Baghdad Zoo]]'', directed by [[Moisés Kaufman]], debuted at the [[Kirk Douglas Theatre]] in [[Culver City, California]] in May, 2009.<ref name=":0">[http://www.centertheatregroup.org/tickets/productiondetail.aspx?id=6746 "'Bengal Tiger at the Baghdad Zoo' Listing"] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080708042853/http://www.centertheatregroup.org/tickets/productiondetail.aspx?id=6746 |date=2008-07-08 }} Center Theatre Group, accessed March 31, 2016</ref> It ran at the [[Mark Taper Forum]] in [[Los Angeles]] April 14 to May 30, 2010 and premiered on [[Broadway theatre|Broadway]] at the [[Richard Rodgers Theatre]] in March 2011, with [[Robin Williams]] playing the titular character.<ref>Jones, Kenneth. [http://www.playbill.com/news/article/144877-Robin-Williams-Will-Prowl-Richard-Rodgers-Theatre-as-Bengal-Tiger-at-the-Baghdad-Zoo "Robin Williams Will Prowl Richard Rodgers Theatre as 'Bengal Tiger at the Baghdad Zoo'"] playbill.com, November 12, 2010</ref> In October 2013 it had its premiere in San Francisco at [[San Francisco Playhouse]] where it was very well received in the press.<ref>{{cite web|work=SF Gate|url=http://www.sfgate.com/performance/article/Bengal-Tiger-at-Baghdad-the-Zoo-Lessons-learned-4876294.php| title=Bengal Tiger at Baghdad the Zoo': Lessons learned| last=Hurwitz |first=Robert| date=2013-10-07| access-date=2014-05-14}}</ref>

''Gruesome Playground Injuries'', starring [[Selma Blair]] and Brad Fleischer, had its world premiere in October 2009 at the [[Alley Theatre]] in [[Houston, Texas]].<ref>[http://www.theatermania.com/houston/news/05-2009/frank-wildhorns-wonderland-to-play-alley-theatre-s_19316.html "Frank Wildhorns Wonderland to Play Alley Theatre"] theatermania.com, 2009</ref>  Another staging at the [[Woolly Mammoth Theatre Company]] in [[Washington, D.C.]] ran in May 2010.<ref>Bacalzo, Dan. [http://www.theatermania.com/washington-dc/news/05-2009/mike-daisey-danai-gurira-rajiv-joseph-charles-l-me_17993.html "Mike Daisey, Danai Gurira, Rajiv Joseph, Charles L. Mee, Bruce Norris Set for Woolly Mammoth Season"] theatermania.com, May 2, 2009</ref> Its [[Off-Broadway]] production opened January 2011 at the [[Second Stage Theatre]], starring [[Pablo Schreiber]] and [[Jennifer Carpenter]].

''The North Pool'' premiered at [[TheatreWorks (Silicon Valley)|TheatreWorks]] in [[Silicon Valley]] (California) in March–April 2011. It was called a "Psychological Thriller" by TheatreWorks.<ref>[http://www.theatreworks.org/shows/production/the-north-pool ''The North Pool''] theatreworks.org, accessed March 31, 2016</ref> The [[Barrington Stage Company]], [[The Berkshires]] presented ''The North Pool'' in July 2012.<ref name=barr>[http://barringtonstageco.org/press-release/rajiv-josephs-the-north-pool-stars-remi-sandri-and-babak-tafti-at-barrington-stage-july-26-aug-11/ "Rajiv Joseph's 'The North Pool' stars Remi Sandri and Babak Tafti at Barrington Stage, July 26-Aug 11"]
barringtonstageco.org, accessed March 31, 2016</ref> The played opened Off-Broadway at the Vineyard Theatre in February 2013 (previews), directed by Giovanna Sardelli.<ref>Jones, Kenneth; Hetrick, Adam. [http://www.playbill.com/article/rajiv-josephs-the-north-pool-opens-at-off-broadways-vineyard-march-6-com-203108# "Rajiv Joseph's 'The North Pool' Opens at Off-Broadway's Vineyard March 6"] Playbill, March 6, 2013</ref>

''The Monster at the Door'' debuted at Houston's [[Alley Theatre]] in May 2011.<ref>[http://www.alleytheatre.org/Alley/The_Medusa_Body_EN.asp?SnID=1873193205 ] {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110725000615/http://www.alleytheatre.org/Alley/The_Medusa_Body_EN.asp?SnID=1873193205 |date=July 25, 2011 }}</ref>

''The Lake Effect'' premiered at Chicago's [[Silk Road Rising]] on April 23, 2013.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.silkroadrising.org/live-theater/the-lake-effect |title=The Lake Effect - Live Theatre |publisher=Silk Road Rising |access-date=2013-03-29}}</ref> The production received the [[Joseph Jefferson Award]] for Best New Work.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://articles.chicagotribune.com/2013-11-05/entertainment/chi-jeff-winner-equity-2013_1_class-act-good-people-second-city-guide |title=2013 Equity Jeff Awards |publisher=Chicago Tribune |access-date=2013-12-12}}</ref> Its [[TheatreWorks (Silicon Valley)|TheatreWorks]] in [[Silicon Valley]] production opened March 2015, starring Adam Poss, Nilanjana Bose and Jason Bowen.

His play ''Guards at the Taj'' premiered Off-Broadway, produced by the Atlantic Theatre Company, in June to July 2015, directed by [[Amy Morton]].<ref>[https://atlantictheater.org/playevents/guardsatthetaj/ ''Guards at the Taj''] atlantictheater.org, accessed March 31, 2016</ref><ref>Stasio, Marilyn. [https://variety.com/2015/legit/reviews/guards-at-the-taj-review-1201517851/ "Off Broadway Review: 'Guards at the Taj' by Rajiv Joseph"] ''Variety'', June 11, 2015</ref> The play was nominated for the 2016 [[Lucille Lortel Awards]] for Outstanding Director, Amy Morton,<ref>[http://lortel.org/Archives/Production/5996 ''Guards at the Taj''] lortel.org, accessed March 31, 2016</ref> and won for Outstanding Play; Outstanding Scenic Design, Timothy R. Mackabee; Outstanding Lighting Design, David Weiner; and Outstanding Sound Design, Rob Milburn and Michael Bodeen.<ref>[http://www.playbill.com/article/futurity-and-guards-at-the-taj-earn-top-lucille-lortel-awards# "'FUTURITY', 'Guards at the Taj' and 'Robber Bridegroom' Earn Top Lucille Lortel Awards"] Playbill, May 1, 2016</ref> The play won the 2016 [[Obie Awards]] for Best New American Play and Performance ([[Omar Metwally]] and [[Arian Moayed]]).<ref>Gans, Andrew. [http://www.playbill.com/article/winners-announced-for-61st-annual-obie-awards# "Winners Announced for 61st Annual Obie Awards"] Playbill, May 23, 2016</ref> The play then received its West Coast premiere at the [[Geffen Playhouse]] in October to November 2015, directed by Giovanna Sardelli and starring [[Raffi Barsoumian]] and Ramiz Monsef (with [[Naren Weiss]] and Danvir Singh understudying), in the [[Audrey Skirball-Kenis]] Theater. <ref>[https://issuu.com/geffenplayhouse/docs/guards_at_the_taj_program-issuu ''Geffen Playhouse''] geffenplayhouse.org, accessed March 18, 2017</ref> The play was nominated for the 2017 [[Ovation Awards]] and won for Best Production of a Play (Large Theater).<ref>[http://www.playbill.com/article/guards-at-the-taj-and-the-boy-from-oz-win-2017-ovation-awards ''Ovation Awards''] playbill.com, accessed March 18, 2017</ref> 

Also in 2015, ''Mr. Wolf'' premiered at the [[South Coast Repertory]] directed by David Emmes, set design Nephelie Andonyadis, lighting design [[Lap Chi Chu]], sound design [[Cricket S. Myers]], costume design Leah Piehl, and production manager was Joshua Marchesi. The show starred [[John de Lancie]] (Mr. Wolf), [[Jon Tenney]] (Michael), Tessa Auberjonois (Hana), Emily James (Thesera), and Kwana Martinez (Julie).

In 2019, Joseph received a Wordsmith Duo commission from [https://playingonair.org/ Playing on Air] to write a short play for radio with Pulitzer Prize winner, [[Doug Wright]]. The episode ''[https://playingonair.org/new-releases/clean-slate Clean Slate]'' was released in Spring 2020 and it featured [[Jeremy Shamos]], [[Eisa Davis]], [[Amy Ryan]], and [[Eden Marryshow]] with direction by [[Mark Brokaw]].

===Film credits===
In December 2013, Joseph and fellow playwright [[Tarell Alvin McCraney]] were featured in [[Robert Levi]]'s [[PBS]] Film, ''Playwright: From Page to Stage'', which aired on [[Independent Lens]].

Rajiv co-wrote the script for ''Draft Day'' in 2014 and ''Army of One'' in 2016.<ref>[https://www.imdb.com/name/nm4396670/bio?ref_=nm_ov_bio_sm ] imdb.com</ref>
