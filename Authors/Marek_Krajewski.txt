'''Marek Krajewski''' (born 4 September 1966, in [[Wrocław]]) is a [[Poland|Polish]] [[crime writer]] and [[linguist]].<ref>"Nominowani do tytułu Ambasadora Wrocławia", ''Gazeta Wyborcza Wrocław'', 2007-05-09, retrieved 2008-07-07 [http://miasta.gazeta.pl/wroclaw/1,78999,4118409.html]</ref>

He is best known for his series of [[novel]]s set in pre-war [[Wrocław]] (which was, at the time, [[Breslau]]) with the policeman [[Eberhard Mock]] as the protagonist.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.dw.com/en/polish-crime-writer-uncovers-wroclaws-german-history/a-4594839 |title=Polish crime writer uncovers Wroclaw's German history |access-date=2018-09-14}}</ref> These novels have been translated into 18 languages: English, Spanish, French, German, Italian, Dutch, Hebrew, and Russian among others.<ref>{{cite web|title=Marek Krajewski|url=http://www.marek-krajewski.pl/index.php?cont=2&id=2&lang=1|agency=www.marek-krajewski.pl|accessdate=2017-11-25}}</ref>

==Life and career==
He was born on 4 September 1966 in [[Wrocław]], [[Polish People's Republic]]. In 1985, he graduated from the [[Juliusz Słowacki]] High School No. 9 in Wrocław. Between 1985-1991 he studied [[classics]] at the [[University of Wrocław]]. In 1992, he obtained an [[MA degree]] and in 1999, he received a [[doctoral degree]]. He worked as a librarian and later as an [[assistant professor]] and lecturer at the Institute of Classical Philology and Ancient Culture of the University of Wrocław. Since 2007, he has focused on pursuing his professional career as a writer of primarily [[crime fiction]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://culture.pl/en/artist/marek-krajewski |title=Marek Krajewski |access-date=2018-09-10}}</ref>

In 2005, he was the recipient of the [[Paszport Polityki]] Award presented by the ''[[Polityka]]'' magazine. His other awards include the High Calibre Award (Polish: ''Nagroda Wielkiego Kalibru''), the Witryna Award conferred by Polish booksellers and the Book Institute Award for best Polish [[crime novel]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://culture.pl/en/artist/marek-krajewski |title=Marek Krajewski |access-date=2018-09-10}}</ref> In 2015, he was awarded Silver [[Medal for Merit to Culture – Gloria Artis]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.mkidn.gov.pl/pages/posts/wreczenie-medali-bdquozasluzony-kulturze---gloria-artisrdquo-5874.php |title=Wręczenie medali "Zasłużony Kulturze - Gloria Artis" |access-date=2018-09-10}}</ref> In 2016, he also won the [[Georg Dehio Book Prize]] – a prize which recognizes authors who "in their literary, scholarly or public work, address the themes of the common culture and history of the German people and their Eastern neighbors at a high level and from a broad perspective."<ref name=restructure>{{cite web|url=http://www.kulturforum.info/de/topic/1006399.georg-dehio-buchpreis.html |title=Georg-Dehio-Buchpreis}}</ref>

In 2018, Polish filmmaker Patryk Vega directed a [[crime thriller]] ''[[The Plagues of Breslau]]'', which is loosely based on a series of crime novels by Krajewski.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.theguardian.com/film/2018/nov/29/plagi-breslau-review-patryk-vega |title=Plagi Breslau review |access-date=2020-04-30}}</ref>
