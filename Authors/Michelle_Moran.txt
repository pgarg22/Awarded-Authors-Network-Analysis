'''Michelle Moran''' (born August 11, 1980) is an American [[novelist]] known for her [[historical fiction]] writing.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Moran, Michelle|url=https://www.encyclopedia.com/arts/educational-magazines/moran-michelle|url-status=live|access-date=2021-07-05|publisher=[[Encyclopedia.com]]}}</ref> 

==Biography==
Michelle Moran was born in [[California]]'s [[San Fernando Valley]], August 11, 1980. She took an interest in writing from an early age, purchasing ''[[Writer's Market]]'' and submitting her stories and novellas to publishers from the time she was twelve. She majored in literature at [[Pomona College]]. Following a summer in Israel where she worked as a volunteer archaeologist, she earned an MA from the [[Claremont Graduate University]].

Her experiences at [[archaeological site]]s were what inspired her to write [[Historical novel|historical fiction]]. A [[public high school]] teacher for six years, Moran is currently a full-time writer living in California.{{Citation needed|date=July 2021}}

Moran is the author of the national bestselling historical fiction novels ''Nefertiti'', ''The Heretic Queen'', and ''Cleopatra's Daughter''.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.penguinrandomhouse.com/|title=Penguin Random House|website=PenguinRandomhouse.com}}</ref> Her fourth book, ''Madame Tussaud'', was optioned by Gaumont for a miniseries in 2011. Michael Hirst is writing the script.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.deadline.com/2011/09/gaumont-launches-u-s-based-division-run-by-katie-oconnell-sets-projects-from-bryan-fuller-and-michael-hirst/.|title = Gaumont Launches U.S.-Based Division Run by Katie O'Connell, Sets Projects from Bryan Fuller and Michael Hirst|date = September 12, 2011}}</ref> Her fifth book, "The Second Empress," explores the lives of the women in Napoleon Bonaparte's world.
Her sixth book, ''Rebel Queen'', is set in India and talks about the queen Lakshmibai of Jhansi. Her latest book is "Mata Hari's Last Dance."

==Bibliography==

* ''Nefertiti: A Novel'' (2007)<ref>hardback Berkley, 2005, {{ISBN|978-0-307-38174-3}}.</ref> This novel tells the story of [[Nefertiti]]'s sister [[Mutnodjmet]] and the events of Nefertiti's marriage to [[Amenhotep IV]] until the time of her death. According to WorldCat, the book is held in 984 libraries.<ref>{{Cite book|url=https://www.worldcat.org/title/nefertiti-a-novel/oclc/76820932&referer=brief_results|title=Nefertiti|first=Michelle|last=Moran|date=July 5, 2007|publisher=Crown Publishers|oclc=76820932|via=Open WorldCat}}</ref>
* ''The Heretic Queen'' (2008)<ref>paperback Crown; 1 edition, 2009, {{ISBN|978-0-307-38176-7}}.</ref> This novel tells the story of [[Nefertari]], wife of [[Ramesses II|Ramesses the Great]] and niece of the "heretic queen", [[Nefertiti]].<ref>{{Cite news|last=Alphonse|first=Lylah M.|date=2008-12-06|title=A novel of romance and intrigue in the court of ancient Egypt|work=[[The Boston Globe]]|url=http://archive.boston.com/ae/books/articles/2008/12/06/a_novel_of_romance_and_intrigue_in_the_court_of_ancient_egypt/|access-date=2021-07-05}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=Fiction Book Review: The Heretic Queen by Michelle Moran|url=https://www.publishersweekly.com/978-0-307-38175-0|url-status=live|access-date=2021-07-05|website=[[Publishers Weekly]]|language=en}}</ref>
* ''Cleopatra's Daughter'' (2009)<ref>hardcover Crown; 1 edition, 2009, {{ISBN|978-0-307-40912-6}}.</ref> This novel tells the story of [[Cleopatra Selene II]] and [[Alexander Helios]], the twin children of [[Cleopatra VII]] and [[Mark Antony]], in the aftermath of the great queen's death as well as living in Rome in the court of [[Augustus|Octavian]].
* ''Madame Tussaud: A Novel of the French Revolution'' (2011)<ref>http://www.michellemoran.com/about/index.html.</ref> This novel tells the story of [[Marie Tussaud|Marie Grosholtz]], the future "Madame Tussaud", and her rise to prominence as a [[Wax sculpture|wax artist]] during the [[French Revolution]].
* ''The Second Empress: A Novel of Napoleons Court'' (2012) This novel tells the story of the [[Marie Louise, Duchess of Parma]], Empress of France and [[Pauline Bonaparte]].
* ''Rebel Queen'' (2015) This novel tells the story of the [[Rani of Jhansi]].
* ''Mata Hari's Last Dance'' (2016) This novel tells the story of the [[Mata Hari]].
* “Maria” This novel tells the story of the real [[Maria von Trapp]], of the Von Trapp Family Singers.
