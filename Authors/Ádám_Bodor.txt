'''Ádám Bodor''' (born 22 February 1936 in [[Cluj]]) is a [[Hungary|Hungarian]] author of Transylvanian Hungarian origin.

== Life and writing ==
Bodor was born in Romania to a staunchly [[Anti-communism|anti-communist]] father and was himself an anti-communist. In his youth he believed in [[Transylvania]]n independence and overthrowing the Communist state.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.literaturfestival.com/archive/participants/authors/2014/adam-bodor?set_language=en|title=Ádám  Bodor  — internationales literaturfestival berlin|website=www.literaturfestival.com|accessdate=11 November 2017}}</ref> At seventeen he was arrested by the [[Securitate]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=VAI7O9prinMC&pg=PA185&dq=%22adam+bodor%22&cd=1#v=onepage&q=%22adam+bodor%22&f=false|title=The Columbia Literary History of Eastern Europe Since 1945|first=Harold B.|last=Segel|date=11 November 2017|publisher=Columbia University Press|accessdate=11 November 2017|via=Google Books}}</ref> After being freed he studied at a Calvinist seminary and began writing. After this he left Romania for Hungary and then spent some time in the West.<ref>[https://www.jstor.org/pss/40157368 World Literature Today, Volume 76, No. 2]</ref> Several of his works have been adapted to film.
