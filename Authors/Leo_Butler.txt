'''Leo Butler''' (born 1974 in [[Sheffield]]) is a British playwright. His plays have been staged, among others, by the [[Royal Court Theatre|Royal Court]], the [[Royal Shakespeare Company]] and the [[Almeida Theatre]].<ref>http://www.doollee.com/PlaywrightsB/butler-leo.html Profile at doolee.com</ref> His plays have been published by Bloomsbury [[A & C Black]].<ref name="A&C Black">{{cite web|url=http://www.acblack.com/Catalogue/details.asp?sku=1425305&dept_id=7&mscssid=R2N2M3SLV9A98LFF9RTXXRWK5EPL10RE|title=404|last=skyron.co.uk|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://archive.is/20120726231328/http://www.acblack.com/Catalogue/details.asp?sku=1425305&dept_id=7&mscssid=R2N2M3SLV9A98LFF9RTXXRWK5EPL10RE|archive-date=26 July 2012|df=dmy-all}}</ref> His 2001 play [[Redundant (play)|Redundant]] won the [[George Devine Award]].  Between 2005 and 2014 he was Playwriting Tutor for the [[Royal Court Theatre|Royal Court]] Young Writers Programme.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://matttrueman.co.uk/2013/08/teach-first-acts-duncan-macmillan-leo-butler.html|title=Teach First Acts – Duncan Macmillan & Leo Butler – Matt Trueman}}</ref>
