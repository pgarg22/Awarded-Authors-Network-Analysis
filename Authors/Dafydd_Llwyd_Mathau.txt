'''Dafydd Llwyd Mathau''' (fl. 1601–1629) was a 17th-century [[Welsh poetry|Welsh poet]] and strolling [[minstrel]]. It was thought by the bibliographer [[John Humphreys Davies]] (1871–1926) that he may have been from the [[Llangeitho]] area of [[Ceredigion]] (Cardiganshire).<ref>{{Cite DWB|id=s-DAVI-HUM-1871|title=John Humphreys Davies|access-date=24 May 2021}}</ref>

==Poetry==
Mathau's works, written in [[Welsh language|Welsh]], include poems in praise of several prominent Welsh families and a number of love poems. He is also known to have composed an [[awdl]]-style verse in 1611.<ref>{{Cite DWB |title=Dafydd Llwyd Mathau |id=s-DAFY-MAT-1601|last=Bowen |first=Geraint |accessdate=24 November 2016}}</ref>
