'''Patrick deWitt''' (born 1975) is a Canadian novelist and screenwriter. Born on [[Vancouver Island]], deWitt lives in [[Portland, Oregon]] and has acquired American citizenship. As of 2018, he has written four novels: ''Ablutions'' (2009), ''[[The Sisters Brothers]]'' (2011), ''[[Undermajordomo Minor]]'' (2015) and ''[[French Exit (novel)|French Exit]]'' (2018).

== Biography ==
DeWitt was born on [[Vancouver Island]] at [[Sidney, British Columbia]].<ref>{{Cite book |last=Neilson |first=Doug |url=https://www.thecanadianencyclopedia.ca/en/article/patrick-dewitt |title=Patrick deWitt |date=December 19, 2011 |publisher=[[Historica Canada]] |location=[[The Canadian Encyclopedia]] |access-date=April 14, 2019}}</ref> The second of three brothers, he spent his childhood moving back and forth across the west coast of North America. He credits his father, a carpenter, with giving him his "lifelong interest in literature."<ref name="Rustin">{{Cite news |last=Rustin |first=Susanna |date=November 14, 2015 |title=Patrick deWitt interview: 'Certain writers look down their noses at plot. I was one of them â€“ until I tried it' |work=[[The Guardian]] |url=https://www.theguardian.com/books/2015/nov/14/patrick-dewitt-interview-certain-writers-look-down-their-noses-at-plot-i-was-one-of-them |access-date=September 1, 2021}}</ref> DeWitt dropped out of high school to become a writer.<ref>{{Cite news |last=Jones |first=Shelley |date=September 10, 2015 |title=Coen brother of fiction Patrick deWitt & the comedy of modern characters in antique settings |work=[[Huck (magazine)|Huck]] |url=https://www.huckmag.com/art-and-culture/print/underdomomajorminor/ |access-date=September 1, 2021}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news |last=July |first=Ashly |date=November 14, 2018 |title=The Scotiabank Giller Prize finalists recall when they decided to become writers |work=[[Canadian Broadcasting Corporation|CBC]] |url=https://www.cbc.ca/books/gillerprize/the-scotiabank-giller-prize-finalists-recall-when-they-decided-to-become-writers-1.4892983 |access-date=September 1, 2021}}</ref> He moved to [[Los Angeles]], working at a bar.<ref>{{Cite news |last=Bishop |first=Ben |date=January 8, 2013 |title=Clear the Bar |work=[[Willamette Week]] |url=https://www.wweek.com/portland/article-20106-clear-the-bar.html |access-date=September 1, 2021}}</ref> He left Los Angeles to move back in with his parents in the [[Seattle]] area,<ref name="Robb">{{Cite web |last=Robb |first=Peter |date=October 22, 2018 |title=Ottawa Writers Festival: Patrick deWitt makes a French Exit |url=https://artsfile.ca/ottawa-writers-festival-patrick-dewitt-makes-a-french-exit/ |access-date=September 1, 2021 |website=artsfile.ca}}</ref> on [[Bainbridge Island, Washington|Bainbridge Island]].<ref name="Rustin" /> When he sold his first book ''Ablutions'' (2009), deWitt quit his job as a [[construction worker]] to become a writer, and moved to [[Portland, Oregon]].<ref name="Robb" />

Although born a Canadian citizen, deWitt was raised primarily in [[Southern California]], and later become a United States citizen.<ref>{{Cite news |last=Steger |first=Jason |author-link=Jason Steger |date=September 7, 2018 |title=Patrick deWitt: On the run with a mother and son and a cat with a human soul |work=[[The Sydney Morning Herald]] |url=https://www.smh.com.au/entertainment/books/patrick-dewitt-on-the-run-with-a-mother-and-son-and-a-cat-with-a-human-soul-20180904-h14whh.html |access-date=September 1, 2021}}</ref> He married screenwriter Leslie Napoles,<ref>{{Cite news |last=Baker |first=Jeff |date=February 12, 2012 |title=Patrick deWitt's hard work pays off with two acclaimed novels and a screenplay |work=[[The Oregonian]] |url=https://www.oregonlive.com/books/2012/02/write_i_write_patrick_dewitts.html |access-date=September 1, 2021}}</ref> an American,<ref>{{Cite news |last=Deahl |first=Rachel |date=September 23, 2011 |title=Portland Author Finds Literary Love on the International Prize Circuit |work=[[Publishers Weekly]] |url=https://www.publishersweekly.com/pw/by-topic/industry-news/awards-and-prizes/article/48827-portland-author-finds-literary-love-on-the-international-prize-circuit.html |access-date=September 1, 2021}}</ref> with whom he has a son named Gustavo.<ref>{{Cite news |last=Bethune |first=Brian |date=October 18, 2011 |title=The new Canadian literary odd couple |work=[[Maclean's]] |url=https://www.macleans.ca/culture/books/the-new-vanadian-literary-odd-couple/ |access-date=September 1, 2021}}</ref> He is separated from his wife, but they are amicable and share the care of their son.<ref name="Rustin" />

==Career==
His first book, ''Ablutions: Notes for a Novel'' (2009), was named a ''New York Times'' Editors' Choice book. His second, ''[[The Sisters Brothers]]'' (2011), was shortlisted for the 2011 [[Man Booker Prize]], the 2011 [[Scotiabank Giller Prize]],<ref name="globe">{{Cite news |last=Barber |first=John |date=October 4, 2011 |title=Generation Giller: New young writers dominate Canada's richest fiction prize |work=[[The Globe and Mail]] |url=https://www.theglobeandmail.com/arts/books-and-media/generation-giller-new-young-writers-dominate-canadas-richest-fiction-prize/article555863/ |access-date=September 1, 2021}}</ref> the [[Rogers Writers' Trust Fiction Prize]],<ref>{{Cite news |last=Barber |first=John |date=September 28, 2011 |title=Booker nominees Edugyan, deWitt make shortlist for Writers' Trust prize |work=[[The Globe and Mail]] |url=https://www.theglobeandmail.com/arts/books-and-media/booker-nominees-edugyan-dewitt-make-shortlist-for-writers-trust-prize/article555763/ |access-date=September 1, 2021}}</ref> and the 2011 [[Governor General's Award for English-language fiction]].<ref>{{Cite news |last=Barber |first=John |date=October 11, 2011 |title=Edugyan and deWitt add GGs to long list of nominations |work=[[The Globe and Mail]] |url=https://www.theglobeandmail.com/arts/books-and-media/edugyan-and-dewitt-add-ggs-to-long-list-of-nominations/article556781/ |access-date=September 1, 2021}}</ref> He was one of two Canadian writers, alongside [[Esi Edugyan]], to make all four award lists in 2011.<ref name="globe" /> On November 1, 2011, he was announced as the winner of the Rogers Prize,<ref>{{Cite news |last=Barber |first=John |date=November 1, 2011 |title=Patrick deWitt's 'The Sisters Brothers' wins Writers' Trust Prize |work=[[The Globe and Mail]] |url=https://www.theglobeandmail.com/arts/books-and-media/patrick-dewitts-the-sisters-brothers-wins-writers-trust-prize/article4248747/ |access-date=September 1, 2021}}</ref> and on November 15, 2011, he was announced as the winner of Canada's 2011 [[Governor General's Award for English-language fiction]].<ref>{{Cite news |last=Medley |first=Mark |date=November 15, 2011 |title=Patrick deWitt wins Governor General's Literary Award for The Sisters Brothers |work=[[National Post]] |url=https://nationalpost.com/afterword/patrick-dewitt-wins-governor-generals-literary-award-for-the-sisters-brothers |access-date=September 1, 2021}}</ref> On April 26, 2012, the novel won the 2012 [[Stephen Leacock Award]]. Alongside Edugyan, ''The Sisters Brothers'' was also a shortlisted nominee for the 2012 [[Walter Scott Prize]] for historical fiction.<ref>{{Cite news |date=April 4, 2012 |title=Edugyan and deWitt face off in yet another literary contest |work=[[The Globe and Mail]] |url=https://www.theglobeandmail.com/arts/books-and-media/edugyan-and-dewitt-face-off-in-yet-another-literary-contest/article4097759/ |access-date=September 1, 2021}}</ref> ''The Sisters Brothers'' was adapted as a [[The Sisters Brothers (film)|film of the same name]] released in 2018.

His third novel, ''[[Undermajordomo Minor]]'', was published in 2015.<ref>{{Cite magazine |last=Beattie |first=Steven W. |date=September 2015 |title=Patrick deWitt: fame, horses, and his new novel |url=http://www.quillandquire.com/authors/patrick-dewitt-fame-horses-and-his-new-novel/ |magazine=[[Quill & Quire]] |access-date=September 1, 2021}}</ref> The novel was longlisted for the 2015 [[Scotiabank Giller Prize]].<ref>{{Cite web |date=September 9, 2015 |title=The Scotiabank Giller Prize Presents its 2015 Longlist |url=https://scotiabankgillerprize.ca/scotiabank-giller-prize-2015-longlist/ |access-date=September 1, 2021 |website=[[Giller Prize|Scotiabank Giller Prize]]}}</ref>

DeWitt's most recent novel, ''[[French Exit (novel)|French Exit]]'', was published in August 2018 by Ecco Press, an imprint of HarperCollins.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.harpercollins.com/9780062864406/french-exit/|title=French Exit - Patrick deWitt|work=HarperCollins Publishers: World-Leading Book Publisher|access-date=June 4, 2018|language=en-US}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://bookmarks.reviews/reviews/french-exit/|title=Book Marks reviews of French Exit by Patrick DeWitt|website=bookmarks.reviews|language=en-US|access-date=September 1, 2018}}</ref> The book was named as a shortlisted finalist for the 2018 Giller Prize.<ref>{{Cite news |last=Dundas |first=Deborah |date=October 1, 2018 |title=Esi Edugyan, Patrick deWitt among finalists for $100,000 Giller Prize |work=[[Toronto Star]] |url=https://www.thestar.com/entertainment/books/2018/10/01/esi-edugyan-patrick-dewitt-among-finalists-for-100000-giller-prize.html |access-date=September 1, 2021}}</ref> He wrote the screenplay for the 2020 [[French Exit (2020 film)|film of the same name]].<ref>{{Cite news |last=Hornaday |first=Ann |author-link=Ann Hornaday |date=March 31, 2021 |title=Michelle Pfeiffer reigns over flawed 'French Exit' with mesmerizing hauteur |work=[[The Washington Post]] |url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/goingoutguide/movies/french-exit-movie-review/2021/03/30/1ff923a8-90b6-11eb-a74e-1f4cf89fd948_story.html |access-date=September 1, 2021}}</ref>
