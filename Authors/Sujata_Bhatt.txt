'''Sujata Bhatt''' (born 6 May 1956) is an Indian poet.

==Life and career==
Sujata Bhatt was born in [[Ahmedabad]], [[Gujarat]] and brought up in [[Pune]] until 1968, when she immigrated to [[United States]] with her family. She has an [[Master of Fine Arts|MFA]] from the [[University of Iowa]], and for a time was writer-in-residence at the [[University of Victoria]], [[Canada]]. She received the [[Commonwealth Poetry Prize]] ([[Asia]]) and [[Alice Hunt Bartlett Prize]] for her first collection ''Brunizem'' in 1987.<ref Name="PA">[http://www.poetryarchive.org/poetryarchive/singlePoet.do?poetId=1500 Profile at the Poetry Archive]</ref> She received a [[Cholmondeley Award]] in 1991 and Italian Tratti Poetry Prize in 2000.<ref Name="PA"/> She has translated [[Gujarati language|Gujarati]] poetry into [[English language|English]] for the ''Penguin Anthology of Contemporary Indian Women Poets''. Combining [[Gujarati language|Gujarati]] and [[English language|English]], Bhatt writes "Indian-English rather than Anglo-Indian poetry."<ref name="Schmidt, Michael p860">Schmidt, Michael: ''Lives of Poets'', p860. Weidenfeld & Nicolson, 1998.</ref> [[Michael Schmidt (poet)]] observed that her "free verse is fast-moving, urgent with narratives, softly spoken.<ref name="Schmidt, Michael p860" /> Bhatt lives in [[Bremen]], [[Germany]] with her husband, the [[Germans|German]] [[writer]] Michael Augustin, and daughter.<ref name="PA" />
