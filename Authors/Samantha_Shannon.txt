'''Samantha Shannon''' (born 8 November 1991) is a British writer of [[Utopian and dystopian fiction|dystopian]], fantasy and paranormal fiction.<ref name="RTI">{{cite web|title=SPOILED: SAMANTHA SHANNON'S THE BONE SEASON|url=http://www.rtbookreviews.com/rt-daily-blog/spoiled-samantha-shannons-bone-season|publisher=RT Book Reviews|accessdate=13 November 2012}}</ref> ''[[The Bone Season]]'', her debut novel and the first of a planned seven-book series, was published in 2013.<ref name=":0" />

==Life and career==
Shannon was born in [[Hammersmith, London ]] in November 1991<ref name="auto"/> and grew up in [[Ruislip]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Samantha Shannon|url=https://sshannonauthor.tumblr.com/post/166874420783/samantha-i-just-saw-on-your-instagram-that-you|publisher=Tumblr|accessdate=19 August 2020}}</ref> She first began writing at the age of fifteen, when she wrote her first novel, ''Aurora'', which remains unpublished.<ref>{{cite news|last=Brooks|first=Richard|title=New JK Rowling in seventh heaven|url=http://www.thesundaytimes.co.uk/sto/news/uk_news/Arts/article1032453.ece|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20161104020349/https://www.thesundaytimes.co.uk/sto/news/uk_news/Arts/article1032453.ece|archive-date=4 November 2016|url-status=dead|newspaper=The Sunday Times|date=6 May 2012}}</ref> Shannon studied English language and literature at [[St Anne's College, Oxford]],<ref name="RTI" /><ref name="oxfordstudent">{{cite web|title=Student author sells film rights|url=http://oxfordstudent.com/2012/10/20/student-author-sells-film-rights/|publisher=Oxford Student|accessdate=13 November 2012}}</ref> and graduated in 2013.<ref name="Kellaway">Kate Kellaway [https://www.theguardian.com/books/2013/aug/11/samantha-shannon-interview-bone-season "Samantha Shannon: 'Writing was a drug I couldn't stop taking'"], ''The Observer'', 11 August 2013</ref>

In 2012 she signed a six-figure book deal with [[Bloomsbury Publishing]], who bid following the [[London Book Fair]], to publish the first three books in a seven-book series, beginning with ''[[The Bone Season]]''.<ref name=":0">{{cite web|last=Williams|first=Charlotte|title=Seven-book fantasy series for Bloomsbury|url=http://www.thebookseller.com/news/seven-book-fantasy-series-bloomsbury.html|publisher=The Bookseller|accessdate=13 November 2012}}</ref> Set in 2059, the events of the novel take place in a London governed by a "security force" called Scion and an Oxford which has become a huge prison.<ref name="Kellaway"/> Following this novel's publication, Shannon was compared favorably to [[J.K. Rowling]].<ref>{{Cite web|last=Hjelmgaard|first=Kim|title=Is Samantha Shannon the next J.K. Rowling?|url=https://www.usatoday.com/story/life/books/2013/08/19/samantha-shannon-bone-season/2607907/|access-date=2021-10-14|website=USA TODAY|language=en-US}}</ref> Film rights to ''The Bone Season'' were optioned by [[Andy Serkis|Andy Serkis's]] film company, [[The Imaginarium Studios]], in November 2012.<ref name="oxfordstudent" />

''The Priory of the Orange Tree'', a standalone [[fantasy novel]] by Shannon, was published in 2019 by [[Bloomsbury Publishing]].<ref>{{cite magazine|last1=Trombetta|first1=Sadie|date=27 February 2019|title='The Priory Of The Orange Tree' Is An Epic Feminist Fantasy Perfect For Fans Of 'Game Of Thrones'|url=https://www.bustle.com/p/the-priory-of-the-orange-tree-is-epic-feminist-fantasy-perfect-for-fans-of-game-of-thrones-16164155|magazine=[[Bustle (magazine)|Bustle]]|access-date=31 May 2019}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Shannon said that it is difficult for her to find a "firm label" for her sexuality, but that she identifies as "[[queer]]" and is attracted to women.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://sshannonauthor.tumblr.com/post/652998967680974848/in-honor-of-pride-would-you-be-comfortable</ref>
