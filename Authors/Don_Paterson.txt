'''Donald Paterson''' {{postnominals|country=GBR|size=100%|OBE|FRSE|FRSL}} (born 1963) is a Scottish poet, writer and musician.

==Background==
Don Paterson was born in Dundee, Scotland in 1963.<ref name="poetry foundation">{{Cite web|url=https://www.poetryfoundation.org/poets/don-paterson|title=Don Paterson|first=Poetry|last=Foundation|date=29 July 2020|website=Poetryfoundation.org|access-date=29 July 2020}}</ref> He won an [[Eric Gregory Award]] in 1990 and his poem ''A Private Bottling'' won the Arvon Foundation International Poetry Competition in 1993.<ref name="Scottish Poetry Library">{{Cite web|url=https://www.scottishpoetrylibrary.org.uk/poet/don-paterson/|title=Don Paterson &#124; Poet|website=Scottishpeotrylibrary.org.uk|access-date=29 July 2020}}</ref> He was included on the list of 20 poets chosen for the [[Poetry Society]]'s 'New Generation Poets' promotion in 1994.<ref name="Scottish Poetry Library"/> In 2002 he was awarded a [[Scottish Arts Council]] Creative Scotland Award.<ref name="poetry foundation"/>

His first collection of poetry, ''Nil Nil'' (1993), won the [[Forward Poetry Prize]] for Best First Collection. ''God's Gift to Women'' (1997) won the [[T. S. Eliot Prize]] and the [[Geoffrey Faber Memorial Prize]]. ''The Eyes'', adaptations of the work of Spanish poet [[Antonio Machado]] (1875â€“1939), was published in 1999. He is also editor of ''101 Sonnets: From [[Shakespeare]] to [[Heaney]]'' (1999) and of ''Last Words: New Poetry for the New Century'' (1999) with [[Jo Shapcott]]. His collection of poems, ''[[Landing Light (book)|Landing Light]]'' (2003), won both the 2003 [[T. S. Eliot Prize]] and the [[2003 Whitbread Awards|2003 Whitbread Poetry Award]].<ref name=Picador>{{Cite web|url=https://www.panmacmillan.com/picador|title=Picador|website=Panmacmillan.com|access-date=29 July 2020}}</ref> He has also published three collections of aphorisms, ''The Book of Shadows'' (2004), ''The Blind Eye'' (2007) and ''Best Thought, Worst Thought'' (2008). ''Orpheus'', his version of [[Rilke]]'s ''Die Sonette an Orpheus'', was published in 2006.<ref name="poetry foundation" />

Don Paterson teaches in the school of English at the [[University of St Andrews]] and is poetry editor for the London publishers [[Picador (imprint)|Picador]].<ref name=Picador/> An accomplished jazz guitarist, he works solo and for ten years ran the jazz-folk ensemble, Lammas, with [[Tim Garland]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://poetryarchive.org/poet/don-paterson/|title=Don Paterson|website=Poetry Archive|access-date=29 July 2020}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.heraldscotland.com/news/14276417.acclaimed-poet-reveals-he-is-writing-play-about-jimmy-savile/|title=Acclaimed poet reveals he is writing play about Jimmy Savile|website=HeraldScotland.com|access-date=29 July 2020}}</ref>

In 2012, Paterson wrote an open letter in [[The Herald (Glasgow)|The Herald]] criticising Scotland's arts funding council [[Creative Scotland]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.heraldscotland.com/arts_ents/13073086.a-post-creative-scotland/|title=A post-Creative Scotland|website=HeraldScotland.com|access-date=29 July 2020}}</ref>

In 2012-13, he was the Weidenfeld Visiting Professor of European Comparative Literature in [[St Anne's College, Oxford]].<ref>[http://www.st-annes.ox.ac.uk/about/weidenfeld-visiting-professorship-in-comparative-european-literature] {{dead link|date=July 2020}}</ref>

==Honours and awards==
He was appointed [[Officer of the Order of the British Empire]] (OBE) in the [[2008 Birthday Honours]].<ref>{{London Gazette|issue=58729 |date=14 June 2008 |page=12 |supp=y }}</ref> He was awarded the [[Queen's Gold Medal for Poetry]] in the [[2010 New Year Honours]].<ref>[http://entertainment.timesonline.co.uk/tol/arts_and_entertainment/books/poetry/article6972944.ece] {{dead link|date=July 2020}}</ref> In 2015 Paterson was elected a Fellow of the [[Royal Society of Edinburgh]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.rse.org.uk/fellow/don-paterson/|title=Professor Don Paterson OBE FRSE |publisher=The Royal Society of Edinburgh|access-date=12 February 2018|language=en-GB}}</ref>
