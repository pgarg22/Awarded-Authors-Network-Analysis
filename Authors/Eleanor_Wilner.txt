{{short description|American poet and editor (born 1937-2021)}}
'''Eleanor Rand Wilner''' (born 1937) is an American [[poet]] and [[editing|editor]].

==Life==
Wilner obtained her bachelor's  from [[Goucher College]] and her Ph.D. from [[Johns Hopkins University]].<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.goucher.edu/x13565.xml |title=Archived copy |access-date=2009-12-10 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20091025080447/http://www.goucher.edu/x13565.xml |archive-date=2009-10-25 |url-status=dead }}</ref> Her graduate dissertation concerned the topic of imagination and was later published as ''Gathering the Winds: Visionary Imagination and Radical Transformation of Self and Society'' (1975).<ref>{{Cite web|title = Eleanor Wilner : The Poetry Foundation|url = http://www.poetryfoundation.org/bio/eleanor-wilner|website = www.poetryfoundation.org|access-date = 2016-01-15}}</ref>

She was editor of ''The American Poetry Review'' and she is Advisory Editor of ''Calyx''.<ref>http://www.poets.org/poet.php/prmPID/274</ref>
She has taught at the [[University of Chicago]], [[Northwestern University]], and [[Smith College]]. 
She is on the faculty of the MFA Program for Writers at [[Warren Wilson College]],<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.warren-wilson.edu/academics/catalog/catalog.php?contents=yes&name=staff_faculty&id=mfa_staff |title=Archived copy |access-date=2009-12-10 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110518235237/http://www.warren-wilson.edu/academics/catalog/catalog.php?contents=yes&name=staff_faculty&id=mfa_staff |archive-date=2011-05-18 |url-status=dead }}</ref> and lives in [[Philadelphia]].

In 2019, she won the [[Robert Frost Medal]], the Poetry Society of America's award for a "distinguished lifetime service to American poetry."

She died in September 28,2021
She has been active in civil rights and peace movements.
