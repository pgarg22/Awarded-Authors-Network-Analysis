'''Florence Henrietta Darwin, Lady Darwin''' (''née'' '''Fisher''', previously Maitland; 31 January 1864{{snd}}5 March 1920), was an English [[playwright]].

==Early life==
'''Florence Henrietta Fisher''' was born in [[Kensington]], London, to [[Herbert William Fisher]] and his wife Mary Louisa Jackson (1841–1916). Florence was sister to [[H. A. L. Fisher|Herbert Fisher]] and Adeline Maria Fisher, first wife of [[Ralph Vaughan Williams]]. She was a first cousin of [[Virginia Woolf]], her siblings [[Vanessa Bell]] and [[Adrian Stephen]] and half-siblings [[George Herbert Duckworth]] and [[Gerald Duckworth]] through her aunt Julia and of [[William Wyamar Vaughan]] through her aunt Adeline.{{citation needed|date=January 2020}} 

As a child, she posed for a series of photographic portraits by her great aunt, [[Julia Margaret Cameron]], including ''A Study of [[St John the Baptist]]''.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.vam.ac.uk/content/articles/j/julia-margaret-cameron-collection-highlights/ |title=Archived copy |access-date=2013-03-10 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130306035125/http://www.vam.ac.uk/content/articles/j/julia-margaret-cameron-collection-highlights/ |archive-date=2013-03-06 |url-status=dead }}</ref>

In ''F. W. Maitland: a Child's-Eye View'', her daughter Ermengard mentions Florence's "menagerie of animals, her hours of violin playing, her feeding of tramps and gypsies, her photography and pony-driving, her story-telling and play-writing," and her liking for [[William Thackeray|Thackeray]].{{citation needed|date=January 2020}}

==Personal life==

In 1886 she married [[Frederic William Maitland]] (1850–1906),<ref>'E.M.', Biographical Essay in Florence Henrietta Darwin, [https://archive.org/details/sixplays00darwiala/page/n7/mode/2up ''Six Plays''] (ed. Cecil Sharp), (W. Heffer & Sons: 1921), pp. ix-xi.</ref> with whom she had two daughters, Ermengard (1888–1968) and [[Fredegond Shove|Fredegond]] (1889–1949).  On 3 March 1913, she became the third wife of [[Sir Francis Darwin]], first cousin once removed (twice over) of her sister's husband Ralph Vaughan Williams, the [[Josiah Wedgwood II|second Josiah Wedgwood]] and his wife Elizabeth being their shared ancestor on one side and [[Robert Darwin]] and his wife Susannah on the other.{{citation needed|date=January 2020}}

She died on 5 March 1920<ref>Deaths. ''[[The Times]]'', Wednesday, Mar 10, 1920; p. 1; Issue 42355; col A</ref> and was buried at the [[Ascension Parish Burial Ground, Cambridge]], along with her second husband and his daughter [[Frances Cornford]].{{citation needed|date=January 2020}}

==Works==

In 1921, family friend [[Cecil Sharp]] posthumously published the compilation ''Six Plays'', which included ''The New Year'', ''The Seeds of Love'', ''Princess Royal'', ''My Man John'', ''[[Bushes and Briars]]'' and ''The Lover's Tasks''. A book entitled ''Green Broom'' was published in 1923.{{citation needed|date=January 2020}}
