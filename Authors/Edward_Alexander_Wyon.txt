'''Edward Alexander Wyon''' (1842; London – 1872; [[Hastings]]) was a London architect and poet, descended from the [[Wyon family]] of engravers. His only known building is St John the Evangelist Church in [[Hollington, Hastings]] in East Sussex.<ref name=SussexChurches-EAWyon /> His posthumous publication, ''A Memorial Volume of Poems'' (1874), continues to be reprinted in the 21st century. He died in Hastings prior to his thirtieth birthday.

== Biography ==
[[File:Edward A Wyon 028.jpg|thumb|right|St John the Evangelist, Hollington<br />in 2014]]

=== Ancestry ===
His father was Edward William Wyon (1811–1885), a sculptor born in [[Southwark Christchurch|Christchurch]], Surrey,<ref name="Census1861">Census 1861: RG/9/102/138; p.37.</ref> who belonged to the Wyon family of engravers and medallists.<ref name=SussexChurches-EAWyon /> Edward Alexander's grandfather was [[Thomas Wyon the elder]] (1792–1817), and his uncles were [[Thomas Wyon the younger]], and [[Benjamin Wyon]] (1802–1858).<ref name=Forrer-MedallistBiogs-1900>{{cite book|last=Forrer|first=Leonard|title=Biographical dictionary of medallists : coin, gem, and sealengravers, mint-masters, &c., ancient and modern, with references to their works B.C. 500-A.D: vol.6|year=1900|url=http://www.ebooksread.com/authors-eng/l-leonard-forrer/biographical-dictionary-of-medallists--coin-gem-and-sealengravers-mint-maste-ala/page-47-biographical-dictionary-of-medallists--coin-gem-and-sealengravers-mint-maste-ala.shtml|accessdate=20 March 2011|page=47}}</ref> Edward Alexander's mother was Elizabeth Smyth,<ref name=Marr-EWWyon-ESmyth-1841 /> (born [[St James's]] 1820; died [[Chipping Barnet|Barnet]] 1890).<ref name="Census1871" /><ref name="Death-ElizWyon-1890">GRO index: death; Elizabeth Wyon; age 70; Barnet; Sept 1890; vol.3a; p.131. {{cite web|url=http://www.freebmd.org.uk/cgi/information.pl?cite=G%2FKZvTjwbA3mZ1fHxrWLkg&scan=1|title=Index entry|accessdate=20 March 2011|work=FreeBMD|publisher=ONS}}</ref> His parents were married in 1841, at Marylebone, London.<ref name=Marr-EWWyon-ESmyth-1841>GRO index: Marriage Edward William Wyon; Marylebone; Dec 1841; vol.1; p.170. {{cite web|url=http://www.freebmd.org.uk/cgi/information.pl?cite=EwxmKFcj47U38eKZABnbsg&scan=1|title=Index entry|accessdate=20 March 2011|work=FreeBMD|publisher=ONS}}</ref>

=== After his death ===
[[File:St John the Evangelist, Hollington, Hastings 1900.jpg|thumb|right|St John the Evangelist church around 1900, showing finial on tower]]
Edward Alexander Wyon is credited with the authorship of the posthumous book, ''A Memorial Volume of Poems'' (1874).<ref name=SussexChurches-EAWyon /> His sister Florence married in 1879 at Marylebone, to Reverend Charles James Goody (born 1835).<ref name="Marriage-FERWyon-1879">GRO index: Marriage; Florence Elizabeth Riddel Wyon; Mar 1879; Marylebone; vol.1a; 718. {{cite web|url=http://www.freebmd.org.uk/cgi/information.pl?cite=%2Fnz6%2FU%2F8b07JADWyh5ypug&scan=1|title=Index entry|accessdate=20 March 2011|work=FreeBMD|publisher=ONS}}</ref> Their son, Charles Edward Goody (born 1881) and Edward Alexander's nephew, was an Oxford undergraduate in 1901.<ref name="Census1901-FlorenceERGoody">Census 1901: RG13/1233/45; p.23.</ref><ref name="Birth-CEGoody-1881">GRO index: birth; Charles Edward Goody; Barnet; Dec 1881; vol.3a; p.206. {{cite web|url=http://www.freebmd.org.uk/cgi/information.pl?cite=g3U5Dn8gIkE5bWEObJ%2BXZw&scan=1|title=Index entry|accessdate=20 March 2011|work=FreeBMD|publisher=ONS}}</ref> His sister Jessie Mary Ann Wyon never married; she died about the same time as her mother in 1890 in Barnet, aged 35 years.<ref name="Death=JMAWyon-1890">GRO index: death; Jessie Mary Ann Wyon age 35; Barnet; Sept 1890; vol.3a; p.130. {{cite web|url=http://www.freebmd.org.uk/cgi/information.pl?cite=aKwKpG2LaAvvsRgdFiz%2FIw&scan=1|title=Index entry|accessdate=20 March 2011|work=FreeBMD|publisher=ONS}}</ref>

== Professional life and works ==
He practised from an office in [[Duke Street, Marylebone|Duke Street]], London.<ref name=SussexChurches-EAWyon /><ref>Adelphi (KD/L).</ref> His only known work is [[:File:St John the Evangelist's Church, Hollington, Hastings.jpg|St John the Evangelist Church]], [[Hollington, Hastings|Hollington]], constructed between 1865 and 1868 by engineer [[John Howell & Son]]<ref name=HastChron-obit-JohnSnr-1893>{{cite web|title=Hastings Chronicle|url=http://www.hastingschronicle.com/1893/12/08/death-of-john-howell/|work=Death of John Howell|accessdate=13 March 2011|date=8 December 1893|url-status=dead|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20110711145632/http://www.hastingschronicle.com/1893/12/08/death-of-john-howell/|archivedate=11 July 2011|df=dmy-all}}</ref> It is built with [[Bath Stone]] dressings; it has one [[aisle]] on the south side and a south-east tower.<ref name=Roughwood-StJohnEvangelist>{{cite web|title=Roughwood|url=http://www.roughwood.net/ChurchAlbum/EastSussex/Hollington/HollingtonStJohnEvangelist2004.htm|work=St John the Evangelist, Hollington, St Leonards, East Sussex – 5th November 2004|accessdate=20 March 2011}}</ref> There was a finial on the slimmer, round tower; removed in the 20th century. It was consecrated by [[Ashurst Gilbert]], [[Bishop of Chichester]], in April 1868.<ref name=HastChron-Consecration-1868>{{cite web|title=Hastings Chronicle|url=http://www.hastingschronicle.com/1868/04/29/st-johns-consecrated/|work=St Johns consecrated|accessdate=20 March 2011|date=29 April 1868|url-status=dead|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20110711150320/http://www.hastingschronicle.com/1868/04/29/st-johns-consecrated/|archivedate=11 July 2011|df=dmy-all}}</ref> This church is not [[Listed buildings in the United Kingdom|listed]].
