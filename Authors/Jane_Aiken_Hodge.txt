==Life==
Born near [[Cambridge, Massachusetts]], the second child of Pulitzer prize-winning poet [[Conrad Aiken]] and his first wife, the writer Jessie McDonald. Jane Hodge was 3 years old when her family moved to Great Britain, settling in [[Rye, East Sussex]] where her younger sister, [[Joan Aiken|Joan]], who would become a novelist and a children's writer, was born. Their parents' marriage was dissolved in 1929.

From 1935, Jane Hodge read English at [[Somerville College]], [[Oxford University]], and in 1938 she took a second degree in English at [[Radcliffe College]], USA, her mother's alma mater. She was a civil servant for a time, and also worked for ''[[Time (magazine)|Time]]'' magazine, before returning to the UK in 1947.

In 1948 she married [[Alan Hodge]], an English historian and journalist.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.findagrave.com/memorial/191855720/jane-hodge|title = Jane Aiken Hodge (1917-2009) - Find a Grave}}</ref> Hodge had divorced his first wife Beryl to allow her to marry the poet [[Robert Graves]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://mediawiki.feverous.co.uk/index.php/Alan_Hodge#First_marriage_Alan_Hodge_section_2|title = Alan Hodge - Wikipedia for FEVERv2}}</ref>

Her works of fiction include historical novels and contemporary detective novels. In 1972 she renounced her United States citizenship and became a British subject.{{citation needed|date=May 2012}}

For many years a believer in the right of people to end their own lives, Hodge chose to end her own life by means of an overdose in June 2009.<ref name=times/> The Times obituary (pub. July 25, 2009) stated that she left "a letter expressing her deep distress that she had felt unable to discuss her plans with her daughters without risking making them accessories.".<ref name=times>[http://www.timesonline.co.uk/tol/comment/obituaries/article6726655.ece "Jane Aiken Hodge: prolific author of romance and suspense novels"], ''[[The Times]]'', 25 July 2009. {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100524160856/http://www.timesonline.co.uk/tol/comment/obituaries/article6726655.ece |date=May 24, 2010 }}</ref> She died at home in [[Lewes]], [[Sussex]] on June 17, 2009 and was survived by two daughters and two granddaughters.
