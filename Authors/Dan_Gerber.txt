'''Dan Gerber''' (born 1940<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.poetryfoundation.org/archive/poet.html?id=2470 |title=Dan Gerber : The Poetry Foundation : Find Poems and Poets. Discover Poetry |publisher=The Poetry Foundation |date= |accessdate=2009-11-14}}</ref> in western [[Michigan]], [[United States]]) is an [[United States|American]] [[poet]].

== Life and work ==
Dan Gerber Jr. is the son of [[Daniel Frank Gerber]], founder of the [[Gerber Products Company]]. At age 26 he was made a Director on the Board of the company, and resigned when he turned 31. <ref> Muson, Howard. The Reluctant Successor. ''Family Business Magazine'' October, 1990. [https://www.lgassoc.com/the-reluctant-successor/] Accessed September 13, 2017 </ref> 

Gerber attended the [[The Leelanau School|Leelanau School]] (class of 1958) and then received a [[Bachelor of Arts]] degree in [[English studies|English]] from [[Michigan State University]] in 1962. He was the co-founder, with [[Jim Harrison]], of the literary magazine ''[[Sumac (magazine)|Sumac]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://msupress.msu.edu/bookTemplate.php?bookID=2268 |title=Michigan State University Press &#124; Sumac Reader, a Limited Numbered Edition, Signed by Jim Harrison, Dan Gerber, and Joseph Bednarik &#124; Joseph Bednarik |publisher=Msupress.msu.edu |accessdate=2009-11-14 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20100213011853/http://msupress.msu.edu/bookTemplate.php?bookID=2268 |archivedate=2010-02-13 }}</ref>
As part of his journalist profession, Gerber made extensive travels, primarily to [[Africa]]. He has served as writer-in-residence at [[Michigan State University]] and [[Grand Valley State University]].<ref name="msu">{{cite web|url=http://www.lib.msu.edu/services/spec_coll/writer/MWCDanGerber.html |title=Michigan Writers Collection: Dan Gerber |publisher=Lib.msu.edu |date=2003-01-02 |accessdate=2009-11-14 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20090813085445/http://www.lib.msu.edu/services/spec_coll/writer/MWCDanGerber.html |archivedate=August 13, 2009 }}</ref>  Gerber currently lives in [[Santa Ynez Valley]], [[California]] with his wife.<ref name="kwls">{{cite web |url=http://www.kwls.org/lit/2010/bio.cfm?auth_id=201 |title=Key West Literary Seminar &#124; 2010 &#124; DanGerber |publisher=Kwls.org |accessdate=2009-11-14 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20090811013121/http://www.kwls.org/lit/2010/bio.cfm?Auth_id=201 |archivedate=2009-08-11 }}</ref>

Gerber's literary works have been recognized and highlighted at Michigan State University in their Michigan Writers Series.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.lib.msu.edu/branches/vvl/writers/index.jsp  |title=Michigan Writers Series |publisher=Michigan State University Libraries |date= |accessdate=2012-07-15}}</ref>
His work has appeared in ''[[Narrative Magazine]]' Poetry, The New Yorker, The Massachusetts Review, The Nation, New Letters, and Best American Poetry'.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.narrativemagazine.com/authors/dan-gerber|title=Dan Gerber &#124; Narrative Magazine|date=7 June 2008}}</ref> ''

His most recent book of poetry, ''Particles: New and Selected Poems'', was published in 2017 by [[Copper Canyon Press]].
