'''János Aczél''' (died 1523) was a royal secretary and poet.

He was replaced from the [[provost (religion)|provostry]] of [[Vác]] to [[Pressburg]] (''Pozsony'', today's [[Bratislava]]) as the secretary of [[Louis II of Hungary and Bohemia|II. Louis]]. He was also the abbot of Garamszentbenedek (now [[Hronský Beňadik]], [[Slovakia]]). He had died before he could take the position of provost in Pressburg. He wrote poems in [[latin language|Latin]], but none of them survived.
