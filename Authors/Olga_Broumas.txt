'''Olga Broumas''' (born 6 May 1949, [[Hermoupolis]]) is a Greek poet, resident in the United States. She has been Poet-in-Residence and Director of Creative Writing at [[Brandeis University]] since 1995.

==Biography==
Born and raised on the island of [[Syros]], Broumas secured a fellowship through the [[Fulbright program]] to study in the United States at the [[University of Pennsylvania]]. There, she earned her bachelor's degree in architecture. She later went on to earn a Master of Fine Arts degree from the [[University of Oregon]].<ref name=":0" />

After earning this degree, Broumas co-founded and taught at Freehand, Inc., a school in [[Provincetown, Massachusetts|Provincetown]], Massachusetts for female writers and artists. The school disbanded in 1987.

Broumas has worked in the creative writing programs at several universities, including the University of Idaho and Goddard College.<ref name=":0" /> She currently is the Professor Emerita of the Practice of English at Brandeis University.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.brandeis.edu/english/faculty/broumas.html|title=Department of English Faculty|website=Brandeis University|access-date=2019-03-02}}</ref>

== Works and honours ==
Her first collection of poems, ''Beginning with O'', was considered groundbreaking in its depiction of explicit lesbian sexuality.<ref name=":0">[http://www.glbtq.com/literature/broumas_o.html GLBTQ encyclopedia : Olga Broumas] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080226233025/http://www.glbtq.com/literature/broumas_o.html |date=2008-02-26 }}</ref> 
Broumas was selected by [[Stanley Kunitz]] for the [[Yale Younger Poets Series]]<ref>[https://query.nytimes.com/gst/fullpage.html?res=9801E1DB1F3CF933A05752C0A9669C8B63&scp=5&sq=Olga+Broumas&st=nyt New York Times, THE GUIDE by Eleanor Charles, January 30, 2000]</ref> in 1977, the first non-native speaker of English to receive this award{{Citation needed|date=February 2008}}. Other honors have included a [[Guggenheim Fellowship]] and a fellowship from the [[National Endowment for the Arts]].
