'''Paulus Johannes "Paul" Biegel''' ({{IPA-nl|ˈpʌulʏs joːˈɦɑnəs ˈpʌul ˈbiɣəl}}; 25 March 1925 – 21 October 2006) was a successful and prolific [[Netherlands|Dutch]] writer of [[children's literature]].

==Biography==
Paul Biegel was born in Bussum in 1925. His father, Hermann Biegel, was of German descent, and owned a building materials shop. With his wife Madeleine Povel-Guillot he had nine children, six girls and three boys, of which Paul was the youngest. He wasn't a prolific reader as a child, preferring to play outside. His favourite books where the fairy tales of the brothers Grimm and the works of [[Jules Verne]]. He studied in Bussum (primary school) and [[Amsterdam]], graduating in 1945.

His first story, ''De ontevreden kabouter'' ("The unhappy gnome"), written when he was 14 years old, was printed in the newspaper ''[[De Tijd]]''.<ref name=Volkskrant>{{cite news |first=Arjan |last=Peters |title=Meesterverteller met een rovershart |trans-title=Master story teller with a robber's heart |url=http://www.volkskrant.nl/kunst/article361530.ece |publisher=[[de Volkskrant]] |date=22 October 2006 |access-date=June 23, 2009 |language=nl}}</ref> He wanted to become a pianist, but decided that he didn't have enough talent.<ref name=NRC>{{cite news |first=Karel |last=Berkhout |title=Muzikale meesterverteller |trans-title=Musical master story teller |url=http://www.nrc.nl/kunst/article1735413.ece/Muzikale_meesterverteller?service=Print |publisher=[[NRC Handelsblad]] |date=23 October 2006 |access-date=June 23, 2009 |language=nl}}</ref> He went to the United States for a year after [[World War II]], where he worked for ''{{ill|The Knickerbocker Weekly|nl}}''. After his return, he worked as an editor for Dutch magazines like the ''{{ill|Avrobode|nl}}''.<ref>{{cite news  |title=Kinderboekenschrijver Paul Biegel overleden |url=http://www.volkskrant.nl/kunst/article361471.ece/Kinderboekenschrijver_Paul_Biegel_overleden |publisher=[[de Volkskrant]] |date=22 October 2006 |access-date=June 23, 2009 |language=nl}}</ref> He commenced Law Studies, but stopped these in 1953.<ref name=Volkskrant/> He wrote several newspaper comics, the most important of which was ''Minter and Hinter'', which appeared in ''[[Het Vrije Volk]]'' for ten stories and 579 episodes. In 1959, he started working in the comic studio of [[Marten Toonder]] as a comics writer for the ''Kappie'' series.<ref name=Generaal>{{cite book |last1=Biegel |first1=Paul |title=De brieven van de generaal |series=Boektoppers |year=2003 |orig-year=1977 |publisher=Malmberg |location=Den Bosch |language=nl |isbn=90-345-1273-8 |pages=152–155 }}</ref>

He only published his first book, ''De gouden gitaar'', in 1962. He became one of the most important Dutch writers for children in 1965, when he received the [[Gouden Griffel]] for ''Het sleutelkruid''. In 1973 he won the [[Theo Thijssen-prijs|Staatsprijs voor kinder- en jeugdliteratuur]] literary award. He has written over 50 books,<ref name=Generaal/> mostly published by [[Holland (publisher)|Holland]], and many of his books have been translated in English, French, German, Danish, Swedish, Welsh, South African, Japanese, Turkish, Greek, and Spanish. His own favourites were ''De tuinen van Dorr'' and ''De soldatenmaker''.<ref name=NRC/> Other authors he appreciated included [[J. R. R. Tolkien]], [[J. K. Rowling]], and [[Hans Christian Andersen]].<ref name=Volkskrant/>

Paul Biegel lived in [[Amsterdam]]. He married Marijke Sträter in 1960, with whom he had a daughter, Leonie, in 1963,<ref name=Generaal/> and in 1964 a son, Arthur, who committed suicide when he was 28.<ref name=NRC/> His marriage ultimately failed, and only at a later age did he publicly admit to being homosexual. He died in 2006<ref name=NRC/> and was buried at [[Zorgvlied (cemetery)|Zorgvlied cemetery]]. In 2007, publishers [[Holland (publisher)]] and [[Lemniscaat]] started reissuing twenty of his best works in the ''Biegelbibliotheek''.<ref>{{cite web |title=Uitgeverij Holland en Lemniscaat en de Biegel Bibliotheek |url=http://www.lemniscaat.nl/static/nieuws/nieuwsbrief/persberichten/BiegelBieb.htm |publisher=Lemniscaat |language=nl |date=February 2009 |access-date=24 June 2009 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110716081931/http://www.lemniscaat.nl/static/nieuws/nieuwsbrief/persberichten/BiegelBieb.htm |archive-date=16 July 2011 |url-status=dead }}</ref>
