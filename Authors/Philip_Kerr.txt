'''Philip Ballantyne Kerr''' (22 February 1956 &ndash; 23 March 2018) was a British author,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.isfdb.org/cgi-bin/ea.cgi?5502|title=Philip Kerr|work=International Science Fiction Database}}</ref><ref>{{cite book|url=https://archive.org/details/internationalwho2004ond|url-access=registration|quote=Philip Kerr 22 February.|title=The International Who's Who 2004|year=2003|page=[https://archive.org/details/internationalwho2004ond/page/875 875]|publisher=Europa Publications}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.wavesound.com.au/Browse/Bestselling-Authors/Philip-Kerr|title=Philip Kerr|publisher=Wavesound|access-date=8 November 2017}}</ref> best known for his Bernie Gunther series of historical detective thrillers.

==Early life==
Kerr was born in [[Edinburgh]], Scotland, where his father was an engineer and his mother worked as a secretary.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Sandomir|first1=Richard|title=Philip Kerr, 62, Author of 'Gunther' Crime Novels, Is Dead|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2018/03/27/obituaries/philip-kerr-62-author-of-gunther-crime-novels-is-dead.html|access-date=30 March 2018|work=[[The New York Times]]|date=27 March 2018}}</ref>  He was educated at a grammar school in [[Northampton]]. He studied at the [[University of Birmingham]] from 1974 to 1980, gaining a master's degree in law and philosophy.<ref name=tele2012/> Kerr worked as an advertising copywriter for [[Saatchi & Saatchi]]<ref name=tele2012>{{cite news|newspaper=[[The Daily Telegraph]]|url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/culture/books/bookreviews/9025756/Philip-Kerr-Interview.html|title=Philip Kerr: Interview|date=23 January 2012|author=Toby Clements|access-date=4 November 2013}}</ref> before becoming a full-time writer in 1989. In a 2012 interview, Kerr noted that he began his literary career at the age of twelve by writing pornographic stories and lending them to classmates for a fee.<ref name=tele2012/>

==Career==
A writer of both adult fiction and non-fiction, he is known for the Bernhard "Bernie" Gunther series of [[historical mystery|historical thrillers]] set in Germany and elsewhere during the 1930s, the [[Second World War]] and the [[Cold War]]. He also wrote children's books under the name '''P. B. Kerr''', including the ''Children of the Lamp'' series. Kerr wrote for ''[[The Sunday Times (UK)|The Sunday Times]]'', the ''[[Evening Standard]]'', and the ''[[New Statesman]]''. He was married to fellow novelist [[Jane Thynne]]; they lived in [[Wimbledon, London]],<ref>{{cite news|newspaper=Your Local Guardian|title=Tom Hanks poised to bring novels of Wimbledon author Philip Kerr to small screen|url=http://www.yourlocalguardian.co.uk/leisure/latest/10672066.Interview__Tom_Hanks_poised_to_bring_novels_of_Wimbledon_author_to_small_screen/|access-date=4 November 2013|date=13 September 2013|author=Lauren May}}</ref> and had three children. He died from cancer on 23 March 2018, aged 62.<ref name=Guardian_obit>{{cite news|url=https://www.theguardian.com/books/2018/mar/24/philip-kerr-author-of-bernie-gunther-novels-dies-aged-62 |title=Philip Kerr, author of Bernie Gunther novels, dies aged 62 |author=Hannah Summers |date=24 March 2018 |newspaper=The Guardian |access-date=25 March 2018}}</ref> Just before he died, he finished a 14th Bernie Gunther novel, ''Metropolis,'' which was published posthumously, in 2019.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.thebookseller.com/news/philip-kerr-dies-755621|title=Quercus pays tribute to 'cherished' author Philip Kerr - The Bookseller|website=www.thebookseller.com|access-date=29 March 2018}}</ref>

==Awards and honours==
In 1993, Kerr was named in [[Granta]]'s list of [[Granta#Granta Best of Young British Novelists|Best Young British Novelists]].<ref name=tele2012/> In 2009, ''If the Dead Rise Not'' won the world's most lucrative crime fiction award, the [[RBA Prize for Crime Writing]] worth €125,000.<ref name=tremlett>{{cite web |url=https://www.theguardian.com/books/2009/sep/04/philip-kerr-wins-rba-prize |title=Philip Kerr wins €125,000 RBA crime writing prize |work=[[The Guardian]] |author=Giles Tremlett |date=3 September 2009 |access-date=13 September 2013}}</ref> The book also won the British Crime Writers' Association's [[Ellis Peters Historical Award|Ellis Peters Historic Crime Award]] that same year.<ref>{{cite press release|publisher=The Crime Writers' Association|url=http://www.thecwa.co.uk/daggers/2009/historical.html|date=29 October 2009|title=Philip Kerr wins the 2009 CWA Ellis Peters Historic Crime Award|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131107060039/http://www.thecwa.co.uk/daggers/2009/historical.html|archive-date=7 November 2013|df=dmy-all}}</ref> His novel, ''Prussian Blue'', was longlisted for the [[Walter Scott Prize|2018 Walter Scott Prize]].

== Death ==

Kerr died from [[bladder cancer]] on 23 March 2018.<ref>{{cite news | url=https://www.theguardian.com/books/2018/mar/25/philip-kerr-obituary | title=Philip Kerr obituary | newspaper=The Guardian | date=25 March 2018 | first=Danuta | last=Kean | access-date=19 May 2018 }}</ref>
