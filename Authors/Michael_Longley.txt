'''Michael Longley''', {{post-nominals|country=GBR|size=100%|commas=on|CBE}} (born 27 July 1939, [[Belfast, Northern Ireland]]) is an Anglo-Irish poet.<!-- term is correct; his parents were English -->

==Life and career==
Michael Longley was born in Belfast to English parents, Longley was educated at the [[Royal Belfast Academical Institution]], and subsequently read [[Classics]] at [[Trinity College, Dublin]], where he edited ''[[Icarus (magazine)|Icarus]]''. He was the [[Ireland Professor of Poetry]] from 2007-10, a cross-border academic post set up in 1998, previously held by [[John Montague (poet)|John Montague]], [[Nuala Ní Dhomhnaill]], and [[Paul Durcan]]. He was succeeded in 2010 by [[Harry Clifton]].<ref>[http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/northern_ireland/6982117.stm "Longley new professor of poetry"], bbc.co.uk; accessed 26 July 2020.</ref> North American editions of Longley's work are published by [[Wake Forest University Press]].

Over 50 years he has spent much time in Carrigskeewaun, County Mayo, which has inspired much of his poetry.<ref>{{cite web |title=An imagination nourished by the landscape of the west |url=https://www.irishtimes.com/culture/an-imagination-nourished-by-the-landscape-of-the-west-1.707420 |website=www.irishtimes.com |access-date=9 August 2020}}</ref>

His wife, [[Edna Longley|Edna]], is a critic on modern Irish and British poetry.<ref>[http://www.wfu.edu/wfupress/authors.php Wake Forest University Press] {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090310225034/http://www.wfu.edu/wfupress/authors.php |date=10 March 2009}}</ref> They have three children. Their daughter is artist [[Sarah Longley]]. An [[atheist]], Longley describes himself as a "sentimental" disbeliever.<ref>[https://www.theguardian.com/books/2011/apr/09/hundred-doors-michael-longley-poetry-review "Michael Longley's reverence for the living and the dead is as evident as ever"], theguardian.com; accessed 26 July 2020.</ref>

On 14 January 2014, he participated in the BBC Radio 3 series ''The Essay – Letters to a Young Poet''. Taking [[Rainer Maria Rilke]]'s classic text ''[[Letters to a Young Poet]]'' as inspiration, leading poets wrote a letter to a protege.<ref>[https://www.bbc.co.uk/iplayer/episode/b03pdg5b/The_Essay_Letters_to_a_Young_Poet_Michael_Longley "The Essay – Letters to a Young Poet"], bbc.co.uk; accessed 26 July 2020.</ref> Longley has provided readings of his poetry for the [https://digital.ucd.ie/?q=michael+longley Irish Poetry Reading Archive (UCD)].

==Awards and honours==
''Gorse Fires'' (1991) won the [[Whitbread Book Awards|Whitbread Poetry Prize]]. ''The Weather in Japan'' (2000) won the [[T.S. Eliot Prize]] and the [[Hawthornden Prize]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.poetryfoundation.org/poets/michael-longley|title=Michael Longley|date=16 January 2018|website=Poetry Foundation|others=Poetry Foundation|access-date=26 July 2020}}</ref>  It also brought him the inaugural Yakamochi Medal in 2018.<ref>{{cite web |title=Ist Yakamochi Medal Award Decision |url=http://www.koshibun.jp/tid=100124 |website=www.koshibun.jp |access-date=8 August 2020}}</ref>He holds honorary doctorates from [[Queen's University Belfast]] (1995) and [[Trinity College, Dublin]] (1999) and was the 2001 recipient of the [[Queen's Gold Medal for Poetry]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Medal crowns Belfast poet's renaissance |url=https://www.theguardian.com/uk/2001/apr/24/books.arts |website=www.theguardian.com |date=24 April 2001 |access-date=8 August 2020}}</ref>Longley was appointed [[Commander of the Order of the British Empire]] (CBE) in the 2010 Birthday Honours.<ref>{{London Gazette |issue=59446 |date=12 June 2010 |page=7 |supp=y}}</ref>

Longley won a 2011 [[London Awards for Art and Performance]]. His collection ''A Hundred Doors'' won the [[Poetry Now Award]] in September 2012.<ref name="it2012">[http://www.irishtimes.com/newspaper/ireland/2012/0908/1224323739993.html "Michael Longley wins €5,000 poetry prize"], ''[[The Irish Times]]'', 8 September 2012.</ref> 

His 2014 collection, ''The Stairwell'', won the 2015 International [[Griffin Poetry Prize]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Michael Longley wins Griffin International Poetry Prize |url=https://www.irishtimes.com/culture/books/michael-longley-wins-griffin-international-poetry-prize-1.2238588 |website=www.irishtimes.com |access-date=8 August 2020}}</ref> In 2015, he received the Ulster Tatler Lifetime Achievement Award.<ref>{{cite news |title=Poet Michael Longley's lifetime achievement gong at Ulster Tatler awards |url=https://www.belfasttelegraph.co.uk/news/northern-ireland/poet-michael-longleys-lifetime-achievement-gong-at-ulster-tatler-awards-31536757.html |newspaper=Belfasttelegraph |access-date=8 August 2020}}</ref> He was awarded the [[PEN Pinter Prize]] in 2017. The Chair of the judges, Don Paterson, said: "For decades now his effortlessly lyric and fluent poetry has been wholly suffused with the qualities of humanity, humility and compassion, never shying away from the moral complexity that comes from seeing both sides of an argument."<ref>{{cite web |title=Michael Longley wins PEN Pinter Prize for unflinching unswerving poetry |url=https://www.guardian.com/books/2017/iun/01/michael-longley-wins-pen-pinter-prize-for-unflinching-unswerving-poetry |website=www.guardian.com |access-date=8 August 2020}}</ref>

In 2015 Longley was elected a Freeman of the City of Belfast.<ref>{{cite book |title=Publisher's note ''Angel Hill''}}</ref>
