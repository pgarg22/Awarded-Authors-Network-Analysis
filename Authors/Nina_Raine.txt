'''Nina Raine''' is an English [[theatre director]] and [[playwright]], the only daughter of [[Craig Raine]] and [[Ann Pasternak Slater]], and a grand niece of the Russian novelist [[Boris Pasternak]].

She graduated from [[Christ Church, Oxford]] in 1998 with a First in [[English Literature]].

==Life and career==
She won the [[Channel Four]]/[[Jerwood Space]] Young Regional Theatre Director bursary in 2000 to train as a director at the [[Royal Court Theatre]] where she assisted on a number of plays including ''[[My Zinc Bed (play)|My Zinc Bed]]'', ''Mouth to Mouth'', ''[[Presence (play)|Presence]]'' and ''Fucking Games''.

She has directed plays in several other theatres since then, including ''[[Unprotected]]'' at the [[Liverpool Everyman]] and the [[Edinburgh Festival]] in 2006, for which she won the [[TMA Awards|TMA]] Best Director Award, and ''Shades'' by [[Alia Bano]] as part of the Royal Court Theatre's Young Writers' Festival in 2009, as well as ''Jumpy'' by [[April De Angelis]] at the [[Royal Court Theatre|Royal Court]] and in the [[West End theatre|West End]].

''Rabbit'', Raine's first work as a dramatist, premiered at the [[Old Red Lion Theatre]] in London in the summer of 2006.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.britishtheatreguide.info/reviews/rabbitraine-rev.htm|title=Theatre review: Rabbit at Old Red Lion|website=www.britishtheatreguide.info}}</ref> The play, which she also directed, transferred to the [[Trafalgar Studios]] later that autumn. She won both the 2006 [[Evening Standard Awards|Evening Standard]]'s [[Charles Wintour]] Award for Most Promising Playwright and the Most Promising Playwright Award at the 2006 [[Critics' Circle Theatre Awards]].

''Rabbit'' was performed in [[New York City]] in June 2007 at the [[Brits Off Broadway]] Festival.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.curtainup.com/britfestival2007.html#Rabbit|title=Brits Off Broadway 2007 , a CurtainUp report|first=CurtainUp, Elyse|last=Sommer|website=www.curtainup.com}}</ref>

Raine's second play ''[[Tribes (play)|Tribes]]'' was produced by the [[Royal Court Theatre|Royal Court]] in London, in October 2010, directed by [[Roger Michell]] and starring [[Harry Treadaway]], [[Michelle Terry]] and [[Stanley Townsend]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.wsj.com/articles/SB10001424052748704779704575553982127094048|title=Raine's Tribal Instinct Breaks Down the Language Barriers|first=Paul|last=Levy|date=22 October 2010|publisher=|via=www.wsj.com}}</ref> It had its Australian premiere at the [[Melbourne Theatre Company]] in February 2012,{{citation needed|date=November 2017}} and its North American premiere at New York City's [[Barrow Street Theatre]], also in 2012.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.broadway.com/shows/tribes/buzz/158562/david-cromer-to-direct-nina-raines-tribes-at-barrow-street-theatre|title=David Cromer to Direct Nina Raine's Tribes at Barrow Street Theatre|publisher=}}</ref> It was then produced by [[Artists Repertory Theatre]] in Portland in February 2015.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.artistsrep.org/onstage/2014-15-season/tribes.aspx|title=2017/18 Season » Artists Repertory Theatre|website=www.artistsrep.org}}</ref> ''Tribes'' is about a deaf son who is raised in a dysfunctional, Jewish, hearing family (Raine is Jewish on her mother's side).<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.jewishjournal.com/the_ticket/item/comedy_drama_tribes_communicates_dysfunction_of_british_jewish_family|title=Comedy-drama 'Tribes' communicates dysfunction of British-Jewish family — Jewish Journal|date=13 March 2013|publisher=}}</ref>

''Tiger Country'', commissioned by Hampstead Theatre and produced by [[Alcove Entertainment]], opened in January 2011.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.offwestend.com/index.php/plays/view/4590|title=, by  at  -  - London UK - more on OffWestEnd.com - Listings and showtimes for over 80 Off West End theatres in London UK|website=www.offwestend.com}}</ref>

Raine also directed and dramaturged ''Behind the Image'' by [[Alia Bano]], which premiered at the Royal Court's 2008 Rough Cuts Season.

In 2013 she directed ''Longing'', an adaptation of two [[Anton Chekov]] stories by novelist [[William Boyd (writer)|William Boyd]].

Her fourth play, ''[[Consent (play)|Consent]]'', premiered at the [[Royal National Theatre|National Theatre]] in April 2017, in a co-production with [[Out of Joint]]. Directed by [[Roger Michell]], it starred [[Anna Maxwell Martin]], [[Ben Chaplin]] and [[Pip Carter]]. Her fifth play, Bach & Sons, is a work on the life of [[Johann Sebastian Bach]] at the [[Bridge Theatre]], with [[Simon Russell Beale]] as the composer in the premiere production.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://bridgetheatre.co.uk/future-projects/|title=Future Projects – The Bridge Theatre|website=bridgetheatre.co.uk}}</ref>
