'''Caleb Lewis''' (born 16 April 1978) is an Australian playwright and game designer. His plays include ''The River at the End of the Road''<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://hothousetheatre.com.au/season-2018/the-river-at-the-end-of-the-road/|title=The River at the End of the Road – HotHouse Theatre|language=en|access-date=2019-12-02}}</ref>''; Destroyer of Worlds'';<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://glamadelaide.com.au/fringe-review-destroyer-of-worlds/|title=Fringe Review: Destroyer of Worlds|date=2015-02-17|website=Glam Adelaide|language=en-AU|access-date=2019-12-02}}</ref> ''The Honey Bees;''<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://redstitch.net/gallery/the-honey-bees/|title=The Honey Bees {{!}} Red Stitch Actors Theatre {{!}} Australia’s leading ensemble theatre company, premiering contemporary works since 2002|last=https://redstitch.net|language=en-US|access-date=2019-12-02}}</ref> ''Maggie Stone''<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.currency.com.au/books/drama/maggie-stone/|title=Maggie Stone|website=Currency Press|language=en-US|access-date=2019-12-02}}</ref>''; Nailed'';<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://australianplays.org/script/ASC-934|title=NAILED|website=AustralianPlays.org|language=en|access-date=2019-12-02}}</ref> ''[[Dogfall]]'',<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.realtime.org.au/moving-and-shaking/|title=moving and shaking|access-date=2019-12-02}}</ref> ''Death in Bowengabbie'',<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.dailytelegraph.com.au/entertainment/review-death-in-bowengabbie-old-fitzroy-theatre/news-story/43f1516df4d21e25e9b10a98aed39fcb|title=Review: Death in Bowengabbie|date=2010-03-19|website=www.dailytelegraph.com.au|language=en|access-date=2019-12-02}}</ref> ''Rust and Bone'',<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://blogs.crikey.com.au/curtaincall/2013/01/21/review-rust-and-bone-griffin-theatre-sydney/|title=REVIEW: Rust And Bone {{!}} Griffin Theatre, Sydney – Curtain Call|website=blogs.crikey.com.au|access-date=2019-12-02}}</ref> ''Aleksander and the Robot Maid'',<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.smh.com.au/entertainment/theatre/friendship-forged-against-the-odds-in-robot-world-20110704-1gywf.html|title=Friendship forged against the odds in robot world|last=Blake|first=Jason|date=2011-07-04|website=The Sydney Morning Herald|language=en|access-date=2019-12-02}}</ref> ''In a Dark Dark Wood''<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://barkinggecko.com.au/play/in-a-dark-dark-wood/|title=In a Dark Dark Wood|website=Barking Gecko Theatre|language=en-AU|access-date=2019-12-02}}</ref> and ''Clinchfield''<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://thewest.com.au/news/wa/circus-elephant-tale-judged-jumbo-effort-ng-ya-195161|title=Circus elephant tale judged jumbo effort|date=2010-10-14|website=The West Australian|language=en|access-date=2019-12-02}}</ref> (winner of the inaugural [[Richard Burton]] Award for new plays).

==Works==
Lewis studied drama and playwriting at the [[Flinders University]] Drama Centre under playwright Verity Laughton. In 2002 he began a year-long mentorship with [[Nick Enright]] and began work on ''Nailed''. In 2003 he won the Independent Arts Foundation Literature Scholarship to research the play ''Krissy Pho in Vietnam'' and also the Naked Theatre Company's Write Now competition for his play ''RocketBaby''.

In 2004 his collection of short plays ''Songs for the Deaf'' was produced by FreshTrack Productions for the [[Adelaide Fringe Festival]] and he began a two-year residency with the [[Griffin Theatre Company]], culminating in the world premiere of ''Nailed'' (short listed for the Phillip Parsons playwright's award). Lewis has completed commissions for Jigsaw Theatre Company in the [[Australian Capital Territory]] and Riverland Youth Theatre Company in South Australia. His short film ''The Half Windsor'' opened in Sydney in May 2007.

After receiving funding from the [[Australia Council for the Arts|Australia Council]]'s theatre and literature boards, his play ''The Sea Bride'' won the Theatrelab Emerging Playwright Award, earning a two-week workshop with playwright [[Edward Albee]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://ehlt.flinders.edu.au/drama_centre/ |title=Drama Centre: Graduate profiles |publisher=Flinders University |accessdate=7 December 2008 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20081201011848/http://ehlt.flinders.edu.au/drama_centre/ |archivedate=1 December 2008 }}</ref>

His play ''[[Dogfall]]'' opened in Adelaide at the Bakehouse Theatre in November 2007 and was shortlisted for the 2008 Phillip Parsons Award.<ref name="aso">{{cite web|url=http://www.australianstage.com.au/news/sydney/2008-philip-parsons-memorial-lecture-2037.html|title=The Philip Parsons Young Playwright’s Award 2008|publisher=Australian Stage Online|accessdate=7 December 2008}}</ref> In January 2008, ''Songs for the Deaf'' toured to Hong Kong. His play ''Men, Love and the Monkeyboy'' was short-listed for the 2007 Griffin Award and is the winner of the 2008 Mitch Matthews award.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.pdproductions.org.au/html/mitch.html|title=The Mitch Mathews Award|publisher=pdproductions.org.au|accessdate=7 December 2008|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190303181708/http://pdproductions.org.au/html/mitch.html|archive-date=3 March 2019|url-status=dead}}</ref> ''Men, Love and the Monkeyboy'' enjoyed successful seasons at the [[Darlinghurst Theatre]] and the [[Riverside Theatre Parramatta]] in 2008. Lewis' most recent play, ''Rust and Bone'', based on [[Craig Davidson]]'s short story collection of the same name, premiered in Sydney in 2013 earning favorable reviews<ref>[http://www.griffintheatre.com.au/whats-on/rust-and-bone Rust and Bone at Griffin Theatre Company]</ref>

Lewis began to work on creating [[immersive theatre]] pieces and game based stories. His work ''If There Was A Colour Darker Than Black I’d Wear It'' won the 2013 Ruby Award for Innovation.<ref>{{cite web |title=Caleb Lewis |url=https://mollisonkeightley.com/artists/caleb-lewis/ |website=Mollison Keightley Management |accessdate=7 October 2020}}</ref> In 2016 he developed an app called ''The City'' which allows the player to explore the lives of four homeless characters.<ref>{{cite web |title=Watch an interview with playwright Caleb Lewis |url=https://www.blasttheory.co.uk/news-item/watch-an-interview-with-playwright-caleb-lewis/ |website=Blast Theory |accessdate=7 October 2020}}</ref> In 2019 he developed ''A Little History Play'' for the Batch Festival at Griffin Theatre Company. Which was an interactive piece where the audience guided the action in the play. Those decisions then carried over to the play's next performance.<ref>{{cite web |title=5 Questions with Caleb Lewis |url=https://griffintheatre.com.au/blog/5-questions-with-caleb-lewis/ |website=Griffin Theatre Company |accessdate=7 October 2020}}</ref>

==Awards==
Lewis was the inaugural winner of the Australian Writers Guild's 2008 Interactive Media [[AWGIE_Awards|AWGIE]] for his work on, ''Otzi&nbsp;&ndash; The Story of the Iceman'',<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.awg.com.au/artman/publish/article_573.shtml |title=2008 Awgie Award Winners |publisher=Australian Writers' Guild |accessdate=7 December 2008 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20081204062726/http://www.awg.com.au/artman/publish/article_573.shtml |archivedate=4 December 2008 }}</ref> the inaugural winner of [[Sally Burton]]'s 2010 Richard Burton Award for New Plays for his play ''Clinchfield''. His play ''Death in Bowengabbie'' was nominated for best stage production at the 2009 Adelaide Fringe Festival winning a commendation for best writing. In 2010, Lewis turned down the prestigious Philip Parsons Award, in protest.<ref>[http://www.smh.com.au/news/entertainment/arts/playwright-opts-for-credibility-over-cash/2010/03/14/1268501412858.html "Playwright opts for credibility over cash"] by Adam Fulton, ''[[The Sydney Morning Herald]]'' (15 March 2010)</ref>
