'''Janny Wurts''' (born December 10, 1953) is an American [[fantasy]] novelist and illustrator.  She has written several standalone novels and series, including the ''[[Wars of Light and Shadow]]'', ''The Cycle of Fire'' trilogy and the internationally best-selling [[Empire trilogy|''Empire'' trilogy]] that she co-authored with [[Raymond E. Feist]].<ref name=tor-2018/> Her short story collection ''That Way Lies Camelot'' was nominated for the [[British Fantasy Award]] in 1995. She often illustrates her own books, and has won [[Chesley Awards]] for her artwork.<ref name=blackgate-2018/> 

==Biography==
Janny Wurts was born in [[Bryn Mawr, Pennsylvania]]. In the 1980s, she worked as an illustrator for role-playing game supplements for [[Mayfair Games]].<ref name=penn-2018>{{Cite web |url=https://pabook.libraries.psu.edu/literary-cultural-heritage-map-pa/bios/Wurts__Janny |title=Janny Wurts |last=Lavelle |first=Matthew |website=[[Center for the Book|Pennsylvania Center for the Book]] |publisher=[[Pennsylvania State University]] |date=2018 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200814005723/https://pabook.libraries.psu.edu/literary-cultural-heritage-map-pa/bios/Wurts__Janny |archive-date=2020-08-14 |url-status=live}}</ref>

Wurts has written numerous novels (20 as of 2021).<ref name=ISFDB/> Her fantasy debut ''Sorcerer's Legacy'' (1982) has been described as a political thriller; it features a female protagonist and court intrigue.<ref name=tor-2015/> Similar elements feature in the [[Empire trilogy|''Empire'' trilogy]], written in collaboration with [[Raymond E. Feist]]. ''Empire'' is set in a non-European culture that has drawn comparisons to feudal Japan and the Byzantine empire.<ref name=fantasy-encyc/><ref name=locus-index/> Wurts has also written 
[[science fantasy]]: her ''Cycle of Fire'' trilogy mixes magic with [[science fiction]]. Her ongoing series, ''[[Wars of Light and Shadow]]'', is a 11-volume epic fantasy (10 books published) with a storyline that spans multiple millennia and planets.<ref name=tor-2018/><ref name=locus-2017/>

Beyond writing, Wurts' award-winning paintings have been showcased in exhibitions of imaginative artwork, among them a commemorative exhibition for NASA's 25th Anniversary; the Art of the Cosmos at [[Hayden Planetarium]] in New York; and two exhibits of fantasy art, at both the Delaware Art Museum and Canton Art Museum.<ref name=penn-2018/>

Wurts was a guest of honor at the 1996 [[World Horror Convention]] and the 2004 [[World Fantasy Convention]].<ref name=WHC/><ref name=WFC-2004/> She resides in [[Florida]] with her husband, artist [[Don Maitz]].<ref name=blackgate-2018/>
