'''Agnes Walsh''' (born 1950) is a [[Canadians|Canadian]] [[poet]], [[playwright]], [[actor]] and [[storytelling|storyteller]] from [[Newfoundland and Labrador]].<ref name="Heritage NL">{{cite web |url=https://www.heritage.nf.ca/articles/arts/agnes-walsh.php |title=Heritage Newfoundland and Labrador - Agnes Walsh |publisher=Heritage Newfoundland and Labrador |accessdate=2019-10-26}}</ref>

Born in [[Placentia, Newfoundland and Labrador]], Walsh has won Newfoundland and Labrador Arts and Letters awards for poetry as well as TickleAce poetry and ballad writing awards. Her poems have been translated into [[French language|French]] and [[Portuguese language|Portuguese]]. She has toured Canada, the eastern [[United States]], [[Portugal]], and [[Ireland]] reading from her work.<ref name="Heritage NL" />

Walsh is also the founder of the Tramore Theatre Troupe on the [[Cape Shore]] of [[Placentia Bay]], an ensemble dedicated to preserving and presenting the [[oral history]] of that area. The group has performed to packed houses in both Newfoundland and Ireland and hosted Irish cultural exchanges to the [[Cape Shore]] area.<ref name="Nolan">{{Cite journal |last=Nolan |first=Michael |title=Agnes Walsh. Answer Me Home: Plays from Tramore Theatre. |url= https://journals.lib.unb.ca/index.php/NFLDS/article/view/21052/24297 |journal=Newfoundland and Labrador Studies |volume=27 |issue=1 |archive-url=https://journals.lib.unb.ca/index.php/NFLDS/article/view/21052 |archive-date=2012-04-12 |access-date=2019-10-26 }}</ref>

In 2006, Walsh was named the first [[poet laureate]] of [[St. John's, Newfoundland and Labrador|St. John's]].<ref name="CBC News">{{cite web |url=https://www.cbc.ca/news/entertainment/agnes-walsh-named-st-john-s-poet-laureate-1.579228 |title=CBC News - Agnes Walsh named St. John's poet laureate |publisher=CBC News |accessdate=2019-10-26}}</ref>
