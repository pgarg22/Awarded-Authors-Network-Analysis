'''Marie Ponsot''' {{IPAc-en|ˌ|p|ɒ|n|ˈ|s|oʊ}} (née '''Birmingham'''; April 6, 1921 – July 5, 2019) was an American [[poet]], [[literary critic]], [[essayist]], teacher, and [[translator]]. Her awards and honors included the [[National Book Critics Circle Award]], Delmore Schwartz Memorial Prize, the Robert Frost Poetry Award, the Shaughnessy Medal of the Modern Language Association, the Ruth Lilly Poetry Prize from the Poetry Foundation, and the Aiken Taylor Award for Modern American Poetry.

==Life==
Ponsot was born in [[Brooklyn]], New York, the daughter of Marie Candee, a public school teacher, and William Birmingham, an importer.<ref name=nyt>{{Cite news|last=Taylor|first=Tess|date=2019-07-06|title=Marie Ponsot, Poet of Love, Divorce and Family, Dies at 98|language=en-US|work=The New York Times|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2019/07/06/obituaries/marie-ponsot-dead-poet.html|access-date=2020-05-01|issn=0362-4331}}</ref> She grew up in [[Jamaica, Queens]] along with her brother. She was already writing poems as a child, some of which were published in the ''[[Brooklyn Daily Eagle]]''. After graduating from [[St. Joseph's College (New York)|St. Joseph's College for Women in Brooklyn]], Ponsot earned her master's degree in seventeenth-century literature from [[Columbia University]]. After the [[Second World War]], she journeyed to Paris, where she met and married [[Claude Ponsot]], a painter<ref name=nyt/> and student of [[Fernand Léger]]. The couple lived in Paris for three years, during which time they had a daughter. Her friend the American artist [[Lawrence Ferlinghetti]] published her first book of poetry, ''True Minds'',  in 1956.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|title=About Marie Ponsot {{!}} Academy of American Poets|url=https://poets.org/poet/marie-ponsot|last=Poets|first=Academy of American|website=poets.org|access-date=2020-05-01}}</ref> Later, Ponsot and her husband relocated to the United States. The couple went on to have six sons before divorcing. She was left with seven children and she was not publishing her poetry.<ref name=nyt/>

Upon returning from France, Ponsot worked as a freelance writer of radio and television scripts. She also translated 69 children's books from the French, including ''The Fables of La Fontaine''.

She co-authored with Rosemary Deen two books about the fundamentals of writing, ''Beat Not the Poor Desk'' and ''Common Sense''.

Ponsot taught a poetry thesis class, as well as writing classes, at the Poetry Center of the [[92nd Street Y]]. She also taught at the [[YMCA]], Beijing United University, [[New York University]], and [[Columbia University]], and she served as an English professor at [[Queens College]] in New York, from which she retired in 1991.

She was the Chancellor of the [[Academy of American Poets]] from 2010 to 2014.<ref name=":0" />

Ponsot lived in [[New York City]] until her death at [[NewYork–Presbyterian Hospital]] on July 5, 2019.<ref>[https://www.nytimes.com/2019/07/06/obituaries/marie-ponsot-dead-poet.html Marie Ponsot, Poet and Winner of National Book Critics Circle Award, Dies at 98]</ref>

==Awards==
Ponsot authored several collections of poetry, including ''The Bird Catcher'' (1998), a finalist for the 1999 [[Lenore Marshall Poetry Prize]]<ref name=comp>{{Cite web|title=Marie Ponsot > Compare Discount Book Prices & Save up to 90% > ISBNS.net|url=https://www.isbns.net/author/Marie_Ponsot|website=www.isbns.net|access-date=2020-05-01}}</ref> and the winner of the [[National Book Critics Circle Award]], and ''Springing: New and Selected Poems'' (2002), which was named a "notable book of the year" by ''The New York Times Book Review''.

Among her awards were a creative writing grant from the [[National Endowment for the Arts]], the Delmore Schwartz Memorial Prize, The Robert Frost Poetry Award, the Shaughnessy Medal of the [[Modern Language Association]],<ref name=":0" /> the 2013 [[Ruth Lilly Poetry Prize]] from the Poetry Foundation,<ref>{{Cite web|last=|first=|date=22 March 2013|title=Marie Ponsot Awarded $100,000 Ruth Lilly Poetry Prize|url=https://www.writerswrite.com/marie-ponsot-awarded-100000-ruth-32220132|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=26 January 2021|website=Writers Write}}</ref> and the 2015 [[Aiken Taylor Award for Modern American Poetry]].<ref>{{Cite web|last=|first=|date=26 February 2015|title=Sewanee Review announces Marie Ponsot as the recipient of Aiken Taylor Award in modern American poetry|url=http://www.sewanee.edu/newstoday/life/all/aiken-taylor-award.html|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=26 January 2021|website=Sewanee Today}}</ref>
