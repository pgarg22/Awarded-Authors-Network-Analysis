'''Kim Addonizio''' (July 31, 1954) is an American [[poet]] and novelist.<ref>{{cite news|title=Kim Addonizio|url=http://www.poets.org/poetsorg/poet/kim-addonizio|access-date=August 14, 2015|publisher=Academy of American Poets}}</ref>

==Life==
Addonizio was born in [[Washington, D.C.]], United States. She is the daughter of tennis champion [[Pauline Betz]] and sports writer [[Bob Addie]].

She briefly attended [[Georgetown University]] and [[American University]] before dropping out of both.<ref>{{cite book|last=Addonizio|first=Kim|title=Ordinary genius : a guide for the poet within|year=2009|publisher=W.W. Norton|location=New York|isbn=978-0-393-33416-6|pages=[https://archive.org/details/ordinarygeniusgu00addo/page/55 55â€“56]|edition=1st|url=https://archive.org/details/ordinarygeniusgu00addo/page/55}}</ref> She later moved to [[San Francisco]] and received a [[Bachelor of Arts|B.A.]] and [[Master of Arts|M.A.]] from [[San Francisco State University]]. She has taught at [[San Francisco State University]] and [[Goddard College]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.poets.org/poet.php/prmPID/725 |title=Kim Addonizio - Poet &#124; Academy of American Poets |website=Poets.org |date=1954-07-31 |access-date=2017-01-16}}</ref>

She has a daughter, [[Aya Cash]], and currently lives in [[Oakland, California]].
