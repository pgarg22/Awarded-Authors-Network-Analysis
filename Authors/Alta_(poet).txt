'''Alta Gerrey''' (born 1942, [[Reno, Nevada]]<ref>{{cite book|last1=Blain|first1=Virginia|last2=Clements|first2=Patricia|last3=Grundy|first3=Isobel|title=The feminist companion to literature in English: women writers from the Middle Ages to the present|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=A4MjAQAAIAAJ|accessdate=9 February 2012|year=1990|publisher=Batsford|isbn=978-0-7134-5848-0}}</ref>) is a British-American [[poet]], prose writer, and publisher,<ref>{{cite book| title=The Oxford companion to women's writing in the United States|editor=Cathy N. Davidson |editor2=Linda Wagner-Martin |editor3=Elizabeth Ammons| publisher=Oxford University Press| year=1995| isbn=978-0-19-506608-1| url=https://archive.org/details/oxfordcompaniont00davi}}</ref> best known as the founder of the feminist press [[Shameless Hussy Press]] and editor of the ''Shameless Hussy Review''.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://bostonreview.net/BR34.2/moore.php|title=After 'Ariel': Celebrating the poetry of the women's movement|last=Moore|first=Honor|date=March–April 2009|work=[[Boston Review]]|accessdate=9 February 2012|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120212194834/http://bostonreview.net/BR34.2/moore.php|archive-date=12 February 2012|url-status=dead}}</ref> Her 1980 collection ''The Shameless Hussy'' won the [[American Book Award]] in 1981. She is featured in the feminist history film ''[[She's Beautiful When She's Angry]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.shesbeautifulwhenshesangry.com/women/|title=The Women|publisher=}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.shesbeautifulwhenshesangry.com/the-film/ |title=The Film — She's Beautiful When She's Angry |publisher=Shesbeautifulwhenshesangry.com |date= |accessdate=2017-04-28}}</ref>

==Biography==

===Shameless Hussy Press===
Alta started Shameless Hussy Press in 1969.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://library.ucsc.edu/reg-hist/alta|title=Alta and the History of Shameless Hussy Press, 1969-1989|publisher=[[University of California, Santa Cruz]]|accessdate=9 February 2012}}</ref> The first women-owned feminist press in California, it opened during the time of [[second-wave feminism]]. Alta used a printing press in her garage to publish books by authors such as [[Susan Griffin]], [[Pat Parker]], and [[Mitsuye Yamada]].<ref name=love>{{cite book|last=Love|first=Barbara J.|title=Feminists who changed America, 1963-1975|url=https://archive.org/details/feministswhochan00love|url-access=registration|accessdate=9 February 2012|year=2006|publisher=U of Illinois P|isbn=978-0-252-03189-2|pages=[https://archive.org/details/feministswhochan00love/page/11 11]–12}}</ref> Yamada later described Alta as an "energetic feminist poet" who promoted Yamada's first volume of poetry "at women’s conferences, women’s health centers, and lesbian bars."<ref>{{cite book|last=Yamada|first=Mitsuye|authorlink=Mitsuye Yamada|editor=Jennifer Sinor|others=Rona Kaufman|title=Placing the academy: essays on landscape, work, and identity|url=http://digitalcommons.usu.edu/cgi/viewcontent.cgi?article=1019&context=usupress_pubs|year=2007|publisher=Utah State UP|isbn=978-0-87421-657-8|pages=125–38|chapter=Living in a Transformed Desert}}</ref> The press published the first edition of ''For Colored Girls Who Have Considered Suicide, When the Rainbow is Enuf'' by [[Ntozake Shange]], and [[Mary Mackey]]'s first novel, ''Immersion'' (1972). They also published poetry by men: "Alta reasoned that since 6 percent of the books published in the U.S. were by women, 6 percent of the books she published should be by men."<ref name=love/>

The press closed in 1989; its archive is held at [[University of California Santa Cruz]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.oac.cdlib.org/findaid/ark:/13030/tf396nb2dv|title=Guide to the Shameless Hussy Press records, 1968-1989|year=2009|publisher=[[University of California, Santa Cruz]]|accessdate=9 February 2012}}</ref>

===Poetry and prose===
Her first volume of [[feminist poetry]], ''Freedom's in Sight'', was published in 1969,<ref>{{cite book|last=Heinemann|first=Sue|title=Timelines of American women's history|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=5kjkZjvnI-sC&pg=PA326|accessdate=9 February 2012|date=1996-03-01|publisher=Penguin|isbn=978-0-399-51986-4|page=326}}</ref> and some of her poems were anthologized in such collections as ''From Feminism to Liberation'' (Philip G. Altbach and Edith S. Hoshino, eds, 1971).<ref>{{cite book|last=Yates|first=Gayle Graham|title=What Women Want: The Ideas of the Movement|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=JkoUaOISQHwC&pg=PA115|accessdate=9 February 2012|date=1975-12-12|publisher=Harvard UP|isbn=978-0-674-95079-5|pages=ix, 115–16}}</ref> Her 1980 collected works ''The Shameless Hussy'' (Crossing Press) won the American Book Award in 1981.<ref name=love/><ref>{{cite web |author=American Booksellers Association |title=The American Book Awards&nbsp;/&nbsp;Before Columbus Foundation [1980–2012] |year=2013 |url=http://www.bookweb.org/btw/awards/The-American-Book-Awards---Before-Columbus-Foundation.html |work=BookWeb |quote='''''1981''''' [...] ''The Shameless Hussy, '''alta''''' |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20130313174235/http://bookweb.org/btw/awards/The-American-Book-Awards---Before-Columbus-Foundation.html |archivedate=March 13, 2013 |accessdate=September 25, 2013}}</ref>

===Personal life===
Alta started the Shameless Hussy Press with her second husband. She wrote a volume of "blatant lesbian poems", ''Letters to Women'' (1969).<ref name=love/> After the press closed she started operating an art gallery in [[Berkeley, California]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.insidebayarea.com/ci_13026815?source=pkg|title=Berkeley gallery owner tries to grasp the art of staying solvent|date=August 9, 2009|first=Chris|last=Metinko|work=East Bay Times|accessdate=9 February 2012}}</ref>
