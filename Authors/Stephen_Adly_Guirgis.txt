'''Stephen Adly Guirgis''' is an American playwright, screenwriter, director, and actor. He is a member and a former co-artistic director of [[New York City]]'s [[LAByrinth Theater Company]].<ref name="Comin' Uptown">Blake, Leslie (Hoban). [http://www.theatermania.com/content/news.cfm/story/2478 "Comin' Uptown"], ''Theatermania'', 23 August 2002.</ref> His plays have been produced both [[Off-Broadway]] and on [[Broadway theatre|Broadway]] as well as in the UK. His play ''[[Between Riverside and Crazy]]'' won the 2015 [[Pulitzer Prize for Drama]].

==Early life==
Guirgis is the son of an [[Egyptians|Egyptian]] father and an [[Irish American]] mother.<ref name="BTG interview">Fisher, Philip. [http://www.britishtheatreguide.info/otherresources/interviews/StephenAdlyGuirgis.htm Interviews: Stephen Adly Guirgis], ''BritishTheatreGuide.info'', 2001 (sic).</ref> He was raised on [[New York City]]'s [[Upper West Side]]. He attended school in nearby [[Harlem]] and graduated from [[University at Albany, SUNY]] in 1992.<ref name="Comin' Uptown" /><ref name="BTG interview" /> He studied theatre at [[HB Studio]].<ref>[https://hbstudio.org/about-hb-studio/alumni/ HB Studio Alumni]</ref>

==Career==
===Writing===
Guirgis' play ''[[Between Riverside and Crazy]]'' premiered [[Off-Broadway]] at the [[Atlantic Theater Company]] in July 2014 and closed on August 23, 2014.<ref name=atlantic>Hetrick, Adam and Purcell, Carey. [http://www.playbill.com/news/article/stephen-adly-guirgis-premiere-between-riverside-and-crazy-closes-at-the-atl-327048 "Stephen Adly Guirgis Premiere, 'Between Riverside And Crazy', Closes at the Atlantic"] playbill.com, August 23, 2014</ref> It then was produced by [[Second Stage Theatre]], opening in previews on January 16, 2015, officially on February 11, 2015. Directed by [[Austin Pendleton]], the play featured [[Stephen McKinley Henderson]].<ref>Cox, Gordon. [https://variety.com/2014/legit/news/off-broadways-second-stage-replaces-american-psycho-with-between-riverside-and-crazy-1201331892/ "Replaces 'American Psycho' with 'Between Riverside and Crazy'"], ''Variety'', October 16, 2014.</ref> The play received a nomination for the 2015 [[Drama League Award]], Outstanding Production of a Broadway or Off-Broadway Play.<ref>Gans, Andrew. [http://www.playbill.com/news/article/2015-drama-league-awards-nominations-announced-more-than-45-will-vie-for-distinguished-performance-honor-347266?r=n "2015 Drama League Awards Nominations Announced; More Than 45 Will Vie for Distinguished Performance Honor"] {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150623164630/http://www.playbill.com/news/article/2015-drama-league-awards-nominations-announced-more-than-45-will-vie-for-distinguished-performance-honor-347266?r=n |date=June 23, 2015 }} playbill.com, April 21, 2015.</ref> The play won the 2015 [[Outer Critics Circle]] Award, Outstanding New Off-Broadway Play.<ref>Clement, Olivia. [http://www.playbill.com/news/article/curious-incident-top-winner-at-outer-critics-circle-awards-348762 " 'Curious Incident' Top Winner at Outer Critics Circle Awards"], playbill.com, May 10, 2015.</ref> The play won the 2015 [[New York Drama Critics Circle Award]] for Best Play.<ref>Hetrick, Adam. [http://www.playbill.com/news/article/hamilton-and-between-riverside-and-crazy-win-2015-new-york-drama-critics-circle-awards-348317 " 'Hamilton' and 'Between Riverside and Crazy' Win 2015 New York Drama Critics' Circle Awards"] playbill.com, May 4, 2015</ref> The play won the 2015 [[Lucille Lortel Award]], Outstanding Play.<ref>Staff. [http://www.playbill.com/news/article/hamilton-breaks-record-with-most-lortel-wins-348764 "'Hamilton' Breaks Record With Most Lortel Wins"], playbill.com, May 10, 2015.</ref>
[[Artists Repertory Theatre]], located in Portland, Oregon, will stage ''Between Riverside and Crazy'' from March 4 to April 1, 2018.<ref>[https://www.artistsrep.org/onstage/201718-season/ "2017/18 Season"] artistsrep.org</ref>

His play ''[[The Motherfucker With the Hat]]'' premiered on [[Broadway theatre|Broadway]] in 2011 and featured [[Bobby Cannavale]], [[Chris Rock]], [[Elizabeth Rodriguez]], Annabella Sciorra and Yul Vazquez.<ref name="NYTimes Hat announce">Healy, Patrick. ''Judas Iscariot'' premiered Off-Broadway on March 2, 2005 at [[The Public Theatre]] and was directed by [[Philip Seymour Hoffman]].<ref>[http://www.lortel.org/LLA_archive/index.cfm?search_by=show&id=4080 " 'Judas Iscariot' Listing"], lortel.org, accessed April 21, 2015.</ref> The play completed a critically acclaimed run in London at the Almeida Theater on May 10, 2008.<ref name="Telegraph When Judas">Rees, Jasper. His play ''Our Lady of 121st Street'' ran originally in an Off-Broadway production by the LAByrinth Theater at Center Stage/NY and then transferred to the Union Square Theatre from March 6, 2003 to July 27, 2003. ''[[Jesus Hopped the 'A' Train]]'', premiered Off-Broadway in a production by LAByrinth Theater Company in 2000.<ref>[http://www.lortel.org/lla_archive/index.cfm?search_by=show&id=3102 "Listing"] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150417023611/http://www.lortel.org/LLA_archive/index.cfm?search_by=show&id=3102 |date=April 17, 2015 }}, lortel.org, accessed May 5, 2015.</ref><ref>Gutman, Les. === Controversy ===
In mid-2017 Guirgis was embroiled in a public copyright debate with a small theater in San Francisco who had produced a highly edited and altered version of ''The Last Days of Judas Iscariot''. While he was initially sympathetic to the issues surrounding the production, when the theater refused to provide a suitable insert in the show notes explaining their edits were made without the playwright's permission, Guirgis became incensed and had the show shut down.<ref name="playbill.com">{{Cite web|url=http://www.playbill.com/article/stephen-adly-guirgis-speaks-out-after-shutting-down-theatre-for-unauthorized-cuts-to-judas-iscariot|title=Stephen Adly Guirgis Speaks Out After Shutting Down Theatre for Unauthorized Cuts to Judas Iscariot {{!}} Playbill|website=Playbill|language=en|access-date=12 April 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |last1=Musbach |first1=Julie |title=UPDATE: Shelton Theatre's Unsanctioned Adaptation is Shut Down Due to Copyright Infringement |url=https://www.broadwayworld.com/article/UPDATE-Shelton-Theatres-Unsanctioned-Adaptation-is-Shut-Down-Due-to-Copyright-Infringement-20170806 |access-date=12 April 2019 |work=Broadway World |date=6 August 2017}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Guirgis has used improvisational theater to "teach [[HIV/AIDS]] prevention, conflict resolution, and leadership" in prisons, schools, shelters, and hospitals.<ref>Klein, Judith M. [https://www.wsj.com/articles/SB10001424052702304432304576372001158163540 "Growing Up With the Hat"], ''The New York Times'', June 21, 2011.</ref> Guirgis was close professional and personal friends with late actor [[Philip Seymour Hoffman]]<ref name="playbill.com"/> with whom he was a frequent collaborator.

==Awards, nominations, and honors==
Guirgis was awarded a 2006 [[PEN/Laura Pels Theater Award]] for a playwright in mid-career,<ref>Hernandez, Ernio. [http://www.playbill.com/news/article/adrienne-kennedy-and-stephen-adly-guirgis-win-2006-pen-laura-pels-awards-fo-132340 "Adrienne Kennedy and Stephen Adly Guirgis Win 2006 PEN/ Laura Pels Awards for Drama"] playbill.com, May 2, 2006.</ref>
a 2006 [[Whiting Awards|Whiting Award]],<ref>[http://www.whiting.org/awards/winners "2006 Winner, Drama"] whiting.org, accessed April 21, 2015.</ref> and a 2004 TCG fellowship. He attended the 2006 Sundance Screenwriter's Lab,
<ref>[http://www.filmmakers.com/news/film_festivals/printer_554.shtml "Six Artists Selected For The 2006 Sundance Playwrights Retreat"] 
filmmakers.com, January 26, 2006</ref> and was named one of 2004's 25 New Faces of Independent Film by ''[[Filmmaker Magazine]]''.

In 2014, Guirgis was awarded the Steinberg Distinguished Playwright Award.

Guirgis is the recipient of new play commissions from [[Manhattan Theatre Club]], Center Theater Group, and [[South Coast Repertory]]. He is a member of New Dramatists, MCC's Playwright's Coalition, New River Dramatists, Primary Stages, and [[The Actor's Studio]] Playwright/Directors Unit.

He received the 2013 [[Windham–Campbell Literature Prize]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://news.yale.edu/2013/03/04/yale-awards-135-million-nine-writers |title=Yale awards $1.35 million to nine writers |work=YaleNews |author=Dorie Baker |date=March 4, 2013 |access-date=March 5, 2013}}</ref>

He received the 2015 [[Pulitzer Prize for Drama]] for ''Between Riverside and Crazy''<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.pulitzer.org/citation/2015-Drama| title=The Pulitzer Prizes Drama}}</ref>

''Our Lady of 121st Street'' received the Lucille Lortel Award nomination, Outstanding Play, Outstanding Director (Hoffman); 2003 Outer Critics Circle Award nomination, John Glassner Award; Drama Desk Award nomination, Director (Hoffman) and Outstanding Play<ref>[http://www.lortel.org/LLA_archive/index.cfm?search_by=show&id=2516 "Listing"] lortel.org, accessed April 22, 2015</ref>

He received a Literature Award from the [[American Academy of Arts and Letters]] in May 2016.
