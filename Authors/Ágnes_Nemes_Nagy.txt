'''Ágnes Nemes Nagy''' (January 3, 1922<ref name=wilson/> &ndash; August 23, 1991<ref>{{cite web |url=http://pim.hu/object.ECB6CD5D-814E-482A-80D9-776811D67AAD.ivy |title=Nemes Nagy Ágnes életrajza |work=Digitális Irodalmi Akadémia |language=hu}}</ref>) was a [[Hungary|Hungarian]] poet, writer, educator, and translator.<ref name=wilson/>

She was born in [[Budapest]] and earned a teaching diploma from the [[University of Budapest]]. From 1945 to 1953, she was employed by the education journal ''Köznevelés'';<ref name=wilson/> from 1953 to 1957, she taught high school.<ref name=geometry/> After 1957, she devoted herself to writing.<ref name=wilson/>

Following [[World War II]], Nemes Nagy worked on a literary periodical ''Újhold'' (New Moon); the editor was critic [[Balázs Lengyel (critic)|Balázs Lengyel]], who she later married.  The magazine was eventually banned by the government of the time.<ref name=geometry/>  In 1946, Nemes Nagy published her first volume of poetry ''Kettős világban'' (In a dual world).  In 1948, she was awarded the [[Baumgarten Prize]].  During the 1950s, her own work was suppressed and she worked as a translator, translating the works of [[Molière]], [[Jean Racine|Racine]], [[Pierre Corneille|Corneille]], [[Bertolt Brecht]] and others.<ref name=wilson>{{cite book |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=ncN7uneLKrcC&pg=PA894 |title=An Encyclopedia of Continental Women Writers |page=894 |volume=Volume 1 |last=Wilson |first=Katharina M |year=1991 |isbn=0824085477}}</ref>
