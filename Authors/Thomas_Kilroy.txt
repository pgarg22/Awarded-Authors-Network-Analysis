'''Thomas F. Kilroy''' (born 23 September 1934) is an Irish playwright and novelist.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.theguardian.com/books/2008/may/03/featuresreviews.guardianreview3|title=Back – due to popular demand|last=Friel|first=Brian|date=3 May 2008|newspaper=The Guardian|accessdate=16 May 2011}}</ref>

He was born in Green Street, [[Callan, County Kilkenny]].{{citation need|date=April 2020}} He attended [[St Kieran's College]] and played [[hurling]] for the school team, captaining the senior team in 1952.<ref>{{cite news|first=Jim|last=Carney|authorlink=Jim Carney (poet)|url=https://www.independent.ie/sport/gaelic-games/why-have-football-and-hurling-remained-a-cultural-wasteland-for-our-writers-and-artists-39103123.html|title=Why have football and hurling remained a cultural wasteland for our writers and artists?|work=[[Sunday Independent (Ireland)|Sunday Independent]]|date=5 April 2020}}</ref> He studied at [[University College Dublin]].{{citation needed|date=April 2020}} In his early career he was play editor at the [[Abbey Theatre, Dublin]]. In the 1980s, he sat on the board of [[Field Day Theatre Company]], founded by [[Brian Friel]] and [[Stephen Rea]] in 1980, and was Director of its touring company.

In 1978, Kilroy was appointed Professor of English at [[NUI Galway|University College Galway]],<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.nuigalway.ie/creativearts/drama/|title=Drama at NUI Galway: 1960s – 1970s|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20170614223246/https://www.nuigalway.ie/creativearts/drama/|archivedate=14 June 2017}}</ref> a post from which he resigned in 1989 to concentrate on writing.

Kilroy now lives in [[County Mayo]] and is a member of the [[Irish Academy of Letters]], the [[Royal Society of Literature]], and [[Aosdána]].

The Thomas Kilroy Collection, his personal archive, was deposited at Galway University's [[James Hardiman Library]]; Kilroy addressed the launch event in March 2011, which was attended by, amongst others, [[Brian Friel]] and the future President of Ireland [[Michael D. Higgins]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://library.nuigalway.ie/collections/archives/depositedcollections/featuredcollections/thomaskilroycollection/|title=Thomas Kilroy Collection|accessdate=14 October 2018}} Shortly after 1 minute 40 seconds into the video, Lionel Pilkington introduces Kilroy as, among other things, "a former Professor of English at this university".</ref>
