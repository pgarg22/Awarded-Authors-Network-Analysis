'''Serafín Álvarez Quintero''' (March 26, 1871 – April 12, 1938) and '''Joaquín Álvarez Quintero''' (January 20, 1873 – June 14, 1944) were [[Spanish people|Spanish]] [[dramatist]]s.

==Lives and career==
Born in [[Utrera]], [[Seville Province]], they wrote almost 200 plays which won {{lang|es|Los hermanos Quintero}} ([[Spanish language]]: Quintero brothers) fame as the Golden Boys of the Madrid theatre. Their first stage piece, {{lang|es|Gilito}}, was written in 1889. Other works include {{lang|es|El buena sombra}} (1898), {{lang|es|El traje de luces}} and {{lang|es|La patria chica}} (1907), {{lang|es|El patinillo}} (1909), {{lang|es|Becquerina}} and {{lang|es|Diana cazadora}} (1915).

They were also famous for having tried to transcribe [[Andalusian dialect]]s to written form.
