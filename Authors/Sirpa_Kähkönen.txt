{{short description|Finnish novelist and translator (born 1964)}}
[[File:Sirpa kahkonen helsinki book fair 2018.jpg|thumb|Sirpa Kähkönen at Helsinki Book Fair, 2018.]]
'''Sirpa Kähkönen''' (born 15 September 1964) is a Finnish [[novelist]] and translator.<ref name=oga>{{cite web|url=http://oga.otava.fi/authors/sirpa-kahkonen/|title=Sirpa Kähkönen|publisher=OGA|accessdate=26 January 2015 |language=}}</ref> Initially writing for young adults, she gained popularity in [[Finland]] with her Kuopio series of historical novels.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://yle.fi/uutiset/six_novelists_vie_for_finlands_top_fiction_prize/7630587|title=Six novelists vie for Finland’s top fiction prize|publisher=yle|accessdate=26 January 2015 |language=}}</ref>

Born in [[Kuopio]], Kähkönen studied literature and history before working as an editor. She embarked on her literary career in 1991 with ''Kuu taskussa'' (Moon in your Pocket) for young adults, publishing her first adult novel ''Mustat morsiamet'' (Black Brides, 1998), which earned her the [[Savonia Award]] in 1999.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.otava.fi/kirjailijat/sirpa_kahkonen/|title=Sirpa Kähkönen|publisher=Otava|accessdate=26 January 2015 |language=Finnish}}</ref> Her latest work ''Graniittimies'' (Granite Man) is a historical novel depicting the lives of young Finns in Soviet times.<ref name=oga/>
