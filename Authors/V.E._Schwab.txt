'''Victoria (V. E.) Schwab''' (born July 7, 1987) is an American [[fantasy]] author best known for her 2013 novel ''[[Vicious (novel)|Vicious]]'', the ''Shades of Magic'' series, ''[[The Invisible Life of Addie LaRue]]'', and her [[children's literature|children's]] and [[young adult fiction]] books published under the name '''Victoria Schwab'''.

==Writing==

''[[The Guardian]]'' called ''Vicious'' "a brilliant exploration of the superhero, ''Mythos'', and a riveting revenge thriller".<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.theguardian.com/books/2014/jan/09/science-fiction-roundup-reviews |title=Science fiction roundup - reviews |date=January 9, 2014 |access-date=January 18, 2015 |first=Eric |last=Brown |newspaper=[[The Guardian]]}}</ref> Additionally, it received a [[starred review]] from ''[[Publishers Weekly]]'',<ref name="PW Vicious">{{cite web |url=http://www.publishersweekly.com/9780765335340 |title=Fiction Review: ''Vicious'' by V. E. Schwab |work=[[Publishers Weekly]] |date=2013 |access-date=January 19, 2015}}</ref> which also named the novel one of its best books of 2013 for Sci-Fi/Fantasy/Horror.<ref name="PW Book Deals">{{cite web |url=http://www.publishersweekly.com/pw/by-topic/industry-news/book-deals/article/60986-book-deals-week-of-february-10-2014.html |title=Book Deals: Week of February 10, 2014 |work=Publishers Weekly |first=Rachel |last=Deahl |date=February 10, 2014 |access-date=January 19, 2015}}</ref> The [[American Library Association]]'s [[Reference and User Services Association]] likewise awarded it the top fantasy book in their 2014 Reading List.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ala.org/news/press-releases/2014/01/rusas-2014-reading-list-winners-revealed-librarians-top-picks-genre-fiction |title=RUSA's 2014 Reading List winners revealed: Librarians' top picks in genre fiction |publisher=[[American Library Association]] |date=January 26, 2014 |access-date=January 20, 2015}}</ref> In late 2013, the rights for a film adaptation of ''Vicious'' were bought jointly by Story Mining & Supply Co and [[Ridley Scott]]'s: [[Scott Free Productions]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://deadline.com/2013/12/ridley-scotts-scott-free-teams-with-story-mining-supply-on-vicious-deal-653785/ |title=Ridley Scott's Scott Free Teams With Story Mining & Supply On ''Vicious'' Deal |first=Mike Jr.|last=Fleming |newspaper=[[Deadline Hollywood]] |date=December 17, 2013}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.tracking-board.com/tb-exclusive-hit-list-scribe-alexander-felix-to-adapt-vicious-for-scott-free-and-story-mining-and-supply-co/ |title=Hit List Scribe Alexander Felix To Adapt ''Vicious'' For Scott Free And Story Mining & Supply Co. |publisher=[[The Tracking Board]] |first=Madison |last=Barnes |date=May 20, 2014 |access-date=January 19, 2015}}</ref>

In 2014, Schwab signed a two-book deal with [[Tor Books]],<ref name="PW Book Deals" /> which included ''Vicious'' and her next novel, ''A Darker Shade of Magic.'' The latter was published in February 2015, and also received a starred review from ''Publishers Weekly''.<ref name="PW Darker">{{cite web |url=http://www.publishersweekly.com/978-0-7653-7645-9 |title=Fiction Review: ''A Darker Shade of Magic'' by V. E. Schwab |work=Publishers Weekly |date=2015 |access-date=January 19, 2015}}</ref> In 2017, she signed another book deal with Tor for ''Vengeful'', the sequel to ''Vicious''; a new trilogy set called ''Threads of Power'', which takes place in the same world as the Shades of Magic series; and an "homage to ''Blade Runner''" called ''Black Tabs''.<ref>Tor.com. [https://www.tor.com/2017/08/28/ve-schwab-new-shades-of-magic-trilogy-tor-books-deal/ "V.E. Schwab Writing New Trilogy Set in the Shades of Magic Universe"]. August 28, 2017</ref>

In May 2018, Schwab gave the sixth annual [[Tolkien Lecture]] at [[Pembroke College, Oxford]].<ref>[https://tolkienlecture.org/2018/05/02/video-for-victoria-v-e-schwabs-tolkien-lecture/], 'Video and Photos for Victoria (V.E.) Schwab's Tolkien Lecture', 2 May 2018. Retrieved 15 May 2020.</ref>

== Education ==
Schwab graduated from [[Washington University in St. Louis]] with a [[Bachelor of Fine Arts]] in 2009. She had originally planned to study [[Astrophysics]], but changed directions after taking art and literature courses. She completed her first novel (unpublished) in her sophomore year.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Rhea|first=Ryan|date=2021-06-21|title=Author V. E. Schwab adds some magic to everyday life|url=https://source.wustl.edu/2021/06/author-v-e-schwab-adds-some-magic-to-everyday-life/|url-status=live|access-date=2021-06-23|website=The Source|language=en-US}}</ref> She sold her debut novel, ''The Near Witch'', to [[Disney]] before graduating.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|last=Charaipotra|first=Sona|date=August 8, 2020|title=The Book of V.E. Schwab's Heart|url=https://www.publishersweekly.com/pw/by-topic/authors/profiles/article/84041-the-book-of-v-e-schwab-s-heart.html|url-status=live|access-date=2021-06-24|website=Publishers Weekly|language=en}}</ref>

== Personal life ==
Schwab grew up in [[Nashville, Tennessee]] and has lived in [[St. Louis]], [[Brooklyn]], [[Liverpool]], and [[Edinburgh]]. She first came out as [[gay]] at age 28.<ref name=":0" />
