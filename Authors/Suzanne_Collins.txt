'''Suzanne Collins''' (born August 10, 1962) is an American television writer and author. She is known as the author of [[The New York Times Best Seller list|''The New York Times'' best-selling series]] ''[[The Underland Chronicles]]'' and ''[[The Hunger Games]]''.

==Early life==
Suzanne Collins was born on August 10, 1962, in [[Hartford, Connecticut]] to Jane Brady Collins (born 1932) and Lieutenant Colonel Michael John Collins (1931–2003),<ref name="Llanas2012">{{cite book |last=Llanas |first=Sheila Griffin |title=How to Analyze the Works of Suzanne Collins |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=Gc0jMjFryM0C&pg=PA13 |access-date=August 8, 2018 |date=August 1, 2012 |publisher=ABDO |isbn=9781614789574 |page=13 }}</ref> a [[United States Air Force|U.S. Air Force]] officer who served in the [[Korean War]] and the [[Vietnam War]]. She is the youngest of four children, who include Kathryn (born 1957), Andrew (born 1958), and Joan (born 1960). As the [[Military brat (U.S. subculture)|daughter of a military officer]], she and her family were constantly moving. She spent her childhood in the eastern United States.<ref name="air force"/>

Collins graduated from the [[Alabama School of Fine Arts]] in [[Birmingham, Alabama|Birmingham]] in 1980 as a Theater Arts major.<ref>{{cite web|last=Harvey|first=Alec|title=Did you know 'Hunger Games' author Suzanne Collins has an Alabama connection |url=http://www.al.com/entertainment/index.ssf/2012/03/hunger_games_suzanne_collins_a.html|work=Birmingham News|access-date=September 13, 2012|date=March 23, 2010}}</ref> She completed her bachelor of arts degree from [[Indiana University Bloomington]] in 1985 with a double major in theater and telecommunications.<ref name="fourth">{{cite web |title=Suzanne Collins Interview by Deborah Hopkinson on BookPage |url=http://www.bookpage.com/books-10012268-Catching-Fire |publisher=BookPage |date=September 2012 |access-date=December 15, 2009 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://archive.today/20130102035706/http://www.bookpage.com/books-10012268-Catching-Fire |archive-date=January 2, 2013 }}</ref><ref name="fifth">{{cite web |title='Hunger Games' author Suzanne Collins graduated from IU |url=http://newsinfo.iu.edu/news/page/normal/21670.html |publisher=Indiana University |date=March 22, 2017 |access-date=March 25, 2013 }}</ref><ref name="biography.com">{{cite web|url=http://www.biography.com/people/suzanne-collins-20903551|title=Suzanne Collins|work=biography.com|publisher=A&E Networks|access-date=September 13, 2013}}</ref> In 1989, Collins earned her [[Master of Fine Arts]] in dramatic writing from the [[New York University]] [[Tisch School of the Arts]].<ref name="biography.com"/>

==Career==
Suzanne Collins began her career in 1991 as a writer for [[children's television]] shows.<ref name="official">{{cite web | title=Biography | url=http://www.suzannecollinsbooks.com/bio.htm | work=www.suzannecollinsbooks.com | access-date=December 14, 2008 }}</ref> She worked on several shows for [[Nickelodeon (TV channel)|Nickelodeon]], including ''[[Clarissa Explains It All]]'', ''[[The Mystery Files of Shelby Woo]]'', ''[[Little Bear (TV series)|Little Bear]]'', and ''[[Oswald (TV series)|Oswald]]''.<ref name="official"/> She did not write the children's book Little Bear, which is sometimes mistaken as her own book. She was also the head writer for Scholastic Entertainment's ''[[Clifford's Puppy Days]]''.<ref name="official"/> She received a [[Writers Guild of America]] nomination in animation for co-writing the critically acclaimed Christmas special, ''Santa, Baby!''<ref name="scholastic">{{cite web | title=Suzanne Collins Biography | url=http://www2.scholastic.com/browse/contributor.jsp?id=2710 | work=Scholastic | access-date=December 14, 2008 | archive-date=March 7, 2010 | archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100307234140/http://www2.scholastic.com/browse/contributor.jsp?id=2710 | url-status=dead }}</ref> After meeting children's author James Proimos while working on the [[Kids' WB]] show ''[[Generation O!]]'', Collins felt inspired to write [[children's literature|children's books]] herself.<ref name="official"/> Her inspiration for ''[[Gregor the Overlander]]'', the first book of ''[[The New York Times]]'' best-selling series ''[[The Underland Chronicles]]'', came from ''[[Alice in Wonderland]]'', when she was thinking about how one was more likely to fall down a manhole than a rabbit hole, and would find something other than a tea party.<ref name="official"/><ref name="scholastic"/>  Between 2003 and 2007 she wrote the five books of the ''[[Underland Chronicles]]'': ''[[Gregor the Overlander]]'', ''[[Gregor and the Prophecy of Bane]]'', ''[[Gregor and the Curse of the Warmbloods]]'', ''[[Gregor and the Marks of Secret]]'', and ''[[Gregor and the Code of Claw]]''. During that time, Collins also wrote a rhyming picture book, ''When Charlie McButton Lost Power'' (2005), illustrated by Mike Lester.<ref name="official"/>

In September 2008, [[Scholastic Press]] released ''[[The Hunger Games (novel)|The Hunger Games]]'', the first book of a trilogy by Collins.<ref>{{cite interview |last=Collins |first=Suzanne |title=Planning the Trilogy |url=http://www.scholastic.ca/thehungergames/videos/planning-the-trilogy.htm |type=Interview: Video |work=[[Scholastic Canada]] |access-date=December 14, 2008 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090212153709/http://www.scholastic.ca/thehungergames/videos/planning-the-trilogy.htm |archive-date=February 12, 2009 }}</ref> ''The Hunger Games'' was partly inspired by the Greek myth of [[Theseus#Theseus and the Minotaur|Theseus and the Minotaur]]. Another inspiration was her father's career in the [[United States Air Force|Air Force]], which gave her insight to poverty, starvation, and the effects of war.<ref name="air force">{{cite web |first=Suzanne |last=Collins |title=A Conversation with Suzanne Collins, Q & A. |url=http://www.scholastic.com/thehungergames/media/qanda.pdf |publisher=Scholastic |access-date=February 9, 2010 }}</ref> The trilogy's second book, ''[[Catching Fire]]'', was released in September 2009, and its third book, ''[[Mockingjay]]'', was released on August 24, 2010.<ref>{{cite web |first=Keith |last=Staskiewicz |title=Final 'Hunger Games' novel has been given a title and a cover |url=http://shelf-life.ew.com/2010/02/11/final-hunger-games-novel-has-been-given-a-title-and-a-cover/ | work=Entertainment Weekly |date=February 11, 2010 |access-date=February 11, 2010 }}</ref> Within 14 months, 1.5 million copies of the first two ''Hunger Games'' books were printed in [[North America]] alone.<ref name="collins">{{cite web |first=Suzanne | last=Collins |title=Suzanne Collins's Third Book in The Hunger Games Trilogy to be Published on August 24, 2010. |url=http://mediaroom.scholastic.com/node/270 |publisher=Scholastic |access-date=February 9, 2010 }}</ref> ''The Hunger Games'' was on ''The New York Times'' Best Seller list for more than 60 weeks in a row.<ref name="collins" /> [[Lions Gate Entertainment]] acquired worldwide distribution rights to [[The Hunger Games (film)|a film adaptation of ''The Hunger Games'']], produced by [[Nina Jacobson]]'s Color Force production company.<ref name="Hungry">{{cite web|url=http://www.publishersweekly.com/article/CA6643639.html?industryid=47141 |title=Hungry? The Latest on 'The Hunger Games' |access-date=March 14, 2009 |first=John A. |last=Sellers |date=March 12, 2009 |work=[[Publishers Weekly]] |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090602053340/http://www.publishersweekly.com/article/CA6643639.html?industryid=47141 |archive-date=2 June 2009 }}</ref><ref name="Lionsgate">{{cite news|first1=Jay A. |last1=Fe |first2=Borys |last2=Kit |title=Lionsgateick'HungerGames'|url=http://www.hollywoodreporter.com/hr/content_display/news/e3ic096395f454426da4ab38f45275a12fc |newspaper=The Hollywood Reporter |date=March 17, 2009 |access-date=March 18, 2009 |url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090322035715/http://www.hollywoodreporter.com/hr/content_display/news/e3ic096395f454426da4ab38f45275a12fc |archive-date=March 22, 2009 }}</ref> Collins adapted the novel for film herself.<ref name="Lionsgate" /> Directed by [[Gary Ross]], filming began in late spring 2011, with [[Jennifer Lawrence]] portraying main character [[Katniss Everdeen]].<ref>Weinstein, Joshua L. [http://www.thewrap.com/movies/column-post/jennifer-lawrence-gets-lead-role-hunger-games-25482 "Jennifer Lawrence Gets Lead Role in 'The Hunger Games'"], TheWrap.com. March 16, 2011. Retrieved March 17, 2011.</ref> [[Josh Hutcherson]] played [[Peeta Mellark]] and [[Liam Hemsworth]] played [[Gale Hawthorne]].<ref name="HungerGamesFan.com">{{cite web|url=http://www.hungergamesfan.com/2011/04/hunger-games-peeta-and-gale-casting-responses/|work=HungerGamesfan.com|title=Hunger Games Peeta and Gale Casting|access-date=April 9, 2011}}</ref> The subsequent two novels were adapted into films as well, with the latter book split into two cinematic installments, for a total of four films representing the three books. As a result of the popularity of ''The Hunger Games'' books, Collins was named one of [[Time (magazine)|''Time'' magazine]]'s most influential people of 2010.<ref name="time">{{cite news |first=Lizzie |last=Skurnick |title=The 2010 Time 100: Suzanne Collins |url=http://www.time.com/time/specials/packages/article/0,28804,1984685_1984940_1985512,00.html |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100501161323/http://www.time.com/time/specials/packages/article/0,28804,1984685_1984940_1985512,00.html |url-status=dead |archive-date=May 1, 2010 |work=Time |date=April 29, 2010 |access-date=May 2, 2010 }}</ref> In March 2012, Amazon announced that she had become the best-selling Kindle author of all time.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://phx.corporate-ir.net/phoenix.zhtml?c=176060&p=irol-newsArticle&ID=1673532&highlight=|title=Hungry for Hunger Games: Amazon.com Reveals the Top Cities in the U.S. Reading The Hunger Games Trilogy|access-date=March 16, 2012|archive-date=April 11, 2016|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160411203757/http://phx.corporate-ir.net/phoenix.zhtml?c=176060&p=irol-newsArticle&ID=1673532&highlight=|url-status=dead}}</ref> Amazon also revealed that Collins had written 29 of the 100 most highlighted passages in Kindle ebooks—and on a separate Amazon list of recently highlighted passages, she had written 17 of the top 20.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.beyond-black-friday.com/2012/03/17/who-is-the-best-selling-kindle-author-of-all-time/ |title=Who is the Best-Selling Kindle Author of All Time?|access-date=March 17, 2012}}</ref> On June 17, 2019, Collins announced that a prequel to ''The Hunger Games'' would be released on May 19, 2020. The premise is based on the life of future President Coriolanus Snow, 64 years before the events of ''The Hunger Games'' trilogy.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.usnews.com/news/business/articles/2019-06-17/apnewsbreak-hunger-games-prequel-novel-coming-in-2020?linkId=69117198|title=Hunger Games' Prequel Novel Coming in 2020|first= Hillel|last=Italie|date=17 June 2019|access-date=18 June 2019|website=US News}}</ref> On October 4, 2019 the title was revealed to be ''[[The Ballad of Songbirds and Snakes]].''<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.sfgate.com/entertainment/article/Title-book-cover-announced-for-The-Hunger-14492269.php|title=Title, book cover announced for 'The Hunger Games' prequel|date=2019-10-04|website=SFGate|access-date=2019-10-04}}</ref>
