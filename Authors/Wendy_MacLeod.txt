'''Wendy A. MacLeod''' (born August 6, 1959)<ref>''U.S. Public Records Index, Vol 1 (Provo, UT: Ancestry.com Operations, Inc.), 2010.</ref> is an [[United States|American]] [[playwright]].

==Life and career==
MacLeod received a [[Bachelor of Arts|BA]] from [[Kenyon College]] in [[Gambier, Ohio]], where she now teaches and is a playwright-in-residence.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.kenyon.edu/x2998.xml |accessdate=2007-02-08|title=Wendy MacLeod, James Michael Playwright-in-Residence|publisher=Kenyon College}}</ref> She also earned a [[Master of Fine Arts|MFA]] from the [[Yale School of Drama]].<ref>{{cite web| url=https://www.playscripts.com/playwrights/bios/277|accessdate=2015-04-30|title=Wendy MacLeod|publisher=Playscripts}}</ref>

Her works include the plays ''Sin'' and ''Schoolgirl Figure'', both of which premiered at [[Chicago]]'s [[Goodman Theatre]] and were directed by [[David Petrarca]]. ''Schoolgirl Figure'' was then optioned for film by [[HBO]] and Anvil Entertainment. MacLeod's ''[[The House of Yes (play)|The House of Yes]]'' premiered in San Francisco at the Magic Theatre and was the theatre's second-longest running show. It became an [[The House of Yes|award-winning film by the same name]] in 1997, starring [[Parker Posey]], which earned a Special Jury Award at the [[Sundance Film Festival]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://act-sf.org/index.cfm?s_id=&pid=nev_pre_dea&pr=151|accessdate=2007-02-08|title=The A.C.T. Young Conservatory Tackles Controversial, and Hysterical, Play by the Author of The House of Yes|publisher=American Conservatory Theater}}</ref> Other works include ''The Water Children'', ''Things Being What They Are'', ''Juvenilia'', ''Apocalyptic Butterflies''. ''Apocalyptic Butterflies'' was filmed by the BBC as Nativity Blues 1988, starring Alfred Molina.

Her play ''Juvenilia'', a comic drama<ref>{{cite web|url=http://wendymacleod.com/plays/juvenilia.shtml| title = Wendy MacLeod: Juvenilia| accessdate = 2007-03-03| last = MacLeod| first = Wendy}}</ref> about college students "attempting to find love", premiered [[off-Broadway]] at [[Playwrights Horizons]], as did her play ''The Water Children'',  both directed by longtime collaborator Petrarca, which has also been seen at [[Los Angeles]]’ Matrix Theater where it was cited as "the most challenging political play of 1998" by the ''[[L.A. Weekly]]'' and earned six L.A. Drama Critics Circle nominations.<ref>{{cite web| url=http://www.samuelfrench.com/author/101843/wendy-macleod|title=Wendy MacLeod|publisher=Samuel French}}</ref><ref>{{cite web| url=http://articles.latimes.com/1999/feb/03/entertainment/ca-4236|accessdate=2015-04-30|title=L.A. Drama Critics Go for the 'Ganesh'--8 Nominations|work=Los Angeles Times}}</ref> ''Things Being What They Are'' premiered at the [[Seattle Repertory Theatre]] and was then seen at [[Steppenwolf Theatre Company|Steppenwolf]] in Chicago in 2003 where its sold-out run was extended twice.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.kenyon.edu/directories/campus-directory/biography/wendy-macleod/|accessdate=2015-04-30|title=Wendy MacLeod|publisher=Kenyon College}}</ref> ''The House of Yes'' has been performed at [[Soho Repertory Theatre]], at the [[Maxim Gorki Theater]] in [[Berlin]] and at The Gate Theater in [[London]], where it was published in ''Plays International''. MacLeod's play, ''[http://www.pioneertheatre.org/2011-2012-season/find-and-sign/ Find and Sign]'', premiered at [http://www.pioneertheatre.org/ Pioneer Theatre Company] in Salt Lake City, Utah in 2012. Set in the New York City music industry (with a slight nod to ''Othello''), ''Find and Sign'' is about a bumpy romance between an on-the-rise young record executive and an idealistic public school teacher.<ref>{{cite web| url=http://wendymacleod.com/plays/find_and_sign.shtml|accessdate=2015-04-30|title=Wendy MacLeod Find and Sign|publisher=Wendy MacLeod}}</ref>

Her critically acclaimed comedy ''Women in Jeopardy!'' premiered at Geva Theater in 2015, directed by Sean Daniels, and her newest play ''Slow Food'' was invited to the 2015 National Playwrights Conference. The play will be premiering at Merrimack Repertory Theater in January 2019.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://variety.com/2015/legit/news/oneill-national-playwrights-conference-2015-plays-1201472465/|accessdate=2015-04-30|title=Women in Jeopardy!|publisher=Geva Theatre Center}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.gevatheatre.org/shows/women-in-jeopardy-new/|accessdate=2015-04-30|title=O'Neill National Playwrights Conference Sets 2015 Slate|publisher=Variety}}</ref> She has been a guest professor at [[Northwestern University]]’s film and theater departments. MacLeod's essay "Name Brand Nostalgia"<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2017/02/17/opinion/sunday/name-brand-nostalgia.html?_r=0|accessdate=2017-05-16|title=Wendy MacLeod:"Name Brand Nostalgia"|work=[[The New York Times]]}}</ref> was recently featured in ''[[The New York Times]]'' and her essay/talk "The Daily Struggle"<ref>{{cite web|url=http://therumpus.net/2016/11/the-daily-struggle/|accessdate=2017-05-16|title=Wendy MacLeod: "The Daily Struggle"}}</ref> was given as part of the Kenyon Review's Writers-on-Writing series in October 2016. Her prose and humor pieces have appeared in [[Poetry Magazine|''Poetry'' magazine]], ''[[The New York Times]]'', [[Salon (website)|''Salon'']], [[Rumpus Magazine|''The Rumpus'']], ''[[McSweeney's Internet Tendency]]'', ''[[The Washington Post]]'', and ''[[All Things Considered]]''.

MacLeod worked as the Executive Story Editor for [[''Popular'' (TV Series)]] for the WB and wrote the pilot "Ivory Tower", commissioned by [[CBS]], produced by Brillstein-Grey ([[The Sopranos]]) and [[Diane Keaton]], with actress [[Jeanne Tripplehorn]] (Big Love). She served as the Artistic Director of the Kenyon Playwrights Conference <ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.kenyoninstitute.org/programs/playwrights-conference/|accessdate=2017-05-16|title=Kenyon Playwrights Conference}}</ref> which supported new work though its commissioning program and offered an intensive playwriting workshop taught by the artistic staff of partner companies including: [[The Atlantic Theater]], [[Playwrights Horizons]], [[Steppenwolf Theater]], [[Roundabout Theatre]], [[Hampstead Theater]], [[The Old Vic]], [[The Royal Court Theater]], [[La Jolla Playhouse]], and [[ACT Theatre]] in Seattle.

She is married to Read Baldwin and has two sons: Foss and Avery Baldwin.
