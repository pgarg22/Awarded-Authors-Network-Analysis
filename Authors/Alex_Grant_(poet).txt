'''Alex Grant''' is a Scottish-born American poet and instructor.<ref>{{cite news|last=Young |first=Nancy |title=Poet Alex Grant Connects with Fuquay-Varina Friday at Lazy Lion |url=http://www.fuquay-varinaindependent.com/view/full_story/18966642/article--Poet-Alex-Grant-connects-with-Fuquay-Varina-Friday-at-Lazy-Lion |archive-url=https://archive.is/20130123142839/http://www.fuquay-varinaindependent.com/view/full_story/18966642/article--Poet-Alex-Grant-connects-with-Fuquay-Varina-Friday-at-Lazy-Lion |url-status=dead |archive-date=January 23, 2013 |access-date=November 1, 2012 |newspaper=[[Fuquay-Varina Independent]] }}</ref>

==Biography==
===Background===
He was born in [[Greenock]], [[Inverclyde]], [[Scotland]],<ref name=PKP>{{cite journal|title=Poetry Final|journal=[[Phi Kappa Phi Forum]] | publisher=[[Phi Kappa Phi]] |date=Summer 2004|volume=84|issue=3|page=33}}</ref> and grew up in [[Kirkcaldy]], [[Fife]], Scotland.<ref>{{cite journal|last=Lodge|first=Oliver|title=Pirene's Fountain Interviews Alex Grant|journal=[[Pirene's Fountain]]|date=April 2011|volume=4|issue=9|url=http://www.pirenesfountain.com/archives/issue_09/showcase/alex-grant_interview.html|access-date=November 1, 2012|issn=1942-2067|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160303213724/http://www.pirenesfountain.com/archives/issue_09/showcase/alex-grant_interview.html|archive-date=March 3, 2016|url-status=dead}}</ref>

===Personal life===
Grant resides in [[Chapel Hill, North Carolina|Chapel Hill]], [[North Carolina]], with his wife, Tristi.<ref>{{cite journal|title=Alex Grant|journal=[[Pirene's Fountain]]|date=April 2010|volume=3|issue=7|url=http://www.pirenesfountain.com/archives/issue_07/current_issue/grant.html|access-date=November 1, 2012|issn=1942-2067|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160303211943/http://www.pirenesfountain.com/archives/issue_07/current_issue/grant.html|archive-date=March 3, 2016|url-status=dead}}</ref>

==Literary career==
Grant's work has appeared in ''[[Arts & Letters]]'', ''[[Best New Poets]] 2007'', ''[[Connecticut Review]]'', ''[[The Missouri Review]]'',  ''[[The Seattle Review]]'' and ''[[Verse Daily]]''.<ref name="One Pause">{{cite web| title=Alex Grant| url=http://www.onepausepoetry.org/explore/poets/profile/alex_grant|publisher= [[One Pause]]|access-date=November 1, 2012}}</ref> Grant has appeared on [[North Carolina Public Radio|WUNC]]'s ''[[The State of Things (radio show)|The State of Things]]'' show with [[Frank Stasio]].<ref>{{cite web|last=Stasio|first=Frank|title=Chains & Mirrors|url=http://wunc.org/tsot/archive/sot0516b08.mp3/view|work=The State of Things|publisher=North Carolina Public Radio|date=May 16, 2008|access-date=November 5, 2012|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080704180614/http://wunc.org/tsot/archive/sot0516b08.mp3/view|archive-date=July 4, 2008|url-status=dead|df=mdy-all}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|last=Stasio|first=Frank|title=The Poems of Wing Lei|url=http://wunc.org/tsot/archive/The_Poems_of_Wing_Lei.mp3/view|work=The State of Things|publisher=North Carolina Public Radio|date=May 11, 2012|access-date=November 5, 2012|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120720102904/http://wunc.org/tsot/archive/The_Poems_of_Wing_Lei.mp3/view|archive-date=July 20, 2012|url-status=dead|df=mdy-all}}</ref>

==Awards and honors==
Grant has been a six-time nominee for the [[Pushcart Prize]], an American literary prize.<ref name="One Pause" />

He has also received the following honors:
* [[Pavel Srut Poetry Fellowship]], 2004<ref>{{cite web|title=Prague Summer Program Update |url=http://www.wmich.edu/studyabroad/prague/newsletter.april.04.doc |publisher=[[Western Michigan University]] |access-date=November 1, 2012 |format=[[Microsoft Word]] |date=April 2004 }}{{dead link|date=June 2017 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref> (a program of [[Western Michigan University]]; named in honor of [[Czech people|Czech]] poet [[Pavel Srut]])
* [[The Pablo Neruda Prize in Poetry]]{{spaced ndash}} Honorable Mention, 2005<ref>{{cite web| title=The ''Nimrod''/Hardman Writing Awards|url=http://www.utulsa.edu/nimrod/awards.html| work = [[Nimrod (journal)|Nimrod]]|publisher=[[University of Tulsa]]|access-date=November 1, 2012|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20050830000431/http://www.utulsa.edu/nimrod/awards.html|archive-date=August 30, 2005}}</ref> (named in honor of [[Chilean people|Chilean]] poet [[Pablo Neruda]])
