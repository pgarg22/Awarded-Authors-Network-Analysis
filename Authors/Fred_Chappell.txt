'''Fred Davis Chappell''' (born May 28, 1936 in [[Canton, North Carolina]]) is an author and poet.<ref>[http://english.unc.edu/programs/wolfe/chappell_bio.html Fred Chappell Biography<!-- Bot generated title -->] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20071025022500/http://english.unc.edu/programs/wolfe/chappell_bio.html |date=2007-10-25 }}</ref>  He was an English professor for 40 years (1964–2004) at the [[UNCG English Department|University of North Carolina at Greensboro]].<ref>[http://www.unc.edu/news/archives/sep05/wolfe092305.htm UNC News release – Poet Fred Chappell to present Thomas Wolfe Lecture Oct. 6<!-- Bot generated title -->]</ref> He was the [[North Carolina Poet Laureate|Poet Laureate of North Carolina]] from 1997–2002.<ref>[http://www.ncarts.org/chappell/bio.cfm North Carolina Poet Laureate | Biography: Fred Chappell<!-- Bot generated title -->] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20050908013503/http://www.ncarts.org/chappell/bio.cfm |date=2005-09-08 }}</ref> He attended [[Duke University]].

His [[1968 in literature|1968]] novel ''[[Dagon (novel)|Dagon]]'', which was named the Best Foreign Book of the Year by the [[Académie française]], is a recasting of a [[Cthulhu Mythos]] [[horror story]] as a psychologically realistic [[Southern Gothic]].

His literary awards include the [[Aiken Taylor Award for Modern American Poetry]], the Prix de Meilleur des Livres Etrangers, the Bollingen Prize, and the T. S. Eliot Prize.
