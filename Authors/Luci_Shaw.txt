'''Luci Shaw''' (born December 29, 1928) is a Christian writer of poetry and essays.<ref name="Bio" /><ref name=":0">{{Cite web|url=https://www.lucishaw.com/about_vitae.html|title=Biographical Information|last=|first=|date=|website=Luci Shaw|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=13 August 2019}}</ref>

== Background ==

Shaw was born on December 29, 1928 in England.<ref name=":0" /> Her parents were medical missionaries, and she lived in [[Canada]] and [[Australia]] before moving to the United States to attend [[Wheaton College (Illinois)|Wheaton College, Illinois]].<ref name=":1">{{Cite web|url=https://chrysostomsociety.org/2011/05/luci-shaw/|title=Luci Shaw|last=|first=|date=|website=Chrysostom Society|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=13 August 2019}}</ref> Shaw graduated from Wheaton in 1953 with high honors.<ref name=":1" /><ref name=":4">{{Cite news|url=https://www.chicagotribune.com/news/ct-xpm-1989-11-19-8903100921-story.html|title=Poet Luci Shaw Deal Honestly with Doubts and Struggles|last=Stetson|first=Nancy|date=19 November 1989|work=Chicago Tribune|access-date=14 August 2019}}</ref>

Shaw became a naturalized citizen of the United States in 1995.<ref name=":0" />

Shaw is now Writer in Residence at [[Regent College]], Vancouver. She lectures on art and spirituality, the Christian imagination, poetry-writing, and journaling as an aid to artistic and spiritual growth.<ref>{{cite web|title=50 Women You Should Know|url=http://www.christianitytoday.com/ct/2012/october/50-women-you-should-know.html|publisher=[[Christianity Today]]|access-date=16 November 2012}}</ref>

She has published ten volumes of poetry (several still in print) and numerous [[non-fiction]] books, and has edited and collaborated on multiple other works, including several with [[Madeleine L'Engle]].<ref name=":2">{{Cite web|url=https://imagejournal.org/article/conversation-luci-shaw/|title=A Conversation with Luci Shaw|last=Overstreet|first=Anne M. Doe|date=|website=Image Journal|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=13 August 2019}}</ref> Her poems are widely [[Anthology|anthologized]].<ref name=Bio>Shaw, Luci. [http://lucishaw.com/about.html Biography].  Accessed October 16, 2007.</ref> Shaw usually works in [[free verse]], and typically her poems are quite short, less than a page. Nevertheless, in tone and content, she affiliates most readily with the [[Transcendentalism|transcendental]] poets, often finding in natural details and themes the [[American poetry#Postcolonial poetry|touch of the eternal or other-worldly]].{{Citation needed|date=August 2019}}

She is a charter member of the Chrysostom Society, an organization of published writers which "serves the Christian community by promoting the development of quality literature."<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://chrysostomsociety.org/|title=Chrysostom Society|last=|first=|date=|website=|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=13 August 2019}}</ref>

Shaw married Harold Shaw and had five children: Robin, Marian, John, Jeffrey, and Kristin.<ref name=":0" /> Shaw and her husband started a publishing house, Harold Shaw Publishers, in the basement of their home in 1972.<ref name=":1" /><ref name=":4" /> After Harold died from [[lung cancer]] in 1986, Shaw became president of Harold Shaw Publishers.<ref name=":1" /><ref name=":4" /><ref name=":3" /> Stephen Board became owner of Harold Shaw Publishers in 1990 and sold it to [[Random House]]'s [[WaterBrook Press]] in 2000.<ref>{{Cite web| title = PW: RH Acquires Harold Shaw| work = PublishersWeekly.com| access-date = 2019-10-14| url = https://www.publishersweekly.com/pw/print/20000131/21046-pw-rh-acquires-harold-shaw.html}}</ref>

Shaw married John Hoyte in 1991.<ref name=":3">{{cite book|last=Stiles|first=Peter|title=The Encyclopedia of Christian Literature, Volume 1|chapter=Luci Shaw (1928-)|year=2010|publisher=[[Scarecrow Press]]|pages=559–560|isbn=9780810872837|chapter-url=https://books.google.com/books?id=dk4G-52QT-8C&pg=PA559}}</ref> They are members of St. Paul's [[Episcopal Church (United States)|Episcopal Church]] in [[Bellingham, Washington]] where they currently live.<ref name="Bio" /><ref name=":2" />

== Use in music ==

A number of Shaw's works have been set to music, by a variety of composers:

* Alan Cline used "God in the Dark" as the basis for a [[cantata]].
* [[Knut Nystedt]] (Norwegian composer) did a setting for "Mary's Song", sung and recorded by the [[Elektra Choir]] of [[Vancouver]], British Columbia, Canada and appears on their album, ''Child of Grace''.
* [[Alice Parker]] (American) set three of Shaw's poems for a song cycle.
* Frederick Frahm (American) composed settings for [[Solo (music)|solo]] and [[choir]] for three of Shaw's poems, "Star Song," "Down He Came From Up," and "Heart Stable". Frahm also composed a Cantata for Michaelmas based on Shaw's poem “Angel Vision" and a Christmas Cantata (“From East to West”) based on texts by Shaw.
* Ed Henderson (Canadian) composed a [[Choral music|choral]] setting for "Star Song".
* Roland Fudge (English) composed a choral setting for "One", "Celestial Light", and "Steadfast Taper".
