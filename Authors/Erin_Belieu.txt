'''Erin Belieu''' (born September 25, 1965) is an American [[poet]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://belieucblock.weebly.com/|title=Erin Belieu|website=Erin Belieu|access-date=2019-02-13}}</ref>

==Early life==
Belieu was born and raised in [[Omaha, Nebraska|Omaha]], [[Nebraska]], graduating from Central High School. She received her [[Bachelor of Fine Arts]] at the [[University of Nebraska Omaha|University of Nebraska-Omaha]], where she learned how to construct poetry.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.sawpalm.org/interview-with-poet-erin-belieu.html|title=Saw Palm interview with Erin Belieu|website=Saw Palm: florida literature and art|language=en|access-date=2019-02-13}}</ref> Belieu then attended [[Boston University]], and [[Ohio State University]] receiving advanced degrees in the area of poetry.

==Career==
Belieu previously taught at [[Washington University]], [[Boston University]], [[Kenyon College]], [[Ohio University]], and [[Florida State University]]. She is presently on faculty at the University of Houston MFA/Ph.D. Creative Writing Program.<ref>http://www.english.fsu.edu/faculty/ebelieu.htm</ref>

Her work has appeared in places such as The New Yorker, ''The Atlantic Monthly'', ''Slate'',<ref>http://www.fray.slate.com/id/3333/year/1997/landing/1/</ref> ''Nerve'', ''The Yale Review'', ''TriQuarterly'', ''Ploughshares'', ''The New York Times'', ''Tin House'', and ''The Virginia Quarterly Review''.<ref>[http://www.vqronline.org/articles/2005/summer/belieu-last-gentlemen-heartbreakers/] {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090703052937/http://www.vqronline.org/articles/2005/summer/belieu-last-gentlemen-heartbreakers/ |date=July 3, 2009 }}</ref> Her poetry collections include ''Infanta, One Above & One Below, Black Box'' and ''Slant Six''.  She has served as managing editor of ''[[AGNI (magazine)|AGNI]]''.

In September 2006, Belieu was invited to join the Wave Press Poetry Bus Tour, along with notable poets such as [[Matthew Zapruder]], [[Joshua Beckman]], [[Eileen Myles]] and [[Arthur Sze]].

In August 2009, Belieu cofounded the national feminist organization VIDA: Women In Literary Arts, notable for its influential annual survey of the rates of publication between male and female authors. This VIDA survey, known as The Count, has been highly influential in addressing sweeping gender bias in contemporary American literary publishing. Belieu formerly served as VIDA's co director, along with notable American poet [[Cate Marvin]].

In August 2015, Belieu was one of 20 authors of ''Poets for Corbyn'', an anthology of poems endorsing [[Jeremy Corbyn]]'s the [[2015 Jeremy Corbyn Labour Party leadership campaign|campaign]] in [[2015 Labour Party (UK) leadership election|Labour Party leadership election]].<ref name="Poets for Corbyn">{{cite book |last=Bennetts|first=Russell|title=Poets for Corbyn|url=http://www.berfrois.com/uploads/2015/08/poets_for_corybn_v2_cover1.pdf|year=2015|publisher=Pendant Publishing|isbn=978-0-9928034-5-2}}</ref><ref name="theguardian">{{cite news |last=Bennetts|first=Russell|url=https://www.theguardian.com/books/booksblog/2015/aug/28/poets-for-jeremy-corbyn |title=Yes we scan: Poets line up for Jeremy Corbyn|newspaper=[[The Guardian]]|date=25 August 2015|access-date=15 July 2017}}</ref>
