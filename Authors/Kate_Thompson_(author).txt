'''Kate Thompson''' (born 10 November 1956)<ref name=LCCN/> is a British-Irish writer best known for [[children's literature|children's novels]]. Most of her children's fiction is [[fantasy]] but several of her books also deal with the consequences of [[genetic engineering]].

==Biography==
Katharine Anna Thompson<ref name=LCCN/> was born in [[Halifax, West Yorkshire|Halifax, Yorkshire]], the youngest child of the social historians and peace activists [[E. P. Thompson]] and [[Dorothy Towers]]. She has lived in Ireland since 1981 and many of her books are set there. She worked with horses and travelled in India, then settled in 1984 in [[Inagh]] in the west of Ireland with her partner Conor Minogue.<ref>[http://www.katethompson.info/biography.asp "katethompson.info ~Biography"] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130527042216/http://www.katethompson.info/biography.asp |date=27 May 2013 }}</ref> They have two daughters, Cliodhna and Dearbhla. She is an accomplished [[fiddle]]r with an interest in [[Music of Ireland|Irish traditional music]], which is reflected in ''The New Policeman''.

She won two major annual awards for ''[[The New Policeman]]'' (Bodley Head, 2005), set in modern [[Kinvara]] and the Irish mythological [[Tír na nÓg]]: the [[Guardian Children's Fiction Prize]]<ref name=prize2005/> and the [[2005 Whitbread Awards|Whitbread Children's Book Award]]. It also won the [[Irish Book Awards|Dublin Airport Authority Children's Book of the Year Award]] for 2005.

She has won the [[Bisto Book of the Year Awards|Bisto Children's Book of the Year Award]] four times, for ''The Beguilers'', ''The Alchemist's Apprentice'', ''Annan Water'' and ''The New Policeman''. ''Creature of the Night'' was shortlisted for the 2008 [[Booktrust Teenage Prize]] and the 2009 [[Carnegie Medal (literary award)|Carnegie Medal]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.carnegiegreenaway.org.uk/2009awards/carnegie_shortlist.php |title=2009 Awards: Carnegie shortlisted books |publisher=[[CILIP]] |accessdate=2012-06-10 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20120222024723/http://www.carnegiegreenaway.org.uk/2009awards/carnegie_shortlist.php |archivedate=22 February 2012 }}</ref>
