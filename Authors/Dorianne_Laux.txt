'''Dorianne Laux''' (born January 10, 1952 in [[Augusta, Maine]]) is an [[United States|American]] [[poet]].

==Biography==
Laux worked as a [[sanatorium]] cook, a gas station manager, and a maid before receiving a [[Bachelor of Arts|B.A.]] in English from [[Mills College]] in 1988.<ref name="poets.org">{{cite web|title=Dorianne Laux|url=http://www.poets.org/poet.php/prmPID/742|work=Poets.org|publisher=The Academy of American Poets|accessdate=February 5, 2013}}</ref>

Laux taught at the [[University of Oregon]]. She is a professor at [[North Carolina State University]]’s creative writing program, and the [[Master of Fine Arts|MFA]] in Writing Program at [[Pacific University]].<ref name="Pacific U">{{cite web|title=Core Faculty|url=http://www.pacificu.edu/as/mfa/faculty/index.cfm|publisher=Pacific University|accessdate=February 5, 2013}}</ref> She is also a contributing editor at [[The Alaska Quarterly Review]].

Her work appeared in ''[[American Poetry Review]]'', ''Five Points'', ''Kenyon Review'', ''Ms.'', ''Orion'', ''Ploughshares'',<ref>{{cite web|title=Dorianne Laux|url=http://www.pshares.org/read/author-detail.cfm?intAuthorID=880|work=Author Detail|publisher=Ploughshares|accessdate=February 5, 2013}}</ref> ''Prairie Schooner'', ''Southern Review'', ''TriQuarterly'', ''Zyzzyva''.<ref>{{cite web|title=Dorianne Laux|url=http://www.pw.org/content/dorianne_laux_1|work=Directory of Writers|publisher=Poets & Writers|accessdate=February 5, 2013}}</ref> She has also appeared in online journals such as ''[[Web Del Sol]]''.<ref name="Web Del Sol">{{cite web|title=Dorianne Laux|url=http://www.webdelsol.com/LITARTS/laux/|publisher=Web Del Sol|accessdate=February 5, 2013}}</ref>

Laux lives in [[Raleigh, North Carolina]] with her husband, poet [[Joseph Millar]].<ref name="Pacific U" /> She has one daughter.<ref name="Web Del Sol" />
