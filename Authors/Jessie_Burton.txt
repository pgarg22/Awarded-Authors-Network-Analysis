'''Jessica Kathryn Burton''' (born 17 August 1982)<ref name="picador2015">Inside back cover of 2015 [[Picador (imprint)|Picador]] UK paperback edition of ''The Miniaturist''</ref> is an English author and actress. {{As of|2021|}}, she has published three novels, ''[[The Miniaturist]]'', ''The Muse'', and ''The Confession,'' and a book for children, ''The Restless Girls''. All three novels were ''[[The Sunday Times|Sunday Times]]'' best-sellers, with both ''The Miniaturist'' and ''The Muse'' reaching no. 1, ''[[The New York Times|New York Times]]'' best-sellers, and Radio 4's ''[[Book at Bedtime|Books at Bedtime]].''  Collectively her novels have been published in almost 40 languages.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.panmacmillan.com/blogs/literary/jessie-burton-books-the-miniaturist-the-muse-the-confession|title=Home is where the heart is: Anita Sethi on Jessie Burton's books}}</ref> Her short stories have been published in ''Harpers Bazaar US'' and ''[[Stylist (magazine)|Stylist]].''<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|url=http://www.jessieburton.co.uk/index.html|title=Jessie Burton|website=Jessie Burton|access-date=9 February 2018}}</ref>

Burton is also a non-fiction writer. Her essays have been published in ''[[The Wall Street Journal]]'', ''[[The Independent]]'', ''[[Vogue (magazine)|Vogue]]'', ''[[Elle (magazine)|Elle]]'', ''Red'', ''[[Grazia]]'', ''Lonely Planet Traveller'' and ''[[The Spectator]]''.<ref name=":0" />

==Early life and career==
Burton studied at [[Lady Margaret School]],<ref name="LadyMargaret">{{cite news |last=Kellaway |first=Kate |authorlink=Kate Kellaway |url=https://www.theguardian.com/books/2016/jun/26/jessie-burton-success-failure-miniaturist-muse-interview |title=Jessie Burton: 'Success can be as fracturing to your self as failure' |work=[[The Observer]] |date=26 June 2016|access-date=26 December 2017}}</ref> [[Brasenose College, Oxford]], and the [[Central School of Speech and Drama]].<ref name="University">{{cite news |last=Blackman |first=Jaines |url=http://www.oxfordmail.co.uk/news/11885413.A_giant_success_for_Miniaturist_writer/ |title= Best-seller has been a life-changer for Jessie Burton |work=[[Oxford Mail]] |date=28 March 2015 |access-date=26 December 2017}}</ref>

Burton's work in theatre includes ''[[The Hour We Knew Nothing of Each Other]]'' at the [[Royal National Theatre|National Theatre]], London in 2008.<ref>[http://www.nationaltheatre.org.uk/thehour National Theatre : Productions : The Hour We Knew Nothing of Each Other] {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080517044711/http://www.nationaltheatre.org.uk/thehour |date=17 May 2008 }}</ref> As well as working as an author and actress, Burton has worked as a personal assistant in the [[City of London]].<ref name="picador2015" />

==Writing career==
Burton's 2014 debut novel ''[[The Miniaturist]]'' is set in 17th-century Amsterdam. The novel is inspired by [[Petronella Oortman]]'s dollhouse now at the [[Rijksmuseum]], although it does not otherwise attempt to be a [[biographical novel]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.usatoday.com/story/life/books/2014/08/29/jessie-burton-on-petronella-oortman-and-her-beautiful-cabinet-house/14757513/|title=Jessie Burton on the dollhouse that inspired her novel|last=Spiegelman|first=Ian|date=29 August 2014|work=[[USA Today]]|access-date=29 January 2015}}</ref> ''The Miniaturist'' took over four years to write. It was the subject of a bidding war at the April 2013 [[London Book Fair]].<ref name="bbc_ambitious" /> It was adapted as a [[The Miniaturist (TV series)|two-part miniseries]] for the [[BBC]] and [[Masterpiece (TV series)|PBS Masterpiece]] in 2017.

Burton's second novel, ''The Muse'', was published in 2016 and is set in a dual time-frame, during the [[Spanish Civil War]] and 30 years later in 1960s London.<ref>{{cite news | last = Quinn|first = Anthony| date = 25 June 2016| title = The Muse by Jessie Burton| url = https://www.theguardian.com/books/2016/jun/25/the-muse-jessie-burton-review-novel| newspaper = [[The Guardian]]|location = London| access-date = 29 June 2016}}</ref> It was nominated for the 2016 [[Books Are My Bag Readers' Awards]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.foyles.co.uk/news/BAMB-Winners-2016|title=Inaugural Books are My Bag Award Winners Announced|website=Foyles|access-date=6 November 2017}}</ref>

Burton's first novel for children, ''The Restless Girls'', was published in September 2018.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.bloomsbury.com/uk/the-restless-girls-9781408886908/|title=The Restless Girls|website =[[Bloomsbury Publishing]] |access-date=5 October 2018}}</ref> The story is based on the [[Brothers Grimm]] tale, ''[[The Twelve Dancing Princesses]].''<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.jessieburton.co.uk/the-restless-girls.html|title=THE RESTLESS GIRLS|website=Jessie Burton|access-date=9 February 2018}}</ref>

Her third novel for adults, ''The Confession'', was published in 2019.<ref name="Confession">{{cite news |last=Hickling |first=Alfred |url=https://www.theguardian.com/books/2019/sep/21/the-confession-by-jessie-burton-review |title=The Confession by Jessie Burton review â€“ an understated triumph |work=[[The Guardian]] |date=21 September 2019 |access-date=3 January 2020 }}</ref><ref name="ConfessionSpectator">{{cite news |last=Rhodes |first=Emily |url=https://www.spectator.co.uk/2019/10/jessie-burtons-the-confession-is-frankly-a-bit-heavy-handed/ |title=Jessie Burton's The Confession is, frankly, a bit heavy-handed |work=[[The Spectator]] |date=5 October 2019 |access-date=3 January 2020 }}</ref> ''Medusa,'' her second book for children, will be published in 2021.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.bloomsbury.com/uk/medusa-9781408886922/|title = Medusa}}</ref>
