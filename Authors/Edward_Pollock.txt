'''Edward Pollock''' (September 2, 1823 [[Philadelphia]] &ndash; December 13, 1858) was an [[United States|American]] [[poet]] best known for writing "The Parting Hour" in 1857.

==Life==
He worked as a child in a cotton factory.  He apprenticed with a sign-painter.  In 1852, he moved to [[San Francisco, California]],<ref>{{cite book| url=https://books.google.com/books?id=AFfqbTjG7OkC&pg=PA342 | title=A Literary history of the American West
| author=Western Literature Association | publisher=TCU Press| year=1987 | isbn=978-0-87565-021-0 }}</ref> and became a contributor to the ''Pioneer'' magazine.  In 1856, he was admitted before the [[California Supreme Court]].<ref>{{cite book| url=https://books.google.com/books?id=BMduZ2-RtaUC&pg=PA46 | title=The story of the files: a review of California writers and literature| author=Ella Sterling Mighels| author-link=Ella Sterling Mighels| publisher=Cooperative printing co.| year=1893 }}</ref>
