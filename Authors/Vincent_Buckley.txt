'''Vincent Thomas Buckley''' (1925–1988) was an Australian poet, teacher, [[Copy editing|editor]], essayist and critic.

==Life==
Buckley was born in 1925 in [[Romsey, Victoria|Romsey]], [[Victoria (Australia)|Victoria]] to Patrick Buckley, a carter and sometime farm labourer, and his wife Frances Margaret Buckley, née Condonto.  He attended St Patrick’s College in [[East Melbourne, Victoria|East Melbourne]]. living in a dormitory.<ref name=":2">{{Citation|last=Wallace-Crabbe|first=Chris|title=Buckley, Vincent Thomas (1925–1988)|url=https://adb.anu.edu.au/biography/buckley-vincent-thomas-12261|work=Australian Dictionary of Biography|place=Canberra|publisher=National Centre of Biography, Australian National University|language=en|access-date=2021-10-17}}</ref>

In 1942, Buckley worked for eight months as a clerk in the Commonwealth Department of Supply and Shipping.  On 13 December 1943, Buckley enlisted in the [[Royal Australian Air Force]], where he served as a recorder. <ref name=":0">[http://www.austlit.com/a/buckley/index.html ALM Australian Literature Resources – Vincent Buckley Contents page]</ref><ref name=":1">[http://www.unimelb.edu.au/150/150people/buckley.html The University of Melbourne 150th Anniversary]</ref><ref name=":2" /> On 5 February 1945, after spending several months in a military hospital in Sydney, he was discharged due to a disability.<ref name=":2" />

In 1946, Buckley entered [[University of Melbourne]] and on 12 July 1947, he married Edna Jean Forbes.  The couple had two daughters and later divorced.  In 1960, Buckley received a bachelor's degree from the university and in 1951 started teaching English there.  In 1954, Buckley was awarded a master's degree from the university.  That same year, he published his first poetry collection, ''The World’s Flesh.''<ref name=":2" />

In 1955, Buckley received a two-year scholarship to [[St Catharine's College, Cambridge|St. Catharine's College]], [[University of Cambridge|Cambridge]], in the United Kingdom to study the moral criticism of [[Matthew Arnold]], [[F. R. Leavis]] and T. S. Eliot.  During this period, he was able to take side trips to Ireland. <ref name=":2" />

In 1957, Buckley returned to the University of Melbourne and published his collection, ''Poetry: Mainly Australian.''  In 1958, he was named the Lockie Fellow at the university.   He edited the magazine, ''Prospect'', from 1958 to 1964, and was the poetry editor of the ''Bulletin'' from 1961 until 1963.  In 1966, Buckley published the collection ''Arcady and Other Places'' and in 1967 held a personal Chair in Poetry at the university.<ref name=":2" />  

Between 1967 and 1979, Buckley lived for periods in [[Ireland]], where he founded the Committee for Civil Rights.  In 1972, the University of Melbourne awarded him the Dublin prize. On 18 September 1976, in Middle Brighton, Buckley married Penelope Jane Curtis, a 33-year-old research student  In 1982, Buckley was awarded the [[Christopher Brennan Award|Christopher Brennan Award.]]  In 1987, due to bad health, Buckley retired from the university.<ref name=":2" />  

On 12 November 1988, Buckley died of a heart attack in [[Kew, Victoria]] .<ref name=":0" /><ref name=":1" />  He was buried in Melton Cemetery in [[Melton, Victoria]].

In 1992, the University of Melbourne established [[The Vincent Buckley Poetry Prize]].  Each year, it allows an Irish poet to visit Australia and an Australian poet to visit Ireland.<ref>[http://uninews.unimelb.edu.au/view.php?articleID=1174 ''Irish poet Mark Granier wins Vincent Buckley Poetry Prize'' (Media Release, 21 January 2004)]</ref>

==Themes and subject matter==
Buckley's subject matter ranged from the personal to the political, with a particular interest in Irish politics, culture and history. Buckley was also heavily involved in Catholic intellectual debate during the period of the [[Cold War]] and the emergence in Australia of the [[Democratic Labour Party (Australia)|Democratic Labour Party]].<ref name = "RVB">[http://home.iprimus.com.au/foo7/buckley.html ''Romsey and Vincent Buckley (1925–1988)]</ref>

Buckley's critical writing includes volumes on poetry, the novelist [[Henry Handel Richardson]], and [[Leonard French]]'s Campion paintings.<ref name = "RVB"/>
