'''Gregory Orr''' (born 1947 in [[Albany, New York]], United States) is an [[United States|American]] [[poet]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.poets.org/poet.php/prmPID/218 |title=Gregory Orr- Poets.org - Poetry, Poems, Bios & More |publisher=Poets.org |date= |accessdate=2014-02-12}}</ref>

[[File:GregOrr.jpg|thumb|Gregory Orr speaking at Plymouth Congregational Church in Minneapolis, Minnesota on Monday, April 9, 2018.]]
Featured on [[National Public Radio]]â€™s ''This I Believe'', Orr has been the recipient of fellowships from the Guggenheim Foundation and the National Endowment for the Arts, and of an Award in Literature from the American Academy of Arts and Letters.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.coppercanyonpress.org/authors/gregory-orr/ |title=Gregory Orr |access-date=2009-10-09}}</ref>

Gregory Orr wrote an opinion piece in the Sunday Magazine of the New York Times on August 29, 2014 about accidentally killing his brother in a hunting accident in response to the fatal shooting with an Uzi machine gun of a gun instructor by a 9 year old in Arizona.<ref>{{cite web|author=|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2014/08/31/opinion/sunday/reflections-on-a-shooting-range-death-from-one-who-knows.html |title=When a Child Kills, Reflections on a Shooting Range Death, From One Who Knows|date=2014-08-29 }}</ref>
