'''Nancy Springer''' (born 5 July 1948) is an American author of [[fantasy]], [[young adult literature]], [[Mystery fiction|mystery]], and [[science fiction]].<ref>{{cite web|last=Davis|first=Stephen M|title=The SF Site Featured Review: I am Mordred: A Tale from Camelot|url=http://www.sfsite.com/06b/mord35.htm|publisher=SF Site|access-date=25 September 2011}}</ref> Her novel ''Larque on the Wing'' won the [[James Tiptree, Jr. Award|Tiptree Award]] in 1994.<ref name=tiptree>{{cite web|title=Previous Awards—James Tiptree, Jr. Literary Award Council|url=http://tiptree.org/award/previous-awards|publisher=James Tiptree, Jr. Literary Award Council|access-date=25 September 2011|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120313222345/http://tiptree.org/award/previous-awards|archive-date=13 March 2012|url-status=dead}}</ref> She also received the [[Edgar Award]] from the [[Mystery Writers of America]]<ref name=edgars>{{cite web|title=Edgar Award Winners and Nominees Database|url=http://www.theedgars.com/edgarsDB/index.php|publisher=TheEdgars.com|access-date=25 September 2011}}</ref> for her novels ''Toughing It'' in 1995 and ''Looking for Jamie Bridger'' in 1996. Additionally, she received the Carolyn W. Field Award from the Pennsylvania Library Association in 1999 for her novel ''I am Mordred''.<ref name=field>{{cite web|title=Carolyn W. Field Award Winners|url=https://www.palibraries.org/page/CWFWinners|publisher=Pennsylvania Library Association|access-date=17 October 2020}}</ref> A prolific author, she has written more than fifty books over a career that has spanned nearly four decades.<ref name=":0" />

She released her first Enola Holmes book in 2006, following which she published 6 sequels in the series. Her other series include ''Book of Isle'' (fantasy) and the ''Tales of Rowan Hood''. Her work, ''[[The Enola Holmes Mysteries]]'', was adapted in 2020 as a Netflix film, ''[[Enola Holmes (film)|Enola Holmes]]''.<ref name=":0" />

==Biography==
Nancy Springer was born in [[Montclair, New Jersey]] to Harry E. and Helen Connor,<ref name=encyclopedia-1>{{cite web|title=Springer, Nancy 1948–|url=https://www.encyclopedia.com/arts/educational-magazines/springer-nancy-1948|website=Encyclopedia.com|access-date=17 October 2020}}</ref> moving to [[Gettysburg, Pennsylvania]] with her family when she was thirteen. 
As a child, she read lots about [[King Arthur]] and his [[Round Table]], [[Robin Hood]] and had often read and reread [[Sherlock Holmes]]. She was raised to "speak grammatically" and is well versed with [[Victorian literature]].<ref name=":0" /> Her parents were born in 1906 and 1909.{{fact|date=November 2020}} Her two older brothers had left the family for college by the time she hit puberty. Her mother was a professional artist, who painted oil portraits of pets. Springer was 14 when her mother's health began to deteriorate due to [[cancer]], [[menopause]] and an early-onset form of [[Alzheimer's disease|Alzheimer's]].<ref name=Nancyssite-1 /><ref name=":1" /> Her parents had purchased a motel, which she helped work. 

She remained in Pennsylvania for forty-six years, raising two children, Jonathan Paul (born in 1974) and Nora Lynn (born in 1978), by her first husband Joel Springer, a minister and fine art photographer. They were divorced in 1996. She met her second husband, Jaime Fernando Pinto, in 1999, while she was working in a no-kill animal shelter.<ref name=Nancyssite-1>{{cite web|title=About Nancy|url=https://www.nancyspringer.com/biography/|website=Nancy Spring|access-date=17 October 2020}}</ref>  In 2007, they moved to Bonifay, Florida, in a secluded part of the [[Florida Panhandle|Florida panhandle]], a place conducive to her hobbies of birdwatching, horseback riding and fishing, and his love of aviation.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|last=Bhattacharya|first=Suryasarathi|date=2020-09-23|title=Enola Holmes author Nancy Springer on her popular mystery series and the Netflix adaptation - Living News , Firstpost|url=https://www.firstpost.com/living/enola-holmes-author-nancy-springer-on-her-popular-mystery-series-and-the-netflix-adaptation-8801141.html|access-date=2020-10-06|website=Firstpost}}</ref><ref name=goodreads-1>{{cite web|title=Nancy Springer|url=https://www.goodreads.com/author/show/22547.Nancy_Springer|website=goodreads|access-date=17 October 2020}}</ref><ref name=":1">{{Cite web|last=Simmons|first=Tony|date=2019-08-13|title='Stranger Things' star Millie Bobby Brown shooting film based on Bonifay author's novels.|url=https://www.newsherald.com/news/20190813/stranger-things-star-millie-bobby-brown-shooting-film-based-on-bonifay-authors-novels|access-date=2020-09-27|website=Panama City News Herald|language=en}}</ref>
