'''Denis Cannan''' (14 May 1919 &ndash; 25 September 2011<ref name=Guardian>[https://www.theguardian.com/stage/2011/oct/17/denis-cannan-obituary Denis Cannan(obituary)], ''[[The Guardian]]'', 17 October 2011)</ref>) was a British dramatist, playwright and script writer. Born '''Denis Pullein-Thompson''', the son of Captain Harold J. Pullein-Thompson and novelist [[Joanna Cannan]], he changed his name by deed poll in 1964.<ref name=Guardian/> His younger sisters were [[Josephine Pullein-Thompson]], [[Diana Pullein-Thompson]] and [[Christine Pullein-Thompson]].

==Life==
Pullein-Thompson was born in [[Oxford]], he was educated at [[Eton College]].<ref>''Who's Who 2007''</ref> After attending Eton he worked as an actor, before joining the [[Queen's Royal Regiment (West Surrey)|Queen's Royal Regiment of West Surrey]] when the Second World War broke out, rising to the rank of captain and being mentioned in dispatches.

He was married to Joan Ross in 1946; the couple had two sons and a daughter. The marriage was dissolved, and he later remarried, to [[Rose Evansky]] in 1965.<ref name=Telegraph>[https://www.telegraph.co.uk/news/obituaries/culture-obituaries/theatre-obituaries/8865604/Denis-Cannan.html Denis Cannan(obituary)], ''[[Telegraph Media Group|The Telegraph]]'', 2 November 2011</ref>

Denis Cannan became a successful playwright and screenwriter known for his comedies.<ref name=Telegraph/> Apart from the plays listed below, he has written several screenplays for television and radio, also adaptions for television series. With [[Christopher Fry]] he adapted ''[[The Beggar's Opera]]'' for the [[The Beggar's Opera (film)|1953 film]] starring [[Laurence Olivier]].<ref name=Guardian/>
He also wrote the screenplay for the 1963 fim, [[Tamahine]], which should be compared in theme to [[The French Mistress]], from 1960, (itself adapted from the 1955 play).
