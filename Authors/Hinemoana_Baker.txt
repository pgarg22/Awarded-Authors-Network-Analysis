'''Hinemoana Baker''' (born 1968) is a New Zealand poet, musician and recording artist, teacher of creative writing and broadcaster.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.anzliterature.com/member/hinemoana-baker/|title=Hinemoana Baker – Academy of New Zealand Literature|work=Academy of New Zealand Literature|access-date=2018-10-14|language=en-US}}</ref>

== Biography ==
Baker was born in [[Christchurch]] in 1968 and grew up in [[Whakatane]] and [[Nelson, New Zealand|Nelson]], and descends from the [[Ngāi Tahu]] tribe in the [[South Island]] of New Zealand, and from [[Ngāti Raukawa]], [[Ngāti Toa]] and [[Te Āti Awa]] in the [[North Island]]. {{As of|2021}} she is living in [[Germany]], after completing 12 months as [[Creative New Zealand]]'s Berlin Writer in Residence in 2016.<ref name="Read NZ">{{Cite web|title=Baker, Hinemoana |url=https://www.read-nz.org/writer/baker-hinemoana/|access-date=5 September 2021|website=Read NZ Te Pou Muramura}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.hinemoana.co.nz|title=HOME|website=Hinemoana Baker|language=en-US|access-date=2018-10-14}}</ref><ref name="Aurelia">{{cite news |last1=Baker |first1=Hinemoana |title=The Sunday Essay: Letter from Aurelia |url=https://thespinoff.co.nz/the-sunday-essay/01-08-2021/the-sunday-essay-letter-from-aurelia/ |access-date=5 September 2021 |work=The Spinoff |date=1 August 2021}}</ref>

Baker holds an MA in creative writing from the [[International Institute of Modern Letters]] at [[Victoria University of Wellington]].

== Career ==
Baker's writing has been published in a number of journals and anthologies.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.anzliterature.com/member/hinemoana-baker/|title=Hinemoana Baker – Academy of New Zealand Literature|work=Academy of New Zealand Literature|access-date=2018-10-14|language=en-US}}</ref> Her works include the poetry collections ''mātuhi | needle'' (2004),<ref>{{Cite web|title=matuhi / needle|url=http://vup.victoria.ac.nz/matuhi-needle/|access-date=2018-10-14|website=Victoria University Press|language=en}}</ref> ''kōiwi kōiwi | bone bone'' (2010),<ref>{{Cite web|title=Koiwi Koiwi|url=http://vup.victoria.ac.nz/koiwi-koiwi/|access-date=2018-10-14|website=Victoria University Press|language=en}}</ref> ''waha | mouth'' (2014)<ref>{{Cite web|title=waha {{!}} mouth|url=http://vup.victoria.ac.nz/waha-mouth/|access-date=2018-10-14|website=Victoria University Press|language=en}}</ref> and ''funkhaus'' (2020).<ref>{{Cite book|last=Baker, Hinemoana, 1968-|url=https://www.worldcat.org/oclc/1153663524|title=Funkhaus|isbn=1-77656-314-X|location=Wellington, New Zealand|oclc=1153663524}}</ref>

As a musician she has recorded albums of original music. Her first album, ''puāwai'' (2004), was a finalist for the [[New Zealand Music Awards]] and the [[APRA Awards (New Zealand)|APRA Silver Scrolls]] Māori Language award.<ref>{{Cite web|last=|first=|date=|title=inemoana Baker awarded writer's residency in Berlin|url=http://wellington.scoop.co.nz/?p=79155|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=2020-11-08|website=Scoop|language=en}}</ref> Bas has co-edited the anthology ''Kaupapa: New Zealand Poets, World Issues'' in 2007 and the ''4th Floor'' online literary journal of [[Whitireia New Zealand|Whitireia Polytechnic]] in 2008.<ref name="Read NZ"/> She is one of six German and New Zealand poets to contribute to a collection ''Transit of Venus'',<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://gisborneherald.co.nz/lifestyle/2201030-135/transit-poets-know-no-bounds|title=Transit poets know no bounds|work=gisborneherald.co.nz|access-date=2018-10-14|language=en|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20181014091259/http://gisborneherald.co.nz/lifestyle/2201030-135/transit-poets-know-no-bounds|archive-date=14 October 2018|url-status=dead}}</ref> published by [[Victoria University Press]] in 2016.<ref name="Read NZ"/>

Baker has written two plays which were presented in [[Taki Rua|Taki Rua Theatre's ''Te Reo Māori Season'']]''. Māua Tāua'', (produced in 1995) and ''Pūkeko Tuawhā''.<ref>{{Cite web|last=|first=|date=|title=Hinemoana Baker 2004|url=https://www.bestnewzealandpoems.org.nz/past-issues/2004-contents/hinemoana-baker/|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=2020-11-08|website=ŌRONGOHAU {{!}} BEST NEW ZEALAND POEMS|publisher=International Institute of Modern Letters|language=en-NZ}}</ref>

In addition to her Creative New Zealand residency in Berlin,<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.stuff.co.nz/entertainment/books/69405058/hinemoana-baker-set-for-berlin-writers-residency|title=Hinemoana Baker set for Berlin writer's residency|website=Stuff|language=en|access-date=2018-10-14}}</ref> Baker was Arts Queensland Poet in Residence in 2009 and writer in residence with the International Writing Programme at the [[University of Iowa]] in 2010.<ref name="Read NZ"/> She spent 2014 as writer in residence at the International Institute of Modern Letters, Victoria University. She has appeared at festivals and events in New Zealand and in Australia, Indonesia, Europe and the US.

''Funkhaus'' was shortlisted for the [[Mary and Peter Biggs Award for Poetry]] at the 2021 [[Ockham New Zealand Book Awards]].<ref>{{Cite web|last=|date=2021-03-03|title=Ockham New Zealand Book Awards 2021 shortlists announced|url=https://www.booksandpublishing.com.au/articles/2021/03/03/163470/ockham-new-zealand-book-awards-2021-shortlists-announced/|url-status=live|access-date=2021-03-03|website=Books+Publishing|language=en-AU}}</ref>
