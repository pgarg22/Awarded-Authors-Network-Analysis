{{short description|American author of historical fiction}}
'''Nancy Horan''' is an American author of historical fiction. Her works include ''[[Loving Frank]]'', a novel about [[Mamah Borthwick]] and her relationship with American architect [[Frank Lloyd Wright]],<ref>Liesl Schillinger, [https://www.nytimes.com/2007/09/23/books/review/Schillinger-t.html "Notes on a Scandal"], ''New York Times'', September 23, 2007. Accessed April 9, 2014.</ref> and ''Under the Wide and Starry Sky'', a novel about the relationship between [[Robert Louis Stevenson]] and his wife.<ref>Susann Cokal, [https://www.nytimes.com/2014/01/19/books/review/under-the-wide-and-starry-sky-by-nancy-horan.html?_r=0 "In Sickness and in Health: 'Under the Wide and Starry Sky,' by Nancy Horan"], ''New York Times'', January 17, 2014. Accessed April 9, 2014.</ref> Horan was awarded the 2009 [[James Fenimore Cooper Prize for Best Historical Fiction]] by the [[Society of American Historians]] for ''Loving Frank''.<ref>The Society of American Historians. [http://sah.columbia.edu/content/past-winners-1 James Fenimore Cooper Prize: Past Winners] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150411013939/http://sah.columbia.edu/content/past-winners-1 |date=2015-04-11 }}. Accessed April 9, 2014.</ref>

A former resident of [[Oak Park, Illinois]], Horan was a middle school English teacher, a freelance journalist, and worked briefly in a public relations firm before moving to an island in Puget Sound, where she lives with her husband.<ref>Kevin Nance, [http://articles.chicagotribune.com/2014-01-24/features/chi-wide-and-starry-sky-nancy-horan-20140124_1_printers-row-journal-loving-frank-frank-lloyd-wright "Loving Robert Louis Stevenson"], ''Chicago Tribune'', January 24, 2014. Accessed April 9, 2014.</ref><ref>Julia Keller, [http://articles.chicagotribune.com/2008-08-03/news/0808010363_1_simple-answer-second-novel-publishers "Frankly, Nancy Horan followed her literary dream"], ''Chicago Tribune'', August 3, 2008. Accessed April 9, 2014.</ref>
